## 📖 单词详解

### 初三词汇

### 1. **coerce** /kəʊˈɜːs/ `初三`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：胁迫；强迫
- **英文释义**：to persuade someone to do something by using force or threats
- **例句**：
  - The witness was coerced into giving false testimony.
  - 证人被胁迫做了伪证。
  - No one should be coerced into making a decision against their will.
  - 不应该胁迫任何人做出违背自己意愿的决定。
- **助记**：co（一起）+ erce（力量）→ 用力量迫使在一起 → 胁迫；名词：coercion（胁迫）

### 2. **emulate** /ˈemjuleɪt/ `初三`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：效仿；模仿；赶超
- **英文释义**：to try to equal or surpass someone by imitating them
- **例句**：
  - Young athletes often emulate their sports heroes.
  - 年轻运动员经常效仿他们的体育偶像。
  - She emulated her older sister's study habits and got top marks.
  - 她效仿姐姐的学习习惯，取得了最高分。
- **助记**：emul（竞争）+ ate → 通过竞争来模仿 → 效仿；名词：emulation（效仿）

### 3. **fabricate** /ˈfæbrɪkeɪt/ `初三`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：捏造；伪造；制造
- **英文释义**：to invent false information; to manufacture
- **例句**：
  - The journalist was fired for fabricating stories.
  - 这名记者因捏造新闻被解雇。
  - The company fabricates electronic components for smartphones.
  - 该公司为智能手机制造电子元件。
- **助记**：fabric（织物/结构）+ ate → 编织出来的 → 捏造/制造；名词：fabrication（捏造）

### 4. **subjugate** /ˈsʌbdʒʊɡeɪt/ `初三`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：征服；制服；使屈从
- **英文释义**：to bring under control by force; to defeat
- **例句**：
  - The empire attempted to subjugate the neighboring kingdoms.
  - 帝国试图征服邻近的王国。
  - She refused to subjugate her own ambitions to please others.
  - 她拒绝压制自己的抱负来取悦他人。
- **助记**：sub（在下面）+ jug（轭）+ ate → 放在轭下 → 征服；名词：subjugation（征服）

### 5. **rapport** /ræˈpɔː(r)/ `初三`

- **词性**：n.
- **中文释义**：融洽关系；和谐
- **英文释义**：a close and harmonious relationship based on mutual understanding
- **例句**：
  - The teacher has an excellent rapport with her students.
  - 这位老师与学生之间关系非常融洽。
  - Building rapport with clients is essential in business.
  - 在商业中与客户建立融洽关系至关重要。
- **助记**：rap（联系）+ port（带来）→ 带来联系 → 融洽关系

### 6. **collateral** /kəˈlætərəl/ `初三`

- **词性**：n./adj.
- **中文释义**：抵押品；附带的；间接的
- **英文释义**：property pledged as security for a loan; additional but subordinate
- **例句**：
  - The bank requires collateral before approving a loan.
  - 银行在批准贷款之前需要抵押品。
  - The war caused significant collateral damage to civilian areas.
  - 战争对平民区域造成了重大附带损害。
- **助记**：col（一起）+ lateral（侧面的）→ 在一侧附带的 → 抵押品/附带的

### 7. **tenacious** /tɪˈneɪʃəs/ `初三`

- **词性**：adj.
- **中文释义**：顽强的；坚持不懈的
- **英文释义**：holding firmly to something; persistent and determined
- **例句**：
  - She is a tenacious student who never gives up on difficult problems.
  - 她是一个顽强的学生，遇到难题从不放弃。
  - The team's tenacious defense helped them win the championship.
  - 球队顽强的防守帮助他们赢得了冠军。
- **助记**：ten（抓住）+ acious → 紧紧抓住不放的 → 顽强的；名词：tenacity（顽强）

### 8. **benevolent** /bɪˈnevələnt/ `初三`

- **词性**：adj.
- **中文释义**：仁慈的；慈善的
- **英文释义**：well-meaning and kindly; generous in helping others
- **例句**：
  - The benevolent old man donated his fortune to charity.
  - 这位仁慈的老人把他的财产捐给了慈善事业。
  - A benevolent leader always puts the people's needs first.
  - 一个仁慈的领导者总是把人民的需求放在第一位。
- **助记**：bene（好）+ vol（愿望）+ ent → 有好愿望的 → 仁慈的；名词：benevolence（仁慈）

### 9. **conspicuously** /kənˈspɪkjuəsli/ `初三`

- **词性**：adv.
- **中文释义**：显眼地；引人注目地
- **英文释义**：in a way that is clearly visible or attracts attention
- **例句**：
  - He was conspicuously absent from the important meeting.
  - 他在重要会议上显眼地缺席了。
  - The error was conspicuously placed at the top of the page.
  - 错误醒目地出现在页面顶部。
- **助记**：conspicuous（显眼的）+ ly → 显眼地；形容词：conspicuous（引人注目的）

### 10. **coalesce** /ˌkəʊəˈles/ `初三`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：合并；联合；融合
- **英文释义**：to come together to form one mass or whole
- **例句**：
  - The small groups coalesced into a powerful movement.
  - 小团体联合成了一个强大的运动。
  - Different ideas coalesced into a single comprehensive plan.
  - 不同的想法融合成了一个综合计划。
- **助记**：co（一起）+ alesce（生长）→ 一起生长 → 合并；名词：coalescence（合并）

### 高一词汇

### 11. **ignominious** /ˌɪɡnəˈmɪniəs/ `高一`

- **词性**：adj.
- **中文释义**：可耻的；屈辱的
- **英文释义**：deserving or causing public disgrace or shame
- **例句**：
  - The team suffered an ignominious defeat in the final match.
  - 球队在决赛中遭受了可耻的失败。
  - His ignominious behavior brought shame to the entire family.
  - 他可耻的行为给全家带来了耻辱。
- **助记**：ig（不）+ nomin（名声）+ ious → 没有好名声的 → 可耻的；名词：ignominy（耻辱）

### 12. **proclivity** /prəˈklɪvəti/ `高一`

- **词性**：n.
- **中文释义**：倾向；癖好
- **英文释义**：a tendency or inclination to do something, especially something bad
- **例句**：
  - He has a proclivity for arriving late to meetings.
  - 他有开会迟到的倾向。
  - Her proclivity towards perfectionism sometimes slows her work.
  - 她追求完美的癖好有时会拖慢她的工作。
- **助记**：pro（向前）+ cliv（倾斜）+ ity → 向前倾斜 → 倾向

### 13. **obsequious** /əbˈsiːkwiəs/ `高一`

- **词性**：adj.
- **中文释义**：谄媚的；奉承的
- **英文释义**：excessively eager to please or obey someone in authority
- **例句**：
  - The obsequious waiter kept interrupting to ask if everything was fine.
  - 谄媚的服务员不断打断来问一切是否满意。
  - She despised the obsequious behavior of some of her colleagues.
  - 她鄙视一些同事谄媚的行为。
- **助记**：ob（朝向）+ sequ（跟随）+ ious → 拼命跟随的 → 谄媚的

### 14. **repudiate** /rɪˈpjuːdieɪt/ `高一`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：否认；拒绝；驳斥
- **英文释义**：to refuse to accept or be associated with something; to deny
- **例句**：
  - The government repudiated the allegations of corruption.
  - 政府否认了腐败指控。
  - He repudiated his earlier statements and apologized publicly.
  - 他否认了之前的声明并公开道歉。
- **助记**：re（回）+ pud（感到羞耻）+ iate → 因羞耻而推回去 → 否认；名词：repudiation

### 15. **impugn** /ɪmˈpjuːn/ `高一`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：质疑；抨击（品格/动机）
- **英文释义**：to dispute the truth or validity of; to challenge someone's honesty
- **例句**：
  - The lawyer impugned the credibility of the witness.
  - 律师质疑了证人的可信度。
  - She felt insulted when her colleague impugned her motives.
  - 当同事质疑她的动机时她感到受辱了。
- **助记**：im（进入）+ pugn（打击）→ 打击进去 → 质疑/抨击
