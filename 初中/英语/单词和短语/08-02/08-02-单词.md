## 📖 单词详解

### 初三词汇

### 1. **dismiss** /dɪsˈmɪs/ `初三`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：解雇；驳回；不予考虑
- **英文释义**：to refuse to consider something; to send someone away; to remove from a job
- **例句**：
  - The boss dismissed him for being late too many times.
  - 老板因为他太多次迟到而解雇了他。
  - Don't dismiss his idea — it might actually work.
  - 别不加考虑就否定他的想法——说不定真的行。
- **助记**：dis-（去掉）+ miss（送）→ 把人送走 → 解雇；驳回

### 2. **dispatch** /dɪˈspætʃ/ `初三`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：派遣；发送
- **英文释义**：to send someone or something to a particular place for a purpose
- **例句**：
  - The government dispatched rescue teams to the earthquake zone.
  - 政府向地震灾区派遣了救援队。
  - The package was dispatched yesterday and should arrive tomorrow.
  - 包裹昨天已发出，明天应该到。
- **助记**：dis-（分散）+ patch（修补）→ 分派出去修补 → 派遣

### 3. **displace** /dɪsˈpleɪs/ `初三`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：取代；迫使离开
- **英文释义**：to take the place of; to force someone to leave their home
- **例句**：
  - Robots may displace workers in some factories.
  - 机器人可能会取代一些工厂的工人。
  - The flood displaced thousands of families from their homes.
  - 洪水迫使数千家庭离开家园。
- **助记**：dis-（离开）+ place（地方）→ 离开原来的地方 → 取代；迫使离开

### 4. **dispute** /dɪˈspjuːt/ `初三`

- **词性**：n./v.
- **中文释义**：争论；纠纷
- **英文释义**：a disagreement or argument; to argue or question something
- **例句**：
  - There is a dispute between the two countries over the border.
  - 两国之间存在边境纠纷。
  - No one can dispute the fact that education is important.
  - 没有人能否认教育的重要性。
- **助记**：dis-（分开）+ pute（想）→ 想法不一致 → 争论

### 5. **dissolve** /dɪˈzɒlv/ `初三`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：溶解；解散
- **英文释义**：to become part of a liquid; to officially end an organisation or agreement
- **例句**：
  - Sugar dissolves quickly in hot water.
  - 糖在热水中很快溶解。
  - The parliament was dissolved and new elections were called.
  - 议会被解散，宣布新的选举。
- **助记**：dis-（分散）+ solve（松开）→ 分散松开 → 溶解

### 6. **distinct** /dɪˈstɪŋkt/ `初三`

- **词性**：adj.
- **中文释义**：明显的；独特的
- **英文释义**：clearly different or separate; easy to see, hear, or recognise
- **例句**：
  - There is a distinct difference between British and American English.
  - 英式英语和美式英语有明显的区别。
  - Each season has its own distinct charm.
  - 每个季节都有其独特的魅力。
- **助记**：di-（分开）+ stinct（刺）→ 能刺穿分辨的 → 明显的

### 7. **distort** /dɪˈstɔːt/ `初三`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：扭曲；歪曲
- **英文释义**：to change the shape or truth of something
- **例句**：
  - The mirror distorted my reflection and made me look taller.
  - 镜子扭曲了我的影像，让我看起来更高。
  - The media sometimes distorts the facts to attract attention.
  - 媒体有时会歪曲事实来吸引眼球。
- **助记**：dis-（偏离）+ tort（扭）→ 扭偏了 → 扭曲

### 8. **disturb** /dɪˈstɜːb/ `初三`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：打扰；使不安
- **英文释义**：to interrupt someone; to cause worry or anxiety
- **例句**：
  - Please don't disturb me while I'm studying.
  - 我学习的时候请不要打扰我。
  - The strange noise disturbed the whole neighbourhood.
  - 奇怪的声音让整个街区不安。
- **助记**：dis-（使…）+ turb（搅动）→ 搅动某人 → 打扰

### 9. **diverse** /daɪˈvɜːs/ `初三`

- **词性**：adj.
- **中文释义**：多种多样的；不同的
- **英文释义**：showing a great deal of variety; very different from each other
- **例句**：
  - Our school has a diverse student body from many countries.
  - 我们学校有来自多个国家的多元学生群体。
  - The city offers diverse cultural activities throughout the year.
  - 这座城市全年提供多种多样的文化活动。
- **助记**：di-（分开）+ verse（转）→ 往不同方向转 → 多种多样的

### 10. **domain** /dəˈmeɪn/ `初三`

- **词性**：n.
- **中文释义**：领域；范围
- **英文释义**：an area of knowledge or activity; a territory
- **例句**：
  - Artificial intelligence is a rapidly growing domain.
  - 人工智能是一个快速增长的领域。
  - This problem is outside my domain of expertise.
  - 这个问题不在我的专业范围内。
- **助记**：domain → "都没"（谐音）→ 这个领域都没人懂 → 领域

### 高一词汇

### 11. **dilapidated** /dɪˈlæpɪdeɪtɪd/ `高一`

- **词性**：adj.
- **中文释义**：破旧的；年久失修的
- **英文释义**：in a state of disrepair due to age or neglect
- **例句**：
  - The dilapidated building was finally torn down last year.
  - 那栋破旧的大楼去年终于被拆了。
  - They bought a dilapidated farmhouse and renovated it beautifully.
  - 他们买了一栋破旧的农舍，翻新得很漂亮。
- **助记**：di-（分开）+ lapid（石头）+ -ated → 石头都散了 → 破旧的

### 12. **dilettante** /ˌdɪlɪˈtænti/ `高一`

- **词性**：n.
- **中文释义**：业余爱好者；浅尝辄止的人
- **英文释义**：a person who has a superficial interest in an art or field without serious commitment
- **例句**：
  - He's merely a dilettante in music — he never practises seriously.
  - 他在音乐方面只是个业余爱好者——从不认真练习。
  - A true scholar is different from a dilettante.
  - 真正的学者和浅尝辄止的人不同。
- **助记**：dilettante → delight（喜悦）→ 只因喜欢而涉猎 → 业余爱好者

### 13. **diminish** /dɪˈmɪnɪʃ/ `高一`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：减少；削弱
- **英文释义**：to make or become less; to reduce in importance
- **例句**：
  - The heavy rain diminished to a light drizzle by noon.
  - 大雨到中午减弱成了毛毛雨。
  - Nothing can diminish the importance of a good education.
  - 没有什么能削弱良好教育的重要性。
- **助记**：di-（加强）+ mini（小）+ -sh → 变得更小 → 减少

### 14. **disdain** /dɪsˈdeɪn/ `高一`

- **词性**：n./v.
- **中文释义**：鄙视；不屑
- **英文释义**：the feeling that someone or something is beneath consideration; to look down upon
- **例句**：
  - She looked at the messy room with disdain.
  - 她不屑地看着凌乱的房间。
  - He disdained cheating as a way to succeed.
  - 他鄙视用作弊来获得成功。
- **助记**：dis-（否定）+ dain（尊敬）→ 不尊敬 → 鄙视

### 15. **disillusion** /ˌdɪsɪˈluːʒn/ `高一`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：使幻灭；使醒悟
- **英文释义**：to cause someone to realise that a belief they held is false
- **例句**：
  - The scandal disillusioned many fans of the celebrity.
  - 这桩丑闻让许多名人粉丝感到幻灭。
  - I was disillusioned with the job after only one month.
  - 才一个月我就对这份工作感到失望了。
- **助记**：dis-（去掉）+ illusion（幻想）→ 去掉幻想 → 使幻灭
