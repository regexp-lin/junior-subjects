## 📖 单词详解

### 初三词汇

### 1. **instinct** /ˈɪnstɪŋkt/ `初三`

- **词性**：n.
- **中文释义**：本能；直觉
- **英文释义**：a natural tendency to behave in a particular way without thinking or learning; an intuitive feeling
- **例句**：
  - Birds have an instinct to build nests.
  - 鸟类有筑巢的本能。
  - Her instinct told her something was wrong.
  - 她的直觉告诉她出了什么问题。
- **助记**：in-（向内）+ stinct（刺、刺激）→ 内在的驱动力 → 本能；直觉

### 2. **institute** /ˈɪnstɪtuːt/ `初三`

- **词性**：n./v.
- **中文释义**：研究所；建立
- **英文释义**：an organization that has a particular purpose, especially for education or research; to introduce or establish a system, rule, etc.
- **例句**：
  - She works at a medical research institute.
  - 她在一家医学研究所工作。
  - The government instituted new regulations for safety.
  - 政府建立了新的安全规章。
- **助记**：in-（加强）+ stitut（站立、建立）+ -e → 建立起来的机构 → 研究所；建立

### 3. **insult** /ɪnˈsʌlt/ `初三`

- **词性**：v./n.
- **中文释义**：侮辱
- **英文释义**：to say or do something that is offensive or rude to someone; an offensive remark or action
- **例句**：
  - He felt insulted when they laughed at his idea.
  - 他们嘲笑他的想法时他感到被侮辱了。
  - That remark was an insult to her intelligence.
  - 那句话是对她智力的侮辱。
- **助记**：in-（on）+ sult（跳）→ 跳上去攻击 → 侮辱

### 4. **insure** /ɪnˈʃʊər/ `初三`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：投保；确保
- **英文释义**：to protect oneself or something against possible loss by paying insurance; to make certain that something happens
- **例句**：
  - We insured our house against fire.
  - 我们给房屋投了火险。
  - Please insure that all doors are locked before leaving.
  - 请确保离开前所有门都锁好了。
- **助记**：in-（使）+ sure（确定）→ 使确定有保障 → 投保；确保

### 5. **intense** /ɪnˈtens/ `初三`

- **词性**：adj.
- **中文释义**：强烈的；紧张的
- **英文释义**：very strong or extreme; involving a lot of effort or activity
- **例句**：
  - The heat was so intense that we had to stay indoors.
  - 炎热如此强烈，我们不得不待在室内。
  - She has an intense interest in classical music.
  - 她对古典音乐有强烈的兴趣。
- **助记**：in-（加强）+ tens（伸展、拉紧）+ -e → 拉得很紧 → 强烈的；紧张的

### 6. **interact** /ˌɪntərˈækt/ `初三`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：互动；交流
- **英文释义**：to communicate with or react to each other; to act on or affect each other
- **例句**：
  - The teacher encourages students to interact with each other.
  - 老师鼓励学生互相交流。
  - Chemicals can interact to produce new substances.
  - 化学物质可以相互作用产生新物质。
- **助记**：inter-（相互）+ act（行动）→ 相互行动 → 互动；交流

### 7. **interior** /ɪnˈtɪriər/ `初三`

- **词性**：n./adj.
- **中文释义**：内部；室内的
- **英文释义**：the inner part of something; inside a building
- **例句**：
  - The interior of the house was beautifully decorated.
  - 房子内部装修得很漂亮。
  - We need interior design advice for our new apartment.
  - 我们需要室内设计建议来布置新公寓。
- **助记**：inter-（在…之间）+ -ior（比较级）→ 在内部的 → 内部；室内的

### 8. **interpret** /ɪnˈtɜːrprɪt/ `初三`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：解释；口译
- **英文释义**：to explain the meaning of something; to translate spoken words from one language to another
- **例句**：
  - How do you interpret this poem?
  - 你如何解读这首诗？
  - The guide interpreted for the foreign visitors.
  - 导游为外国游客做口译。
- **助记**：inter-（在…之间）+ pret（价值、价格）→ 在中间判定价值 → 解释；口译

### 9. **interval** /ˈɪntərvl/ `初三`

- **词性**：n.
- **中文释义**：间隔；间歇
- **英文释义**：a period of time between two events or actions; a pause or break
- **例句**：
  - There will be a 15-minute interval between the two acts.
  - 两幕之间将有15分钟的幕间休息。
  - Trains run at 10-minute intervals.
  - 火车每10分钟一班。
- **助记**：inter-（在…之间）+ val（墙）→ 两墙之间的空间 → 间隔；间歇

### 10. **intimate** /ˈɪntɪmət/ `初三`

- **词性**：adj.
- **中文释义**：亲密的
- **英文释义**：having a very close relationship; private and personal
- **例句**：
  - They have been intimate friends for many years.
  - 他们是多年的亲密朋友。
  - She shared her intimate thoughts only with her diary.
  - 她只把私密想法写在日记里。
- **助记**：intim（最里面的）+ -ate → 最内心的 → 亲密的

### 高一词汇

### 11. **jeopardize** /ˈdʒepərdaɪz/ `高一`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：危及；损害
- **英文释义**：to put something in danger of being harmed or destroyed; to risk
- **例句**：
  - Smoking can jeopardize your health.
  - 吸烟可能危及你的健康。
  - His careless actions jeopardized the entire project.
  - 他粗心的行为危及了整个项目。
- **助记**：jeopard（危险）+ -ize（使）→ 使处于危险 → 危及；损害

### 12. **judicious** /dʒuːˈdɪʃəs/ `高一`

- **词性**：adj.
- **中文释义**：明智的
- **英文释义**：having or showing good judgment; sensible and wise
- **例句**：
  - She made a judicious decision to save money for the future.
  - 她做出了明智的决定，为未来存钱。
  - A judicious use of resources will help the company succeed.
  - 明智地使用资源将有助于公司成功。
- **助记**：judic（判断）+ -ious → 善于判断的 → 明智的

### 13. **juxtapose** /ˌdʒʌkstəˈpoʊz/ `高一`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：并列；并置
- **英文释义**：to place two things next to each other for comparison or contrast
- **例句**：
  - The artist juxtaposed images of war and peace.
  - 艺术家将战争与和平的画面并列展示。
  - Juxtaposing the two versions reveals their differences.
  - 将两个版本并置能看出它们的差异。
- **助记**：juxta-（旁边）+ pose（放置）→ 放在旁边 → 并列；并置

### 14. **kinetic** /kɪˈnetɪk/ `高一`

- **词性**：adj.
- **中文释义**：运动的；动力学的
- **英文释义**：relating to or resulting from motion; characterized by movement
- **例句**：
  - Kinetic energy is the energy of motion.
  - 动能是运动的能量。
  - The sculpture has a kinetic quality—it seems to move.
  - 这座雕塑具有动感——它似乎在动。
- **助记**：kine（运动）+ -tic → 与运动有关的 → 运动的；动力学的

### 15. **labyrinth** /ˈlæbərɪnθ/ `高一`

- **词性**：n.
- **中文释义**：迷宫
- **英文释义**：a complicated network of paths or passages; something complex and confusing
- **例句**：
  - The garden has a labyrinth of hedges.
  - 花园里有灌木迷宫。
  - The legal system can be a labyrinth for ordinary people.
  - 法律制度对普通人来说可能像一个迷宫。
- **助记**：来自希腊神话中的迷宫（Labyrinth），传说中关押牛头怪的复杂建筑
