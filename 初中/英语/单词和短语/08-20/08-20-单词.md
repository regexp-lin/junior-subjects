## 📖 单词详解

### 初三词汇

### 1. **image** /ˈɪmɪdʒ/ `初三`

- **词性**：n.
- **中文释义**：形象；图像
- **英文释义**：a picture of someone or something; the way someone or something is perceived by others
- **例句**：
  - The company tried to improve its public image after the scandal.
  - 丑闻发生后，该公司试图改善其公众形象。
  - She saw her image reflected in the mirror.
  - 她看到镜中自己的影像。
- **助记**：image 源自拉丁语 imago"像、复制品"→ 事物的视觉呈现 → 形象；图像

### 2. **imitate** /ˈɪmɪteɪt/ `初三`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：模仿
- **英文释义**：to copy someone's behaviour, speech, or appearance; to reproduce something
- **例句**：
  - Children often imitate their parents' actions.
  - 孩子经常模仿父母的行为。
  - He can imitate the sound of various birds perfectly.
  - 他能完美地模仿各种鸟的叫声。
- **助记**：imitate 源自拉丁语 imitari"复制"→ 照着做 → 模仿

### 3. **immense** /ɪˈmens/ `初三`

- **词性**：adj.
- **中文释义**：巨大的
- **英文释义**：extremely large; enormous
- **例句**：
  - The project required an immense amount of work.
  - 这个项目需要大量的工作。
  - The view from the mountain top was truly immense.
  - 山顶的景色无比壮阔。
- **助记**：im-（不）+ mense（测量）→ 无法测量的 → 巨大的

### 4. **immune** /ɪˈmjuːn/ `初三`

- **词性**：adj.
- **中文释义**：免疫的
- **英文释义**：protected against a particular disease; not affected by something
- **例句**：
  - After recovery, he became immune to that virus.
  - 康复后，他对该病毒产生了免疫力。
  - No one is immune to criticism.
  - 没有人能免受批评。
- **助记**：immune 源自拉丁语 immunis"豁免"→ 免除（疾病）侵害的 → 免疫的

### 5. **impact** /ˈɪmpækt/ `初三`

- **词性**：n./v.
- **中文释义**：影响；冲击
- **英文释义**：the strong effect or influence of something; a collision or hitting with force
- **例句**：
  - Social media has a huge impact on young people's lives.
  - 社交媒体对年轻人的生活影响巨大。
  - The comet's impact with Earth would be catastrophic.
  - 彗星撞击地球将是灾难性的。
- **助记**：im-（向内）+ pact（撞击）→ 撞进去 → 冲击；影响

### 6. **import** /ɪmˈpɔːrt/ `初三`

- **词性**：v./n.
- **中文释义**：进口
- **英文释义**：to bring goods or services into a country from abroad; goods or services brought in from another country
- **例句**：
  - China imports oil from many countries.
  - 中国从多个国家进口石油。
  - The import of luxury goods has increased significantly.
  - 奢侈品的进口大幅增加。
- **助记**：im-（进入）+ port（港口）→ 从港口运进来 → 进口

### 7. **impose** /ɪmˈpoʊz/ `初三`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：强加；征收
- **英文释义**：to force something on someone; to officially order a tax, fine, etc.
- **例句**：
  - The government imposed a new tax on luxury goods.
  - 政府对奢侈品征收了新税。
  - She didn't want to impose on their hospitality.
  - 她不想强求他们招待。
- **助记**：im-（ upon）+ pose（放置）→ 把…放在（某人）身上 → 强加；征收

### 8. **impress** /ɪmˈpres/ `初三`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：使印象深刻
- **英文释义**：to make someone feel admiration or respect; to have a strong positive effect on someone
- **例句**：
  - Her performance impressed the judges.
  - 她的表现给评委留下了深刻印象。
  - I was impressed by his knowledge of history.
  - 他对历史的了解令我印象深刻。
- **助记**：im-（向内）+ press（压）→ 压入脑海 → 使印象深刻

### 9. **impulse** /ˈɪmpʌls/ `初三`

- **词性**：n.
- **中文释义**：冲动；脉冲
- **英文释义**：a sudden strong desire to do something; a short burst of energy or signal
- **例句**：
  - He bought the watch on impulse.
  - 他一时冲动买了那块表。
  - She resisted the impulse to shout at him.
  - 她克制住了想朝他大喊的冲动。
- **助记**：im-（推动）+ pulse（脉搏）→ 推动的力 → 冲动；脉冲

### 10. **incident** /ˈɪnsɪdənt/ `初三`

- **词性**：n.
- **中文释义**：事件；事故
- **英文释义**：something that happens, especially something unusual or unpleasant
- **例句**：
  - There was a minor incident at the border.
  - 边境发生了一起小事件。
  - The incident was reported in the local newspaper.
  - 这起事故被刊登在地方报纸上。
- **助记**：in-（ onto）+ cid（落）+ -ent → 落在…上的事 → 事件；事故

### 高一词汇

### 11. **innate** /ɪˈneɪt/ `高一`

- **词性**：adj.
- **中文释义**：天生的；固有的
- **英文释义**：existing from birth; natural and not learned
- **例句**：
  - She has an innate talent for music.
  - 她天生有音乐天赋。
  - Humans have an innate desire to belong.
  - 人类天生渴望归属感。
- **助记**：in-（内）+ nate（出生）→ 与生俱来的 → 天生的；固有的

### 12. **insatiable** /ɪnˈseɪʃəbl/ `高一`

- **词性**：adj.
- **中文释义**：贪得无厌的
- **英文释义**：impossible to satisfy; always wanting more
- **例句**：
  - He has an insatiable appetite for success.
  - 他对成功有着贪得无厌的渴望。
  - The child's insatiable curiosity drove his parents crazy.
  - 孩子永无止境的好奇心让父母头疼。
- **助记**：in-（不）+ satiable（可满足的）→ 无法满足的 → 贪得无厌的

### 13. **insidious** /ɪnˈsɪdiəs/ `高一`

- **词性**：adj.
- **中文释义**：阴险的；潜伏的
- **英文释义**：spreading gradually and harmfully without being noticed; subtly treacherous
- **例句**：
  - The disease has an insidious onset with few early symptoms.
  - 这种疾病起病隐匿，早期症状很少。
  - She was the victim of insidious gossip that spread slowly.
  - 她是慢慢传播的阴险流言的受害者。
- **助记**：in-（向内）+ sid（坐）+ -ious → 坐着悄悄潜入的 → 阴险的；潜伏的

### 14. **instigate** /ˈɪnstɪɡeɪt/ `高一`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：煽动；教唆
- **英文释义**：to cause something to happen, especially something bad; to urge or encourage someone to do something wrong
- **例句**：
  - He was accused of instigating the riot.
  - 他被指控煽动骚乱。
  - Someone must have instigated him to steal the money.
  - 一定有人教唆他偷了那笔钱。
- **助记**：in-（加强）+ stig（刺、驱使）+ -ate → 用刺激驱使 → 煽动；教唆

### 15. **intangible** /ɪnˈtændʒəbl/ `高一`

- **词性**：adj.
- **中文释义**：无形的；难以捉摸的
- **英文释义**：not able to be touched; difficult to define or grasp
- **例句**：
  - Trust is an intangible asset that takes years to build.
  - 信任是无形资产，需要多年才能建立。
  - There was an intangible atmosphere of tension in the room.
  - 房间里有一种难以捉摸的紧张气氛。
- **助记**：in-（不）+ tangible（可触摸的）→ 触摸不到的 → 无形的；难以捉摸的
