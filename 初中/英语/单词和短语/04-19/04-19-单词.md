## 📖 单词详解

### 初三词汇

### 1. **circumscribe** /ˈsɜːkəmskraɪb/ `初三`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：限制；约束；在…周围画线
- **英文释义**：to restrict or limit something; to draw a line around
- **例句**：
  - The government's power is circumscribed by the constitution.
  - 政府的权力受到宪法的限制。
  - Our options are circumscribed by the available budget.
  - 我们的选择受到可用预算的限制。
- **助记**：circum（周围）+ scribe（写/画）→ 在周围画线 → 限制

### 2. **supplant** /səˈplɑːnt/ `初三`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：取代；替换
- **英文释义**：to take the place of someone or something
- **例句**：
  - E-books have not yet supplanted printed books.
  - 电子书还没有完全取代纸质书。
  - New technology is gradually supplanting traditional methods.
  - 新技术正在逐渐取代传统方法。
- **助记**：sup（在下面）+ plant（种植）→ 在下面种上新的 → 取代

### 3. **insinuate** /ɪnˈsɪnjueɪt/ `初三`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：暗示；含沙射影
- **英文释义**：to suggest something bad in an indirect way
- **例句**：
  - Are you insinuating that I cheated on the test?
  - 你是在暗示我在考试中作弊了吗？
  - She insinuated that the project failed because of poor leadership.
  - 她含沙射影地说项目失败是因为领导不力。
- **助记**：in（进入）+ sinu（弯曲）+ ate → 弯弯绕绕地进入 → 暗示；名词：insinuation

### 4. **abrogate** /ˈæbrəɡeɪt/ `初三`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：废除；取消（法律/协议）
- **英文释义**：to officially end a law, agreement, or practice
- **例句**：
  - The government decided to abrogate the outdated law.
  - 政府决定废除过时的法律。
  - The treaty was abrogated after both sides failed to reach a compromise.
  - 双方未能达成妥协后，条约被废除。
- **助记**：ab（离开）+ rog（提议/法律）+ ate → 把法律拿走 → 废除；名词：abrogation

### 5. **trajectory** /trəˈdʒektəri/ `初三`

- **词性**：n.
- **中文释义**：轨迹；弹道；发展轨迹
- **英文释义**：the path followed by a moving object; the course of development
- **例句**：
  - The trajectory of the rocket was carefully monitored by scientists.
  - 科学家们仔细监测了火箭的轨迹。
  - Her career trajectory has been impressive — from intern to CEO.
  - 她的职业发展轨迹令人印象深刻——从实习生到CEO。
- **助记**：tra（穿过）+ ject（投/掷）+ ory → 投掷穿过的路径 → 轨迹

### 6. **precinct** /ˈpriːsɪŋkt/ `初三`

- **词性**：n.
- **中文释义**：区域；管辖区；选区
- **英文释义**：a division of a city for administrative or electoral purposes
- **例句**：
  - The shopping precinct was crowded during the holiday season.
  - 假日季节购物区非常拥挤。
  - Voters must go to their assigned precinct to cast their ballots.
  - 选民必须到指定的选区投票。
- **助记**：pre（前）+ cinct（围绕）→ 被围起来的区域 → 管辖区

### 7. **fortuitous** /fɔːˈtjuːɪtəs/ `初三`

- **词性**：adj.
- **中文释义**：偶然的；幸运的
- **英文释义**：happening by chance; lucky
- **例句**：
  - Their meeting in Paris was entirely fortuitous.
  - 他们在巴黎的相遇纯属偶然。
  - A fortuitous discovery led to a major scientific breakthrough.
  - 一个偶然的发现促成了一项重大的科学突破。
- **助记**：fortuit（偶然）+ ous → 偶然的；近义词：accidental, coincidental

### 8. **pernicious** /pəˈnɪʃəs/ `初三`

- **词性**：adj.
- **中文释义**：有害的；恶性的
- **英文释义**：having a very harmful effect, especially in a gradual way
- **例句**：
  - Gossip can have a pernicious effect on people's reputations.
  - 八卦可能对人们的名声产生有害影响。
  - The pernicious influence of social media addiction is a growing concern.
  - 社交媒体成瘾的恶性影响越来越令人担忧。
- **助记**：per（完全）+ nic（伤害）+ ious → 完全造成伤害的 → 有害的

### 9. **inordinately** /ɪnˈɔːdɪnətli/ `初三`

- **词性**：adv.
- **中文释义**：过度地；极其
- **英文释义**：to an unusually or excessively large degree
- **例句**：
  - She spent an inordinately long time getting ready for the party.
  - 她花了过多的时间为聚会做准备。
  - The task proved inordinately difficult for the students.
  - 这个任务对学生们来说难度过大。
- **助记**：in（不）+ ordinately（有序地）→ 不按正常秩序的 → 过度地；形容词：inordinate

### 10. **amalgamate** /əˈmælɡəmeɪt/ `初三`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：合并；混合
- **英文释义**：to combine or unite two or more things into one
- **例句**：
  - The two schools amalgamated to form a larger institution.
  - 两所学校合并成了一所更大的学校。
  - The company amalgamated several departments to improve efficiency.
  - 公司合并了几个部门以提高效率。
- **助记**：a + malgam（混合物）+ ate → 使混合 → 合并；名词：amalgamation（合并）

### 高一词汇

### 11. **recalcitrant** /rɪˈkælsɪtrənt/ `高一`

- **词性**：adj.
- **中文释义**：顽抗的；不服从的
- **英文释义**：stubbornly refusing to obey rules or follow instructions
- **例句**：
  - The recalcitrant student refused to follow the teacher's instructions.
  - 那个不服从的学生拒绝遵从老师的指示。
  - Dealing with recalcitrant employees requires patience and firm leadership.
  - 处理不服从的员工需要耐心和坚定的领导力。
- **助记**：re（回）+ calcitrant（踢）→ 往回踢 → 顽抗的

### 12. **belligerent** /bəˈlɪdʒərənt/ `高一`

- **词性**：adj.
- **中文释义**：好斗的；寻衅的；交战的
- **英文释义**：hostile and aggressive; engaged in a war
- **例句**：
  - His belligerent attitude made it impossible to have a calm discussion.
  - 他好斗的态度使冷静的讨论变得不可能。
  - The belligerent nations finally agreed to a ceasefire.
  - 交战国终于同意停火。
- **助记**：belli（战争）+ ger（进行）+ ent → 进行战争的 → 好斗的；名词：belligerence

### 13. **exonerate** /ɪɡˈzɒnəreɪt/ `高一`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：免除责任；宣布无罪
- **英文释义**：to officially declare someone not guilty of a crime or free from blame
- **例句**：
  - DNA evidence exonerated the man who had been wrongly imprisoned for 20 years.
  - DNA证据为被错误关押20年的男子洗清了罪名。
  - The investigation exonerated the company from any wrongdoing.
  - 调查为该公司免除了任何不当行为的责任。
- **助记**：ex（出）+ oner（负担）+ ate → 把负担拿出去 → 免除责任；名词：exoneration

### 14. **acquiesce** /ˌækwiˈes/ `高一`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：默许；默认；勉强同意
- **英文释义**：to accept something reluctantly but without protest
- **例句**：
  - She acquiesced to the plan, although she had reservations about it.
  - 她虽然有保留意见，但还是默许了这个计划。
  - The workers acquiesced in the new regulations to avoid further conflict.
  - 工人们为了避免进一步冲突而默认了新规定。
- **助记**：ac（朝向）+ quiesce（安静）→ 朝着安静接受 → 默许；名词：acquiescence

### 15. **inexorable** /ɪnˈeksərəbl/ `高一`

- **词性**：adj.
- **中文释义**：不可阻挡的；无法改变的
- **英文释义**：impossible to stop or prevent; relentless
- **例句**：
  - The inexorable march of technology continues to change our lives.
  - 科技不可阻挡的进步持续改变着我们的生活。
  - Despite protests, the inexorable rise in housing prices continued.
  - 尽管有抗议，房价的不可阻挡的上涨仍在继续。
- **助记**：in（不）+ exor（请求）+ able → 无法请求改变的 → 不可阻挡的；副词：inexorably
