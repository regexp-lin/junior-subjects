# 中考英语完形填空：校医院的护士

## 完形填空原文

I had always been nervous about the school hospital. The white walls and the smell of medicine made me ___1___. But last semester, I sprained my ankle during PE class and had to go there.

Nurse Wang met me at the door. She had a warm smile and gentle eyes. "Don't worry," she said. "Let me take a ___2___ at it." Her calm voice made me feel safer.

She helped me sit down and examined my ankle carefully. While she was wrapping it with a bandage, she asked about my grade and favorite subjects. The ___3___ made me forget the pain a little. She wasn't just treating my injury — she was treating me as a person.

"I see a lot of students here," Nurse Wang said. "Some come with colds, some with stomachaches. But do you know what many of them really need? Someone to ___4___. They're stressed about exams, worried about friends, or feeling lonely. I try to listen."

Her words stayed with me. I had never thought of the school hospital as a place for emotional support. But Nurse Wang had turned it into a ___5___ — a place where students could feel heard.

Over the next few weeks, I had to visit the hospital several times to change the bandage. Each time, Nurse Wang remembered my name and asked how I was doing. She even noticed when I looked sad one day. "Everything okay?" she asked. I wasn't used to adults ___6___ me like that. Most teachers only asked about grades.

I told her about the pressure I felt — the fear of disappointing my parents, the competition with my classmates. She listened without judging. "It's okay to feel overwhelmed," she said. "But remember, your worth is not ___7___ by your grades. You're more than a score."

Those words changed my perspective. I had been measuring myself only by numbers. Nurse Wang helped me see that I was ___8___ of respect and care, no matter what my test results said.

Now I'm not afraid of the school hospital anymore. I even stop by sometimes just to say hello. Nurse Wang taught me that healing isn't only about medicine — it's also about ___9___ and being seen. In a place that could feel cold and clinical, she created warmth. That's a kind of ___10___ that no textbook can teach.

## 答案与解析

**1. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 形容词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：白墙和药味让我___。A 项意为"兴奋"；B 项意为"不舒服"；C 项意为"放松"；D 项意为"自豪"。"uncomfortable"与"nervous about"呼应，医院环境让人不适。故选 B。

**2. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 固定搭配
**【解析】** 本句意为：让我___一下。A 项意为"拍照"；B 项意为"看一下"；C 项意为"走"；D 项意为"休息"。"take a look at"（看一下）是检查伤口的常用说法。故选 B。

**3. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 名词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：___让我稍微忘了疼痛。A 项意为"问题"；B 项意为"对话"；C 项意为"沉默"；D 项意为"药"。"conversation"与护士询问年级、爱好对应，分散注意力。故选 B。

**4. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：许多人真正需要的是有人___。A 项意为"教"；B 项意为"倾听"；C 项意为"惩罚"；D 项意为"跟随"。"someone to listen"与后文"I try to listen"呼应。故选 B。

**5. 【答案】C**
**【考点】** 名词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：她把它变成了一个___。A 项意为"教室"；B 项意为"操场"；C 项意为"避风港"；D 项意为"图书馆"。"shelter"（庇护所）形容能让人感到被倾听、被支持的地方。故选 C。

**6. 【答案】C**
**【考点】** 动词短语辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：我不习惯成年人这样___我。A 项意为"表扬"；B 项意为"检查"；C 项意为"关心"；D 项意为"嘲笑"。"caring about"与护士主动询问、关注情绪对应。故选 C。

**7. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：你的价值不是由成绩___的。A 项意为"决定"；B 项意为"衡量"；C 项意为"提高"；D 项意为"忘记"。"measured by"（用……衡量）与"your worth"搭配。故选 B。

**8. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 形容词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：我___被尊重和关心。A 项意为"充满"；B 项意为"值得"；C 项意为"疲惫"；D 项意为"害怕"。"worthy of"（值得）是固定搭配。故选 B。

**9. 【答案】C**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：治愈不仅是药物，也关乎___和被看见。A 项意为"学习"；B 项意为"竞争"；C 项意为"倾听"；D 项意为"争论"。"listening"与全文护士倾听学生的主题一致。故选 C。

**10. 【答案】C**
**【考点】** 名词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：这是一种教科书无法教授的___。A 项意为"技能"；B 项意为"药物"；C 项意为"智慧"；D 项意为"考试"。"wisdom"（智慧）指护士展现的人文关怀与处世态度。故选 C。
