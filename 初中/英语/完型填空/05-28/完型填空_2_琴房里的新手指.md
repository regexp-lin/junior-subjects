# 中考英语完形填空：琴房里的新手指

## 完形填空原文

I started learning piano when I was twelve. Most of my classmates had begun years earlier. When I walked into the practice room for the first time, I could hear beautiful music from the next room. The girl there was playing a piece I had only heard on the radio. I felt so ___1___ that I almost wanted to quit before I began.

My teacher, Mr. Chen, noticed my expression. "Don't compare yourself to others," he said. "Everyone has their own ___2___. You're here to write yours, not to copy someone else's."

His words comforted me a little. But the first weeks were still difficult. My fingers were stiff. I often hit the wrong keys. The simple tunes I played sounded nothing like the music I had ___3___. I practiced for an hour every day after school. Sometimes my fingertips hurt. I wondered if I would ever get better.

One afternoon, I was practicing a scale when I heard a knock on the door. It was the girl from the next room — the one who played so well. "I used to sound like that too," she said with a smile. "When I first started, I ___4___ my teacher would ask me to leave. But he never did. He said every expert was once a beginner."

Her words gave me ___5___. I kept practicing. Month after month, my fingers became more flexible. The wrong notes became fewer. One day, I finally played a complete piece without mistakes. I was so ___6___ that I ran to tell Mr. Chen. He smiled and said, "See? I told you. Your story is different from everyone else's — but it's yours."

The girl and I became friends. We sometimes practiced together. She taught me how to listen to the music inside me, not just the notes on the page. "Music is not about being ___7___," she said. "It's about expressing what you feel."

Now I'm in my third year of piano. I'm not the best in our school, but I'm proud of how far I've come. I still remember those ___8___ days when every note seemed impossible. They taught me that the best growth happens when we refuse to ___9___. Every expert was once a beginner — and every beginner can become an expert, as long as they keep playing their own ___10___, one note at a time.

## 选项

1. A. excited &emsp;&emsp; B. proud &emsp;&emsp; C. discouraged &emsp;&emsp; D. interested
2. A. music &emsp;&emsp; B. story &emsp;&emsp; C. dream &emsp;&emsp; D. talent
3. A. written &emsp;&emsp; B. imagined &emsp;&emsp; C. heard &emsp;&emsp; D. loved
4. A. hoped &emsp;&emsp; B. feared &emsp;&emsp; C. believed &emsp;&emsp; D. wished
5. A. time &emsp;&emsp; B. hope &emsp;&emsp; C. help &emsp;&emsp; D. advice
6. A. sad &emsp;&emsp; B. tired &emsp;&emsp; C. excited &emsp;&emsp; D. worried
7. A. first &emsp;&emsp; B. best &emsp;&emsp; C. perfect &emsp;&emsp; D. fast
8. A. happy &emsp;&emsp; B. easy &emsp;&emsp; C. early &emsp;&emsp; D. difficult
9. A. try &emsp;&emsp; B. practice &emsp;&emsp; C. give up &emsp;&emsp; D. slow down
10. A. piece &emsp;&emsp; B. song &emsp;&emsp; C. tune &emsp;&emsp; D. melody

## 答案与解析

**1. 【答案】C**
**【考点】** 形容词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：我感到很___，差点在开始前就放弃。A 项意为"兴奋"；B 项意为"骄傲"；C 项意为"沮丧"；D 项意为"感兴趣"。"almost wanted to quit"（差点放弃）说明听到别人弹得好后感到"discouraged"（沮丧）。故选 C。

**2. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 名词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：每个人都有自己的___。A 项意为"音乐"；B 项意为"故事"；C 项意为"梦想"；D 项意为"天赋"。"write yours"（写你的）与"story"（故事）搭配，指每个人都有独特的成长经历。故选 B。

**3. 【答案】C**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：我弹的简单曲子和我___过的音乐一点也不像。A 项意为"写"；B 项意为"想象"；C 项意为"听过"；D 项意为"爱"。"heard on the radio"（在收音机里听过）与"heard"呼应，指之前听过的优美旋律。故选 C。

**4. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：刚开始时我___老师会让我离开。A 项意为"希望"；B 项意为"害怕"；C 项意为"相信"；D 项意为"愿望"。"feared"（害怕）与"used to sound like that too"相呼应，初学时担心被放弃。故选 B。

**5. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 名词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：她的话给了我___。A 项意为"时间"；B 项意为"希望"；C 项意为"帮助"；D 项意为"建议"。"hope"（希望）与"I kept practicing"（我继续练习）相呼应，说明受到鼓舞。故选 B。

**6. 【答案】C**
**【考点】** 形容词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：我太___了，跑去告诉陈老师。A 项意为"难过"；B 项意为"累"；C 项意为"兴奋"；D 项意为"担心"。"played a complete piece without mistakes"（完整无误地弹完一曲）后自然是"excited"（兴奋）。故选 C。

**7. 【答案】C**
**【考点】** 形容词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：音乐不是关于___。A 项意为"第一"；B 项意为"最好"；C 项意为"完美"；D 项意为"快速"。"expressing what you feel"（表达你的感受）与"perfect"（完美）形成对比，强调感受比技巧重要。故选 C。

**8. 【答案】D**
**【考点】** 形容词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：我仍记得那些每个音符似乎都不可能做到的___日子。A 项意为"快乐"；B 项意为"容易"；C 项意为"早期"；D 项意为"困难"。"every note seemed impossible"（每个音似乎都不可能）说明是"difficult"（困难的）阶段。故选 D。

**9. 【答案】C**
**【考点】** 动词短语辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：最好的成长发生在我们拒绝___的时候。A 项意为"尝试"；B 项意为"练习"；C 项意为"放弃"；D 项意为"放慢"。"give up"（放弃）与"the best growth"（最好的成长）呼应，坚持才有进步。故选 C。

**10. 【答案】C**
**【考点】** 名词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：只要他们一个音符一个音符地继续弹奏自己的___。A 项意为"曲子"；B 项意为"歌"；C 项意为"曲调、旋律"；D 项意为"旋律"。"tune"与"one note at a time"搭配，指属于自己的旋律、属于自己的进步之路。故选 C。
