# 中考英语完形填空：被退回的作文

## 完形填空原文

In seventh grade, I dreamed of becoming a writer. I spent every free moment scribbling stories in a notebook. My characters went on adventures, solved mysteries, and ___1___ dragons. Writing made me feel alive.

One day, our English teacher, Ms. Chen, gave us a homework assignment: write a short story about something ___2___. I was thrilled. This was my chance to show everyone what I could do.

I stayed up until midnight working on my story. It was about a boy who discovered a hidden door behind his school library. I thought it was the ___3___ thing I had ever written.

The next week, Ms. Chen returned our papers. When she handed me mine, I saw a large red "C" at the top. My heart ___4___. Below the grade, she had written: "Good imagination, but the writing needs more work. See me after class."

I wanted to throw the paper away. A "C" was not what I had ___5___. I felt embarrassed and angry. Some classmates got A's, and their stories weren't even half as creative as mine.

After class, Ms. Chen sat down with me. "I can see you have talent," she said. "But talent alone is not ___6___. You need to learn structure, grammar, and how to revise. A diamond needs polishing before it can ___7___."

She gave me a book on creative writing and asked me to rewrite the story. At first, I didn't want to. But ___8___, I opened the book and started reading. It taught me things I had never thought about — how to build tension, how to develop characters, how to choose the right words.

I rewrote the story three times. Each version was ___9___ than the last. When I finally turned it in, Ms. Chen read it and smiled. "Now THIS is writing," she said.

That experience taught me an important lesson: being told you're not good enough is not the end. It's the ___10___. The people who push you to improve are the ones who truly believe in you.

1. A. fed &emsp;&emsp; B. feared &emsp;&emsp; C. fought &emsp;&emsp; D. followed
2. A. real &emsp;&emsp; B. imaginary &emsp;&emsp; C. boring &emsp;&emsp; D. terrible
3. A. worst &emsp;&emsp; B. longest &emsp;&emsp; C. funniest &emsp;&emsp; D. best
4. A. sank &emsp;&emsp; B. raced &emsp;&emsp; C. stopped &emsp;&emsp; D. burned
5. A. feared &emsp;&emsp; B. expected &emsp;&emsp; C. accepted &emsp;&emsp; D. noticed
6. A. easy &emsp;&emsp; B. fair &emsp;&emsp; C. enough &emsp;&emsp; D. necessary
7. A. break &emsp;&emsp; B. shine &emsp;&emsp; C. melt &emsp;&emsp; D. grow
8. A. slowly &emsp;&emsp; B. suddenly &emsp;&emsp; C. luckily &emsp;&emsp; D. proudly
9. A. shorter &emsp;&emsp; B. longer &emsp;&emsp; C. better &emsp;&emsp; D. simpler
10. A. excuse &emsp;&emsp; B. beginning &emsp;&emsp; C. problem &emsp;&emsp; D. ending

## 答案与解析

**1. 【答案】C**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：我的角色们去冒险、解谜，还___龙。A 项意为"喂养"；B 项意为"害怕"；C 项意为"战斗"；D 项意为"跟随"。冒险故事里的角色与龙"战斗"（fought）最符合语境，与adventures、solved mysteries构成递进。故选 C。

**2. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 形容词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：写一个关于___事物的短篇故事。A 项意为"真实的"；B 项意为"想象的"；C 项意为"无聊的"；D 项意为"可怕的"。后文"我"写了一个发现图书馆后面隐藏之门的故事，这是虚构/想象的内容。故选 B。

**3. 【答案】D**
**【考点】** 形容词辨析（最高级）
**【解析】** 本句意为：我觉得这是我写过的___的东西。A 项意为"最差的"；B 项意为"最长的"；C 项意为"最有趣的"；D 项意为"最好的"。"我"对自己的作品很自信，认为是写过的"最好的"（best）。故选 D。

**4. 【答案】A**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：我的心___了。A 项意为"下沉"；B 项意为"飞奔"；C 项意为"停止"；D 项意为"燃烧"。"My heart sank"（心沉了下去）是表达失望、沮丧的固定表达，看到低分时的失落感。故选 A。

**5. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：C不是我所___的。A 项意为"害怕"；B 项意为"期望"；C 项意为"接受"；D 项意为"注意到"。"我"对自己的作品很有信心，期望（expected）得到高分，结果却是C。故选 B。

**6. 【答案】C**
**【考点】** 形容词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：但光有天赋是不___的。A 项意为"容易的"；B 项意为"公平的"；C 项意为"足够的"；D 项意为"必要的"。老师说天赋还不"够"（enough），还需要学习结构、语法和修改技巧。故选 C。

**7. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：钻石需要打磨才能___。A 项意为"破碎"；B 项意为"闪耀"；C 项意为"融化"；D 项意为"生长"。老师用钻石比喻：有天赋的人需要磨练才能"闪耀"（shine），发挥真正的光彩。故选 B。

**8. 【答案】A**
**【考点】** 副词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：但___地，我打开了那本书开始读。A 项意为"慢慢地"；B 项意为"突然"；C 项意为"幸运地"；D 项意为"骄傲地"。一开始不想做，但最终"慢慢地"（slowly）接受了，体现了心态转变的过程。故选 A。

**9. 【答案】C**
**【考点】** 形容词辨析（比较级）
**【解析】** 本句意为：每一版都比上一版___。A 项意为"更短"；B 项意为"更长"；C 项意为"更好"；D 项意为"更简单"。反复修改的目的是让文章越来越"好"（better），体现进步。故选 C。

**10. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 名词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：被告知不够好不是终点，而是___。A 项意为"借口"；B 项意为"开始"；C 项意为"问题"；D 项意为"结束"。与前文"not the end"形成对比，批评不是终点，而是新的"开始"（beginning），表达成长型心态。故选 B。
