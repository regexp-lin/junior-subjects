# 中考英语完形填空：深夜书店的灯光

## 完形填空原文

There is a bookstore on our street that stays open until midnight. I discovered it by accident one evening when I missed my bus and had to walk home. The warm light from its windows ___1___ me inside.

The owner, Mr. Zhang, is an elderly man with glasses. "Students are always welcome here," he said. "You can read without buying. Just take care of the books." I couldn't believe it — a place where I could ___2___ for hours without pressure.

That night, I sat in a corner and read a novel. Around me, a few other people were also reading — a worker in uniform, a woman with a baby, an old man. We were strangers, but we shared something: the quiet ___3___ of losing ourselves in a good book.

I started visiting the bookstore regularly. It became my escape from the stress of school. When exams made me anxious, I would walk there, pick up a book, and feel my worries ___4___ away. Mr. Zhang never bothered me. He would sometimes bring me a cup of tea. "Reading," he said, "is one of the few things that can ___5___ us to different worlds without leaving our seat."

One night, I asked him why he kept the store open so late. "Because some people need a place to go when the world feels too heavy," he replied. "A bookstore at midnight is like a ___6___ — it welcomes anyone who needs shelter."

His words stayed with me. I had never thought of a bookstore as a refuge. But he was right. In that quiet space, surrounded by books, I felt safe. I felt that I could breathe.

Before the winter holiday, I bought several books from Mr. Zhang. "Thank you for letting me read here," I said. He smiled. "Thank you for ___7___ my books. A book is meant to be read. When you read, you give it life."

Now I understand. A bookstore is not just a place to buy books. It's a place where stories live, where tired souls can ___8___, and where a single light can guide someone home on a dark night. Mr. Zhang didn't just sell books — he created a space for people to find ___9___ and peace. That kind of generosity can't be measured in money. It's a gift that ___10___ forever.

## 答案与解析

**1. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：温暖的灯光___我进去。A 项意为"推"；B 项意为"吸引"；C 项意为"开车送"；D 项意为"送"。"pulled me inside"（把我吸引进去）形容灯光温馨诱人。故选 B。

**2. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：一个可以无压力___几个小时的地方。A 项意为"学习"；B 项意为"阅读"；C 项意为"睡觉"；D 项意为"玩耍"。"read"与书店场景及"without buying"对应。故选 B。

**3. 【答案】A**
**【考点】** 名词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：沉浸在好书中的宁静___。A 项意为"快乐"；B 项意为"悲伤"；C 项意为"愤怒"；D 项意为"恐惧"。"joy"与"losing ourselves in a good book"呼应。故选 A。

**4. 【答案】C**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：感受焦虑___。A 项意为"跑"；B 项意为"飞"；C 项意为"逐渐消失"；D 项意为"增长"。"fade away"（逐渐消失）形容阅读带来的放松。故选 C。

**5. 【答案】D**
**【考点】** 固定搭配
**【解析】** 本句意为：阅读能___我们去不同世界。A 项意为"携带"；B 项意为"送"；C 项意为"带来"；D 项意为"带"。"take us to"（带我们去）是常用搭配。故选 D。

**6. 【答案】C**
**【考点】** 名词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：午夜书店就像一个___。A 项意为"学校"；B 项意为"医院"；C 项意为"家"；D 项意为"监狱"。"home"与"shelter"（庇护所）、"welcomes anyone"呼应。故选 C。

**7. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：感谢你___我的书。A 项意为"写"；B 项意为"喜爱"；C 项意为"卖"；D 项意为"借"。"loving"指用心阅读、珍视书籍。故选 B。

**8. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：疲惫的灵魂可以___的地方。A 项意为"工作"；B 项意为"休息"；C 项意为"争论"；D 项意为"竞争"。"rest"与"tired souls"搭配，书店是休憩之所。故选 B。

**9. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 名词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：人们可以找到___和安宁的空间。A 项意为"金钱"；B 项意为"慰藉"；C 项意为"名声"；D 项意为"权力"。"comfort"与"peace"并列，形容精神上的安抚。故选 B。

**10. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：这种礼物会___ forever。A 项意为"花费"；B 项意为"持久"；C 项意为"停止"；D 项意为"失败"。"lasts forever"（永恒留存）形容善意与关怀的持久价值。故选 B。
