# 中考英语完形填空：养蚕宝宝

## 完形填空原文

In spring, our science teacher gave each student five silkworm eggs. She told us to observe the silkworms as they grew and to write a report at the end. I was very ___1___ to take care of them because I had never raised any insects before.

I put the tiny eggs in a small box lined with fresh mulberry leaves. After about ten days, the eggs hatched. Five little silkworms appeared. They were so small that I could barely ___2___ them. They looked like thin white threads.

Every day, I picked fresh mulberry leaves from the tree in our school garden. The silkworms ate a lot — much more than I had expected. I could hear them chewing if I put my ear close to the box. It was a soft, steady sound that I found ___3___.

As the weeks passed, the silkworms grew bigger and fatter. They changed their skin several times, which is called moulting. Each time they moulted, they would stop eating for a day or two and stay very ___4___. I was worried at first, thinking they were sick. But the teacher explained that this was completely normal.

After about a month, something amazing happened. The silkworms began to spin silk. They moved their heads back and forth, producing a thin, shiny ___5___. Slowly, each silkworm wrapped itself inside a cocoon. Some cocoons were white, and one was slightly yellow. The process took about three days.

I waited patiently for two weeks. Then, one morning, I saw a small hole in one cocoon. A moth was pushing its way ___6___. It had damp wings and could not fly at first. I watched it rest and dry its wings in the sunlight. By the afternoon, it was fully ___7___ and began to flutter around.

The whole experience was ___8___. I learned that silkworms go through four stages: egg, larva, pupa, and moth. This is called complete metamorphosis. Writing the report was easy because I had observed every detail with great interest.

My science teacher gave me a high mark for the project. She said my report was detailed and well-organised. But the ___9___ reward was not the mark — it was the knowledge I gained and the joy of watching life unfold right before my eyes.

I decided that next spring, I would raise silkworms again. This time, I would take photos at each stage and share them with my classmates. I believe that hands-on experience is the best way to ___10___ about nature.

7. A. asleep &emsp;&emsp; B. dry &emsp;&emsp; C. lost &emsp;&emsp; D. wet
8. A. boring &emsp;&emsp; B. tiring &emsp;&emsp; C. educational &emsp;&emsp; D. dangerous
9. A. worst &emsp;&emsp; B. smallest &emsp;&emsp; C. real &emsp;&emsp; D. only
10. A. worry &emsp;&emsp; B. complain &emsp;&emsp; C. argue &emsp;&emsp; D. learn

## 答案与解析

**1. 【答案】C**
**【考点】** 形容词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：我非常___去照顾它们，因为我从未养过昆虫。A 项意为"害怕的"；B 项意为"抱歉的"；C 项意为"渴望的"；D 项意为"懒惰的"。第一次养蚕充满期待和渴望，"eager"正确。故选 C。

**2. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：它们太小了，我几乎___不到它们。A 项意为"抓住"；B 项意为"看见"；C 项意为"触摸"；D 项意为"喂养"。蚕卵刚孵化非常细小，几乎看不见，"see"正确。故选 B。

**3. 【答案】C**
**【考点】** 形容词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：那是一种柔和稳定的声音，我觉得很___。A 项意为"恼人的"；B 项意为"可怕的"；C 项意为"放松的"；D 项意为"令人困惑的"。轻柔的咀嚼声让人感到放松，"relaxing"正确。故选 C。

**4. 【答案】D**
**【考点】** 形容词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：每次蜕皮时它们会停止进食一两天，保持非常___。A 项意为"饥饿的"；B 项意为"活跃的"；C 项意为"吵闹的"；D 项意为"静止的"。蜕皮期间蚕不动不吃，"still"正确。故选 D。

**5. 【答案】A**
**【考点】** 名词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：它们来回摆头，吐出细细的、闪亮的___。A 项意为"丝线"；B 项意为"叶子"；C 项意为"树枝"；D 项意为"石头"。蚕吐的是丝线，"thread"正确。故选 A。

**6. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 介词/副词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：一只蛾正在努力向___推。A 项意为"下"；B 项意为"外"；C 项意为"里"；D 项意为"远离"。蛾从茧中破壳而出，"out"正确。故选 B。

**7. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 形容词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：到下午它的翅膀完全___了，开始扑扇。A 项意为"睡着的"；B 项意为"干的"；C 项意为"迷路的"；D 项意为"湿的"。前文说翅膀是湿的，晒干后才能飞，"dry"正确。故选 B。

**8. 【答案】C**
**【考点】** 形容词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：整个经历是___的。A 项意为"无聊的"；B 项意为"累人的"；C 项意为"有教育意义的"；D 项意为"危险的"。通过养蚕学到了知识，"educational"正确。故选 C。

**9. 【答案】C**
**【考点】** 形容词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：但___的收获不是分数。A 项意为"最差的"；B 项意为"最小的"；C 项意为"真正的"；D 项意为"唯一的"。真正的收获是知识和快乐，"real"正确。故选 C。

**10. 【答案】D**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：动手实践是___自然的最好方式。A 项意为"担心"；B 项意为"抱怨"；C 项意为"争论"；D 项意为"学习"。"learn about nature"意为"了解自然"。故选 D。
