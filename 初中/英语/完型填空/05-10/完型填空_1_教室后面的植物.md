# 中考英语完形填空：教室后面的植物

## 完形填空原文

At the beginning of eighth grade, our biology teacher, Ms. Lin, placed a small potted plant on the shelf at the back of the classroom. "This is your class plant," she announced. "It's your ___1___ to keep it alive for the whole year."

Nobody took it seriously at first. It was just a tiny green plant in a plastic pot — nothing ___2___. But Ms. Lin had rules. Every Monday, one student was assigned to water it. Every Friday, someone had to check if it needed more sunlight.

The first few weeks went smoothly. But then, during exam season, everyone ___3___ about the plant. When I finally looked at it in late October, its leaves had turned yellow and were starting to fall off. It looked like it was dying.

Ms. Lin didn't say a word. She just ___4___ at the plant and then at us. Her silence was louder than any lecture.

I felt guilty. I had been the one assigned to water it that week, but I had been too ___5___ studying for exams to remember. That afternoon, I stayed after class, watered the plant, moved it closer to the window, and removed the dead leaves.

Over the next few days, others started helping too. Someone brought fertilizer from home. Another classmate made a watering ___6___ and posted it on the wall. A few students even started talking to the plant, encouraging it like a sick friend.

Slowly, the plant recovered. New green leaves appeared, and by December, it had even grown a small ___7___. The whole class cheered when they saw it.

"Do you understand now?" Ms. Lin asked us one day. "This plant is just like anything else in life. If you ___8___ it, it suffers. If you give it attention and care, it thrives."

She paused and looked around the room. "Friendships, health, studies — they all need ___9___ watering. You can't just remember them when they're dying."

That little plant taught me more than any textbook. By the end of the year, it had grown so tall that we had to move it to a bigger pot. And our class had grown too — not in size, but in the way we cared for each other.

On the last day of school, Ms. Lin asked, "Who wants to take the plant home for summer?"

Every ___10___ in the room went up.

## 答案与解析

**1. 【答案】C**
**【考点】** 名词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：让它活一整年是你们的___。A 项意为"计划"；B 项意为"梦想"；C 项意为"工作/任务"；D 项意为"愿望"。照顾植物是全班的"任务"（job）/责任。故选 C。

**2. 【答案】A**
**【考点】** 形容词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：只是一株小绿植，没什么___。A 项意为"特别的"；B 项意为"危险的"；C 项意为"昂贵的"；D 项意为"不同的"。塑料盆里的小植物看起来没什么"特别的"（special），大家不以为意。故选 A。

**3. 【答案】C**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：考试季，所有人都___了植物。A 项意为"担心"；B 项意为"谈论"；C 项意为"忘记"；D 项意为"争论"。考试忙碌，大家"忘了"（forgot）照顾植物。故选 C。

**4. 【答案】C**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：她只是___了植物一眼，然后看着我们。A 项意为"扔"；B 项意为"指"；C 项意为"看"；D 项意为"喊"。老师用目光无声地"看"（looked）着，比言语更有力量。故选 C。

**5. 【答案】C**
**【考点】** 形容词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：我太___复习考试了以至于忘了。A 项意为"厌倦"；B 项意为"擅长"；C 项意为"忙碌的"；D 项意为"害怕"。"too busy studying"（太忙于复习），忘了浇水的责任。故选 C。

**6. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 名词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：另一个同学做了浇水___贴在墙上。A 项意为"机器"；B 项意为"时间表"；C 项意为"书"；D 项意为"歌"。制定了浇水"时间表"（schedule），确保有人负责。故选 B。

**7. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 名词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：到十二月它甚至长出了一朵小___。A 项意为"叶子"；B 项意为"花"；C 项意为"果实"；D 项意为"根"。全班欢呼，说明植物不仅复活还开了"花"（flower），是最明显的好兆头。故选 B。

**8. 【答案】D**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：如果你___它，它就受苦。A 项意为"观看"；B 项意为"保护"；C 项意为"浇水"；D 项意为"忽视"。与后半句"给它关注和照顾就会茁壮"形成对比，"忽视"（neglect）则会受苦。故选 D。

**9. 【答案】C**
**【考点】** 形容词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：友谊、健康、学业都需要___浇灌。A 项意为"仔细的"；B 项意为"快速的"；C 项意为"定期的"；D 项意为"大量的"。像植物一样需要"定期"（regular）照料，不能等到出问题才想起。故选 C。

**10. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 名词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：教室里每只___都举了起来。A 项意为"声音"；B 项意为"手"；C 项意为"头"；D 项意为"眼睛"。所有人举"手"（hand）表示想带植物回家过暑假，体现全班对植物的感情。故选 B。
