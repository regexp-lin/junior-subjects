# 中考英语完形填空：食堂里的温暖

## 完形填空原文

Lunch at our school canteen was always crowded. Students rushed in as soon as the bell rang, pushing toward the counter to buy food. Finding a seat was the hardest part. If you were late, you had to eat standing up or wait for someone to ___1___.

One Tuesday, I was the last to finish my homework. By the time I got to the canteen, every table was full. I stood there with my tray, looking for a place to sit. I felt a tap on my shoulder. A boy from Grade 8 — I didn't know his name — pointed to an empty chair at his table. "You can sit ___2___," he said.

I was grateful. I sat down and ate in silence. Before I left, I said "Thank you." He smiled. "No problem. I've been there before."

The next week, I finished class early. I got to the canteen while there were still many ___3___ seats. I sat down and started eating. Then I saw a younger student — probably from Grade 7 — holding a tray and looking around. He seemed lost. I remembered how I had felt the week before. I ___4___ my hand and waved at him. "There's space here!"

He came over with a relieved smile. "Thank you! I thought I'd have to eat standing up."

We had a nice chat. His name was Zhang Wei. He was new to the school and didn't have many friends yet. I told him about the boy who had helped me. "Maybe we should both do that — help others find seats when we have ___5___," I said.

Zhang Wei agreed. From then on, whenever we had empty chairs at our table, we would look for students who were standing with their trays. A nod, a wave, or a simple "Come sit here" — it didn't take much ___6___, but it made a difference.

One day, our head teacher noticed what we were doing. She said in the next class meeting, "I've seen some students ___7___ others find seats in the canteen. That's a small act of kindness that makes our school a better place. Let's all try to do the same."

Slowly, more students began to offer seats. The canteen became a friendlier place. People talked to each other more. New students felt more welcome.

I never learned the name of the boy who first gave me his chair. But his kindness ___8___ me. It taught me that helping others doesn't require grand gestures. Sometimes, the simplest thing — like offering a seat — can make someone's day and ___9___ a chain of goodwill. I hope that chain never ___10___.

## 答案与解析

**1. 【答案】A**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：如果迟到，你得站着吃或等有人___。A 项意为"离开"；B 项意为"停留"；C 项意为"等待"；D 项意为"坐"。等有人"离开"（leave）才有空位，符合食堂拥挤的场景。故选 A。

**2. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 副词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：你可以___坐。A 项意为"那里"；B 项意为"这里"；C 项意为"离开"；D 项意为"坐下"。"sit here"指"坐这里"，男孩邀请作者坐他桌子旁的位子。故选 B。

**3. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 形容词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：我到食堂时还有很多___座位。A 项意为"干净的"；B 项意为"空的"；C 项意为"大的"；D 项意为"小的"。去得早，所以还有很多"空的"（empty）座位。故选 B。

**4. 【答案】A**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：我___手向他挥了挥。A 项意为"举起"；B 项意为"放低"；C 项意为"摇"；D 项意为"藏"。招手示意要先"举起"（raised）手。故选 A。

**5. 【答案】C**
**【考点】** 名词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：当我们有___时，帮助别人找座位。A 项意为"时间"；B 项意为"食物"；C 项意为"空间/空位"；D 项意为"钱"。桌上有空椅子时邀请别人坐，指的是有"空间"（space）。故选 C。

**6. 【答案】A**
**【考点】** 名词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：不需要太多___，但很有用。A 项意为"努力"；B 项意为"食物"；C 项意为"钱"；D 项意为"帮助"。点头、招手或说一句话，花不了多少"努力"（effort），却能帮到人。故选 A。

**7. 【答案】A**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：我看到有些学生在___别人在食堂找座位。A 项意为"帮助"；B 项意为"看着"；C 项意为"问"；D 项意为"告诉"。班主任表扬的是"帮助"（helping）别人找座位的行为。故选 A。

**8. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：但他的善意___了我。A 项意为"伤害"；B 项意为"改变"；C 项意为"惊讶"；D 项意为"担心"。陌生人的善举"改变"（changed）了作者，让他学会传递善意。故选 B。

**9. 【答案】C**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：可以让人开心并___一连串的善意。A 项意为"打破"；B 项意为"停止"；C 项意为"开始"；D 项意为"跟随"。一个善举会"引发"（start）更多善举，形成善意的链条。故选 C。

**10. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：我希望这条链条永远不要___。A 项意为"成长"；B 项意为"结束"；C 项意为"出现"；D 项意为"移动"。希望善意链条不要"断开/结束"（ends），能一直传递下去。故选 B。
