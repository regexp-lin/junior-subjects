# 中考英语完形填空：消失的蝴蝶

## 完形填空原文

When I was little, the field behind our house was full of butterflies in spring. They came in every color — orange, yellow, white, and the most beautiful ones were deep ___1___. My sister and I would chase them for hours, laughing and tumbling through the tall grass.

But as the years passed, the butterflies became fewer and fewer. New buildings went up around the field. The grass was ___2___ to make way for a parking lot. By the time I reached eighth grade, the field was gone, and so were the butterflies.

I didn't think much about it until our biology teacher, Mr. Jiang, showed us a documentary about insect ___3___ around the world. Millions of species were disappearing because of pollution, habitat loss, and climate change. I was shocked to learn that butterflies were among the most ___4___ affected.

Mr. Jiang challenged us to do something about it. "You're young," he said. "But that doesn't mean you're ___5___. Even small actions can make a big difference."

Our class decided to create a butterfly garden on a small patch of unused land next to the school. We ___6___ native flowers that butterflies loved — lavender, daisies, and milkweed. We also built a small water station and put up signs asking people not to use pesticides nearby.

The first few weeks were ___7___. Nothing seemed to happen. A few bees came, but no butterflies. Some students lost interest. "Maybe they're not coming back," said Li Ming.

But we kept watering and weeding. Then, one warm morning in late April, I saw it — a single orange butterfly landing on a lavender bush. I held my ___8___, afraid to scare it away. Within a week, more followed. By May, our little garden was alive with color and movement.

The butterfly garden became the most popular spot in our school. Students came to read, draw, and simply ___9___ the beauty of nature. Even teachers brought their classes for outdoor lessons.

That experience changed the way I see the world. We may not be able to ___10___ every problem, but we can start with what's right in front of us. Sometimes, all it takes is a garden.

## 答案与解析

**1. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 名词辨析（颜色词）
**【解析】** 本句意为：最美丽的是深___色的。A 项意为"红色"；B 项意为"蓝色"；C 项意为"绿色"；D 项意为"黑色"。前面已提到橙色、黄色、白色的蝴蝶，最美的通常是深"蓝色"（blue）的蝴蝶，如蓝闪蝶。故选 B。

**2. 【答案】A**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：草地被___掉以便建停车场。A 项意为"割/砍"；B 项意为"种植"；C 项意为"浇水"；D 项意为"画"。为了建停车场，草地被"铲除"（cut）了。故选 A。

**3. 【答案】C**
**【考点】** 名词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：一部关于全球昆虫___的纪录片。A 项意为"增长"；B 项意为"行为"；C 项意为"消失/损失"；D 项意为"美丽"。数百万物种正在消失，讲的是昆虫的"消亡"（loss）。故选 C。

**4. 【答案】A**
**【考点】** 副词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：蝴蝶是受影响最___的物种之一。A 项意为"严重地"；B 项意为"幸运地"；C 项意为"缓慢地"；D 项意为"快乐地"。蝴蝶受环境变化影响"严重"（seriously），与文章主题呼应。故选 A。

**5. 【答案】D**
**【考点】** 形容词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：你们年轻，但这不意味着你们___。A 项意为"错的"；B 项意为"弱的"；C 项意为"无用的"；D 项意为"无力的"。老师鼓励学生：年轻不代表"无能为力"（powerless），即使小行动也能改变。故选 D。

**6. 【答案】C**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：我们___了蝴蝶喜欢的本地花卉。A 项意为"画"；B 项意为"买"；C 项意为"种植"；D 项意为"发现"。建蝴蝶花园需要"种植"（planted）花卉来吸引蝴蝶。故选 C。

**7. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 形容词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：最初几周是___的。A 项意为"令人兴奋的"；B 项意为"令人失望的"；C 项意为"危险的"；D 项意为"令人放松的"。什么也没发生，没有蝴蝶来，结果"令人失望"（disappointing）。故选 B。

**8. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 名词辨析与固定搭配
**【解析】** 本句意为：我屏住了___，怕吓跑它。A 项意为"手"；B 项意为"呼吸"；C 项意为"书"；D 项意为"手机"。"hold one's breath"（屏住呼吸）是固定搭配，形容紧张又激动的心情。故选 B。

**9. 【答案】B**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：学生们来阅读、画画，简单地___大自然的美。A 项意为"想象"；B 项意为"享受"；C 项意为"描述"；D 项意为"保护"。花园成了受欢迎的地方，人们来这里"享受"（enjoy）自然之美。故选 B。

**10. 【答案】D**
**【考点】** 动词辨析
**【解析】** 本句意为：我们可能无法___每个问题。A 项意为"引起"；B 项意为"解释"；C 项意为"创造"；D 项意为"解决"。不能"解决"（solve）所有问题，但可以从眼前做起，表达积极行动的态度。故选 D。
