**Guide Dogs: Eyes for People Who Cannot See**

For people who are blind or have low vision, moving around safely can be difficult. Streets, stairs, and busy crossings can be full of danger. [1] Guide dogs—specially trained dogs that lead their owners safely through daily life—have changed the lives of thousands of people around the world. These dogs do more than just walk; they stop at curbs, avoid obstacles, and help their partners feel confident and independent. The bond between a guide dog and its owner is built on trust and many months of training together.

Not every dog can become a guide dog. [2] The best candidates are calm, confident, and love to work with people. They must be healthy and have a friendly nature. Labs and golden retrievers are often chosen because they are gentle and smart. Training starts when the dog is still a puppy. First, they live with a volunteer family and learn basic manners and how to behave in different places. This stage usually lasts about a year.

After the puppy stage, the dog goes to a guide dog school for professional training. [3] There, trainers teach the dog to follow commands, stop at curbs and steps, and refuse to move forward when there is danger ahead—a skill called "intelligent disobedience"（智能抗命）. For example, if the handler orders the dog to walk forward but a car is coming, the dog must stay put to protect its owner. The training takes several more months. Only about 30–40% of dogs complete it successfully.

When training is finished, the dog is matched with a person who needs one. [4] The new team spends several weeks living and practicing together at the school. They learn each other's habits and build a strong partnership. The owner learns how to give commands, care for the dog, and trust its judgment. At the end of this period, they go home as a team. For the owner, life becomes easier and safer. They can go to work, visit friends, and enjoy outdoor activities with more freedom.

[5] Guide dogs work for about eight to ten years, and then they usually retire and often stay with their owners as family pets. These amazing animals show us that with the right training and care, dogs can do much more than we imagine. They are not just pets—they are true partners that help people live full and independent lives.

## 选项

A. In conclusion, guide dogs are living proof that animals can bring hope and freedom to people in need.
B. The training center helps the new owner and dog get to know each other and practice daily routes.
C. These dogs learn to put their owner's safety first, even when it means not following a direct order.
D. That is why guide dog organizations choose their puppies very carefully.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] E   [2] D   [3] C   [4] F   [5] A

## 深度解析

- **[1] E**：
  - **线索**：问题引入与解决方案概括。空前描述盲人或弱视者出行困难（"Streets, stairs, and busy crossings can be full of danger"），空后具体介绍导盲犬的功能（"specially trained dogs that lead their owners safely...stop at curbs, avoid obstacles"）。E选项"For decades, guide dogs have offered a practical solution: a pair of faithful 'eyes' that never get tired"（数十年来，导盲犬提供了一种实用的解决方案：一双永不疲倦的忠诚"眼睛"）中"practical solution"回应空前困难，"faithful eyes that never get tired"与空后"lead...safely...confident and independent"自然衔接，引出导盲犬主题，逻辑顺畅。

- **[2] D**：
  - **线索**：选犬标准与严格筛选。空前提出"Not every dog can become a guide dog"，空后详细列举选犬条件（"calm, confident, love to work with people, healthy, friendly nature"）及犬种（"Labs and golden retrievers"）、训练阶段。D选项"That is why guide dog organizations choose their puppies very carefully"（因此导盲犬机构非常仔细地挑选幼犬）中"That is why"承接空前"Not every dog can"，"choose very carefully"自然引出空后对选犬标准与犬种的详述，衔接得当。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：智能抗命与安全优先。空前介绍训练内容（"follow commands, stop at curbs and steps, refuse to move forward when there is danger ahead—a skill called 'intelligent disobedience'"），空后举例说明（"if the handler orders...but a car is coming, the dog must stay put"）。C选项"These dogs learn to put their owner's safety first, even when it means not following a direct order"（这些狗学会将主人的安全放在首位，即使这意味着不服从直接命令）中"put owner's safety first"与"intelligent disobedience"对应，"not following a direct order"与空后"handler orders...but the dog must stay put"精准呼应，逻辑紧密。

- **[4] F**：
  - **线索**：配对后的磨合与团队训练。空前描述"the dog is matched with a person who needs one"，空后具体说明"new team spends several weeks...learn each other's habits...build a strong partnership...learn how to give commands...At the end of this period, they go home as a team"。F选项"During this time, both the owner and the dog must learn to work as one team before they go home"（在这段时间里，主人和狗必须学会作为一个团队工作后才能回家）中"During this time"指代配对后的训练期，"learn to work as one team"与空后"build a strong partnership...they go home as a team"完美对应，"before they go home"与"At the end of this period...go home"呼应，衔接自然。

- **[5] A**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后提及导盲犬工作年限（"eight to ten years"）、退休及意义（"bring hope and freedom...true partners...full and independent lives"）。A选项"In conclusion, guide dogs are living proof that animals can bring hope and freedom to people in need"（总之，导盲犬是动物能为需要帮助的人带来希望和自由的鲜活证明）用"In conclusion"总结全文，"living proof"与空后"these amazing animals"呼应，"bring hope and freedom"与空后"hope and freedom...independent lives"精准对应，收束得当。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **B. The training center helps the new owner and dog get to know each other and practice daily routes.**：描述培训中心帮助主人与狗相识并练习日常路线，易被误填[4]。但空[4]空前为"the dog is matched with a person"，空后已详细描述"several weeks...learn each other's habits...give commands...care for the dog"，B的"get to know each other and practice daily routes"信息与空后部分重叠，且空[4]需强调的是"learn to work as one team before they go home"这一时间与目标，F更精准。B属"信息相关但不够概括"型干扰。
