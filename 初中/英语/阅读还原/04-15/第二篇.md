# 04-15 第二篇

**Saving Water: Small Steps, Big Difference**

Water covers about 70% of the Earth's surface, but only a tiny amount is fresh water that we can drink or use for farming. As the world's population grows, more and more people need this limited resource. [1] The good news is that everyone can help. Simple habits at home—like turning off the tap while brushing your teeth or fixing leaky faucets—can save hundreds of liters of water each year. These small actions add up and make a real difference to our communities and the planet.

In the bathroom, we use a lot of water without realizing it. [2] A running tap can waste up to 6 liters of water per minute. To cut down on waste, take shorter showers—even reducing your shower time by one or two minutes helps. You can also collect cold water while waiting for the hot water to arrive and use it for washing plants or flushing the toilet. Another tip is to turn off the tap when you are soaping your hands or brushing your teeth, instead of letting it run the whole time.

The kitchen is another place where water can be saved. [3] When washing dishes by hand, fill the sink halfway with soapy water instead of letting the tap run. This uses much less water than a running tap. For families with a dishwasher, running it only when it is full saves both water and energy. Small changes like these might seem unimportant, but millions of people doing them together can protect our water supply for the future.

Outside the home, we can save water in gardens and outdoor spaces. [4] Watering plants early in the morning or late in the evening reduces evaporation—the process of water turning into vapor and disappearing into the air. In dry climates, choosing plants that need less water can also help. Some cities encourage "rainwater harvesting"（雨水收集）, which means collecting rainwater in tanks or barrels to use for gardening instead of using tap water. Every drop saved counts.

[5] Saving water does not require huge efforts or expensive tools. It starts with awareness and a few small changes in our daily routines. By teaching children good habits and setting an example, we pass on the value of caring for our planet. Remember: the water we save today is the water that will be there for tomorrow's generations.

## 选项

A. The good news is that water conservation is something we can all take part in, no matter where we live.
B. In fact, most of the water we use at home flows out of the bathroom.
C. The amount may surprise you—and so will the easy ways to reduce it.
D. For instance, use a basin when washing vegetables, and reuse the water for your plants.
E. From short showers to full dishwashers, small habits can lead to big savings.
F. These methods help use water more wisely and reduce the need for treated tap water outdoors.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] C   [3] D   [4] F   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：资源紧张与个人参与引出。空前说明淡水稀缺（"only a tiny amount is fresh water"）及人口增长带来的压力，空后列举个人可做的简单习惯（"turning off the tap...fixing leaky faucets...save hundreds of liters"）。A选项"The good news is that water conservation is something we can all take part in, no matter where we live"（好消息是，节约用水是我们都能参与的事，无论我们生活在何处）中"The good news"承接空前资源紧张的基调并转向积极面，"water conservation...we can all take part in"与空后"Simple habits...can save hundreds of liters"自然衔接，"no matter where we live"强调普适性，引出下文从家庭各处入手的具体建议，逻辑顺畅。

- **[2] C**：
  - **线索**：浴室用水量与减量方法过渡。空前指出"we use a lot of water without realizing it"，空后具体说明"running tap can waste up to 6 liters per minute"及多种节水方法（"shorter showers...collect cold water...turn off the tap"）。C选项"The amount may surprise you—and so will the easy ways to reduce it"（用量可能会让你惊讶——减少用水的方法也同样简单）中"The amount"指代用水量，与空前"a lot of water"及空后"6 liters per minute"呼应，"easy ways to reduce it"自然引出空后"shorter showers...collect...turn off"等具体建议，衔接得当。

- **[3] D**：
  - **线索**：厨房节水与具体做法举例。空前提出"kitchen is another place where water can be saved"，空后介绍"washing dishes by hand...fill the sink halfway...dishwasher...run only when full"。D选项"For instance, use a basin when washing vegetables, and reuse the water for your plants"（例如，洗菜时用盆接水，然后把水再利用浇花）中"For instance"引出例证，"use a basin...reuse the water"与空后"fill the sink...instead of letting the tap run"及"running it only when full"形成并列的厨房节水方法，衔接自然。注：basin（盆）为中考常见词。

- **[4] F**：
  - **线索**：户外节水方法总结。空前列举多种户外节水方式（"watering early or late...plants that need less water...rainwater harvesting"），空后强调"Every drop saved counts"。F选项"These methods help use water more wisely and reduce the need for treated tap water outdoors"（这些方法有助于更明智地用水，并减少户外对处理后自来水的需求）中"These methods"指代空前"early/late watering, drought-tolerant plants, rainwater harvesting"，"use water more wisely"与"reduces evaporation...need less water"对应，"reduce the need for treated tap water"与"use...for gardening instead of tap water"呼应，逻辑紧密。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与习惯总结。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后强调"does not require huge efforts...starts with awareness and small changes...teaching children...value of caring for our planet"。E选项"From short showers to full dishwashers, small habits can lead to big savings"（从缩短淋浴到装满洗碗机再洗，小习惯能带来大节约）中"From...to..."呼应全文浴室、厨房、户外等多个场景，"small habits...big savings"与空后"small changes...pass on the value"形成升华，收束得当。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **B. In fact, most of the water we use at home flows out of the bathroom.**：陈述家庭用水多来自浴室，易被误填[2]。但空[2]空前为"bathroom, we use a lot of water without realizing it"，B若填入则与空前"in the bathroom"重复且未推进文意；空后需引出"amount"与"easy ways to reduce it"，C的"The amount may surprise you"直接承接"a lot of water"并过渡到具体数据与方法，B属"重复前文、缺乏推进"型干扰。
