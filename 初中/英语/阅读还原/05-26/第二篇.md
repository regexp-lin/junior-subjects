# 05-26 第二篇

**Chinese Martial Arts: More Than Fighting**

When people hear "Chinese martial arts" or "kung fu（功夫）," they often think of action movies and exciting fight scenes. But Chinese martial arts, or wushu（武术）, are much more than that. They have a history of thousands of years and are deeply connected to Chinese culture, philosophy, and health. [1] In fact, many styles focus not only on self-defence but also on building strength, flexibility, and peace of mind. Practitioners（练习者）learn to control their bodies and their emotions. That is why Chinese martial arts are often called "moving meditation"（动中禅）. They combine physical exercise with mental training, making them valuable for people of all ages.

What are some well-known styles? [2] Tai Chi（太极拳）is famous for its slow, flowing movements—it is popular among older people for its health benefits. Shaolin Kung Fu is known for its powerful kicks and punches, and it comes from the Shaolin Temple in China. Wing Chun（咏春拳）emphasises close-range fighting and quick movements. There are hundreds of other styles, each with its own history and character. In other words, Chinese martial arts are rich and varied. Whether you prefer slow, gentle forms or fast, powerful techniques, there is a style that can suit you.

What do students learn? [3] Beyond kicks and punches, students learn discipline, respect, and patience. They practise forms, or "taolu"（套路）—set sequences of movements that tell a story. They also learn about the philosophy behind the arts: balance, harmony, and the idea that the best defence is often to avoid conflict. For this reason, martial arts training is as much about character as it is about technique. Many masters say that the real fight is not against others but against our own weaknesses. The goal is to become a better person, not just a skilled fighter.

How can martial arts benefit young people today? [4] They improve fitness, coordination, and confidence. They teach focus and self-control—skills that help in school and in life. In a world of screens and busy schedules, martial arts offer a chance to move, breathe, and connect with a tradition that values both body and mind. It is true that martial arts can benefit people of all ages and backgrounds. Whether you train for competition, health, or self-defence, the lessons go far beyond the gym. So more and more schools and clubs are offering martial arts programmes for young learners.

[5] In short, Chinese martial arts are a gift to the world. They offer physical fitness, mental clarity, and a connection to centuries of wisdom. Whether you practise Tai Chi in a park or study kung fu in a school, you are part of a living tradition. Remember: martial arts are not about violence—they are about respect, discipline, and inner strength. So the next time you see someone practising in a park or a film, look beyond the kicks and punches. There is a whole world of philosophy, art, and health waiting to be discovered.

## 选项

A. That is because Chinese martial arts combine physical and mental training.
B. In other words, there are many different styles to choose from.
C. That is why martial arts train both the body and the character.
D. It is true that martial arts can benefit people of all ages and backgrounds.
E. In conclusion, Chinese martial arts offer lasting value to the world.
F. For example, some people learn martial arts to compete in tournaments.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：总领中国武术的身心双重训练。空前描述"When people hear 'Chinese martial arts' or 'kung fu,' they often think of action movies...But Chinese martial arts, or wushu, are much more than that...deeply connected to Chinese culture, philosophy, and health"，空后说明"In fact, many styles focus not only on self-defence but also on building strength, flexibility, and peace of mind...Practitioners learn to control their bodies and their emotions...Chinese martial arts are often called 'moving meditation'...They combine physical exercise with mental training"。A选项"That is because Chinese martial arts combine physical and mental training"（那是因为中国武术将身体与心智训练相结合）中"That is because"解释武术特点，"combine physical and mental training"与空后strength/flexibility/peace of mind、moving meditation、physical exercise with mental training精准对应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：武术流派众多与选择多样性。空前设问"What are some well-known styles?"，空后列举"Tai Chi is famous for its slow, flowing movements...Shaolin Kung Fu is known for its powerful kicks and punches...Wing Chun emphasises close-range fighting and quick movements...There are hundreds of other styles, each with its own history and character...Chinese martial arts are rich and varied...Whether you prefer slow, gentle forms or fast, powerful techniques, there is a style that can suit you"。B选项"In other words, there are many different styles to choose from"（换言之，有许多不同流派可供选择）中"In other words"概括空后内容，"many different styles to choose from"与空后Tai Chi、Shaolin、Wing Chun、hundreds of other styles、suit you完美呼应，衔接自然。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：武术对品格与心性的塑造。空前设问"What do students learn?"，空后详述"Beyond kicks and punches, students learn discipline, respect, and patience...They practise forms, or 'taolu'...They also learn about the philosophy behind the arts: balance, harmony...martial arts training is as much about character as it is about technique...Many masters say that the real fight is not against others but against our own weaknesses...The goal is to become a better person, not just a skilled fighter"。C选项"That is why martial arts train both the body and the character"（因此武术既训练身体也塑造品格）中"That is why"承接空前及空后结论，"train both the body and the character"与空后discipline/respect/patience、about character、become a better person精准对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：武术对当代年轻人的益处及普适性。空前设问"How can martial arts benefit young people today?"，空后说明"They improve fitness, coordination, and confidence...They teach focus and self-control—skills that help in school and in life...martial arts offer a chance to move, breathe, and connect with a tradition...martial arts can benefit people of all ages and backgrounds...the lessons go far beyond the gym...more and more schools and clubs are offering martial arts programmes"。D选项"It is true that martial arts can benefit people of all ages and backgrounds"（的确，武术能使各年龄层、各背景的人受益）中"It is true that"加强衔接，"benefit people of all ages and backgrounds"与空后martial arts can benefit people of all ages and backgrounds完美呼应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与中国武术的世界价值。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"Chinese martial arts are a gift to the world...They offer physical fitness, mental clarity, and a connection to centuries of wisdom...you are part of a living tradition...martial arts are not about violence—they are about respect, discipline, and inner strength...There is a whole world of philosophy, art, and health waiting to be discovered"。E选项"In conclusion, Chinese martial arts offer lasting value to the world"（总之，中国武术为世界提供持久价值）用"In conclusion"总结全文，"lasting value to the world"与空后gift to the world、centuries of wisdom形成呼应，收束有力。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, some people learn martial arts to compete in tournaments.**：涉及为参赛而习武，易被误填[4]或[5]。但F讨论的是习武动机的一种，而空[4]概括武术对年轻人的多重益处（fitness、confidence、focus），空[5]强调武术的文化与哲学价值；F属于动机举例，空[4]已提及"Whether you train for competition, health, or self-defence"，F的论述层级偏窄。F属"主题相关但论述层级错位"型干扰。
