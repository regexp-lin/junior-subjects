# 06-20 第二篇

**Teen Entrepreneurs: Stories of Young Business Builders**

Starting a business is not just for adults. More and more teenagers are turning their ideas into real products or services, learning skills that will help them for life. Some sell handmade crafts online, others create apps, and some find creative ways to solve problems in their communities. [1] In other words, teen entrepreneurship is growing and shows that young people can be creative and capable. These stories inspire others to believe in their own ideas and take the first step.

Many teen entrepreneurs start with a simple idea or a personal problem. [2] For example, a student might notice that classmates struggle to find old textbooks and decide to set up a used-book exchange. Another might love baking and start selling cookies at school events. Some teens see a need in their community—such as tutoring younger kids or walking dogs for busy neighbours—and turn it into a small business. The key is to find something you care about and that others need. Starting small helps you learn without taking too much risk.

Running a business teaches valuable lessons. [3] Teen entrepreneurs learn to plan, manage money, talk to customers, and solve problems when things go wrong. They discover that success often requires hard work, patience, and the willingness to try again after failure. Many say that running a business is like a real-world class—you learn by doing. Balancing school, hobbies, and a business is not easy, but it helps develop time management and responsibility. These skills are useful no matter what path they choose later.

Support from family, teachers, and the community makes a big difference. [4] Parents can offer advice and a quiet place to work; teachers may allow projects to count as part of school work; local shops might display products or share the business on social media. Some schools and organisations run programmes to help young entrepreneurs—offering training, mentoring, or even small grants. When adults believe in teens and give them a chance, young businesses have a better chance to grow.

[5] Teen entrepreneurship shows that age is not a limit to creativity and action. By starting early, young people gain experience, build confidence, and learn that they can make a real difference. Whether the business grows big or stays small, the journey itself is valuable. The next generation of inventors, leaders, and problem-solvers may be starting their first venture right now—in a classroom, a garage, or a bedroom.

## 选项

A. In other words, teen entrepreneurship is growing and shows that young people can be creative and capable.
B. They often begin with a simple idea, a personal hobby, or a need they see in their community.
C. In fact, running a business teaches planning, money management, and problem-solving—skills for life.
D. This support helps young entrepreneurs get advice, resources, and chances to grow.
E. In short, teen entrepreneurship proves that young people can make a difference and learn from the journey.
F. For example, Bill Gates started Microsoft when he was still in his teens.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：概括青少年创业的趋势与意义。空前描述"Starting business not just for adults...more teenagers turning ideas into real products or services...learning skills for life...some sell handmade crafts online, others create apps, some solve problems in communities"，空后说明"teen entrepreneurship is growing and shows young people can be creative and capable...These stories inspire others to believe in own ideas and take first step"。A选项"In other words, teen entrepreneurship is growing and shows that young people can be creative and capable"（换言之，青少年创业正在增长，表明年轻人可以富有创造力和能力）中"In other words"概括空前现象，"growing...creative and capable"与空后"growing...creative and capable"精准呼应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：青少年创业的起点——想法、爱好与社区需求。空前提出"Many teen entrepreneurs start with a simple idea or a personal problem"，空后详述"Student notice classmates struggle find old textbooks...set up used-book exchange...another love baking, sell cookies at school events...see need in community—tutoring, dog walking—turn into small business...key: find something you care about, others need...starting small...learn without too much risk"。B选项"They often begin with a simple idea, a personal hobby, or a need they see in their community"（他们往往从简单想法、个人爱好或看到的社区需求起步）中"simple idea, personal hobby, need in community"与空后 used-book、baking、tutoring、dog walking 等完美对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：创业带来的能力与品格培养。空前提出"Running a business teaches valuable lessons"，空后列举"Learn to plan, manage money, talk to customers, solve problems when wrong...success requires hard work, patience, willingness to try again after failure...like real-world class...learn by doing...balancing school, hobbies, business...time management, responsibility...skills useful no matter path later"。C选项"In fact, running a business teaches planning, money management, and problem-solving—skills for life"（事实上，经营企业教会规划、理财和问题解决——这些是终身有用的技能）中"In fact"强化教育价值，"planning, money management, problem-solving...skills for life"与空后 plan、manage money、solve problems、skills useful 精准对应，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：家庭、学校与社区对青少年创业的支持。空前提出"Support from family, teachers, and the community makes a big difference"，空后描述"Parents advice, quiet place...teachers allow projects as school work...local shops display products, share on social media...schools, organisations run programmes...training, mentoring, small grants...when adults believe, give chance...young businesses better chance to grow"。D选项"This support helps young entrepreneurs get advice, resources, and chances to grow"（这种支持帮助年轻创业者获得建议、资源和成长机会）中"This support"指代空后各类支持，"advice, resources, chances to grow"与空后 advice、training、grants、chance to grow 完美对应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与青少年创业的价值。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"Teen entrepreneurship shows age not limit to creativity and action...starting early...gain experience, build confidence, learn can make real difference...whether business grows big or stays small, journey itself valuable...next generation...may be starting first venture...classroom, garage, bedroom"。E选项"In short, teen entrepreneurship proves that young people can make a difference and learn from the journey"（简言之，青少年创业证明年轻人可以产生影响并从历程中学习）用"In short"总结全文，"make a difference...learn from the journey"与空后"make real difference...journey itself valuable"形成呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, Bill Gates started Microsoft when he was still in his teens.**：涉及比尔·盖茨青少年时期创业，易被误填[1]或[2]。但F以"For example"引出单一名人案例，而空[1]讨论的是青少年创业的普遍趋势与意义，F的"Bill Gates"属于个别成功案例，与"more teenagers...creative and capable"的群体现象焦点不同；空[2]讨论创业的常见起点（想法、爱好、需求），F的微软属于高科技初创，与"used-book exchange、baking、tutoring"的多元起点论述层次不符。F属"主题相关但论述层次错位"型干扰。
