# 08-02 第2篇

**Why We Need Rules**

Rules exist everywhere—in schools, at home, on the roads, and in sports. You might sometimes feel that rules limit your freedom, but in fact, they help create a fair and safe environment for everyone. Without rules, chaos would follow. Imagine a classroom where students could talk whenever they wanted, or a road where drivers could go in any direction. [1] Rules give us clear expectations so we know how to behave and what others will do. When everyone follows the same rules, we can trust each other and work together peacefully.

Rules promote fairness. In a game, if some players ignore the rules, the match becomes unfair and no one enjoys it. [2] The same idea applies to real life: rules make sure that everyone has an equal chance and is treated the same way. Laws against cheating and stealing protect honest people. When rules are applied consistently, people feel that society is just.

Rules also keep us safe. Traffic lights and speed limits prevent accidents. School rules against running in hallways protect students from getting hurt. [3] Safety rules often come from past experiences—someone was harmed, so we created a rule to prevent it from happening again. Fire drills and emergency plans exist because we have learned from disasters.

Some people might think that having too many rules is a bad thing. [4] However, good rules are not meant to control people but to help them live better. Rules about honesty, respect, and responsibility teach us values that make us better citizens. When we understand why a rule exists, we are more willing to follow it.

[5] Rules are not enemies of freedom—they are the foundation that makes freedom possible. By following rules, we protect our own rights and the rights of others, creating a world where everyone can thrive.

## 选项

A. They create order and predictability, which reduces conflict and confusion.
B. Fair rules ensure that no one gets special treatment or unfair advantages.
C. This is why we have safety rules in places like laboratories and swimming pools.
D. The best rules balance individual freedom with the common good of the community.
E. In this way, rules support both personal growth and a harmonious society.
F. For example, wearing a helmet when riding a bike can greatly reduce head injuries.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前举例说明无规则会导致混乱，空后说 “Rules give us clear expectations”。选项 A 的 “create order and predictability” 与 “reduces conflict and confusion” 正好承接前文的 chaos，并引出后文的 “clear expectations”，逻辑连贯。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：本段主题为 “Rules promote fairness”，空前用游戏类比，空后提到 “equal chance” 和 “treated the same way”。选项 B 强调 “no one gets special treatment or unfair advantages”，与 “fairness” 和 “equal” 完全一致，是最贴合的概括句。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说 “Safety rules often come from past experiences”，空后举例 “Fire drills and emergency plans”。选项 C 的 “This is why we have safety rules in places like laboratories and swimming pools” 用 “This” 指代前文因果，并用更多场景呼应，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前提出 “too many rules is a bad thing”，空后说 “good rules are not meant to control...but to help”，“balance” 是关键词。选项 D 的 “balance individual freedom with the common good” 直接回应质疑，与后文 “help them live better” 形成对应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：结尾段需总结规则与自由的关系。空前讲规则教导价值观，空后强调 “rules support both personal growth and a harmonious society”。选项 E 的 “personal growth and a harmonious society” 与段尾完全呼应，作收束句恰当。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, wearing a helmet when riding a bike can greatly reduce head injuries.**：具体安全案例，与 [3] 空前的 “safety rules often come from past experiences” 易混淆。但 [3] 更需承上启下的总结性句子（如 C），而非单例；F 若放在 [3] 会与后文 “Fire drills” 举例重复，层次错位。
