# 03-25 第二篇

**The Power of Saying Thank You**

When someone holds the door open for you or helps you with your homework, do you remember to say "thank you"? These two simple words can do much more than show good manners. [1] Studies have found that people who often express gratitude tend to feel happier and more satisfied with their lives.

Why does saying "thank you" have such a strong effect? When we thank others, we shift our attention from what we lack to what we already have. [2] This change in thinking helps us notice the positive things around us, instead of always focusing on problems and difficulties.

Gratitude also strengthens our relationships. When you thank a friend for their help, they feel valued and appreciated. [3] In this way, gratitude builds trust and brings people closer together.

You can develop the habit of gratitude in many simple ways. Try writing down three good things that happened each day before you go to bed. It could be a kind word from a teacher, a sunny afternoon, or a meal shared with family. [4] You might be surprised by how many small blessings you usually overlook when you take the time to look for them.

[5] A grateful heart can turn ordinary days into joyful ones. Start with a simple "thank you" today, and see the difference it makes in your life. The more you practice, the easier it becomes.

## 选项

A. In fact, grateful people often have fewer friends because they care too much about politeness.
B. This simple practice helps train your brain to notice and appreciate the good things in life.
C. Research shows that thanking others regularly can actually improve both physical and mental well-being.
D. This change in thinking helps us notice the positive things around us, instead of always focusing on problems and difficulties.
E. That positive feeling often makes them want to support you again when you need help in the future, which deepens your friendship.
F. The good news is that gratitude is a skill you can learn and practice every day.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] C   [2] D   [3] E   [4] B   [5] F

## 深度解析

- **[1] C**：
  - **线索**：研究证据与论点支撑。空前指出"thank you"这两个词能做的远不止展示礼貌。C选项"Research shows that thanking others regularly can actually improve both physical and mental well-being"（研究表明定期感谢他人确实能改善身心福祉）用"Research shows"与后文"Studies have found"形成同义呼应，为"作用远超礼貌"提供科学依据。

- **[2] D**：
  - **线索**：因果衔接与指代关系。空前解释感谢他人时我们会把注意力从"所缺"转向"已有"。D选项"This change in thinking helps us notice the positive things around us, instead of always focusing on problems and difficulties"（这种思维转变帮助我们关注周围积极的事物，而非总是盯着问题和困难）中的"This change in thinking"指代前文的"shift our attention"，与前句构成因果链。

- **[3] E**：
  - **线索**：因果衔接与代词指代。空前描述感谢朋友时他们会感到被重视和欣赏。E选项"That positive feeling often makes them want to support you again when you need help in the future, which deepens your friendship"（那种积极感受常会让他们在你将来需要时再度伸出援手，从而加深你们的友谊）中"That positive feeling"指代"valued and appreciated"，具体解释感谢如何让人愿意再次相助、加深友谊。空后"In this way, gratitude builds trust and brings people closer together"对E选项所描述的机制进行总结概括。

- **[4] B**：
  - **线索**：建议与解释衔接。空前建议每晚睡前写下三件好事。B选项"This simple practice helps train your brain to notice and appreciate the good things in life"（这个简单练习有助于训练大脑去发现并欣赏生活中的美好）中"This simple practice"指代写三件好事的做法，解释其作用。空后"You might be surprised by how many small blessings you usually overlook"与"notice and appreciate"形成语义呼应。

- **[5] F**：
  - **线索**：结尾过渡与行动号召。空[5]位于末段段首。前文已介绍感恩的益处及练习方法。F选项"The good news is that gratitude is a skill you can learn and practice every day"（好消息是感恩是一种可以每天学习和练习的技能）用"The good news"收束全篇，强调可习得性，自然引出"A grateful heart can turn ordinary days into joyful ones"及"Start with a simple thank you today"的行动建议。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **A. In fact, grateful people often have fewer friends because they care too much about politeness.**：该选项含"In fact""grateful""friends"等词汇。但其主张"感恩的人因过分讲究礼貌反而朋友更少"与全文"感恩强化人际关系""感谢让朋友感到被重视"的核心论点完全相反。属"话题词复现+立场对立"型干扰。
