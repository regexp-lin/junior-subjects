# 03-25 第一篇

**The Secret Life of Bees and Why We Need Them**

When you see a bee flying from flower to flower, you might think it is just looking for food. But that little insect is actually doing one of the most important jobs on our planet. Bees help plants reproduce by carrying pollen from one flower to another. [1] Without them, many of the fruits, vegetables, and nuts we eat every day would simply disappear from our tables.

A single bee hive can have more than 50,000 bees working together. Each bee has a clear role: some gather food, some build the hive, and some protect the queen. [2] They communicate by performing a special "dance" that tells other bees exactly where to find the best flowers and how far away they are.

Sadly, bee populations around the world have been dropping rapidly in recent years. Scientists have found several reasons for this worrying trend. [3] Farmers often use chemicals on their crops to kill insects that eat the plants, but these chemicals also harm bees and other helpful insects.

What can we do to help? [4] You can plant flowers that bees love, such as lavender, sunflowers, and roses, in your garden or on your balcony. Avoid using pesticides (杀虫剂) in your home garden. Even leaving a small dish of water outside can give thirsty bees a place to drink on hot summer days.

[5] Every small action counts. If enough people take these simple steps, we can create a safer world for bees—and for ourselves.

## 选项

A. The use of pesticides on farms is one of the main causes of this problem.
B. In fact, one out of every three bites of food we eat depends on their pollination work.
C. Therefore, scientists advise people to remove all flowers from their gardens to protect bees.
D. The good news is that everyone can do something to support these tiny workers.
E. You do not need to be a scientist or a farmer to make a difference.
F. This amazing teamwork has helped bees survive for millions of years.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] B   [2] F   [3] A   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] B**：
  - **线索**：数据支撑与代词指代。空前指出蜜蜂帮助植物繁殖，没有它们许多果蔬坚果会从餐桌上消失。B选项"In fact, one out of every three bites of food we eat depends on their pollination work"（事实上，我们每吃三口食物中就有一口依赖于它们的传粉工作）中的"their"指代前文的bees，"In fact"引出具体数据强化"重要"这一论点，与"Without them...would disappear"形成因果呼应。

- **[2] F**：
  - **线索**：总结评价与指代关系。空前描述了蜂巢内蜜蜂分工协作，以及通过"舞蹈"沟通花源位置的奇妙行为。F选项"This amazing teamwork has helped bees survive for millions of years"（这种惊人的团队合作帮助蜜蜂存活了数百万年）中的"This amazing teamwork"指代前文的分工与舞蹈沟通，对整段内容进行总结升华。

- **[3] A**：
  - **线索**：因果解释与问题列举。空前指出全球蜜蜂数量近年急剧下降，科学家发现了多项原因。A选项"The use of pesticides on farms is one of the main causes of this problem"（农场使用杀虫剂是这一问题的主要原因之一）直接点出具体原因之一，"this problem"指代蜜蜂数量下降。空后"Farmers often use chemicals..."是对A选项的进一步展开说明。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：问答衔接与建议引入。空前设问"What can we do to help?"。D选项"The good news is that everyone can do something to support these tiny workers"（好消息是每个人都能做些什么来支持这些小小的劳动者）用"The good news"正面回答，引出后文列举的具体行动——种花、避免杀虫剂、提供水源。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：结尾总结与行动号召。空[5]位于全文末段段首。前文已给出多种普通人可采取的护蜂行动。E选项"You do not need to be a scientist or a farmer to make a difference"（你不需要成为科学家或农民也能有所作为）强调人人可参与，自然过渡到空后"Every small action counts"（每一个小行动都算数），形成"门槛低→行动有价值"的递进。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **C. Therefore, scientists advise people to remove all flowers from their gardens to protect bees.**：该选项含"Therefore""scientists""flowers""bees"等话题高频词。但其主张"科学家建议人们移除花园里所有花朵以保护蜜蜂"与全文"种花吸引蜜蜂""避免杀虫剂"等护蜂建议完全相反。蜜蜂恰恰依赖花朵生存，移除花朵会加剧蜜蜂危机，属"词汇复现+逻辑完全颠倒"型干扰。
