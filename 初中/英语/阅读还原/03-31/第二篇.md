# 03-31 第二篇

**The Amazing Mind of Dolphins**

Dolphins are among the most intelligent animals on Earth. Scientists have studied them for decades and continue to be amazed by their abilities. [1] They can recognize themselves in a mirror, solve puzzles, and even use tools—behaviors that were once thought to exist only in humans and great apes. These skills show that dolphins have a highly developed brain.

One of the most surprising discoveries is that dolphins have their own "names." Each dolphin develops a unique whistle sound that acts like a name. [2] When dolphins meet, they introduce themselves by sharing these signature whistles, just as we exchange names when we meet someone new. This is one of the few examples in the animal kingdom of animals calling each other by individual sounds.

Dolphins are also skilled at working together. In the wild, they cooperate to hunt fish by forming circles or driving prey toward the shore. [3] They have been observed helping injured group members stay afloat and even protecting humans from sharks. Such behavior suggests that dolphins may have a sense of empathy and caring for others.

Communication is another area where dolphins shine. They produce a wide variety of clicks, whistles, and body movements to share information. [4] Researchers are still trying to understand exactly what dolphins are "saying" to each other, but it is clear that their communication system is far more complex than we once believed. Some scientists compare it to a language, though we have not yet decoded it.

[5] As we learn more about these remarkable creatures, we are reminded of the importance of protecting the oceans they call home. Pollution, overfishing, and climate change threaten dolphin populations worldwide.

## 选项

A. In addition, dolphins show signs of social intelligence that go beyond simple survival.
B. For this reason, many countries have passed laws to protect dolphins from being kept in small tanks.
C. To sum up, the more we discover about dolphins, the more we realize how much we share with them—and how much we still have to learn.
D. Dolphins have been found to pass the "mirror test," which means they understand that the reflection is themselves.
E. Their sense of care even extends to other species, including humans in danger.
F. Despite years of research, scientists admit that we have only scratched the surface of dolphin intelligence.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] D   [2] A   [3] E   [4] F   [5] C

## 深度解析

- **[1] D**：
  - **线索**：例证引入与同义呼应。空前总述科学家对其能力感到惊奇，空后列举"recognize themselves in a mirror, solve puzzles, use tools"。D选项"Dolphins have been found to pass the 'mirror test,' which means they understand that the reflection is themselves"（海豚能通过"镜像测试"，说明它们明白镜中是自己的倒影）与空后"recognize themselves in a mirror"形成同义复现。D先点出"mirror test"这一科学发现，空后"recognize themselves in a mirror"是其通俗化表达，逻辑自然。

- **[2] A**：
  - **线索**：段落过渡与主题递进。空前介绍海豚用独特哨音作为"名字"，空后描述"When dolphins meet, they introduce themselves by sharing these signature whistles"。A选项"In addition, dolphins show signs of social intelligence that go beyond simple survival"（此外，海豚展现出超越单纯生存的社会智能迹象）用"In addition"承接上一段认知能力，引入社会智能主题。"social intelligence"自然引出空后的"introduce themselves""exchange names"等社交行为，空后正是A中"social intelligence"的具体体现。

- **[3] E**：
  - **线索**：承上启下与语义衔接。空前描述海豚合作狩猎（"forming circles or driving prey toward the shore"），空后"They have been observed helping injured group members stay afloat and even protecting humans from sharks"。E选项"Their sense of care even extends to other species, including humans in danger"（它们的关爱之心甚至延伸到其他物种，包括处于危险中的人类）中"Their"指代海豚，"sense of care"承接合作狩猎体现的协作精神，并自然过渡到空后"helping injured"与"protecting humans"——前者为本物种内互助，后者恰对应"other species...humans in danger"，逻辑严密。

- **[4] F**：
  - **线索**：转折递进与语义呼应。空前描述海豚用多种信号交流（"clicks, whistles, body movements"），空后"Researchers are still trying to understand exactly what dolphins are 'saying'...far more complex than we once believed"。F选项"Despite years of research, scientists admit that we have only scratched the surface of dolphin intelligence"（尽管多年研究，科学家承认我们只触及海豚智能的表面）与空后"still trying to understand""complex"完美呼应，且" scratched the surface"与"far more complex"形成递进——既肯定研究进展又强调未知仍多。

- **[5] C**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与自然过渡。空[5]位于结尾段段首，前文已从认知、社会性、互助、交流多角度展现海豚智能。C选项"To sum up, the more we discover about dolphins, the more we realize how much we share with them—and how much we still have to learn"（总之，我们对海豚了解越多，就越意识到与它们的共同之处——以及我们仍要学习的还有很多）用"To sum up"总结全文，"learn more"与空后"As we learn more about these remarkable creatures"形成呼应，"protecting the oceans"则是"still have to learn"后的行动延伸。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **B. For this reason, many countries have passed laws to protect dolphins from being kept in small tanks.**：含"protect""dolphins"等关键词，且与空[5]后"protecting the oceans"有语义关联。但B聚焦"laws...small tanks"（立法禁止圈养），而全文从未讨论水族馆或圈养问题，而是从认知、社会性、交流等角度介绍海豚，结尾自然过渡到海洋保护。该选项属"偷换概念"型干扰——将"海洋生态保护"偷换为"圈养法律"，且文中无"this reason"可指代的具体原因。
