# 03-31 第一篇

**The Power of a Short Nap**

After a morning of classes, many students feel tired and find it hard to focus in the afternoon. A short nap of 15 to 30 minutes can make a big difference. [1] During a brief rest, your brain gets a chance to process what you learned in the morning and store it in your memory. This is why napping is often called "nature's reset button" for the mind.

Naps are not just good for your brain—they also help your body. When you are tired, your reaction time slows down and you may make more mistakes. [2] A quick nap can restore your alertness and improve your performance in sports or other activities. Many professional athletes and musicians take short naps before important events.

The key to a good nap is keeping it short. If you sleep for more than 30 minutes, you may enter deep sleep and wake up feeling groggy instead of refreshed. [3] Setting an alarm for 20 minutes will help you wake up at the right time and feel energized. The best time for a nap is usually between 1 p.m. and 3 p.m., when most people naturally feel a bit sleepy.

Some people worry that napping during the day will affect their sleep at night. [4] A short afternoon nap does not usually interfere with nighttime sleep, especially if you nap before 3 p.m. In fact, napping can reduce your overall fatigue, so you might even sleep better at night.

[5] Try finding a quiet place to rest your eyes for 15 to 20 minutes. You might be surprised by how much more focused and productive you become for the rest of the day.

## 选项

A. If you want to try napping, start with a simple plan.
B. However, this concern is often unfounded for short naps.
C. Napping gives your brain a chance to rest and recharge.
D. In that case, a nap might be exactly what you need.
E. That is why experts suggest limiting your nap to 20 or 30 minutes.
F. Studies show that people who take short naps perform better on memory tasks than those who do not.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] C   [2] D   [3] E   [4] B   [5] A

## 深度解析

- **[1] C**：
  - **线索**：主题引入与同义呼应。空前指出短睡（15-30分钟）能带来大不同，空后描述"During a brief rest, your brain...process...store it in your memory"。C选项"Napping gives your brain a chance to rest and recharge"（小睡让大脑有机会休息和充电）与空后"brain gets a chance to process"形成递进衔接。"rest and recharge"精准概括空后"process...store"的认知功能，且"your brain"与后文主语一致。

- **[2] D**：
  - **线索**：因果递进与语义对应。空前描述疲劳导致"reaction time slows down""make more mistakes"。D选项"In that case, a nap might be exactly what you need"（那样的话，小睡可能正是你所需要的）中"In that case"指代前文疲劳与表现下降的情形，"exactly what you need"自然引出空后"A quick nap can restore your alertness and improve your performance"——前后形成"问题→解决"的完整逻辑链。

- **[3] E**：
  - **线索**：问题—解答与逻辑衔接。空前强调"keeping it short"，并解释睡超过30分钟会进入深睡、醒来昏沉。E选项"That is why experts suggest limiting your nap to 20 or 30 minutes"（因此专家建议将小睡限制在20或30分钟内）用"That is why"承接前文因果。"limiting...to 20 or 30 minutes"与空后"Setting an alarm for 20 minutes"完美对应，空后是E的具体执行方式。

- **[4] B**：
  - **线索**：反驳担心与转折衔接。空前提出"担心白天小睡影响夜间睡眠"。B选项"However, this concern is often unfounded for short naps"（然而，对短睡而言这种担心往往没有根据）用"However"转折，"this concern"指代前文担忧，"unfounded"直接回应。空后"A short afternoon nap does not usually interfere with nighttime sleep"正是对B中"unfounded"的展开论证。

- **[5] A**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与行动号召。空[5]位于结尾段段首，前文已论证小睡对大脑、身体、时长、夜间睡眠的要点。A选项"If you want to try napping, start with a simple plan"（若想尝试小睡，从简单计划开始）用"If you want to try"呼应读者，引出空后具体建议"finding a quiet place""15 to 20 minutes"，"simple plan"与后文"finding...rest your eyes"形成呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. Studies show that people who take short naps perform better on memory tasks than those who do not.**：含"naps""memory"等关键词，且与空[1]后"store it in your memory"有语义关联。但空[1]需的是概括性引入句（大脑休息充电），而F是具体研究结论，更适合放在段落中间作论据。且F若填[1]会与空后"During a brief rest, your brain gets a chance to process..."重复（都在讲记忆/处理），缺乏递进感。属"语义相近但位置不当"型干扰。
