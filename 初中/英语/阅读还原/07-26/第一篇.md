**The Future of Solar Energy**

As the world seeks cleaner ways to power our lives, solar energy has emerged as one of the most promising solutions. The sun sends enough energy to Earth every hour to meet global needs for a full year. Yet we have only begun to tap this vast resource. [1] Solar power is becoming cheaper, more efficient, and more widespread. Understanding its potential and challenges helps us see what the future of energy might look like.

Solar technology has improved dramatically in recent decades. [2] Solar panels are now much more efficient at turning sunlight into electricity. The cost of solar power has dropped by over 90% since 2010, making it competitive with traditional fuels in many places. New materials and designs are being developed to capture more energy from weak or indirect light. Some panels can now work on cloudy days or even indoors. These advances make solar power more practical for homes, schools, and businesses around the world.

Storage remains a key challenge. [3] The sun does not shine at night, and cloudy weather reduces output. To use solar power around the clock, we need better ways to store excess energy. Battery technology is improving quickly—today's batteries can store more power and last longer. Some projects use pumped water or molten salt to store energy for later use. Researchers are also working on new storage methods. Solving the storage problem will unlock solar power's full potential.

Solar energy is creating new opportunities. [4] In remote villages without access to the electrical grid, solar panels provide light, power for phones, and energy for small businesses. Rooftop solar allows homeowners to produce their own electricity and even sell extra power back to the grid. Solar farms are being built in deserts and on water, using space that would otherwise be unused. The solar industry is creating millions of jobs worldwide. From rural schools to urban offices, solar is changing how we live and work.

The path to a solar-powered future is clear, but it requires commitment. [5] By investing in solar technology, improving storage, and making it accessible to more people, we can build a cleaner and more sustainable energy system. Governments, businesses, and individuals all have a role to play. The sun will keep shining—the question is whether we will make the most of it. The future of energy is bright, and solar power is leading the way.

## 选项

A. It is growing more affordable, efficient, and widely used.
B. Panels have become more efficient and cost-effective over time.
C. Better storage solutions are needed to use solar power day and night.
D. It is transforming how we access and use energy in many settings.

F. For example, some countries get over half their electricity from solar.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍太阳能潜力与未充分开发，空后总领"potential and challenges""future of energy"，A项"它正变得更便宜、更高效、更普及"承上启下，概括当前发展趋势，与后文展望呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：本段主题为技术进步，空后具体描述效率提升、成本下降、新材料、弱光与室内应用等，B项"光伏板随技术进步变得更高效、成本更低"准确概括段意。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：本段讲"Storage remains a key challenge"，空后分析昼夜与阴天限制、电池进步、抽水/熔盐储能为解决方向，C项"需要更好的储能方案以实现全天候使用"概括储能挑战与需求。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：本段讲太阳能带来的新机遇，空后列举偏远村庄、屋顶光伏、沙漠与水面电站、就业等，D项"它正在多种场景中改变我们获取和使用能源的方式"概括应用与变革。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：结尾段总结并号召，空前总述路径清晰但需承诺，空后"investing""improving storage""accessible""cleaner and more sustainable"，E项"我们必须投资太阳能以实现清洁、可持续的能源未来"呼应行动号召。

  - **F. For example, some countries get over half their electricity from solar.**：以 For example 开头，虽与太阳能应用相关，但仅为数据举例，无法作为段首概括句，且与 [2][3][4] 各段层次（技术、储能、机遇）不匹配，为干扰项。
