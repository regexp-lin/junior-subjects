# 05-03 第二篇

**The Art of Origami: More Than Just Paper Folding**

Origami（折纸） is the Japanese art of folding paper into beautiful shapes without using scissors or glue. With just a single sheet of paper, you can create birds, flowers, boxes, and even dragons. [1] Although origami may seem like a simple hobby, it has a long history and is used in surprising ways today—from classrooms to space stations.

Origami has been practiced in Japan for hundreds of years. [2] In the past, it was used mainly for religious ceremonies（宗教仪式） and special occasions like weddings. Folded paper decorations were considered gifts from the gods. Over time, origami spread to other countries and became a popular art form enjoyed by people of all ages. Today, there are thousands of different origami designs, ranging from simple to incredibly complex.

Learning origami can benefit people in many ways. [3] For students, it helps develop patience, focus, and problem-solving skills. Following the step-by-step instructions for a difficult model requires careful attention to detail. Origami also strengthens hand-eye coordination（手眼协调） and spatial thinking—the ability to imagine how flat shapes can become three-dimensional objects. Some teachers use origami in math classes to help students understand geometry（几何）.

Scientists and engineers have found practical uses for origami, too. [4] The folding patterns used in origami have inspired the design of solar panels（太阳能板） for satellites. These panels are folded tightly for the rocket launch and then unfold like a flower once in space. Doctors have used origami ideas to design tiny medical devices that can be inserted into the body and then expand to do their job. The science of folding is being used to solve real-world problems.

[5] What began as a simple art form centuries ago has become a powerful tool in science, education, and design. The next time you fold a paper crane（纸鹤）, remember that you are taking part in an art that connects history, creativity, and innovation.

## 选项

A. It is amazing how the simple act of folding paper can have such wide-ranging effects.
B. The principles of origami are now being applied in fields far beyond art.
C. It is much more than just a fun activity—it is also a valuable learning tool.
D. Originally, only the wealthy could afford paper, so origami was a luxury.
E. In short, origami is a perfect example of how ancient wisdom can inspire modern progress.
F. For example, the most complex origami models can take hours or even days to complete.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] D   [3] C   [4] B   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：引出折纸的广泛影响力。空前描述"With just a single sheet of paper, you can create birds, flowers, boxes, and even dragons"，空后说明"Although origami may seem like a simple hobby, it has a long history and is used in surprising ways today—from classrooms to space stations"。A选项"It is amazing how the simple act of folding paper can have such wide-ranging effects"（令人惊讶的是，简单的折纸行为竟能产生如此广泛的影响）中"simple act of folding paper"呼应空前的创作描述，"wide-ranging effects"自然引出空后"from classrooms to space stations"的多领域应用，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] D**：
  - **线索**：折纸的历史背景。空前提出"Origami has been practiced in Japan for hundreds of years"，空后说明"In the past, it was used mainly for religious ceremonies and special occasions like weddings...Folded paper decorations were considered gifts from the gods"。D选项"Originally, only the wealthy could afford paper, so origami was a luxury"（最初，只有富人才买得起纸，因此折纸是一种奢侈）中"Originally...wealthy...luxury"承接空前"hundreds of years"的历史背景，"only the wealthy could afford paper"解释了空后"religious ceremonies...special occasions"的限定用途，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：折纸对学习的价值。空前提出"Learning origami can benefit people in many ways"，空后列举"For students, it helps develop patience, focus, and problem-solving skills...strengthens hand-eye coordination and spatial thinking...Some teachers use origami in math classes"。C选项"It is much more than just a fun activity—it is also a valuable learning tool"（它不仅仅是一项有趣的活动——还是一种宝贵的学习工具）中"more than just a fun activity"呼应空前"benefit people in many ways"，"valuable learning tool"自然引出空后学生发展耐心、专注力等具体好处，衔接自然。

- **[4] B**：
  - **线索**：折纸原理在科学工程中的应用。空前提出"Scientists and engineers have found practical uses for origami, too"，空后具体描述"The folding patterns used in origami have inspired the design of solar panels for satellites...Doctors have used origami ideas to design tiny medical devices"。B选项"The principles of origami are now being applied in fields far beyond art"（折纸的原理现在正被应用于远超艺术的领域）中"principles of origami...fields far beyond art"承接空前"practical uses"，"far beyond art"自然引出空后太阳能板和医疗设备的科学应用，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"What began as a simple art form centuries ago has become a powerful tool...connects history, creativity, and innovation"。E选项"In short, origami is a perfect example of how ancient wisdom can inspire modern progress"（简言之，折纸是古代智慧如何启发现代进步的完美范例）用"In short"总结全文，"ancient wisdom...modern progress"与空后"began as a simple art form centuries ago...connects history, creativity, and innovation"精准呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the most complex origami models can take hours or even days to complete.**：涉及折纸的复杂度，易被误填[3]。但F以"For example"引出完成复杂模型所需时间，而空[3]前后文讨论的是折纸的学习价值与教育功能，空后具体列举耐心、专注力、手眼协调等好处；F侧重"耗时"本身而非"教育价值"，与段落主旨不匹配。F属"内容相关但功能角度偏移"型干扰。
