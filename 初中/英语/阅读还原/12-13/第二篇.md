**Winter Sports: Fun and Fitness in the Cold**

When temperatures drop and snow covers the ground, many people head outdoors for winter sports. Skiing, snowboarding, ice skating, and sledding are popular activities that turn cold weather into an opportunity for exercise and enjoyment. Winter sports are not just recreational—they build strength, improve balance, and boost mental health by getting people active during the darkest months of the year. [1] Winter sports offer a unique combination of physical challenge and natural beauty, making them an appealing way to stay fit and have fun even in the coldest seasons.

Skiing is one of the oldest and most widely practiced winter sports. It originated thousands of years ago in Scandinavia as a means of transportation across snowy landscapes. Today, millions of people visit ski resorts every winter. There are different styles, including alpine skiing, which focuses on speed and downhill runs, and cross-country skiing, which covers flat and hilly terrain and provides an intense cardiovascular workout. [2] The variety within skiing means that there is something for everyone, from beginners seeking gentle slopes to experienced athletes looking for thrilling downhill challenges.

Ice skating is another beloved winter activity. Natural frozen lakes and rivers once served as the only skating surfaces, but today indoor and outdoor rinks make the sport accessible year-round. Figure skating combines athletic ability with artistic expression, while speed skating tests endurance and power. Many children learn to skate at a young age, and for some, it becomes a lifelong passion. [3] Whether practiced on a frozen pond or in a modern arena, ice skating develops coordination, grace, and strength, and it brings joy to people of all ages.

Winter sports can carry risks. Cold temperatures may cause frostbite or hypothermia if people are not properly dressed. High-speed sports like skiing and snowboarding can lead to injuries such as broken bones or sprains. Wearing helmets, following safety rules, and taking lessons from qualified instructors all help reduce the danger. [4] Proper preparation and respect for safety guidelines allow people to enjoy winter sports with confidence, minimizing the chance of injury while maximizing the fun.

The popularity of winter sports continues to grow worldwide. The Winter Olympics showcase the highest level of competition in events from skiing to curling. Many countries are investing in indoor snow facilities, bringing winter sports to places that rarely see natural snowfall. [5] As access to winter sports expands through technology and investment, more people around the world are discovering the excitement and health benefits of staying active in the winter months.

- **[1] A**：

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说滑雪历史悠久——源自北欧交通工具、如今数百万人去滑雪场、分高山和越野两种风格，空后需总结滑雪的多样性。B的"variety within skiing means that there is something for everyone"概括两种风格，"from beginners seeking gentle slopes to experienced athletes"与不同难度对应。

- **[3] C**：
