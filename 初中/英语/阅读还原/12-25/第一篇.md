# 12-25 第一篇

**The World of Mushrooms**

Mushrooms are neither plants nor animals—they belong to a separate kingdom called fungi. They grow in forests, on logs, in soil, and sometimes even on other organisms. While we often notice the cap and stem above ground, the real action happens below: a vast network of fine threads called mycelium spreads through the soil, absorbing nutrients and breaking down dead matter. This hidden web connects trees and plants, helping forests communicate and share resources. [1]

Mushrooms play crucial roles in nature. They decompose dead wood and leaves, recycling nutrients back into the soil. Some fungi form partnerships with plant roots, helping plants absorb water and minerals in exchange for sugars. Without fungi, forests would struggle to thrive. At the same time, some mushrooms are poisonous, so identifying species correctly is essential for anyone who gathers wild mushrooms for food. [2]

People have used mushrooms for thousands of years—as food, medicine, and even in spiritual practices. Common edible varieties include button mushrooms, shiitake, and oyster mushrooms. In traditional Chinese medicine, certain mushrooms are believed to support health and immunity. Modern science is exploring how compounds in mushrooms might help fight disease or boost well-being. Research continues, but mushrooms remain a fascinating bridge between kitchen and lab. [3]

Growing mushrooms at home has become increasingly popular. Kits with pre-inoculated substrate make it easy to start. Different species need different conditions—some prefer cool and dark, others warmer and humid. With patience and the right setup, you can harvest your own mushrooms in a few weeks. Learning about mushrooms also means learning to respect their role in ecosystems—picking only what you need and leaving the rest to support wildlife. [4]

From forest floors to dinner plates, mushrooms connect us to the hidden life of the natural world. Understanding them deepens our appreciation for the complexity and mystery of life on Earth. [5]

## 选项

A. Mushrooms are part of a hidden world beneath our feet, playing a central role in how ecosystems function and thrive.
B. In this way, fungi are essential to the health of forests and the cycle of life—though respect and care are needed when we interact with them.
C. These uses show that mushrooms have long been valued by humans for both nutrition and healing.

F. For example, the fly agaric mushroom with its red cap and white spots is one of the most recognizable—and toxic—mushrooms in the world.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

## 深度解析

  - **线索**：空前介绍蘑菇既非植物也非动物、属真菌界、生长于森林原木土壤或其它生物、地上可见菌盖菌柄、地下菌丝网吸收养分分解死亡物质、连接树木植物助森林交流共享资源，空后需总结蘑菇在生态系统中的核心作用。A的"part of a hidden world beneath our feet, playing a central role in how ecosystems function"与前文呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说蘑菇在自然界起关键作用、分解枯木落叶回收养分、有的真菌与植物根系共生、助植物吸收水分矿物质换取糖、无真菌森林难繁荣、部分蘑菇有毒、正确识别对采食者至关重要，空后需总结真菌对森林健康与生命循环的重要性及与人类互动时的谨慎。B的"In this way"承接前文，"essential to the health of forests and the cycle of life"与前文呼应，"respect and care are needed when we interact with them"与前文对应。

- **[3] C**：
