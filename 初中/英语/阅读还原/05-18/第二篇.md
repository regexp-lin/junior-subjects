# 05-18 第二篇

**Sign Language and Deaf Culture: A World of Visual Communication**

For many deaf people, sign language is not just a way to communicate—it is at the heart of their culture and identity. Sign language uses hand shapes, facial expressions, and body movements to express meaning, and it is as rich and complete as any spoken language. [1] In fact, there are many different sign languages around the world, such as American Sign Language (ASL), British Sign Language (BSL), and Chinese Sign Language (CSL). Each has its own grammar and vocabulary. That is why we cannot simply "translate" sign language word by word from one country to another; they are different languages.

How does sign language work? [2] Signs are made with the hands, but the face and body are also important. For example, raising your eyebrows can change a statement into a question. The speed and size of a sign can show whether something is big or small, fast or slow. In other words, sign language is a full visual language that uses space, movement, and expression to convey meaning. Learning it takes time and practice, but it opens the door to a whole community.

Why is sign language important for deaf people? [3] For those who cannot hear, sign language is often the most natural way to communicate. It allows them to express themselves fully, share ideas, tell stories, and build friendships. Many deaf people feel that being forced to use only lip-reading or written words is limiting. Sign language gives them access to education, work, and social life on equal terms. For this reason, recognising sign language as an official language helps protect the rights of deaf people.

Deaf culture is rich and proud. [4] Deaf communities have their own history, art, poetry, and humour. Deaf artists create works that celebrate sign language and deaf identity. Many deaf people do not see deafness as a disability to be "fixed" but as a difference to be respected. They value their community and the bonds they share. So understanding deaf culture means seeing deaf people not as people who "lack" something, but as people with a unique way of living and communicating.

[5] In short, sign language and deaf culture deserve our respect and support. Learning a few basic signs can help us communicate with deaf friends or classmates. Schools and public places can become more inclusive by offering sign language classes or hiring sign language interpreters（手语翻译）. By valuing sign language, we build a society where everyone can participate fully. Remember: communication is a human right, and sign language helps make that right real for millions of people.

  - **线索**：手语的视觉与肢体表达方式。空前设问"How does sign language work?"，空后详述"Signs are made with the hands, but the face and body are also important...raising your eyebrows can change a statement into a question...The speed and size of a sign can show whether something is big or small, fast or slow...sign language is a full visual language that uses space, movement, and expression to convey meaning...Learning it takes time and practice"。B选项"In other words, sign language uses the whole body to express meaning"（换言之，手语用整个身体来表达意义）中"In other words"概括空后内容，"whole body to express meaning"与空后face、body、eyebrows、speed、size、visual language完美呼应，衔接自然。

  - **线索**：手语对聋人社会参与的重要性。空前设问"Why is sign language important for deaf people?"，空后说明"For those who cannot hear, sign language is often the most natural way to communicate...It allows them to express themselves fully, share ideas, tell stories, and build friendships...Being forced to use only lip-reading or written words is limiting...Sign language gives them access to education, work, and social life on equal terms...recognising sign language as an official language helps protect the rights of deaf people"。C选项"That is why sign language is essential for deaf people's full participation in society"（因此手语对聋人充分参与社会至关重要）中"That is why"承接空前及空后结论，"essential...full participation in society"与空后education、work、social life、rights精准对应，逻辑紧密。
