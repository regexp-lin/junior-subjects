# 05-12 第一篇

**Chinese Calligraphy: The Art of Writing with Brush and Ink**

Chinese calligraphy（书法） is one of the oldest art forms in the world. For thousands of years, people in China have used brushes, ink, and paper to create beautiful writing. [1] In fact, calligraphy is much more than just writing—it connects the mind, the body, and the spirit. When a calligrapher holds a brush, they must control their breath and movements carefully. Each stroke（笔画） shows not only the meaning of the words but also the feelings of the writer. That is why many people see calligraphy as a form of meditation（冥想） as well as an art.

Learning calligraphy takes time and patience. [2] First, students must practice basic strokes over and over again. They learn how to hold the brush correctly and how much ink to use. They copy famous works from masters of the past. Progress is slow, but with daily practice, their skills improve. In other words, calligraphy cannot be learned in a day—it requires years of dedication.

Calligraphy is closely linked to Chinese culture. [3] In the past, good handwriting was a sign of education and good character. People believed that the way a person wrote showed their inner qualities. Today, calligraphy is still taught in schools and practised during festivals. Many families hang calligraphy pieces in their homes for good luck. For this reason, calligraphy remains an important part of Chinese life and tradition.

Different styles of calligraphy have developed over time. [4] Some styles are clear and neat, while others are bold and free. Each style has its own rules and beauty. Masters like Wang Xizhi（王羲之） are still studied today because their works set high standards for later generations. However, modern calligraphers also experiment with new ideas while keeping the spirit of the art alive. So calligraphy continues to grow while honoring its long history.

[5] In short, Chinese calligraphy is a rich art that blends skill, culture, and personal expression. Whether you are a beginner or a master, there is always more to learn. If you have a chance to try it, pick up a brush and feel the peace that comes from writing with care. You may discover a lifelong passion for this beautiful art.

## 选项

A. That is because it combines physical skill with deep thinking and feeling.
B. In other words, becoming good at calligraphy demands long and steady effort.
C. That is why calligraphy has always been valued in Chinese society.
D. It is true that calligraphy has both traditional and modern forms.
E. In conclusion, calligraphy offers something valuable for everyone who tries it.
F. For example, some people use calligraphy to write birthday cards and wishes.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：总领书法超越书写层面的意义。空前描述"people in China have used brushes, ink, and paper to create beautiful writing"，空后详述"In fact, calligraphy is much more than just writing—it connects the mind, the body, and the spirit...calligrapher must control their breath and movements...Each stroke shows not only the meaning of the words but also the feelings of the writer...many people see calligraphy as a form of meditation as well as an art"。A选项"That is because it combines physical skill with deep thinking and feeling"（那是因为它将身体技艺与深邃的思考和情感相结合）中"That is because"解释书法不仅是书写的原因，"combines physical skill with deep thinking and feeling"与空后身心合一、呼吸控制、笔画承载情感及冥想属性精准对应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：概括学习书法需要持之以恒。空前提出"Learning calligraphy takes time and patience"，空后列举"First, students must practice basic strokes over and over again...learn how to hold the brush correctly...copy famous works from masters...Progress is slow, but with daily practice, their skills improve...calligraphy cannot be learned in a day—it requires years of dedication"。B选项"In other words, becoming good at calligraphy demands long and steady effort"（换言之，学好书法需要长期而持续的投入）中"In other words"概括空前"takes time and patience"，"long and steady effort"与空后反复练习、多年坚持及"years of dedication"完美呼应，衔接自然。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：书法在中国社会中的历史地位。空前提出"Calligraphy is closely linked to Chinese culture"，空后说明"In the past, good handwriting was a sign of education and good character...way a person wrote showed their inner qualities...still taught in schools and practised during festivals...families hang calligraphy pieces for good luck...remains an important part of Chinese life and tradition"。C选项"That is why calligraphy has always been valued in Chinese society"（因此书法在中国社会历来受到重视）中"That is why"承接空前"closely linked to Chinese culture"，"always been valued"与空后教育象征、品格体现、学校教授、节日练习及居家悬挂等形成呼应，逻辑紧密。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：书法风格的古今并存。空前提出"Different styles of calligraphy have developed over time"，空后对比"Some styles are clear and neat, while others are bold and free...Masters like Wang Xizhi are still studied...modern calligraphers also experiment with new ideas while keeping the spirit of the art alive...calligraphy continues to grow while honoring its long history"。D选项"It is true that calligraphy has both traditional and modern forms"（的确，书法既有传统形态也有现代形式）中"It is true that"加强衔接，"traditional and modern forms"与空后经典大师研习与现代创新实验精准对应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与开放邀请。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"Chinese calligraphy is a rich art that blends skill, culture, and personal expression...Whether you are a beginner or a master, there is always more to learn...pick up a brush and feel the peace...You may discover a lifelong passion"。E选项"In conclusion, calligraphy offers something valuable for everyone who tries it"（总之，书法为每个尝试者都提供有价值的内容）用"In conclusion"总结全文，"something valuable for everyone"与空后"whether beginner or master"及"lifelong passion"完美呼应，收束有力。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, some people use calligraphy to write birthday cards and wishes.**：涉及书法在日常中的应用场景，易被误填[3]。但F讨论的是书写生日贺卡等个人用途，而空[3]前后文强调书法与中国文化的深层联系，包括教育象征、品格体现、学校与节日及家庭悬挂祈福等社会文化层面；F属于具体生活用途举例，与空[3]的文化价值论述层级不匹配。F属"主题相关但论述层级错位"型干扰。
