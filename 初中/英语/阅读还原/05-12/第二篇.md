# 05-12 第二篇

**Color Psychology: How Colors Affect Our Feelings and Behavior**

Colors are everywhere in our daily life. We choose the color of our clothes, our room walls, and even the apps on our phones. [1] In fact, colors do more than just look nice—they can influence how we feel and act. Studies show that blue often makes people feel calm and focused, while red can increase energy and excitement. Yellow is linked to happiness and creativity. That is why designers and businesses pay careful attention to color when they create products and spaces.

Some colors are often used in specific places. [2] Hospitals and clinics often use soft blue or green because these colors help patients feel relaxed. Restaurants may use red or orange to make people feel hungry and eat more. Schools sometimes choose bright colors to keep students alert and interested. In other words, the colors around us are often chosen with a purpose, not by chance.

Colors can mean different things in different cultures. [3] In Western countries, white is often seen at weddings as a symbol of purity. In some Asian cultures, however, white is linked to sadness and is worn at funerals. Red can mean luck and joy in China, but in other places it may suggest danger or stop signs. For this reason, understanding color meanings helps us communicate better across cultures.

Colors also affect our choices as buyers. [4] Shops and brands use colors to attract attention and influence what we buy. A product with a bright package may stand out on the shelf. Ads with certain color combinations can make us feel trust or urgency. Marketers have studied which colors work best for different products and goals. So the next time you pick something from a store, think about whether color played a role in your choice.

[5] In short, colors are a powerful tool that shapes our emotions, decisions, and daily life. By becoming more aware of how colors affect us, we can make better choices—from the clothes we wear to the spaces we live in. So pay attention to the colors around you; they may be influencing you more than you think.

## 选项

A. That is because colors have a real effect on our minds and behavior.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：引出色彩对情绪与行为的影响。空前描述"We choose the color of our clothes, our room walls, and even the apps on our phones"，空后说明"In fact, colors do more than just look nice—they can influence how we feel and act...Studies show that blue often makes people feel calm and focused...red can increase energy...Yellow is linked to happiness and creativity...designers and businesses pay careful attention to color"。A选项"That is because colors have a real effect on our minds and behavior"（那是因为色彩对我们的心理和行为有真实影响）中"That is because"解释为何要关注色彩选择，"real effect on our minds and behavior"与空后研究结论及不同颜色的情绪效应精准对应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：总领不同场所对色彩的刻意运用。空前提出"Some colors are often used in specific places"，空后列举"Hospitals and clinics often use soft blue or green...help patients feel relaxed...Restaurants may use red or orange...make people feel hungry...Schools sometimes choose bright colors...keep students alert and interested...colors around us are often chosen with a purpose, not by chance"。B选项"In other words, the colors around us are often chosen with a purpose"（换言之，我们周围的色彩往往是有目的选择的）中"In other words"概括空后内容，"chosen with a purpose"与空后医院、餐厅、学校等场所的针对性选色完美呼应，衔接自然。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：色彩文化差异的重要性。空前提出"Colors can mean different things in different cultures"，空后对比"In Western countries, white is often seen at weddings as a symbol of purity...In some Asian cultures, white is linked to sadness and worn at funerals...Red can mean luck and joy in China, but in other places it may suggest danger...understanding color meanings helps us communicate better across cultures"。C选项"That is why we need to consider cultural differences when we talk about colors"（因此我们讨论色彩时需考虑文化差异）中"That is why"承接空前"different things in different cultures"，"consider cultural differences"与空后中西白色、红色寓意对比及跨文化沟通形成呼应，逻辑紧密。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：商业中色彩的营销功能。空前提出"Colors also affect our choices as buyers"，空后详述"Shops and brands use colors to attract attention and influence what we buy...bright package may stand out on the shelf...Ads with certain color combinations can make us feel trust or urgency...Marketers have studied which colors work best...think about whether color played a role in your choice"。D选项"It is true that businesses use colors to influence our buying decisions"（的确，商家用色彩影响我们的购买决定）中"It is true that"加强衔接，"influence our buying decisions"与空后吸引注意、影响购买、制造信任或紧迫感等精准对应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与行动建议。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"colors are a powerful tool that shapes our emotions, decisions, and daily life...By becoming more aware of how colors affect us, we can make better choices...pay attention to the colors around you...they may be influencing you more than you think"。E选项"In conclusion, understanding colors can help us live more wisely"（总之，理解色彩能帮助我们更明智地生活）用"In conclusion"总结全文，"live more wisely"与空后"make better choices"及"become more aware"完美呼应，收束有力。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, some artists prefer to work with only black and white.**：涉及艺术家对色彩的偏好，易被误填[1]或[3]。但F讨论的是艺术创作中的个人选择，而空[1]前后文是色彩对大众情绪行为的普遍影响，空[3]讨论的是不同文化中色彩的文化寓意；F属于特定群体（艺术家）的创作偏好，与空[1]的普遍心理效应、空[3]的文化象征均不匹配。F属"主题相关但论述对象与层级错位"型干扰。
