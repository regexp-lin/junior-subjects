**Volunteering: Giving Back to the Community**

Volunteering means giving your time and effort to help others without expecting payment. People volunteer at animal shelters, hospitals, libraries, community centers, and many other places. They might tutor students, clean up parks, visit the elderly, or organize events for those in need. Volunteering is not only good for the community—it can also be rewarding for the volunteer. [1] Through volunteering, we can make a positive difference in the lives of others while growing as individuals and strengthening our connection to the world around us.

Volunteering offers valuable experiences that are hard to get elsewhere. You might learn new skills, such as organizing events or communicating with different types of people. You can explore interests and discover what you are passionate about. Many employers and colleges value volunteer experience because it shows responsibility, teamwork, and a willingness to contribute. [2] The skills and experiences gained through volunteering can benefit both your personal growth and your future education or career, often in unexpected ways.

Volunteering brings people from different backgrounds together. When you work alongside others for a common goal, you build friendships and develop a stronger sense of community. You may meet people you would never have met otherwise—older adults, people from different cultures, or those facing challenges you have not experienced. This broadens your perspective and helps you understand the world better. [3] Volunteering builds bridges between people and creates a sense of belonging that strengthens neighborhoods and society as a whole.

Finding the right volunteer opportunity is important. Think about your interests, skills, and how much time you can give. Local organizations often need help, and schools sometimes organize volunteer projects. You can start small—even a few hours a month can make a difference. The key is to choose something you care about and stick with it. [4] Matching your volunteer work to your interests and availability increases the chance that you will enjoy it and make a lasting impact, rather than burning out or losing motivation.

The world faces many challenges—poverty, environmental problems, loneliness, and more. No single person can solve them all, but each of us can contribute. Volunteering is one way to do that. Whether you help for an hour or for years, your contribution matters. [5] In a world that often feels divided and busy, volunteering reminds us of our shared humanity and the power of people coming together to help one another.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍志愿活动——无偿付出时间与精力，在收容所、医院、图书馆、社区中心等处，辅导、清理公园、探访老人、组织活动等，强调对社区和志愿者本人都有益，空后需概括志愿活动的双向价值。A的"make a positive difference in the lives of others"与前文帮助他人对应，"growing as individuals and strengthening our connection to the world"与"rewarding for the volunteer"呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说志愿活动带来独特经历——学习组织活动、沟通等技能，探索兴趣与热爱，雇主和高校看重志愿经历体现的责任心与团队精神，空后需总结技能与经历对个人及未来的价值。B的"skills and experiences gained through volunteering"与前文呼应，"benefit both your personal growth and your future education or career"与雇主和高校重视对应，"often in unexpected ways"与前文"explore interests and discover"呼应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说志愿活动凝聚不同背景的人——共同目标中建立友谊与社区感，接触老人、不同文化者、面对困境者，拓宽视野、加深理解，空后需总结志愿活动的人际联结作用。C的"builds bridges between people"与前文"brings people from different backgrounds together"对应，"creates a sense of belonging that strengthens neighborhoods and society"与"stronger sense of community"呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说如何找到合适的志愿机会——考虑兴趣、技能、可投入时间，本地机构和学校常有需求，从小事做起、每月几小时也有意义，关键是选自己关心并能坚持的事，空后需总结匹配兴趣与时间的重要性。D的"Matching your volunteer work to your interests and availability"与前文"Think about your interests, skills, and how much time"呼应，"enjoy it and make a lasting impact, rather than burning out"与"stick with it"对应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说世界面临贫困、环境、孤独等挑战，个人虽无法全部解决但可以贡献，志愿是途径之一，无论投入多少时间都有价值，空后需总结志愿在当今社会的意义。E的"In a world that often feels divided and busy"与前文挑战呼应，"reminds us of our shared humanity and the power of people coming together"与"each of us can contribute"对应，是合理收尾。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, some high school students spend their weekends helping at a local food bank, packing boxes of groceries for families who cannot afford enough to eat.**：以For example开头，为高中生参与食品银行志愿的具体案例。各空均为概括或过渡句；[4]易被误选因涉及志愿活动，但该空需概括如何匹配兴趣与时间选择志愿，而非具体志愿场景的举例。
