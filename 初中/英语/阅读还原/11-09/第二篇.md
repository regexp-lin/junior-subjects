# 11-09 第二篇

**E-books vs Paper Books: The Reading Debate**

For centuries, people have read books made of paper. Today, e-books—digital books read on phones, tablets, or e-readers—have become increasingly popular. Many people enjoy the convenience of carrying hundreds of books in one device, while others prefer the feel and smell of physical pages. The debate over which is better continues, but both formats have their own strengths. [1] The choice between e-books and paper books often comes down to personal preference, lifestyle, and what kind of reading experience you are looking for.

E-books offer several advantages. They are lightweight and portable—you can access a whole library on a single device. E-books are often cheaper than print versions, and you can buy and download them instantly. Built-in dictionaries, search functions, and adjustable font sizes make reading easier for some people, especially those with visual difficulties. E-books also save paper, which can be good for the environment. [2] For readers who value convenience, affordability, and flexibility, e-books are an attractive option that fits well with our digital lifestyles.

Paper books have their own appeal. Many readers find that holding a physical book helps them focus and remember what they read better. Paper books do not require batteries or screens, so they can be read anywhere without worrying about power. They make good gifts, can be shared or lent easily, and some people simply enjoy collecting them. For children, turning pages and seeing illustrations in print can support learning and imagination. [3] The tactile experience and sense of ownership that paper books provide continue to attract millions of readers, even in the digital age.

Libraries and bookstores are adapting to both formats. Many libraries now offer both physical books and e-books through digital lending. Bookstores sell both print and digital editions. Some readers use both—e-books for travel and paper books for home. The important thing is that people keep reading, regardless of the format. [4] What matters most is not whether we choose paper or screen, but that we nourish a love of reading and make time for books in our busy lives.

The future of reading is likely to include both paper and digital options. New technologies may bring new ways to read and interact with text. Whatever format we prefer, books remain a source of knowledge, escape, and inspiration. [5] As long as we continue to read and value stories and ideas, the format we choose is less important than the act of reading itself—and the worlds it opens up to us.

## 选项

A. The choice between e-books and paper books often comes down to personal preference, lifestyle, and what kind of reading experience you are looking for.
B. For readers who value convenience, affordability, and flexibility, e-books are an attractive option that fits well with our digital lifestyles.
C. The tactile experience and sense of ownership that paper books provide continue to attract millions of readers, even in the digital age.
D. What matters most is not whether we choose paper or screen, but that we nourish a love of reading and make time for books in our busy lives.
E. As long as we continue to read and value stories and ideas, the format we choose is less important than the act of reading itself—and the worlds it opens up to us.
F. For instance, Kindle and other e-readers use special screens that reduce eye strain and look more like printed text than typical phone or tablet screens.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前对比纸质书与电子书——纸质书历史悠久，电子书在手机、平板、电纸书上阅读日益普及，有人爱便携、有人爱纸质感，争论持续但各有优势，空后需概括选择取决于什么。A的"The choice between e-books and paper books often comes down to personal preference, lifestyle, and what kind of reading experience you are looking for"与前文"both have their own strengths"和不同偏好对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前列举电子书优势——轻便、可携带大量书籍、较便宜、即买即下、内置词典与可调字体、环保省纸，空后需总结电子书适合的读者群体。B的"For readers who value convenience, affordability, and flexibility"与前文各项优势呼应，"fits well with our digital lifestyles"与便携、即时下载等对应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说纸质书的吸引力——实体书助专注与记忆、无需电与屏幕、易赠易借易收藏、对儿童翻页与插图有益，空后需总结纸质书提供的触感与归属感。C的"The tactile experience and sense of ownership that paper books provide"与前文"holding a physical book"和"collecting them"对应，"continue to attract millions of readers, even in the digital age"与纸质书仍有市场呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说图书馆与书店同时提供两种形式、部分读者两者兼用——电子书出行、纸质书在家，强调持续阅读比形式更重要，空后需总结核心在于培养阅读热爱与挤出时间。D的"What matters most is not whether we choose paper or screen"与前文"important thing is that people keep reading, regardless of the format"呼应，"nourish a love of reading and make time for books"与"keep reading"对应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前展望阅读未来——纸质与数字并存、新技术或带来新阅读方式，强调书是知识、逃避与灵感之源，空后需总结形式次要、阅读行为本身及其打开的世界更重要。E的"As long as we continue to read and value stories and ideas"与前文"books remain a source of knowledge, escape, and inspiration"呼应，"the format we choose is less important than the act of reading itself"与"D"中"not whether we choose paper or screen"逻辑一致，是合理收尾。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For instance, Kindle and other e-readers use special screens that reduce eye strain and look more like printed text than typical phone or tablet screens.**：以For instance开头，为 Kindle 等电纸书屏幕技术的具体案例。各空均为概括或过渡句；[2]易被误选因涉及电子书优势，但该空需概括电子书在便利、价格、灵活性上的整体吸引力，而非单一设备屏幕技术的举例。
