# 07-17 第一篇

**Earthquake Safety: What You Need to Know**

Earthquakes can happen anywhere, at any time. Although we cannot predict exactly when they will occur, we can prepare ourselves to stay safe when they do. Earthquakes are caused by the sudden movement of tectonic plates beneath the Earth's surface. They can cause buildings to collapse, trigger landslides, and sometimes create tsunamis in coastal areas. Knowing what to do before, during, and after an earthquake can save lives. [1]

[2] Before an earthquake, every household should have an emergency kit. This kit should include water, non-perishable food, a flashlight, a first-aid kit, and important documents. Families should also discuss and practice an evacuation plan. Identify safe spots in each room—under sturdy tables or against inner walls—and make sure everyone knows where to go.

[3] When the ground starts shaking, do not run outside. The greatest danger is often from falling objects and collapsing ceilings. Drop to the ground, take cover under a strong table or desk, and hold on until the shaking stops. If you are outdoors, move away from buildings, trees, and power lines. Stay calm and protect your head.

[4] After an earthquake, expect aftershocks. Check yourself and others for injuries before moving. If you are indoors, leave the building carefully once the shaking stops, watching for debris. Avoid using elevators. Listen to the radio or official announcements for updates. Do not re-enter damaged buildings until they have been declared safe.

[5] Earthquakes are frightening, but preparedness reduces fear and saves lives. By learning the right actions and practicing them with our families, we can protect ourselves when the unexpected happens.

## 选项

A. This is why earthquake preparedness is essential for everyone.
B. Preparation starts long before the ground ever shakes.
C. During an earthquake, the right actions can make all the difference.
D. The danger does not end when the shaking stops.
E. Knowledge and practice are our best defense against disaster.
F. For example, Japan conducts nationwide earthquake drills every year on Disaster Prevention Day.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：第一段介绍地震不可预测、成因、可能后果及事前事中事后准备的重要性。空格前强调"Knowing what to do... can save lives"。选项A "This is why earthquake preparedness is essential for everyone" 承接因果关系，点明 preparedness 的必要性，承上启下。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：第二段讲震前准备：应急包、家庭演练、安全点。选项B "Preparation starts long before the ground ever shakes" 概括震前准备的时间维度，与后文"before an earthquake"及具体措施一致。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：第三段讲震中应对：勿跑 outdoors、趴下掩护抓牢、户外避开建筑物树木电线。选项C "During an earthquake, the right actions can make all the difference" 点明震中时段及正确行动的重要性。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：第四段讲震后：余震、检查伤势、小心撤离、听官方信息、勿入危房。选项D "The danger does not end when the shaking stops" 自然引出震后仍存在危险，需持续警惕。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：末段总结：地震可怕但准备可减恐惧救生命，学习与演练是保护。选项E "Knowledge and practice are our best defense against disaster" 呼应"learning"与"practicing"，收束有力。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, Japan conducts nationwide earthquake drills every year on Disaster Prevention Day.**：与地震演练相关，可支撑震前准备。但第二段空格在段首，需主题句引出应急包与计划，插具体国家案例会打断"家庭准备"的论述 focus；且日本之例更适合"政府/社会层面"的段落。属主题相关但论述层次错位型干扰。
