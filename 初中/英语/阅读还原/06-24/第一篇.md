**Protecting Our World Heritage**

World Heritage Sites are places of outstanding value to all humanity—ancient temples, historic cities, natural wonders, and cultural landscapes. UNESCO, the United Nations organisation for education and culture, lists and protects these sites. [1] In other words, World Heritage Sites belong to everyone, and protecting them helps preserve our shared history and nature for future generations. However, many sites face threats from pollution, tourism, climate change, and conflict, so we all have a role to play in keeping them safe.

World Heritage Sites are chosen for their unique value. [2] For example, the Great Wall of China represents ancient military defence; the Pyramids of Egypt show the skills of early builders; the Amazon rainforest is home to countless species found nowhere else. Some sites are cultural—they tell the story of human creativity and history. Others are natural—they protect ecosystems（生态系统）and rare wildlife. To be listed, a site must meet strict criteria and be considered of "outstanding universal value." Once listed, countries promise to protect and manage the site for the benefit of all people.

Tourism brings both benefits and challenges. [3] Millions of visitors each year help local economies and raise awareness of these special places. Yet too many visitors can damage fragile sites—wearing away ancient stones, littering, or disturbing wildlife. To balance tourism and protection, many sites limit visitor numbers, set up walking paths, and use fees to fund conservation. Visitors can help by following rules, staying on marked trails, and respecting the site. Sustainable tourism means enjoying these places without harming them for future visitors.

Climate change is becoming a major threat. [4] Rising sea levels threaten coastal sites; melting ice affects mountain glaciers and polar heritage; stronger storms can damage historic buildings. Warmer temperatures and changing rainfall patterns also affect ecosystems in natural heritage sites. UNESCO and scientists are working to understand these risks and find ways to adapt—for example, by building barriers, moving artefacts to safer places, or monitoring sites more closely. Protecting heritage in a changing climate will require global cooperation.

[5] By supporting conservation, travelling responsibly, and understanding the value of these places, we can help ensure they remain for generations to come. The next time you visit or learn about a World Heritage Site, remember that it belongs to all of us—and so does the responsibility to protect it.

## 选项

A. In other words, World Heritage Sites belong to everyone, and protecting them helps preserve our shared history and nature for future generations.
B. For example, the Great Wall, the Pyramids, and the Amazon rainforest each represent different kinds of outstanding value.
C. Tourism brings income and awareness, but too many visitors can damage sites—sustainable tourism is the goal.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：概括世界遗产的普世价值与保护责任。空前描述"World Heritage Sites...places of outstanding value to all humanity—ancient temples, historic cities, natural wonders, cultural landscapes...UNESCO lists and protects"，空后说明"However many sites face threats from pollution, tourism, climate change, conflict...we all have role to play in keeping them safe"。A选项"In other words, World Heritage Sites belong to everyone, and protecting them helps preserve our shared history and nature for future generations"（换言之，世界遗产属于全人类，保护它们有助于为子孙后代保存我们共同的历史与自然）中"In other words"概括空前，"belong to everyone...protect...preserve...future generations"与空后"threats...we all have role"形成逻辑衔接，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：世界遗产的遴选标准与多元类型举例。空前提出"World Heritage Sites are chosen for their unique value"，空后详述"Great Wall...ancient military defence...Pyramids...skills of early builders...Amazon rainforest...countless species...Some sites cultural...human creativity, history...Others natural...ecosystems, rare wildlife...strict criteria...outstanding universal value...countries promise to protect"。B选项"For example, the Great Wall, the Pyramids, and the Amazon rainforest each represent different kinds of outstanding value"（例如，长城、金字塔和亚马逊雨林各自代表不同类别的突出价值）中"For example"引出案例，"Great Wall...Pyramids...Amazon...different kinds...outstanding value"与空后列举完美对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：旅游的双面性与可持续旅游。空前提出"Tourism brings both benefits and challenges"，空后描述"Millions of visitors...help local economies, raise awareness...Yet too many visitors can damage fragile sites...wearing away stones, littering, disturbing wildlife...balance tourism and protection...limit visitor numbers, walking paths, fees for conservation...Visitors follow rules, stay on trails, respect site...Sustainable tourism...enjoy without harming for future visitors"。C选项"Tourism brings income and awareness, but too many visitors can damage sites—sustainable tourism is the goal"（旅游带来收入与关注，但游客过多会损害遗址——可持续旅游是目标）中"income and awareness...too many visitors...damage...sustainable tourism goal"与空后内容精准对应，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：气候变化对遗产的威胁与应对。空前提出"Climate change is becoming a major threat"，空后列举"Rising sea levels threaten coastal sites...melting ice affects mountain glaciers, polar heritage...stronger storms damage historic buildings...Warmer temperatures, changing rainfall...affect ecosystems...UNESCO, scientists...understand risks, find ways to adapt...barriers, move artefacts, monitor...Protecting heritage in changing climate will require global cooperation"。D选项"Rising sea levels, melting ice, and stronger storms pose new risks that require global cooperation to address"（海平面上升、冰川融化和更强风暴带来新风险，需要全球合作应对）中"Rising sea levels...melting ice...stronger storms...new risks...global cooperation"与空后表述完美对应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与世界遗产的代际传承责任。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后为"By supporting conservation, travelling responsibly, and understanding the value of these places, we can help ensure they remain for generations to come. The next time you visit or learn about a World Heritage Site, remember that it belongs to all of us—and so does the responsibility to protect it."。E选项"World Heritage Sites are a gift from the past that we must pass on to the future"（世界遗产是过去的馈赠，我们必须将其传承给未来）用"gift from the past...pass on to the future"总结主题，"must pass on"与空后"support conservation...ensure remain for generations"形成呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. Some countries charge high entrance fees to famous heritage sites to control visitor numbers.**：涉及对著名遗产收取高门票以控制人数，易被误填[3]。但F以"high entrance fees"引出单一管理手段，而空[3]讨论的是旅游的利弊与可持续旅游的整体平衡（游客影响、限流、步道、规则、尊重等），F的"high fees"仅为众多措施之一，且与"balance...limit visitor numbers...fees to fund conservation"的全面论述层次不同；且F未涉及" sustainable tourism"这一核心概念。F属"主题相关但论述范围过窄"型干扰。
