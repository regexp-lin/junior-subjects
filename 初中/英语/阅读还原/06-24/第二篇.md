**Managing Your Emotions: A Skill for Life**

Everyone experiences strong emotions—anger, sadness, fear, joy, or anxiety. These feelings are normal and part of being human. [1] However, how we handle our emotions can greatly affect our relationships, our health, and our success at school or work. Learning to manage emotions is not about hiding or ignoring them; it is about understanding them and choosing how to respond. This skill, often called emotional intelligence, can be developed with practice.

The first step in managing emotions is to recognise what we feel. [2] When you feel upset, try to name the emotion—am I angry, disappointed, or stressed? Sometimes we mix up different feelings, and putting a name to them helps us understand ourselves better. It is also useful to notice what triggers our emotions—a difficult test, a conflict with a friend, or pressure from parents. Once we know what we feel and why, we can start to choose our response instead of reacting without thinking. Taking a few deep breaths or stepping away for a moment can give us time to calm down before we act.

Talking about emotions helps. [3] Sharing our feelings with a trusted friend, a parent, or a teacher can lighten the load and often leads to new perspectives. Sometimes we need to hear that our feelings are valid and that others have felt the same way. If emotions feel too heavy to handle alone, it is okay to seek help from a school counsellor or a professional. There is no shame in asking for support—it shows courage and self-awareness. Bottling up emotions for too long can harm our mental and physical health.

Healthy habits support emotional wellbeing. [4] Regular exercise, enough sleep, and a balanced diet all help our bodies and brains manage stress better. Hobbies such as reading, drawing, or playing music can be good outlets for emotions. Mindfulness and relaxation techniques—like simple breathing exercises or meditation—can train us to notice our feelings without being overwhelmed by them. Building a routine that includes rest, exercise, and activities we enjoy creates a foundation for emotional balance.

[5] It does not mean we will never feel angry or sad—it means we learn to ride the waves of emotion instead of being swept away. The next time you feel a strong emotion, pause, name it, and choose how you want to respond. You have more control than you might think.

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：从情绪普遍性转折到情绪管理的重要性。空前描述"Everyone experiences strong emotions—anger, sadness, fear, joy, anxiety...normal, part of being human"，空后说明"Learning to manage emotions not about hiding or ignoring...understanding and choosing how to respond...emotional intelligence...developed with practice"。A选项"However, how we handle our emotions can greatly affect our relationships, our health, and our success at school or work"（然而，我们如何应对情绪会极大影响我们的人际关系、健康和学业或工作上的成功）中"However"转折，"handle...affect relationships, health, success"与空后"manage...choose response...emotional intelligence"形成逻辑衔接，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：识别情绪及其诱因是管理的第一步。空前提出"The first step in managing emotions is to recognise what we feel"，空后详述"When upset, try to name emotion—angry, disappointed, stressed...sometimes mix up feelings...naming helps understand...notice triggers—difficult test, conflict, pressure...Once know what feel and why...choose response instead of reacting...deep breaths, step away...time to calm down"。B选项"When you feel upset, try to name the emotion and notice what triggers it; then you can choose your response"（当你感到不安时，试着给情绪命名并留意是什么引发它；然后你可以选择如何回应）中"name emotion...notice triggers...choose response"与空后内容完美对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：倾诉与求助对情绪管理的作用。空前提出"Talking about emotions helps"，空后描述"Sharing with trusted friend, parent, teacher...lighten load...new perspectives...hear feelings valid, others felt same...If too heavy...seek help school counsellor, professional...no shame...shows courage, self-awareness...Bottling up...harm mental, physical health"。C选项"Sharing feelings with others and seeking help when needed can reduce the weight of emotions"（与他人分享感受并在需要时求助可以减轻情绪的负担）中"Sharing...seeking help...reduce weight of emotions"与空后 lighten load、seek help、bottling up harm 精准对应，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：健康习惯对情绪健康的支持。空前提出"Healthy habits support emotional wellbeing"，空后列举"Regular exercise, enough sleep, balanced diet...manage stress better...Hobbies—reading, drawing, music...outlets for emotions...Mindfulness, relaxation—breathing, meditation...notice feelings without overwhelmed...Building routine—rest, exercise, enjoyable activities...foundation for emotional balance"。D选项"Exercise, sleep, a good diet, and hobbies can all support emotional wellbeing"（运动、睡眠、良好饮食和爱好都能支持情绪健康）中"Exercise...sleep...diet...hobbies...support emotional wellbeing"与空后列举完美对应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与情绪管理作为终身技能。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后为"It does not mean we will never feel angry or sad—it means we learn to ride the waves of emotion instead of being swept away.
