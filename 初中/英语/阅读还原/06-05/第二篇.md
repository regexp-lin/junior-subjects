# 06-05 第二篇

**Mathematics in Daily Life**

Many students ask, "When will I ever use math in real life?" The truth is, we use mathematics every day without even realizing it. [1] From telling the time and counting money to measuring ingredients for a recipe, math is everywhere. It helps us make decisions, solve problems, and understand the world around us. Whether you love it or find it challenging, math is a practical tool that connects to almost every part of daily life.

Think about shopping. [2] When you compare prices, calculate discounts, or decide how much of something you can afford, you are using arithmetic（算术）. If a shirt is 30% off, you need to work out the new price. When you split a bill with friends at a restaurant, you need to divide the total. Even planning a budget for the month requires adding and subtracting. Math helps us make smart choices about how we spend our money.

Math is also essential in the kitchen. [3] Recipes often use fractions—half a cup of flour, a quarter teaspoon of salt. If you want to double a recipe to serve more people, you need to multiply each ingredient. Baking is especially dependent on exact measurements, because small changes can affect how a cake rises or how cookies turn out. Cooking teaches us that math is not just numbers on paper—it has real, tasty results.

Travel and time depend on math, too. [4] When you plan a trip, you need to calculate distance, travel time, and perhaps fuel costs. Reading a bus schedule or a train timetable requires understanding hours and minutes. If your lesson starts at 9:15 and lasts 45 minutes, you use math to know when it ends. Time zones, speed, and maps—all of these involve numbers and logic that we use without thinking.

[5] Mathematics is not just a school subject—it is a language we use to navigate everyday tasks. It builds logical thinking and problem-solving skills that last a lifetime. So the next time you do math in class, remember that you are practising skills you will use again and again, from the supermarket to the kitchen to your first job.

## 选项

A. In other words, math is a hidden but constant part of our routines.
B. That is because everyday tasks like shopping involve numbers, percentages, and calculations.
C. In fact, cooking and baking are full of opportunities to use fractions and measurements.
D. These examples show that math connects to almost every activity we do outside the classroom.
E. In short, math matters because it equips us for the real world.
F. For example, architects use math to design buildings that are strong and safe.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：引出数学在生活中的无处不在。空前描述"we use mathematics every day without even realizing it"，空后列举"From telling the time and counting money to measuring ingredients...math is everywhere...make decisions, solve problems, understand the world...connects to almost every part of daily life"。A选项"In other words, math is a hidden but constant part of our routines"（换言之，数学是我们日常生活中隐蔽却恒常的一部分）中"In other words"概括空前"without realizing it"，"hidden but constant part of our routines"与空后"everywhere...every part of daily life"精准呼应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：购物中的数学运用。空前提出"Think about shopping"，空后描述"When you compare prices, calculate discounts, decide how much you can afford...30% off...work out new price...split a bill...divide...planning a budget...adding and subtracting"。B选项"That is because everyday tasks like shopping involve numbers, percentages, and calculations"（那是因为像购物这样的日常任务涉及数字、百分比和计算）中"That is because"解释购物与数学的关系，"numbers, percentages, and calculations"与空后比价、折扣、分摊、预算等具体计算完美对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：烹饪中的数学运用。空前提出"Math is also essential in the kitchen"，空后详述"Recipes often use fractions—half a cup, quarter teaspoon...double a recipe...multiply each ingredient...Baking...exact measurements...math has real, tasty results"。C选项"In fact, cooking and baking are full of opportunities to use fractions and measurements"（事实上，烹饪和烘焙充满使用分数和测量的机会）中"In fact"强化空前论点，"fractions and measurements"与空后半杯、四分之一茶匙、加倍、精确测量等内容精准对应，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：出行与时间管理中的数学。空前提出"Travel and time depend on math, too"，空后列举"plan a trip...calculate distance, travel time, fuel costs...bus schedule, train timetable...hours and minutes...lesson 9:15, lasts 45 minutes...time zones, speed, maps"。D选项"These examples show that math connects to almost every activity we do outside the classroom"（这些例子表明数学与我们课堂外的几乎所有活动都有关联）中"These examples"总领空前购物、厨房及本段出行、时间，"connects to almost every activity...outside the classroom"与空后多种场景呼应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"not just a school subject—a language we use to navigate everyday tasks...builds logical thinking and problem-solving skills...practising skills you will use...supermarket to kitchen to first job"。E选项"In short, math matters because it equips us for the real world"（简言之，数学重要是因为它为我们应对现实世界做准备）用"In short"总结全文，"equips us for the real world"与空后"navigate everyday tasks...use again and again"完美呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, architects use math to design buildings that are strong and safe.**：涉及建筑师用数学设计建筑，易被误填[4]。但F以"For example"引出建筑师这一职业应用，而空[4]前后文讨论的是普通人日常生活中的出行与时间（计划旅行、公交时刻表、课程时长、时区等），"architects"属于专业职业，与"daily life"中普通人活动层级不匹配；全文聚焦学生"when will I use math"的日常场景，F的专业应用超出该范围。F属"主题相关但应用场景错位"型干扰。
