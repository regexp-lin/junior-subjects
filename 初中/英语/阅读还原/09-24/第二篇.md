# 09-24 第二篇

**Endangered Languages**

There are about 7,000 languages spoken in the world today, but experts warn that nearly half of them could disappear within this century. When a language dies, we lose not just words and grammar, but an entire way of seeing the world. [1] Protecting endangered languages is about preserving the knowledge, stories, and identities of the communities that speak them.

Languages disappear for many reasons. When younger generations stop learning their parents' language and switch to a more dominant one, the old language slowly fades. [2] Globalization, urbanization, and the spread of major languages like English and Mandarin have accelerated this process in many regions.

Losing a language means losing unique knowledge. Many indigenous languages contain specialized vocabulary for local plants, animals, weather patterns, and medical practices that exist in no other language. [3] When these languages vanish, centuries of accumulated wisdom about the natural world disappear with them.

Efforts to save endangered languages are growing. Linguists work with communities to record and document languages before they are lost. Schools in some areas teach children in their native language alongside the national language. [4] Technology also helps—apps, websites, and digital dictionaries make it easier for people to learn and practice their ancestral tongues.

Language preservation is not about resisting change. People can speak multiple languages and still keep their heritage alive. [5] By supporting language diversity, we enrich the world's cultural heritage and ensure that future generations can access the unique perspectives and knowledge that each language carries.

## 选项

A. Every language represents a unique worldview, and its loss diminishes the richness of human culture.
B. This shift often happens gradually, driven by economic pressure and the desire for social mobility.
C. This irreplaceable knowledge makes endangered languages valuable not only culturally but also scientifically.
D. These combined efforts give hope that many languages can be revitalized and passed on to new generations.
E. Embracing multilingualism allows communities to honor their roots while participating fully in the modern world.
F. For example, the Aboriginal languages of Australia hold detailed knowledge about local ecosystems developed over 60,000 years.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前说语言消失意味着失去整个看待世界的方式，空后说保护濒危语言是保护知识、故事和身份。A的"Every language represents a unique worldview"承接前文，"its loss diminishes the richness of human culture"概括损失的严重性。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说年轻一代不再学母语转向强势语言，空后说全球化和城市化加速了这一过程。B的"This shift often happens gradually"承接语言转变，"driven by economic pressure and social mobility"解释深层原因。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说许多土著语言包含独特的本地知识词汇，空后说这些语言消亡时数百年智慧随之消失。C的"This irreplaceable knowledge"承接独特知识，"valuable not only culturally but also scientifically"概括双重价值。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前列举保护语言的多种努力（记录、学校教学、技术工具），空后无直接后续。D的"These combined efforts give hope"概括多方努力的成效，"many languages can be revitalized"传递乐观前景。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说人们可以说多种语言同时保持传统，空后说支持语言多样性丰富文化遗产。E的"Embracing multilingualism"承接多语言能力，"honor their roots while participating fully in the modern world"概括平衡传统与现代。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the Aboriginal languages of Australia hold detailed knowledge about local ecosystems developed over 60,000 years.**：以For example开头，属具体案例；易误放[3]，但[3]需的是濒危语言知识价值的概括，非具体语言案例。
