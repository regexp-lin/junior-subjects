# 05-02 第一篇

**Sleep Well, Live Well: Why Teenagers Need More Rest**

Most teenagers do not get enough sleep. Doctors say that young people between the ages of 13 and 18 need eight to ten hours of sleep each night, but many only get six or seven. [1] Without enough rest, the body and brain cannot work at their best. Over time, poor sleep can lead to serious problems with health, mood, and school performance.

Why do teenagers stay up so late? One reason is biology. During puberty（青春期）, the body's internal clock shifts, making teenagers feel sleepy later at night. [2] This means that even if a teenager goes to bed at 10 p.m., they may not fall asleep until midnight. When the alarm goes off early the next morning, they have not had nearly enough rest. This is not laziness—it is simply the way the teenage brain works.

Lack of sleep affects teenagers in many ways. [3] Students who are tired find it hard to pay attention in class and remember what they have learned. They may also feel more anxious or sad. Studies show that teenagers who sleep less than seven hours a night are more likely to gain weight and get sick. Good sleep is just as important as good food and regular exercise for staying healthy.

What can teenagers do to sleep better? [4] First, try to go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even on weekends. This helps your body develop a regular rhythm. Second, avoid screens for at least 30 minutes before bedtime, because the blue light from phones and computers can trick your brain into staying awake. Third, make your bedroom dark, quiet, and cool.

[5] Getting enough sleep is not a luxury—it is a necessity. By making small changes to your daily routine, you can improve your sleep and feel better in every part of your life. Your future self will thank you for it.

## 选项

A. In other words, their bodies are programmed to stay awake later than adults.
B. Here are some simple tips that can help.
C. The effects on both the body and the mind can be quite serious.
D. In short, sleep should be a top priority for every teenager.
E. This is becoming a growing concern among parents and doctors.
F. For example, drinking warm milk before bed has been shown to help some people relax.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] E   [2] A   [3] C   [4] B   [5] D

## 深度解析

- **[1] E**：
  - **线索**：引出青少年睡眠不足的担忧。空前描述"Most teenagers do not get enough sleep...many only get six or seven"，空后说明"Without enough rest, the body and brain cannot work at their best...poor sleep can lead to serious problems"。E选项"This is becoming a growing concern among parents and doctors"（这正在成为家长和医生日益关注的问题）中"This...growing concern"承接空前睡眠不足的现状，"parents and doctors"体现问题的严重性，自然过渡到空后"serious problems"的具体后果，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] A**：
  - **线索**：解释青少年生物钟的特点。空前说明"the body's internal clock shifts, making teenagers feel sleepy later at night"，空后具体举例"even if a teenager goes to bed at 10 p.m., they may not fall asleep until midnight...This is not laziness—it is simply the way the teenage brain works"。A选项"In other words, their bodies are programmed to stay awake later than adults"（换言之，他们的身体被设定为比成年人更晚入睡）中"In other words"概括上文"internal clock shifts"，"programmed to stay awake later"与空后"not fall asleep until midnight"和"the way the teenage brain works"完美呼应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：睡眠不足的严重影响。空前提出"Lack of sleep affects teenagers in many ways"，空后列举"tired...hard to pay attention...remember what they have learned...feel more anxious or sad...gain weight and get sick"。C选项"The effects on both the body and the mind can be quite serious"（对身体和心理的影响可能相当严重）中"effects on...body and mind"与空前"affects teenagers"呼应，"quite serious"总领空后身体（gain weight, get sick）和心理（anxious, sad）两方面的具体影响，衔接自然。

- **[4] B**：
  - **线索**：引出改善睡眠的建议。空前提问"What can teenagers do to sleep better?"，空后列举"First, try to go to bed and wake up at the same time...Second, avoid screens...Third, make your bedroom dark, quiet, and cool"。B选项"Here are some simple tips that can help"（以下是一些有用的简单建议）直接回应空前提问，"some simple tips"自然引出空后"First...Second...Third..."的建议列表，衔接得当。

- **[5] D**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"Getting enough sleep is not a luxury—it is a necessity...improve your sleep and feel better...Your future self will thank you"。D选项"In short, sleep should be a top priority for every teenager"（简言之，睡眠应成为每个青少年的首要事项）用"In short"总结全文，"top priority"与空后"necessity"形成语义呼应，"every teenager"点明对象，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, drinking warm milk before bed has been shown to help some people relax.**：涉及助眠小方法，易被误填[4]。但F以"For example"引出喝热牛奶的单一具体方法，而空[4]后已有"First...Second...Third..."的并列建议结构，F作为举例与该并列结构不匹配；且空[4]需要的是引出建议的过渡句而非具体的单一例子。F属"内容相关但衔接层级与结构不匹配"型干扰。
