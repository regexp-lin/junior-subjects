# 11-12 第一篇

**Museums: Windows to the Past and the Future**

Museums are places where we can learn about art, history, science, and culture. They preserve precious objects and share stories that might otherwise be lost. A visit to a museum can take you back thousands of years or show you the latest discoveries in technology. People of all ages can discover something new. [1]

There are many types of museums. Art museums display paintings, sculptures, and other works from around the world. History museums tell the stories of past events and how people lived long ago. Science museums let you experiment with hands-on exhibits. Natural history museums show fossils, animals, and plants. Some museums focus on a single topic, such as music, sports, or ships. [2]

Visiting a museum does not have to be boring. Many museums now use videos, touch screens, and interactive displays to make learning fun. You can join a guided tour, attend a workshop, or explore at your own pace. Some museums offer free admission on certain days or discounts for students. Planning your visit in advance can help you focus on what you want to see most. [3]

Museums also play an important role in protecting our heritage. They care for fragile objects, restore damaged artworks, and keep records for future generations. When natural disasters or wars threaten cultural treasures, museums work to safeguard them. They also work with schools to bring learning to life. [4]

In a world of screens and quick information, museums offer something different. They encourage us to slow down, look closely, and think deeply. A single painting or artifact can tell a story that stays with you for years. [5]

## 选项

A. Museums connect us to human history and creativity, offering a unique way to learn beyond the classroom.
B. No matter what your interests are, there is likely a museum that can spark your curiosity and deepen your understanding.
C. With the right approach, a museum visit can be both educational and enjoyable, leaving you with lasting memories and new questions to explore.
D. By preserving and sharing our cultural and scientific heritage, museums help us understand where we come from and inspire us to imagine where we are going.
E. Museums remind us that some things are worth slowing down for—and that the best learning often happens when we take time to wonder and reflect.
F. For example, the British Museum in London holds over 8 million objects and welcomes more than 6 million visitors each year from all over the world.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前说博物馆的作用——学习艺术历史科学文化、保存珍贵物品、分享可能失传的故事、可回望千年或接触最新科技、各年龄段都能发现新事物；空后需总结博物馆与人类历史及创造力的连接。A的"connect us to human history and creativity"与前文呼应，"unique way to learn beyond the classroom"与"learn about"和"discover"对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前列举博物馆类型——艺术馆、历史馆、科学馆、自然史馆、专题馆（音乐体育船舶等）；空后需总结各类博物馆对兴趣与理解的促进作用。B的"No matter what your interests are"与前文多种类型对应，"spark your curiosity and deepen your understanding"与前文各类内容呼应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说参观可以有趣——视频触摸屏互动、导览工作坊自助探索、免费日或学生优惠、提前规划；空后需总结正确方式下参观的收获。C的"With the right approach"与前文 planning、guided tour 等呼应，"educational and enjoyable"与"learning fun"对应，"lasting memories and new questions"与"explore"对应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说博物馆的保护职能——护理脆弱物品、修复受损艺术品、为后世留存记录、应对灾害战争、与学校合作；空后需总结 preserving 与 sharing 的深远意义。D的"By preserving and sharing our cultural and scientific heritage"与前文 protecting、care for、restore 呼应，"understand where we come from and inspire us to imagine where we are going"是合理升华。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前对比屏幕时代与博物馆——慢下来、细看、深思、单件作品可留下长久记忆；空后需总结博物馆对节奏与反思的提醒。E的"some things are worth slowing down for"与前文"slow down, look closely, think deeply"呼应，"take time to wonder and reflect"与"think deeply"对应，是合理收尾。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the British Museum in London holds over 8 million objects and welcomes more than 6 million visitors each year from all over the world.**：以 For example 开头，为大英博物馆的具体数据举例。各空均为概括或过渡句；[1] 易被误选因涉及博物馆规模与参观，但该空需概括博物馆与人类历史创造力的连接，而非某一馆的统计数据。
