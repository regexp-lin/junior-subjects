# 06-03 第二篇

**Cave Exploration: Adventures Under the Ground**

Caves have always fascinated humans. These dark, mysterious spaces underground hold secrets that have taken millions of years to form. [1] Cave exploration, or caving, is an exciting activity that mixes adventure, science, and a love of nature. People who explore caves, called cavers or speleologists（洞穴学家）, discover stunning rock formations, underground rivers, and sometimes even ancient fossils or cave paintings.

What makes caves so special? Over thousands of years, water slowly dissolves rock and creates hollow spaces. [2] Stalactites（钟乳石） hang from the ceiling like stone icicles, while stalagmites（石笋） grow up from the floor. When they meet, they form columns that can be several meters tall. Some caves have underground lakes or rivers that have carved beautiful passages through the rock. The shapes and colours in caves often look like something from another world.

Caving requires careful preparation. [3] Cavers need a helmet with a headlamp, strong ropes, and warm, waterproof clothing. They must work as a team and follow safety rules, because caves can be dark, wet, and full of narrow passages. Getting lost or injured in a cave can be very dangerous. That is why beginners should always go with experienced guides. Many countries have caves that are open to the public, where visitors can see the wonders underground in a safe way.

Caves are also important for science. [4] Scientists study caves to learn about Earth's history, climate change, and even to discover new species of animals that live only in the dark. Some cave fish and insects have lost their sight or colour because they have adapted to life without light. Caves can also protect ancient human remains and artefacts（文物）, helping us understand how our ancestors lived.

[5] Caves are natural treasures that offer adventure, beauty, and knowledge. Whether you are a scientist, an adventurer, or simply curious, there is something in the underground world to amaze you. So the next time you think about exploration, remember—some of the greatest wonders are right beneath your feet.

## 选项

A. In other words, caving attracts people who enjoy both physical challenge and scientific discovery.
B. That is because water and minerals create amazing structures over long periods of time.

E. In short, caves are remarkable places that connect us to the past and inspire wonder.
F. For example, the Lascaux cave in France contains paintings that are over 17,000 years old.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：引出洞穴探险的性质与吸引力。空前描述"Caves have always fascinated humans...dark, mysterious spaces...hold secrets...millions of years to form"，空后说明"Cave exploration, or caving, is an exciting activity that mixes adventure, science, and a love of nature...cavers discover stunning rock formations, underground rivers, fossils, cave paintings"。A选项"In other words, caving attracts people who enjoy both physical challenge and scientific discovery"（换言之，洞穴探险吸引既喜欢身体挑战又热爱科学发现的人）中"In other words"概括空前对人类吸引力，"physical challenge and scientific discovery"与空后"adventure, science, love of nature"及"fossils, cave paintings"等发现呼应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：洞穴内部结构的形成原理。空前提出"What makes caves so special? Over thousands of years, water slowly dissolves rock and creates hollow spaces"，空后详述"Stalactites hang from the ceiling...stalagmites grow up from the floor...form columns...underground lakes or rivers...carved beautiful passages"。B选项"That is because water and minerals create amazing structures over long periods of time"（那是因为水和矿物质在漫长岁月中创造出令人惊叹的结构）中"That is because"回答空前问句，"water and minerals...long periods"与空后钟乳石、石笋、地下河的形成机制精准对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：洞穴探险的安全要求。空前提出"Caving requires careful preparation"，空后列举"Cavers need helmet with headlamp, ropes, waterproof clothing...work as a team, follow safety rules...beginners should always go with experienced guides...caves open to the public...safe way"。C选项"In fact, proper equipment and trained guides are essential for a safe caving experience"（事实上，合适的装备和受过培训的向导对安全的洞穴探险至关重要）中"In fact"强化空前准备的重要性，"proper equipment and trained guides"与空后装备清单和"experienced guides"完美对应，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：洞穴的科研价值。空前提出"Caves are also important for science"，空后列举"Scientists study...Earth's history, climate change...discover new species...cave fish and insects...ancient human remains and artefacts...understand how ancestors lived"。D选项"This makes caves valuable not only for visitors but also for researchers"（这使得洞穴不仅对游客有价值，对研究者也同样重要）中"This"承接空前"important for science"，"valuable for researchers"与空后科学家研究历史、气候、新物种、古人遗存等内容精准对应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"natural treasures...adventure, beauty, and knowledge...scientist, adventurer, or curious...greatest wonders...beneath your feet"。E选项"In short, caves are remarkable places that connect us to the past and inspire wonder"（简言之，洞穴是连接我们与过去并激发惊叹的非凡之地）用"In short"总结全文，"connect us to the past"与空后"ancient...knowledge"呼应，"inspire wonder"与"amaze you"对应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the Lascaux cave in France contains paintings that are over 17,000 years old.**：涉及法国拉斯科洞穴的史前壁画，易被误填[4]。但F以"For example"引出单一洞穴的具体案例，而空[4]前后文讨论的是洞穴对科学的整体价值，空后列举的是研究 Earth's history、climate、new species、ancient remains 等广义科研范畴；F的 Lascaux 可作为"ancient human remains and artefacts"的例证，但空[4]需要的是对"caves valuable for researchers"的总结句，F为具体例子无法担任该承上启下功能。F属"主题相关但衔接功能错位"型干扰。
