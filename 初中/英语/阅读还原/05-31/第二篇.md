**Traditional Chinese Medicine Basics: An Introduction**

Traditional Chinese Medicine, or TCM, has been used in China for thousands of years. It includes herbal medicine（中药）, acupuncture（针灸）, massage, and exercises like Tai Chi. TCM sees the body differently from Western medicine—it focuses on balance and the flow of energy, or "qi"（气）. [1] That is because TCM aims to prevent illness and treat the whole person, not just a single symptom. Instead of targeting one part of the body, TCM doctors look at how all parts work together. They may suggest changes in diet, lifestyle, and herbs to restore balance. Many people use TCM along with Western medicine. In short, TCM offers a different way to understand health. It has influenced medicine and wellness around the world.

What are Chinese herbs? [2] In other words, they are plants, roots, and minerals used to treat or prevent illness. Common herbs include ginseng（人参）for energy, chrysanthemum（菊花）for cooling, and goji berries（枸杞）for the eyes. Herbs are often combined in formulas—several ingredients work together for a specific purpose. Some herbs are boiled in water to make a tea-like drink; others are made into pills or powders. TCM doctors choose herbs based on each person's condition. What works for one person may not work for another. Herbs have been used for centuries, and modern research is studying how they work. Many herbs are now used in health products worldwide.

What is acupuncture? [3] That is why it uses thin needles to stimulate points on the body. According to TCM, the body has pathways called meridians（经络）through which qi flows. When qi is blocked, pain or illness may occur. Acupuncture aims to unblock these pathways by inserting needles at specific points. The needles are very thin, and many people feel little or no pain. Acupuncture is often used for pain, headaches, stress, and nausea（恶心）. Scientists are still learning how it works, but many studies suggest it can help with certain conditions. For this reason, acupuncture is now offered in some hospitals and clinics in the West. It has become one of the most well-known TCM practices globally.

How does TCM view diet? [4] It is true that food is seen as medicine in TCM. Different foods have different natures—some are "warming," others are "cooling." TCM doctors may suggest certain foods to balance the body. For example, ginger（姜）is often used to warm the stomach; mung beans（绿豆）may be suggested to cool the body in summer. Eating according to the seasons and one's condition is an important TCM idea. There is a saying: "Food and medicine share the same origin." This means that what we eat can support our health as much as medicine. A balanced diet is a key part of TCM wellness.

[5] It has a long history and is still used by millions of people today. Whether you use herbs, acupuncture, or dietary advice, TCM can complement modern medicine. So the next time you hear about TCM, remember: it is not a replacement for Western medicine, but another way to support your health. Let us appreciate the wisdom of ancient practices while embracing the best of modern science.
