**Food Waste: A Global Problem**

Every year, about one third of all food produced in the world is wasted. That is nearly 1.3 billion tons of food thrown away while hundreds of millions of people go hungry. Food waste happens at every stage—from farms and factories to supermarkets and homes. [1] Reducing food waste is one of the most effective things we can do to fight hunger, save money, and protect the environment.

On farms, food is wasted because crops may not meet appearance standards or because there are not enough workers to harvest everything. In factories and stores, food is discarded when it passes its "sell by" date, even if it is still safe to eat. [2] These losses add up to enormous amounts of perfectly good food that never reaches anyone's plate.

At home, food waste is also a big problem. People buy more than they need, forget about leftovers in the fridge, or throw away food that looks slightly imperfect. [3] Planning meals, making shopping lists, and storing food properly are simple habits that can significantly cut down on household waste.

Food waste also harms the environment. When food ends up in landfills, it produces methane—a greenhouse gas that is far more powerful than carbon dioxide. [4] The water, energy, and land used to grow wasted food are also lost, making food waste a major contributor to environmental damage.

Solutions exist at every level. Governments can improve food distribution systems, businesses can donate unsold food, and individuals can change their daily habits. [5] By working together to value food and reduce waste, we can build a more sustainable world where fewer people go hungry and fewer resources are squandered.

## 选项

A. Tackling food waste is not just about saving food—it is about creating a fairer and more efficient food system for all.
B. At each step in the supply chain, perfectly edible food is lost due to inefficiency, strict standards, or poor planning.
C. With a little effort and awareness, families can save both food and money while helping the planet.
D. This means that wasting food is not only an ethical issue but also a serious environmental one.
E. When everyone plays their part, even small changes in behavior can lead to a massive reduction in global food waste.
F. For example, in the UK alone, households throw away about 6.6 million tons of food each year.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述食物浪费的惊人规模，空后说减少浪费能抗饥饿、省钱、保护环境。A的"not just about saving food—it is about creating a fairer and more efficient food system"概括多层面意义，与后文三个目标呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前分别列举农场和工厂/商店的浪费原因，空后说这些损失使大量好食物无人食用。B的"At each step in the supply chain"概括供应链各环节，"perfectly edible food is lost"与"perfectly good food"呼应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前列举家庭浪费的原因（买多了、忘了剩菜、扔不完美食物），空后说计划膳食、列购物清单等简单习惯能减少浪费。C的"With a little effort and awareness"承接简单行动，"save both food and money while helping the planet"概括多重好处。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说食物进入垃圾填埋场产生甲烷，空后说生产浪费食物的水、能源和土地也浪费了。D的"wasting food is not only an ethical issue but also a serious environmental one"概括伦理与环境双重问题。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前列举各层面的解决方案（政府、企业、个人），空后说共同努力减少浪费。E的"When everyone plays their part"承接各方行动，"small changes in behavior can lead to a massive reduction"概括集体效果。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, in the UK alone, households throw away about 6.6 million tons of food each year.**：以For example开头，属具体国家数据；易误放[3]，但[3]需的是家庭减废行动的概括，非具体数据。
