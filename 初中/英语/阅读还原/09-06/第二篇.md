# 09-06 第二篇

**The Art of Public Speaking**

Public speaking is a skill that almost everyone needs at some point in life. Whether you are giving a presentation at school, speaking at a meeting, or making a toast at a family event, being able to communicate clearly in front of others is valuable. [1] While many people feel nervous about public speaking, it is a skill that anyone can learn and improve with practice.

One of the keys to good public speaking is preparation. Knowing your topic well gives you confidence and helps you stay on track. [2] Organizing your ideas clearly—with a strong opening, a logical middle, and a memorable conclusion—makes your speech easier for the audience to follow and remember.

Body language plays an important role too. Making eye contact, standing up straight, and using natural hand gestures all help convey your message. [3] When your body language matches your words, your audience is more likely to trust you and pay attention to what you are saying.

Dealing with nervousness is something every speaker faces. Even experienced speakers feel butterflies before going on stage. [4] Taking deep breaths, practicing beforehand, and focusing on the message rather than on yourself can help turn nervous energy into enthusiasm and excitement.

Public speaking is not just about talking—it is about connecting with your audience. The best speakers make their listeners feel involved, inspired, or moved to action. [5] By developing your public speaking skills, you gain the ability to share your ideas, influence others, and make your voice heard in the world.

## 选项

A. The ability to speak well in public opens doors in education, career, and personal relationships.
B. A well-structured speech is like a journey—it takes the listener somewhere meaningful step by step.
C. These nonverbal signals are just as important as the words you choose, sometimes even more so.
D. The secret is not to eliminate nervousness but to learn how to manage it and use it to your advantage.
E. Mastering this skill empowers you to express yourself with clarity, confidence, and purpose in any situation.
F. For example, Martin Luther King Jr.'s "I Have a Dream" speech is remembered for its powerful delivery and emotion.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前列举需要公开演讲的场景，空后说很多人紧张但可以通过练习提高。A的"opens doors in education, career, and personal relationships"概括公开演讲的广泛价值，承接前文场景。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说准备充分带来自信，空后说用清晰结构组织想法使演讲更易跟随。B的"A well-structured speech is like a journey"比喻结构的重要性，"takes the listener somewhere meaningful"与"easier for the audience to follow"呼应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前列举肢体语言要素（眼神、站姿、手势），空后说肢体语言与语言匹配时更有说服力。C的"These nonverbal signals"承接前文肢体语言，"just as important as the words"概括其与语言同等重要。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说即使有经验的演讲者也会紧张，空后列举缓解紧张的方法。D的"The secret is not to eliminate nervousness but to learn how to manage it"概括应对策略，"use it to your advantage"引出将紧张转化为热情。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说最好的演讲者让听众参与、受启发或行动，空后无。E的"Mastering this skill empowers you to express yourself"升华主题，"clarity, confidence, and purpose"总结演讲的核心目标。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, Martin Luther King Jr.'s "I Have a Dream" speech is remembered for its powerful delivery and emotion.**：以For example开头，属名人演讲具体案例；易误放[3]或[5]，但两空分别需肢体语言重要性的概括和演讲能力的升华总结，非个案。
