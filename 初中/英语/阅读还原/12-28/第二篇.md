**The Circus and Acrobatics: Art of the Body**

Circus has entertained audiences for centuries. It grew from fairgrounds and travelling shows into today's mix of acrobatics, clown acts, animal performances, and aerial stunts. Modern circuses often focus on human skills—flips, balancing, and teamwork—rather than animals. Companies like Cirque du Soleil have turned circus into a form of theatre and art. [1]

Acrobats train for years to master their craft. They build strength, flexibility, and timing through daily practice. A simple handstand or cartwheel may look easy, but it requires control of every muscle. Partners must trust each other completely when performing lifts or pyramids. One mistake can lead to serious injury, so safety and discipline are essential. [2]

The circus brings people together. Families and friends gather to watch shows under the big top or in modern theatres. Laughter, gasps, and applause create a shared experience. In some cultures, circus skills are passed down through generations. Schools and camps teach children the basics, helping them build confidence and coordination. [3]

Today, circus arts are recognised as a serious discipline. Athletes compete in acrobatic gymnastics at international level. Dance and circus often combine in contemporary performances. Street performers use juggling and tricks to earn a living in cities around the world. The skills stay the same; the stages and styles keep evolving. [4]

Whether on a stage, in a tent, or on the street, circus continues to amaze us. It shows what the human body can achieve with dedication and teamwork. For those who watch or participate, it offers wonder, joy, and a reminder of what we can accomplish when we push our limits. [5]

## 选项

A. In short, circus has evolved from traditional shows into a blend of sport, art, and entertainment.
B. For these reasons, acrobatics demands not only talent but also long-term dedication and trust between partners.
C. As a result, circus creates a sense of community and has become part of cultural heritage in many places.
D. Thanks to these developments, circus skills are now valued as both competition and performance art.
E. In other words, circus remains a timeless source of wonder and a celebration of human ability.
F. For example, trapeze artists often perform high above the audience, swinging between bars.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍马戏历史——从市集巡演到杂技小丑动物高空、现代侧重人文技能、Cirque du Soleil 将马戏发展为戏剧与艺术，空后需总结马戏的演变与融合。A的"evolved from traditional shows into a blend of sport, art, and entertainment"与前文对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说杂技演员多年训练、培养力量柔韧与节奏、简单动作也需全身控制、搭档需完全信任、失误可致重伤、安全与纪律重要，空后需总结杂技对天赋、投入与信任的要求。B的"not only talent but also long-term dedication and trust between partners"与前文呼应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说马戏凝聚观众、家人朋友共赏、欢笑惊呼与掌声形成共享体验、部分文化中技能代代相传、学校营地教基础帮助建立自信与协调，空后需总结马戏的社群感与文化传承。C的"sense of community"与"cultural heritage in many places"与前文对应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说马戏艺术被视为严肃学科、杂技体操有国际竞技、舞蹈与马戏在当代演出中结合、街头艺人在全球城市谋生、技能不变舞台与风格在变，空后需总结这些发展的意义。D的"these developments"指代前文，"valued as both competition and performance art"与前文呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说无论在舞台帐篷还是街头马戏仍令人惊叹、展示人体在专注与协作下可达成的成就、为观众与参与者带来惊叹欢乐与突破极限的提醒，空后需总结马戏的持久魅力。E的"timeless source of wonder"与"celebration of human ability"与前文对应。

  - **F. For example, trapeze artists often perform high above the audience...**：以For example开头，为具体表演形式案例（高空秋千）。各空均为概括或过渡句；[2]易被误选因涉及表演技能，但该空需总结天赋、投入与信任的整体要求，而非某一表演形式的具体描述。
