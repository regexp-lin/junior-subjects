**Urban Farming: Growing Food in the City**

More than half of the world's people now live in cities, and that number is growing. Cities have concrete, buildings, and traffic—but they also have rooftops, balconies, vacant lots, and community gardens. Urban farming uses these spaces to grow vegetables, herbs, and sometimes even fruit or raise small animals. [1] Urban farming can provide fresh, local food, reduce the distance food travels from farm to table, and bring greenery and nature into neighborhoods that lack parks and gardens.

One of the simplest forms of urban farming is growing plants in pots or small raised beds. Tomatoes, herbs, lettuce, and peppers can all thrive on a sunny balcony or patio. Even a windowsill can hold herbs or small vegetables. [2] Starting small is a good way to learn—and many people find that growing their own food is relaxing, rewarding, and a fun activity to share with family or neighbors.

Community gardens are another popular option. A group of neighbors or a school may share a plot of land where everyone can grow their own plants. These gardens create spaces for people to meet, learn from each other, and work together. [3] Community gardens often become hubs for learning, sharing seeds and tips, and building stronger, more connected neighborhoods—especially in areas where fresh, affordable produce is hard to find.

Urban farming can also address environmental issues. Rooftop gardens can cool buildings in summer, reduce stormwater runoff, and provide habitat for birds and insects. Growing food locally cuts down on the fuel used to transport food from distant farms. [4] When cities support urban farming—through policies, land use, and education—they can improve public health, reduce their environmental footprint, and make cities more livable for everyone.

Urban farming is not a replacement for rural agriculture, but it is a valuable addition. It connects city dwellers to where food comes from and encourages healthier eating. [5] As more people discover the joys and benefits of growing food in the city, urban farming is likely to grow—transforming empty lots and rooftops into productive, green spaces that feed both bodies and communities.

## 选项

A. Urban farming offers benefits beyond food—it shortens supply chains, brings fresh produce closer to where people live, and adds green space to crowded urban areas.
B. You do not need a large farm or a lot of experience to start—a few pots, some soil, and a little patience can lead to your first harvest and often to a lasting interest in gardening.
C. These shared spaces do more than grow food—they create opportunities for education, friendship, and community building, especially in areas where access to fresh food or green space is limited.
D. Supporting urban farming through planning, funding, and training can help cities become healthier, greener, and more resilient—and it gives residents more control over what they eat and where it comes from.
E. Urban farming is growing in popularity, and as interest increases, cities have a chance to turn underused land into productive gardens that nourish people, strengthen communities, and make urban life more sustainable.
F. For example, Tokyo has thousands of rooftop and balcony gardens where residents grow vegetables and herbs, and some buildings even have farm plots on their roofs that supply local restaurants.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前说城市人口增加、可利用屋顶阳台等空间，空后说"Urban farming can provide fresh, local food, reduce the distance food travels from farm to table, and bring greenery and nature into neighborhoods"。A的"Urban farming offers benefits beyond food—it shortens supply chains, brings fresh produce closer... adds green space to crowded urban areas"与空后三方面好处呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说盆栽、小型菜床等简单形式，空后说"Starting small is a good way to learn—and many people find that growing their own food is relaxing, rewarding, and a fun activity"。B的"You do not need a large farm or a lot of experience to start—a few pots, some soil... can lead to your first harvest"与空后starting small、learn、relaxing对应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说社区花园众人共享、交流学习，空后说"Community gardens often become hubs for learning, sharing seeds and tips, and building stronger, more connected neighborhoods—especially in areas where fresh, affordable produce is hard to find"。C的"These shared spaces do more than grow food—they create opportunities for education, friendship... especially where access to fresh food or green space is limited"与空后呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说屋顶绿化降温、减径流、缩短运输等环境效益，空后说"When cities support urban farming—through policies, land use, and education—they can improve public health, reduce their environmental footprint"。D的"Supporting urban farming through planning, funding, and training can help cities become healthier, greener... gives residents more control"与空后城市支持、健康、环境对应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说城市农业是有益补充、连接城市与食物来源，空后说"As more people discover the joys and benefits of growing food in the city, urban farming is likely to grow—transforming empty lots and rooftops"。E的"Urban farming is growing in popularity... cities have a chance to turn underused land into productive gardens"与空后更多人参与、空间转化呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
