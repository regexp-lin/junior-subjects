**Getting Started with Stargazing**

Looking up at the night sky has fascinated humans for thousands of years. You do not need expensive equipment to begin exploring the stars. [1] With a little knowledge and patience, anyone can discover the wonders above. Stargazing is a hobby that connects us to the universe.

The first step is to find a dark location. [2] City lights create "light pollution" that hides many stars. Drive to a park, a countryside road, or a beach away from buildings. The darker the sky, the more stars you will see. Your eyes need about 20 minutes to adjust to the darkness.

Learn to identify a few key objects. [3] The Big Dipper and Orion are easy to find and help you navigate the sky. Planets like Venus and Mars often appear brighter than stars. A simple star chart or a free phone app can guide you. Start with one or two constellations and expand from there.

The best time for stargazing depends on the season. [4] In summer, the Milky Way stretches across the sky. In winter, Orion dominates the southern sky. Check the weather and moon phase—a full moon makes faint stars hard to see. Plan your outing for clear, moonless nights.

Stargazing teaches patience and curiosity. [5] You will learn that the sky changes every night. Some objects are only visible at certain times of year. Keep a journal of what you see. This ancient hobby can become a lifelong passion.

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前说明观星不需昂贵设备；空后强调知识与耐心即可发现天上奇景。A 项“这是任何人经过基本准备都能享受的活动”承接“无需昂贵设备”并引出后文具体建议。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前提出第一步是找暗处；空后解释城市光污染及选择郊野、公园、海滩等。B 项“远离人造光对看清星星至关重要”概括选址原因，与后文“光污染”和“越暗看见越多”呼应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前提出学习识别关键天体；空后举例北斗七星、猎户座、金星和火星及星图与APP。C 项“新手可以从辨认几个著名图案和行星开始”概括入门方法，与后文举例衔接。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说观星最佳时间因季节而异；空后具体说明夏冬不同天象及天气、月相影响。D 项“不同季节有不同的天象亮点可观测”概括主旨，与后文总分关系明确。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前指出观星培养耐心与好奇心；空后说明天空每日变化、部分天体仅在特定季节可见及记录所见。E 项“观察越多，越能体会宇宙的韵律”总结本段主旨，与后文呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, telescopes can magnify distant galaxies...**：虽与观星相关，但本文强调“无需昂贵设备”的入门观星，望远镜属进阶工具，与各段逻辑层次不符。
