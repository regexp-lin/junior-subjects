# 07-30 第二篇

**The Right Attitude Toward Failure**

Nobody enjoys failing, but failure is a natural part of life. Every successful person has faced setbacks. [1] What matters is not whether we fail, but how we respond to it. Learning from failure can make us stronger and wiser.

When things go wrong, our first reaction is often disappointment. [2] It is normal to feel sad or frustrated. Allow yourself a moment to process these feelings. Suppressing emotions can make recovery harder. Acknowledging your feelings is the first step toward moving forward.

After the initial shock, look for lessons. [3] Ask yourself: What went wrong? What could I do differently? Honest reflection turns failure into feedback. Sometimes the problem was beyond your control—that is valuable to know too. Each failure teaches something if we are willing to learn.

Avoid comparing yourself to others. [4] Someone else's success does not mean your failure is permanent. Everyone has a different path and timetable. Focus on your own progress. Small improvements add up over time. Success often comes after many attempts.

Failure can be a stepping stone. [5] Many inventions and breakthroughs came after repeated failures. The key is to keep trying while adjusting your approach. Resilience—the ability to bounce back—is a skill worth developing. Your attitude toward failure shapes your future.

## 选项

A. The real test is our willingness to learn and grow from setbacks.
B. Giving yourself permission to feel bad actually helps you recover.
C. Careful analysis can turn mistakes into useful information.
D. Your journey is unique, and progress matters more than comparison.
E. History shows that persistence after failure often leads to success.
F. For example, Thomas Edison failed many times before inventing the light bulb.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前指出失败是生活的常态，成功者都经历过挫折；空后转入“如何应对失败”比“是否失败”更重要。A 项“真正的考验是我们是否愿意从挫折中学习与成长”承接转折，与后文“学习失败使我们更强更智”呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前描述失败后的常见反应是失望；空后说明允许自己感受情绪、不要压抑。B 项“允许自己感到难受实际上有助于恢复”概括该段主旨，与后文“承认情绪是前进的第一步”衔接。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前提出在初 shock 之后寻找教训；空后给出反思问题及诚实反思的重要性。C 项“仔细分析可以把错误转化为有用信息”概括“反思—反馈”的逻辑，与后文呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前提醒不要与他人比较；空后说明他人成功不意味自己失败是永久的、各人路径不同。D 项“你的旅程是独特的，进步比比较更重要”总结该段主旨，与后文自然衔接。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前提出失败可以是垫脚石；空后举例发明与突破多来自反复失败，强调坚持与调整。E 项“历史表明，失败后坚持往往通向成功”概括本段主旨，与后文举例和韧性论述呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, Thomas Edison failed many times...**：虽是典型失败后成功的例子，但仅为单一事实举例，未起到段首概括或承上启下的作用，且与其他选项相比缺乏抽象概括力，易被误选为 [5] 答案。
