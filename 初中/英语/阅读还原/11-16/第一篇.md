# 11-16 第一篇

**Friendship: The Bonds That Shape Our Lives**

Friendship is one of the most important relationships we can have. Friends laugh with us in good times and stand by us in difficult ones. They help us feel less alone, give honest advice when we need it, and make everyday life more fun. Research shows that people with strong friendships tend to be happier and healthier. [1]

Good friendships do not happen by accident. They require time, effort, and care. Listening when a friend needs to talk, remembering important dates, and being there when it matters—these small actions build trust. Friendships also need balance: both people should give and receive. If one person always takes and never gives, the relationship may not last. [2]

Friends can be different from us in many ways. They might have different hobbies, come from different backgrounds, or hold different opinions. That diversity can make friendships richer. We learn new perspectives, try new things, and grow as people. The key is respect: valuing each other even when we disagree. [3]

Friendship can face challenges. People move away, grow apart, or have arguments. Misunderstandings happen, and sometimes feelings get hurt. When conflicts arise, honest communication and a willingness to forgive can repair the bond. Not every friendship lasts forever, and that is okay. Some friendships are for a season; others last a lifetime. [4]

In the age of social media, friendship has new dimensions. We can stay connected with friends across the world, share moments instantly, and meet people with similar interests online. But screens cannot replace face-to-face connection. Spending real time together—talking, playing, or simply being present—still matters most. [5]

## 选项

A. True friendship enriches our lives in countless ways, offering support, joy, and a sense of belonging that few other relationships can provide.
B. Building and maintaining friendship takes intentional effort, but the rewards—trust, loyalty, and mutual support—are worth it.
C. Friendships that embrace differences often become the strongest, as they teach us to understand, accept, and appreciate people who are not like us.
D. Navigating the ups and downs of friendship is part of growing up, and learning to handle conflict and change helps us become better friends and better people.
E. No matter how we connect, the heart of friendship remains the same: genuine care, mutual respect, and the choice to be there for each other through thick and thin.
F. For instance, a famous study found that people with strong social connections live longer and have lower rates of heart disease and depression compared to those who feel lonely.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前说友谊的重要——欢笑陪伴、困难时支持、减少孤独、真诚建议、增添乐趣，研究显示好朋友多的人更幸福健康；空后需总结真正友谊带来的多方面丰富。A的"enriches our lives in countless ways"与前文多种益处呼应，"support, joy, and a sense of belonging"与 stand by、feel less alone、make life fun 对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说友谊需经营——时间努力关怀、倾听记住日期在场、平衡付出与收获、单方索取难以持久；空后需总结有意经营的意义与回报。B的"Building and maintaining friendship takes intentional effort"与前文"require time, effort, and care"呼应，"rewards—trust, loyalty, and mutual support—are worth it"与前文 build trust、balance 对应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说朋友可与我们不同——爱好背景观点各异、差异使友谊更丰富、我们学到新视角、关键是尊重；空后需总结接纳差异的友谊的价值。C的"Friendships that embrace differences often become the strongest"与前文 diversity、richer 呼应，"teach us to understand, accept, and appreciate"与"learn new perspectives"和 respect 对应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说友谊面临的挑战——搬迁疏远争吵、误解与伤害、需诚实沟通与原谅、并非所有友谊永恒；空后需总结应对起伏与冲突的成长意义。D的"Navigating the ups and downs of friendship is part of growing up"与前文 challenges、conflicts 呼应，"handle conflict and change"与"honest communication"和"willingness to forgive"对应，"better friends and better people"是升华。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说社交媒体时代的友谊——全球连接、即时分享、线上结识，但屏幕不能取代面对面、真实共处仍最重要；空后需总结无论方式如何友谊本质不变。E的"No matter how we connect"与前文 social media、face-to-face 呼应，"heart of friendship remains the same: genuine care, mutual respect, and the choice to be there"与全文 support、trust、being there 对应，是合理收尾。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For instance, a famous study found that people with strong social connections live longer and have lower rates of heart disease and depression compared to those who feel lonely.**：以 For instance 开头，为某项研究关于社交连接与健康的具体数据举例。各空均为概括或过渡句；[1] 易被误选因涉及研究，但该空需概括友谊的多方面丰富价值，而非引用某一研究的具体统计数据。
