**Solar Energy: Power from the Sun**

The sun provides more energy in one hour than the whole world uses in a year. Solar power turns that energy into electricity using special panels that capture sunlight. Unlike fossil fuels, solar energy does not produce air pollution or greenhouse gases. It is a clean, renewable resource that will last as long as the sun shines. [1]

Solar panels can be installed in many places. Rooftops of homes, schools, and office buildings can host panels. Large solar farms spread across open land, generating power for whole communities. In remote areas without access to the electrical grid, solar panels with batteries can provide light and power. Even small devices like calculators and street lights can run on solar energy. [2]

The cost of solar power has dropped greatly over the past decade. Panels are more efficient and affordable than ever. Many governments offer incentives, such as tax cuts or rebates, to encourage people and businesses to go solar. In some regions, solar power is now cheaper than electricity from coal or gas. [3]

Solar energy has some limitations. It works best when the sun is shining—cloudy days and night reduce or stop production. Energy storage, such as batteries, can help by storing extra power for later use. Advances in battery technology are making this easier. Some places also lack space or suitable conditions for solar installation. [4]

The shift to renewable energy is underway. Countries, cities, and companies are setting goals to use more solar and wind power. Schools are teaching students about clean energy. As more people choose solar, the demand for fossil fuels may decline, helping to slow climate change. [5]

## 选项

A. Solar energy offers a promising path toward a cleaner, more sustainable future, and its use is growing rapidly around the world.
B. From rooftops to vast solar farms, solar technology can be adapted to fit different needs and scales.
C. As solar technology improves and becomes more affordable, it is becoming an increasingly practical choice for families, businesses, and entire nations.
D. Despite these challenges, ongoing research and innovation are making solar energy more reliable and accessible every year.
E. Solar energy is no longer a technology of the future—it is here now, and it is playing a vital role in powering our world while protecting the planet for generations to come.
F. For example, China leads the world in solar panel production and installation, with enough solar capacity to power millions of homes across the country.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前说太阳能量巨大、光伏将光能转为电能、无污染无温室气体、清洁可再生；空后需总结太阳能的前景与全球发展。A的"promising path toward a cleaner, more sustainable future"与前文 clean、renewable 呼应，"use is growing rapidly around the world"与前文潜力对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说光伏安装场所多样——屋顶、大型电站、偏远地区配储能、小型设备；空后需总结太阳能技术的适用性与规模。B的"From rooftops to vast solar farms"与前文各类场所对应，"adapted to fit different needs and scales"与"many places"和"different"呼应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说成本大幅下降、效率提高、政府激励、部分地区已低于煤电；空后需总结技术改进与成本降低带来的实用性。C的"As solar technology improves and becomes more affordable"与前文 cost dropped、efficient、affordable、cheaper 呼应，"increasingly practical choice"与 incentives、families、businesses 对应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说太阳能局限——依赖日照、阴天夜间受限、储能可弥补、电池技术进步、部分地区缺空间或条件；空后需转折指出研究创新带来的改善。D的"Despite these challenges"与前文 limitations 呼应，"ongoing research and innovation are making solar energy more reliable and accessible"与 batteries、advances 对应，是典型让步后转折。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说向可再生能源转型——国家城市企业设目标、学校教学、更多人选择太阳能减少化石燃料、减缓气候变化；空后需总结太阳能的当下地位与作用。E的"Solar energy is no longer a technology of the future—it is here now"与前文 shift underway 呼应，"playing a vital role in powering our world while protecting the planet"与全文清洁、可持续主题对应，是合理收尾。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, China leads the world in solar panel production and installation, with enough solar capacity to power millions of homes across the country.**：以 For example 开头，为中国光伏产能与装机的具体数据举例。各空均为概括或过渡句；[1] 或 [2] 易被误选因涉及全球发展与规模，但该空需概括性陈述而非某一国家的具体数据举例。
