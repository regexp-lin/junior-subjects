**The Amazing Journey of Migratory Birds**

Every year, billions of birds travel thousands of kilometers across continents, following ancient routes between their breeding and wintering grounds. This incredible journey, known as migration, is one of nature's most awe-inspiring events. [1] Understanding bird migration helps us appreciate the remarkable abilities of these creatures and the importance of protecting the habitats they depend on.

Birds migrate for survival. As seasons change, food becomes scarce in colder regions, and birds fly to warmer areas where food is plentiful. [2] This seasonal movement ensures that birds can find the resources they need to feed, breed, and raise their young throughout the year.

The navigational skills of migratory birds are astonishing. They use the sun, the stars, Earth's magnetic field, and even landmarks to find their way across vast distances. [3] Some species return to the exact same nesting spot year after year, demonstrating a precision that scientists are still working to fully understand.

Migration is a dangerous journey. Birds face storms, exhaustion, predators, and the challenge of finding safe places to rest and refuel along the way. [4] Human activities such as building development, light pollution, and habitat destruction have made these journeys even more perilous for many species.

Conservation efforts are crucial for protecting migratory birds. International agreements, protected wetlands, and wildlife corridors help ensure that birds have safe stopover points during their travels. [5] By working across borders to protect migratory routes, we safeguard not only the birds but also the ecosystems that depend on their presence.

## 选项

A. Bird migration reveals the deep connection between animals and the rhythms of the natural world.
B. By following the seasons, birds have developed one of the most successful survival strategies in the animal kingdom.
C. This extraordinary sense of direction is one of the great mysteries of the natural world.
D. These added threats mean that many migratory species are now in decline and in urgent need of protection.
E. Global cooperation is essential because migratory birds do not recognize national boundaries.
F. For example, the Arctic Tern migrates from the Arctic to the Antarctic and back each year, covering about 70,000 kilometers.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述候鸟迁徙是自然界最令人敬畏的事件之一，空后说理解迁徙有助于欣赏鸟类能力和保护栖息地。A的"deep connection between animals and the rhythms of the natural world"概括迁徙的自然意义，与"awe-inspiring"呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说季节变化导致食物稀缺、鸟类飞往温暖地区，空后说这种季节性移动确保鸟类找到资源。B的"By following the seasons"承接季节变化，"most successful survival strategies"概括迁徙的进化意义。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说候鸟利用太阳、星星、地磁场和地标导航，空后说有些物种年年回到同一巢穴。C的"extraordinary sense of direction"承接导航能力，"great mysteries of the natural world"引出科学家仍在研究的主题。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说人类活动（建设、光污染、栖息地破坏）使迁徙更危险，空后无直接后续。D的"These added threats"承接人类活动威胁，"many migratory species are now in decline"概括严峻现状。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前列举保护努力（国际协议、保护湿地、野生动物走廊），空后说跨国合作保护迁徙路线。E的"Global cooperation is essential"承接国际保护，"migratory birds do not recognize national boundaries"解释跨国合作的必要性。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the Arctic Tern migrates from the Arctic to the Antarctic and back each year, covering about 70,000 kilometers.**：以For example开头，属具体物种案例；易误放[1]或[3]，但两空分别需迁徙自然意义的概括和导航能力的概括，非个案数据。
