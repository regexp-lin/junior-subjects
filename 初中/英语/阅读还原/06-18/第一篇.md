# 06-18 第一篇

**The Secrets of Body Language**

We communicate not only with words but also with our bodies. The way we stand, move our hands, make eye contact, or fold our arms can send strong messages—sometimes without us even realising it. [1] In other words, body language is a powerful form of communication that often speaks louder than words. Learning to read and use body language wisely can help us understand others better and express ourselves more clearly.

Some body language is understood across cultures. [2] A smile usually means friendliness and warmth, while a frown may show worry or displeasure. Nodding the head often signals agreement, and shaking it often means "no." Eye contact can show interest and honesty, though in some cultures too much eye contact is seen as impolite. Open postures—such as uncrossed arms and facing someone directly—tend to suggest openness and confidence. These signals help people connect even when they do not speak the same language.

However, body language can vary from place to place. [3] A thumbs-up may mean "good" in one country but something rude in another. In some cultures, touching the head is disrespectful, while in others it is a sign of affection. The same gesture can have different meanings depending on where you are. When we travel or meet people from different backgrounds, it is important to be aware of these differences so we do not send the wrong message by mistake. Respect for local customs shows that we care about others.

Body language affects how others see us. [4] In job interviews, a firm handshake, good posture, and steady eye contact can make a positive impression. In class, leaning forward and nodding can show the teacher that we are paying attention. Slouching or looking at our phone may suggest we are bored or disinterested. Being aware of our own body language helps us appear more confident and approachable. It also helps us notice when someone else might be nervous, upset, or in need of support.

[5] Body language is a silent but powerful tool in our daily communication. By paying attention to both our own signals and those of others, we can build stronger relationships and avoid misunderstandings. The next time you talk to someone, notice not just the words—but also the message that the body is sending.

## 选项

A. In other words, body language is a powerful way of communicating that often matters more than words.
B. For example, smiles, nods, eye contact, and open postures often send similar messages across cultures.
C. In fact, gestures and customs differ from culture to culture and we should be respectful of local ways.
D. These effects show that body language influences how others judge us and how we come across.
E. In short, body language is a powerful tool and we should pay attention to it in our communication.
F. For instance, a handshake is a common way to greet someone in many Western countries.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：概括身体语言作为交流方式的重要性。空前描述"We communicate not only with words but also with bodies...stand, move hands, eye contact, fold arms can send strong messages...sometimes without realising"，空后说明"body language is powerful form of communication that often speaks louder than words...Learning to read and use...understand others better, express ourselves more clearly"。A选项"In other words, body language is a powerful way of communicating that often matters more than words"（换言之，身体语言是一种强大的交流方式，往往比言语更重要）中"In other words"概括空前，"powerful...often matters more than words"与空后"powerful...speaks louder than words"精准呼应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：跨文化共通的身体语言信号。空前提出"Some body language is understood across cultures"，空后详述"Smile usually means friendliness, warmth...frown worry or displeasure...nodding agreement, shaking no...eye contact interest, honesty...open postures...uncrossed arms, facing directly...suggest openness, confidence...help people connect...same language"。B选项"For example, smiles, nods, eye contact, and open postures often send similar messages across cultures"（例如，微笑、点头、眼神接触和开放姿势在不同文化中往往传达相似的信息）中"For example"引出例证，"smiles, nods, eye contact, open postures...similar messages across cultures"与空后各项共通信号完美对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：身体语言的文化差异及尊重必要性。空前提出"However, body language can vary from place to place"，空后列举"Thumbs-up may mean good in one country, rude in another...touching head disrespectful in some, affection in others...same gesture different meanings...travel or meet different backgrounds...important to be aware...do not send wrong message...respect for local customs...care about others"。C选项"In fact, gestures and customs differ from culture to culture and we should be respectful of local ways"（事实上，手势与习俗因文化而异，我们应尊重当地习俗）中"In fact"强化差异，"gestures and customs differ...respectful of local ways"与空后 different meanings、aware、respect 精准对应，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：身体语言对他人的印象影响。空前提出"Body language affects how others see us"，空后描述"Job interviews...firm handshake, good posture, steady eye contact...positive impression...class...leaning forward, nodding...paying attention...slouching, looking at phone...bored, disinterested...aware of own body language...appear confident, approachable...notice when others nervous, upset, need support"。D选项"These effects show that body language influences how others judge us and how we come across"（这些影响表明身体语言影响他人对我们的评价以及我们给人的印象）中"These effects"指代空后各类情景，"influences how others judge us...how we come across"与空后 see us、impression、appear 完美对应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与善用身体语言的重要性。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"Body language silent but powerful tool in daily communication...paying attention to own signals and others...build stronger relationships, avoid misunderstandings...next time talk...notice not just words—message body is sending"。E选项"In short, body language is a powerful tool and we should pay attention to it in our communication"（简言之，身体语言是强有力的工具，我们应在交流中留意它）用"In short"总结全文，"powerful tool...pay attention"与空后"powerful tool...paying attention"形成呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For instance, a handshake is a common way to greet someone in many Western countries.**：涉及握手为常见问候方式，易被误填[2]或[4]。但F以"For instance"引出单一手势，而空[2]讨论的是跨文化共通信号（smile、nod、eye contact、open postures），F的"handshake...Western"属于地域习俗，与"understood across cultures"的共通性焦点不同；空[4]讨论身体语言对印象的影响，F的问候方式仅为举例，与"job interviews、class、impression"的多种情境不符。F属"主题相关但论述层次错位"型干扰。
