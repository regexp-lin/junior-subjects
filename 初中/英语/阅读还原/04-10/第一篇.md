# 04-10 第一篇

**The Science of Twins**

Have you ever wondered why some people look exactly the same and even share the same birthday? They might be twins. Twins are born when a mother carries two babies at the same time, and they come in two main types: identical twins and fraternal twins. [1] Identical twins form when one fertilized egg splits into two, so they share exactly the same genes. Fraternal twins, on the other hand, come from two separate eggs, so they are no more alike than any other siblings. Understanding this difference helps scientists learn about how genes and the environment shape who we are.

Scientists have long been fascinated by twin studies. [2] When researchers compare identical twins who grew up in different families, they can see how much of our personality and behavior comes from genetics and how much comes from our life experiences. If identical twins raised apart still share many traits, those traits may be strongly influenced by genes. If they differ more, the environment may play a bigger role. Such studies have taught us a great deal about everything from intelligence to health risks.

Twins often develop a special bond. [3] Many twins report feeling each other's emotions or knowing when the other is in trouble, even when they are far apart. Scientists are still unsure whether this is due to shared genes, similar upbringing, or something else. Some studies suggest that twins who spend a lot of time together may pick up on each other's habits and patterns, which could explain some of these experiences. Whether the connection is purely biological or partly learned, the twin bond remains a fascinating topic.

Identical twins can sometimes confuse people. [4] Teachers may mix them up at school, and even close friends can have trouble telling them apart at first. Some twins find this amusing and enjoy playing tricks, while others prefer to dress differently or style their hair in distinct ways to make it easier for people to recognize them. Each pair of twins finds their own way to handle being unique yet similar at the same time.

[5] From helping scientists understand genetics to sharing a lifelong bond, twins offer a unique window into human nature. Whether identical or fraternal, twins remind us that our genes and our experiences work together to make us who we are. The next time you meet a pair of twins, you might appreciate a little more how special their connection—and their contribution to science—really is.

## 选项

A. In fact, studies that follow twins over many years have helped unlock secrets about heredity and disease.
B. To put it simply, the main difference lies in how they are formed before birth.
C. For example, some twins say they can sense when their sibling is sad or hurt, even miles away.
D. This can lead to funny or sometimes frustrating situations in daily life.
E. All in all, twins hold a special place in both science and human relationships.
F. Researchers often compare twins to understand which traits are inherited and which are learned.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] B   [2] F   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] B**：
  - **线索**：概念引入与区分说明。空前介绍"two main types: identical twins and fraternal twins"，空后分别解释"Identical twins form when one fertilized egg splits...Fraternal twins...come from two separate eggs"。B选项"To put it simply, the main difference lies in how they are formed before birth"（简言之，主要区别在于它们出生前的形成方式）中"main difference"与空前"two main types"呼应，"how they are formed before birth"自然引出空后对 identical 与 fraternal 形成过程的详细解释，逻辑衔接紧密。

- **[2] F**：
  - **线索**：研究目的与具体方法。空前指出"Scientists have long been fascinated by twin studies"，空后"When researchers compare identical twins who grew up in different families...can see how much...comes from genetics...how much...from life experiences"。F选项"Researchers often compare twins to understand which traits are inherited and which are learned"（研究者常通过比较双胞胎来了解哪些特质是遗传的、哪些是习得的）中"compare twins"与空后"compare identical twins who grew up in different families"直接对应，"inherited and learned"与"genetics...life experiences"语义一致，完美衔接。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：双胞胎纽带与例证引入。空前指出"Twins often develop a special bond"，空后"Many twins report feeling each other's emotions or knowing when the other is in trouble"。C选项"For example, some twins say they can sense when their sibling is sad or hurt, even miles away"（例如，一些双胞胎说他们能感知到兄弟姐妹悲伤或受伤，即使相隔数英里）中"For example"引出例证，"sense when their sibling is sad or hurt"与空后"feeling each other's emotions...knowing when the other is in trouble"高度对应，"even miles away"与"even when they are far apart"呼应，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：混淆情境与后果说明。空前指出"Identical twins can sometimes confuse people"，空后具体描述"Teachers may mix them up...even close friends can have trouble telling them apart...Some twins find this amusing...others prefer to dress differently"。D选项"This can lead to funny or sometimes frustrating situations in daily life"（这在日常生活中可能导致有趣或令人沮丧的情况）中"This"指代"confuse people"，"funny or sometimes frustrating"与空后"amusing...play tricks...prefer to dress differently"的应对方式形成因果衔接，逻辑顺畅。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后"From helping scientists understand genetics to sharing a lifelong bond...remind us that our genes and our experiences work together...special their connection—and their contribution to science"。E选项"All in all, twins hold a special place in both science and human relationships"（总之，双胞胎在科学与人际关系中都占据特殊地位）用"All in all"总结，"science and human relationships"与空后"genetics...lifelong bond...contribution to science"全面呼应，收束得当。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **A. In fact, studies that follow twins over many years have helped unlock secrets about heredity and disease.**：提及双胞胎研究揭示遗传与疾病奥秘，与[2]前后文的"twin studies...genetics...environment"高度相关，易被误选。但空[2]空后"When researchers compare identical twins who grew up in different families"强调的是**比较方法**（compare...different families），而非"follow over many years"的时间跨度。F的"compare twins to understand which traits are inherited and which are learned"精准对应"compare...how much from genetics...how much from life experiences"；A的"follow over many years...heredity and disease"更偏长期追踪与疾病，与空后"personality and behavior...intelligence to health risks"的侧重略有不同，且缺乏"compare"这一核心动作。属"主题相关但功能侧重不同"型干扰。
