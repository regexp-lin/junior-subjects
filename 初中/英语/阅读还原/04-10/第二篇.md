# 04-10 第二篇

**The Art of Origami**

When you fold a piece of paper into a crane, a boat, or a flower, you are practicing origami—the ancient Japanese art of paper folding. The word "origami" comes from the Japanese words "ori" (to fold) and "kami" (paper). [1] Although paper was invented in China over 2,000 years ago, it was in Japan that folding paper into shapes became a valued art form. At first, origami was used mainly in religious ceremonies. Over time, it spread to everyday life, and people began to create animals, plants, and geometric designs for both decoration and play.

Origami is more than just a hobby. [2] Engineers and scientists have discovered that folding patterns can be used to design everything from solar panels in space to tiny medical tools. For example, a solar panel that folds like an accordion can fit inside a small rocket and then open up to a large size once in orbit. Surgeons use folded instruments that can enter the body through a tiny cut and then unfold to perform delicate operations. The math behind origami has inspired new ideas in technology and medicine.

Learning origami has many benefits for the mind. [3] When you follow a series of folds step by step, you train your brain to focus and plan ahead. Origami also improves hand-eye coordination and spatial reasoning—the ability to imagine how a flat sheet will look when folded into a 3D shape. Many teachers use origami in the classroom to help students understand geometry and problem-solving. It is a fun way to learn without feeling like you are doing homework.

Origami can be simple or highly complex. [4] A basic paper airplane might need only a few folds, while an elaborate dragon or insect can require hundreds of precise steps and take hours or even days to complete. Some artists create origami using a single square of paper with no cuts or glue—this is called "pure origami" and is considered the most challenging form. Whether you are a beginner or an expert, there is always something new to fold and discover.

[5] From ancient traditions to modern technology, origami continues to inspire people around the world. It combines art, math, and patience in a way that few other activities do. Whether you fold for fun, for learning, or for the beauty of the result, a single piece of paper can become almost anything in your hands. That is the magic of origami.

## 深度解析

- **[1] C**：
  - **线索**：历史渊源与地域引入。空前介绍"origami...Japanese...ori...kami"，空后"Although paper was invented in China over 2,000 years ago, it was in Japan that folding paper into shapes became a valued art form...At first, origami was used mainly in religious ceremonies"。C选项"In the beginning, this art had its roots in Japan, where paper folding became a cultural tradition"（起初，这门艺术根植于日本，折纸在那里成为一种文化传统）中"In the beginning"与"At first"呼应，"roots in Japan...cultural tradition"与空后"in Japan...became a valued art form...religious ceremonies...spread to everyday life"形成递进衔接，逻辑顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：功能拓展与科技应用。空前指出"Origami is more than just a hobby"，空后具体列举"Engineers and scientists have discovered...solar panels in space...tiny medical tools...solar panel that folds like an accordion...Surgeons use folded instruments"。B选项"Today, origami has found surprising applications far beyond art and decoration"（如今，折纸在艺术与装饰之外找到了令人惊讶的应用）中"far beyond art and decoration"与空前"more than just a hobby"呼应，"surprising applications"自然引出空后对太空、医疗等科技应用的详述，衔接紧密。

- **[3] F**：
  - **线索**：心智益处与例证引入。空前指出"Learning origami has many benefits for the mind"，空后"When you follow a series of folds step by step, you train your brain to focus and plan ahead...improves hand-eye coordination and spatial reasoning"。F选项"For instance, following diagrams step by step can boost concentration and logical thinking"（例如，按步骤跟随图示能提升专注力与逻辑思维）中"For instance"引出例证，"following...step by step"与空后"follow a series of folds step by step"直接对应，"concentration and logical thinking"与"focus and plan ahead"及"spatial reasoning"语义一致，完美衔接。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：难易对比与具体展开。空前指出"Origami can be simple or highly complex"，空后"A basic paper airplane might need only a few folds, while an elaborate dragon or insect can require hundreds of precise steps"。D选项"Some models take only a minute, while others demand skill, time, and careful planning"（有些模型只需一分钟，而另一些则需要技巧、时间和细心的规划）中"Some...only a minute"与"basic paper airplane...few folds"对应，"others demand skill, time, and careful planning"与"elaborate...hundreds of precise steps...hours or even days"呼应，衔接自然。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后"From ancient traditions to modern technology...combines art, math, and patience...single piece of paper can become almost anything...magic of origami"。E选项"To sum up, origami bridges the past and the present in a unique way"（总之，折纸以一种独特的方式连接过去与现在）用"To sum up"总结，"bridges the past and the present"与空后"ancient traditions to modern technology"完美呼应，"unique way"与"few other activities do...magic"形成升华，收束得当。
