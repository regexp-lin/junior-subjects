# 07-13 第二篇

**Cultivating Creativity in Young People**

Creativity is not just for artists or musicians—it is a skill that helps everyone solve problems and think in new ways. In today's fast-changing world, creative thinking helps people adapt and find solutions that others might miss. Schools and parents often focus on test scores, but developing creativity and giving children the freedom to explore are equally important for a child's future. [1]

[2] Research shows that creativity can be nurtured through certain practices. Giving children time for free play allows their minds to explore without pressure. When kids are not graded or judged, they feel safe to try new ideas.

[3] Exposure to different experiences also fuels creativity. Visiting museums, reading various books, and trying new hobbies open up the mind. Each new experience connects to what we already know, forming fresh combinations and ideas.

[4] Another key is to welcome mistakes as part of learning. Many great inventions came from accidents or "failed" experiments. When children are afraid to make mistakes, they stick to safe answers and stop exploring.

[5] Creativity is not a gift that only some people have. With the right environment and encouragement, anyone can develop their creative abilities. Parents and teachers play a vital role in providing that support.

## 选项

A. This is why we should value creativity as much as we value academic achievement.
B. This freedom often leads to surprising and original thinking.
C. Parents and teachers can encourage this by offering diverse activities.
D. Teaching children that failure is a step toward success helps them take creative risks.
E. When we support young people in these ways, we help them become the innovators of tomorrow.
F. For example, the microwave oven was invented when a scientist noticed chocolate melting in his pocket near a radar machine.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：第一段提出创造力对所有人重要，强调在变化世界中适应和解决问题，并指出学校家长重考试。空格前形成对比：考试 vs 创造力。选项A "This is why we should value creativity as much as we value academic achievement" 承接"equally important"，点明应同等重视，承上启下引出后文如何培养。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：第二段讲通过自由玩耍培养创造力，空格前描述"不给压力、不评判"的环境。选项B "This freedom often leads to surprising and original thinking" 中"This freedom"指代前文free play与无压力环境，"leads to"揭示因果关系，自然衔接研究结论。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：第三段讲接触不同经历激发创造力，列举博物馆、读书、新爱好。空格后"Each new experience connects..."是具体机制。选项C "Parents and teachers can encourage this by offering diverse activities" 中"this"指代exposure to different experiences，"diverse activities"与前后列举对应，指明谁来做、如何做。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：第四段讲接纳错误，举例大发明来自意外。空格前讲孩子怕错就不探索，空格需给出对策。选项D "Teaching children that failure is a step toward success helps them take creative risks" 正面回应：把失败重构为进步，从而敢于冒险，逻辑连贯。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：末段总结创造力可培养、环境与鼓励重要、家长教师有作用。选项E "When we support young people in these ways, we help them become the innovators of tomorrow" 概括前文多种支持方式，"these ways"回指全文策略，收束有力。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the microwave oven was invented when a scientist noticed chocolate melting in his pocket near a radar machine.**：微波炉发明确是"意外带来发明"的实例，与第四段主题相关。但第四段空格前已用"Many great inventions came from accidents"概括，若再插具体事例会冗余；且空格位置需承接"afraid to make mistakes"提出对策，而非举例论证。该选项层次错位，易误选。
