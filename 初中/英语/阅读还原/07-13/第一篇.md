# 07-13 第一篇

**The Importance of Bees in Our Ecosystem**

Bees are often seen as simple insects that make honey, but they play a far more critical role in our world than most people realize. As pollinators, bees help plants reproduce by carrying pollen from one flower to another. In fact, about one-third of the food we eat depends on pollination. Without bees, many fruits and vegetables would disappear from our tables. [1]

[2] Scientists have noticed a worrying trend: bee populations are declining worldwide. Climate change, pesticides, and habitat loss are the main threats to these tiny workers. Warmer temperatures disrupt the timing of flowers blooming and bees waking from hibernation. When flowers bloom too early or too late, bees may find nothing to eat. Pesticides used on crops can also harm bees, weakening their immune systems and navigation skills.

[3] Farmers and gardeners can take action to protect bees. Planting a variety of native flowers provides bees with food throughout the growing season. Avoiding chemicals in the garden helps keep bees safe.

[4] Governments and communities worldwide are starting to act. Some cities have created "bee corridors" with wildflowers along roads and in parks. Schools teach children about the importance of pollinators.

[5] Bees may be small, but their impact is enormous. Every apple, almond, and cucumber we enjoy exists partly because of their hard work. Protecting bees means protecting our food supply and the health of our planet.

## 选项

A. This is why we must understand and protect these amazing creatures.
B. This has raised serious concerns among researchers.
C. Creating "bee hotels" or leaving some areas wild gives bees places to nest.
D. Businesses are also joining the effort, with some companies setting up hives on rooftops.
E. We all have a part to play in ensuring bees continue to buzz for generations to come.
F. For example, honeybees can communicate the location of flowers through a special "waggle dance."

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：第一段先介绍蜜蜂作为传粉者的重要性，指出三分之一食物依赖传粉，末句"Without bees, many fruits and vegetables would disappear"强调后果。选项A "This is why we must understand and protect these amazing creatures" 承接上文因果关系，自然引出保护主题，完美承上启下。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：第二段开头需要承接"蜜蜂数量下降"这一新话题。空格后详细列举气候变化、农药、栖息地丧失等威胁。选项B "This has raised serious concerns among researchers" 中的"This"指代上段提到的蜜蜂重要性/食物依赖，"raised serious concerns"自然引出本段对威胁的讨论，符合段落开头引入功能。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：第三段讲农场主和园丁可以采取的行动：种花、避免化学品。空格前已列举两项措施，空格后应为第三项。选项C "Creating 'bee hotels' or leaving some areas wild gives bees places to nest" 与前后形成并列结构，都是具体可操作的保护措施。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：第四段讲政府社区行动：城市建花廊、学校教学。空格前为政府学校层面，空格后需拓展。选项D "Businesses are also joining the effort, with some companies setting up hives on rooftops" 与前文形成递进，"also"表明扩展至企业层面，层次清晰。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：末段总结蜜蜂影响大，保护蜜蜂即保护食物和地球。选项E "We all have a part to play in ensuring bees continue to buzz for generations to come" 是典型的总结号召句，呼应"保护"主题，收束全文。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, honeybees can communicate the location of flowers through a special "waggle dance."**：内容与蜜蜂相关，且"waggle dance"是真实生物学知识，但属于具体事例细节。第一段在讨论宏观重要性和保护必要性，插在此处会打断论证层次；且"For example"通常支撑前文观点，前文并未提出需要例证的观点，故为层次错位型干扰。
