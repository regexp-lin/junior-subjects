# 04-22 第二篇

**Sleep and Memory: Why Your Brain Needs Rest**

Have you ever stayed up late to study for an exam and found that you could not remember much the next day? Scientists have discovered that sleep plays an important role in how we learn and remember. [1] When we sleep, our brains do not simply "turn off"—they work hard to sort through the day's experiences and save what matters. During deep sleep, the brain transfers information from short-term memory to long-term memory. That is why getting enough sleep before a test is often more helpful than studying all night.

How much sleep do we need? Most teenagers need about 8 to 10 hours each night. [2] Younger children may need even more, while adults can usually manage with 7 to 8 hours. However, many students get less than 7 hours because of homework, phones, or other activities. Lack of sleep can lead to poor concentration, mood swings, and lower grades. Building a regular sleep schedule and avoiding screens before bed can help improve both sleep quality and memory.

Sleep is also important for creativity and problem-solving. [3] Have you ever gone to bed with a difficult question and woken up with the answer? That is because during sleep, the brain makes new connections between ideas. It can "replay" what we learned and find patterns we missed when we were awake. Some researchers call this process "sleep on it"—and it really works. A good night's sleep can help you see solutions that seemed impossible before.

What happens if we do not get enough sleep? [4] Over time, poor sleep can weaken the immune system and increase the risk of illness. It can also affect growth, especially in teenagers, because growth hormones are released mainly during deep sleep. In addition, studies show that long-term lack of sleep may be linked to problems with attention and learning. The message is clear: sleep is not a waste of time—it is essential for a healthy body and mind.

[5] From improving memory to boosting creativity, sleep is one of the most powerful tools we have for learning and health. Making sleep a priority can help you do better in school, feel happier, and stay healthier. So tonight, put down your phone a bit earlier and give your brain the rest it needs.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] C   [2] B   [3] D   [4] E   [5] A

## 深度解析

- **[1] C**：
  - **线索**：解释睡眠时大脑的活动。空前说明"sleep plays an important role in how we learn and remember"，空后描述"our brains do not simply 'turn off'—they work hard to sort through...transfer information from short-term to long-term memory"。C选项"In other words, your brain uses sleep time to organize and strengthen what you learned"（换言之，大脑利用睡眠时间整理并巩固所学内容）中"organize and strengthen"与空后"sort through...save what matters...transfers...to long-term"精准对应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：不同年龄段所需睡眠量。空前提问"How much sleep do we need? Most teenagers need about 8 to 10 hours"，空后详述"Younger children may need even more, while adults can usually manage with 7 to 8 hours...many students get less than 7 hours"。B选项"The amount varies by age, but one thing is clear: most people need more sleep than they get"（所需量因年龄而异，但有一点很清楚：大多数人需要的睡眠比实际得到的多）中"varies by age"与空后不同年龄段对应，"need more than they get"与空后"many students get less"呼应，衔接自然。

- **[3] D**：
  - **线索**：睡眠对创造力与问题解决的帮助。空前说明"Sleep is also important for creativity and problem-solving"，空后举例"Have you ever gone to bed with a difficult question and woken up with the answer? That is because during sleep, the brain makes new connections..."。D选项"This is not a coincidence—sleep helps the brain process and connect ideas in new ways"（这并非巧合——睡眠帮助大脑以新方式加工和联系想法）中"not a coincidence"承接空后"gone to bed with a difficult question and woken up with the answer"，"process and connect ideas in new ways"与空后"makes new connections...find patterns"完美呼应，逻辑紧密。

- **[4] E**：
  - **线索**：睡眠不足的严重后果。空前提问"What happens if we do not get enough sleep?"，空后列举"poor sleep can weaken the immune system...affect growth...problems with attention and learning...essential for a healthy body and mind"。E选项"Research shows that sleep deprivation can lead to serious health problems over time"（研究表明，睡眠不足长期来看会导致严重的健康问题）直接回答问句，"serious health problems"与空后"weaken immune system...affect growth...problems with attention"精准对应，衔接得当。

- **[5] A**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后列举"From improving memory to boosting creativity...do better in school, feel happier, stay healthier...give your brain the rest it needs"。A选项"In short, good sleep is the key to a sharp mind and a strong body"（简言之，优质睡眠是敏锐头脑和强健身体的关键）用"In short"总结全文，"sharp mind...strong body"与空后"learning and health...do better...happier...healthier"形成呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F.
