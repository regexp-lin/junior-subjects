# 04-22 第一篇

**QR Codes: How a Small Square Can Do Big Things**

You may have seen those black and white square patterns on product packages, restaurant tables, or bus stop signs. They are called QR codes（二维码）. [1] With just a quick scan from your phone's camera, you can open a website, pay for a meal, or get directions without typing anything. QR codes make life easier by turning a simple picture into a bridge to information.

QR codes were first used in Japan in the 1990s to track car parts in factories. Today, they are everywhere. [2] Restaurants put them on tables so customers can view the menu and order food without touching paper. Museums use them next to exhibits so visitors can learn more by scanning. Schools even use QR codes on homework sheets to link students to videos or extra practice. The uses keep growing as people find new and creative ways to use them.

Why are QR codes so popular? [3] Unlike long web addresses that are hard to remember or type, a QR code holds a lot of information in one small image. You do not need to type anything—you just point your phone at the square and the link or information appears. This saves time and reduces mistakes. That is why many businesses and public places choose QR codes to share information quickly.

However, QR codes can also be used by scammers（诈骗者）to steal information or money. [4] When you scan a code, you may be taken to a fake website that looks real. To stay safe, only scan QR codes from places you trust, such as official stores or known brands. Avoid scanning codes on strange stickers or papers you find on the street. A little caution can protect you from harm.

[5] From ordering food to learning at museums, QR codes have become a part of our daily life. They show how a simple technology can solve real problems and connect people to useful information. As phones and cameras get smarter, we will likely see even more creative uses for QR codes in the future.

## 选项

A. In short, QR codes are a small tool that has changed the way we get and use information.
B. They work like a shortcut—instead of typing a long website address, you simply scan the code.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] B   [2] E   [3] F   [4] D   [5] A

## 深度解析

- **[1] B**：
  - **线索**：引出二维码的功能与便利性。空前说明"those black and white square patterns...called QR codes"，空后描述"With just a quick scan...you can open a website, pay for a meal, or get directions without typing anything"。B选项"They work like a shortcut—instead of typing a long website address, you simply scan the code"（它们像快捷方式一样工作——你只需扫描就能取代输入长网址）中"They"指代QR codes，"shortcut"与空后"without typing anything"呼应，自然承接"turning a simple picture into a bridge to information"，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] E**：
  - **线索**：从历史过渡到如今的应用场景。空前描述"first used in Japan in the 1990s...Today, they are everywhere"，空后举例"Restaurants put them on tables...Museums use them...Schools even use QR codes"。E选项"Many places have found useful ways to use them in everyday situations"（许多地方在日常生活中找到了使用它们的有用方式）中"Many places"与空后餐厅、博物馆、学校等多场景对应，"everyday situations"自然引出具体举例，衔接自然。

- **[3] F**：
  - **线索**：解释二维码受欢迎的原因。空前提问"Why are QR codes so popular?"，空后详述"Unlike long web addresses...holds a lot of information...do not need to type anything...saves time and reduces mistakes"。F选项"The reason is simple: they are fast, easy, and can hold a lot of data"（原因很简单：它们快速、简便，能存储大量数据）直接回答问句，"fast, easy, hold a lot of data"与空后"hard to remember or type...holds a lot of information...saves time"一一对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：安全风险与应对建议。空前描述"can also be used by scammers...steal information or money...scan a code, you may be taken to a fake website"，空后给出建议"only scan from places you trust...Avoid scanning codes on strange stickers"。D选项"That is why it is important to be careful and check before you scan"（因此扫描前要小心核实很重要）中"That is why"承接空前风险，"be careful and check before you scan"与空后具体防范措施呼应，衔接精准。

- **[5] A**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后列举"From ordering food to learning at museums"并总结"simple technology can solve real problems...see even more creative uses"。A选项"In short, QR codes are a small tool that has changed the way we get and use information"（简言之，二维码是一种改变了我们获取和使用信息方式的工具）用"In short"总结全文，"small tool...changed the way we get and use information"与空后应用场景及未来展望形成呼应，收束得当。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **C. For example, some schools use QR codes to track student attendance and homework.**：举例学校使用二维码，易被误填[2]。但空[2]空前已提到"Today, they are everywhere"，空后列举了餐厅、博物馆、学校三处应用；C仅为学校一例，与空后"Restaurants...Museums...Schools"并列结构不符。E用"Many places...everyday situations"概括引出，更符合过渡需求。C属"内容相关但层级与功能错位"型干扰。
