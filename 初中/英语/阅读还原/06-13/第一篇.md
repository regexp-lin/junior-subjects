# 06-13 第一篇

**School Drama Performances**

Putting on a play at school is more than just fun—it is a powerful way to learn and grow. Drama clubs and school plays bring together students with different talents: acting, writing, building sets, or managing lights and sound. [1] In other words, school drama creates a team project where everyone can contribute in their own way. From the first rehearsal（彩排）to the final curtain, students learn to cooperate, solve problems, and share the joy of creating something together.

Drama builds confidence and expression. [2] Standing on stage in front of an audience requires courage. With practice, students learn to use their voice, body, and face to show emotions and tell a story. Shy students often discover that they can speak clearly and hold attention when playing a character. Drama also helps with language skills—memorising lines improves vocabulary and pronunciation, while understanding a script（剧本）develops reading comprehension. Many students find that drama boosts their confidence not only on stage but in everyday life.

Drama teaches empathy（同理心）and perspective. [3] When you play a character, you must step into their shoes and see the world from their point of view. Understanding why a character acts a certain way helps you understand people in real life. Drama activities often explore themes like friendship, conflict, and belonging—topics that matter to teenagers. Through acting, students learn that everyone has a story and that listening to others is as important as speaking.

Putting on a play takes planning and teamwork. [4] A successful performance needs directors, actors, costume designers, stage crew, and many others working together. There are deadlines to meet, problems to solve, and last-minute changes to handle. Students learn to manage their time, respect different roles, and support each other when things go wrong. Some may discover a lifelong interest in theatre; others simply gain skills that apply to any future job.

[5] School drama offers far more than entertainment. It builds confidence, develops empathy, and teaches skills that last well beyond the stage. If your school has a drama club or a yearly play, consider joining. You might surprise yourself with what you can achieve when you step into the spotlight.

## 选项

A. In other words, school drama is a collaborative project where each student can contribute their skills.
B. That is because performing on stage develops voice, body language, and courage in front of others.
C. In fact, acting helps students understand different perspectives and develop empathy for others.
D. These demands show that drama requires planning, teamwork, and skills useful beyond the theatre.
E. In short, school drama delivers lasting benefits beyond mere entertainment.
F. For example, William Shakespeare wrote many famous plays that are still performed today.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：引出校园戏剧作为团队协作项目的特点。空前描述"Drama clubs and school plays bring together students with different talents: acting, writing, building sets, managing lights and sound"，空后说明"school drama creates a team project where everyone can contribute in their own way...From first rehearsal to final curtain...learn to cooperate, solve problems, share joy of creating together"。A选项"In other words, school drama is a collaborative project where each student can contribute their skills"（换言之，校园戏剧是每个学生都能贡献技能的合作项目）中"In other words"概括空前，"collaborative project...contribute their skills"与空后"team project...everyone can contribute"精准呼应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：舞台表演如何培养自信与表达能力。空前提出"Drama builds confidence and expression"，空后详述"Standing on stage in front of audience requires courage...use voice, body, face to show emotions and tell story...Shy students discover can speak clearly and hold attention...Drama helps language skills—memorising lines improves vocabulary and pronunciation...boosts confidence on stage and in everyday life"。B选项"That is because performing on stage develops voice, body language, and courage in front of others"（那是因为在舞台上表演能培养面对他人时的声音、肢体语言和勇气）中"That is because"解释自信与表达的来源，"voice, body language, courage"与空后 use voice/body/face、shy students speak clearly、boosts confidence 完美对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：戏剧如何帮助理解他人视角与培养同理心。空前提出"Drama teaches empathy and perspective"，空后列举"When you play a character, step into their shoes, see world from their point of view...Understanding why character acts certain way helps understand people in real life...explore themes like friendship, conflict, belonging...everyone has a story...listening as important as speaking"。C选项"In fact, acting helps students understand different perspectives and develop empathy for others"（事实上，表演帮助学生理解不同视角并培养对他人的同理心）中"In fact"强化戏剧的认知价值，"understand different perspectives...develop empathy"与空后 step into shoes、point of view、understand people 精准对应，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：戏剧制作需要策划、团队协作及可迁移技能。空前提出"Putting on a play takes planning and teamwork"，空后描述"A successful performance needs directors, actors, costume designers, stage crew...deadlines to meet, problems to solve, last-minute changes...learn to manage time, respect different roles, support each other...discover lifelong interest in theatre...gain skills that apply to any future job"。D选项"These demands show that drama requires planning, teamwork, and skills useful beyond the theatre"（这些要求表明戏剧需要策划、团队协作以及超越剧场的实用技能）中"These demands"指代空后角色分工、截止日期、应变等，"planning, teamwork, skills useful beyond theatre"与空后"apply to any future job"完美对应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与戏剧的持久价值。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"School drama offers far more than entertainment...builds confidence, develops empathy, teaches skills that last well beyond stage...consider joining...surprise yourself with what you can achieve"。E选项"In short, school drama delivers lasting benefits beyond mere entertainment"（简言之，校园戏剧带来的持久益处远超娱乐本身）用"In short"总结全文，"lasting benefits beyond mere entertainment"与空后"far more than entertainment...last well beyond stage"形成呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, William Shakespeare wrote many famous plays that are still performed today.**：涉及莎士比亚名剧，易被误填[1]或[3]。但F以"For example"引出经典剧作家，而空[1]讨论的是校园戏剧作为学生协作项目，F的"Shakespeare"属于戏剧史，与"students with different talents...team project"的校园场景焦点不同；空[3]讨论同理心与视角，F的"famous plays"侧重作品知名度，与"step into character's shoes...empathy"的体验式学习不符。F属"主题相关但论述层次错位"型干扰。
