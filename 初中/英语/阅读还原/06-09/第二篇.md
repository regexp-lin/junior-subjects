**Protecting Coral Reefs**

Coral reefs are often called the "rainforests of the sea" because they support an amazing variety of life. Thousands of species of fish, crabs, and other creatures depend on reefs for food and shelter. [1] In other words, coral reefs are vital ecosystems that benefit both marine life and humans. Yet these colourful underwater worlds are under threat from climate change, pollution, and overfishing. Protecting them has become a global priority.

Coral is actually a living animal. [2] Each coral polyp（珊瑚虫） is a tiny creature that builds a hard skeleton around itself. Millions of polyps live together in colonies, forming the reef structure we see. They get much of their colour from algae（藻类） that live inside their tissues. When ocean water gets too warm, the corals become stressed and expel the algae—a process called "bleaching". Without the algae, the coral turns white and may eventually die. Rising sea temperatures are one of the biggest threats to reefs today.

What can we do to help? [3] Reducing carbon emissions slows global warming and gives reefs a better chance to survive. On a local level, avoiding chemical fertilizers and sunscreen that harm corals, and not touching or stepping on reefs when swimming, can make a difference. Some communities have set up marine protected areas where fishing and development are limited. Scientists are also experimenting with growing coral in labs and planting it back onto damaged reefs—a technique known as "coral restoration".

Reefs provide important services to people. [4] They protect coastlines from storms and erosion by softening the force of waves. They support fishing industries that feed millions. Tourism centred on reefs brings income to many coastal communities. When reefs are damaged, these benefits shrink. Investing in reef protection is not just about saving fish—it is about protecting the livelihoods and safety of people who depend on the sea.

[5] Coral reefs are treasures worth saving. From government policies to individual choices, everyone can play a part. Whether you live by the ocean or far inland, the actions we take to reduce pollution and slow climate change will help reefs—and the countless species that call them home—survive for generations to come.

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：引出珊瑚礁对海洋生物与人类社会的必要性。空前描述"rainforests of the sea...support amazing variety of life...Thousands of species depend on reefs for food and shelter"，空后说明"coral reefs are vital ecosystems that benefit both marine life and humans...under threat...Protecting them global priority"。A选项"In other words, coral reefs are essential to both ocean life and human societies"（换言之，珊瑚礁对海洋生物和人类社会都至关重要）中"In other words"概括空前价值，"essential to both ocean life and human societies"与空后"vital ecosystems...benefit both"精准呼应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：珊瑚结构依赖珊瑚虫与藻类的关系。空前提出"Coral is actually a living animal"，空后详述"Each coral polyp tiny creature...builds hard skeleton...Millions of polyps in colonies...form reef structure...get colour from algae inside tissues...water too warm...expel algae—bleaching...turns white, may die...Rising temperatures biggest threat"。B选项"That is because coral structure depends on tiny animals and their relationship with algae"（那是因为珊瑚结构依赖微小的动物及其与藻类的关系）中"That is because"解释珊瑚本质，"tiny animals...relationship with algae"与空后 polyp、algae、bleaching 完美对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：从全球到地方的多层次保护措施。空前提出"What can we do to help?"，空后列举"Reducing carbon emissions...local level: avoid fertilizers, sunscreen, not touching reefs...marine protected areas...Scientists growing coral in labs, planting back...coral restoration"。C选项"In fact, there are many steps we can take from global to local levels to protect reefs"（事实上，我们可以从全球到地方多个层面采取措施保护珊瑚礁）中"In fact"强化可行措施，"from global to local levels"与空后减排、地方避免污染、保护区、实验室恢复等精准对应，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：珊瑚礁带来的经济与安全效益。空前提出"Reefs provide important services to people"，空后描述"protect coastlines from storms and erosion...softening waves...support fishing industries...feed millions...Tourism brings income...When damaged, benefits shrink...protecting livelihoods and safety of people who depend on sea"。D选项"These benefits explain why reef protection matters for economies and safety"（这些效益解释了为何珊瑚礁保护对经济和安全重要）中"These benefits"指代空后防风暴、渔业、旅游、生计等，"economies and safety"与空后内容完美对应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与人人参与呼吁。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"treasures worth saving...From government policies to individual choices, everyone can play a part...Whether by ocean or inland...reduce pollution, slow climate change...help reefs and species survive for generations"。E选项"In short, saving coral reefs requires effort from all of us"（简言之，拯救珊瑚礁需要我们每个人的努力）用"In short"总结全文，"effort from all of us"与空后"everyone can play a part...government to individual"形成呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the Great Barrier Reef in Australia is the world's largest coral system.**：涉及大堡礁为最大珊瑚系统，易被误填[1]或[2]。但F以"For example"引出大堡礁这一实例，而空[1]讨论的是珊瑚礁的生态与人类价值及受威胁现状，F的"largest"提供规模信息，但未涉及"vital ecosystems"或"under threat"的论述焦点；空[2]讨论的是珊瑚虫、藻类、白化等生物机制，F的地理事实与生物学解释无直接逻辑联系。F属"主题相关但论述维度错位"型干扰。
