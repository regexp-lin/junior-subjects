# 06-09 第一篇

**The Work of Forest Firefighters**

When wildfires spread through forests, a special group of people goes into action. Forest firefighters, also called wildland firefighters, work in some of the most dangerous conditions on Earth. [1] In other words, they risk their lives to protect communities, wildlife, and natural resources. Their job requires courage, physical strength, and a deep understanding of how fire behaves in different terrains and weather.

Firefighting starts with prevention. [2] Before the fire season begins, crews clear dry grass, dead branches, and underbrush（林下灌木） that could fuel a fire. They create firebreaks—strips of land where vegetation has been removed—to slow or stop a fire from spreading. Controlled burns are sometimes used to safely remove flammable material in advance. These measures cannot prevent all fires, but they reduce the chance that a small spark will grow into a disaster.

When a fire breaks out, speed matters. [3] Firefighters use planes and helicopters to drop water or fire retardant（阻燃剂） from the air. On the ground, teams dig lines, set up pumps, and direct water toward the flames. They must read the wind, the slope of the land, and the type of fuel—trees, grass, or brush—to predict where the fire will move next. Experience and training help them make quick decisions that can save lives and property.

The conditions are extremely tough. [4] Firefighters often work long shifts in intense heat and smoke. They carry heavy equipment and sleep in camps with minimal comforts. Dehydration, exhaustion, and injuries are common risks. Despite these challenges, many firefighters say they are driven by a sense of purpose—protecting nature and helping people in need. It is a career that demands both physical and mental resilience.

[5] Forest firefighters are unsung heroes who protect our forests and the communities that depend on them. As climate change leads to longer and more intense fire seasons, their work becomes even more critical. The next time you hear about a wildfire being contained, remember the men and women who stand between the flames and everything we hold dear.

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：引出森林消防员在保护人与自然中的关键角色。空前描述"wildfires spread...special group goes into action...work in most dangerous conditions"，空后说明"risk their lives to protect communities, wildlife, natural resources...requires courage, physical strength, understanding of fire"。A选项"In other words, forest firefighters play a vital role in protecting people and nature"（换言之，森林消防员在保护人类和自然方面扮演着关键角色）中"In other words"概括空前职责，"vital role in protecting people and nature"与空后"protect communities, wildlife, natural resources"精准呼应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：清除可燃物降低大火风险的原因。空前提出"Firefighting starts with prevention"，空后列举"clear dry grass, dead branches, underbrush...create firebreaks...vegetation removed...slow or stop fire...Controlled burns...remove flammable material...reduce chance small spark grows into disaster"。B选项"That is because removing potential fuel reduces the risk of large-scale wildfires"（那是因为清除潜在可燃物可降低大规模野火的风险）中"That is because"解释预防措施原理，"removing potential fuel...reduces risk"与空后清除、防火带、受控燃烧等完美对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：扑火需要空地配合与专业判断。空前提出"When a fire breaks out, speed matters"，空后详述"planes and helicopters...drop water or retardant from air...On ground, dig lines, set up pumps, direct water...read wind, slope, type of fuel...predict where fire will move...Experience and training...quick decisions...save lives and property"。C选项"In fact, fighting fires requires both aerial and ground efforts and skilled judgment"（事实上，扑火需要空中与地面配合以及专业判断）中"In fact"强化扑火复杂性，"aerial and ground efforts...skilled judgment"与空后飞机、地面作业、预判火势等精准对应，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：艰苦条件对身心素质的要求。空前提出"The conditions are extremely tough"，空后描述"long shifts in intense heat and smoke...carry heavy equipment...sleep in camps with minimal comforts...Dehydration, exhaustion, injuries common...driven by sense of purpose...demands both physical and mental resilience"。D选项"These harsh conditions require firefighters to be both strong and mentally tough"（这些艰苦条件要求消防员既要有体力又要心理坚韧）中"These harsh conditions"指代空后高温、浓烟、重负、脱水等，"strong and mentally tough"与空后 physical and mental resilience 完美对应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与感恩表达。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"unsung heroes...protect forests and communities...climate change...longer, more intense fire seasons...work even more critical...remember the men and women who stand between flames and everything we hold dear"。E选项"In short, we owe a debt of gratitude to those who battle wildfires"（简言之，我们应对与野火作战的人们心怀感恩）用"In short"总结全文，"debt of gratitude"与空后"unsung heroes...remember"形成呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, some forest fires are caused naturally by lightning strikes.**：涉及闪电引发森林火灾，易被误填[2]或[3]。但F以"For example"引出火灾成因，而空[2]讨论的是预防措施（清除可燃物、防火带、受控燃烧），与火灾成因无直接逻辑联系；空[3]讨论的是扑火时的空地配合与判断，F的"lightning causes fire"属于起火原因，非扑救方法。F属"主题相关但论述阶段错位"型干扰。
