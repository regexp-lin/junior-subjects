# 08-12 第一篇

**The Miracle of Bird Migration**

Every year, millions of birds fly thousands of miles across continents and oceans. They follow ancient routes that have been used for thousands of years. Scientists have long wondered how these small creatures can make such incredible journeys. [1] The answer lies in a combination of instinct, physical adaptation, and remarkable endurance that has fascinated humans for centuries.

Birds prepare for migration by eating more food and storing fat. This fat provides energy for the long trip ahead. Some species double their body weight before leaving. [2] They use the sun, stars, and Earth's magnetic field to find their way. Young birds often follow older, experienced birds on their first migration. This helps them learn the correct route to their winter or summer home.

Different birds migrate at different times of the year. Many songbirds travel at night to avoid predators and to use the stars for navigation. Large birds like geese and cranes often fly during the day in V-shaped formations. [3] Flying in a V saves energy because each bird uses the air currents created by the bird in front. The lead bird tires quickly, so they take turns being at the front of the formation.

Migration is full of dangers. Birds face storms, hunger, and predators along the way. Many do not survive the journey. [4] They must find safe places to rest and feed. Wetlands, forests, and coastlines serve as important rest stops. Protecting these areas is crucial for the survival of migrating birds. Human activities have destroyed many of these critical habitats.

Bird migration connects different parts of our world. [5] When we protect migration routes and rest stops, we help ensure that future generations can witness this amazing natural phenomenon. Everyone can contribute by keeping cats indoors, making windows visible to birds, and supporting conservation organizations that work to preserve bird habitats.

## 选项

A. However, these amazing feats are made possible by several key factors.
B. This teamwork allows them to travel farther with less effort.
C. Their bodies are specially built for long-distance flight.
D. Along the way, birds need places to stop and regain their strength.
E. It reminds us that we all share responsibility for protecting nature.
F. For example, the Arctic tern flies from the North Pole to the South Pole every year.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] C   [3] B   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前提出疑问"how these small creatures can make such incredible journeys"，空后"The answer lies in..."说明要引出答案的铺垫。A项"However, these amazing feats are made possible by several key factors"承上启下，既回应前文的"不可思议"，又为后文"a combination of..."做铺垫。

- **[2] C**：
  - **线索**：第二段主题是鸟类如何为迁徙做准备（eating more, storing fat），空后"They use the sun, stars, and Earth's magnetic field"讲的是导航方式。C项"Their bodies are specially built for long-distance flight"承接前文的身体准备（增肥、储能），引出后文的定向能力，符合段落逻辑。

- **[3] B**：
  - **线索**：空前描述V字队形飞行，空后"Flying in a V saves energy because each bird uses the air currents"。B项"This teamwork allows them to travel farther with less effort"概括V字队形的本质——团队协作省力，与后文" saves energy"完美衔接。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：段首提到"Migration is full of dangers"和"Many do not survive"，空前讲危险，空后"They must find safe places to rest and feed"。D项"Along the way, birds need places to stop and regain their strength"承上启下，解释生存需休息站，与后文"find safe places"对应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：结尾段主题是"Bird migration connects different parts of our world"，空前强调全球连接，空后"When we protect..."呼吁行动。E项"It reminds us that we all share responsibility for protecting nature"升华主题，从现象到责任，自然引出保护措施。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the Arctic tern flies from the North Pole to the South Pole every year**：F是具体例子，但原文五处空白均为承上启下的概括句或过渡句，不要求举例。且北极燕鸥的例子若放在[1]会打断"疑问-答案"逻辑；放在其他位置则与段落主题不符，易使考生误选。
