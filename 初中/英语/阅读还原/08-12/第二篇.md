# 08-12 第二篇

**The Power of Polite Language**

Words have the power to build bridges or create walls between people. How we speak to others can change the entire mood of a conversation. Polite language is not just about following rules; it shows respect and kindness. [1] Using "please," "thank you," and "excuse me" can transform everyday interactions into pleasant experiences for everyone involved.

Being polite at school creates a positive learning environment. When students speak respectfully to teachers and classmates, everyone feels valued. [2] Teachers respond better to polite questions, and students work together more smoothly. Simple phrases like "May I ask a question?" instead of shouting out make the classroom run more effectively. Respect goes both ways—when we show it, we often receive it back.

Politeness also matters in the digital world. Online communication can feel impersonal, so kind words become even more important. [3] Saying "Thanks for your help!" in a message shows appreciation. Avoid typing in ALL CAPS, which can seem like shouting. Emojis can add warmth, but they cannot replace genuine polite words. A well-written, respectful message can prevent misunderstandings.

Some people think being polite means being fake. This is a misunderstanding. [4] True politeness comes from caring about others' feelings, not from pretending. It does not mean we always agree or never speak up. We can be both polite and honest. Expressing disagreement in a respectful way often leads to better outcomes than rude criticism.

The habit of politeness grows with practice. [5] Start with small actions—hold the door for someone, say "please" when asking, and thank people for their help. Over time, these habits become natural. Polite language costs nothing but can bring great rewards in friendships, at school, and in future careers.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] C   [2] B   [3] D   [4] A   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] C**：
  - **线索**：空前讲polite language的定义与作用（shows respect and kindness），空后举例"please, thank you, excuse me"。C项"Small words can have a big impact on how others feel"总结小词大用，既呼应前文"words have power"，又自然引出具体词汇例子。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：第二段主题是学校中的礼貌，空前"When students speak respectfully...everyone feels valued"，空后"Teachers respond better...students work together more smoothly"。B项"It makes a real difference in how people work and learn together"概括礼貌对师生协作的促进作用，与前后内容一致。

- **[3] D**：
  - **线索**：第三段讲网络世界的礼貌，空前"kind words become even more important"，空后"Saying 'Thanks for your help!'...Emojis can add warmth"。D项"Politeness can be shown through simple, thoughtful messages"点明网络礼貌可通过简单讯息体现，与后文举例呼应。

- **[4] A**：
  - **线索**：空前驳斥"polite means being fake"，空后"True politeness comes from caring...not from pretending"。A项"In fact, it reflects our true character and improves our relationships"正面回应误解，强调礼貌反映真实品格，与后文"true politeness"逻辑连贯。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：结尾段"The habit of politeness grows with practice"，空前讲练习养成习惯，空后"Start with small actions"。E项"The good news is that anyone can learn to be more polite"承上启下，从"可练习"过渡到"人人可学"，自然引出具体建议。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, in Japan people bow when greeting others as a sign of respect**：F为文化习俗举例，与文章"通用礼貌用语"主题相关，但全文五处均为概括或过渡句，无插入具体文化案例的位置。若放[1]会偏离"日常用语"；放[3]与数字礼仪无关，易干扰考生思路。
