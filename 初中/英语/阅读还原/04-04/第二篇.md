# 04-04 第二篇

**The Story of Tea: From Leaf to Cup**

Tea is one of the most popular drinks in the world. People in many countries enjoy it every day, either in the morning or during a break. [1] It is believed that tea was first discovered in China over 4,000 years ago when some leaves fell into boiling water. Since then, tea has traveled across oceans and mountains to reach almost every corner of the globe.

How did tea reach so many different places? The Silk Road played an important role. [2] Traders carried tea along this ancient route from China to Central Asia and further west. Later, European ships brought tea to new lands. In each new place, people developed their own ways of drinking tea—some added milk and sugar, while others preferred it plain.

Today, tea is more than just a drink. In some cultures, serving tea is a sign of respect and friendship. [3] In Japan, the tea ceremony is a traditional art that takes years to learn. In Britain, afternoon tea with sandwiches and cakes is a beloved custom. These traditions show how one simple drink can connect people and cultures in special ways.

Scientists have also studied tea for its possible health benefits. Some research suggests that tea may help people feel more relaxed and focused. [4] Green tea, in particular, contains substances that many believe are good for the body. Of course, tea alone cannot replace a healthy diet or exercise, but enjoying a cup can be a small pleasure in a busy day.

[5] Whether you like it hot or cold, with honey or with nothing at all, tea has found its place in the hearts of millions. It brings people together, warms cold hands, and offers a moment of peace in our fast-paced world.

## 选项

A. No matter how you prefer to drink it, tea remains a drink that crosses borders and unites people.
B. According to legend, a Chinese emperor noticed the pleasant smell and taste of the water by chance.
C. For centuries, camel caravans（商队）and horse-drawn carts made this long journey possible.
D. From formal ceremonies to simple daily habits, tea culture takes many forms around the world.
E. However, most experts agree that moderate amounts of tea are generally safe for most people.
F. Interestingly, tea is now grown in more than 50 countries, including India, Kenya, and Sri Lanka.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] B   [2] C   [3] D   [4] E   [5] A

## 深度解析

- **[1] B**：
  - **线索**：起源叙述与因果衔接。空前总述茶在世界流行，空后具体说明"tea was first discovered in China over 4,000 years ago when some leaves fell into boiling water"。B选项"According to legend, a Chinese emperor noticed the pleasant smell and taste of the water by chance"（据传说，一位中国皇帝偶然注意到水的香味和滋味）与空后"when some leaves fell into boiling water"形成完整传说叙述链：树叶落入沸水→皇帝偶然发现→茶被发明，逻辑自然衔接。

- **[2] C**：
  - **线索**：运输方式与历史细节。空前指出丝绸之路在茶的传播中扮演重要角色，空后描述"Traders carried tea along this ancient route...to Central Asia and further west"。C选项"For centuries, camel caravans and horse-drawn carts made this long journey possible"（几个世纪以来，骆驼商队和马车使这段漫长的旅程成为可能）具体说明古代贸易的运输方式，"this long journey"与空前"Silk Road"及空后"Traders carried tea"呼应，补充历史细节。

- **[3] D**：
  - **线索**：文化多样性与举例衔接。空前总述"tea is more than just a drink...serving tea is a sign of respect and friendship"，空后分别举例日本茶道与英国下午茶。D选项"From formal ceremonies to simple daily habits, tea culture takes many forms around the world"（从正式仪式到简单的日常习惯，茶文化在世界各地呈现多种形式）精准概括前后，"formal ceremonies"对应日本茶道，"simple daily habits"对应日常饮茶，与后文举例完美衔接。

- **[4] E**：
  - **线索**：科学研究与适度建议。空前描述茶可能有助于放松和集中注意力，并提及绿茶含有的物质，空后指出"tea alone cannot replace a healthy diet or exercise"。E选项"However, most experts agree that moderate amounts of tea are generally safe for most people"（然而，多数专家认为适量饮茶对大多数人总体上是安全的）与空后形成呼应——既肯定茶的可能益处，又以"moderate amounts"和"generally safe"体现客观与适度，避免夸大效果。

- **[5] A**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后"Whether you like it hot or cold, with honey or with nothing at all...brings people together, warms cold hands, and offers a moment of peace"。A选项"No matter how you prefer to drink it, tea remains a drink that crosses borders and unites people"（无论你偏爱如何饮用，茶始终是一种跨越疆界、凝聚人心的饮品）中"No matter how you prefer to drink it"与空后"hot or cold, with honey or with nothing"完美呼应，"crosses borders and unites people"与"brings people together"形成情感升华。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. Interestingly, tea is now grown in more than 50 countries, including India, Kenya, and Sri Lanka.**：补充茶种植的地理分布，与全文主题相关。但各空均需承上启下的衔接句：空[1]需茶起源传说，空[2]需丝绸之路运输方式，空[3]需茶文化多样性概括，空[4]需科学研究后的客观建议，空[5]需总结升华。F仅提供种植地信息，无法与任一空前后形成自然逻辑衔接，插入会造成话题跳跃。属"背景拓展但逻辑角色不符"型干扰。
