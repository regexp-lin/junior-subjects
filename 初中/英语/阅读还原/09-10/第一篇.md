# 09-10 第一篇

**Urban Transportation: Moving Cities Forward**

Cities grow as more people move in for work and study. With growth comes traffic—cars, buses, bikes, and pedestrians all competing for space. Congestion leads to wasted time, polluted air, and stress for everyone. [1] Finding smarter ways to move people around is one of the biggest challenges that modern cities face today.

Public transit—buses, subways, and trains—carries many people at once and uses less space per person than cars. Cities that invest in good transit see fewer cars on the road and cleaner air. [2] Expanding and improving these systems can reduce traffic, cut pollution, and make cities more livable for millions of residents.

Bikes and walking offer another solution. Short trips by bike or on foot are healthy, free, and produce no emissions. Many cities now build bike lanes and pedestrian zones to encourage these choices. [3] Making streets safer and more inviting for cyclists and walkers helps shift people away from cars for short distances.

New technology is also changing how we get around. Ride-sharing apps, electric scooters, and self-driving buses are being tested in some cities. [4] These innovations may one day make travel faster, cleaner, and more convenient, though they must be planned carefully to benefit everyone.

There is no single answer to urban transport. Each city must find a mix that fits its geography, economy, and culture. [5] The cities that succeed will be those that put people first—making it easy to move, breathe, and thrive in the places we call home.

## 选项

A. Solving the problem of how to move millions of people efficiently is essential for urban quality of life.
B. When more people choose transit over cars, the benefits spread to the whole community.
C. Creating better conditions for active travel can change how people choose to move.
D. Technology alone will not fix traffic, but it can be part of a larger solution.
E. The best transport systems are those that balance convenience, health, and sustainability for all.
F. For example, Tokyo's subway system carries over 8 million passengers every single day.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前指出城市扩张带来交通拥堵、时间浪费、空气污染和压力，空后强调"Finding smarter ways to move people around is one of the biggest challenges"。A的"Solving the problem of how to move millions of people efficiently is essential for urban quality of life"概括应对交通挑战的重要性，与"biggest challenges"完美呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前描述公交系统的优势（载客多、占地少、改善空气），空后说"Expanding and improving these systems can reduce traffic, cut pollution, and make cities more livable"。B的"When more people choose transit over cars, the benefits spread to the whole community"与公交投资带来的集体效益一致，概括该段逻辑。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前讨论自行车与步行、自行车道和步行区，空后说"Making streets safer and more inviting... helps shift people away from cars"。C的"Creating better conditions for active travel can change how people choose to move"准确概括改善基础设施对出行方式的影响，与"shift people away from cars"呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前列举新技术（拼车、电动滑板、无人驾驶公交），空后说"These innovations may one day make travel faster... though they must be planned carefully"。D的"Technology alone will not fix traffic, but it can be part of a larger solution"既肯定技术作用又强调需谨慎规划，与"planned carefully to benefit everyone"呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前总结无单一答案、每城需因地制宜，空后无。E的"The best transport systems are those that balance convenience, health, and sustainability for all"升华主题，与"put people first"和"easy to move, breathe, and thrive"完美对应，适合作全文收束。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, Tokyo's subway system carries over 8 million passengers every single day.**：以For example开头，属具体数据举例；各空均为概括或过渡句，F与公交系统相关但为个案，易误放[2]，但该空需公交扩建改善带来的概括，非举例。
