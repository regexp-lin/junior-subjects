# 04-12 第二篇

**The Science of Origami: From Paper Folds to Space**

When you think of origami, you might picture a paper crane or a folded frog. But this ancient Japanese art is no longer just a hobby—engineers, doctors, and even NASA scientists are using origami principles to solve real-world problems. [1] The key is that folding allows flat materials to expand, shrink, or change shape in clever ways without adding extra parts. A folded structure can be packed small for travel and then opened up when it is needed. This makes origami ideal for situations where space and weight matter, such as in spacecraft, medical devices, or disaster relief.

Space is one of the most exciting applications. Satellites and telescopes need large solar panels or antennas to work well, but rockets have limited room inside them. [2] Engineers have designed solar panels that fold up like a fan or an accordion so they fit inside the rocket during launch. Once in orbit, they unfold automatically and spread out to catch sunlight. Some designs are inspired by origami patterns that have been studied for centuries. Without folding, we could never send such large structures into space efficiently.

Medicine is another field where origami is making a difference. [3] Surgeons sometimes need to insert tools or implants through very small cuts to reduce scarring and healing time. A stent—a tube that holds open a blocked blood vessel—can be folded flat, pushed through a narrow catheter, and then expanded inside the body. The same idea is used for heart implants and even tiny robots that could one day move inside the human body to deliver medicine. Folding lets big devices work through small openings.

Origami also helps in emergencies. [4] Temporary shelters for people who have lost their homes in disasters can be made from flat panels that fold into 3D structures. These shelters are easy to transport and can be set up quickly without heavy machinery. In some places, foldable solar panels and water-purification devices use the same principle—pack small, deploy big. When every kilogram and cubic meter counts, origami designs save both space and time.

[5] From space missions to life-saving medical tools and disaster relief, the simple act of folding paper has led to inventions that reach far beyond the craft table. The next time you fold a paper plane, remember that you are using the same principles that engineers use to explore the universe.

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：太空难题与折纸解决方案。空前提出"Satellites and telescopes need large solar panels or antennas...but rockets have limited room inside them"，空后具体描述"Engineers have designed solar panels that fold up like a fan...fit inside the rocket...Once in orbit, they unfold automatically"。B选项"The solution is to use origami: fold everything down for the journey, then unfold it in space"（解决方案是使用折纸：在旅程中将一切折叠收起，到太空后再展开）中"The solution"直接回应空前难题，"fold...for the journey, then unfold in space"与空后"fold up...fit inside...unfold automatically...in orbit"完美对应，逻辑紧密。

  - **线索**：应急场景与可折叠结构优势。空前提出"Temporary shelters for people who have lost their homes...can be made from flat panels that fold into 3D structures"，空后说明"easy to transport...set up quickly without heavy machinery...foldable solar panels and water-purification devices...pack small, deploy big"。E选项"In these cases, foldable structures can be shipped flat and assembled quickly on site"（在这些情况下，可折叠结构可以平装运输并在现场快速组装）中"In these cases"指代应急场景，"shipped flat"与空后"flat panels...easy to transport...pack small"对应，"assembled quickly on site"与"set up quickly...deploy big"呼应，逻辑顺畅。
