**Communication Skills: Building Better Relationships Through Talking and Listening**

Good communication is the key to strong relationships—with family, friends, classmates, and teachers. Yet many people find it difficult to express their feelings clearly or to understand what others really mean. [1] In fact, communication is not just about speaking; it also includes listening carefully, reading body language, and choosing the right words at the right time. When we improve these skills, we avoid misunderstandings and build trust. That is why learning to communicate well is one of the most useful skills we can develop.

Listening is half of good communication. [2] When someone is talking to you, give them your full attention. Do not look at your phone or think about what you will say next. Show that you are listening by nodding, making eye contact, and asking follow-up questions. The speaker will feel valued, and you will understand them better. In other words, being a good listener means focusing on the other person and showing that you care.

Choosing the right words matters. [3] The same idea can sound very different depending on how we say it. "You never listen to me" can make someone defensive. "I feel unheard sometimes—can we talk?" is more likely to open a dialogue. Using "I" instead of "you" when sharing feelings often helps avoid blame and争吵（quarrel）. For this reason, the way we speak can either build bridges or create walls between people.

Non-verbal communication carries meaning too. [4] Our facial expressions, gestures, and tone of voice often say more than our words. A smile can show warmth; crossed arms may suggest that we are closed off. When our words and body language match, people trust us more. When they conflict, others may feel confused or suspicious. So paying attention to how we look and sound helps us communicate more clearly and honestly.

[5] In short, good communication is a skill we can all learn and practise. By listening well, choosing our words carefully, and being aware of our body language, we can build stronger, healthier relationships. Remember: every conversation is a chance to understand someone better and to be understood. Start with one small change today—you might be surprised by the difference it makes.

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：总领沟通的多维构成。空前描述"Good communication is the key to strong relationships...Yet many people find it difficult to express their feelings clearly or to understand what others really mean"，空后说明"In fact, communication is not just about speaking; it also includes listening carefully, reading body language, and choosing the right words at the right time...When we improve these skills, we avoid misunderstandings and build trust...learning to communicate well is one of the most useful skills"。A选项"That is because communication involves much more than just talking"（那是因为沟通远不止说话）中"That is because"解释为何沟通不易，"much more than just talking"与空后listening、body language、choosing words等多元要素精准对应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：有效倾听的核心要素。空前提出"Listening is half of good communication"，空后列举"When someone is talking to you, give them your full attention...Do not look at your phone or think about what you will say next...Show that you are listening by nodding, making eye contact, and asking follow-up questions...The speaker will feel valued, and you will understand them better...being a good listener means focusing on the other person and showing that you care"。B选项"In other words, real listening means focusing on the speaker and showing you care"（换言之，真正的倾听意味着专注说话者并表达关心）中"In other words"概括空前"listening is half"，"focusing on the speaker and showing you care"与空后全神贯注、眼神交流、追问及"feel valued"完美呼应，衔接自然。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：措辞对人际连接的影响。空前提出"Choosing the right words matters"，空后对比"The same idea can sound very different depending on how we say it...'You never listen to me' can make someone defensive...'I feel unheard sometimes—can we talk?' is more likely to open a dialogue...Using 'I' instead of 'you'...often helps avoid blame...the way we speak can either build bridges or create walls between people"。C选项"That is why the words we choose can either connect us or distance us from others"（因此我们选择的措辞既能拉近距离也能疏远彼此）中"That is why"承接空前"choosing the right words matters"，"connect us or distance us"与空后build bridges or create walls精准对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：非语言信息的传递作用。空前提出"Non-verbal communication carries meaning too"，空后详述"Our facial expressions, gestures, and tone of voice often say more than our words...A smile can show warmth; crossed arms may suggest that we are closed off...When our words and body language match, people trust us more...When they conflict, others may feel confused or suspicious...paying attention to how we look and sound helps us communicate more clearly and honestly"。D选项"It is true that our body language also sends important messages"（的确，我们的肢体语言也传递重要信息）中"It is true that"加强衔接，"body language...sends important messages"与空后面部表情、手势、语气及match/conflict效应完美呼应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与关系提升总结。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"good communication is a skill we can all learn and practise...By listening well, choosing our words carefully, and being aware of our body language, we can build stronger, healthier relationships...every conversation is a chance to understand someone better and to be understood...Start with one small change today—you might be surprised by the difference"。E选项"In conclusion, we can improve our relationships by improving how we communicate"（总之，我们可以通过改善沟通方式来改善人际关系）用"In conclusion"总结全文，"improve relationships by improving how we communicate"与空后三大要点及"stronger, healthier relationships"形成呼应，收束有力。
