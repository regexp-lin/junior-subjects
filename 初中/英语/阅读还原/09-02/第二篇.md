# 09-02 第二篇

**Ocean Plastic Pollution**

Our oceans are filling up with plastic. Every year, millions of tons of plastic waste enter the sea, harming marine life and polluting the water we all depend on. From tiny microplastics to large floating garbage patches, plastic pollution has become one of the most serious environmental problems of our time. [1] If we do not act quickly, the damage to our oceans and the creatures that live in them could become irreversible.

Plastic is everywhere in modern life. We use it for packaging, bottles, bags, and countless other products. The problem is that most plastic does not break down naturally—it can last for hundreds of years. [2] When plastic reaches the ocean, it breaks into smaller and smaller pieces, but it never truly disappears, and these tiny particles enter the food chain.

Marine animals suffer greatly from plastic pollution. Sea turtles mistake plastic bags for jellyfish, and seabirds swallow small plastic pieces thinking they are food. [3] Scientists have found plastic inside the stomachs of fish, whales, and even deep-sea creatures, showing just how widespread the problem has become.

What can we do? Reducing our use of single-use plastics is a good start. Bringing reusable bags, bottles, and containers helps cut down on waste. [4] Recycling properly and supporting companies that use less packaging are also important steps that individuals can take every day.

Governments and organizations are taking action too. Some countries have banned plastic bags or straws, and cleanup projects are working to remove plastic from beaches and oceans. [5] Solving the plastic crisis will require effort from everyone—individuals, businesses, and governments—working together to protect the oceans for future generations.

## 选项

A. The scale of ocean plastic pollution demands urgent and coordinated global action.
B. This durability, which makes plastic so useful, is also what makes it so dangerous for the environment.
C. These encounters with plastic often lead to injury, starvation, and death for countless marine species.
D. Every small choice to refuse unnecessary plastic adds up to a meaningful reduction in ocean pollution.
E. While progress has been made, much more needs to be done to turn the tide against plastic waste.
F. For example, the Great Pacific Garbage Patch is now twice the size of Texas.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述海洋塑料污染的严重性，空后说"If we do not act quickly, the damage... could become irreversible"。A的"The scale of ocean plastic pollution"承接前文规模描述，"demands urgent and coordinated global action"与"act quickly"呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说塑料不能自然分解、可存在数百年，空后说塑料在海中碎裂但不消失。B的"This durability, which makes plastic so useful"承接耐久性，"also what makes it so dangerous"概括矛盾本质，过渡到环境危害。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前举例海龟和海鸟误食塑料，空后说科学家在各种海洋生物体内发现塑料。C的"These encounters with plastic"承接前文具体案例，"injury, starvation, and death"概括后果的严重性。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前列举个人减塑行动（可重复使用袋子、瓶子等），空后说回收和支持环保企业也很重要。D的"Every small choice to refuse unnecessary plastic"概括前文行动，"adds up to a meaningful reduction"连接到更广泛的影响。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说政府和组织在采取行动（禁塑令、清理项目），空后总结需要全社会合作。E的"While progress has been made"肯定前文努力，"much more needs to be done"引出仍需努力的呼吁，承上启下。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the Great Pacific Garbage Patch is now twice the size of Texas.**：以For example开头，属具体数据举例；易误放[1]，但[1]需的是对问题紧迫性的概括呼吁，非具体数据。
