# 10-22 第一篇

**Mindfulness and Meditation**

Life can be busy and stressful. We often rush from one task to the next, thinking about the past or worrying about the future. Mindfulness is a way of paying attention to the present moment—without judging it or trying to change it. [1] Learning to be mindful can help us feel calmer, focus better, and enjoy life more, especially when things get stressful.

Mindfulness has roots in ancient traditions, but it has become popular in modern life. Many schools now teach students simple mindfulness exercises, such as focusing on their breath for a few minutes. Some companies offer meditation classes to employees. [2] Mindfulness is no longer seen as something strange or religious—it is increasingly accepted as a practical tool for managing stress and improving well-being in everyday life.

Research supports the benefits of mindfulness. Studies show that regular practice can reduce anxiety, improve attention, and even strengthen the immune system. It can help students perform better on tests and help workers concentrate at their desks. [3] The science behind mindfulness suggests that taking a few minutes each day to slow down and focus can bring real, measurable benefits to both mind and body.

You do not need special equipment or a lot of time to practice mindfulness. You can start by sitting quietly and noticing your breath for five minutes. You can practice while eating—paying attention to the taste and texture of each bite. You can even do it while walking, by focusing on the feeling of your feet on the ground. [4] Mindfulness can be part of daily life, and small, regular practice is often more effective than occasional long sessions.

Mindfulness is not a magic cure for all problems. It takes practice, and results may come slowly. But for many people, it has become a valuable habit. [5] With patience and commitment, mindfulness can help us build a calmer, more focused mind—and a life that feels less rushed and more meaningful.

## 选项

A. Mindfulness offers a simple but powerful way to slow down, become more aware, and respond to life's challenges with greater calm and clarity.
B. Today, mindfulness is used in classrooms, offices, and hospitals—and more people are discovering its benefits every year.
C. What was once considered a spiritual practice has now been studied by scientists, and the results point to real improvements in mental and physical health.
D. The key is to make mindfulness a habit—a few minutes each day can add up to big changes over time.
E. Over time, regular practice can lead to lasting changes in how we think, feel, and respond to stress.
F. For example, some hospitals offer mindfulness-based stress reduction (MBSR) programs to help patients cope with pain and anxiety.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍正念的定义（关注当下、不评判），空后说"Learning to be mindful can help us feel calmer, focus better, and enjoy life more, especially when things get stressful"。A的"Mindfulness offers a simple but powerful way to slow down, become more aware, and respond to life's challenges with greater calm and clarity"与空后feel calmer、focus、enjoy呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说正念源于古老传统、现被学校公司采用，空后说"Mindfulness is no longer seen as something strange or religious—it is increasingly accepted as a practical tool for managing stress and improving well-being"。B的"Today, mindfulness is used in classrooms, offices, and hospitals—and more people are discovering its benefits every year"与空后accepted、practical tool呼应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前举研究结论（减焦虑、提升注意力、增强免疫等），空后说"The science behind mindfulness suggests that taking a few minutes each day to slow down and focus can bring real, measurable benefits to both mind and body"。C的"What was once considered a spiritual practice has now been studied by scientists, and the results point to real improvements in mental and physical health"与空后science、measurable benefits完美衔接。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说无需特殊器材、可从呼吸、进食、步行等简单方式练习，空后说"Mindfulness can be part of daily life, and small, regular practice is often more effective than occasional long sessions"。D的"The key is to make mindfulness a habit—a few minutes each day can add up to big changes over time"与空后daily life、small regular practice呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说正念非万能、需练习、效果渐进，空后说"With patience and commitment, mindfulness can help us build a calmer, more focused mind—and a life that feels less rushed and more meaningful"。E的"Over time, regular practice can lead to lasting changes in how we think, feel, and respond to stress"与空后patience、commitment、lasting benefits呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
