# 03-27 第二篇

**Why Museums Are Worth Your Time**

When you think of a weekend activity, visiting a museum might not be your first choice. Some students find museums boring or too serious. However, museums offer experiences that you cannot get from books or screens. [1] They bring history, science, and art to life in ways that can change how you see the world.

First, museums let you see real objects from the past. Reading about ancient tools or dinosaur bones is one thing, but standing in front of them is completely different. [2] This direct experience creates memories that often last a lifetime. You might forget what you read in a textbook, but you will remember the day you saw a real dinosaur skeleton (骨架) for the first time.

Museums also encourage curiosity and critical thinking. When you walk through the exhibits, you naturally ask questions: How did people live hundreds of years ago? Why does this painting look so different from that one? [3] These questions lead to deeper learning and a greater appreciation for human achievement throughout history.

What is more, many museums now offer interactive (互动的) activities. You can touch replicas, try simple experiments, or join guided tours. [4] Such experiences make learning fun and help students of all ages discover new interests. A visit to a science museum might spark your love for chemistry, or an art museum might inspire you to pick up a paintbrush.

[5] Next time you have a free afternoon, consider stepping into a museum near you. You might be surprised at how much you enjoy it—and how much you learn.

## 选项

A. In short, museums are much more than old buildings full of dusty objects.
B. As a result, experts strongly advise students to stay away from museums and study only at home.
C. For this reason, museums have become important places for education and discovery.
D. Unlike photos or videos, being there in person allows you to feel the size, the detail, and the wonder of these objects.
E. The best way to get the full benefit is to plan your visit and choose a museum that matches your interests.
F. Asking "why" and "how" turns a simple visit into an adventure of the mind.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] C   [2] D   [3] F   [4] E   [5] A

## 深度解析

- **[1] C**：
  - **线索**：总结概括与代词指代。空前指出博物馆提供书籍和屏幕无法替代的体验，并概括其价值。C选项"For this reason, museums have become important places for education and discovery"（因此，博物馆已成为教育和探索的重要场所）中"For this reason"指代前文"offer experiences that you cannot get from books or screens"。"education and discovery"与空后"bring history, science, and art to life"形成同义呼应——都是对博物馆教育价值的概括。

- **[2] D**：
  - **线索**：对比递进与语义衔接。空前对比了"reading about"与"standing in front of"的差异，强调亲身体验的不同。D选项"Unlike photos or videos, being there in person allows you to feel the size, the detail, and the wonder of these objects"（与照片或视频不同，亲临现场让你能感受这些物品的尺寸、细节和奇妙之处）延续了这一对比逻辑：reading/photos/videos vs. being there。空后"This direct experience"直接指代D选项中的"being there in person"，完美衔接。

- **[3] F**：
  - **线索**：具体化阐释与同义替换。空前列举了参观时自然产生的问题——"How did people live...? Why does this painting look...?"。F选项"Asking 'why' and 'how' turns a simple visit into an adventure of the mind"（追问"为什么"和"如何"将简单的参观变成思维的探险）中"Asking 'why' and 'how'"正是对前文问题的概括。空后"These questions"指代前文+空[3]中的问题，F选项说明这些问题的价值——"lead to deeper learning"与"adventure of the mind"语义呼应。

- **[4] E**：
  - **线索**：总结评价与逻辑递进。空前描述博物馆的互动项目——触摸复制品、做实验、参加导览。E选项"The best way to get the full benefit is to plan your visit and choose a museum that matches your interests"（获得充分收获的最佳方式是规划参观并选择符合你兴趣的博物馆）中的"get the full benefit"承接前文互动体验带来的收获。空后"Such experiences make learning fun and help students...discover new interests"与E选项"choose a museum that matches your interests"形成呼应——选对博物馆→发现新兴趣。

- **[5] A**：
  - **线索**：全文总结与打破偏见。空[5]位于结尾段段首。开篇提到"Some students find museums boring"，前文从实物体验、好奇思维、互动活动三方面论证博物馆价值。A选项"In short, museums are much more than old buildings full of dusty objects"（简而言之，博物馆远不止是堆满陈旧物品的旧建筑）用"In short"收束全文，并直接回应开篇偏见——"boring"往往源于认为博物馆是"dusty objects"。空后"Next time...consider stepping into a museum"是受A启发后的行动号召。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **B. As a result, experts strongly advise students to stay away from museums and study only at home.**：该选项含有"experts""students""museums"等文章关键词和因果连词"As a result"。但其主张"专家强烈建议学生远离博物馆、只在家学习"与全文大力倡导参观博物馆的立场完全对立。属于"关键词+逻辑词误导但立场相反"型干扰。
