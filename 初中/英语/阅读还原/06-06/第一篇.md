**Why Do Birds Migrate?**

Every autumn, millions of birds fly thousands of kilometres south. In spring, they return north to breed（繁殖）. [1] In other words, migration is one of the most amazing journeys in the natural world. Scientists have studied these travellers for decades, and we now understand much about why they take such long and risky trips each year.

The main reason for migration is food. [2] When winter comes to northern regions, insects die, seeds are buried under snow, and lakes freeze. Birds that stay behind would simply starve. Flying south to warmer places means more food and better chances of survival. Some birds travel to tropical（热带的） rainforests, while others stop at wetlands or coastal areas where food remains plentiful all winter.

Navigation is another wonder. [3] How do birds know which way to go? Research shows that they use the sun, stars, Earth's magnetic field（磁场）, and even landmarks like rivers and mountains. Young birds often fly with older, experienced birds on their first migration. Some species can even sense changes in air pressure and wind direction to choose the best route. Their ability to find their way across continents remains a subject of ongoing study.

Migration is not without danger. [4] Birds face storms, predators, and exhaustion. Many collide with tall buildings or communication towers. Light pollution in cities can confuse them and make them fly in circles until they drop. Despite these risks, birds have been migrating for millions of years. Their instincts drive them to complete the journey, no matter how difficult it becomes.

[5] Migration reminds us that animals are connected to places far apart. Protecting migration routes and stopover sites helps ensure that these incredible travellers can continue their ancient journey. When you see birds flying in a V-formation in autumn or spring, take a moment to appreciate the great effort behind their flight.

## 选项

A. In other words, migration is a necessary part of many birds' yearly cycle.
B. That is because cold weather makes food scarce and hard to find.
C. In fact, birds rely on several natural signals to find their way.
D. These challenges make migration one of the riskiest events in a bird's life.
E. In short, bird migration is a shared treasure that we must help protect.
F. For example, some birds can remember the exact location of food caches from years ago.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：引出迁徙作为年度周期的必要部分。空前描述"millions of birds fly thousands of kilometres south...return north to breed"，空后说明"In other words"引导的总结与"migration is one of the most amazing journeys...why they take such long and risky trips each year"。A选项"In other words, migration is a necessary part of many birds' yearly cycle"（换言之，迁徙是许多鸟类年度周期的必要部分）中"In other words"承接空前南北往返，"necessary part of yearly cycle"与空后"amazing journeys...such long and risky trips each year"精准呼应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：冬季食物稀缺的原因。空前提出"The main reason for migration is food"，空后详述"When winter comes...insects die, seeds buried under snow, lakes freeze...would simply starve... Flying south...more food and better chances of survival"。B选项"That is because cold weather makes food scarce and hard to find"（那是因为寒冷天气使食物稀缺且难以找到）中"That is because"解释迁徙原因，"cold weather...food scarce and hard to find"与空后昆虫死亡、种子被雪埋、湖面结冰、挨饿等完美对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：鸟类导航的方式。空前提出"Navigation is another wonder"及"How do birds know which way to go?"，空后列举"use the sun, stars, Earth's magnetic field...landmarks...sense changes in air pressure and wind direction"。C选项"In fact, birds rely on several natural signals to find their way"（事实上，鸟类依赖多种自然信号辨别方向）中"In fact"强化导航能力，"several natural signals"与空后太阳、星辰、磁场、地标、气压、风向等精准对应，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：迁徙面临的危险与风险。空前提出"Migration is not without danger"，空后描述"face storms, predators, exhaustion...collide with buildings...light pollution...confuse...Despite these risks...instincts drive them...no matter how difficult"。D选项"These challenges make migration one of the riskiest events in a bird's life"（这些挑战使迁徙成为鸟类一生中最危险的事件之一）中"These challenges"指代空前 storms、predators、exhaustion、collisions、light pollution，"riskiest events"与空后风险描述完美对应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与保护呼吁。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"animals connected to places far apart...Protecting migration routes and stopover sites...incredible travellers...ancient journey...appreciate the great effort"。E选项"In short, bird migration is a shared treasure that we must help protect"（简言之，鸟类迁徙是我们必须共同保护的珍贵财富）用"In short"总结全文，"shared treasure...help protect"与空后保护迁徙路线、停歇地、延续古老旅程形成呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, some birds can remember the exact location of food caches from years ago.**：涉及鸟类记忆食物储藏地点，易被误填[2]或[3]。但F以"For example"引出记忆能力这一特例，而空[2]讨论的是冬季食物稀缺导致迁徙的必要性，与"记忆食物位置"无关；空[3]讨论的是导航方式（太阳、星辰、磁场等），F的"remember location"虽与方向有关，但属于长期记忆能力，非导航所用的自然信号。F属"主题相关但功能错位"型干扰。
