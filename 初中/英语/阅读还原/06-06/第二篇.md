**Smart Choices About Snacks**

Snacking between meals is common, especially among teenagers. A small bite can help you stay focused and give you energy for the rest of the day. [1] However, not all snacks are created equal—some fuel your body well, while others leave you tired and hungry again soon after. Learning to choose healthy snacks is an important skill that can improve your health and your grades.

What makes a snack healthy? [2] Good snacks often contain protein, fibre, or healthy fats that keep you full longer. An apple with a handful of nuts, yogurt with fruit, or whole-grain crackers with cheese are examples of balanced choices. They provide steady energy instead of a sudden sugar rush that crashes quickly. In contrast, chips, candy, and sugary drinks cause a quick spike in blood sugar followed by a drop that makes you feel sleepy and unable to concentrate.

The timing of snacks matters too. [3] Eating a snack when you feel hungry between meals is fine, but grazing（不停地吃零食） all day can lead to eating too much without realizing it. It helps to plan your snacks—perhaps one mid-morning and one mid-afternoon—rather than reaching for whatever is nearby. Keeping healthy options like fruit or nuts in your bag or desk makes it easier to avoid the vending machine（自动售货机） when hunger strikes.

Reading labels is a useful habit. [4] Many packaged snacks look healthy but are full of added sugar, salt, or unhealthy fats. Words like "natural" or "low-fat" do not always mean the product is good for you. Check the ingredients list and nutrition facts. If sugar or sodium is near the top of the list, consider choosing something else. A little effort in reading labels can lead to much better choices over time.

[5] Snacking does not have to be unhealthy. With a bit of knowledge and planning, you can enjoy snacks that support your body and your mind. Start by swapping one unhealthy snack this week for a healthier option, and see how you feel. Your body will thank you.

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：引出零食质量对能量与学习的影响。空前描述"small bite can help you stay focused and give you energy"，空后转折"However, not all snacks are created equal—some fuel your body well, while others leave you tired and hungry again soon after...Learning to choose healthy snacks...improve your health and your grades"。A选项"In other words, the quality of your snacks can affect both your energy and your learning"（换言之，零食的质量会影响你的精力和学习）中"quality of snacks"与空后"not all created equal...some fuel well, others leave tired"呼应，"energy and learning"与空后"health and grades"精准对应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：健康零食的成分为何有效。空前提出"What makes a snack healthy?"，空后列举"Good snacks often contain protein, fibre, or healthy fats that keep you full longer...apple with nuts, yogurt with fruit, whole-grain crackers with cheese...provide steady energy instead of sugar rush that crashes"。B选项"That is because these nutrients help stabilize energy and reduce hunger between meals"（那是因为这些营养素有助于稳定能量并减少两餐之间的饥饿感）中"That is because"解释健康成分的作用，"stabilize energy and reduce hunger"与空后"keep you full longer...steady energy"完美对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：吃零食的时间与频率的影响。空前提出"The timing of snacks matters too"，空后详述"snack when hungry is fine, but grazing all day...eating too much...plan your snacks—mid-morning and mid-afternoon...rather than reaching for whatever is nearby"。C选项"In fact, when and how often you snack can make a big difference"（事实上，何时吃零食以及吃零食的频率会产生很大影响）中"In fact"强化空前论点，"when and how often"与空后 timing、grazing、plan snacks 等内容精准对应，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：包装零食的营销陷阱。空前提出"Reading labels is a useful habit"，空后描述"Many packaged snacks look healthy but full of added sugar, salt, unhealthy fats...'natural' or 'low-fat' do not always mean good...check ingredients...sugar or sodium near top"。D选项"These marketing tricks often hide the truth about what is really inside the package"（这些营销伎俩常常掩盖包装内真实成分）中"These marketing tricks"指代空后"natural"、"low-fat"等标签，"hide the truth"与空后"look healthy but full of..."完美对应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与习惯建议。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"Snacking does not have to be unhealthy...knowledge and planning...enjoy snacks that support body and mind...swap one unhealthy snack...Your body will thank you"。E选项"In short, smart snacking is a habit worth building for a healthier life"（简言之，明智吃零食是值得培养的、通向更健康生活的习惯）用"In short"总结全文，"habit worth building"与空后"knowledge and planning...swap...see how you feel"形成呼应，收束精准。
