# 06-04 第二篇

**Street Art: From Walls to Museums**

Street art has grown from something seen as vandalism（故意破坏） to a respected form of art around the world. What was once spray paint on walls is now displayed in galleries and sold for millions. [1] Street art includes murals（壁画）, stencils（模板画）, and graffiti（涂鸦） that transform ordinary urban spaces into open-air galleries. Artists use the city as their canvas, turning grey walls into colourful scenes that tell stories, share opinions, or simply add beauty to daily life.

Why do artists choose the street? [2] Public spaces allow art to reach people who might never step into a museum. A mural on a school wall can inspire children every morning. A painting on a busy street can make thousands of passers-by smile. Street art is free, open to everyone, and does not require a ticket or a formal invitation. It brings art out of elite（精英的） spaces and into the lives of ordinary people.

Street art often carries strong messages. [3] Many artists use their work to comment on social issues such as pollution, inequality, or human rights. A famous British artist, Banksy, is known for his satirical（讽刺的） and politically charged pieces that appear overnight on walls around the world. Other artists create works to celebrate local culture or to remember important historical events. In this way, street art can start conversations and raise awareness in communities.

Cities have started to welcome street art instead of fighting it. [4] Some governments now invite artists to paint legal murals in certain areas, turning dull walls into tourist attractions. Businesses commission artists to design colourful storefronts. Art festivals and street art tours have become popular in many cities. What was once removed as illegal graffiti is now sometimes protected and preserved as public art.

[5] Street art has proven that creativity can flourish anywhere—on a corner, under a bridge, or on a forgotten wall. It challenges our ideas about where art belongs and who gets to create it. Whether you love it or debate it, street art has become an undeniable part of modern urban culture and will continue to shape our cities for years to come.

## 选项

A. In other words, street art has become a diverse and influential art form that belongs to the public.
B. That is because the street offers a direct and democratic way to share art with the public.
C. In fact, many street artists use their work to express ideas about society and politics.
D. This shows how attitudes toward street art have shifted from rejection to acceptance.
E. In short, street art has changed how we think about art, space, and creativity in cities.
F. For example, some street artists have become famous and their works are collected by museums.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：引出街头艺术的形态与公共属性。空前描述"Street art has grown from vandalism to respected form of art...spray paint on walls is now displayed in galleries"，空后说明"Street art includes murals, stencils, graffiti...transform ordinary urban spaces into open-air galleries...use the city as their canvas...add beauty to daily life"。A选项"In other words, street art has become a diverse and influential art form that belongs to the public"（换言之，街头艺术已成为一种多元且有影响力的、属于公众的艺术形式）中"In other words"概括空前地位的转变，"diverse...belongs to the public"与空后多种类型及"open-air galleries...city as canvas"呼应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：街头艺术家选择公共空间的原因。空前提问"Why do artists choose the street?"，空后描述"Public spaces allow art to reach people who might never step into a museum...mural on school wall...painting on busy street...free, open to everyone...brings art out of elite spaces into ordinary people's lives"。B选项"That is because the street offers a direct and democratic way to share art with the public"（那是因为街头提供了一种直接且民主的与公众分享艺术的方式）中"That is because"回答空前问句，"direct and democratic...share with the public"与空后"reach people...free, open...out of elite spaces"精准对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：街头艺术的社会与政治表达。空前提出"Street art often carries strong messages"，空后列举"Many artists use their work to comment on social issues...pollution, inequality, human rights...Banksy...satirical and politically charged...celebrate local culture...remember historical events...start conversations"。C选项"In fact, many street artists use their work to express ideas about society and politics"（事实上，许多街头艺术家用作品表达关于社会与政治的观点）中"In fact"强化空前论点，"express ideas about society and politics"与空后评论社会议题、政治讽刺、文化纪念等内容完美对应，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：城市对街头艺术态度的转变。空前描述"Cities have started to welcome street art instead of fighting it"，空后列举"governments invite artists...legal murals...tourist attractions...Businesses commission...Art festivals and street art tours...What was once removed as illegal...now protected and preserved"。D选项"This shows how attitudes toward street art have shifted from rejection to acceptance"（这表明人们对街头艺术的态度已从排斥转为接受）中"This shows"承接空前"welcome instead of fighting"，"shifted from rejection to acceptance"与空后从"removed as illegal"到"protected and preserved"的对比精准呼应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"creativity can flourish anywhere...challenges our ideas about where art belongs and who gets to create it...undeniable part of modern urban culture...shape our cities"。E选项"In short, street art has changed how we think about art, space, and creativity in cities"（简言之，街头艺术改变了我们对城市中艺术、空间与创意的看法）用"In short"总结全文，"changed how we think about art, space, creativity"与空后"challenges our ideas...shape our cities"呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, some street artists have become famous and their works are collected by museums.**：涉及街头艺术家成名、作品被博物馆收藏，易被误填[1]或[4]。但F以"For example"引出艺术家商业成功的个案，而空[1]需要的是对街头艺术形态与公共属性的概括，F聚焦"famous"和"museums"，与空后"murals, stencils, graffiti...open-air galleries"的形态描述不匹配；空[4]讨论的是城市政策的接纳（政府邀请、合法壁画、艺术节），F的"museums collect"属于艺术市场层面，非政策态度。F属"主题相关但衔接维度错位"型干扰。
