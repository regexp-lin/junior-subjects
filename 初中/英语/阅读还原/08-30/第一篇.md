# 08-30 第一篇

**Human Exploration of Mars**

Mars has fascinated humans for thousands of years. It appears as a red dot in the night sky and has inspired countless stories and dreams. Today, we are no longer just watching Mars from afar—we are sending robots and planning to send people there. [1] Exploring Mars is one of the most ambitious goals in the history of space science, and it raises both excitement and important questions.

Robots have been exploring Mars since the 1970s. They have taken photos, collected soil samples, and searched for signs of water or past life. [2] Each new mission brings better technology and new discoveries, helping scientists understand whether Mars could ever support human life.

Why do we want to go to Mars? Some say it is our nature to explore. Others hope that Mars could one day become a second home if Earth faces serious problems. [3] Whatever the reason, the journey to Mars requires huge amounts of money, time, and cooperation among nations—and not everyone agrees that it is worth the cost.

Sending humans to Mars is extremely difficult. The trip takes months, and astronauts would face radiation, isolation, and many technical challenges. [4] Scientists and engineers are developing new spacecraft, life-support systems, and ways to grow food in space to make such a mission possible.

Despite the challenges, Mars exploration continues to move forward. Space agencies from different countries are working together and sharing knowledge. [5] Whether we reach Mars in ten years or fifty, the spirit of exploration drives us to push the limits of what humans can achieve—and perhaps one day, to take our first steps on the Red Planet.

## 选项

A. Mars exploration is both a scientific adventure and a subject of debate about our priorities as a species.
B. Rovers and landers have provided valuable data that shapes our plans for future human missions.
C. The debate over Mars missions often centers on whether we should focus on solving problems on Earth first.
D. These challenges have led to innovations that could benefit both space travel and life on Earth.
E. The dream of stepping on Mars keeps inspiring new generations of scientists, engineers, and dreamers.
F. For example, NASA's Perseverance rover found signs that water once flowed on the Martian surface.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前从古人对火星的想象过渡到当今探索，空后说"raises both excitement and important questions"。A的"both a scientific adventure and a subject of debate"准确概括兴奋与争议并存，与"important questions"呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前列举机器人任务（拍照、取样、寻找水与生命迹象），空后说"Each new mission brings better technology and new discoveries"。B的"Rovers and landers have provided valuable data"与前文对应，"shapes our plans for future human missions"引出后文对人类火星任务的理解。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前列举探索火星的不同动机，空后说"requires huge amounts of money... not everyone agrees that it is worth the cost"。C的"debate... whether we should focus on solving problems on Earth first"与成本争议、优先顺序相符，作为该段争议焦点的概括。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前描述载人火星任务的困难，空后说科学家在研发新飞船、生命支持系统等。D的"These challenges have led to innovations"承接前文困难，"benefit both space travel and life on Earth"概括研发的双重价值，承上启下。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说各国合作、探索继续推进，空后无。E的"The dream of stepping on Mars"与"first steps on the Red Planet"呼应，"inspiring new generations"升华主题，适合作全文收束。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, NASA's Perseverance rover found signs that water once flowed on the Martian surface.**：以For example开头，与火星探索相关，但属具体任务举例，各空均为概括或过渡句；易误放[2]，但[2]需的是对机器人探索整体作用的概括，非单一任务案例。
