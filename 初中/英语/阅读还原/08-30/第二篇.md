**Being a Responsible Person**

Responsibility means doing what you are supposed to do and accepting the results of your actions. It is a quality that grows as we grow—from finishing homework as a child to taking care of our work and family as adults. [1] Learning to be responsible early in life helps us build habits that will benefit us in school, at work, and in all our relationships.

At school, responsibility shows in many ways. It means arriving on time, completing assignments, and being prepared for class. [2] When we take responsibility for our own learning, we stop blaming others for our mistakes and start looking for ways to improve ourselves.

At home, responsibility might mean doing chores, helping family members, or keeping our promises. Small acts like making your bed or doing the dishes add up. [3] These habits show that we care about our family and that we are willing to contribute to the household, which strengthens bonds and builds trust.

Responsibility also means thinking about how our actions affect others. Before we speak or act, we can ask ourselves: Will this harm anyone? Am I keeping my word? [4] Pausing to consider the impact of our choices helps us make better decisions and become people others can depend on.

Being responsible is not always easy. We may feel tired or tempted to take shortcuts. [5] Yet when we choose to do the right thing even when it is hard, we build a strong character that earns respect and opens doors to greater opportunities in life.

## 选项

A. The sooner we develop this habit, the more confident and capable we become in facing life's challenges.
B. Students who take ownership of their education tend to perform better and feel more motivated.
C. Sharing the work at home teaches us cooperation and shows that everyone has a role to play.
D. Reflecting on our choices before we act is a key step toward becoming more responsible.
E. Choosing responsibility over convenience is what defines true maturity and integrity.
F. For example, if you say you will help a friend with their project, you should follow through.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前定义责任、说从童年到成年的发展，空后强调"Learning to be responsible early... helps us build habits"。A的"The sooner we develop this habit"与"early"对应，"more confident and capable"与"benefit us"一致，概括早期培养责任心的价值。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前列举学校责任表现，空后说"take responsibility for our own learning... stop blaming others... start looking for ways to improve"。B的"take ownership of their education"与"take responsibility"同义，"perform better and feel more motivated"与"improve ourselves"呼应，概括该段主旨。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说做家务、帮忙、守信，空后说"These habits show... we care... willing to contribute... strengthens bonds"。C的"Sharing the work at home"与做家务对应，"teaches cooperation... everyone has a role"与"contribute to the household"一致，概括家庭责任的意义。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前提出自问（会伤害人吗？守信吗？），空后说"Pausing to consider... helps us make better decisions"。D的"Reflecting on our choices before we act"与"Pausing to consider"对应，"key step toward becoming more responsible"概括该段主旨。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说负责任不容易、代价大，空后强调"when we choose to do the right thing even when it is hard"。E的"Choosing responsibility over convenience"与"tempted to take shortcuts"对照，"defines true maturity and integrity"升华主题，适合作结尾。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, if you say you will help a friend with their project, you should follow through.**：以For example开头，与守信相关，但属具体举例，各空均为概括或过渡句；易误放[3]或[4]，但两空分别需家庭责任与反思选择的概括，非举例。
