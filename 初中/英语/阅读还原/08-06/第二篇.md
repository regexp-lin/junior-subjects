**How to Build Confidence**

Confidence is the belief in your own abilities. It helps you take risks, speak up, and pursue your goals. Yet many people struggle with self-doubt, especially during their teenage years when they are still figuring out who they are. The good news is that confidence is not something you are born with—it is a skill you can develop. [1] Building confidence takes time and practice, but with the right mindset and habits, anyone can grow. Here are some strategies that can help you feel more confident in yourself.

Start by setting small, achievable goals. When you succeed at something, even something minor, your brain gets a positive signal. [2] Each small win reinforces the idea that you are capable. Over time, these experiences add up and change how you see yourself. Instead of aiming for perfection right away, focus on making progress. A student who improves from a C to a B in math has taken a real step forward, and that deserves recognition.

Another powerful tool is positive self-talk. The way you talk to yourself matters. If you constantly tell yourself "I can't do it" or "I'm not good enough," you will start to believe it. [3] Replacing negative thoughts with encouraging ones can slowly shift your mindset. Try saying "I'm learning" instead of "I'm failing," or "I'll try again" instead of "I give up." These small changes in language can have a big impact on how you feel and behave.

It also helps to step outside your comfort zone. When you try new things—joining a club, speaking in class, or learning a new skill—you prove to yourself that you can handle challenges. [4] Even if you do not succeed at first, the act of trying builds courage and resilience. Failure is part of learning; what matters is that you do not let fear stop you from trying. Every attempt is a chance to grow.

Surround yourself with supportive people. Friends and family who believe in you can make a huge difference. [5] When you have people who encourage you and celebrate your progress, confidence grows naturally. Remember, everyone feels unsure sometimes—the key is to keep moving forward.

- **[3] C**：
