**The Formation and Effects of Volcanoes**

Volcanoes are one of the most powerful forces of nature. They form when molten rock, called magma, rises from deep inside the Earth and reaches the surface. This happens because the Earth's crust is broken into large plates that float on the hot, soft layer beneath. When these plates move, they can create cracks or weak spots. [1] Magma then pushes up through these openings, and when it erupts, we see lava, ash, and gases. Volcanoes can be found on land and under the ocean, often along the boundaries where tectonic plates meet.

Not all volcanoes are the same. Some erupt frequently and violently, while others may sleep for hundreds of years. Scientists classify volcanoes based on their shape and how they erupt. [2] Shield volcanoes, like those in Hawaii, have gentle slopes and produce slow, flowing lava. Stratovolcanoes, such as Mount Fuji in Japan, are steeper and can produce explosive eruptions with ash and rocks. Understanding these differences helps scientists predict eruptions and keep people safe.

Volcanic eruptions can have both destructive and beneficial effects. On the negative side, they can bury villages, poison the air with toxic gases, and disrupt air travel by spreading ash into the sky. [3] The 1980 eruption of Mount St. Helens in the USA and the 1991 eruption of Mount Pinatubo in the Philippines are examples of how powerful and damaging volcanoes can be. Yet volcanoes also create fertile soil. Many people choose to live near volcanoes because the land is good for farming.

Volcanic activity has also shaped our planet in important ways. Over millions of years, volcanic eruptions have built mountains and islands. [4] The Hawaiian Islands, Iceland, and much of Japan were formed by volcanic activity. Some of the heat inside the Earth comes from its formation billions of years ago, and the rest comes from radioactive elements decaying. This heat drives the movement of tectonic plates and keeps our planet geologically active.

[5] Volcanoes remind us that the Earth is a dynamic, ever-changing place. By studying them, we learn not only about our planet's history but also how to live more safely with these natural wonders.

## 选项

A. This process connects the Earth's interior with the surface we live on.
B. The type of volcano depends on the kind of magma and how it is released.
C. History has shown that volcanic eruptions can cause great loss of life and property.
D. Without volcanic activity, many of the world's most beautiful landscapes would not exist.
E. Science helps us understand, prepare for, and respect volcanic forces.
F. For example, the eruption of Pompeii in 79 AD preserved an ancient Roman city.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述板块运动形成裂缝，空后说 “Magma then pushes up through these openings...we see lava, ash, and gases”。选项 A 的 “This process connects the Earth's interior with the surface” 概括 magma 从内部到地表的过程，与 “pushes up” “erupts” 逻辑连贯，承上启下。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说 “Scientists classify volcanoes based on their shape and how they erupt”，空后举例 shield 与 stratovolcano 两种类型。选项 B 的 “The type of volcano depends on the kind of magma and how it is released” 解释分类依据，与 “shape and how they erupt” 呼应，并为后文举例做铺垫。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：本段讲火山破坏性，空前列举 “bury villages, poison the air...disrupt air travel”，空后举 St. Helens 与 Pinatubo 为例。选项 C 的 “History has shown that volcanic eruptions can cause great loss of life and property” 概括破坏性，与 “damaging” 和 “examples” 形成逻辑链。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：本段讲火山的建设性作用，空前说 “built mountains and islands”，空后举例 “Hawaiian Islands, Iceland, much of Japan”。选项 D 的 “Without volcanic activity, many...landscapes would not exist” 从反面强调火山的塑造作用，与举例完全匹配。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：结尾段总结，空前说 “Volcanoes remind us...dynamic, ever-changing...By studying them...learn...how to live more safely”。选项 E 的 “Science helps us understand, prepare for, and respect volcanic forces” 与 “studying” “live more safely” 对应，收尾恰当。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the eruption of Pompeii in 79 AD preserved an ancient Roman city.**：庞贝案例体现火山“保存”遗迹的独特效果，与 [3] 段强调的“破坏”相反。若放在 [3] 会与 “destructive” 主题冲突；放在 [4] 则与 “built mountains and islands” 的塑造作用不符，属主题错位，易造成误选。
