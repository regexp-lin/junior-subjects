# 06-21 第二篇

**Campus Reading Corners: A Space for Every Reader**

Many schools now set up small reading areas in hallways, corners, or empty classrooms. These "reading corners" offer a quiet place for students to enjoy books during breaks or after class. [1] In other words, reading corners make books more accessible and encourage students to read for pleasure, not just for exams. Studies show that when students have easy access to books and a comfortable place to read, they are more likely to develop a lifelong reading habit.

Reading corners are usually simple to set up. [2] A few shelves of books, some chairs or bean bags（豆袋椅）, and good lighting are enough to create a welcoming space. Teachers and students can donate books they have finished reading—storybooks, magazines, comic books, or non-fiction. Some schools add plants or posters to make the corner feel cozy. The key is to keep the space inviting so that students want to sit down and open a book. No expensive equipment is needed; even a small corner can make a big difference.

Reading corners help students discover new interests and reduce stress. [3] When students choose what to read by themselves, they often find topics they enjoy—sports, science, adventure, or art. Reading for fun can also help them relax during busy school days. Many students say that spending a few minutes in the reading corner between classes helps them feel calmer and more focused. Some reading corners offer both Chinese and English books, which supports language learning in a relaxed way.

Teachers and student volunteers play an important role. [4] They can organise book swaps, recommend new titles, or run short reading clubs. Some volunteers help younger students pick books that suit their level and interests. By sharing their favourite books and talking about what they read, they create a culture of reading in the school. When older students read alongside younger ones, everyone benefits—the school becomes a community of readers.

[5] Reading corners may seem small, but they can have a big impact. They turn empty spaces into places where students can explore, relax, and grow a love of reading. The next time you pass a reading corner at school, why not stop, pick up a book, and see where it takes you?

## 选项

A. In other words, reading corners make books more accessible and encourage students to read for pleasure, not just for exams.
B. A few shelves of books, some chairs or bean bags, and good lighting are enough to create a welcoming space.
C. When students choose what to read by themselves, they discover new interests and can relax during busy days.
D. They can organise book swaps, recommend new titles, or run short reading clubs.
E. Reading corners may seem small, but they can have a big impact on students' reading habits.
F. For example, some famous writers started their love of reading in school libraries.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：概括校园读书角的目的与价值。空前描述"Many schools set up small reading areas in hallways, corners, empty classrooms...offer quiet place for students to enjoy books during breaks or after class"，空后说明"Studies show when students have easy access to books and comfortable place to read...more likely to develop lifelong reading habit"。A选项"In other words, reading corners make books more accessible and encourage students to read for pleasure, not just for exams"（换言之，读书角使书籍更容易获取，并鼓励学生为乐趣而读书，而不仅仅是为了考试）中"In other words"概括空前现象，"more accessible...read for pleasure"与空后"easy access...lifelong reading habit"精准呼应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：读书角的简单布置方式。空前提出"Reading corners are usually simple to set up"，空后详述"A few shelves of books, some chairs or bean bags, good lighting...enough to create welcoming space...teachers and students donate books...storybooks, magazines, comics, non-fiction...plants or posters...cozy...keep space inviting...No expensive equipment needed"。B选项"A few shelves of books, some chairs or bean bags, and good lighting are enough to create a welcoming space"（几架书、几把椅子或豆袋椅、良好的照明就足以营造一个温馨的空间）中"shelves, chairs, bean bags, lighting...welcoming space"与空后列举完美对应，直接承接"simple to set up"，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：读书角帮助学生发现兴趣与缓解压力。空前提出"Reading corners help students discover new interests and reduce stress"，空后列举"When students choose what to read by themselves...find topics they enjoy—sports, science, adventure, art...Reading for fun help relax during busy school days...spending few minutes in reading corner...feel calmer, more focused...Chinese and English books...language learning in relaxed way"。C选项"When students choose what to read by themselves, they discover new interests and can relax during busy days"（当学生自主选书时，他们会发现新兴趣，并能在繁忙的日子里放松）中"choose by themselves...discover new interests...relax during busy days"与空后 choose、find topics、relax、calmer 精准对应，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：教师与学生志愿者在读书角中的作用。空前提出"Teachers and student volunteers play an important role"，空后描述"They can organise book swaps, recommend new titles, run short reading clubs...volunteers help younger students pick books suit level and interests...sharing favourite books, talking about what read...create culture of reading...older students read alongside younger...everyone benefits...community of readers"。D选项"They can organise book swaps, recommend new titles, or run short reading clubs"（他们可以组织图书交换、推荐新书或开展短期读书会）中"They"指代 teachers and volunteers，"organise book swaps, recommend titles, run reading clubs"与空后首句"They can organise..."精准对应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与读书角的影响力。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后为"They turn empty spaces into places where students can explore, relax, and grow a love of reading. The next time you pass a reading corner at school, why not stop, pick up a book, and see where it takes you?"。E选项"Reading corners may seem small, but they can have a big impact on students' reading habits"（读书角看似很小，却能对学生的阅读习惯产生很大影响）用"may seem small, but...big impact"总结全文，"big impact...reading habits"与空后"turn empty spaces into...explore, relax, grow love of reading"形成呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, some famous writers started their love of reading in school libraries.**：涉及著名作家在图书馆开始热爱阅读，易被误填[1]或[3]。但F以"For example"引出名人案例，而空[1]讨论的是读书角的普遍价值（便捷、激发阅读兴趣），F的"famous writers...school libraries"属于个别成功案例，与"reading corners...accessible...read for pleasure"的校园读书角焦点不同；空[3]讨论学生自主选书、发现兴趣、放松减压，F的作家与"choose by themselves...discover interests...relax"的论述层次不符。F属"主题相关但论述层次错位"型干扰。
