# 09-12 第二篇

**Adapting to Climate Change**

The Earth's climate is changing. Temperatures are rising, sea levels are inching up, and extreme weather—heatwaves, floods, and storms—is becoming more common. While we must work to reduce greenhouse gas emissions, we also need to prepare for changes that are already happening. [1] Adaptation means finding ways to live with a changing climate, protecting people, nature, and economies from its worst effects.

For farmers, adaptation might mean growing different crops that can handle drought or flood. Some regions are building stronger levees to hold back rising rivers; others are restoring wetlands to absorb storm water. [2] These practical measures can make communities more resilient and reduce the damage when disasters strike.

Cities face special challenges. Pavement and buildings absorb heat, making urban areas hotter than the countryside. Planting trees, creating parks, and painting roofs white can lower temperatures. [3] Smart urban design that takes climate into account can make cities safer and more comfortable for millions of people.

Coastal areas are especially at risk. Rising seas threaten homes, roads, and fresh water supplies. Some communities may need to move inland; others may build higher seawalls or protect mangrove forests that slow storm surges. [4] Preparing for sea-level rise now can save lives and property later, even as the full extent of change remains uncertain.

Adaptation alone will not solve climate change. We must still cut emissions to slow future warming. But adaptation buys us time and reduces harm. [5] Investing in both—reducing the problem and preparing for its effects—gives us the best chance of a safe and stable future for all.

## 选项

A. Learning to adjust to new conditions is essential for survival and prosperity in a warming world.
B. Actions taken today can lessen the impact of future weather events and protect livelihoods.
C. Planning for a hotter, wetter, or stormier future can turn cities into better places to live.
D. Starting to adapt before the worst arrives is wiser than waiting until damage is done.
E. Combining efforts to slow climate change with efforts to adapt to it offers the most hope.
F. For example, the Netherlands has built elaborate systems of dikes and pumps to protect against flooding.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述气候变化现状及减排放与适应并行的必要性，空后定义"Adaptation means finding ways to live with a changing climate, protecting people, nature, and economies"。A的"Learning to adjust to new conditions is essential for survival and prosperity in a warming world"概括适应的核心意义，与"live with"和"protecting"完美呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前列举农业、堤坝、湿地等适应措施，空后说"These practical measures can make communities more resilient and reduce the damage when disasters strike"。B的"Actions taken today can lessen the impact of future weather events and protect livelihoods"与提前采取行动、减少灾害影响的逻辑一致，概括该段主旨。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前讨论城市的 heat island 及应对（植树、公园、白屋顶），空后说"Smart urban design that takes climate into account can make cities safer and more comfortable"。C的"Planning for a hotter, wetter, or stormier future can turn cities into better places to live"准确概括气候导向的城市规划，与"safer and more comfortable"呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前描述沿海风险及应对（迁居、海堤、红树林），空后说"Preparing for sea-level rise now can save lives and property later, even as the full extent of change remains uncertain"。D的"Starting to adapt before the worst arrives is wiser than waiting until damage is done"与提前准备、减少损失的主题对应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前强调适应与减排需并重，空后总结"Investing in both—reducing the problem and preparing for its effects—gives us the best chance"。E的"Combining efforts to slow climate change with efforts to adapt to it offers the most hope"升华主题，与" Investing in both"完美对应，适合作全文收束。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the Netherlands has built elaborate systems of dikes and pumps to protect against flooding.**：以For example开头，属具体国家案例；各空均为概括或过渡句，F与沿海适应相关但为个案，易误放[2]或[4]，但两空分别需农业与社区措施概括、沿海准备概括，非举例。
