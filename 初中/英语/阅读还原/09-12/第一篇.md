# 09-12 第一篇

**The Value of Traditional Crafts**

Long before factories and machines, people made things by hand—pottery, textiles, woodwork, and more. These crafts were passed down from generation to generation, each family adding its own style and skill. Today, many of these arts are fading as fewer young people learn them and cheap factory goods replace handmade items. [1] Preserving traditional crafts is not just about keeping old techniques alive; it is about honoring our cultural heritage and the human touch that machines cannot replace.

Traditional crafts often tell stories. A weaving pattern might show the history of a village; a pottery style might reflect the soil and climate of a region. Learning these skills connects us to our ancestors and to the land we come from. [2] These arts carry meaning that goes beyond their practical use, linking the past to the present and giving us a sense of identity and belonging.

Yet craftspeople face real challenges. Materials can be expensive, and handmade goods take time to produce. Many young people choose faster, more profitable careers. [3] Supporting artisans through fair prices, workshops, and cultural programs can help ensure that these skills survive and even thrive in the modern world.

Tourists and collectors often value handmade items for their uniqueness. When we buy from local artisans instead of mass-produced goods, we support both the economy and the culture of a place. [4] Choosing handcrafted products is one way to vote with our wallets for a world that values quality, tradition, and human creativity.

The future of traditional crafts depends on the choices we make today. Schools can teach craft skills; governments can fund cultural projects; and each of us can appreciate and buy handmade when we can. [5] The effort to keep these arts alive is an investment in our shared history and in a richer, more diverse world for generations to come.

## 选项

A. Saving these skills from disappearance matters for culture, identity, and the richness of human creativity.
B. Each piece holds layers of meaning that connect the maker, the buyer, and the community to a larger story.
C. Without such support, many crafts could vanish within a generation, leaving only photographs and memories.
D. Every purchase we make sends a message about what we value and what kind of world we want to build.
E. Small steps—by individuals, schools, and societies—can make a real difference in whether these arts continue.
F. For example, Japanese kimono weaving has been practiced for over a thousand years and is still taught in some schools today.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述传统手工艺的传承危机，空后强调"Preserving traditional crafts is not just about keeping old techniques alive; it is about honoring our cultural heritage"。A的"Saving these skills from disappearance matters for culture, identity, and the richness of human creativity"概括保护传统工艺的意义，与"cultural heritage"和"human touch"完美呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说传统工艺承载故事、图案与地域历史，空后强调"These arts carry meaning that goes beyond their practical use, linking the past to the present"。B的"Each piece holds layers of meaning that connect the maker, the buyer, and the community to a larger story"与意义、联结、认同的逻辑一致，概括该段主旨。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前列举手工艺人面临的困难（成本、时间、青年择业），空后说"Supporting artisans through fair prices, workshops, and cultural programs can help ensure that these skills survive"。C的"Without such support, many crafts could vanish within a generation"从反面强调支持的必要性，与"survive and even thrive"呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前讨论购买手工艺品的意义，空后说"Choosing handcrafted products is one way to vote with our wallets for a world that values quality, tradition, and human creativity"。D的"Every purchase we make sends a message about what we value and what kind of world we want to build"准确概括消费选择的意义，与"vote with our wallets"对应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前列举各方可采取的行动，空后强调"The effort to keep these arts alive is an investment in our shared history"。E的"Small steps—by individuals, schools, and societies—can make a real difference in whether these arts continue"升华主题，与"choices we make today"和"investment"完美对应，适合作全文收束。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, Japanese kimono weaving has been practiced for over a thousand years and is still taught in some schools today.**：以For example开头，属具体工艺举例；各空均为概括或过渡句，F与传承相关但为个案，易误放[3]或[5]，但两空分别需支持必要性概括与多方行动升华，非举例。
