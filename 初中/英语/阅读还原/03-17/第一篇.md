# 03-17 第一篇

**The Reality of Being a Professional Gamer**

Playing video games used to be just a fun hobby for teenagers. Today, however, "e-sports" (电子竞技) has grown into a billion-dollar global industry. [1] They travel the world, play in huge stadiums, and entertain millions of fans online.

For many young people, playing games for a living sounds like the perfect dream job. It seems like all you have to do is sit in a comfortable chair and have fun. [2] In reality, being a professional gamer is incredibly stressful and requires intense hard work.

First of all, reaching the top level demands an unbelievable amount of practice. A professional player usually trains for up to 10 or 12 hours every single day. [3] They must constantly study new strategies, analyze their opponents' moves, and practice their quick reactions until they are perfect.

Furthermore, the physical demanding of e-sports is often ignored by the public. [4] This leads to dangerous health problems like serious back pain, weak eyesight, and terrible wrist injuries (手腕受伤). Many players are forced to retire in their mid-twenties because their bodies can no longer handle the pressure.

[5] While it can be an exciting career for a talented few, it is definitely not just a simple game. Anyone hoping to become a pro must be prepared for the extreme challenges ahead.

## 选项

A. Besides practicing hard, they also need to pass difficult math exams to join a team.
B. Sitting in exactly the same position for hours without moving is terrible for the body.
C. Therefore, parents should strongly encourage their children to play more video games.
D. But the truth behind the bright lights and big prize money is very different.
E. Top professional players can now earn as much money as famous basketball stars.
F. It is not just about happily playing the game over and over again.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] E   [2] D   [3] F   [4] B   [5] (Wait, need a concluding transition)

# 03-17 第一篇

**The Reality of Being a Professional Gamer**

Playing video games used to be just a fun hobby for teenagers. Today, however, "e-sports" (电子竞技) has grown into a billion-dollar global industry. [1] They travel the world, play in huge stadiums, and entertain millions of fans online.

For many young people, playing games for a living sounds like the perfect dream job. It seems like all you have to do is sit in a comfortable chair and have fun. [2] In reality, being a professional gamer is incredibly stressful and requires intense hard work.

First of all, reaching the top level demands an unbelievable amount of practice. A professional player usually trains for up to 10 or 12 hours every single day. [3] They must constantly study new strategies, analyze their opponents' moves, and practice their quick reactions until they are perfect.

Furthermore, the physical demanding of e-sports is often ignored by the public. [4] This leads to dangerous health problems like serious back pain, weak eyesight, and terrible wrist injuries (手腕受伤). Many players are forced to retire in their mid-twenties because their bodies can no longer handle the pressure.

[5] While it can be an exciting career for a talented few, it is definitely not just a simple game. Anyone hoping to become a pro must be prepared for the extreme challenges ahead.

## 选项

A. Besides practicing hard, they also need to pass difficult school exams to join a team.
B. Sitting in exactly the same position for hours without moving is terrible for the body.
C. Ultimately, becoming an e-sports champion takes huge sacrifices (牺牲).
D. But the truth behind the bright lights and big prize money is very different.
E. Top professional players can now earn as much money as famous traditional athletes.
F. It is not just about happily playing the game over and over again.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] E   [2] D   [3] F   [4] B   [5] C

## 深度解析

- **[1] E**：
  - **线索**：代词指代与逻辑递进。空前说电子竞技已经成长为十亿美元的全球产业。E选项“Top professional players can now earn as much money as famous traditional athletes”（顶尖的职业选手现在能赚和著名的传统运动员一样多的钱）是用具体的“挣钱多”来进一步说明前文提到的“十亿美元产业（很赚钱）”。更关键的是，空后一句是以代词“They（他们）”开头：“他们环游世界在巨大体育场比赛”，“They”完美指代了E选项中的“Top professional players（顶尖职业选手）”。
- **[2] D**：
  - **线索**：强转折与语境呼应。空前描述了年轻人的幻想：“看起来你要做的就是坐在舒服的椅子上享受乐趣，完美梦想工作”。D选项“But the truth behind the bright lights and big prize money is very different”（但在明亮的聚光灯和巨额奖金背后的真相却截然不同）运用了强烈的“But”转折。这打破了前面描述的幻想，并非常自然地引出了空后的核心论点句：“In reality（实际上），成为一名职业玩家是不可思议地充满压力的，并且需要极大努力工作”。
- **[3] F**：
  - **线索**：概念澄清与动作并列。空前指出了职业选手每天要训练10-12小时。F选项“It is not just about happily playing the game over and over again”（这不仅仅是一遍又一遍开心地打游戏）纠正了普通人对“训练长达12小时”产生的误解。随后空后用“他们必须不断研究新策略、分析对手并练习反应......”等一串排比句，具体说明了他们那12个小时究竟在干什么（动脑筋而不是傻玩），前后逻辑连贯。
- **[4] B**：
  - **线索**：因果衔接与细节对应。空前抛出了段落主旨：电子竞技对身体的要求（the physical demanding）常被公众忽视。B选项“Sitting in exactly the same position for hours without moving is terrible for the body”（几个小时坐在完全相同的位置一动不动对身体很糟糕）正是对“身体要求”的具象化说明。而这种久坐不动的行为，直接引发了空后以“This leads to...（这导致了...）”开头的各种生理疾病（背痛、视力差、手腕受伤）。
- **[5] C**：
  - **线索**：尾段总结与情感定调。空[5]位于结论段段首。在前面揭露了电竞需要地狱般强度的训练和带来严重的身体伤病之后，C选项“Ultimately, becoming an e-sports champion takes huge sacrifices”（最终，成为电子竞技冠军需要巨大的牺牲）作为终极总结登场。其中“sacrifices（牺牲）”概括了前文所述的努力和病痛。这也引出后文“对于天才来说让人兴奋，但绝非简单游戏，想成为职业选手必须为极端挑战做好准备”的客观寄语。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **A. Besides practicing hard, they also need to pass difficult school exams to join a team.**：该项包含了“practicing hard”和“join a team”这些打擦边球的话题词，有些学生可能会考虑放入第三段。但句子核心意思是“除了刻苦练习，他们加入战队还需要通过困难的学校考试”。这种说法完全不符合社会常识：职业电竞选手的选拔完全看游戏技术和反应，并不跟学校文化考试挂钩。属于完全脱离现实认知的常识漏洞型干扰。
