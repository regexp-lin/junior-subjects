# 03-17 第二篇

**The Mystery of the Bermuda Triangle**

For nearly a century, the Bermuda Triangle (百慕大三角) has been one of the most famous mysteries in the world. Located in the western part of the North Atlantic Ocean, it covers a huge area of dark, deep water. [1] It is famous because dozens of ships and airplanes have supposedly disappeared there without a single trace.

The mysterious stories began around the 1950s when reporters started writing about missing planes. The most famous case was "Flight 19," a group of five US Navy airplanes. [2] No bodies or parts of the airplanes were ever found, leaving the whole world completely shocked. 

Many wild theories have been suggested to explain these strange events. [3] Others think that sea monsters or even time machines are hiding under the deep water. These exciting stories have made the Bermuda Triangle a popular subject for countless movies and books.

However, modern scientists have a much more boring but logical explanation. [4] This kind of extreme weather can easily sink small boats or bring down airplanes in minutes. Furthermore, the ocean floor in that area is covered with deep canyons (峡谷), which makes it almost impossible to find sunken ships.

[5] According to global records, ships and planes do not actually disappear there any more frequently than in any other heavily-travelled ocean in the world. The mystery was largely created by people who love telling a good story.

## 选项

A. Some people believe that aliens from outer space kidnapped the missing pilots.
B. They point out that the area is famous for its sudden and violent tropical storms (热带风暴).
C. In reality, the Bermuda Triangle is not as dangerous as the legends suggest.
D. Therefore, all modern airplane pilots absolutely refuse to fly over that area today.
E. It is roughly shaped like a triangle connecting Miami, Bermuda, and Puerto Rico.
F. They were on a simple training mission in 1945 but mysteriously never returned home.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] E   [2] F   [3] A   [4] B   [5] C

## 深度解析

- **[1] E**：
  - **线索**：指代归属与地理位置描述。空前提到前一句“位于北大西洋西部的百慕大三角（Bermuda Triangle），覆盖了一大片又黑又深的水域”。E选项“It is roughly shaped like a triangle connecting Miami, Bermuda, and Puerto Rico”（它大致呈连接迈阿密、百慕大和波多黎各的三角形形状）中的“It”完美指代了前面的“Bermuda Triangle（百慕大三角）”，并且进一步补充说明了其具体的地理边界。然后空后顺畅地讲述了它为何闻名（因为船机消失）。
- **[2] F**：
  - **线索**：具体举例与时间顺承。空前指出了一个最著名的例子——“第19次航班（Flight 19），这是一组五架美国海军飞机”。F选项“They were on a simple training mission in 1945 but mysteriously never returned home”（它们在1945年执行一项简单的训练任务，但神秘地再也没有回家）中，“They”极好地指代了“five US Navy airplanes”。并且讲述了它们去了没回的过程，为后面空后那句“再也没找到尸体和零件，震惊世界”的情境做了直接铺路。
- **[3] A**：
  - **线索**：并列结构与语义照应。空前提出了段落总括句：为了解释这些奇怪事件，产生了很多“wild theories（疯狂的/荒诞的理论）”。A选项“Some people believe that aliens from outer space kidnapped the missing pilots”（有些人相信来自外太空的外星人绑架了失踪的飞行员）就是这些“疯狂理论”的典型例子之一。同时，A项的“Some people believe...（有些人相信）”与空后的“Others think that...（另一些人认为海怪或时间机器）”构成了完美的排比列举结构。
- **[4] B**：
  - **线索**：代词指代与逻辑铺垫。空前提到“modern scientists have a much more boring but logical explanation（现代科学家有一个更加无聊但合乎逻辑的解释）”。B选项“They point out that the area is famous for its sudden and violent tropical storms”（他们指出了该地区以突发猛烈的热带风暴而闻名）中，“They”指代的就是前一句提到的“modern scientists”。另外，B选项中抛出的“violent tropical storms（猛烈的热带风暴）”，完美契合了空后一句主语定语“This kind of extreme weather（这种极端天气）”。
- **[5] C**：
  - **线索**：总论定调与前后呼应。空[5]位于文章的最后一段开头。在讲述完科学解释后，作者要进行盖棺定论。C选项“In reality, the Bermuda Triangle is not as dangerous as the legends suggest”（实际上，百慕大三角并不像传说中表明的那样危险）是对全文最终的定性。它顺畅地引出了空后的核心证据链：“根据全球记录，那里的飞机船只失踪频率并不比世界上任何其他繁忙海域更高（即：并不更加危险）。这个谜团很大程度上是因为人们爱讲故事造出来的。”

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **D. Therefore, all modern airplane pilots absolutely refuse to fly over that area today.**：此项中除了“Therefore”诱导外，还带有“pilots”、“fly”以及“that area”等核心词定语。但表述的内容十分武断：“因此，所有现代飞机飞行员今天绝对拒绝飞越那个区域。”只要稍有常识并读完最后一段就能明白，其实那个区域是“heavily-travelled ocean（通行繁忙的海域）”，根本就不比别的地方危险，每天都有不计其数的航班经过。该选项属于由于过度渲染恐怖气氛而完全违背事实和全文最终主旨的干扰项。
