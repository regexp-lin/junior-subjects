**Urban Noise Pollution**

City life comes with many benefits: jobs, entertainment, and access to services. But cities are also loud. Traffic, construction, sirens, and crowds create a constant background of noise. For some people, this noise is more than annoying—it can harm health and well-being. [1] Unlike air or water pollution, noise is invisible and often accepted as a normal part of city life, but its effects should not be ignored.

Loud noise can disrupt sleep, even when we do not fully wake up. Our bodies need quiet, restful sleep to repair and recharge. Studies show that people who live near busy roads or airports often sleep worse than those in quieter areas. [2] The consequences go beyond feeling tired—research has linked long-term noise exposure to higher rates of anxiety, high blood pressure, and even cognitive decline in older adults.

Noise also affects learning and productivity. Students in noisy classrooms have more trouble concentrating than those in quiet ones. Workers in open offices may find it harder to focus. [3] For this reason, architects and city planners are increasingly paying attention to acoustic design when building new schools, hospitals, and offices.

What can be done? Cities can plant trees along busy streets to absorb sound. Buildings can be designed with better insulation. Laws can limit noisy activities at night. [4] Small changes, such as keeping windows closed during rush hour or using white noise machines to mask traffic sounds, can also make a difference at home.

Individuals can also take steps to protect themselves. Wearing earplugs at night, choosing a bedroom away from the street, and reducing the volume of devices can all help. [5] The goal is not to make cities silent but to create spaces where people can rest, work, and learn without constant noise taking a toll on their health and quality of life.

C. Reducing background noise in schools and workplaces can therefore improve performance and well-being for many people.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述城市噪音来源及对健康的潜在危害，空后说"Unlike air or water pollution, noise is invisible and often accepted as a normal part of city life"。A的"Noise pollution is often overlooked"与空后"often accepted as normal"呼应，"affects millions... sleep problems, stress, hearing loss"概括影响。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说噪音干扰睡眠、靠近繁忙道路的人睡眠更差，空后说"The consequences go beyond feeling tired—research has linked long-term noise exposure to... anxiety, high blood pressure..."。B的"Over time, poor sleep can weaken the immune system, affect concentration"与空后健康后果对应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说噪音影响学习与工作效率，空后说"For this reason, architects and city planners are increasingly paying attention to acoustic design"。C的"Reducing background noise in schools and workplaces... improve performance"与空后设计关注点衔接。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前列举城市可采取的措施，空后说"Small changes, such as keeping windows closed... or using white noise machines... can also make a difference at home"。D的"Each of these measures, from urban planning to personal habits"与空后"Small changes... at home"呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说个人可采取的防护措施，空后说"The goal is not to make cities silent but to create spaces where people can rest, work, and learn..."。E的"balance the excitement of urban life with the need for peace and quiet"与空后"not... silent but... spaces where people can rest"衔接。
