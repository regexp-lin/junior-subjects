# 03-13 第一篇

**The Microscopic World of Bacteria**

When we hear the word "bacteria" (细菌), most of us immediately think of diseases and dirty places. [1] However, this common belief is actually far from the whole truth. In reality, the vast majority of bacteria are either harmless or highly beneficial to humans.

In fact, human bodies are home to trillions of these tiny living things. [2] For example, the bacteria living in our stomachs help us break down food and absorb important nutrients. Without them, our digestive system simply could not work properly. 

These microscopic helpers also play a necessary role in the environment. [3] They break down dead plants and animals, turning them into rich soil. This natural process allows new seeds to grow and keeps the earth's ecosystem in perfect balance.

Furthermore, scientists have discovered amazing ways to use bacteria in modern industry. [4] Some special types are even being used to clean up harmful oil spills in the ocean. They literally "eat" the dangerous chemicals and turn them into safe substances.

[5] So, the next time you hear about bacteria, don't just think about sickness. Remember that these invisible little workers are essential for keeping us and our planet alive!

## 选项

A. They are used to make everyday foods like cheese, yogurt, and soy sauce.
B. However, some dangerous bacteria still spread quickly from person to person.
C. In short, human life would be completely impossible without their help.
D. Deep in the forest, soil bacteria act as the earth's natural recycling system.
E. We are taught from a young age to wash our hands to get rid of these "bad germs".
F. We depend on them every day to keep our bodies healthy and strong.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] E   [2] F   [3] D   [4] A   [5] C

## 深度解析

- **[1] E**：
  - **线索**：顺承语境与观点递进。首句提到我们一听到“细菌”就想到疾病和脏地方。E选项“We are taught from a young age to wash our hands to get rid of these 'bad germs'”（我们从小就被教导要洗手以除掉这些“坏病菌”），这正是对“细菌=病菌”这一刻板印象的进一步说明和深入。此句的铺陈使得紧接其后的转折句“However, this common belief is actually far from the whole truth”（然而，这个普遍的信念其实远非全部真相）显得格外有说服力。
- **[2] F**：
  - **线索**：代词指代与主旨句奠定。空前指出了“人体是数万亿这些微小生物的家园”。F选项“We depend on them every day to keep our bodies healthy and strong”（我们每天都依赖它们来保持身体健康强壮）是对这些细菌正面作用的肯定，充当了本段的主旨句。“them”指代前一句的“tiny living things”。随后，空后用“For example（例如）”具体列举了胃部细菌如何帮助消化从而使身体健康的例子，证明了F项的观点。
- **[3] D**：
  - **线索**：环境呼应与拟人比喻。空前指出了段落主旨：这些微小的帮手在**环境**中也扮演着必要角色。D选项“Deep in the forest, soil bacteria act as the earth's natural recycling system”（在森林深处，土壤细菌充当着地球的自然回收系统）很好地展开了“环境”这一概念（具体到了森林和土壤）。随后解释了它们是如何“回收”的：“They break down dead plants and animals, turning them into rich soil（它们分解死去的动植物，把它们变成肥沃的土壤）”。
- **[4] A**：
  - **线索**：同义解释与语境归属。空前指出科学家在“modern industry（现代工业）”中使用了细菌。A选项“They are used to make everyday foods like cheese, yogurt, and soy sauce”（它们被用来制作奶酪、酸奶和酱油等日常食品）就是“现代（食品）工业”里利用细菌最贴近生活的极佳例子。随后提到另一种特殊类型的工业应用（清理石油泄漏），这构成了两个工业使用并列的生动例证。
- **[5] C**：
  - **线索**：总结全文与结语升华。空[5]位于文章的尾段段首位置，具有很强的归纳性。C选项中“In short”（简言之）标志着对上文的总结。“human life would be completely impossible without their help”（没有它们的帮助，人类生活将是完全不可能的）高度概括了前面罗列的关于健康、环境和工业的各种好处。也极其自然地引出了后文的劝告：“所以，下次听到细菌，别光想到生病”。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **B. However, some dangerous bacteria still spread quickly from person to person.**：该选项中包含“bacteria（细菌）”和“dangerous（危险）”等高频词汇。并且因为常识告诉我们有些细菌确实会人传人，考生如果在阅读时不注重整体语篇的情感倾向便容易选。但这篇文章整篇都在“平反”——即极力证明“绝大多数细菌是无害的或高度有益的”。放入强调危险细菌的句子与作者的核心观点“平反和正名”相悖，是一个基于生活常识设立的高级干扰项。
