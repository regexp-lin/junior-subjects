# 03-13 第二篇

**Overcoming the Fear of Public Speaking**

For many people, standing on a stage to give a speech is a terrifying experience. In fact, studies show that public speaking is often feared more than death itself. [1] However, the good news is that with the right strategies, anyone can become a confident speaker.

The first step to overcoming this fear is thorough preparation. You should know your topic inside and out before you step onto the stage. [2] This confidence will naturally calm your nerves down. Furthermore, practicing in front of a mirror or recording yourself can help you find out and correct any awkward body limits. 

It is also important to change the way you think about your audience. [3] They are actually hoping that you will succeed and deliver an interesting speech. Thinking of them as friendly listeners rather than strict judges can take a lot of pressure off your shoulders. 

Deep breathing is another magical tool for public speakers. Right before you go on stage, taking a few slow, deep breaths can lower your heart rate. [4] This extra oxygen in your brain helps you think more clearly and prevents your voice from shaking.

Lastly, do not worry about making a tiny mistake. [5] If you forget a word or stumble over a sentence, just smile, pause for a second, and keep going. Usually, the audience hardly even notices!

## 选项

A. When you feel completely prepared, you will not worry about forgetting your words.
B. Instead of seeing them as enemies, remember that people are there to learn from you.
C. This physical relaxation sends a signal to your brain that everything is perfectly safe.
D. Therefore, experts suggest avoiding any speech opportunities if you feel nervous.
E. Even the most famous and experienced speakers in the world are not perfect.
F. This intense fear can cause a pounding heart, shaking hands, and sweating (出汗).

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] F   [2] A   [3] B   [4] C   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] F**：
  - **线索**：同义解释与具体描述。空前提到一个惊人事实：研究显示公开演讲常常比死亡更让人害怕。F选项“This intense fear can cause a pounding heart, shaking hands, and sweating”（这种强烈的恐惧会导致心跳加速、双手发抖和出汗）中，“This intense fear（这种强烈恐惧）”完美指代了前一句中所说的对演讲的恐惧感（terrifying experience / feared），并且具体列举了恐惧时的生理表现。随后一句“However, the good news is...（然而好消息是...）”非常自然地形成了语气的转折。
- **[2] A**：
  - **线索**：因果衔接与指代关系。空前强调了“thorough preparation（充分准备）”并且具体要求“在登台前你要彻底了解你的主题”。A选项“When you feel completely prepared, you will not worry about forgetting your words”（当你觉得准备完全充分时，你就不会担心忘词了），直接承接了准备充分的动作带来的心理变化。更重要的是，空后第一句的“This confidence...（这种自信...）”正完美指代了A项中“不担心忘词产生的底气和自信”。
- **[3] B**：
  - **线索**：对比转折与词汇呼应。空前提出段落主旨“It is also important to change the way you think about your audience（改变你看待观众的方式也很重要）”。B选项“Instead of seeing them as enemies, remember that people are there to learn from you”（与其把他们当作敌人，记住人们在那儿是为了向你学习的），其中的“them（他们）”完美指代“audience（观众）”。随后空后继续以“They...（他们）”开头：“他们实际上正在期盼你能成功”，逻辑非常一致。
- **[4] C**：
  - **线索**：生理表现与代词呼应。空前讲的是深呼吸的作用：“...taking a few slow, deep breaths can lower your heart rate”（做几个缓慢深呼吸可以降低心率）。C选项“This physical relaxation sends a signal to your brain that everything is perfectly safe”（这种身体放松向你的大脑发送一个信号，即一切都很安全），其中“This physical relaxation”指代的正是前文“心率降低/深呼吸”带来的生理变化效果。然后紧接着的一句“This extra oxygen...（这多余的氧气）”也正是源自深呼吸，文段衔接严丝合缝。
- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：论点陈述与举例前置。空前提到了“最后，别担心犯小错误”。E选项“Even the most famous and experienced speakers in the world are not perfect”（即使是世界上最著名、最有经验的演讲者也不完美）给出了不用担心犯错的理由，即任何人都不完美，以此宽慰读者。这句话作为垫手，自然而然地引出了随后的具体支招建议：“If you forget a word... just smile... keep going（假如你忘词了……微笑继续就好）”。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **D. Therefore, experts suggest avoiding any speech opportunities if you feel nervous.**：这个选项带有“Therefore（因此）”和“experts（专家）”，看似极具权威性和总结意味。然而句子意思是“专家建议，如果你感到紧张，就避开任何演讲机会”。这与全文“克服恐惧、成为自信演讲者”的积极进取的主旋律相去甚远（属于消极逃避），逻辑上更是对第二段提出的“通过策略克服恐惧”的直接否定。属于三观偏离、立意相左的干扰项。
