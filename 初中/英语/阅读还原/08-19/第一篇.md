**The Effects of Light Pollution**

When we think of pollution, we usually think of dirty air or polluted water. But there is another kind of pollution that affects our lives every night: light pollution. [1] It refers to the excessive or misdirected artificial light that brightens the night sky and disrupts the natural rhythms of both humans and wildlife. As cities grow, this problem is getting worse.

Light pollution comes from many sources. Streetlights, billboards, office buildings, and car headlights all add to the glow. [2] In big cities, the night sky is often so bright that people can barely see the stars. Astronomers find it harder to observe the universe, and ordinary people lose the chance to enjoy the beauty of a starry night. The wonder of the cosmos feels farther away than ever.

For wildlife, light pollution can be harmful. Many animals use darkness to hunt, migrate, or rest. [3] Birds that fly at night may get confused by bright lights and crash into buildings. Sea turtle hatchlings often crawl toward streetlights instead of the moonlight on the ocean, and they may never reach the sea. When natural patterns are disturbed, entire ecosystems can suffer.

Humans are also affected. Our bodies have internal clocks that depend on darkness to know when to sleep. [4] Too much artificial light at night can disrupt our sleep and may even increase the risk of certain health problems. Studies suggest that people who live in brightly lit areas often sleep less well than those in darker neighborhoods. Restful sleep is essential for our well-being.

The good news is that light pollution can be reduced. [5] We can use shields on streetlights to direct light downward, turn off unnecessary lights at night, and choose warmer, softer lighting. Everyone can help by being mindful of outdoor lights at home. Small changes can make the night sky darker and our world healthier.
