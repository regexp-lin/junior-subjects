**How Sleep Helps Our Memory**

Students often stay up late to study for exams, but research suggests that getting enough sleep might be more important than extra hours of cramming（临时抱佛脚）. [1] In other words, sleep is not just rest—it is when the brain processes and strengthens what we have learned during the day. Scientists have found strong links between sleep quality and memory, and the findings apply to everyone from young children to adults.

During sleep, the brain does important work. [2] While we sleep, the brain sorts through the day's experiences and decides what to keep and what to forget. New information is moved from short-term memory into long-term storage. Connections between nerve cells are strengthened, especially for things we practiced or repeated. This process is sometimes called "memory consolidation"（记忆巩固）—the brain is literally organizing and filing away the events and facts of the day so we can recall them later.

Not all sleep is the same. [3] Deep sleep and REM sleep (when we dream) each play different roles. Deep sleep is especially important for storing facts and concepts—like vocabulary words or historical dates. REM sleep helps with skills and creative connections—like learning to play an instrument or solving a puzzle in a new way. A full night of sleep includes several cycles of both stages, so cutting sleep short means losing some of this important processing time.

Teenagers need more sleep than adults. [4] During adolescence, the brain is still developing, and sleep supports that growth. Studies show that students who sleep eight to nine hours perform better on tests and report feeling less stressed. On the other hand, lack of sleep leads to poor concentration, mood swings, and weaker memory. Skipping sleep to study often backfires—you may remember less, not more, because the brain never gets the chance to consolidate what you learned.

[5] Sleep is a powerful tool for learning. Instead of staying up all night before a test, try reviewing your notes and then getting a good night's rest. Your brain will work on the material while you sleep, and you will likely wake up with a clearer mind and better recall. Remember: sleep well, remember well.

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：引出睡眠对学习与记忆的积极作用。空前描述"stay up late to study...getting enough sleep might be more important than cramming"，空后说明"sleep is not just rest—it is when the brain processes and strengthens what we learned...strong links between sleep quality and memory...apply to everyone"。A选项"In other words, sleep actively supports learning and memory formation"（换言之，睡眠积极支持学习和记忆形成）中"In other words"承接空前对比，"actively supports learning and memory"与空后"processes and strengthens...strong links"精准呼应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：睡眠期间大脑的组织与存储工作。空前提出"During sleep, the brain does important work"，空后详述"sorts through day's experiences...decides what to keep and forget...New information moved from short-term to long-term...connections strengthened...memory consolidation...organizing and filing away...recall later"。B选项"That is because the brain uses sleep time to organize and store new information"（那是因为大脑利用睡眠时间组织和储存新信息）中"That is because"解释重要工作的内容，"organize and store new information"与空后 consolidation、sorting、moving、filing 完美对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：不同睡眠阶段对不同记忆类型的作用。空前提出"Not all sleep is the same"，空后列举"Deep sleep and REM sleep play different roles...Deep sleep for facts and concepts—vocabulary, dates...REM for skills and creative connections—instrument, puzzle...several cycles...cutting sleep short means losing processing time"。C选项"In fact, different stages of sleep help with different types of memory"（事实上，不同的睡眠阶段有助于不同类型的记忆）中"In fact"强化差异，"different stages...different types of memory"与空后 deep/REM、事实/技能等精准对应，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：青少年尤其需要优先保证睡眠。空前提出"Teenagers need more sleep than adults"，空后描述"During adolescence brain still developing...sleep supports growth...students who sleep eight to nine hours perform better...lack of sleep leads to poor concentration, mood swings, weaker memory...Skipping sleep to study backfires"。D选项"These findings highlight why teenagers especially should prioritize sleep"（这些发现凸显了为何青少年尤其应优先保证睡眠）中"These findings"指代空后研究结果，"teenagers especially...prioritize sleep"与空后"need more...brain developing...perform better"完美对应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与学习策略建议。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"Sleep is powerful tool for learning...Instead of staying up...review notes and get good night's rest...brain will work on material while you sleep...clearer mind and better recall...sleep well, remember well"。E选项"In short, good sleep is one of the best study strategies you can use"（简言之，良好睡眠是你可以使用的最佳学习策略之一）用"In short"总结全文，"best study strategies"与空后"powerful tool...review then sleep"形成呼应，收束精准。
