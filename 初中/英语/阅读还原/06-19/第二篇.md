# 06-19 第二篇

**Ancient Water Engineering**

Long before modern machines and technology, people found clever ways to control water. They built canals, dams, and reservoirs to water crops, prevent floods, and supply cities. Some of these projects were so well designed that they still work today, thousands of years later. [1] In other words, ancient water engineering shows the wisdom and skill of our ancestors. Studying these systems helps us understand how human society grew and how people solved problems with limited tools.

One famous example is the Dujiangyan Irrigation System in China. [2] Built over 2,200 years ago, it was designed to tame the Min River—which often flooded—and to bring water to dry farmland. Instead of building a tall dam that would block the river and cause new problems, the engineers dug a channel to redirect part of the water and used bamboo cages filled with stones to control the flow. The system still waters millions of hectares of land today and has been named a World Heritage Site. It is a model of working with nature rather than against it.

The Romans were also masters of water engineering. [3] They built long aqueducts（导水管）—stone bridges and underground tunnels—to carry fresh water from mountains and springs into cities. Some aqueducts stretched for dozens of kilometres. The water was used for drinking, bathing, and fountains. Roman aqueducts were so strong that parts of them still stand in places like Spain and France. The Romans understood that clean water was essential for health and for the growth of cities.

Ancient water projects had a huge impact on society. [4] Reliable water supply allowed farmers to grow more food, which supported larger populations. Cities could grow where water was once scarce. Trade and industry developed along canals and rivers. However, building these projects was hard: it took many workers, years of labour, and careful planning. Rulers who invested in water projects were often remembered as wise leaders because they improved the lives of their people.

[5] Ancient water engineering reminds us that people have always found creative ways to meet their needs. The canals, dams, and aqueducts of the past not only solved practical problems but also laid the foundation for the world we live in today. By studying and protecting these treasures, we honour the creativity of those who came before us.

## 选项

A. In other words, ancient water engineering shows the wisdom and skill of our ancestors.
B. It was built to control floods and irrigate farmland using channels and stone-filled cages, and still works today.
C. In fact, the Romans built long aqueducts to bring fresh water from mountains and springs into cities.
D. These projects allowed societies to grow, but required great effort and careful planning to build.
E. In short, ancient water works were creative solutions that shaped the world we live in today.
F. For example, the Nile River in Egypt has been used for irrigation for thousands of years.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：概括古代水利工程所体现的智慧。空前描述"Long before modern machines...people found clever ways to control water...canals, dams, reservoirs...water crops, prevent floods, supply cities...some so well designed still work today, thousands of years later"，空后说明"ancient water engineering shows wisdom and skill of our ancestors...Studying...understand how human society grew...solved problems with limited tools"。A选项"In other words, ancient water engineering shows the wisdom and skill of our ancestors"（换言之，古代水利工程展现了我们祖先的智慧与技艺）中"In other words"概括空前，"wisdom and skill of our ancestors"与空后"wisdom and skill of our ancestors"精准呼应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：都江堰的设计原理与现状。空前提出"One famous example is the Dujiangyan Irrigation System in China"，空后详述"Built over 2,200 years ago...tame Min River...often flooded...bring water to dry farmland...instead of tall dam...dug channel redirect part of water...bamboo cages filled with stones control flow...still waters millions of hectares today...World Heritage Site...model of working with nature"。B选项"It was built to control floods and irrigate farmland using channels and stone-filled cages, and still works today"（它被用来通过渠道和石笼控制洪水并灌溉农田，至今仍在运行）中"built to control floods...irrigate...channels...stone-filled cages...still works today"与空后 tame、floods、channel、bamboo cages、still waters today 完美对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：罗马 aqueduct 的建造与作用。空前提出"The Romans were also masters of water engineering"，空后列举"Aqueducts—stone bridges, underground tunnels—carry fresh water from mountains, springs into cities...some stretched dozens of kilometres...drinking, bathing, fountains...so strong parts still stand Spain, France...understood clean water essential for health, city growth"。C选项"In fact, the Romans built long aqueducts to bring fresh water from mountains and springs into cities"（事实上，罗马人建造了长长的导水管，将山泉的淡水引入城市）中"In fact"强化罗马水利成就，"long aqueducts...fresh water from mountains and springs into cities"与空后 aqueducts、mountains、springs、cities 精准对应，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：古代水利对社会的影响及建造难度。空前提出"Ancient water projects had a huge impact on society"，空后描述"Reliable water...farmers grow more food...larger populations...cities grow where water scarce...trade, industry along canals, rivers...building hard: many workers, years of labour, careful planning...rulers who invested remembered wise leaders...improved lives"。D选项"These projects allowed societies to grow, but required great effort and careful planning to build"（这些工程使社会得以发展，但建造需要大量人力与周密规划）中"These projects"指代空后各类水利工程，"allowed societies to grow...great effort and careful planning"与空后 grow、many workers、years、careful planning 完美对应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与古代水利的当代意义。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"Ancient water engineering reminds us...creative ways to meet needs...canals, dams, aqueducts...not only solved practical problems but laid foundation for world today...studying and protecting...honour creativity of those who came before"。E选项"In short, ancient water works were creative solutions that shaped the world we live in today"（简言之，古代水利工程是创造性解决方案，塑造了我们今日所处的世界）用"In short"总结全文，"creative solutions...shaped the world we live in today"与空后"creative ways...laid foundation for world today"形成呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the Nile River in Egypt has been used for irrigation for thousands of years.**：涉及尼罗河灌溉历史，易被误填[2]或[4]。但F以"For example"引出尼罗河这一自然河流，而空[2]讨论的是都江堰这一具体人造工程的设计与原理，F的"Nile...irrigation"与"channel、stone cages、Dujiangyan"的工程焦点不同；空[4]讨论古代水利对社会的影响及建造难度，F的尼罗河为自然水系，与"canals, dams, reservoirs...building...workers, planning"的人造工程论述层次不符。F属"主题相关但论述层次错位"型干扰。
