**Guide Dog Training**

Guide dogs help people who are blind or have low vision move around safely. They learn to stop at curbs（路缘）, avoid obstacles, and follow commands such as "forward," "left," and "right." A well-trained guide dog can give its owner greater freedom and confidence. [1] In other words, guide dogs are highly trained companions that help blind people live more independently. Understanding how these dogs are trained helps us appreciate the bond between humans and animals and the hard work behind each successful partnership.

Not every dog can become a guide dog. [2] Puppies are chosen for their calm temperament, intelligence, and willingness to work. Breeds such as Labrador Retrievers and Golden Retrievers are often used because they are friendly, easy to train, and the right size for guiding. The puppies first live with volunteer families for about a year, where they learn basic manners and get used to everyday life—buses, shops, streets, and crowds. This early experience is important because a guide dog must feel calm in many different environments before it can start formal training.

Formal training usually lasts several months. [3] Trainers teach the dogs to walk in a straight line, stop at steps and curbs, and refuse to move when there is danger ahead—even if the owner gives a command to go. The dog learns to "intelligently disobey" when following a command would put its owner at risk. Trainers use positive methods: when the dog does well, it gets praise and treats. The dogs practise in real streets with traffic, pedestrians, and noise so they are ready for the real world. Only dogs that pass all tests are matched with an owner.

Matching a dog with its owner is a careful process. [4] The organisation considers the owner's lifestyle, walking speed, and daily routes. A fast walker might need a more energetic dog; someone who lives in a quiet area might suit a calmer one. Once matched, the owner and dog train together for a few weeks so they learn to work as a team. The owner learns the commands and how to care for the dog. After that, the pair can go home and begin their new life together. Many guide dog owners say their dogs become not just helpers but beloved family members.

[5] Guide dogs change lives by offering safety, independence, and companionship. The training of each guide dog takes time, money, and the effort of many people—from puppy raisers to professional trainers. When we see a guide dog at work, we should remember not to distract it, as it is always "on duty" when wearing its harness.

  - **线索**：导盲犬的筛选与早期养育。空前提出"Not every dog can become a guide dog"，空后详述"Puppies chosen for calm temperament, intelligence, willingness to work...Labrador, Golden Retriever...friendly, easy to train, right size...first live with volunteer families about a year...learn basic manners, get used to everyday life—buses, shops, streets, crowds...early experience important...must feel calm in many environments before formal training"。B选项"That is because they are chosen for good temperament and intelligence, and raised in families before formal training"（那是因为它们因性情和智力被挑选出来，并在正式训练前在家庭中养育）中"That is because"解释筛选与养育，"chosen for temperament and intelligence...raised in families before formal training"与空后 chosen、volunteer families、before formal training 完美对应，逻辑紧密。

  - **线索**：正式训练内容与通过标准。空前提出"Formal training usually lasts several months"，空后列举"Teach walk straight, stop at steps and curbs, refuse to move when danger ahead...even if owner gives command...'intelligently disobey' when command would put owner at risk...positive methods...praise, treats...practise in real streets...traffic, pedestrians, noise...ready for real world...only pass all tests matched with owner"。C选项"In fact, the dogs learn to guide safely, disobey when necessary, and pass tests in real-world conditions"（事实上，犬只学会安全引导、在必要时违抗命令，并在真实环境中通过测试）中"In fact"强化训练要点，"guide safely, disobey when necessary, pass tests in real-world"与空后 guide、intelligently disobey、pass all tests、real streets 精准对应，衔接自然。
