**Archaeological Discoveries: Uncovering the Past**

Archaeology is the study of human history through material remains—bones, tools, buildings, and everyday objects. Archaeologists dig carefully, record what they find, and use science to date and understand artefacts. Their work has revealed ancient cities, lost civilisations, and how our ancestors lived. Each discovery adds a piece to the puzzle of human history. [1]

Famous finds have changed what we know. The Terracotta Army in China showed the power of the Qin dynasty. Pompeii in Italy preserved a Roman city under volcanic ash. Tutankhamun's tomb in Egypt gave us treasures from 3,000 years ago. These sites attract millions of visitors and inspire new research. They remind us that history is written not only in books but also in the ground. [2]

Archaeology uses many methods. Ground-penetrating radar can "see" buried structures without digging. DNA from bones tells us about ancient migrations and diseases. Drones map large areas from above. Computers help analyse thousands of fragments. Technology has made archaeology faster and more accurate than ever before. [3]

Protecting sites is a constant challenge. Development, looting, and climate change threaten archaeological heritage. Governments and organisations work to guard important sites and recover stolen artefacts. Public education helps people understand why these places matter. Once a site is destroyed, the information it holds is lost forever. [4]

Archaeology connects us to our shared past. It shows how cultures developed, traded, and influenced each other. It challenges myths and corrects old mistakes. Whether we are professional archaeologists or curious visitors, we can all learn from what lies beneath our feet. [5]

## 选项

A. In short, archaeology brings the past to life through careful study of what humans have left behind.
B. For these reasons, major discoveries have reshaped our understanding of ancient civilisations.
C. Thanks to these tools, archaeologists can explore the past in ways that were once impossible.
D. That is why preserving archaeological sites is urgent and requires global cooperation.
E. In other words, archaeology is a gift that helps us understand who we are and where we come from.
F. For example, carbon-14 dating measures the age of organic materials by analysing radioactive decay.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍考古学定义——通过物质遗存研究人类历史、考古学家发掘记录与科学测年、揭示古城失落文明与先民生活、每项发现都是历史拼图的一 piece，空后需总结考古学如何借助遗存再现过去。A的"brings the past to life through careful study of what humans have left behind"与前文对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前举例著名发现——兵马俑展现秦朝、庞贝保存罗马城、图坦卡蒙墓出土宝藏、吸引游客并推动研究、历史不只写在书中也埋在地下，空后需总结重大发现对理解古代文明的影响。B的"major discoveries have reshaped our understanding of ancient civilisations"与前文呼应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说考古方法多样——探地雷达、骨骼DNA、无人机测绘、计算机分析，技术使考古更快更准，空后需总结这些工具带来的突破。C的"Thanks to these tools"指代前文，"explore the past in ways that were once impossible"与前文对应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说保护遗址是持续挑战——开发盗掘气候变化威胁遗产、政府与组织努力守卫与追回、公众教育很重要、遗址一旦损毁信息永失，空后需总结保护工作的紧迫性与国际合作。D的"preserving archaeological sites is urgent and requires global cooperation"与前文呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说考古连结共同过去、展示文化发展贸易与相互影响、挑战神话修正谬误、无论专业考古还是普通访客都能从地下学得，空后需总结考古对我们理解自身与起源的价值。E的"helps us understand who we are and where we come from"与前文对应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, carbon-14 dating measures the age of organic materials...**：以For example开头，为具体技术案例（碳14测年）。各空均为概括或过渡句；[3]易被误选因涉及科技方法，但该空需总结工具带来的整体突破，而非单一技术的原理说明。
