# 10-23 第二篇

**Soil Protection**

Soil is one of the most important resources on Earth. It grows our food, filters our water, and supports forests and grasslands. Yet we often take it for granted. Erosion, pollution, and overuse have damaged soil in many places. Healthy soil takes hundreds or thousands of years to form—but it can be lost in just a few years. [1] Protecting soil is essential for food security, clean water, and a healthy environment—and the choices we make today will affect future generations.

Erosion is a major threat. When rain or wind carries away the top layer of soil, the land loses its fertility. Farming on steep slopes, cutting down forests, and leaving soil bare between crops can speed up erosion. [2] Slowing erosion requires careful land management—using terraces on hillsides, planting cover crops, and keeping roots in the ground to hold the soil in place.

Soil can also be harmed by chemicals. Too much fertilizer can pollute groundwater and damage soil life. Pesticides can kill helpful insects and microorganisms. Factories and mining can leave heavy metals in the soil. [3] Reducing chemical use and cleaning up polluted land are important steps toward healthier soil—and healthier food and water for everyone.

Farmers and scientists are finding better ways to care for soil. "No-till" farming leaves crop residues on the ground to protect and feed the soil. Crop rotation and mixing crops can improve fertility. Composting and organic farming add nutrients without harsh chemicals. [4] These practices show that we can grow enough food while also protecting the soil—if we are willing to change how we farm and garden.

Everyone can help protect soil. Gardeners can compost kitchen scraps and avoid harsh chemicals. Consumers can choose food from farms that use sustainable practices. Governments can support soil conservation and research. [5] By working together—farmers, scientists, policymakers, and ordinary people—we can keep our soil healthy and productive for generations to come.

## 选项

A. Soil is a precious resource that we cannot afford to waste—and protecting it should be a priority for all of us.
B. Keeping soil in place means protecting it from rain and wind through practices that have been used for centuries and improved with modern knowledge.
C. Healthy soil depends on reducing harmful inputs and repairing damage—and the benefits extend to our food, water, and ecosystems.
D. Sustainable farming is not just possible—it is necessary, and many farmers are already proving that it works.
E. The future of our food and our planet depends on how we treat the soil today—and that is a responsibility we all share.
F. For example, the Dust Bowl in the 1930s was caused partly by poor farming practices that left soil exposed to wind, leading to massive erosion in the American Midwest.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍土壤的重要性及侵蚀污染过度利用的威胁，空后说"Protecting soil is essential for food security, clean water, and a healthy environment—and the choices we make today will affect future generations"。A的"Soil is a precious resource that we cannot afford to waste—and protecting it should be a priority for all of us"与空后essential、choices、future呼应。

  - **线索**：空前说侵蚀原因（陡坡耕种、砍伐、裸露），空后说"Slowing erosion requires careful land management—using terraces on hillsides, planting cover crops, and keeping roots in the ground to hold the soil in place"。B的"Keeping soil in place means protecting it from rain and wind through practices that have been used for centuries"与空后terrace、cover crops、hold soil呼应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说化肥农药、工厂采矿对土壤的损害，空后说"Reducing chemical use and cleaning up polluted land are important steps toward healthier soil—and healthier food and water for everyone"。C的"Healthy soil depends on reducing harmful inputs and repairing damage—and the benefits extend to our food, water, and ecosystems"与空后呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前举免耕、轮作、堆肥有机农业等可持续做法，空后说"These practices show that we can grow enough food while also protecting the soil—if we are willing to change how we farm and garden"。D的"Sustainable farming is not just possible—it is necessary, and many farmers are already proving that it works"与空后"grow enough food while protecting"呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说园丁、消费者、政府可采取的行动，空后说"By working together—farmers, scientists, policymakers, and ordinary people—we can keep our soil healthy and productive for generations to come"。E的"The future of our food and our planet depends on how we treat the soil today—and that is a responsibility we all share"与空后working together、generations呼应。
