**Internet Safety: Staying Smart Online**

The internet is a powerful tool. It helps us learn, connect with others, and access information from around the world. But just as we learn to cross the street safely, we need to learn how to stay safe online. Scammers, hackers, and bullies use the internet too, and young people are often targets. Knowing how to protect yourself can make your online experience enjoyable and secure. [1] Internet safety is about making wise choices and being aware of risks, so that we can enjoy the benefits of the digital world without falling into its traps.

One of the most important rules of internet safety is protecting your personal information. Never share your full name, address, phone number, or school name with strangers online. Be careful about what you post—photos and comments can be seen by many people and may stay online for a long time. Use strong, unique passwords for different accounts, and never share them with anyone, not even friends. [2] Keeping your personal information private is the first line of defense against identity theft, scams, and unwanted contact from people you do not know.

Be wary of what you see and who you meet online. Not everyone is who they claim to be; someone who says they are a teenager might be an adult. Be cautious about links in messages or emails—they could lead to viruses or scam sites. If something seems too good to be true, like winning a free phone or inheriting money from a stranger, it probably is. [3] Developing a healthy sense of doubt about online offers and strangers can protect you from many common threats and help you stay one step ahead of scammers.

Social media and messaging apps are fun, but they can also be places where cyberbullying happens. If someone sends you mean messages or threatens you online, do not respond with anger. Save the evidence, block the person, and tell a trusted adult. Remember that you do not have to face online problems alone—parents, teachers, and counselors are there to help. [4] Knowing how to respond to cyberbullying and when to seek help is essential for maintaining your well-being and confidence in the online world.

The internet will continue to change, and new apps and platforms will bring new risks. The best defense is to keep learning. Stay informed about the latest safety tips, talk with your family about your online activities, and think before you click. [5] Building good internet safety habits now will serve you well throughout your life, as the digital world becomes an even larger part of work, study, and social life.

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍互联网的双面性——强大工具用于学习、联络、获取信息，但也需像过马路一样学会安全使用，骗子、黑客、霸凌者也在利用网络，青少年常为目标，空后需概括网络安全的核心要义。A的"making wise choices and being aware of risks"与前文"learn how to stay safe"呼应，"enjoy the benefits... without falling into its traps"与双面性对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说保护个人信息——不向陌生人透露姓名、地址、电话、学校，注意发布内容、使用强密码且不与他人共享，空后需总结保护隐私的重要性。B的"Keeping your personal information private"与前文各项保护措施呼应，"first line of defense against identity theft, scams, and unwanted contact"概括了隐私泄露的后果。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前提醒警惕网络信息与人——身份可能造假、链接可能含病毒或诈骗、过于诱人的承诺多为骗局，空后需总结保持怀疑态度的作用。C的"Developing a healthy sense of doubt about online offers and strangers"与前文"Not everyone is who they claim to be"和"too good to be true"呼应，"stay one step ahead of scammers"与防范诈骗对应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说网络霸凌——社交媒体和聊天应用中的恶意信息与威胁，建议不愤怒回应、保存证据、拉黑、告知可信成人，强调不必独自面对，空后需总结应对霸凌与求助的重要性。D的"Knowing how to respond to cyberbullying and when to seek help"与前文具体建议呼应，"maintaining your well-being and confidence"与"do not have to face... alone"对应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说网络会持续变化、新应用带来新风险，最佳防御是不断学习——了解安全贴士、与家人沟通、三思而后点，空后需总结养成习惯的长期价值。E的"Building good internet safety habits now"与前文"keep learning"和"think before you click"呼应，"serve you well throughout your life"与"digital world becomes an even larger part"对应，是合理收尾。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, phishing emails often look like messages from banks or companies but are designed to steal passwords and personal data.**：以For example开头，为钓鱼邮件这一具体诈骗手法的案例。各空均为概括或过渡句；[3]易被误选因涉及诈骗，但该空需概括对网络信息与陌生人保持怀疑的总体态度，而非单一钓鱼邮件的具体说明。
