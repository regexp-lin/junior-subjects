# 11-08 第二篇

**Climate Change: A Global Challenge**

The Earth's climate is changing. Scientists have observed rising temperatures, melting ice at the poles, more intense storms, and shifts in rainfall patterns around the world. These changes are largely driven by human activities—burning fossil fuels for energy, cutting down forests, and increasing industry and transportation. The effects are already being felt: droughts in some regions, floods in others, and threats to wildlife and farming. [1] Climate change is one of the most urgent challenges of our time, affecting every country and requiring action from all of us.

The main cause of climate change is the increase in greenhouse gases in the atmosphere. When we burn coal, oil, and gas for electricity, cars, and factories, we release carbon dioxide and other gases. These gases trap heat from the sun, causing the Earth to warm—a process known as the greenhouse effect. Forests, which absorb carbon dioxide, are being cut down in many places, reducing the planet's ability to soak up these gases. [2] Reducing greenhouse gas emissions and protecting forests are among the most effective ways to slow down climate change and limit its impact on future generations.

Many countries and communities are already taking action. Governments are setting targets to cut emissions and invest in clean energy such as solar and wind power. Cities are improving public transportation and encouraging cycling and walking. Schools and businesses are finding ways to use less energy and produce less waste. Individuals can contribute by using less electricity, traveling in greener ways, and supporting policies that protect the environment. [3] From governments to individuals, everyone has a role to play in addressing climate change, and small actions can add up to make a real difference.

Young people around the world are raising their voices about climate change. Student-led movements have drawn attention to the need for action, and many young activists are urging leaders to make bolder decisions. Education about climate change helps students understand the science and the solutions. [4] The next generation will inherit the consequences of today's choices, so their voices and actions matter greatly in shaping a more sustainable future for all.

The path ahead is challenging, but not hopeless. New technologies are making renewable energy cheaper and more efficient. More people are choosing sustainable lifestyles. International agreements bring countries together to set common goals. The choices we make today—in how we produce energy, how we travel, and how we use resources—will shape the world for decades to come. [5] With determination and cooperation, we can slow climate change and build a healthier, more resilient planet for ourselves and for generations to come.

## 选项

A. Climate change is one of the most urgent challenges of our time, affecting every country and requiring action from all of us.
B. Reducing greenhouse gas emissions and protecting forests are among the most effective ways to slow down climate change and limit its impact on future generations.
C. From governments to individuals, everyone has a role to play in addressing climate change, and small actions can add up to make a real difference.
D. The next generation will inherit the consequences of today's choices, so their voices and actions matter greatly in shaping a more sustainable future for all.
E. With determination and cooperation, we can slow climate change and build a healthier, more resilient planet for ourselves and for generations to come.
F. For instance, the European Union has set a goal to become carbon neutral by 2050, meaning it aims to balance the amount of carbon emitted with the amount removed from the atmosphere.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述气候变化现象——升温、极地冰川融化、风暴加剧、降水分布改变，归因于人类活动——化石燃料、砍伐森林、工业与交通，已带来干旱、洪水及对动植物与农业的影响，空后需总结气候变化的紧迫性与全球性。A的"one of the most urgent challenges of our time"与前文现象与影响呼应，"affecting every country and requiring action from all of us"与"every country"和全球效应对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说明温室效应——燃煤油气释放二氧化碳等气体、吸收热量、森林被砍减少吸收能力，空后需提出减排与保护森林的应对措施。B的"Reducing greenhouse gas emissions and protecting forests"与前文温室气体来源及森林作用对应，"most effective ways to slow down climate change and limit its impact"是前文因果的合理对策。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前列举多方行动——政府设定减排目标与清洁能源投资、城市改善公交与推广骑行步行、学校与企业节能减废、个人节电与绿色出行，空后需总结各方共同参与与积少成多。C的"From governments to individuals, everyone has a role to play"与前文各层级行动呼应，"small actions can add up to make a real difference"与个人贡献对应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说青年参与——学生运动推动关注、年轻活动家呼吁更大胆决策、气候变化教育帮助理解科学与对策，空后需总结下一代的声音与行动的重要性。D的"The next generation will inherit the consequences of today's choices"与前文"young people"和"inheriting"逻辑呼应，"their voices and actions matter greatly"与"raising their voices"和"urging leaders"对应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说前景——挑战大但非无望，可再生能源技术进步、更多人选择可持续生活、国际协议凝聚共识，今日选择将影响未来数十年，空后需总结决心与合作的积极展望。E的"With determination and cooperation"与前文多方努力呼应，"slow climate change and build a healthier, more resilient planet"与"not hopeless"和"renewable energy"对应，是合理收尾。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For instance, the European Union has set a goal to become carbon neutral by 2050, meaning it aims to balance the amount of carbon emitted with the amount removed from the atmosphere.**：以For instance开头，为欧盟碳中和目标的具体案例。各空均为概括或过渡句；[3]易被误选因涉及政府行动，但该空需概括从政府到个人的共同参与，而非单一地区或组织的具体目标。
