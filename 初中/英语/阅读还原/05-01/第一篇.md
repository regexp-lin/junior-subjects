# 05-01 第一篇

**The Power of Reading: How Books Change Our Lives**

Reading is one of the best habits a person can develop. Whether you read novels, newspapers, or online articles, you are exercising your brain every time you open a book. [1] Studies show that people who read regularly have better memory, stronger focus, and a wider vocabulary. Even reading for just 20 minutes a day can make a big difference over time.

Why is reading so good for our brains? When we read, our brains work hard to understand the words and imagine the scenes. [2] This mental exercise strengthens the connections between brain cells and helps us think more clearly. Reading fiction, for example, can improve our ability to understand other people's feelings, because we learn to see the world through the eyes of different characters. Reading non-fiction teaches us new facts and ideas that we can use in our daily lives.

Reading also helps us do better in school. [3] Students who read for pleasure outside of class often perform better in exams than those who do not. They find it easier to understand long texts, answer comprehension questions, and write essays. Reading builds the language skills that are needed in almost every subject, from English to science to history.

However, many young people today spend more time on their phones than reading books. [4] Social media and short videos can be fun, but they do not give our brains the same deep workout that reading does. Experts suggest setting aside a specific time each day for reading, such as before bed or during lunch break. Choosing books that interest you makes it much easier to stick with the habit.

[5] In the end, reading is a gift we give to ourselves. It opens doors to new worlds, helps us grow as people, and prepares us for success. So pick up a book today—you might be surprised by how much it changes your life.

## 选项

A. In fact, reading is one of the most effective ways to improve academic performance.
B. That is why many scientists compare reading to a workout for the mind.
C. In short, developing a reading habit is one of the best things you can do for your future.
D. In other words, the benefits of reading go far beyond simple entertainment.
E. This is a worrying trend because it means they are missing out on the benefits of reading.
F. For instance, some schools have started book clubs to encourage students to read more.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] D   [2] B   [3] A   [4] E   [5] C

## 深度解析

- **[1] D**：
  - **线索**：引出阅读的多方面益处。空前描述"you are exercising your brain every time you open a book"，空后列举"people who read regularly have better memory, stronger focus, and a wider vocabulary...reading for just 20 minutes a day can make a big difference"。D选项"In other words, the benefits of reading go far beyond simple entertainment"（换言之，阅读的好处远超简单的娱乐）中"In other words"对上文进行概括，"benefits...go far beyond entertainment"自然引出空后具体的记忆力、专注力、词汇量等多重好处，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：解释阅读对大脑的锻炼作用。空前描述"our brains work hard to understand the words and imagine the scenes"，空后说明"This mental exercise strengthens the connections between brain cells...helps us think more clearly...improve our ability to understand other people's feelings"。B选项"That is why many scientists compare reading to a workout for the mind"（因此许多科学家将阅读比作大脑的锻炼）中"That is why"承接空前大脑的工作过程，"workout for the mind"与空后"This mental exercise"完美呼应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] A**：
  - **线索**：阅读与学业表现的关系。空前提出"Reading also helps us do better in school"，空后详述"Students who read for pleasure outside of class often perform better in exams...find it easier to understand long texts, answer comprehension questions, and write essays"。A选项"In fact, reading is one of the most effective ways to improve academic performance"（事实上，阅读是提高学业表现最有效的方式之一）中"In fact"加强空前论点，"improve academic performance"与空后"perform better in exams"精准对应，衔接自然。

- **[4] E**：
  - **线索**：年轻人远离阅读的忧虑。空前说明"many young people today spend more time on their phones than reading books"，空后建议"Social media and short videos can be fun, but they do not give our brains the same deep workout...Experts suggest setting aside a specific time each day for reading"。E选项"This is a worrying trend because it means they are missing out on the benefits of reading"（这是一个令人担忧的趋势，因为这意味着他们正在错过阅读的好处）中"This...worrying trend"承接空前手机取代阅读的现象，"missing out on the benefits"与空后对比社交媒体与阅读的差异形成因果关系，衔接得当。

- **[5] C**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后列举"reading is a gift we give to ourselves...opens doors to new worlds...helps us grow...prepares us for success...pick up a book today"。C选项"In short, developing a reading habit is one of the best things you can do for your future"（简言之，培养阅读习惯是你能为未来做的最好的事之一）用"In short"总结全文，"best things...for your future"与空后"prepares us for success"形成呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For instance, some schools have started book clubs to encourage students to read more.**：涉及学校推广阅读的举措，易被误填[3]。但F以"For instance"引出学校读书俱乐部的具体例子，而空[3]前后文讨论的是阅读与学业表现的因果关系，空后具体列举的是"perform better in exams...understand long texts...write essays"等学业层面的好处，并非学校推广阅读的措施；F的"book clubs"与"academic performance"话题不匹配。F属"主题相关但衔接功能错位"型干扰。
