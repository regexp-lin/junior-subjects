# 05-01 第二篇

**Community Gardens: Growing Food and Friendships**

In many cities around the world, people are turning empty lots（空地） into community gardens. These shared spaces allow neighbors to grow vegetables, fruits, and flowers together. [1] A community garden is not just a place to grow food—it is a place where people come together, share knowledge, and build stronger neighborhoods. Anyone can join, whether they are experienced gardeners or complete beginners.

Community gardens provide fresh, healthy food for families who might not be able to afford it otherwise. [2] Growing your own tomatoes, peppers, and herbs costs much less than buying them at a supermarket. For families with low incomes, this can make a real difference in their diet and health. Children who help in the garden also learn where food comes from and are more willing to eat vegetables.

These gardens also bring people together. [3] Neighbors who might never have spoken to each other find common ground while planting seeds or pulling weeds. Older gardeners share their experience with younger ones, and children learn patience and responsibility by taking care of plants. Many community gardens also hold events like harvest festivals（丰收节） and cooking classes, which further strengthen the bonds between neighbors.

Community gardens are good for the environment, too. [4] Plants absorb carbon dioxide（二氧化碳） and release oxygen, helping to clean the air. Gardens also reduce the amount of rainwater that flows into drains（排水管）, which can prevent flooding. Green spaces in cities provide homes for birds, bees, and butterflies, supporting local biodiversity（生物多样性）. Even a small garden can have a positive effect on the surrounding area.

[5] Community gardens show that when people work together, great things can happen. They turn unused land into something beautiful and useful. If your neighborhood does not have a community garden yet, perhaps it is time to start one.

## 选项

A. In other words, they offer an affordable way to improve nutrition for the whole family.
B. That is because gardening is a social activity that naturally encourages conversation and cooperation.
C. In conclusion, community gardens benefit people, communities, and the planet in many ways.
D. In fact, these gardens have become an important part of city life in recent years.
E. They help make cities greener and healthier in several important ways.
F. For example, some community gardens donate extra produce to local food banks.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] D   [2] A   [3] B   [4] E   [5] C

## 深度解析

- **[1] D**：
  - **线索**：引出社区花园的重要性。空前描述"people are turning empty lots into community gardens...shared spaces allow neighbors to grow vegetables, fruits, and flowers together"，空后说明"A community garden is not just a place to grow food—it is a place where people come together, share knowledge, and build stronger neighborhoods"。D选项"In fact, these gardens have become an important part of city life in recent years"（事实上，这些花园近年来已成为城市生活的重要组成部分）中"In fact"加强上文信息，"important part of city life"自然过渡到空后"not just...grow food"的多重功能介绍，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] A**：
  - **线索**：社区花园的经济与营养价值。空前提出"Community gardens provide fresh, healthy food for families who might not be able to afford it otherwise"，空后具体说明"Growing your own tomatoes, peppers, and herbs costs much less than buying them at a supermarket...make a real difference in their diet and health"。A选项"In other words, they offer an affordable way to improve nutrition for the whole family"（换言之，它们为全家提供了一种负担得起的改善营养的方式）中"In other words"概括空前信息，"affordable...improve nutrition"与空后"costs much less"和"diet and health"完美呼应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] B**：
  - **线索**：社区花园的社交功能。空前提出"These gardens also bring people together"，空后描述"Neighbors who might never have spoken to each other find common ground...Older gardeners share their experience with younger ones...children learn patience and responsibility"。B选项"That is because gardening is a social activity that naturally encourages conversation and cooperation"（那是因为园艺是一项自然鼓励交流与合作的社交活动）中"That is because"解释空前"bring people together"的原因，"conversation and cooperation"与空后"find common ground...share their experience"精准对应，衔接自然。

- **[4] E**：
  - **线索**：社区花园的环境好处。空前提出"Community gardens are good for the environment, too"，空后列举"Plants absorb carbon dioxide and release oxygen...reduce rainwater...prevent flooding...provide homes for birds, bees, and butterflies"。E选项"They help make cities greener and healthier in several important ways"（它们以多种重要方式帮助城市变得更绿色、更健康）中"several important ways"总领空后的多个具体环境好处，"greener and healthier"与空后吸收二氧化碳、防洪、支持生物多样性等内容呼应，衔接得当。

- **[5] C**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"when people work together, great things can happen...turn unused land into something beautiful and useful...perhaps it is time to start one"。C选项"In conclusion, community gardens benefit people, communities, and the planet in many ways"（总之，社区花园在许多方面惠及个人、社区和地球）用"In conclusion"总结全文三大主题（食物/社交/环境），"people, communities, and the planet"与全文三个主体段落呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, some community gardens donate extra produce to local food banks.**：涉及社区花园捐赠多余农产品，易被误填[2]。但F以"For example"引出具体捐赠案例，而空[2]前后文讨论的是种植对家庭经济和营养的直接好处，空后的"costs much less"和"diet and health"聚焦家庭层面，并非社区层面的捐赠行为；F的"donate...food banks"属于社区公益话题，与空后内容层级不匹配。F属"主题相关但衔接层级错位"型干扰。
