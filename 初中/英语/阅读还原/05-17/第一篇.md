# 05-17 第一篇

**The Mystery of Photosynthesis: How Plants Feed the World**

Have you ever wondered how plants can grow without eating food like animals do? The answer lies in a wonderful process called photosynthesis. [1] In fact, it is through this process that plants use sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide from the air to make their own food—sugar. At the same time, they release oxygen into the air, which is what we breathe. That is why plants are often called the "lungs" of our planet. Without photosynthesis, life on Earth as we know it would not exist.

How does it work? [2] The process mainly happens in the leaves, where a green substance called chlorophyll（叶绿素）traps sunlight. Chlorophyll gives plants their green colour. When sunlight hits the leaf, the plant uses its energy to combine water from the roots with carbon dioxide from the air. The result is glucose（葡萄糖）—a kind of sugar that gives the plant energy—and oxygen, which leaves through tiny pores（气孔）on the leaf surface. So leaves are like small factories working day and night.

Why is sunlight so important? [3] Without light, photosynthesis cannot take place. That is why plants grow better in bright places and may turn pale or weak in dark corners. Some plants, like sunflowers, even turn their heads to follow the sun across the sky. In winter, when days are shorter and sunlight is weaker, many plants slow down or lose their leaves. Light is the power source that drives the whole process.

Plants also need water and air. [4] Water travels from the roots up through the stem to the leaves. Carbon dioxide enters through the pores on the underside of leaves. If a plant does not get enough water, its leaves may wilt（枯萎）and photosynthesis slows. If the air is polluted, plants may struggle to get clean carbon dioxide. In other words, clean air and enough water are necessary for healthy photosynthesis.

[5] In short, photosynthesis is one of the most important processes on Earth. It feeds not only plants but also the animals that eat plants, and it provides the oxygen we breathe. By protecting forests and planting more trees, we help keep this amazing process working for all of us. Remember: every green leaf is a tiny power station keeping our world alive.
