# 03-18 第一篇

**The Reality of Life as a Zookeeper**

For many animal lovers, working as a zookeeper seems like the perfect job. Spending all day feeding cute monkeys and playing with baby pandas is a dream come true. [1] However, the reality of this unusual profession involves much more hard work and dirty tasks than most people realize.

First of all, being a zookeeper requires a huge amount of physical strength. A large part of the day is spent cleaning animal enclosures (围栏) and preparing food. [2] This daily routine includes lifting heavy buckets of water, carrying large bags of hay, and shoveling unbelievable amounts of animal waste. 

Secondly, zookeepers must always stay highly focused, as dealing with wild animals can sometimes be quite dangerous. Even an animal that has been raised by humans can suddenly become aggressive. [3] Therefore, keepers must strictly follow safety rules and never turn their backs on powerful creatures like tigers or gorillas.

Furthermore, this job demands emotional strength as well. Zookeepers often build deep connections with the animals they care for. [4] When an older animal becomes sick or eventually passes away, it can be absolutely heartbreaking for the staff.  

[5] To them, protecting endangered species and teaching the public about wildlife is far more important than the low pay or the dirty boots. 

## 选项

A. In fact, they may suddenly attack a person without any obvious warning.
B. Despite all these challenges, most zookeepers truly love what they do.
C. It is undoubtedly an exhausting job that requires excellent physical health.
D. They often give them special names and treat them almost like family members.
E. We naturally imagine that they spend all their time happily petting the animals.
F. Therefore, everyone is encouraged to touch and feed the animals directly.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] E   [2] C   [3] A   [4] D   [5] B

## 深度解析

- **[1] E**：
  - **线索**：语义递进与铺垫。空前提到当动物园管理员听起来像完美工作，一整天都在喂可爱猴子、和熊猫宝宝玩。E选项“We naturally imagine that they spend all their time happily petting the animals”（我们自然而然地想象他们把所有的时间都花在开心地抚摸动物上），是对这种“完美假想”的进一步描述。这种假想刚好为空后一句的强转折“However（然而），这门不寻常职业的现实涉及更多的艰苦工作”做足了铺垫。
- **[2] C**：
  - **线索**：段落呼应与下文总括。空前一段指出管理员需要极大的体力（physical strength），且大把时间花在打扫和准备食物上。C选项“It is undoubtedly an exhausting job that requires excellent physical health”（毫无疑问，这是一份极其令人疲惫的工作，需要极好的身体健康）完美呼应了首句的体力要求。这也统领了接下来的空后一句“这日常工作（This daily routine）包括提重水桶、扛着大袋干草、铲大量的动物粪便...”。
- **[3] A**：
  - **线索**：代词指代与因果逻辑。空前警告说：“即使是被人类养大的动物也可能突然变得具有攻击性（aggressive）”。A选项“In fact, they may suddenly attack a person without any obvious warning”（事实上，它们可能会在没有任何明显预警的情况下突然攻击人）中的“they”完美指代前一句发生攻击性的“animal(s)”。正由于这种突然的无预警攻击性，空后一句用“Therefore（因此），饲养员必须严格遵守规定并绝不背对动物”得出了严密的因果结论。
- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：同义解释与代词双指。空前提出了段落主旨副线：饲养员常与照顾的动物建立很深的连接。D选项“They often give them special names and treat them almost like family members”（他们经常给它们起特别的名字，并且几乎把它们当作家庭成员一样对待）。这里的第一个“They”指代“zookeepers”，第二个“them”指代“animals”。“当作家人们对待起名字”正是“深厚连接（deep connections）”的具体体现。也正是因为如同家人，所以空后说“当老动物病死时，让人心碎”才显得极其合乎情理。
- **[5] B**：
  - **线索**：结论引领与代词照应。空[5]位于文章结语段段首。前面分别详细刻画了饲养员生理上的疲惫、安全上的危险、心理上的痛苦（老动物死去）。B选项“Despite all these challenges, most zookeepers truly love what they do”（尽管有所有这些挑战，大多数动物园管理员真心地热爱他们所做的事情），这里的“these challenges（这些挑战）”就是对前面三段各种困难的完美概括。紧接着空后一句的代词“To them...”中的“them（他们）”，也就是正好指代了B项中的“most zookeepers”。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. Therefore, everyone is encouraged to touch and feed the animals directly.**：该选项中包含总结词“Therefore”和高频词汇“animals”、“feed”。如果考生被第一段开头提到的看似美好的“喂猴子、玩熊猫”误导，可能会选这个选项。但这句意为“因此，鼓励每个人直接触摸和喂食动物”，完全忽略了全文尤其是第三段着重强调的“对待野生动物需要极度小心、随时可能有危险、切不可背对”的严禁乱摸乱碰的严肃安全基调，属于逻辑相悖干扰项。
