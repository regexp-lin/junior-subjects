# 03-18 第二篇

**The Silent Language of Color**

Have you ever wondered why fast-food restaurants are often painted red and yellow? Or why hospitals mostly use light blue and pale green walls? [1] According to psychologists, colors can powerfully affect our mood, feelings, and even our physical behavior. 

Let's take red, for example. It is a highly energetic color that naturally catches our attention. [2] Surprisingly, scientists have also found that looking at the color red can actually make people feel hungry. This completely explains the color choice of many famous burger chains!

On the other hand, blue has a very different effect on the human body. [3] Looking at the ocean or a clear blue sky makes our heart rate drop and helps our muscles relax. Because it creates a sense of peace and trust, many important banks use blue in their logos.

Green is another fascinating color linked to nature and health. It is the easiest color for the human eye to process. [4] For this reason, green is often used in waiting rooms or on classroom boards to help people stay calm and focused.

[5] By understanding this silent language, we can decorate our rooms, choose our clothes, and design our environments much more cleverly.

B. It is considered a cool, calming color that directly reduces stress and anxiety.

D. Therefore, reading in a room painted green causes much less eye tiredness.
E. These specific choices are certainly not an accident or a random design.
F. That is the main reason why stop signs and danger warnings use it worldwide.

# 03-18 第二篇

**The Silent Language of Color**

Have you ever wondered why fast-food restaurants are often painted red and yellow? Or why hospitals mostly use light blue and pale green walls? [1] According to psychologists, colors can powerfully affect our mood, feelings, and even our physical behavior. 

Let's take red, for example. It is a highly energetic color that naturally catches our attention. [2] Surprisingly, scientists have also found that looking at the color red can actually make people feel hungry. This completely explains the color choice of many famous burger chains!

On the other hand, blue has a very different effect on the human body. [3] Looking at the ocean or a clear blue sky makes our heart rate drop and helps our muscles relax. Because it creates a sense of peace and trust, many important banks use blue in their logos.

Green is another fascinating color linked to nature and health. It is the easiest color for the human eye to process. [4] For this reason, green is often used in waiting rooms or on classroom boards to help people stay calm and focused.

[5] By understanding this silent language, we can decorate our rooms, choose our clothes, and design our environments much more cleverly.

## 选项

A. Clearly, color is not just something we see; it is something we deeply feel.
B. It is considered a cool, calming color that directly reduces stress and anxiety.
C. Therefore, reading a book printed on thick red paper makes everyone sleepy.
D. Therefore, being surrounded by green causes much less eye tiredness.
E. These specific choices are certainly not an accident or a random design.
F. That is the main reason why stop signs and emergency warnings use it worldwide.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] E   [2] F   [3] B   [4] D   [5] A

## 深度解析

- **[1] E**：
  - **线索**：代词双重指代与语义揭示。空前连续提出了两个现象级疑问：为什么快餐店是红黄的？为什么医院用浅蓝浅绿墙壁？E选项“These specific choices are certainly not an accident or a random design”（这些具体的选择绝对不是偶然的或随机的设计）中的主语“These specific choices”完美概括了前文那两个特定的颜色选择。既然不是偶然，那么空后就顺理成章地给出心理学家的理论原因：“颜色可以有力地影响我们的情绪和感受...”。
- **[2] F**：
  - **线索**：代词指代与逻辑连接。空前指出了红色是“highly energetic（高能量）”且“catches our attention（吸引我们的注意力）”的。F选项“That is the main reason why stop signs and emergency warnings use it worldwide”（这就是世界各地的停车标志和紧急警告使用它的主要原因）中的“That（那）”指代上文提到的引人注意的特质；“it”则指代前文的“the color red”。而且这与紧接其后的空后的“Surprisingly... also found...（令人惊讶的是，还发现...）”构成了红色的另一项特质描述。
- **[3] B**：
  - **线索**：主体置换与同义说明。空前起笔转到了对蓝色（blue）的探讨。B选项“It is considered a cool, calming color that directly reduces stress and anxiety”（它被认为是一种凉爽平静的颜色，直接减少压力和焦虑）中的代词“It”完美指代了前句的“blue”。而且这种“平静和减少压力”的形容，为紧随其后的句子“看着海洋会让心率下降、肌肉放松”提供了坚实的概念基础。
- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：因果论证与词汇联结。空前提出了一个非常有意思的生理特质：绿色是“the easiest color for the human eye to process（人眼最容易处理的颜色）”。既然对人眼最友好，随之便导致了D选项陈述的客观结果：“Therefore, being surrounded by green causes much less eye tiredness（因此，被绿色包围会引起极少的眼部疲劳）”。由于眼睛少疲劳，也就非常顺畅地引出了空后的那句“由于这个原因，教室黑板常使用绿色帮助人保持专注”。
- **[5] A**：
  - **线索**：主题升华与定语指代。空[5]位于结论段段首。在分别分析了红、蓝、绿三种颜色对视觉、食量、心率、心理等的影响后，A选项“Clearly, color is not just something we see; it is something we deeply feel”（很明显，颜色不仅仅是我们看到的东西；它是我们深深感受到的东西）极高明地总结了全文（因为前文列举了饥饿、放松、专注等全部属于feel的范畴）。同时，空后一句提到了“this silent language（这种无声的语言）”，其正得名于颜色不仅能看，其实还在偷偷影响“feelings（感受）”这一核心特性。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **C. Therefore, reading a book printed on thick red paper makes everyone sleepy.**：该选项由于携带总结词“Therefore（因此）”及对前面提到的“color（红色）”与“sleepy”之间的关系判定，容易误导学生。首先，第二段主要讲红色能“引起注意”、“让人充满能量”和“让人感到饥饿”，而不是“让人困倦（sleepy）”。而相反，蓝色或绿色才是带来平静放松（类似困倦）的色系。将能让人兴奋的红色与“犯困”通过因果词连接，在事实和逻辑上南辕北辙。
