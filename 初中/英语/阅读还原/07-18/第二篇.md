# 07-18 第二篇

**Preventing Bullying in Schools**

Bullying has long been a serious problem in schools around the world, affecting students' mental health and academic performance. Today, many schools are taking active steps to create safer and more inclusive environments. [1] Understanding what bullying looks like and why it happens is the first step toward preventing it. Bullying can take many forms—physical, verbal, social, or online—and it often involves a power imbalance between the bully and the victim.

[2] Schools that have reduced bullying successfully often start by training both teachers and students. Teachers learn to recognize the signs of bullying and respond quickly, while students are taught how to stand up for themselves and others safely. Programs that bring older students together with younger ones as mentors have also shown positive results. When everyone in the school community understands their role, prevention becomes much more effective.

[3] Creating a culture of respect and kindness is another key to preventing bullying. When students feel connected to their school and valued by their peers, they are less likely to bully others or to stand by when someone else is being hurt. Activities that promote teamwork, empathy, and inclusion help build this culture. A simple act like greeting each other in the hallway can make a big difference.

[4] Parents and families also play an important role in bullying prevention. Open communication between parents and children about daily experiences at school can help identify problems early. Parents can teach their children to treat others with respect and to report bullying when they see it. When schools and families work together, the message against bullying becomes stronger.

[5] Preventing bullying is not a one-time effort but an ongoing process that requires the commitment of everyone involved. By combining education, a positive school culture, and strong partnerships with families, schools can create environments where every student feels safe and supported. The goal is clear: no student should have to face bullying alone.

A. This knowledge helps adults and students alike to identify and address problems before they grow worse.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前指出学校开始积极采取措施，空后解释欺凌的多种形式及权力不平衡。A 句 "This knowledge" 指代空后关于欺凌形态的说明，"helps adults and students... identify and address problems" 承接 "first step toward preventing"，完成从认识到行动的过渡。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：段落主题是学校通过培训教师和学生来成功减少欺凌。空后分别描述教师培训、学生培训及导师项目。B 句 "equipping both educators and students with the right skills and knowledge" 准确概括本段核心策略。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：段落强调营造尊重与友善文化的重要性。空后提到学生感到被接纳时更少欺凌他人或旁观。C 句 "A welcoming and supportive environment reduces the conditions in which bullying tends to occur" 与段落主题完全对应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：段落论述家长和家庭在预防欺凌中的作用。空后强调家校合作能强化反欺凌信息。D 句 "schools and families form a united front... much more successful" 精准总结本段家校合作的要义。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：结尾段总结预防欺凌是持续过程，需各方共同努力，并点明目标。E 句 "With continued effort and cooperation, schools can move closer to achieving this important goal" 作为结论句，与前文 "ongoing process" 和 "goal" 自然呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example...**：虽与网络欺凌相关，但仅为举例层面，应嵌入第 1 段 "online" 形式内部。作为独立选项，其概括层级不足，且未与任一空位的上下文形成完整衔接，易因主题相关而误选。
