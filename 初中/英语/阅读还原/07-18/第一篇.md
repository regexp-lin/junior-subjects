# 07-18 第一篇

**The Value of Handwriting in the Digital Age**

In an age when typing dominates our daily communication, handwriting might seem like a skill from the past. However, recent research suggests that putting pen to paper offers benefits that keyboards simply cannot provide. [1] Studies have shown that students who take notes by hand tend to remember information better than those who type. The physical act of writing activates different parts of the brain and helps form stronger memory connections.

[2] Handwriting also improves our ability to think clearly and express ourselves. When we write by hand, we naturally slow down and give ourselves more time to process ideas. This slower pace allows us to organize our thoughts before putting them on paper. Many great writers and thinkers have preferred handwriting for their creative work because it supports deeper reflection.

[3] Another advantage of handwriting is its personal touch. A handwritten letter or card carries emotional weight that a text message cannot match. In our fast-paced world, receiving something handwritten feels special and shows that the sender took extra time and care. Handwriting helps us connect with others in a more meaningful way.

[4] Furthermore, learning to write by hand supports the development of fine motor skills, especially in young children. The movements required for forming letters help strengthen the connections between the brain and the hands. These skills are important not only for writing but also for other activities that require precise hand control.

[5] While technology will continue to play a large role in our lives, we should not abandon the practice of handwriting. Making time to write by hand—whether in a journal, a letter to a friend, or simply for taking important notes—can enrich our minds and strengthen our connections with others. The pen may be older than the keyboard, but it still has much to offer.

## 选项

A. This connection between physical writing and memory explains why many educators still encourage handwritten notes in the classroom.
B. The process of forming letters by hand engages the brain in ways that support clearer thinking and better organization of ideas.
C. Handwriting creates a sense of warmth and sincerity that digital communication often lacks.
D. Developing these physical skills through handwriting has lasting benefits for children's growth.
E. Therefore, keeping handwriting as part of our daily habits is a choice worth making.
F. For example, some people write in journals every morning to start their day with reflection.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前提到研究表明手写笔记的学生记忆更好，空后继续举例说明手写激活大脑不同区域、帮助形成更强记忆。A 句 "This connection between physical writing and memory" 直接承接前文 "memory connections"，并自然引出后文对教育者的建议，承上启下。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：段落主题是手写提高思考和表达能力。空后具体描述手写时我们放慢速度、更好地组织思路。B 句 "forming letters by hand engages the brain... support clearer thinking and better organization" 与段落主题句及后文细节完全对应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：段落讨论手写的个人化、情感价值。空后提到手写信件或卡片的情感份量、手写体现关心。C 句 "sense of warmth and sincerity that digital communication often lacks" 精准概括本段主旨。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：段落讲手写促进儿童精细运动发展，空后强调这些技能对书写及其他需要手部精确控制的活动很重要。D 句 "Developing these physical skills... lasting benefits for children's growth" 总结本段，与后文 "fine motor skills" 呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：结尾段总结全文，提出不应放弃手写、应保留手写习惯的建议。E 句 "Therefore, keeping handwriting as part of our daily habits is a choice worth making" 作为总结句，与前文建议完美衔接。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example...**：虽然涉及手写（写日记），但属于举例层次，应嵌入某段内部作支撑，而非独立填空。且 "journal" 与任何段落主题句的概括层级不符，易因 "reflection" 与第 2 段 "deeper reflection" 混淆而误选。
