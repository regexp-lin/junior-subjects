**First Aid Basics: What Everyone Should Know**

First aid is the immediate help given to someone who is injured or suddenly ill before professional medical care arrives. You do not need to be a doctor to save a life. Basic first aid skills can make a huge difference in emergencies. Learning a few simple steps can prepare you to act when it matters most. Every minute counts when someone's life or health is at risk. [1]

The most important skill is calling for help. In many countries, dial 120 for an ambulance. Tell the operator your location clearly and describe what happened. Stay on the line—they may guide you through steps while waiting for help. If someone is unconscious and not breathing, CPR can keep blood flowing to the brain until paramedics arrive. Many community centers offer free CPR courses. [2]

For cuts and bleeding, apply direct pressure with a clean cloth or bandage. Elevate the wound if possible. For burns, run cool water over the area for at least 10 minutes—do not use ice. For choking, the Heimlich maneuver can dislodge an object from the airway. Each situation requires specific actions, but staying calm and acting quickly is key. [3]

A first aid kit should be in every home, car, and workplace. It should include bandages, gauze, tape, scissors, gloves, and pain relievers. Check the kit regularly and replace used or expired items. Knowing where the kit is and how to use its contents can save precious time. [4]

First aid is not about being perfect—it is about doing something. Even basic knowledge can reduce pain, prevent further injury, or save a life. Taking a first aid course is one of the best investments you can make. [5]
