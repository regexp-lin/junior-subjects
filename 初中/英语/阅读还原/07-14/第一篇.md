# 07-14 第一篇

**Reducing Food Waste: A Growing Global Concern**

Every year, about one-third of all food produced in the world goes to waste. That is enough to feed billions of hungry people. Food waste happens at every stage—from farms and factories to supermarkets and our own kitchens. When we throw away food, we also waste the water, energy, and labor that went into producing it. The environmental cost is huge: rotting food in landfills produces methane, a gas that harms our climate. [1]

[2] In many developed countries, people often buy more food than they need. "Best before" dates sometimes confuse shoppers, who throw away food that is still safe to eat. Restaurants serve large portions that customers cannot finish. Understanding these causes helps us find solutions.

[3] Planning meals before shopping reduces impulse buys. Making a list and sticking to it means we buy only what we will use. Storing food properly in the fridge or freezer helps it last longer. Learning to use leftovers in new dishes cuts waste at home.

[4] Schools and communities can organize food-sharing programs. Some supermarkets now donate unsold food to charities instead of throwing it away. Apps connect people with surplus food to those who need it. These efforts show that everyone has a role to play.

[5] Food waste is a problem we can solve together. Small changes in our daily habits can make a big difference. When we reduce waste, we save money, help the environment, and move closer to a world where no one goes hungry.

## 选项

A. This is why reducing food waste matters for both people and the planet.
B. Studies have identified several reasons why so much food is wasted.
C. There are many simple steps we can take in our own homes.
D. Beyond individual action, collective efforts are also making an impact.
E. Every meal we save is a step toward a more sustainable future.
F. For example, bread and fruit can often be frozen to extend their shelf life.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：第一段列举食物浪费的严重性：全球三分之一被浪费、可养活数十亿人、各环节都发生、浪费资源、产生甲烷危害气候。空格前累积多重问题。选项A "This is why reducing food waste matters for both people and the planet" 承接因果关系，自然引出为何要减少浪费，承上启下。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：第二段开头需引入本段主题。空格后具体列举原因：过量购买、日期混淆、餐厅分量大。选项B "Studies have identified several reasons why so much food is wasted" 作为 Topic sentence，自然引出后文对原因的展开。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：第三段讲个人可采取的措施：计划购物、正确储存、利用剩菜。选项C "There are many simple steps we can take in our own homes" 概括本段主题，与后文列举形成总分关系。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：第四段讲学校、社区、超市、app等集体行动。空格前文为个人层面，本段转向集体。选项D "Beyond individual action, collective efforts are also making an impact" 中"Beyond"完成层次转换，"also"表明递进，衔接自然。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：末段总结：可共同解决、小改变大影响、省钱环保、无人挨饿。选项E "Every meal we save is a step toward a more sustainable future" 呼应"small changes"与"sustainable"，收束有力。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, bread and fruit can often be frozen to extend their shelf life.**：与第三段"Storing food properly in the fridge or freezer"相关，属具体储藏建议。但第三段空格在段首，需主题句而非例证；且若插在列举中间会打断"planning→storing→leftovers"的逻辑链。属于主题相关但位置错位型干扰。
