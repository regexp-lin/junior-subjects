# 03-24 第二篇

**The Power of Saying No**

Many people, especially teenagers, find it very hard to say no when others ask them for help or favor. They worry that refusing might make others think they are selfish or unkind. [1] However, learning to say no when necessary is actually an important skill for protecting your time, energy, and well-being.

When you always say yes to every request, you may end up with too much to do. Your weekends fill up with activities you did not really choose, and your evenings disappear. [2] You might have to give up sleep, miss important deadlines, or feel stressed all the time. In the end, your health and your schoolwork both suffer.

Saying no does not mean you are a bad person. It simply means you know your limits and you choose to respect them. In fact, people who understand their boundaries often have healthier and more honest friendships. [3] For example, if a friend asks you to lend them your homework right before a test, you can politely explain that you are not comfortable with that and suggest another way to help.

Of course, how you say no matters a lot. A rude or cold refusal can hurt feelings and damage relationships. The key is to be honest without being unkind. [4] A kind but clear "I'm sorry, I can't do that this time" works much better than a long excuse that might sound untrue.

[5] Start by saying no to one small thing this week—perhaps an invitation you do not really want to accept. As you practice, you will find it becomes easier, and your life will become more balanced and peaceful.

## 选项

A. The good news is that you can practice this skill step by step.
B. In fact, saying yes to everyone is the best way to make more friends at school.
C. On the other hand, a gentle and honest reply often keeps the friendship strong.
D. As a result, your schedule becomes too full and you have no time for yourself.
E. They believe that a real friend should always agree to help no matter what happens.
F. You have the right to turn down requests that make you feel uncomfortable or go against your values.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] E   [2] D   [3] F   [4] C   [5] A

## 深度解析

- **[1] E**：
  - **线索**：代词指代与动机解释。空前说"他们担心拒绝会让人觉得自己自私或不友善"，描述的是不敢说"不"的人的心理。E选项"They believe that a real friend should always agree to help no matter what happens"（他们相信真正的朋友无论如何都应该答应帮忙）中的"They"指代前文的"many people, especially teenagers"；"believe"与前文"worry"形成心理动机的补充说明。空后"However, learning to say no when necessary is actually an important skill"用转折引出与这种信念相反的观点。

- **[2] D**：
  - **线索**：因果衔接与后果具体化。空前指出"如果你对每个请求都说好，可能会面临太多要做的事"。D选项"As a result, your schedule becomes too full and you have no time for yourself"（结果，你的日程变得太满，没有属于自己的时间）用"As a result"承接因果关系，"schedule becomes too full"与"too much to do"同义替换。空后"You might have to give up sleep, miss deadlines..."则是在D选项基础上的进一步具体列举。

- **[3] F**：
  - **线索**：论点支撑与实例衔接。空前说"说不并不代表你是个坏人，而是意味着你了解自己的界限并选择尊重它们"。F选项"You have the right to turn down requests that make you feel uncomfortable or go against your values"（你有权拒绝让你感到不适或违背你价值观的请求）从"权利"角度强化了"尊重自己的界限"这一论点。空后"For example, if a friend asks you to lend them your homework..."正是对F选项中"requests that make you feel uncomfortable"的具体举例。

- **[4] C**：
  - **线索**：对比转折与建议呼应。空前指出"粗鲁或冷淡的拒绝会伤害感情、破坏关系"，强调"怎么说不"很重要。C选项"On the other hand, a gentle and honest reply often keeps the friendship strong"（另一方面，温和而真诚的回答往往能保持友谊稳固）用"On the other hand"与前文形成对比；"gentle and honest"与"rude or cold"形成对立。空后"A kind but clear 'I'm sorry, I can't do that this time'"正是对"gentle and honest reply"的具体示范。

- **[5] A**：
  - **线索**：建议引入与实践过渡。空[5]位于结尾段段首。前文从心理障碍、过度承诺后果、拒绝的权利、拒绝的方式四方面论述"学会说不"。A选项"The good news is that you can practice this skill step by step"（好消息是，你可以一步步练习这项技能）用"The good news"从理论转向实践，用"practice...step by step"自然引出空后的具体行动——"Start by saying no to one small thing this week"。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **B. In fact, saying yes to everyone is the best way to make more friends at school.**：该选项含有"saying yes""friends""school"等与青少年社交场景相关的高频词。但全文主旨是"学会在必要时说不"的重要性，强调过度答应会损害时间、精力和健康。B选项主张"对所有人说好是交更多朋友的最佳方式"，与全文"应学会拒绝"的核心立场完全相反，且"the best way"这种绝对化表述也是常见干扰特征。属于"话题相关+立场对立"的典型干扰。
