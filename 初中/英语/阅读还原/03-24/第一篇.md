# 03-24 第一篇

**The Benefits of Keeping a Diary**

Have you ever thought about writing down your thoughts at the end of each day? Keeping a diary is one of the oldest and most useful habits for students. It can help you in many different ways. [1] It does not require any special skills or expensive tools—just a notebook and a few minutes of your time.

First of all, writing a diary helps you understand your feelings better. When you put your thoughts on paper, you slow down and think more clearly about what is really bothering you. [2] Sometimes you may discover that what seemed like a big problem is actually something you can deal with after a good night's sleep.

A diary also improves your writing skills over time. The more you write, the easier it becomes to express yourself in words. Your sentences will become clearer and your ideas will flow more naturally. [3] Many successful writers started their journey by keeping a simple daily journal when they were young.

What is more, a diary becomes a precious record of your life. Years later, when you read your old entries, you will be surprised at how much you have changed and how far you have come. [4] Those memories might otherwise fade away, but your diary keeps them alive forever.

[5] Start with just a few sentences each night about what happened during the day and how you felt. Before long, you will find that this small habit brings big rewards to your life.

## 选项

A. In fact, keeping a diary is the main reason why most people forget important events in their lives.
B. The best part about diary writing is that it is easy to begin.
C. You might even find answers to your own questions as you write.
D. This regular practice is like free training for your brain.
E. It helps you look back at both the happy moments and the difficult ones you have overcome.
F. If you would like to try, beginning is simpler than you might think.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] B   [2] C   [3] D   [4] E   [5] F

## 深度解析

- **[1] B**：
  - **线索**：逻辑衔接与主题延续。空前提出写日记是学生最古老、最有用的习惯之一。B选项"The best part about diary writing is that it is easy to begin"（写日记最棒的一点就是很容易开始）延续了"有用"这一 positive 评价，并自然过渡到空后"It does not require any special skills or expensive tools"——两者共同强调日记的"易得性"和"低门槛"。

- **[2] C**：
  - **线索**：同义替换与递进补充。空前描述"把想法写在纸上会让你放慢节奏、想得更清楚"，空后提到"有时你会发现原本看似很大的问题，睡一觉后其实可以应对"。C选项"You might even find answers to your own questions as you write"（你甚至可能在书写的过程中找到自己问题的答案）中的"find answers"与空后的"discover"形成同义替换，"as you write"与"put your thoughts on paper"呼应，构成"书写→思考→发现问题可解决"的完整逻辑链。

- **[3] D**：
  - **线索**：比喻衔接与功能解释。空前指出"写得越多，表达自己就越容易"，强调日常写作练习的效果。D选项"This regular practice is like free training for your brain"（这种定期练习就像给大脑做免费训练）中的"This regular practice"指代前文的"the more you write"，"free training"与前文的"improves your writing skills"形成比喻性解释。空后"Many successful writers started..."则用实例佐证了这种"训练"的长期价值。

- **[4] E**：
  - **线索**：代词指代与功能概括。空前说"多年后读旧日记，你会惊讶于自己变化有多大"，空后说"Those memories might otherwise fade away, but your diary keeps them alive forever"。E选项"It helps you look back at both the happy moments and the difficult ones you have overcome"（它帮助你回顾那些快乐的时刻以及你曾克服的艰难时刻）中的"It"指代前文"a diary"；"look back"与"read your old entries"同义；"happy moments and difficult ones"与后文"memories"和"keeps them alive"完美衔接。

- **[5] F**：
  - **线索**：建议引入与行动过渡。空[5]位于结尾段段首。前文从入门简单、情绪理解、写作提升、人生记录四方面论述日记益处。F选项"If you would like to try, beginning is simpler than you might think"（如果你想试一试，开始比你想象的要简单）用"If you would like to try"向读者发出邀请，用"beginning is simpler"自然引出空后的具体建议——"Start with just a few sentences each night..."。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **A. In fact, keeping a diary is the main reason why most people forget important events in their lives.**：该选项含有"keeping a diary""forget""important events"等与文章主题相关的高频词。但全文主旨是写日记能保留记忆、记录生活，而A选项声称日记是"人们忘记重要事件的主要原因"，与第四段"your diary keeps them alive forever"（日记让记忆永存）完全矛盾，属于"话题复现+立场对立"的典型干扰。
