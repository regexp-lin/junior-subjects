# 10-24 第一篇

**Campus Safety**

Schools should be places where students feel safe to learn and grow. When students feel afraid or worried, it is harder for them to concentrate, make friends, and do their best. Campus safety includes physical safety—buildings, equipment, and emergency plans—and emotional safety—a positive environment where bullying and violence are not tolerated. [1] A safe campus is the foundation for everything else that happens at school; without it, learning and growth suffer.

Physical safety starts with the school building itself. Stairs, hallways, and playgrounds need to be well-maintained. Fire drills and earthquake drills help everyone know what to do in an emergency. Schools should have clear procedures for visitors and secure entrances. [2] When schools invest in safe facilities and regular drills, students and staff are better prepared for both everyday risks and rare emergencies.

Emotional safety is equally important. Bullying—whether in person or online—can cause lasting harm. Students need to know that adults will listen and take action when they report problems. Anti-bullying programs and counseling services can help. [3] Creating a culture where every student feels respected and supported requires effort from teachers, parents, and students—but it makes the school a better place for everyone.

Technology can support campus safety. Cameras in hallways can help staff respond quickly to incidents. Apps can allow students to report concerns anonymously. Some schools use ID badges or sign-in systems to track who is on campus. [4] Technology alone cannot keep schools safe, but when combined with caring adults and clear policies, it can make a real difference.

Campus safety is everyone's responsibility. Teachers, principals, and staff must create and follow safety rules. Parents can teach their children about respect and report concerns to the school. Students can speak up when they see something wrong. [5] When the whole school community works together, safety improves—and every student has a better chance to learn and succeed.

## 选项

A. Campus safety is not an extra—it is the base that allows teaching, learning, and friendship to flourish.
B. Planning ahead and maintaining facilities can prevent many accidents and ensure that everyone knows how to respond when something goes wrong.

D. Tools like cameras and reporting apps can help, but they work best when they support—not replace—human care and attention.
E. Safety is a shared goal, and when everyone plays a part, the whole school benefits.
