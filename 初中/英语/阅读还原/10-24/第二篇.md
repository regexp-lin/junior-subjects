# 10-24 第二篇

**Exploring the Moon**

Humans have dreamed of reaching the Moon for thousands of years. In 1969, that dream came true when Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first people to walk on the Moon. Since then, robots and spacecraft have continued to explore our nearest neighbor in space. [1] The Moon has always captured our imagination, and exploring it has taught us not only about the Moon itself but also about Earth, our place in the universe, and what humans can achieve when they work together.

The first Moon landing was the result of huge effort. Thousands of scientists, engineers, and workers built the rockets and spacecraft. The Apollo program cost billions of dollars and faced many setbacks. [2] The success of the Moon missions proved that with enough resources, talent, and determination, humanity could achieve what had once seemed impossible.

Today, the Moon is still a target for exploration. Several countries have sent orbiters and landers. Private companies are developing new rockets and lunar landers. NASA plans to send astronauts back to the Moon in the coming years. [3] The Moon is no longer a one-time destination—it is becoming a place for ongoing science, technology development, and perhaps future human settlements.

Scientists learn a lot from the Moon. Lunar rocks brought back by Apollo astronauts have helped us understand how the Moon and Earth formed. The Moon has no atmosphere, so its surface preserves a record of impacts from space. [4] Studying the Moon helps us understand the history of our solar system and may even teach us how to live and work in space one day.

The future of Moon exploration looks bright. New missions will search for water ice, which could support future astronauts. Some imagine lunar bases for scientific research or mining. Others see the Moon as a stepping stone to Mars. [5] Whatever the next chapter holds, the Moon will remain a symbol of human curiosity and our desire to explore the unknown—and to reach beyond what we thought was possible.

## 选项

A. The Moon has inspired poets, scientists, and explorers—and the journey to understand it has changed how we see ourselves and our planet.
B. Reaching the Moon was a triumph of human ingenuity and teamwork—one that showed the world what was possible when nations set ambitious goals.
C. The Moon is once again at the center of space exploration, and this time the goal is to stay longer and learn more.
D. What we learn from the Moon today may shape how we explore other worlds tomorrow—and how we protect and understand our own.
E. The story of the Moon is still being written, and the next generation of explorers will add new chapters to a journey that began thousands of years ago.
F. For example, China's Chang'e missions have landed on the far side of the Moon and brought back lunar samples, expanding our knowledge of the Moon's geology.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍1969年登月及后续探索，空后说"The Moon has always captured our imagination, and exploring it has taught us not only about the Moon itself but also about Earth, our place in the universe, and what humans can achieve"。A的"The Moon has inspired poets, scientists, and explorers—and the journey to understand it has changed how we see ourselves and our planet"与空后captured imagination、taught us about Earth、our place呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说登月需要大量人力物力、面临挫折，空后说"The success of the Moon missions proved that with enough resources, talent, and determination, humanity could achieve what had once seemed impossible"。B的"Reaching the Moon was a triumph of human ingenuity and teamwork—one that showed the world what was possible when nations set ambitious goals"与空后success、proved、achieve呼应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说多国发射探测器、私企研发、NASA计划重返月球，空后说"The Moon is no longer a one-time destination—it is becoming a place for ongoing science, technology development, and perhaps future human settlements"。C的"The Moon is once again at the center of space exploration, and this time the goal is to stay longer and learn more"与空后ongoing、future settlements衔接。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说月岩研究、无大气保留撞击记录，空后说"Studying the Moon helps us understand the history of our solar system and may even teach us how to live and work in space one day"。D的"What we learn from the Moon today may shape how we explore other worlds tomorrow—and how we protect and understand our own"与空后understand history、live in space呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说未来任务（水冰、基地、火星跳板），空后说"Whatever the next chapter holds, the Moon will remain a symbol of human curiosity and our desire to explore the unknown—and to reach beyond what we thought was possible"。E的"The story of the Moon is still being written, and the next generation of explorers will add new chapters to a journey that began thousands of years ago"与空后next chapter、symbol、curiosity呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, China's Chang'e missions have landed on the far side of the Moon...**：以For example开头，是具体任务举例。各空均需概括性或过渡性句子，F为举例型干扰项。
