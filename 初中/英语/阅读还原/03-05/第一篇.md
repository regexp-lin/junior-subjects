# 03-05 第一篇

**Overcoming Procrastination**

Almost everyone has experienced the desire to put off a difficult task until tomorrow. [1] However, when it becomes a daily habit, it can lead to serious problems like stress and poor grades. Understanding why we do it is the first step to solving the problem.

Many people believe that procrastination (拖延症) is simply a sign of laziness. [2] Rather, it is often a way of dealing with negative feelings about a task. When we feel a piece of work is too boring or too difficult, our brain tries to protect us by avoiding it. 

Fortunately, there are several effective ways to beat this bad habit. The most common method is breaking a large project into smaller, manageable parts. [3] For example, instead of writing an entire essay in one night, you can start by just writing an outline. This makes the work seem much less frightening.

Another helpful strategy is to use the "Pomodoro Technique". This involves working completely focused for 25 minutes, followed by a 5-minute break. [4] It gives your brain a chance to rest and prepare for the next period of work.

Finally, remember to be kind to yourself. If you waste a day doing nothing, don't beat yourself up. [5] Just forgive yourself and promise to do better the next day. Small changes in your routine can make a big difference over time.

## 选项

A. These short rest periods are extremely important for your brain.
B. Such negative self-talk will only make the situation worse.
C. However, studies show that it has little to do with being lazy.
D. This common behavior is something we all share from time to time.
E. By focusing on one small step at a time, you can easily get started.
F. Therefore, breaking tasks down will only take you much more time.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] D   [2] C   [3] E   [4] A   [5] B

## 深度解析

- **[1] D**：
  - **线索**：代词指代与上下文语境。空前提到“almost everyone has experienced the desire to put off a difficult task”（几乎每个人都经历过推迟困难任务的欲望），D选项中的代词“This common behavior”（这个常见的行为）完美指代前一句提到的这种欲望和行为。同时，D句意“这是我们时不时都会共有的体会”与下一句引出的转折“However, when it becomes a daily habit”（然而，当它变成日常习惯时）形成严密的逻辑对比。
- **[2] C**：
  - **线索**：逻辑关联与词汇复现。空前提及“Many people believe that procrastination is simply a sign of laziness.”（很多人认为拖延症仅仅是懒惰的表现）。C选项以“However”（然而）开头表示转折，句中的“it has little to do with being lazy”（这与懒惰几乎没有关系）直接反驳了前文的观点，并与空后的“Rather, it is often a way of dealing with negative feelings”（相反，它通常是处理负面情绪的一种方式）在逻辑上完美衔接。
- **[3] E**：
  - **线索**：同义替换与举例说明。空前提到了克服拖延的方法是“breaking a large project into smaller, manageable parts”（把大项目分解成小的、易于控制的部分）。E选项中的“focusing on one small step at a time”（一次只关注一个小步骤）是对前句“smaller parts”的同义替换；且E项填入后，紧随其后的“For example”（例如）正好是以“只写大纲”来具体说明这种从小步骤开始的方法，逻辑顺畅。
- **[4] A**：
  - **线索**：代词指代与词汇复现。空前介绍了番茄工作法，具体为专注工作25分钟，“followed by a 5-minute break”（随后休息5分钟）。A选项中的“These short rest periods”（这些短暂的休息时间）完美指代前一句的“5-minute break”，且A句中提到的“important for your brain”与空后一句“gives your brain a chance to rest”主语和话题一致，承上启下。
- **[5] B**：
  - **线索**：词义连贯与代词指代。空前的建议是“don't beat yourself up”（不要过度责备自己）。B选项中的“Such negative self-talk”（这种负面的自我对话）准确概括了前文的责备自己的行为。填入B项后，说明了过度自责的坏处（只会让情况更糟），从而引出后文“Just forgive yourself”（只要原谅自己就好），前后逻辑因果自洽。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. Therefore, breaking tasks down will only take you much more time.**：该选项包含了文章的高频词汇“breaking tasks down”（分解任务），看似与第三段的内容相关。但其带有表示劣势的语义“will only take you much more time”（只会花费你更多时间），与第三段主旨“effective ways to beat this bad habit”（克服坏习惯的有效方法）的积极逻辑完全相悖。虽然有关键词复现，但逻辑背离，为典型的高质量“貌合神离”干扰项。
