# 03-05 第二篇

**The Hidden Value of Failure**

In our society, success is always celebrated, while failure is often seen as something to be ashamed of. As a result, many of us are afraid of making mistakes. [1] But what if we change how we think about failing? 

History is full of great people who experienced huge failures before achieving success. Take Thomas Edison, for example. [2] He famously said, "I have not failed. I've just found 10,000 ways that won't work." His story proves that failure is just a stepping stone to success.

Furthermore, failing teaches us valuable lessons that success cannot. When everything goes smoothly, we seldom stop to ask why. [3] This self-reflection helps us discover our weaknesses and improve our skills.

Moreover, failing builds resilience (韧性), which is the ability to bounce back from difficult situations. Each time we fall and get back up, we become mentally stronger. [4] With this strength, we are much better prepared to face future challenges in our lives.

In short, we should not let the fear of failure stop us from trying new things. [5] Next time you fail at something, don't just give up. Instead, smile and ask yourself, "What can I learn from this?"

## 选项

A. He failed thousands of times before inventing the light bulb.
B. Therefore, successful people usually make fewer mistakes in life.
C. This quality teaches us how to survive when things go wrong.
D. After all, every mistake is a new chance to grow and improve.
E. We usually try our best to avoid it in order to protect our pride.
F. However, when we fail, we are forced to think about what went wrong.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] E   [2] A   [3] F   [4] C   [5] D

## 深度解析

- **[1] E**：
  - **线索**：代词指代与逻辑递进。空前指出“many of us are afraid of making mistakes”（我们中很多人害怕犯错）。E选项“We usually try our best to avoid it in order to protect our pride”（我们通常尽力避免它以保护自尊），其中代词“it”指代前文的“failure”或“making mistakes”。E句是对前一句“害怕”的自然行为延续（因为害怕，所以尽力避免），并且很好地引出了空后作者的发问“But what if we change how we think about failing?”（但如果我们改变对失败的看法呢？），逻辑顺畅。
- **[2] A**：
  - **线索**：代词指代与上下文语境。空前提到了具体人物“Thomas Edison”（托马斯·爱迪生）。A选项以代词“He”开头，直接指代爱迪生，且“failed thousands of times before inventing the light bulb”（在发明电灯前失败了数千次）不仅符合常识，也完美衔接了后一句他的名言“I have not failed. I've just found 10,000 ways that won't work.”（我没有失败，只是找出了1万种行不通的方法）。
- **[3] F**：
  - **线索**：逻辑关联词与同义指代。空前提及“When everything goes smoothly, we seldom stop to ask why.”（一切顺利时，我们很少停下来问为什么）。F选项以“However”（然而）转折，提出“when we fail, we are forced to think about what went wrong”（失败时，我们被迫思考哪里出了错），形成了鲜明的成功与失败的境遇对比。此外，F选项中的“think about what went wrong”正是空后一句中“This self-reflection”（这种自我反思）所指代的具体行为。
- **[4] C**：
  - **线索**：核心概念复现与代词指代。段落首句提出了“resilience（韧性）”的概念，并在空前解释为“Each time we fall and get back up, we become mentally stronger”（每次跌倒再爬起来，精神上都会变得更强大）。C选项中的“This quality”（这种品质）指代正是前文描述的“韧性”。“teaches us how to survive when things go wrong”对应了前文的“bounce back from difficult situations”，同时与空后“With this strength”（带着这种力量）相呼应。
- **[5] D**：
  - **线索**：句意总结与呼应。空前作为结论段首句，倡导“不要让对失败的恐惧阻止我们尝试新事物”。D选项“After all, every mistake is a new chance to grow and improve.”（毕竟，每一次错误都是成长和进步的新机会）是对全文主旨的完美提炼，解释了为什么我们不要害怕失败。同时，它也自然过渡到了空后的具体建议“Next time you fail at something, don't just give up.”（下次失败时，别轻易放弃）。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **B. Therefore, successful people usually make fewer mistakes in life.**：这一项极具迷惑性。它包含了“successful people”和“mistakes”这两个文章谈论的话题词汇，看似可以放在第二段（谈论伟人）。但由于选项使用了因果连词“Therefore”（因此），且句意为“成功人士一生中犯的错更少”，这与整篇文章的核心立意——“失败具有隐藏价值，爱迪生犯过数千次甚至一万次错”（成功者也经常失败）——是彻底相悖的。因此从深层语意和逻辑上应予排除。
