# 10-18 第二篇

**Light Pollution: When Night Loses Its Darkness**

When we look up at the night sky in a big city, we often see only a few stars. The rest are hidden by the glow of streetlights, signs, and buildings. This is called light pollution—the excessive or misuse of artificial light that brightens the night. [1] Light pollution is a growing problem that affects not only our view of the stars but also the health of humans, animals, and the environment.

For humans, too much artificial light at night can disturb sleep. Our bodies need darkness to produce melatonin, a hormone that helps us rest. Bright screens and streetlights can trick our brains into thinking it is still daytime. [2] As a result, people who live in brightly lit areas may have more trouble sleeping, feel more tired during the day, and face a higher risk of certain health problems over time.

Wildlife is also harmed by light pollution. Many animals use natural day and night cycles to find food, migrate, or avoid predators. Artificial light can confuse them. Sea turtle hatchlings may crawl toward streetlights instead of the ocean. Birds that migrate at night can be drawn off course by tall, lit-up buildings. [3] When we change the natural balance of day and night, we put many species at risk and damage the ecosystems that depend on them.

Light pollution also wastes energy and money. Lights that shine upward or stay on when not needed do not help people see better—they simply add to the glow in the sky. Cities and businesses can reduce light pollution by using shields on streetlights, turning off unnecessary lights, and choosing bulbs that direct light downward. [4] Simple changes like these can cut energy use, save money, and bring back the beauty of a dark, starry night.

Thankfully, more people are becoming aware of light pollution. Some cities have adopted "dark sky" policies to protect certain areas from excess light. Schools teach students about the importance of dark nights for science and nature. [5] With greater awareness and better technology, we can enjoy the benefits of light while still preserving the wonder of a truly dark night sky.

## 选项

A. Light pollution touches almost every part of our lives, and its effects are far more serious than just losing sight of the stars.
B. Poor sleep has been linked to tiredness, difficulty concentrating, and even long-term health issues, which is why reducing light at night matters for public health.
C. Protecting wildlife from light pollution means protecting the natural rhythms that have guided life on Earth for millions of years.
D. Solving the problem of light pollution does not require giving up light—it only requires using it more wisely.
E. The goal is clear: we can have both safe, well-lit streets and a night sky full of stars, if we choose to act.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍光污染定义，空后说"Light pollution is a growing problem that affects not only our view of the stars but also the health of humans, animals, and the environment"。A的"touches almost every part of our lives, and its effects are far more serious than just losing sight of the stars"与空后"affects... view... health... environment"完美呼应。

  - **线索**：空前说人造光干扰睡眠、影响褪黑素，空后说"As a result, people who live in brightly lit areas may have more trouble sleeping, feel more tired... higher risk of certain health problems"。B的"Poor sleep has been linked to tiredness, difficulty concentrating, and even long-term health issues"与空后健康后果衔接，"reducing light at night matters for public health"呼应主题。

  - **线索**：空前举海龟、鸟类等野生动物受光污染影响的例子，空后说"When we change the natural balance of day and night, we put many species at risk"。C的"Protecting wildlife from light pollution means protecting the natural rhythms that have guided life on Earth"与空后生态破坏、物种风险呼应。

  - **线索**：空前说灯具屏蔽、关闭不必要灯光、选用向下照明的灯泡等办法，空后说"Simple changes like these can cut energy use, save money, and bring back the beauty of a dark, starry night"。D的"Solving the problem... does not require giving up light—it only requires using it more wisely"与空前具体措施、空后"simple changes"呼应。
