# 10-18 第一篇

**How Pets Improve Our Health**

Many people keep pets at home—dogs, cats, fish, or birds. What they may not realize is that caring for an animal can actually improve their health. Research over the past few decades has shown surprising benefits. [1] Pets do more than provide company; they can lower stress, improve mood, and even help people live longer and healthier lives.

One of the clearest benefits is reduced stress. When people spend time with their pets, their bodies often produce less of the stress hormone cortisol. Petting a dog or watching fish swim can make people feel calmer. [2] This calming effect is especially helpful for people who face high pressure at work or school, as it gives them a way to relax and recharge at home.

Pets also encourage people to be more active. Dog owners, for example, need to walk their dogs every day. This means more time outdoors, more exercise, and often more social contact with other pet owners. [3] Regular activity is linked to better heart health, stronger muscles, and a lower risk of obesity, which is why doctors sometimes recommend pets for people who need to move more.

For children and the elderly, pets can offer special support. Children who grow up with pets often learn responsibility and empathy. Older adults may feel less lonely when they have a pet to care for and talk to. [4] Pets can act as companions that provide comfort, reduce feelings of isolation, and bring joy to people of all ages.

Of course, owning a pet is a big responsibility. Pets need food, exercise, and regular visits to the vet. Before getting a pet, families should think about whether they have the time and space. [5] When people are ready for the commitment, the health benefits of pet ownership can make the effort worthwhile—both for the pet and for their own well-being.

## 选项

A. The science behind pet ownership suggests that our furry (or feathered) friends offer real health advantages that go beyond simple companionship.
B. Having a pet at home creates natural reasons to get up, move around, and stay active throughout the day.
C. Studies show that people who exercise regularly have healthier hearts and bodies, and pets are often the reason they get that exercise.
D. Whether it is a child learning to feed a hamster or an elderly person chatting with a cat, pets fill important emotional and social roles in our lives.
E. The benefits are real, but so is the work—and for those who are prepared, the rewards can be great.
F. For example, a study in Sweden found that dog owners had a 33% lower risk of death from heart disease, partly because they walked more often.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前说养宠有健康益处的研究发现，空后说"Pets do more than provide company; they can lower stress, improve mood, and even help people live longer"。A的"our furry friends offer real health advantages that go beyond simple companionship"与空后"more than provide company"和具体益处呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说与宠物相处可减压、使人平静，空后说"This calming effect is especially helpful for people who face high pressure... relax and recharge"。B的"creates natural reasons to get up, move around, and stay active"点出宠物带来的日常活动，这些活动本身就是一种放松与恢复，与空后"relax and recharge"呼应，并为下段运动益处做铺垫。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说养狗需每日遛狗、更多户外运动和社会接触，空后说"Regular activity is linked to better heart health, stronger muscles, and a lower risk of obesity"。C的"people who exercise regularly have healthier hearts and bodies, and pets are often the reason they get that exercise"与空前pet促活动、空后运动带来健康完美衔接。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说儿童和老人从宠物获得责任、同理心、陪伴，空后说"Pets can act as companions that provide comfort, reduce feelings of isolation"。D的"Whether it is a child learning to feed a hamster or an elderly person chatting with a cat, pets fill important emotional and social roles"与空后companions、comfort、isolation呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说养宠是责任、需时间空间，空后说"When people are ready for the commitment, the health benefits... can make the effort worthwhile"。E的"The benefits are real, but so is the work—and for those who are prepared, the rewards can be great"与空前责任、空后付出与回报呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, a study in Sweden found that dog owners had a 33% lower risk...**：以For example开头，是具体研究案例。各空均需概括性或过渡性句子，F为举例型干扰项。
