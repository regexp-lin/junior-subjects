**Paper Art and Origami: Folding Creativity**

Paper folding, or origami, began in Japan centuries ago but has spread around the world. The art uses only a single sheet of paper—no cuts, no glue—to create everything from simple cranes to complex dragons. With just folds, artists can turn a flat square into a 3D shape. This simplicity is part of what makes origami both accessible and deeply satisfying. [1]

Modern origami has grown beyond traditional designs. Mathematicians and engineers study folding patterns to understand structures in nature and design better products. Folded paper can be strong and stable—think of cardboard boxes or pleated materials. Some scientists use origami principles to design solar panels that fold for space travel or medical stents that expand inside the body. [2]

Learning origami builds patience and spatial thinking. You must follow steps carefully and visualize how each fold changes the shape. Mistakes are easy to make, but they also teach you to slow down and pay attention. Many people find folding meditative; the focus required can help calm the mind. Schools sometimes use origami to teach geometry and problem-solving. [3]

You can start with basic models like the classic crane or a simple boat. Plenty of free tutorials exist online, and all you need is paper. Thinner paper works better for complex folds. As you progress, you can try modular origami, where many small pieces lock together to form larger structures. There is no rush—the joy is in the process as much as the result. [4]

Whether as a hobby, a learning tool, or an inspiration for science and design, paper folding continues to connect people across cultures and ages. A single sheet and a few folds can unlock endless possibilities. [5]

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍折纸起源日本、传遍世界、仅用单张纸无剪切无胶水、从简单鹤到复杂龙、仅靠折叠变平面为立体、这种简洁使其易上手又令人满足，空后需总结折纸的创意门槛。A的"creative expression does not require expensive tools"与"only a single sheet of paper"呼应，"paper, patience, and imagination"与前文对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说现代折纸超越传统、数学家和工程师研究折痕理解自然结构设计产品、折纸可坚固稳定、科学家用折纸原理设计太空折叠太阳能板或体内扩张支架，空后需总结古老艺术的新用途。B的"In this way"承接前文，"the ancient art has found new roles in science, technology, and medicine"与前文对应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说学习折纸培养耐心与空间思维、需仔细跟步想象每次折叠的变化、易出错但也让人放慢注意力、许多人觉得折叠具冥想效果、学校用折纸教几何与解题，空后需总结折纸的教育价值。C的"These skills"指代前文，"more than a pastime—it becomes a way to develop focus and logical thinking"与前文呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说可从经典鹤或简单船起步、网上有免费教程、只需纸张、薄纸更适合复杂折叠、进阶可尝试模块折纸、不必着急，乐趣在过程与结果，空后需总结入门与进阶的灵活性。D的"Anyone can begin with simple models"与前文对应，"gradually take on more challenging projects at their own pace"与前文呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说无论作为爱好、学习工具或科学设计灵感、折纸持续连接不同文化与年龄、一张纸几次折叠可解锁无限可能，空后需总结折纸的广泛影响。E的"From traditional craft to modern innovation"与前文呼应，"simple ideas can have a wide impact"与前文对应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the traditional origami crane has become...**：以For example开头，为具体折纸案例（纸鹤象征和平）。各空均为概括或过渡句；[1]或[4]易被误选因涉及折纸种类，但该空需总结折纸的入门价值或创意门槛，而非补充纸鹤的文化象征细节。
