# 05-14 第一篇

**Staying Safe Online: Internet Safety Tips for Teens**

The Internet has become a big part of our daily life. We use it for study, entertainment, and staying in touch with friends. However, the online world also has risks that we need to understand. [1] In fact, many young people have been tricked by strangers, had their personal information stolen, or seen content that made them uncomfortable. That is why learning to stay safe online is as important as learning to cross the street safely in the real world.

Protecting your personal information is the first rule. [2] Never share your password with anyone, not even your best friend. Do not post your full name, address, phone number, or school name where strangers can see it. Be careful with links in messages—they may lead to fake（假冒的） websites that steal your data. In other words, what you share online can stay online forever, so think before you post.

Be careful who you talk to online. [3] Not everyone on the Internet is who they say they are. Some people create fake profiles to cheat or hurt others. If someone you do not know asks for your photos, personal details, or wants to meet you in person, say no and tell a trusted adult. Meeting online friends in real life can be dangerous if you have never met them before. For this reason, staying cautious with strangers online can protect you from real harm.

Cyberbullying is another risk to watch out for. [4] If someone sends you mean messages, posts embarrassing things about you, or tries to make you feel bad online, that is cyberbullying. Do not reply to bullies or share their messages. Save the evidence and report it to your parents, teachers, or the website. You have the right to block or unfollow anyone who makes you feel unsafe. So remember: you do not have to put up with online abuse—help is available.

[5] In short, the Internet can be a useful and fun place if we use it wisely. By protecting our information, being careful with strangers, and standing up to cyberbullying, we can enjoy the good side of the web while avoiding its dangers. Stay smart, stay safe, and always ask for help when something feels wrong.

## 选项

A. That is because young people face real dangers when they use the Internet.
B. In other words, guarding your private information online is very important.
C. That is why we must be careful about who we trust on the Internet.
D. It is true that we should not accept cyberbullying and can seek help.
E. In conclusion, we can enjoy the Internet safely by following basic rules.
F. For example, some apps help parents monitor their children's screen time.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：引出青少年面临的真实网络风险。空前描述"The Internet has become a big part of our daily life...we use it for study, entertainment, staying in touch...However, the online world also has risks"，空后说明"In fact, many young people have been tricked by strangers, had their personal information stolen, or seen content that made them uncomfortable...learning to stay safe online is as important as learning to cross the street safely"。A选项"That is because young people face real dangers when they use the Internet"（那是因为青少年在使用网络时面临真实危险）中"That is because"解释为何需重视网络安全，"real dangers"与空后被欺骗、信息被盗、不良内容等具体风险精准对应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：总领个人信息保护的核心原则。空前提出"Protecting your personal information is the first rule"，空后列举"Never share your password with anyone...Do not post your full name, address, phone number, or school name...Be careful with links in messages—they may lead to fake websites that steal your data...what you share online can stay online forever, so think before you post"。B选项"In other words, guarding your private information online is very important"（换言之，在网上保护你的个人信息非常重要）中"In other words"概括空前"protecting your personal information"，"guarding your private information"与空后密码、身份、链接及"think before you post"完美呼应，衔接自然。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：网络陌生人的信任风险。空前提出"Be careful who you talk to online"，空后详述"Not everyone on the Internet is who they say they are...Some people create fake profiles to cheat or hurt others...If someone you do not know asks for your photos, personal details, or wants to meet you in person, say no and tell a trusted adult...Meeting online friends in real life can be dangerous...staying cautious with strangers online can protect you from real harm"。C选项"That is why we must be careful about who we trust on the Internet"（因此我们必须谨慎对待在网上信任谁）中"That is why"承接空前"be careful who you talk to"，"careful about who we trust"与空后虚假身份、拒绝陌生人索取及线下见面风险精准对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：应对网络欺凌的态度与途径。空前提出"Cyberbullying is another risk to watch out for"，空后说明"If someone sends you mean messages, posts embarrassing things...that is cyberbullying...Do not reply...Save the evidence and report it...You have the right to block or unfollow...you do not have to put up with online abuse—help is available"。D选项"It is true that we should not accept cyberbullying and can seek help"（的确，我们不应忍受网络欺凌且可以寻求帮助）中"It is true that"加强衔接，"should not accept...can seek help"与空后不回复、保存证据、举报、屏蔽及"help is available"完美呼应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与安全用网总结。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"the Internet can be a useful and fun place if we use it wisely...By protecting our information, being careful with strangers, and standing up to cyberbullying, we can enjoy the good side of the web while avoiding its dangers...Stay smart, stay safe, and always ask for help when something feels wrong"。E选项"In conclusion, we can enjoy the Internet safely by following basic rules"（总之，我们可通过遵循基本规则安全享受网络）用"In conclusion"总结全文，"enjoy...safely by following basic rules"与空后三大要点及"stay smart, stay safe"形成呼应，收束有力。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, some apps help parents monitor their children's screen time.**：涉及家长监控应用，易被误填[2]或[5]。但F讨论的是家长监管工具，而空[2]前后文是青少年自身应如何保护个人信息，空[5]总结的是个人应遵循的用网规则；F属于家长视角的外部监管，与空[2]的自我防护、空[5]的个人规则论述主体不同。F属"主题相关但主体错位"型干扰。
