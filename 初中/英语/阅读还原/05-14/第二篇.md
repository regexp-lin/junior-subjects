**The Importance of Our Mother Tongue: Why Our First Language Matters**

A mother tongue is the first language we learn as children—usually from our parents and family. For many people, it is the language they use at home, with friends, and in their hearts. [1] In fact, our mother tongue shapes how we think, express ourselves, and connect with our culture. It carries the stories, songs, and wisdom of our ancestors. That is why keeping our mother tongue strong is important, even when we learn other languages at school or for work.

Learning a second language is valuable, but it should not replace the first. [2] Some children grow up in places where the school language is different from their home language. They may feel pressure to use only the school language and forget their mother tongue. However, research shows that being strong in your first language actually helps you learn other languages better. In other words, we do not have to choose one over the other—both can grow together.

The mother tongue connects us to our roots. [3] Through our first language, we learn about our family history, traditions, and values. Grandparents can pass down stories and advice in the language they know best. When we keep our mother tongue, we keep a link to who we are and where we came from. In a world that is becoming more global, holding on to our roots can give us a sense of belonging and identity. For this reason, the mother tongue is not just a tool for communication—it is part of our identity.

Schools and families can help protect mother tongues. [4] Parents can speak their mother tongue at home and read books in that language to their children. Schools can offer classes or activities that celebrate different languages and cultures. When children see that their mother tongue is valued, they feel proud and are more willing to use it. So everyone—families, schools, and communities—has a role in keeping languages alive.

[5] In short, our mother tongue is a precious gift that connects us to our past and shapes our future. By valuing it at home and at school, we can keep our cultural heritage alive while still enjoying the benefits of learning other languages. Remember: the language of your heart deserves to be spoken and heard.

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：总领母语对思维与文化的深层影响。空前描述"A mother tongue is the first language we learn...from our parents and family...the language they use at home, with friends, and in their hearts"，空后说明"In fact, our mother tongue shapes how we think, express ourselves, and connect with our culture...carries the stories, songs, and wisdom of our ancestors...keeping our mother tongue strong is important, even when we learn other languages"。A选项"That is because our first language influences our thinking and our cultural identity"（那是因为我们的母语影响我们的思维和文化认同）中"That is because"解释母语的重要性，"influences our thinking and cultural identity"与空后shapes how we think、connect with our culture及stories of ancestors精准对应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：母语与二语可并行发展。空前提出"Learning a second language is valuable, but it should not replace the first"，空后说明"Some children...school language is different from their home language...may feel pressure to use only the school language and forget their mother tongue...research shows that being strong in your first language actually helps you learn other languages better...we do not have to choose one over the other—both can grow together"。B选项"In other words, we can be strong in both our mother tongue and other languages"（换言之，我们可以在母语和其他语言上都保持强劲）中"In other words"概括空前及空后结论，"strong in both"与空后"both can grow together"及"first language helps learn other languages"完美呼应，衔接自然。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：母语与根脉、身份的联系。空前提出"The mother tongue connects us to our roots"，空后详述"Through our first language, we learn about our family history, traditions, and values...Grandparents can pass down stories and advice in the language they know best...When we keep our mother tongue, we keep a link to who we are and where we came from...holding on to our roots can give us a sense of belonging and identity...the mother tongue is not just a tool for communication—it is part of our identity"。C选项"That is why our mother tongue is tied to who we are and where we come from"（因此母语与我们的身份和来历紧密相连）中"That is why"承接空前"connects us to our roots"，"tied to who we are and where we come from"与空后family history、roots、identity精准对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：家庭与学校在保护母语中的角色。空前提出"Schools and families can help protect mother tongues"，空后列举"Parents can speak their mother tongue at home and read books in that language...Schools can offer classes or activities that celebrate different languages and cultures...When children see that their mother tongue is valued, they feel proud...everyone—families, schools, and communities—has a role in keeping languages alive"。D选项"It is true that families and schools can work together to support mother tongues"（的确，家庭和学校可以共同支持母语）中"It is true that"加强衔接，"families and schools work together"与空后家长在家使用、学校开设课程及"everyone has a role"精准对应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与代际传承。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"our mother tongue is a precious gift that connects us to our past and shapes our future...By valuing it at home and at school, we can keep our cultural heritage alive while still enjoying the benefits of learning other languages...the language of your heart deserves to be spoken and heard"。E选项"In conclusion, we should cherish and protect our mother tongue for generations to come"（总之，我们应珍惜和保护母语，使之代代相传）用"In conclusion"总结全文，"cherish and protect...for generations"与空后"precious gift"及"keep cultural heritage alive"形成呼应，收束有力。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, some people use translation apps to communicate when traveling abroad.**：涉及翻译应用与多语交际，易被误填[2]。但F讨论的是出国旅行时用翻译工具沟通，而空[2]前后文强调母语与二语可共同发展、母语强有助于学二语，与旅行沟通场景无关；F属于实用工具层面的多语使用，与空[2]的母语与二语关系及语言发展论述不匹配。F属"主题相关但论述场景错位"型干扰。
