# 10-25 第二篇

**Fighting Waste in Daily Life**

Waste has become a serious problem in many parts of the world. Every day, we throw away food, plastic, paper, and other materials that could often be reused or recycled. This harms the environment, wastes resources, and contributes to pollution and climate change. [1] Reducing waste is not just a personal responsibility—it is something that everyone, from families to schools to businesses, can help achieve.

One of the biggest sources of waste is food. Studies show that about one-third of all food produced is never eaten. Some goes bad before it reaches stores; more is thrown away by families and restaurants. [2] Wasting food wastes the water, land, and energy used to grow it, and when it rots in landfills, it produces harmful gases that affect the climate.

Plastic is another major problem. Single-use bags, bottles, and packaging are used for minutes but can last in the environment for hundreds of years. Many countries are now banning or charging for plastic bags, and more people are choosing reusable bottles and containers. [3] Changing our habits around plastic requires both personal choices and support from governments and companies that produce and sell these products.

Schools and communities can play an important role. Teaching children about waste reduction from a young age helps them form good habits. Community programs that collect and recycle materials, or share unwanted items with those who need them, are also growing. [4] When we work together, small actions add up to real change, and young people learn that their choices matter.

Fighting waste does not mean living without comfort or convenience. It means being more mindful about what we buy, use, and throw away. Simple steps like planning meals, composting food scraps, and choosing products with less packaging can make a big difference. [5] Every step we take toward less waste is a step toward a healthier planet and a better future for all.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前指出浪费问题严重、危害环境与资源，空后需概括全社会参与的必要性。A的"not just a personal responsibility"与"everyone, from families to schools to businesses"呼应前文问题的广泛性，并引出"can help achieve"的共同行动方向。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说约三分之一食物被浪费，有的变质、有的被家庭和餐馆丢弃，空后需说明食物浪费的多重负面影响。B的"Wasting food wastes the water, land, and energy"承接食物浪费，"when it rots in landfills... harmful gases"与空后气候变化形成因果。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说塑料问题、各国限塑、越来越多人用可重复使用物品，空后需总结改变的途径。C的"Changing our habits around plastic requires both personal choices and support from governments and companies"与空后个人选择与政策、企业责任呼应，逻辑完整。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说学校教育和社区项目的作用——从小培养习惯、回收与分享，空后需总结协作与青少年影响。D的"When we work together, small actions add up"与社区项目呼应，"young people learn that their choices matter"与"teaching children from a young age"衔接。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说抗浪费不牺牲舒适，只需更 mindful，并举例具体措施，空后需总结每步行动的意义。E的"Every step we take toward less waste"与"simple steps"呼应，"healthier planet and better future"是全文主旨的收尾升华。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, some supermarkets now sell "ugly" vegetables at lower prices to reduce food waste.**：以For example开头，为具体案例（超市卖丑蔬菜）。各空均为概括或过渡句；[2]虽涉及食物浪费，但需的是食物浪费的多重危害概括，而非单一商业做法举例。
