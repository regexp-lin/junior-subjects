**Fairy Tales: More Than Just Stories for Children**

Fairy tales like "Cinderella," "The Three Little Pigs," and "The Little Red Riding Hood" have been told for generations. Many people think they are only for young children, but these stories have deeper meanings and can teach valuable lessons at any age. [1] In fact, fairy tales often deal with universal themes—good versus evil, courage, kindness, and the importance of hard work. Characters face problems, make choices, and learn from their mistakes. Through these stories, children and adults can explore difficult feelings like fear, jealousy（嫉妒）, and hope in a safe way. That is why fairy tales have lasting value far beyond simple entertainment.

How do fairy tales help children grow? [2] Stories with clear heroes and villains（反派）help children understand the difference between right and wrong. When the hero overcomes challenges through courage or cleverness, children learn that effort and perseverance（毅力）pay off. Fairy tales also show that bad choices can lead to bad outcomes, while good choices bring rewards. In other words, fairy tales are a gentle way to teach moral lessons without preaching（说教）. Children absorb these lessons while enjoying the magic and adventure of the story.

Fairy tales also build imagination and language skills. [3] Talking animals, enchanted forests（魔法森林）, and brave princes or princesses open up a world of possibilities. When children listen to or read fairy tales, they imagine scenes and characters in their minds. This helps develop creativity and the ability to think beyond the here and now. Many fairy tales use repeated phrases and clear story structure, which makes them easier for young readers to follow and remember. For this reason, fairy tales are excellent tools for both emotional and cognitive（认知的）development.

Some people worry that fairy tales are too scary or show old-fashioned ideas. [4] It is true that some stories include scary moments like a wolf eating a grandmother, or outdated roles like girls waiting to be rescued. Parents and teachers can choose versions that suit their children's age, skip the scariest parts, or discuss the stories together. We can also talk about how ideas have changed over time—for example, today we know that everyone can be a hero, not just princes. So fairy tales can still be useful if we use them thoughtfully and adapt them to our times.

[5] In short, fairy tales are more than bedtime stories. They offer lessons about life, build imagination, and help us understand our feelings. Whether we read them as children or rediscover them as adults, fairy tales remind us of the power of storytelling and the wisdom that has been passed down through the ages. Next time you hear a fairy tale, look for the lesson hidden inside—you might be surprised by what you find.

- **[2] B**：

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：童话对情感与认知发展的双重促进。空前提出"Fairy tales also build imagination and language skills"，空后详述"Talking animals, enchanted forests, and brave princes or princesses open up a world of possibilities...When children listen to or read fairy tales, they imagine scenes and characters in their minds...This helps develop creativity and the ability to think beyond the here and now...Many fairy tales use repeated phrases and clear story structure...fairy tales are excellent tools for both emotional and cognitive development"。C选项"That is why fairy tales support both emotional growth and thinking skills"（因此童话同时支持情感发展和思维能力）中"That is why"承接空前及空后结论，"emotional growth and thinking skills"与空后imagination、creativity、cognitive development精准对应，逻辑紧密。
