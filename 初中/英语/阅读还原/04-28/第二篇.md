# 04-28 第二篇

**Learning Across Generations: Wisdom That Connects Us**

Young people and older people often live in different worlds. The young spend much of their time online and in school; the elderly may have grown up without computers or the Internet. [1] Yet when different generations spend time together and share what they know, both sides gain something valuable. Grandparents can teach grandchildren about family history and old skills. Young people can show their elders how to use smartphones or video chat. This exchange of knowledge and experience makes both generations feel more connected and understood.

In many cultures, elders have always been the keepers of wisdom. [2] They remember how things were done before—how to fix a broken tool, how to cook a traditional dish, or how to handle difficult situations. These skills are often not written in books; they are passed down through stories and daily life. When young people take the time to listen, they receive gifts that no school can offer. At the same time, elders who stay curious and open-minded can learn new things from the young.

The modern world, however, has created a gap between generations. [3] Families sometimes live far apart, and busy schedules leave little time for visits. Technology can help bridge this gap. Grandparents and grandchildren can stay in touch through video calls even when they live in different cities or countries. Some schools now invite grandparents to share their stories in class. Community projects bring together people of all ages to work on the same goals. When we make an effort to connect, the gap shrinks.

What do we gain from learning across generations? For the young, it is a sense of roots and belonging. [4] They learn where they come from and what their family and culture value. For the old, teaching and sharing can bring purpose and joy. Studies show that elders who stay socially active and share their knowledge tend to feel happier and healthier. The exchange is not one-way; it benefits everyone involved. We all have something to teach and something to learn.

[5] In short, learning across generations is not a luxury but a need. It helps us understand the past, live better in the present, and prepare for the future. When young and old reach out to each other, they build a bridge that makes our society stronger and kinder.

## 选项

A. In other words, both the young and the old have skills and knowledge that the other can learn from.
B. That is why programs that bring generations together are becoming more and more important.
C. In fact, the best learning often happens when people of different ages work and learn together.
D. Their experience and knowledge come from years of living and solving real problems.
E. Today, however, distance and busy lives make it harder for generations to meet and share.
F. In summary, intergenerational（跨代的）learning enriches everyone and builds a stronger community.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] C   [2] D   [3] E   [4] A   [5] F

## 深度解析

- **[1] C**：
  - **线索**：引出跨代学习的基本概念与双向获益。空前描述"Young people and older people often live in different worlds...young spend time online and in school; elderly grew up without computers"，空后举例"Yet when different generations spend time together and share...both sides gain...Grandparents teach...Young people show...This exchange makes both generations feel more connected"。C选项"In fact, the best learning often happens when people of different ages work and learn together"（事实上，当不同年龄的人一起工作和学习时，往往能产生最好的学习效果）中"In fact"承接空前对比，"people of different ages work and learn together"与空后"spend time together and share...both sides gain"完美对应，自然引出具体例子，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] D**：
  - **线索**：长者智慧的来源与传递方式。空前说明"In many cultures, elders have always been the keepers of wisdom"，空后详述"They remember how things were done before—fix a broken tool, cook a traditional dish, handle difficult situations...not written in books...passed down through stories and daily life"。D选项"Their experience and knowledge come from years of living and solving real problems"（他们的经验和知识来自多年生活和解决实际问题的积累）中"Their"指代 elders，"experience and knowledge come from years of living and solving real problems"与空后"remember how things were done...passed down through stories"呼应，衔接自然。

- **[3] E**：
  - **线索**：现代社会中代际隔阂的原因与应对。空前描述"The modern world, however, has created a gap between generations"，空后列举"Families sometimes live far apart...busy schedules leave little time...Technology can help bridge this gap...video calls...schools invite grandparents...Community projects bring together people of all ages"。E选项"Today, however, distance and busy lives make it harder for generations to meet and share"（然而今天，距离和忙碌的生活使代际之间更难见面和分享）中"Today, however"承接空前"modern world...created a gap"，"distance and busy lives...harder to meet and share"与空后"live far apart...busy schedules leave little time"精准对应，自然过渡到"Technology can help"等解决方式，衔接得当。

- **[4] A**：
  - **线索**：跨代学习对双方的收获总结。空前提问"What do we gain from learning across generations?"并分别说明"For the young, it is a sense of roots and belonging...For the old, teaching and sharing can bring purpose and joy...Studies show elders who stay socially active feel happier"，空后强调"The exchange is not one-way; it benefits everyone involved. We all have something to teach and something to learn"。A选项"In other words, both the young and the old have skills and knowledge that the other can learn from"（换言之，年轻人和老年人都有对方可以学习的技能和知识）中"In other words"承接空前双方面收获，"both...have skills and knowledge that the other can learn from"与空后"exchange is not one-way...something to teach and something to learn"完美呼应，逻辑紧密。

- **[5] F**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后列举"learning across generations is not a luxury but a need...understand the past, live better in the present, prepare for the future...young and old reach out...build a bridge...society stronger and kinder"。F选项"In summary, intergenerational learning enriches everyone and builds a stronger community"（总之，跨代学习使每个人获益，并建设更强大的社区）用"In summary"总结全文，"enriches everyone and builds a stronger community"与空后"not a luxury but a need...society stronger and kinder"形成呼应，收束精准。intergenerational 为超纲词，已标注中文释义。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **B. That is why programs that bring generations together are becoming more and more important.**：强调跨代项目的必要性，易被误填[3]或[5]。但B以"That is why"开头，需前文有"原因"作为铺垫；空[3]空前为"modern world has created a gap"，空后为"Families live far apart...busy schedules"，E的"distance and busy lives make it harder"更直接承接；空[5]需全文总结升华，B仅论及"programs"，未涵盖"enrich everyone""understand past/present/future"等更广泛意义。B属"语义相关但逻辑衔接与层次不足"型干扰。
