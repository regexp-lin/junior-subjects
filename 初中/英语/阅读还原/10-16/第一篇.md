**Underwater Archaeology: Discovering History Beneath the Sea**

Shipwrecks, sunken cities, and ancient ports lie hidden beneath the ocean. Underwater archaeology is the study of these remains—ships, cargo, and structures that have been lost to the sea for hundreds or thousands of years. These sites hold clues to trade, warfare, and daily life in the past. [1] Underwater sites are often well preserved because cold water, lack of light, and low oxygen slow down decay, allowing archaeologists to find objects and structures that would have been lost or damaged on land.

Exploring underwater sites is not easy. Divers must be trained in both archaeology and diving. They work in conditions that can be cold, dark, and sometimes dangerous. Strong currents, poor visibility, and the need to protect fragile sites from damage make the work challenging. [2] Advances in technology—such as underwater robots, 3D mapping, and special cameras—are helping archaeologists explore deeper waters and document sites without disturbing them, opening new possibilities for discovery.

Underwater archaeology has revealed important finds. Ancient ships have been found with their cargo still on board—amphorae, coins, and goods that tell us about trade routes and cultures. Some sites have changed our understanding of history. [3] Each wreck or ruin is a time capsule, preserving a moment in history and offering evidence that cannot be found in books or on land—evidence that helps us understand how people lived, traded, and traveled across the seas.

Protecting underwater heritage is a concern. Looters sometimes take objects from shipwrecks to sell. Pollution and fishing nets can damage sites. International laws and agreements aim to protect important underwater sites and to ensure that finds are studied and shared rather than lost or sold. [4] Countries and organizations are working together to survey, protect, and share knowledge about underwater cultural heritage, so that these treasures can be studied and enjoyed by people around the world for generations to come.

Underwater archaeology connects us to our maritime past. It shows how ships carried goods and ideas across oceans, how cities rose and fell by the sea, and how human history is written not only on land but also in the waves. [5] As technology improves and more sites are discovered, underwater archaeology will continue to reveal new chapters of human history—stories that have been waiting beneath the sea for centuries.

## 选项

A. The underwater environment can protect artifacts from many of the forces that destroy them on land—and this preservation can give archaeologists a rare glimpse into the past that would otherwise be lost.
B. New tools and methods are making it possible to study underwater sites more safely and accurately—and to share discoveries with the public through virtual tours and 3D models, even when the sites themselves are too fragile or deep to visit easily.
C. What makes underwater archaeology valuable is the unique evidence it provides—objects and structures frozen in time—that fills gaps in our knowledge and challenges what we thought we knew about the past.
D. Safeguarding these sites for future study and for the public requires cooperation across borders, strong laws, and a shared commitment to treat underwater heritage as a treasure that belongs to all humanity.
E. The field is still growing, and each new discovery adds to our understanding of history—reminding us that the sea holds secrets we are only beginning to uncover.
F. For example, the wreck of the Titanic has been studied for decades, and researchers have learned much about the ship's design, the passengers' belongings, and the conditions that led to its sinking.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前说沉船、沉城、古代港口等水下遗存，空后说"Underwater sites are often well preserved because cold water, lack of light, and low oxygen slow down decay, allowing archaeologists to find objects... that would have been lost or damaged on land"。A的"The underwater environment can protect artifacts... this preservation can give archaeologists a rare glimpse into the past"与空后preserved、slow decay、lost on land呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说水下作业困难、需双重训练、条件危险，空后说"Advances in technology—such as underwater robots, 3D mapping, and special cameras—are helping archaeologists explore deeper waters and document sites"。B的"New tools and methods are making it possible to study underwater sites more safely and accurately"与空后technology、explore、document对应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说重要发现、古船货物、改变历史认知，空后说"Each wreck or ruin is a time capsule, preserving a moment in history and offering evidence that cannot be found in books or on land"。C的"What makes underwater archaeology valuable is the unique evidence it provides—objects and structures frozen in time"与空后time capsule、unique evidence衔接。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说保护水下遗产、盗捞、污染、国际法规，空后说"Countries and organizations are working together to survey, protect, and share knowledge about underwater cultural heritage"。D的"Safeguarding these sites... requires cooperation across borders, strong laws... treat underwater heritage as a treasure that belongs to all humanity"与空后国际合作、保护、共享呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说水下考古连接航海史、技术改进、新发现，空后说"As technology improves and more sites are discovered, underwater archaeology will continue to reveal new chapters of human history"。E的"The field is still growing... each new discovery adds to our understanding... the sea holds secrets we are only beginning to uncover"与空后continue、new chapters、centuries对应。
