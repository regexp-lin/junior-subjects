**Digital Literacy in the Modern World**

We live in a world where phones, computers, and the internet are part of daily life. Students use them for homework, parents use them for work, and everyone uses them to stay in touch. Yet using technology is not the same as using it wisely. [1] Digital literacy—knowing how to find, evaluate, and use information online—is becoming as essential as reading and writing.

The internet is full of information, but not all of it is true. Fake news, misleading advertisements, and biased opinions can spread quickly. Before sharing a post or believing a story, it is important to check the source. [2] Learning to question what you see and to look for reliable evidence protects you from being fooled and helps you make better decisions.

Staying safe online is another key skill. Personal information like your name, address, and school should not be shared with strangers. Passwords should be strong and kept private. [3] Understanding these basic rules reduces the risk of identity theft, cyberbullying, and other online dangers that can affect young people.

Screen time also matters. Spending too many hours on devices can harm sleep, grades, and friendships. It is healthy to set limits and to take breaks for exercise, hobbies, and face-to-face conversation. [4] Balancing online and offline life is a skill that will serve you well both now and in the future.

Technology will continue to change, and so will the skills we need. The goal is not to avoid technology but to use it thoughtfully. [5] Building strong digital literacy today prepares you to navigate an increasingly digital world with confidence and care.

## 选项

A. Being able to think critically about online content is just as important as being able to access it.
B. Developing the habit of checking facts before believing or sharing builds a foundation for lifelong learning.
C. Simple safety habits, when practiced consistently, can prevent many of the problems that worry parents and educators.
D. Finding the right mix of digital and real-world activities supports both mental health and academic success.
E. The ability to use technology responsibly and wisely will only grow more valuable as the years go by.
F. For example, some students use apps to block distracting websites while studying for exams.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前说使用科技不等于明智使用，空后给出digital literacy的定义"knowing how to find, evaluate, and use information online"并强调其重要性。A的"Being able to think critically about online content is just as important as being able to access it"概括批判性使用信息的重要性，与"evaluate"和"essential"呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前指出网络信息真假混杂，空后强调"check the source"和"Learning to question... look for reliable evidence protects you"。B的"Developing the habit of checking facts before believing or sharing"与"check the source"和"reliable evidence"对应，"foundation for lifelong learning"升华该段主题。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前列举网络安全基本规则（不透露个人信息、密码安全），空后说"Understanding these basic rules reduces the risk of... online dangers"。C的"Simple safety habits, when practiced consistently, can prevent many of the problems"与基本规则及减少风险逻辑一致，概括该段主旨。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前讨论屏幕时间、设限、运动与面对面交流，空后说"Balancing online and offline life is a skill"。D的"Finding the right mix of digital and real-world activities supports both mental health and academic success"准确概括平衡的重要性，与"set limits"和"serve you well"呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前总结科技会变化、目标是用之有思，空后无。E的"ability to use technology responsibly and wisely will only grow more valuable"升华主题，与"use it thoughtfully"和"navigate... with confidence and care"完美对应，适合作全文收束。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, some students use apps to block distracting websites while studying for exams.**：以For example开头，属具体举例；各空均为概括或过渡句，F与屏幕时间管理相关但为个案，不适用于任何空位，易误放[4]，但该空需平衡线上线下的概括句，非举例。
