# 09-09 第一篇

**Natural Disaster Preparedness**

Earthquakes, floods, and typhoons can strike without much warning. When they do, they can damage homes, cut off power, and put lives at risk. Many people think disasters only happen to others, but the truth is that no place on Earth is completely safe. [1] Learning how to prepare for emergencies can reduce fear, protect your family, and even save lives when a disaster occurs.

The first step in preparing is to make a family emergency plan. Talk with your parents about where to meet if you cannot return home. Decide who will pick up younger children from school, and choose a contact person who lives far away—often calls work better than local ones when phone lines are busy. [2] Having these details decided ahead of time means everyone knows what to do when every second counts.

Next, put together an emergency kit. Your kit should include water, non-perishable food, a flashlight, batteries, a first-aid kit, and copies of important documents. Keep it in a place that is easy to reach, and check it every few months to replace expired items. [3] A well-stocked kit can keep you safe and comfortable for at least three days until help arrives or conditions improve.

Schools and communities also run drills and safety exercises. Practicing what to do during an earthquake or fire helps you react quickly instead of freezing in panic. [4] The more you practice, the more natural these responses become, and the more likely you are to stay calm when a real emergency happens.

Disasters are scary, but they do not have to be hopeless. With proper planning and preparation, we can face them with confidence. [5] Taking action now—rather than waiting until it is too late—is the best way to protect yourself and those you love.

## 选项

A. Preparing for the unexpected is one of the most important things you can do for yourself and your family.
B. Written plans remove confusion and help families stay connected during chaos.
C. Without such supplies, even a short power outage or road block can become dangerous.
D. Training builds muscle memory and reduces the fear that comes with the unknown.
E. Investing time in preparedness today can make all the difference when tomorrow brings a crisis.
F. For example, during a severe typhoon, many families had to evacuate to shelters within hours.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前指出灾害可能突袭、影响广泛，且人们常以为不会发生在自己身上，空后强调"Learning how to prepare... can reduce fear, protect your family, and even save lives"。A的"Preparing for the unexpected is one of the most important things you can do"概括应对未知灾害的重要性，与"reduce fear, protect... save lives"完美呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前列举家庭应急计划的具体内容（会合点、联系人、远距离联系人等），空后说"Having these details decided ahead of time means everyone knows what to do"。B的"Written plans remove confusion and help families stay connected"与提前制定细节、减少混乱的逻辑一致，概括该段主旨。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前描述应急包的物品和保养方法，空后说"A well-stocked kit can keep you safe... for at least three days until help arrives"。C的"Without such supplies, even a short power outage... can become dangerous"从反面强调物资的重要性，与"well-stocked kit"和"until help arrives"形成呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说学校与社区进行演练，空后强调"The more you practice... the more natural these responses become... stay calm when a real emergency happens"。D的"Training builds muscle memory and reduces the fear that comes with the unknown"准确概括演练的作用，与"practice"和"stay calm"呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前总结灾害虽可怕但可通过准备建立信心，空后无。E的"Investing time in preparedness today can make all the difference when tomorrow brings a crisis"升华主题，与"Taking action now—rather than waiting until it is too late"完美对应，适合作全文收束。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, during a severe typhoon, many families had to evacuate to shelters within hours.**：以For example开头，属具体情境举例，与台风疏散相关；各空均为概括或过渡句，F作为举例不适用于任何空位，易误放[1]或[3]，但两空分别需主旨概述与应急包/物资的概括，非举例。
