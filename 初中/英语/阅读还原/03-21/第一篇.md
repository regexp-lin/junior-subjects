# 03-21 第一篇

**The Science Behind Music and Emotions**

Have you ever noticed that a sad song can make you cry, while an upbeat tune instantly makes you want to dance? [1] Scientists have spent many years trying to understand this powerful connection between music and our feelings.

When we listen to music, our brain releases a chemical called dopamine (多巴胺). This is the same chemical that makes us feel happy when we eat our favorite food or receive a gift. [2] That is why listening to a song you love can give you actual physical chills running down your spine.

Music also has the power to bring back old memories. A single melody can instantly transport you back to a specific moment in your past. [3] For instance, hearing a song your grandmother used to sing might suddenly fill your heart with warmth and love, even if she is far away.

Hospitals around the world have started to use music as a form of therapy (治疗). [4] Doctors have found that patients who listen to calming music before surgery feel much less anxious and recover faster afterward.

[5] So the next time you feel sad, try putting on your favorite song. It might just be the best medicine you can find.

## 选项

A. In other words, music can directly affect the chemistry of your brain.
B. This is called "music therapy," and the results have been truly remarkable.
C. In fact, the relationship between music and the human mind is surprisingly deep.
D. Therefore, learning to play a musical instrument is simply a waste of valuable time.
E. You might hear a song and suddenly remember exactly where you were years ago.
F. In short, music is a powerful natural tool that can heal both the body and the mind.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] C   [2] A   [3] E   [4] B   [5] F

## 深度解析

- **[1] C**：
  - **线索**：语义承接与总结铺垫。空前举了两个具体例子——悲伤的歌让你哭泣，欢快的曲子让你想跳舞。C选项"In fact, the relationship between music and the human mind is surprisingly deep"（事实上，音乐和人类心灵之间的关系出奇地深刻）是对前文两个现象的高度归纳。同时，"surprisingly deep"引出了后文"Scientists have spent many years trying to understand this powerful connection"（科学家花了很多年试图理解这种强大的联系），形成了"现象→总结→深入研究"的递进逻辑。

- **[2] A**：
  - **线索**：因果解释与代词衔接。空前解释了听音乐时大脑会释放多巴胺，这和吃美食、收礼物时的化学物质相同。A选项"In other words, music can directly affect the chemistry of your brain"（换句话说，音乐可以直接影响你大脑的化学物质）是前文信息的同义概括。"In other words"起到了转述总结作用。而空后"That is why listening to a song you love can give you actual physical chills"（这就是为什么听一首你喜爱的歌能给你真实的身体颤栗）中的"That is why"正是承接A选项得出的结论。

- **[3] E**：
  - **线索**：同义替换与举例引导。空前提出了音乐的第二个特殊能力："一段旋律可以瞬间把你传送回过去的某个特定时刻"。E选项"You might hear a song and suddenly remember exactly where you were years ago"（你可能会听到一首歌，然后突然准确地想起多年前你在哪里）是对前文"transport you back to a specific moment"的生动细化。空后的"For instance"（例如）紧接着就给出了一个具体的场景——听到奶奶唱过的歌心里充满温暖——进一步佐证了E选项的表述。

- **[4] B**：
  - **线索**：指代解释与承上启下。空前提到"世界各地的医院开始把音乐当作一种治疗形式"。B选项"This is called 'music therapy,' and the results have been truly remarkable"（这被称为"音乐治疗"，效果确实非凡）中的"This"完美指代前文医院使用音乐治疗这件事，且给出了专有名称"music therapy"。空后"Doctors have found that patients who listen to calming music before surgery feel much less anxious"（医生发现手术前听平静音乐的患者焦虑少得多）就是对"remarkable results"的具体实证。

- **[5] F**：
  - **线索**：总结全文与定调收尾。空[5]位于结尾段段首。前文分别从大脑化学（第二段）、记忆唤起（第三段）、医疗治疗（第四段）三个角度阐述了音乐的力量。F选项"In short, music is a powerful natural tool that can heal both the body and the mind"（简而言之，音乐是一种强大的天然工具，可以治愈身体和心灵）用"In short"总结全篇，"body and mind"精准覆盖了前文涉及的生理与心理两个维度。随后的建议"下次难过时就放首歌"是自然延伸。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **D. Therefore, learning to play a musical instrument is simply a waste of valuable time.**：该选项含有"Therefore（因此）"的因果逻辑词和"musical instrument（乐器）"等与音乐话题相关的词汇。但其意思是"学乐器纯粹是浪费时间"，这与全文始终歌颂音乐对人类身心的巨大益处的正面基调完全背道而驰。属于典型的"话题相关但立场相反"的干扰项。
