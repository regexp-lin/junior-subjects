# 06-02 第一篇

**The Invention of Braille**

Before the 19th century, blind people had very few ways to read or write. Most books were impossible for them to understand, and they often had to depend on others for information. [1] This changed in 1824 when a young French boy named Louis Braille invented a simple but powerful system of raised dots. His invention allowed blind people to read and write independently for the first time in history.

Louis Braille lost his sight at the age of three after an accident. He was a bright and curious child who loved learning. [2] At his school for the blind in Paris, he learned about a system of raised dots that soldiers used to read messages in the dark. Louis was only 15 when he improved this system and created a much easier version. Each letter of the alphabet was represented by a different pattern of one to six dots, which could be felt with the fingertips.

The Braille system quickly spread around the world. [3] Books, signs, and even music sheets could now be written in Braille, giving blind people access to education and information. Today, Braille appears on medicine bottles, elevator buttons, and public signs in many countries. It has become a symbol of independence and equality for people with visual impairments（视力障碍）.

Learning Braille takes time and practice, but the benefits are great. [4] Blind students who master Braille often do better in school and have more job opportunities later. Technology has also helped—special machines can now print Braille, and computers can turn regular text into Braille. Some people worry that screen readers and audio books might replace Braille, but many experts say that Braille is still important for developing literacy（读写能力） and thinking skills.

[5] Louis Braille's invention has touched millions of lives. It opened the door to knowledge and opportunity for blind people everywhere. His simple dots remind us that a great idea can come from anyone, at any age, and change the world forever.

## 选项

A. In other words, his system gave blind people a new way to access the world of words and ideas.
B. That is because he was inspired by an earlier system and made it simpler and more practical.
C. In fact, Braille has become a bridge to education, work, and daily life for blind people worldwide.
D. This shows that Braille skills continue to matter, even in the age of digital technology.
E. In short, Braille is one of the most important inventions in human history for people with disabilities.
F. For example, some museums offer Braille guides so that blind visitors can explore independently.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：引出盲文发明的革命性意义。空前描述"Before the 19th century, blind people had very few ways to read or write...had to depend on others"，空后说明"1824 when Louis Braille invented a simple but powerful system...allowed blind people to read and write independently for the first time"。A选项"In other words, his system gave blind people a new way to access the world of words and ideas"（换言之，他的系统为盲人提供了接触文字与思想世界的新方式）中"In other words"承接空前困境的转折，"new way to access...words and ideas"与空后"read and write independently"精准呼应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：路易·布莱尔改良盲文的由来。空前描述"At his school for the blind in Paris, he learned about a system of raised dots that soldiers used"，空后详述"Louis was only 15 when he improved this system and created a much easier version...Each letter...represented by a different pattern"。B选项"That is because he was inspired by an earlier system and made it simpler and more practical"（那是因为他受早期系统的启发，并将其变得更简单、更实用）中"That is because"解释改良的由来，"inspired by an earlier system"与空后"learned about...improved"对应，"simpler and more practical"与"much easier version"呼应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：盲文在各地的广泛应用。空前提出"The Braille system quickly spread around the world"，空后列举"Books, signs, music sheets...medicine bottles, elevator buttons, public signs...symbol of independence and equality"。C选项"In fact, Braille has become a bridge to education, work, and daily life for blind people worldwide"（事实上，盲文已成为全球盲人通往教育、工作和日常生活的桥梁）中"In fact"加强空前扩散的成果，"bridge to education, work, and daily life"与空后书籍、标识、药品瓶、电梯按钮等多领域应用完美对应，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：盲文在数字时代的持续价值。空前说明"Blind students who master Braille often do better...Technology has also helped...Some people worry that screen readers and audio books might replace Braille"，空后指出"many experts say that Braille is still important for developing literacy and thinking skills"。D选项"This shows that Braille skills continue to matter, even in the age of digital technology"（这表明盲文技能在数字时代仍然重要）中"This shows"承接空前"worry"与专家观点的对比，"continue to matter...digital technology"与空后"still important"呼应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"touched millions of lives...opened the door to knowledge and opportunity...a great idea can come from anyone...change the world forever"。E选项"In short, Braille is one of the most important inventions in human history for people with disabilities"（简言之，盲文是人类历史上对残障人士最重要的发明之一）用"In short"总结全文，"most important inventions"与空后"change the world forever"形成呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, some museums offer Braille guides so that blind visitors can explore independently.**：涉及博物馆提供盲文导览，易被误填[3]。但F以"For example"引出博物馆这一具体应用场景，而空[3]前后文讨论的是盲文在全球范围的广泛传播与多领域应用，空后列举的是书籍、标识、药品瓶、电梯、公共标识等更广泛的场景；F的"museums"只是众多应用之一，无法总领空后"Books, signs, music sheets...medicine bottles, elevator buttons"等丰富内容。F属"主题相关但衔接层级错位"型干扰。
