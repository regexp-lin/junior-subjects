**Staying Safe in the Summer Heat**

Summer brings sunshine, holidays, and outdoor fun. But when temperatures rise, the heat can also bring risks— dehydration（脱水）, heat exhaustion, and even heatstroke（中暑）if we are not careful. [1] That is why it is important to know how to stay safe and enjoy the warm months without harm. Whether you are playing sports, walking in the city, or relaxing at the beach, a few simple habits can protect you and your family. This article will help you understand the dangers of summer heat and how to prevent them.

Stay cool and hydrated. [2] Drink plenty of water throughout the day—do not wait until you feel thirsty, because thirst is already a sign that your body needs water. Avoid too many sweet or caffeine-heavy drinks; they can make you lose more water. Wear light-coloured, loose clothes that let your skin breathe. When it is very hot, try to stay in the shade, use a fan, or spend time in air-conditioned places. Take rest breaks if you are active outdoors. Children and older people need extra attention because their bodies cope with heat less easily. Small steps like these can keep you comfortable and safe.

Recognise the signs of heat illness. [3] Heat exhaustion can make you feel dizzy, weak, and tired; your skin may be pale and sweaty. If you or someone else has these signs, move to a cool place, drink water, and rest. Heatstroke is more serious—the person may stop sweating, have a high fever, feel confused, or even faint. Heatstroke is a medical emergency: call for help at once, move the person to shade, loosen their clothes, and cool them with wet cloths or a cool bath while waiting for help. Knowing these signs can save a life. Do not ignore early warning—acting quickly matters.

Plan outdoor activities wisely. [4] Avoid being outside during the hottest hours—usually between 10 a.m. and 4 p.m.—when the sun is strongest. If you must be outdoors, wear a hat and sunscreen, and take frequent breaks in the shade. Never leave children or pets in a parked car, even for a few minutes; the temperature inside can rise dangerously fast. When exercising, start slowly and gradually increase intensity. Listen to your body—if you feel unwell, stop and rest. A little planning can prevent most heat-related problems and let you enjoy summer safely.

[5] Summer heat can be enjoyable when we respect it and prepare well. Drink water, stay cool, know the signs of heat illness, and plan your activities wisely. Share these tips with your family and friends—everyone deserves a safe and happy summer. The warm months are meant for fun, not for trips to the hospital. With the right knowledge, you can make the most of the sunshine and avoid the dangers. Stay cool and stay safe!
