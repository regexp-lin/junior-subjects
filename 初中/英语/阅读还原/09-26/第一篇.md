**Staying Safe in Cyberspace**

The internet connects us to friends, information, and opportunities across the globe. Yet it also brings risks. Hackers steal data, scammers trick people online, and strangers may try to contact you in harmful ways. Knowing how to protect yourself is as important as knowing how to use the web. [1] Cyber security starts with understanding that not everyone online is who they claim to be, and not everything you see is true.

Passwords are your first line of defense. A weak password—like "123456" or your birthdate—can be guessed in seconds. Strong passwords mix letters, numbers, and symbols, and they are different for each important account. [2] Taking time to create and remember strong passwords may seem like a hassle, but it is one of the easiest ways to keep your accounts safe from attackers.

Be careful what you share. Personal details like your full name, address, school name, and photos can be used by strangers in harmful ways. Once you post something online, it can be copied and spread, and you may not be able to delete it fully. [3] Thinking before you post helps you avoid risks that can follow you for years.

Phishing is a common trick. Scammers send fake emails or messages that look like they come from real companies or friends. They ask you to click a link or share your password. [4] Learning to spot these tricks—checking the sender, avoiding suspicious links, and never sharing passwords by message—can protect you from most online scams.

Staying safe online is an ongoing task. New threats appear as technology changes. The good news is that a few simple habits can protect you from most dangers. [5] Building these habits now will help you enjoy the benefits of the internet while avoiding many of its risks.
