**The Role of Plants in Cities**

Cities are often seen as places of concrete and steel, but green spaces matter more than many people think. Trees, parks, and gardens are not just for beauty. They help clean the air, cool the streets, and provide homes for birds and insects. [1] Plants in cities do much more than decorate—they make urban life healthier and more livable for everyone.

Air quality is a major concern in many cities. Cars, factories, and construction send pollutants into the air. Trees absorb carbon dioxide and other harmful gases through their leaves. They also catch dust and small particles. [2] Urban trees act as natural air filters, improving the air that millions of city residents breathe every day.

Cities tend to be hotter than the countryside. Pavement and buildings absorb heat and release it slowly, creating what scientists call the "urban heat island" effect. Trees provide shade and release moisture through their leaves, which cools the surrounding air. [3] Green spaces help lower temperatures and make summer days more comfortable for people who live and work in the city.

Plants also support wildlife in urban areas. Birds, bees, and butterflies need flowers for food and trees for shelter. Rooftop gardens, street trees, and park plantings create corridors that connect green spots across the city. [4] When we add more plants to cities, we give nature a place to thrive alongside human life.

Bringing more nature into cities requires planning and care. City governments, schools, and communities can work together to plant trees, create parks, and maintain green spaces. Even small actions, like planting flowers on a balcony, help. [5] Investing in urban green spaces today benefits both people and the planet for decades to come.

## 选项

A. Urban greenery serves essential functions that support human health and wellbeing.
B. Planting trees is one of the most effective ways to fight air pollution in crowded areas.
C. More trees and parks mean cooler, more pleasant urban environments.
D. Greening our cities creates habitat for wildlife that would otherwise have nowhere to go.
E. The benefits of urban plants grow over time and extend far beyond the present.
F. For example, Central Park in New York receives over 40 million visitors each year.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前指出城市绿色不只是美化，还能净化空气、降温、提供栖息地，空后说"Plants in cities do much more than decorate—they make urban life healthier and more livable"。A的"Urban greenery serves essential functions that support human health and wellbeing"概括城市绿化的功能价值，与"much more than decorate"和"healthier and more livable"完美呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前描述树木吸收二氧化碳、过滤粉尘与颗粒物，空后强调"Urban trees act as natural air filters, improving the air that millions of city residents breathe"。B的"Planting trees is one of the most effective ways to fight air pollution in crowded areas"概括树木对改善空气的作用，与"natural air filters"逻辑一致。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前讨论城市热岛效应及树木遮荫、蒸腾降温，空后说"Green spaces help lower temperatures and make summer days more comfortable"。C的"More trees and parks mean cooler, more pleasant urban environments"准确概括绿地的降温与舒适作用，与"lower temperatures"对应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前列举城市动植物需求及屋顶花园、行道树、公园等绿廊，空后强调"When we add more plants to cities, we give nature a place to thrive alongside human life"。D的"Greening our cities creates habitat for wildlife that would otherwise have nowhere to go"概括城市绿化对野生动物的栖息地作用，与"give nature a place to thrive"呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前总结多方协作及小行动也能发挥作用，空后说"Investing in urban green spaces today benefits both people and the planet for decades to come"。E的"The benefits of urban plants grow over time and extend far beyond the present"升华主题，与"for decades to come"和"people and the planet"完美对应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, Central Park in New York receives over 40 million visitors each year.**：以For example开头，属具体举例；各空均为概括或过渡句，F为某公园游客量个案，不适用于任何空位，易误放[1]或[5]，但两空分别需城市绿化功能的概括与长期效益的概括，非举例。
