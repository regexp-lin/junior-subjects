**Endangered Species: Why We Need to Act**

Thousands of species of animals and plants are in danger of disappearing forever. Tigers, pandas, elephants, and many lesser-known creatures face threats such as habitat loss, pollution, and climate change. When a species goes extinct, it is lost forever—we can never bring it back. Protecting these species is not just about saving a few animals; it is about keeping our planet's ecosystems healthy and balanced. [1] The loss of biodiversity affects us all, as every species plays a role in the delicate web of life that supports humans and nature alike.

Habitat destruction is one of the main causes of species decline. Forests are cut down for farms and cities; wetlands are drained for development; oceans are polluted by plastic and chemicals. When animals lose their homes, they cannot find food, shelter, or mates. Many species need large areas to survive, but human activities are shrinking their living space. [2] Protecting and restoring natural habitats is essential if we want to give endangered species a chance to recover and thrive again.

Conservation efforts around the world are making a difference. National parks and nature reserves provide safe spaces for wildlife. Scientists work to breed endangered species in captivity and then release them into the wild. Laws against poaching and illegal trade help reduce the pressure on species like elephants and rhinos. Community programs teach local people how to live alongside wildlife without conflict. [3] Through a combination of legal protection, scientific research, and community involvement, many species have been brought back from the edge of extinction.

Climate change adds another threat to endangered species. Rising temperatures, changing rainfall patterns, and melting ice affect where animals can live and what food is available. Polar bears struggle as Arctic ice melts; coral reefs suffer from warming seas. Species that cannot adapt quickly enough may not survive. [4] Addressing climate change is therefore closely linked to protecting biodiversity, as the two challenges are deeply connected and require global action.

What can young people do to help? You can learn about endangered species and share what you know with others. You can support organizations that work on conservation. Making choices that reduce your environmental footprint—such as using less plastic and saving energy—also helps. Every action counts. [5] The future of endangered species depends on the choices we make today, and young people have an important role to play in building a world where wildlife can flourish.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前列举濒危物种及威胁——老虎、熊猫、大象、栖息地丧失、污染、气候变化，强调灭绝即永久失去，保护不仅为动物也为生态系统，空后需总结生物多样性丧失的影响。A的"The loss of biodiversity affects us all"与前文"protecting... is not just about saving a few animals"呼应，"delicate web of life that supports humans and nature"与"ecosystems healthy and balanced"对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前描述栖息地破坏——森林砍伐、湿地开发、海洋污染，动物失去家园无法觅食、避难、繁衍，生存空间被压缩，空后需提出栖息地保护与恢复的必要性。B的"Protecting and restoring natural habitats"针对前文问题，"give endangered species a chance to recover and thrive"与前文"cannot find food, shelter, or mates"形成解决方案。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前列举保护措施——国家公园、自然保护区、人工繁育与野化放归、反盗猎与非法贸易法、社区共管项目，空后需总结这些多管齐下的成效。C的"legal protection, scientific research, and community involvement"与前文各项措施一一对应，"brought back from the edge of extinction"总结保护成果。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说气候变化带来的威胁——温度上升、降水变化、冰川融化影响动物分布与食物，北极熊、珊瑚礁等举例，适应慢的物种可能灭绝，空后需强调气候与生物多样性的关联。D的"Addressing climate change is therefore closely linked to protecting biodiversity"与前文气候变化威胁呼应，"require global action"与全球性问题对应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前提出青少年可做的事——学习宣传、支持保护组织、减少塑料与节能，强调每一点行动都有意义，空后需总结未来取决于今日选择及青年角色。E的"The future of endangered species depends on the choices we make today"承接前文行动，"young people have an important role to play"与"Young people"和"You can"呼应，是合理收尾。
