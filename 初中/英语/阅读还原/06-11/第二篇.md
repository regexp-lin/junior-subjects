# 06-11 第二篇

**The Benefits of Board Games**

Chess, Go, and other board games have been played for hundreds or even thousands of years. They are more than simple pastimes（消遣）—they offer real benefits for the mind and for social connection. [1] In other words, board games train your brain while bringing people together face to face. Whether you play with family, friends, or classmates, these games create moments of shared focus and fun.

Board games improve thinking skills. [2] Players must plan ahead, consider different moves, and anticipate（预料）what the other player might do. Games like chess demand logic and strategy; others require quick decisions under time pressure. Over time, regular play strengthens your ability to concentrate, solve problems, and think creatively. Many students find that board games help them do better in maths and science, as they practise similar kinds of reasoning.

Board games build social skills. [3] Sitting around a table with others, you learn to take turns, follow rules, and accept both winning and losing with grace. Young children develop patience and fairness; older players practise negotiation and teamwork in games that require cooperation. Unlike staring at a screen alone, board games encourage eye contact, conversation, and laughter. They remind us that spending time together in person still matters.

You do not need expensive equipment. [4] A simple set of chess pieces or a pack of cards can provide years of enjoyment. Many classic games are easy to learn but take a lifetime to master—which is part of their appeal. Libraries and community centres often lend games for free. Schools can start chess or board game clubs with minimal cost. The real value lies in the time spent thinking and playing together, not in fancy materials.

[5] Board games are a timeless way to exercise your mind and strengthen relationships. In a world full of digital distractions, they offer a chance to unplug, focus, and connect with the people around you. Pick up a game, invite someone to play, and discover the joy of shared strategy and friendly competition.

## 选项

A. In other words, board games exercise the mind and bring people together for face-to-face interaction.
B. That is because these games require planning, strategy, and the ability to think ahead.
C. In fact, playing board games helps players learn turn-taking, rule-following, and how to handle winning and losing.
D. These points show that board games can be affordable and accessible to almost everyone.
E. In short, board games remain a valuable way to train the mind and bond with others.
F. For example, the first world chess champion was crowned in the late 19th century.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：引出棋类游戏对大脑与社交的双重价值。空前描述"Chess, Go...more than simple pastimes—they offer real benefits for the mind and for social connection"，空后说明"board games train your brain while bringing people together face to face...play with family, friends, classmates...create moments of shared focus and fun"。A选项"In other words, board games exercise the mind and bring people together for face-to-face interaction"（换言之，棋类游戏锻炼思维并促进面对面互动）中"In other words"概括空前，"exercise the mind...face-to-face interaction"与空后"train your brain...bringing people together face to face"精准呼应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：解释棋类游戏提升思维技能的原因。空前提出"Board games improve thinking skills"，空后详述"Players must plan ahead, consider different moves, anticipate what other player might do...chess demand logic and strategy...quick decisions under time pressure...strengthens ability to concentrate, solve problems, think creatively...help in maths and science...similar reasoning"。B选项"That is because these games require planning, strategy, and the ability to think ahead"（那是因为这些游戏需要规划、策略以及提前思考的能力）中"That is because"解释提升思维的原因，"planning, strategy, think ahead"与空后 plan ahead、strategy、anticipate 完美对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：棋类游戏如何培养社交技能。空前提出"Board games build social skills"，空后列举"Sitting around table...learn to take turns, follow rules, accept winning and losing with grace...Young children develop patience and fairness...older players practise negotiation and teamwork...Unlike screen alone...encourage eye contact, conversation, laughter"。C选项"In fact, playing board games helps players learn turn-taking, rule-following, and how to handle winning and losing"（事实上，玩棋类游戏有助于玩家学会轮流、遵守规则以及如何处理输赢）中"In fact"强化社交学习，"turn-taking, rule-following, handle winning and losing"与空后 take turns、follow rules、accept winning and losing 精准对应，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：棋类游戏成本低、易获得。空前提出"You do not need expensive equipment"，空后描述"simple set of chess pieces or pack of cards...years of enjoyment...easy to learn but lifetime to master...Libraries and community centres lend games for free...Schools start clubs with minimal cost...real value lies in time spent thinking and playing, not fancy materials"。D选项"These points show that board games can be affordable and accessible to almost everyone"（这些要点表明棋类游戏几乎人人都负担得起且易获得）中"These points"指代空后低成本、可借阅、学校俱乐部等，"affordable and accessible"与空后"simple...free...minimal cost"完美对应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与棋类游戏的持久价值。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"timeless way to exercise mind and strengthen relationships...world full of digital distractions...unplug, focus, connect...Pick up a game...invite someone...discover joy of shared strategy and friendly competition"。E选项"In short, board games remain a valuable way to train the mind and bond with others"（简言之，棋类游戏仍是锻炼思维、增进人际联系的有价值方式）用"In short"总结全文，"train the mind and bond with others"与空后"exercise mind and strengthen relationships"形成呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the first world chess champion was crowned in the late 19th century.**：涉及首位国际象棋世界冠军的历史，易被误填[1]或[4]。但F以"For example"引出国际象棋历史案例，而空[1]讨论的是棋类游戏的普遍益处以吸引读者，F的"first world champion"属于竞技史，与"train brain and bring people together"的功能论述焦点不同；空[4]讨论 affordability 与 accessibility，F的"crowned"与成本、可获得性无关。F属"主题相关但论述角度错位"型干扰。
