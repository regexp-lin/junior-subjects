# 06-11 第一篇

**Migratory Birds and Wetland Protection**

Every year, millions of birds fly thousands of kilometres across the world to find food and safe places to rest. These migratory（迁徙的）birds depend on wetlands—lakes, marshes（沼泽）, and coastal areas—as crucial stops along their journey. [1] In other words, wetlands serve as essential rest areas where birds can feed, rest, and regain strength before continuing their long travels. Without these places, many birds would not survive the journey.

Wetlands face serious threats. [2] Human activities such as building cities, draining（排水）water for farm use, and pollution have destroyed or damaged many wetlands worldwide. When wetlands disappear, birds lose their feeding grounds and safe resting spots. Some species have already seen their numbers drop sharply. Protecting existing wetlands and restoring damaged ones has become an urgent task for scientists and governments.

People can help in many ways. [3] Schools and communities can join in projects to clean up local ponds and rivers, plant native trees near water, and create small gardens that attract birds. Even simple actions—such as reducing plastic waste and not disturbing birds during their stopover—make a difference. Young people can take part in bird-counting events to help scientists track bird populations and migration patterns.

[4] Wetlands are not only important for birds but also for humans. They filter（过滤）polluted water, reduce flooding, and provide homes for fish and other wildlife. A healthy wetland benefits everyone who lives nearby. By protecting wetlands, we protect the birds that depend on them and the natural systems that support our own lives.

[5] Migratory birds connect countries and continents through their amazing journeys. Protecting wetlands today ensures that future generations can still witness the wonder of flocks of birds flying across the sky. Each small step we take—whether as individuals, schools, or communities—helps keep these ancient migration routes alive for years to come.

## 选项

A. In other words, wetlands act as vital rest stops where birds can eat, rest, and prepare for the next part of their journey.

D. These benefits show that wetlands support both wildlife and human communities in important ways.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：引出湿地作为候鸟重要停歇地的功能。空前描述"millions of birds fly thousands of kilometres...depend on wetlands as crucial stops"，空后说明"wetlands serve as essential rest areas where birds can feed, rest, and regain strength...Without these places, many birds would not survive"。A选项"In other words, wetlands act as vital rest stops where birds can eat, rest, and prepare for the next part of their journey"（换言之，湿地是鸟类进食、休息并为下一段旅程做准备的重要停歇地）中"In other words"概括空前，"vital rest stops...eat, rest, prepare"与空后"essential rest areas...feed, rest, regain strength"精准呼应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：解释湿地面临的威胁来自人类活动。空前提出"Wetlands face serious threats"，空后详述"Human activities such as building cities, draining water for farm use, pollution have destroyed or damaged many wetlands...When wetlands disappear...species have seen numbers drop...Protecting and restoring has become urgent"。B选项"That is because development, agriculture, and pollution have damaged or removed many wetland areas around the world"（那是因为发展、农业和污染已破坏或消除全球许多湿地）中"That is because"解释威胁来源，"development, agriculture, pollution...damaged or removed"与空后 building cities、draining、pollution、destroyed 完美对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：公众尤其是年轻人可参与的环保行动。空前提出"People can help in many ways"，空后列举"Schools and communities join projects...clean up ponds and rivers, plant native trees...create gardens that attract birds...reducing plastic waste...not disturbing birds...Young people take part in bird-counting...track populations and migration"。C选项"In fact, young people and local groups can take part in activities like cleaning water bodies and monitoring bird populations"（事实上，年轻人和本地团体可参与清理水域、监测鸟类数量等活动）中"In fact"强化参与可能，"cleaning water bodies and monitoring bird populations"与空后 clean up、bird-counting、track 精准对应，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：湿地对人类及生态系统的综合价值。空前提出湿地不只对鸟类重要，空后描述"filter polluted water, reduce flooding, provide homes for fish and wildlife...A healthy wetland benefits everyone nearby...protecting wetlands protects birds and natural systems that support our lives"。D选项"These benefits show that wetlands support both wildlife and human communities in important ways"（这些好处表明湿地在重要方面支持野生动物与人类社区）中"These benefits"指代空后过滤、防洪、栖息地等，"support both wildlife and human communities"与空后"important for birds but also for humans"完美对应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与保护湿地的长远意义。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"Migratory birds connect countries and continents...Protecting wetlands today ensures future generations can witness flocks flying across the sky...Each small step...helps keep migration routes alive"。E选项"In short, protecting wetlands today helps safeguard bird migration for the future"（简言之，今日保护湿地有助于保障未来鸟类迁徙）用"In short"总结全文，"safeguard bird migration for the future"与空后"ensures future generations...keep migration routes alive"形成呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the East Asian-Australasian Flyway is one of the busiest migration routes in the world.**：涉及东亚—澳大拉西亚迁飞路线，易被误填[1]或[5]。但F以"For example"引出具体路线案例，而空[1]讨论的是湿地作为停歇地的普遍功能，F的"busiest migration route"属于路线知名度，与"rest stops where birds feed and rest"的湿地功能论述焦点不同；空[5]总结保护湿地对未来的意义，F的"East Asian-Australasian Flyway"为单一案例，与"each small step...keep migration routes alive"的普世行动号召不符。F属"主题相关但论述层次错位"型干扰。
