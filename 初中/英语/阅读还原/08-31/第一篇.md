**Safe Drinking Water**

Water is essential for life. Our bodies need it to function, and we use it for drinking, cooking, and hygiene every day. Yet not everyone has access to clean, safe water. In many parts of the world, water sources are polluted or scarce. [1] Ensuring that everyone has access to safe drinking water is one of the most important challenges facing our world today.

What makes water unsafe? Bacteria, viruses, and chemicals can contaminate water and cause serious illness. Pollution from factories, farms, and waste can seep into rivers and groundwater. [2] Understanding these risks is the first step toward protecting our water sources and keeping ourselves and our families healthy.

Governments and organizations work to improve water safety. They build treatment plants, set quality standards, and test water regularly. [3] In places where clean water is hard to get, simple solutions like boiling water or using filters can make a big difference for communities and families.

At home, we can also play a part. We can save water, avoid pouring harmful chemicals down drains, and support efforts to protect local waterways. [4] Every small action counts, and when many people act together, we can help ensure that clean water is available for future generations.

Safe drinking water is a basic human need and a human right. As the world's population grows and climate change affects water supplies, the need for action becomes more urgent. [5] Protecting our water today is not just about our health—it is about the health of our planet and the wellbeing of generations to come.

## 选项

A. Solving this problem requires global cooperation, better technology, and fair distribution of resources.
B. Knowing where contamination comes from helps us take the right measures to prevent it.
C. Such efforts have improved water quality in many regions, but much work remains to be done.
D. Individual choices, when combined with community action, can create real change.
E. The fight for safe water is a fight for a healthier, fairer, and more sustainable future for all.
F. For example, in some developing countries, women and children walk hours each day to fetch water.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前指出并非人人能获得安全饮用水、污染与短缺普遍，空后强调"Ensuring... is one of the most important challenges"。A的"Solving this problem requires global cooperation, better technology, and fair distribution"概括应对挑战所需的条件，与"challenges facing our world"呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前列举水不安全的原因（细菌、病毒、化学物、污染），空后说"Understanding these risks is the first step"。B的"Knowing where contamination comes from"与前列举对应，"take the right measures to prevent it"与"protecting our water sources"一致，概括该段逻辑。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前列举政府与组织的努力，空后说"simple solutions like boiling or using filters can make a big difference"。C的"Such efforts have improved... but much work remains"承上启下，既肯定已有成效又引出在欠发达地区仍需简易方案的现实。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前列举个人可做之事，空后说"Every small action counts... when many people act together"。D的"Individual choices, when combined with community action, can create real change"准确概括个人与集体结合的观点，与"future generations"呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前强调水是基本需求与权利、人口增长与气候变化使行动更紧迫，空后无。E的"fight for safe water is a fight for... healthier, fairer, and more sustainable future"升华主题，与"health of our planet and wellbeing of generations"呼应，适合作全文收束。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, in some developing countries, women and children walk hours each day to fetch water.**：以For example开头，与获取清洁水的困难相关，但属具体情境举例，各空均为概括或过渡句；易误放[1]或[3]，但两空分别需挑战概述与各方努力的概括，非举例。
