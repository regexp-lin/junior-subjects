**Healthy Eating Habits**

What we eat affects how we feel, how we think, and how we grow. Food is the fuel that keeps our body and mind running. Yet with so many snacks, fast food, and sugary drinks around, it can be hard to make good choices. [1] Understanding the basics of nutrition helps you decide what to put on your plate and why it matters for your health.

A balanced diet includes different types of food. Fruits and vegetables give you vitamins and fiber. Proteins like meat, eggs, and beans help build muscle. Whole grains provide energy that lasts. [2] Eating a variety of foods from each group gives your body everything it needs to stay strong and active.

Breakfast is often called the most important meal of the day, and for good reason. After a long night of sleep, your body needs food to refuel. Skipping breakfast can make you feel tired and find it hard to focus in class. [3] Starting the day with a healthy meal sets you up for better concentration and energy throughout the morning.

Drinking enough water is another habit that many people overlook. Water helps digest food, regulates body temperature, and keeps your skin healthy. Sugary drinks like soda may taste good, but they add extra calories without nutrition. [4] Choosing water as your main drink supports your health without the downsides of added sugar.

Healthy eating does not mean giving up all your favorite foods. It means making smarter choices most of the time. An occasional treat is fine, but daily habits shape your long-term health. [5] Building good eating habits now will benefit you not only today but for many years to come.

## 选项

A. Learning what your body needs and how different foods help is the first step toward making better choices.
B. No single food has everything, so mixing different kinds ensures you get full nutrition.
C. A good morning meal improves both your mind and body for the hours ahead.
D. Water is the simplest and healthiest choice for staying hydrated.
E. The habits you form as a young person often stay with you for life.
F. For example, a student who eats breakfast every day often scores better on morning tests than those who skip it.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前指出食物影响身心，但面对零食、快餐、含糖饮料难以选择，空后说"Understanding the basics of nutrition helps you decide what to put on your plate"。A的"Learning what your body needs and how different foods help is the first step toward making better choices"概括营养知识的作用，与"Understanding the basics"和"decide"完美呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前列举各类食物及其作用（蔬果、蛋白质、全谷物），空后强调"Eating a variety of foods from each group gives your body everything it needs"。B的"No single food has everything, so mixing different kinds ensures you get full nutrition"准确概括多样化饮食的重要性，与"variety"和"each group"对应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说明早餐的重要性及不吃早餐的后果，空后说"Starting the day with a healthy meal sets you up for better concentration and energy"。C的"A good morning meal improves both your mind and body for the hours ahead"概括早餐对身心和上午表现的提升，与"concentration and energy"呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前讨论水的作用及含糖饮料的弊端，空后强调"Choosing water as your main drink supports your health without the downsides of added sugar"。D的"Water is the simplest and healthiest choice for staying hydrated"概括选水饮用的好处，与"main drink"和"without... added sugar"逻辑一致。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前总结健康饮食不等于完全放弃美食，强调习惯的重要性，空后说"Building good eating habits now will benefit you not only today but for many years to come"。E的"The habits you form as a young person often stay with you for life"升华主题，与"daily habits shape your long-term health"和"many years to come"完美对应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, a student who eats breakfast every day often scores better on morning tests than those who skip it.**：以For example开头，属具体举例；各空均为概括或过渡句，F与早餐相关但为个案数据，不适用于任何空位，易误放[3]，但该空需早餐重要性的概括句，非举例。
