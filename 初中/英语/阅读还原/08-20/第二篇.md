# 08-20 第二篇

**How to Be a Good Listener**

Listening is more than just hearing words. It is about understanding the person speaking, noticing their feelings, and showing that you care. [1] Good listeners make others feel valued and respected, which strengthens friendships and builds trust. In school, at home, or with friends, the ability to listen well is a skill that will benefit you everywhere.

The first step is to give your full attention. Put away your phone, stop thinking about what you will say next, and focus on the speaker. [2] Making eye contact and nodding occasionally shows that you are following along. When you are fully present, the speaker feels that their words matter. Distractions send the message that something else is more important than they are.

Try to understand before responding. Do not interrupt or jump in with advice too quickly. [3] Sometimes people need to express their feelings before they want solutions. Ask questions like "How did that make you feel?" or "What happened next?" to show interest and encourage them to share more. Your role is to support, not necessarily to fix.

Pay attention to what is not said. Body language, tone of voice, and pauses often tell us as much as words. [4] A person might say "I'm fine" but look upset; their behavior can reveal what they really feel. Learning to read these signals helps you respond in a more caring and appropriate way. Listening with your eyes and heart, not just your ears, makes a real difference.

Becoming a good listener takes practice. [5] Start with one conversation today: put down your phone, listen without judging, and see how it feels. The more you practice, the more natural it becomes. Good listening can transform your relationships and help you understand the people around you more deeply. It is a gift you can give to everyone you meet.

- **[1] A**：

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前建议"全神贯注、放下手机、专注说话者"，空后说"眼神接触和点头显示你在跟上"。选项 B"你的肢体语言也在传递信号，说话者会注意到你是否投入"与前文"全神贯注"呼应，解释"眼神接触、点头"的意义，与后文" fully present 让说话者感到被重视"形成逻辑链。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前建议"先理解再回应、不打断、不急于给建议"，空后提到"有时人们需要先表达感受再谈解决办法"。选项 C"给他们表达空间，往往能帮助他们自己找到答案"与前文"不急于给建议"一致，与后文"你的角色是支持而非一定要解决"完美契合。
