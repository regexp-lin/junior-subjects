# 08-20 第一篇

**The Importance of Tropical Rainforests**

Tropical rainforests are often called the "lungs of the Earth" because they produce much of the oxygen we breathe. They cover only about 6% of the world's land surface, yet they are home to more than half of all plant and animal species. [1] Losing these forests would mean losing countless lives and weakening the planet's ability to regulate climate. Their protection should matter to everyone.

Rainforests play a key role in the water cycle. Trees absorb water through their roots and release it into the air through their leaves. [2] This process helps form clouds and brings rain to nearby areas and even distant regions. When forests are cut down, the local climate can become drier, and rainfall patterns may change. Farmers and cities that depend on this water often suffer when forests disappear.

The biodiversity in rainforests is astonishing. Scientists discover new species there almost every year. [3] Many medicines we use today come from plants and animals found in these forests. When we destroy rainforests, we may be destroying cures for diseases we have not yet learned to treat. Protecting rainforests is not only about saving nature; it is about saving human lives too.

Rainforests also store huge amounts of carbon. Trees absorb carbon dioxide from the air and lock it in their trunks, branches, and roots. [4] When forests are burned or cut down, this carbon is released back into the atmosphere, adding to climate change. Keeping forests standing is one of the most effective ways to slow down global warming. Every tree counts.

What can we do to help? [5] We can support products that do not harm rainforests, learn about the issue, and share what we know with others. Young people can join or start environmental clubs at school. Even small actions, when many people do them, can create real change. The future of rainforests depends on the choices we make today.

## 选项

A. That is why the loss of rainforests is a concern for the whole world, not just for the countries where they grow.
B. The water that evaporates from one forest can fall as rain thousands of kilometers away.
C. Researchers believe that many more useful compounds are still waiting to be discovered.
D. Deforestation, therefore, accelerates the warming of our planet.
E. Everyone can take part in efforts to protect these precious ecosystems.
F. For example, the Amazon rainforest alone holds about 10% of all known species.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前强调雨林仅占 6% 陆地却承载过半物种，空后指出"失去这些森林意味着无数生物灭绝、地球气候调节能力下降"。选项 A"这就是为什么雨林消失是整个人类而非仅生长国的问题"承接物种与气候的重要地位，自然引出后文"保护雨林人人有责"。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前描述树木吸水、通过叶片释放到空气中的过程，空后说"该过程帮助形成云、带来降雨"。选项 B"一片森林蒸发的降水可在数千公里外形成降雨"与前文水循环机制呼应，补充说明水汽远距离输送，与后文"森林砍伐导致局部变干、降水格局改变"形成对比。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前提到科学家每年在雨林中发现新物种，空后说"许多现代药物来自雨林动植物"。选项 C"研究者相信还有更多有用化合物有待发现"与前文"新物种"和后文"药物来源"衔接，强调雨林潜在的科研与医疗价值，支撑"毁林可能摧毁未解疾病的解药"这一论点。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说明树木吸收二氧化碳并固定在体内，空后指出"森林被烧毁或砍伐时碳释放回大气、加剧气候变化"。选项 D"因此，毁林加速了地球变暖"与前文碳储存功能形成因果，与后文"保持森林是减缓全球变暖的有效途径"自然衔接。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前提出问题"我们能做什么"，空后列举具体行动。选项 E"每个人都能参与到保护这些珍贵生态系统的努力中"总领后文"支持环保产品、学习与传播、成立环保社团"等内容，与"小行动汇聚大改变"形成呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the Amazon rainforest alone holds about 10% of all known species**：以 For example 开头的典型干扰项。与第三段物种多样性高度相关，易被误选为 [3]。但 F 为单一区域、单一数字的举例，而 [3] 处需要的是对"雨林药物价值与未知潜力"的概括；且 F 的论述层次属于例证，与"药物来源、未发现化合物"的主题有偏差，属举例型干扰。
