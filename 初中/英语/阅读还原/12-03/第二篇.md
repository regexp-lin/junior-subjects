**Organic Farming: Growing Food the Natural Way**

Organic farming is a way of growing crops and raising animals without synthetic chemicals. Instead of artificial fertilizers and pesticides, organic farmers use natural methods—compost, crop rotation, and beneficial insects. The goal is to produce food that is healthy for people and kinder to the environment. As more people care about what they eat and how it is grown, organic farming has become increasingly popular. [1]

One key practice in organic farming is crop rotation. Farmers plant different crops in the same field in different years. This keeps the soil healthy and reduces pest problems. Legumes, such as beans and peas, add nitrogen to the soil naturally. Cover crops protect the soil between growing seasons and prevent erosion. [2]

Organic farmers also avoid synthetic pesticides. Instead, they use natural pest control. Ladybugs eat harmful insects. Certain plants repel pests. Keeping the soil healthy helps crops resist disease. These methods take more planning and labor, but they avoid chemicals that can harm water, wildlife, and human health. [3]

Organic food often costs more than conventionally grown food. Organic farming usually produces lower yields and requires more human labor. Some people choose to pay more because they believe organic food is healthier or better for the environment. Others buy organic for specific items, such as fruits and vegetables that are usually heavily sprayed. [4]

The debate about organic vs. conventional farming continues. What is clear is that organic farming offers an alternative that reduces chemical use and supports biodiversity. Whether you buy all organic or only some items, being aware of how your food is grown is a step toward making better choices. [5]

## 选项

A. Organic farming has grown from a small movement into a major choice for consumers who want food that is good for both their health and the planet.
B. Through these practices, organic farmers build healthy soil and reduce their need for chemical inputs.
C. For organic farmers, working with nature rather than against it is the core of their approach.
D. Understanding these trade-offs helps consumers decide what works best for their budget and values.
E. Whatever your view, learning about how food is produced can lead to more informed and thoughtful eating habits.
F. For example, organic milk comes from cows that are not given antibiotics or growth hormones.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前定义有机农业为不用合成化学品、用堆肥轮作益虫等自然方法、目标为健康环保、随消费者关注增加而流行，空后需总结有机农业的发展与消费选择。A的"grown from a small movement into a major choice"与"increasingly popular"呼应，"good for both their health and the planet"与前文目标对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前列举轮作等实践——不同年份种不同作物、保持土壤健康减少害虫、豆科固氮、覆盖作物防侵蚀，空后需总结这些实践的效果。B的"Through these practices"指代前文，"build healthy soil and reduce their need for chemical inputs"与前文呼应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说有机农避免合成农药、用瓢虫驱虫植物健土等自然防治、需更多规划与劳力、但减少对水土生物及人的危害，空后需总结核心理念。C的"For organic farmers, working with nature rather than against it"与前文自然方法对应，"core of their approach"概括本段主旨。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说有机食品常更贵、产量较低劳力更多、有人愿为健康环保付费、有人只对特定作物选有机，空后需总结消费者权衡。D的"Understanding these trade-offs"指代前文价格与选择，"decide what works best for their budget and values"与前文对应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说有机与传统之争持续、有机减少化学品并支持生物多样性、不论全有机或部分、了解生产方式有助于更好选择，空后需总结认知价值。E的"Whatever your view"承接前文争议，"learning about how food is produced can lead to more informed and thoughtful eating habits"与前文呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, organic milk comes from cows...**：以For example开头，为具体有机产品案例。各空均为概括或过渡句；[3]易被误选因涉及有机畜牧，但该空需总结有机农“顺应自然”的核心理念，而非补充某类有机产品的生产标准。
