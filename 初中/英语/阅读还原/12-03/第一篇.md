**Bird Watching: A Window into Nature**

Bird watching, or birding, is a hobby enjoyed by millions of people around the world. It does not require expensive equipment—a pair of binoculars and a field guide are enough to start. Bird watchers go to parks, wetlands, forests, and even their own backyards to observe birds. The hobby combines outdoor activity, patience, and learning, offering a peaceful way to connect with nature. [1]

Birds can be found almost everywhere, from city parks to remote mountains. Different habitats attract different species. Wetlands draw herons and ducks; forests are home to woodpeckers and owls; open fields host hawks and sparrows. Learning where to look helps birders find the birds they want to see. [2]

Identifying birds takes practice. Beginners start by noticing size, color, and shape. Bird calls and songs are also useful clues—many experienced birders can identify a species by sound alone. Keeping a list of birds seen, or a "life list," adds a sense of achievement and fun to the hobby. [3]

Bird watching contributes to science as well. Programs like the Christmas Bird Count and eBird allow people to report what they see. Scientists use this data to track bird populations, migration patterns, and how climate change affects species. Ordinary birders become citizen scientists. [4]

Bird watching is a hobby that can last a lifetime. It can be done alone or with family and friends. It encourages people to explore new places and pay attention to the natural world. In a fast-paced life, spending a quiet hour watching birds can be a wonderful way to slow down and recharge. [5]

- **[2] B**：

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说辨识需练习、初学者关注大小颜色外形、叫声是线索、记录列表增加成就感，空后需总结辨识学习的结果。C的"With time and practice"与前文对应，"anyone can learn to recognize birds and enjoy the satisfaction"与前文呼应。

- **[4] D**：
