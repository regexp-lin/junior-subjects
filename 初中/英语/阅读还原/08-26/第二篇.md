**The True Meaning of Courage**

Many people think that courage means not being afraid. In movies, brave heroes rush into danger without hesitation. In real life, however, courage often looks different. True courage is not the absence of fear—it is taking action despite being afraid. [1] Real courage involves feeling fear and still choosing to do what is right or necessary. This understanding can help us recognize and develop courage in ourselves and others.

Courage shows itself in many forms. Speaking up when you see someone being treated unfairly takes courage. Admitting a mistake and apologizing takes courage. [2] Standing up for your beliefs, even when others disagree, requires inner strength. So does trying something new when you might fail. These everyday acts may not seem dramatic, but they are genuine expressions of courage. We often overlook them because they lack the excitement of movie scenes.

Courage is closely linked to honesty. It takes courage to be honest with yourself about your weaknesses and mistakes. [3] Facing the truth, whether about our own failures or difficult situations, is a brave act. Some people avoid difficult conversations or hide from problems. Choosing to confront them honestly, even when it is uncomfortable, shows real courage. Honesty with oneself and others builds trust and respect.

Courage also means perseverance. Giving up is easy when things get tough. [4] Continuing to try when you face setbacks, criticism, or failure shows true courage. Students who keep studying after a bad grade, athletes who train after a loss, and anyone who refuses to quit when the path is hard—these people demonstrate courage. It is not about never falling; it is about getting up again and again.

[5] Courage is a quality we can all develop. We do not need to perform heroic deeds to be courageous. We can practice courage by speaking up in class, helping someone in need, or trying again after a failure. Each small act of courage strengthens us for bigger challenges. When we understand that courage means acting despite fear, we realize that we are capable of more than we think.

## 选项

A. True courage means feeling fear and still choosing to do what is right or necessary.
B. Defending your beliefs and trying new things despite the risk of failure are acts of courage.
C. Being honest about difficult truths, with oneself and others, requires and demonstrates courage.
D. Persisting through difficulties, setbacks, and failure is a genuine form of courage.
E. Courage can be built through practice and does not require extreme or heroic actions.
F. For example, firefighters risk their lives to save people from burning buildings.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前对比电影中“无畏”与现实中“尽管害怕仍采取行动”；空后强调“这种理解能帮助我们认识和发展自己与他人的勇气”。A项“真正的勇气是感到恐惧仍选择做正确或必要的事”承接上文定义，启下引出对勇气的新理解，起承上启下作用。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前举例为不公正发声、承认错误道歉；空后写“坚持自己的信念（即使他人反对）需要内心力量，尝试可能失败的新事物也是如此”。B项“坚持自己的信念并勇于尝试可能失败的新事物，都是勇气的表现”概括本段多种勇气形式，与前后一致。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前讲勇气与诚实相关，勇于坦诚弱点和错误；空后写面对关于自己失败或艰难境况的真相是勇敢之举，避免困难对话或逃避问题则不是。C项“面对真相，无论关于自己的失败还是困难处境，都是勇敢行为”概括诚实与勇气的关系。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前强调放弃很容易；空后举例考试失利后继续学习、失败后继续训练的运动员、在困难道路上不放弃的人。D项“在挫折、批评或失败面前坚持尝试，体现真正的勇气”准确概括本段“坚持”主题。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前为第四段；空后写不需要英雄壮举就能勇敢，可通过课堂发言、帮助他人、失败后重试来练习勇气。E项“勇气可以通过练习培养，不需要极端或英雄式的行为”作为结尾主题句，总结全文并给出可操作性。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, firefighters risk their lives to save people from burning buildings.**：以For example开头，为消防员救人的极端例子。虽与勇气相关，但属职业英雄主义层面，与文中强调的“日常勇气”“不必英雄壮举”形成错位；无法承担[1]–[5]的概括功能，易误选为[2]或[5]。
