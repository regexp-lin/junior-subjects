# 04-16 第一篇

**The Spirit of the Marathon: More Than Just Running**

A marathon is a race of 42.195 kilometers. To many people, it seems like an impossible distance. Yet every year, millions of runners around the world train for months to take part in marathons. [1] For them, finishing the race matters more than winning. They push through pain, doubt, and tiredness to reach the finish line. This spirit—never giving up even when things get hard—is what makes the marathon special. It teaches us that ordinary people can achieve extraordinary things if they keep going.

Training for a marathon takes time and patience. Runners usually start with short distances and gradually increase their weekly mileage（每周跑量） over several months. [2] They learn to listen to their bodies, rest when needed, and stay consistent even on days when they do not feel like running. This slow build-up helps avoid injuries and prepares both the body and the mind for race day. Many runners say the hardest part is not the race itself, but sticking to the training plan day after day.

On race day, the atmosphere is unlike any other sporting event. People of all ages and backgrounds line the streets to cheer. [3] Strangers shout encouragement, hand out water, and hold signs with messages like "You can do it!" or "Almost there!" For runners who are struggling, this support can make all the difference. It reminds them that they are not alone—thousands of others believe in them. Some runners even stop to thank volunteers before continuing on their way.

The final kilometers are often the toughest. Legs feel heavy, and the mind starts to doubt. [4] At this point, many runners think about why they started: a promise to a friend, a personal goal, or the wish to prove something to themselves. These thoughts give them the strength to take one more step, then another, until they finally see the finish line. Crossing it brings a mix of tears, smiles, and pride—proof that they did what once seemed impossible.

[5] Whether you run a marathon or face challenges in school, work, or life, the marathon spirit can guide you. Train step by step, accept support from others, and never stop moving forward. That is how ordinary people become extraordinary.

## 选项

A. In fact, the real victory is not about being the fastest, but about refusing to quit.
B. Family members and friends often wait at the finish line to celebrate together.
C. They follow a plan that builds strength and endurance（耐力） little by little.
D. The crowd's energy helps runners forget their pain and find new motivation.
E. They dig deep and remember the reasons that brought them to the starting line.
F. To sum up, the marathon teaches us lessons that go far beyond the race itself.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] C   [3] D   [4] E   [5] F

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：马拉松精神核心定义。空前描述"finishing the race matters more than winning...push through pain, doubt, and tiredness...never giving up"。A选项"In fact, the real victory is not about being the fastest, but about refusing to quit"（事实上，真正的胜利不在于跑得最快，而在于拒绝放弃）中"the real victory...not about being the fastest"与空前"finishing matters more than winning"呼应，"refusing to quit"与"never giving up...keep going"精准对应，自然引出"spirit"一词，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] C**：
  - **线索**：训练方法与渐进式原则。空前提出"start with short distances and gradually increase...over several months"，空后详述"listen to their bodies...rest when needed...stay consistent...slow build-up helps avoid injuries"。C选项"They follow a plan that builds strength and endurance（耐力） little by little"（他们遵循一个循序渐进增强体力和耐力的计划）中"follow a plan"与空后"training plan"呼应，"builds strength and endurance little by little"与空前"gradually increase"及空后"slow build-up"完美对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] D**：
  - **线索**：观众支持与跑者心理。空前描述"Strangers shout encouragement, hand out water, hold signs...For runners who are struggling, this support can make all the difference"。D选项"The crowd's energy helps runners forget their pain and find new motivation"（人群的能量帮助跑者忘记痛苦、获得新的动力）中"The crowd's energy"指代空前 cheering、signs、water，"forget their pain and find new motivation"与空后"make all the difference...believe in them"形成因果，衔接自然。

- **[4] E**：
  - **线索**：最后阶段的内心力量。空前指出"The final kilometers are often the toughest...Legs feel heavy, the mind starts to doubt"，空后举例"think about why they started: a promise to a friend, a personal goal...These thoughts give them the strength"。E选项"They dig deep and remember the reasons that brought them to the starting line"（他们深入内心，想起带他们来到起跑线的原因）中"dig deep"与"toughest"呼应，"remember the reasons that brought them to the starting line"与空后"think about why they started...a promise...personal goal"精准对应，衔接顺畅。

- **[5] F**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后列举"marathon or face challenges in school, work, or life"并总结"Train step by step...accept support...never stop moving forward"。F选项"To sum up, the marathon teaches us lessons that go far beyond the race itself"（总之，马拉松教会我们超越比赛本身的人生功课）用"To sum up"总结全文，"lessons that go far beyond the race itself"与空后"challenges in school, work, or life...guide you"完美呼应，收束得当。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **B. Family members and friends often wait at the finish line to celebrate together.**：描述亲友在终点等候，与马拉松主题相关，易被误填[3]。但空[3]空前明确描写"Strangers shout encouragement...hand out water...hold signs"，主角是街道旁的观众与志愿者，非终点亲友；空后"this support can make all the difference"需指代现场助威，D的"crowd's energy"与"strangers...encouragement"对应。B属"主题相关但场景错位"型干扰。
