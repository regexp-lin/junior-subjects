# 03-23 第二篇

**The Amazing Migration of Monarch Butterflies**

Every autumn, millions of monarch butterflies (帝王蝶) begin an incredible journey from Canada and the northern United States all the way to central Mexico. [1] It is one of the longest insect migrations in the entire natural world, covering up to 4,800 kilometers.

What makes this migration even more remarkable is that no single butterfly completes the entire round trip. The butterflies that fly south in autumn will lay eggs in the spring and then die. [2] Somehow, these young butterflies know exactly which direction to fly, even though they have never made the journey before.

Scientists are still trying to understand how monarch butterflies navigate so accurately. [3] They also seem to use the position of the sun and the Earth's magnetic field as a natural compass to stay on course.

Sadly, monarch butterflies are now facing serious threats. The forests in Mexico where they spend the winter are being cut down at an alarming rate. [4] Climate change is also making weather patterns more unpredictable, which can be deadly for these delicate travelers.

[5] Many communities have started planting milkweed, the only plant where monarchs lay their eggs, in gardens and along roadsides. If we all do our part, these beautiful butterflies will continue their incredible journey for generations to come.

## 选项

A. It is their children and grandchildren that will make the return trip northward.
B. One theory is that they have tiny magnetic particles in their bodies that act like a built-in GPS.
C. Fortunately, people around the world are working hard to protect these amazing creatures.
D. In addition, the use of pesticides on farms destroys the plants that these butterflies need to survive.
E. Therefore, catching and collecting monarch butterflies is highly recommended for science students.
F. This breathtaking journey takes about two months to complete and requires unbelievable endurance.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] F   [2] A   [3] B   [4] D   [5] C

## 深度解析

- **[1] F**：
  - **线索**：补充描述与数据呼应。空前介绍了帝王蝶从加拿大和美国北部到墨西哥中部的秋季迁徙。F选项"This breathtaking journey takes about two months to complete and requires unbelievable endurance"（这段令人叹为观止的旅程需要大约两个月才能完成，需要难以置信的耐力）中的"This breathtaking journey"指代前文的迁徙旅程，并补充了时间信息。空后"It is one of the longest insect migrations..."与F选项一同对这段旅程做出全面描绘。

- **[2] A**：
  - **线索**：逻辑衔接与生命周期解释。空前指出秋天南飞的蝴蝶会在春天产卵然后死去，暗示了它们无法返回。A选项"It is their children and grandchildren that will make the return trip northward"（是它们的子女和孙辈将完成北上的返程）直接回答了"那谁来返回？"的隐含问题。空后"Somehow, these young butterflies know exactly which direction to fly"中的"these young butterflies"指代A选项提到的子孙后代。

- **[3] B**：
  - **线索**：理论假说与科学解释。空前提出了科学家仍在试图理解帝王蝶如何精确导航的问题。B选项"One theory is that they have tiny magnetic particles in their bodies that act like a built-in GPS"（一种理论认为它们体内有微小的磁性颗粒，像内置GPS一样工作）直接提供了一个科学假说作为回答。空后"They also seem to use the position of the sun and the Earth's magnetic field"中的"also"表示这是第二种导航方式，与B选项的磁性颗粒理论构成并列补充关系。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：并列威胁与递进补充。空前指出墨西哥的越冬森林正被大量砍伐。D选项"In addition, the use of pesticides on farms destroys the plants that these butterflies need to survive"（此外，农场使用杀虫剂摧毁了这些蝴蝶赖以生存的植物）用"In addition"引入了第二个威胁——杀虫剂破坏食源植物。空后"Climate change is also making weather patterns more unpredictable"中的"also"则引出第三个威胁——气候变化。三个威胁层层递进。

- **[5] C**：
  - **线索**：转折希望与引出行动。空[5]位于结尾段段首。前一段详述了帝王蝶面临的多重威胁（森林砍伐、杀虫剂、气候变化）。C选项"Fortunately, people around the world are working hard to protect these amazing creatures"（幸运的是，世界各地的人们正在努力保护这些神奇的生物）用"Fortunately"从悲观转向积极。空后"Many communities have started planting milkweed..."就是人们采取保护行动的具体例证。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **E. Therefore, catching and collecting monarch butterflies is highly recommended for science students.**：该选项含有"Therefore（因此）"和"monarch butterflies（帝王蝶）""science（科学）"等话题词汇。但其主张"强烈建议科学学生捕捉和收集帝王蝶"与全文第四、五段竭力呼吁保护帝王蝶的主旨完全矛盾。且"catching and collecting"正是对濒危物种的进一步伤害，立场与全文完全背道而驰。
