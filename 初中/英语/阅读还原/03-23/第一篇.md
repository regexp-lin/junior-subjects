# 03-23 第一篇

**The Danger of Too Much Screen Time**

Today's teenagers spend an average of seven to nine hours per day staring at screens. This includes smartphones, computers, tablets, and televisions. [1] However, health experts are warning that this amount of screen time is seriously damaging to both physical and mental health.

One of the biggest problems is the effect on our eyes. Staring at a bright screen for hours without a break can cause a condition called "digital eye strain." [2] To protect your eyes, doctors recommend the "20-20-20 rule": every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds.

Too much screen time also hurts our sleep quality. The blue light from screens tricks our brain into thinking it is still daytime. [3] This is why many teenagers who use their phones before bed find it extremely difficult to fall asleep.

Furthermore, spending too many hours on social media can harm our mental health. Constantly comparing yourself to the perfect-looking lives of others online often leads to feelings of sadness and low self-worth. [4] Remember that most people only post the best moments of their lives, not the difficult, ordinary ones.

[5] Try setting a daily time limit for your devices, taking regular breaks, and putting your phone away at least one hour before bedtime. Your body and mind will certainly thank you.

## 选项

A. The symptoms include dry eyes, headaches, and blurry vision that gets worse over time.
B. As a result, the brain does not produce enough melatonin, the chemical that helps us sleep.
C. In fact, most of them do not even realize just how many hours they are actually spending online.
D. Therefore, experts believe that using screens more often is the best way to improve your grades.
E. It is important to remember that what you see online is rarely the complete truth about someone's life.
F. The good news is that there are several simple steps you can take to reduce your screen time.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] C   [2] A   [3] B   [4] E   [5] F

## 深度解析

- **[1] C**：
  - **线索**：补充说明与引出转折。空前给出了一个惊人的数据：青少年平均每天花七到九个小时盯着屏幕。C选项"In fact, most of them do not even realize just how many hours they are actually spending online"（事实上，他们中大多数人甚至没有意识到自己实际上在线花了多少小时）中的"them"指代前文的"teenagers"。这句话补充说明了一个令人担忧的细节——他们自己都没意识到。而空后"However, health experts are warning..."用转折词引入健康专家的警告，形成"事实→无意识→专家警告"的递进链。

- **[2] A**：
  - **线索**：具体症状描述与名词复现。空前引入了"digital eye strain（数字眼疲劳）"这一病症名称。A选项"The symptoms include dry eyes, headaches, and blurry vision that gets worse over time"（症状包括干眼、头痛和随时间恶化的视力模糊）直接列举了该病症的具体表现，"The symptoms"完美指代前文的"condition"。空后"To protect your eyes, doctors recommend..."给出了应对建议，构成"病因→症状→建议"的完整论证链。

- **[3] B**：
  - **线索**：因果解释与科学机制。空前指出蓝光会欺骗大脑以为还是白天。B选项"As a result, the brain does not produce enough melatonin, the chemical that helps us sleep"（结果，大脑不会产生足够的褪黑素，即帮助我们入睡的化学物质）用"As a result"承接因果关系，解释了蓝光导致的具体生理后果。空后"This is why many teenagers who use their phones before bed find it extremely difficult to fall asleep"中的"This is why"回指B选项提出的褪黑素不足问题。

- **[4] E**：
  - **线索**：论点深化与立场呼应。空前指出不断与他人在网上完美的生活进行比较会导致悲伤和低自我价值感。E选项"It is important to remember that what you see online is rarely the complete truth about someone's life"（重要的是要记住，你在网上看到的很少是某人生活的全部真相）为这种心理困扰提供了解药——网上看到的并非全貌。空后"Remember that most people only post the best moments"是对E选项的进一步具体化，两句话共同构成了"事实提醒"。

- **[5] F**：
  - **线索**：转折引入与建议过渡。空[5]位于结尾段段首。前文四段分别从眼睛、睡眠、心理三个层面阐述了过度屏幕时间的危害。F选项"The good news is that there are several simple steps you can take to reduce your screen time"（好消息是，你可以采取几个简单的步骤来减少屏幕时间）用"The good news"从负面分析转向正面解决方案。空后紧接着就列举了三个具体建议——设置时间限制、定期休息、睡前放下手机。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **D. Therefore, experts believe that using screens more often is the best way to improve your grades.**：该选项含有"Therefore（因此）"和"experts（专家）""screens（屏幕）"等高频词。但其"使用屏幕越多越能提高成绩"的意思与全文"屏幕时间过多有害"的核心主旨完全对立。且"the best way"这种绝对化表述也是常见的干扰项特征。属于"话题词汇复现+立场完全对立"的典型干扰。
