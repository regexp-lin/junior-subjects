**How Trees Talk to Each Other**

When we walk through a quiet forest, we usually think of trees as lonely giants standing silently in the soil. [1] However, scientists have recently discovered that trees actually communicate with each other in amazing ways. Beneath our feet, a hidden world of conversation is happening all the time.

How do they do this without speaking a word? The secret lies in a massive underground network of fungi (真菌). [2] It connects the roots of almost every tree in the forest, creating something similar to the human internet. Scientists playfully call this system the "Wood Wide Web".

Using this living network, trees can share vital resources. [3] Through the fungal connections, it will send extra sugar and nutrients to a younger, weaker tree growing in the dark. This incredible teamwork helps the entire forest stay healthy and strong.

Besides sharing food, trees also use this system to send out warnings. [4] These chemical messages tell the neighboring trees to start producing their own bad-tasting chemicals to defend themselves against the attackers. 

[5] So, next time you lean against a towering oak tree, remember that it is not alone. It is constantly chatting and sharing with its leafy friends in a beautiful, invisible community.

A. For example, if an older, taller tree is getting plenty of sunlight, it does not just keep it all.
B. This natural network is completely separate from the tree roots and works independently.
C. When bugs begin to eat the leaves of a certain tree, it releases special warning signals.
D. Therefore, planting trees alone in the city is a very bad idea for the environment.
E. We imagine that they do not interact with anything else around them.
F. This network consists of tiny white threads that spread everywhere under the ground.

# 03-15 第二篇

**How Trees Talk to Each Other**

When we walk through a quiet forest, we usually think of trees as lonely giants standing silently in the soil. [1] However, scientists have recently discovered that trees actually communicate with each other in amazing ways. Beneath our feet, a hidden world of conversation is happening all the time.

How do they do this without speaking a word? The secret lies in a massive underground network of fungi (真菌). [2] It connects the roots of almost every tree in the forest, creating something similar to the human internet. Scientists playfully call this system the "Wood Wide Web".

Using this living network, trees can share vital resources. [3] Through the fungal connections, it will send extra sugar and nutrients to a younger, weaker tree growing in the dark. This incredible teamwork helps the entire forest stay healthy and strong.

Besides sharing food, trees also use this system to send out warnings. [4] These chemical messages tell the neighboring trees to start producing their own bad-tasting chemicals to defend themselves against the attackers. 

[5] So, next time you lean against a towering oak tree, remember that it is not alone. It is constantly chatting and sharing with its leafy friends in a beautiful, invisible community.

## 选项

A. For example, if a taller tree is getting plenty of sunlight, it does not just keep the energy.
B. Because of this, many types of trees have stopped growing flowers and fruits altogether.
C. When insects begin to eat the leaves of a tree, it releases special warning signals underground.
D. Clearly, the forest is much more than just a simple collection of individual plants.
E. We naturally imagine that they do not interact with anything else living around them.
F. This network consists of tiny white threads that spread everywhere under the soft dirt.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] E   [2] F   [3] A   [4] C   [5] D

## 深度解析

- **[1] E**：
  - **线索**：语义递进与转折铺垫。空前提到“我们通常认为树木是孤独的巨人静静站在土壤里”。E选项“We naturally imagine that they do not interact with anything else living around them”（我们自然而然地想象它们不与周围的任何其他生物互动），其中代词“they”指代前文的“trees”，是对前一句“孤独巨人”的具体化阐释。这种固有的刻板印象，为紧接着的空后一句“However（然而），科学家最近发现树木实际上在以惊人的方式交流”提供了完美的让步反差衬托。
- **[2] F**：
  - **线索**：指代关系与逻辑延续。空前提到了“巨大的地下真菌网络（massive underground network of fungi）”。F选项“This network consists of tiny white threads that spread everywhere under the soft dirt”（这个网络由遍布柔软泥土下的微小白线组成）中的“This network”紧密指代和延续了上一句刚刚抛出的概念。随后空后一句“It connects the roots...（它连接树根）”，其中“It”则再次指代F项中的网络或白线，三句一脉相承。
- **[3] A**：
  - **线索**：代词复现与举例说明。空前是段落主旨“树木可以分享重要资源”。A选项“For example, if a taller tree is getting plenty of sunlight, it does not just keep the energy”（例如，如果一棵较高的树获得了充足的阳光，它不仅保留所有的能量）正是对如何分享资源的举例。更重要的是，空后不仅接上了怎么处理这些能量（发送额外糖分和营养给弱小的树），而且空后的主语“it”完美指代了A选项里提到的那棵“taller tree（高大的树）”。
- **[4] C**：
  - **线索**：情景预设与词汇照应。空前的引导语是“树木也使用这个系统发送警告”。C选项“When insects begin to eat the leaves of a tree, it releases special warning signals underground”（当昆虫开始吃一棵树的树叶时，它会在地下释放特殊的警告信号），这完美呈现了发出警告的具体危险情境。同时，由于C项中提到了“warning signals（警告信号）”，这也就让空后一句的主语“These chemical messages（这些化学信息）”有了明确的指代对象。
- **[5] D**：
  - **线索**：文意升华与归纳总结。空[5]位于结论段段首。在描述完树林间的找补资源和发送警告的协作后，作者需要得出结论。D选项“Clearly, the forest is much more than just a simple collection of individual plants”（显然，森林远不只是单个植物的简单集合）正是水到渠成的总结与升华（森林是一个互联的整体）。同时，这引出了最后“下次靠在树上记住它并不孤单，它在和朋友聊天”的浪漫式结语。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **B. Because of this, many types of trees have stopped growing flowers and fruits altogether.**：该干扰项包含“Because of this（因为这个原因）”以及“trees（树木）”。但其表述的意思是“因为交流，很多树完全停止了开花和长果实”。首先这个结论违背基础生物学常识；其次，全篇在讲述的是树木间互相合作、分享资源以“stay healthy and strong（保持健康强壮）”。干扰项表达的功能丧失和萎缩，与文章的赞颂、正向的主基调大相径庭，利用逻辑违和感构成的典型反向干扰。
