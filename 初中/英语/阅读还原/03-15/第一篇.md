# 03-15 第一篇

**The Reality of Life in Space**

When we watch movies about astronauts, life in space always looks exciting and full of adventure. They float around, wear cool spacesuits, and explore unknown planets. [1] In reality, living on the International Space Station (ISS) is actually very difficult and requires strict daily routines.

One of the biggest challenges in space is the lack of gravity. Without gravity pulling them down, astronauts' muscles and bones begin to weaken very quickly. [2] Therefore, they are required to spend at least two hours exercising every single day just to stay healthy.

Sleeping is another tricky part of daily life in orbit. Since there is no "up" or "down" in space, astronauts cannot sleep in normal beds. [3] This prevents them from floating around the cabin and accidentally bumping into important computer equipment while they dream.

Eating in space also requires special care. Food must be carefully packaged so it does not fly away. [4] If tiny crumbs (碎屑) float into the machines or into an astronaut's eye, they could cause a serious accident. That is why space food is usually sticky or wet.

[5] Despite these everyday difficulties, most astronauts agree that the amazing view of Earth makes all the hard work completely worthwhile.

## 选项

A. To solve this problem, they zip themselves into special sleeping bags attached to the wall.
B. However, the truth of space travel is much less glamorous than Hollywood suggests.
C. For this reason, many astronauts choose to sleep floating freely in the middle of the room.
D. It is extremely important that meals do not create any loose pieces while eating.
E. Therefore, future space missions plan to build artificial gravity machines.
F. To fight this dangerous effect, the space station is equipped with special running machines.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] B   [2] F   [3] A   [4] D   [5] (Wait, where is the conclusion choice?)

# 03-15 第一篇

**The Reality of Life in Space**

When we watch movies about astronauts, life in space always looks exciting and full of adventure. They float around, wear cool spacesuits, and explore unknown planets. [1] In reality, living on the International Space Station (ISS) is actually very difficult and requires strict daily routines.

One of the biggest challenges in space is the lack of gravity. Without gravity pulling them down, astronauts' muscles and bones begin to weaken very quickly. [2] Therefore, they usually spend at least two hours exercising every single day just to stay healthy.

Sleeping is another tricky part of daily life in orbit. Since there is no "up" or "down" in space, astronauts cannot sleep in normal beds. [3] This prevents them from floating around the cabin and accidentally bumping into important computer equipment while they dream.

Eating in space also requires special care. Food must be carefully packaged so it does not fly away. [4] If tiny crumbs (碎屑) float into the machines or into an astronaut's eye, they could cause a serious accident. That is why space food is usually sticky or wet.

[5] Despite these everyday difficulties, most astronauts agree that the amazing view of Earth makes all the hard work completely worthwhile.

## 选项

A. To solve this problem, they zip themselves into special sleeping bags attached to the wall.
B. However, the truth of space travel is much less glamorous than Hollywood suggests.
C. For this reason, many astronauts choose to sleep floating freely in the middle of the room.
D. It is extremely important that meals do not create any loose pieces while eating.
E. In short, being an astronaut is certainly not an easy relaxing holiday.
F. To fight this dangerous effect, the space station is equipped with special running machines.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] B   [2] F   [3] A   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] B**：
  - **线索**：主旨转折与同义替换。空前描述了电影里的太空生活（exciting/adventure/cool spacesuits）。B选项用“However（然而）”引出真实情况，其语意“太空旅行的真相远没有好莱坞暗示的那样光鲜亮丽”完美承接。“the truth”呼应后一句的“In reality（实际上）”，“Hollywood”则是对前文“movies（电影）”的同义替换，点明了第一段主旨——澄清关于太空旅行的浪漫误解。
- **[2] F**：
  - **线索**：指代关系与因果照应。空前提到了一个因为无重力导致的严重问题：宇航员的肌肉和骨骼会很快变弱。F选项“To fight this dangerous effect, the space station is equipped with special running machines”（为了对抗这种危险的后果，空间站配备了特殊的跑步机）。其中，“this dangerous effect（这种危险后果）”完美指代“肌肉骨骼变弱”。并且，“配备特殊跑步机”的行为自然顺滑地引出了后文的“因此，他们通常每天花至少两小时锻炼（exercising）”。
- **[3] A**：
  - **线索**：代词指代与逻辑动作。空前提出了“在太空不能像在正常床上睡觉”的难题。A选项“To solve this problem, they zip themselves into special sleeping bags attached to the wall”（为了解决这个问题，他们把自己装进固定在墙上的特殊睡袋里拉上拉链）。“this problem”指代前文的睡觉难题，而把自己装在“固定（attached）”的睡袋里，完美契合空后一句所描述的结果行为：“This prevents them from floating around...（这就防止了他们四处漂浮……）”。
- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：上下文词汇复现与逻辑推演。空前说道食物必须小心包装，以免飞走。D选项“It is extremely important that meals do not create any loose pieces while eating”（极其重要的是，吃饭时食物不要产生任何松散的碎屑）。这句话是对如何小心包装、避免飞走的最核心要求阐释。紧接着的空后一句中的“tiny crumbs（微小碎屑）”正是对选项中“loose pieces（松散碎屑）”的同义替换和具象化，并引出碎屑带来的灾难性后果。
- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：总结全文与语境过渡。空[5]位于结论段段首。前面用三个段落分别论述了在太空中运动、睡觉、吃饭的困难和不易。E选项“In short, being an astronaut is certainly not an easy relaxing holiday”（简而言之，成为一名宇航员绝不是一个轻松放松的假期）借助总结词“In short”，将前文所有的辛苦作了完美结语。之后自然引出让步状语“Despite these everyday difficulties...（尽管有这些日常困难……）”，完成对全篇的升华（风景让一切值得）。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **C. For this reason, many astronauts choose to sleep floating freely in the middle of the room.**：该项包含了“astronauts（宇航员）”和“sleep floating（飘着睡）”等核心词汇，使用了“For this reason（由于这个原因）”。然而，空前的客观事实是“太空里没有上下之分，不能睡普通床”。如果按照C选项的逻辑任由自己飘在中间睡，那么必然会导致空后说的“撞到重要电脑设备”的危险。本句逻辑不仅内部不连贯，还与第四段讲述太空生活需要极度严谨小心的主基调相悖。
