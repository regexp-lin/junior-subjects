# 05-04 第一篇

**Why Do We Dream?**

Every night, when you close your eyes and fall asleep, your brain does not shut down. Instead, it enters a mysterious world of dreams. Most people dream several times a night, though they may not remember all of their dreams in the morning. [1] Scientists have been studying dreams for centuries, but there is still no single answer that everyone agrees on. Dreams remain one of the great mysteries of the human mind.

One popular theory is that dreams help us process emotions. [2] During the day, we experience many feelings—happiness, sadness, fear, excitement. When we sleep, our brains sort through these emotions and try to make sense of them. This is why you might dream about a test you are worried about or a friend you miss. In this way, dreams may help us deal with stress and feel better when we wake up.

Another theory suggests that dreams help strengthen memory. [3] While we sleep, our brains replay events from the day and decide which information to keep and which to forget. Important facts and skills are moved into long-term memory, while unimportant details are cleared away. Some scientists believe that this is why students who get a good night's sleep before an exam often perform better—their brains have had time to organize what they learned.

Not all dreams are pleasant, of course. Nightmares（噩梦） can be frightening and may wake you up in the middle of the night. [4] Nightmares are often caused by stress, anxiety, or watching scary movies before bed. While they can be unpleasant, most nightmares are harmless. If you have frequent nightmares, talking about your worries with someone you trust can often help reduce them.

[5] Whether dreams are messages from our unconscious mind or simply the brain's way of cleaning up, they are a fascinating part of human experience. Sweet dreams!

## 选项

A. In other words, dreams may act as a kind of emotional therapy during sleep.
B. This process is similar to organizing files on a computer.
C. However, most experts agree that nightmares serve a useful purpose as well.
D. The question of why we dream has puzzled thinkers for thousands of years.
E. In the end, the true purpose of dreams may never be fully understood.
F. For instance, some ancient cultures believed that dreams could predict the future.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] D   [2] A   [3] B   [4] C   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] D**：
  - **线索**：引出梦的未解之谜。空前描述"Most people dream several times a night, though they may not remember all of their dreams"，空后说明"Scientists have been studying dreams for centuries...no single answer...Dreams remain one of the great mysteries"。D选项"The question of why we dream has puzzled thinkers for thousands of years"（为什么做梦这个问题困扰了思想家数千年）中"why we dream...puzzled...for thousands of years"自然引出空后"Scientists have been studying...for centuries"及"great mysteries"，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] A**：
  - **线索**：梦帮助处理情绪。空前提出"One popular theory is that dreams help us process emotions"，空后描述"During the day, we experience many feelings...our brains sort through these emotions...dream about a test you are worried about...deal with stress and feel better"。A选项"In other words, dreams may act as a kind of emotional therapy during sleep"（换言之，梦可能是一种睡眠中的情绪治疗）中"In other words"概括空前理论，"emotional therapy"与空后"sort through emotions...deal with stress...feel better"完美呼应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] B**：
  - **线索**：梦帮助巩固记忆。空前提出"Another theory suggests that dreams help strengthen memory"，空后说明"our brains replay events from the day and decide which information to keep and which to forget...Important facts...moved into long-term memory...unimportant details are cleared away"。B选项"This process is similar to organizing files on a computer"（这个过程类似于整理电脑上的文件）中"This process"指代记忆巩固，"organizing files"与空后"decide which to keep and which to forget...moved into long-term memory...cleared away"形成生动类比，衔接自然。

- **[4] C**：
  - **线索**：噩梦的积极意义。空前描述"Nightmares can be frightening and may wake you up in the middle of the night"，空后说明"Nightmares are often caused by stress, anxiety...most nightmares are harmless...talking about your worries...can help reduce them"。C选项"However, most experts agree that nightmares serve a useful purpose as well"（然而，大多数专家认为噩梦也有其有用的作用）中"However"转折空前噩梦的可怕，"serve a useful purpose"引出空后噩梦虽不愉快但无害、可通过倾诉缓解的积极信息，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"Whether dreams are messages from our unconscious mind or simply the brain's way of cleaning up...a fascinating part of human experience. Sweet dreams!"。E选项"In the end, the true purpose of dreams may never be fully understood"（最终，梦的真正目的可能永远无法被完全理解）中"In the end"收束全文，"never be fully understood"与空后"Whether...or simply..."的开放式总结及"fascinating"形成呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For instance, some ancient cultures believed that dreams could predict the future.**：涉及古代文化对梦的理解，易被误填[1]。但F以"For instance"引出古代文化的单一例证，而空[1]后文聚焦于"Scientists have been studying..."的科学研究视角，F的"ancient cultures...predict the future"与科学研究主线不符；且空[1]需要的是引出研究困惑的过渡句，而非具体文化案例。F属"历史话题相关但与科学讨论主线偏移"型干扰。
