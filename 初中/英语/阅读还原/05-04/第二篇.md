# 05-04 第二篇

**Bike-Sharing: A Green Way to Get Around**

In cities around the world, colorful bicycles line the streets, waiting for their next rider. Bike-sharing programs have become hugely popular in recent years. [1] Users can pick up a bike at one station, ride it to their destination, and leave it at another station nearby. It is a quick, cheap, and environmentally friendly way to travel short distances.

Bike-sharing is good for the environment in several ways. [2] Every time someone chooses a shared bike instead of a car or taxi, they reduce air pollution and carbon emissions（碳排放）. Cars are one of the biggest sources of greenhouse gases in cities. By replacing short car trips with bike rides, bike-sharing programs help clean the air and fight climate change. Even small changes in how people travel can add up to a big difference.

Bike-sharing also improves people's health. [3] Riding a bicycle is excellent exercise for the heart, lungs, and muscles. Many people who use shared bikes say they feel more energetic and less stressed. For office workers who sit at a desk all day, a short bike ride to or from work is a great way to stay active. Studies show that regular cycling can lower the risk of heart disease and help people maintain a healthy weight.

Of course, bike-sharing programs face some challenges. [4] Some bikes are damaged or left in the wrong places, blocking sidewalks and causing problems for pedestrians（行人）. In some cities, there are too many shared bikes, creating clutter（杂乱） on the streets. To solve these problems, companies are using GPS tracking and fines to encourage users to park bikes properly. Governments are also building more bike lanes to make cycling safer.

[5] Despite these challenges, bike-sharing remains one of the best transportation ideas of recent times. It is a win-win solution that benefits individuals, cities, and the planet. As more people discover the joy of cycling, our cities will become cleaner, healthier, and more livable.

## 选项

A. The idea is simple: borrow a bike when you need one and return it when you are done.
B. The biggest benefit is that bikes produce zero emissions while being ridden.
C. It is not just the environment that benefits—our bodies do, too.
D. However, not every user treats the bikes with care.
E. All things considered, bike-sharing is a step in the right direction for urban transportation.
F. For example, some cities offer free bike-sharing during festivals to promote green travel.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：解释共享单车的基本使用方式。空前描述"Bike-sharing programs have become hugely popular in recent years"，空后说明"Users can pick up a bike at one station, ride it to their destination, and leave it at another station...quick, cheap, and environmentally friendly"。A选项"The idea is simple: borrow a bike when you need one and return it when you are done"（理念很简单：需要时借一辆自行车，用完后归还）中"The idea is simple"承接空前"popular"，"borrow...return"与空后"pick up...ride...leave"的使用流程完美呼应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：共享单车的环保优势。空前提出"Bike-sharing is good for the environment in several ways"，空后具体说明"Every time someone chooses a shared bike instead of a car or taxi, they reduce air pollution and carbon emissions...Cars are one of the biggest sources of greenhouse gases"。B选项"The biggest benefit is that bikes produce zero emissions while being ridden"（最大的好处是骑行时自行车零排放）中"biggest benefit...zero emissions"承接空前"good for the environment"，与空后"reduce air pollution and carbon emissions"形成因果呼应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：从环保好处过渡到健康好处。空前提出"Bike-sharing also improves people's health"，空后列举"Riding a bicycle is excellent exercise for the heart, lungs, and muscles...feel more energetic and less stressed...stay active...lower the risk of heart disease"。C选项"It is not just the environment that benefits—our bodies do, too"（受益的不仅是环境——我们的身体也是如此）中"not just the environment...our bodies do, too"从上段环保主题过渡到本段健康主题，与空后"excellent exercise...more energetic"呼应，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：共享单车面临的问题。空前提出"bike-sharing programs face some challenges"，空后描述"Some bikes are damaged or left in the wrong places, blocking sidewalks...too many shared bikes, creating clutter"。D选项"However, not every user treats the bikes with care"（然而，并非每个用户都爱护单车）中"However"承接前文好处转向问题，"not every user treats...with care"自然引出空后"damaged...left in the wrong places...blocking sidewalks"的具体问题，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"bike-sharing remains one of the best transportation ideas...a win-win solution that benefits individuals, cities, and the planet...cities will become cleaner, healthier, and more livable"。E选项"All things considered, bike-sharing is a step in the right direction for urban transportation"（总的来说，共享单车是城市交通朝正确方向迈出的一步）用"All things considered"总结全文，"step in the right direction"与空后"best transportation ideas...win-win solution"形成呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, some cities offer free bike-sharing during festivals to promote green travel.**：涉及城市推广共享单车的具体举措，易被误填[2]。但F以"For example"引出节日免费骑行的单一案例，而空[2]讨论的是共享单车环保优势的总论，空后已有"Every time someone chooses...reduce air pollution"的具体分析；F的"free...during festivals"是推广措施，与空[2]段落的"环保原理"话题不匹配。F属"内容相关但讨论维度偏移"型干扰。
