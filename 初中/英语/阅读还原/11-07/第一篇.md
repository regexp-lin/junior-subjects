# 11-07 第一篇

**Healthy Eating: Fuel for Body and Mind**

What we eat affects not only our bodies but also our minds. A balanced diet gives us the energy we need for school, sports, and daily activities. It helps us grow, strengthens our immune system, and keeps our bones and muscles healthy. On the other hand, too much junk food, sugar, or processed food can leave us tired, affect our mood, and increase the risk of health problems later in life. [1] Making smart food choices is one of the most important things we can do for our health, and it is never too early—or too late—to start.

A healthy diet includes a variety of foods from different groups. Fruits and vegetables provide vitamins, minerals, and fiber. Grains like rice and bread give us energy. Protein from meat, fish, eggs, or beans helps build and repair our bodies. Dairy products support strong bones. No single food provides everything we need, so eating a mix of different foods is the key. [2] Variety is the secret to a balanced diet, ensuring that our bodies get the full range of nutrients they need to function well.

Breakfast is often called the most important meal of the day, and for good reason. After a night of sleep, our bodies need fuel to start the morning. Students who eat breakfast tend to concentrate better in class and perform better on tests. Skipping breakfast can lead to low energy, irritability, and overeating later. A simple breakfast—such as oatmeal, fruit, or eggs—can make a big difference. [3] Starting the day with a nutritious meal sets the tone for better choices throughout the day and supports both physical and mental performance.

In today's busy world, many people find it hard to eat well. Fast food is convenient but often high in salt, sugar, and fat. Advertising pushes unhealthy snacks and drinks, especially toward young people. However, healthy eating does not have to be complicated or expensive. Cooking at home, packing lunches, and choosing water over sugary drinks are small changes that add up. [4] With a little planning and awareness, we can develop eating habits that support our goals and improve our quality of life.

The habits we form when we are young often last a lifetime. Learning to enjoy healthy foods, understanding what our bodies need, and cooking simple meals are skills that will serve us well into adulthood. Families and schools can help by offering nutritious meals and teaching about nutrition. [5] Building a foundation of healthy eating habits in childhood is an investment in a healthier, happier future, and it is something we can all work toward together.
