# 04-06 第二篇

**Why Dogs Are Man's Best Friend**

"Dogs are man's best friend" is a saying we hear often, and there is good reason for it. For thousands of years, humans and dogs have lived and worked together. [1] Today, dogs can be found in millions of homes around the world, not just as pets but as companions that bring joy, comfort, and even protection. This special bond is built on trust, loyalty, and a long history of cooperation.

The friendship between humans and dogs began a very long time ago. Scientists believe that wolves, the ancestors of modern dogs, first came near human camps to find food. [2] Over time, the wolves that were less fearful and more friendly stayed closer to humans. These animals were eventually tamed and became the first dogs. This process may have started more than 15,000 years ago, making the human-dog relationship one of the oldest partnerships in history.

Dogs have helped humans in countless ways. In the past, they guarded homes, hunted, and pulled sleds. [3] Today, some dogs are trained to guide people who cannot see, to detect illnesses, or to comfort people in hospitals and schools. Their sharp senses and willingness to learn make them excellent helpers. Many families also rely on dogs for companionship, especially when people feel lonely or stressed.

What makes the bond between humans and dogs so strong? One answer lies in the way dogs respond to us. [4] Dogs can read our emotions and often seem to know when we are sad or need support. They show loyalty and excitement when we come home, and many owners report that their dogs make them feel happier and less anxious. Scientists have found that spending time with a dog can even lower blood pressure and reduce stress.

[5] Whether you have a dog or simply enjoy meeting them, it is clear that these animals have earned their title as our best friends. They give us love, support, and loyalty without asking for much in return—and that is something truly special.

## 选项

A. Gradually, those that were calmer and more social began to form bonds with people.
B. In return, people offered them food and shelter, and both sides benefited.
C. The connection goes beyond practical help—it is also deeply emotional.
D. In fact, no other animal has lived alongside humans for as long or as closely as the dog.
E. Their roles have changed, but their value to humans has only grown.
F. For many people, a dog is more than a pet; it is a member of the family.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] D   [2] A   [3] E   [4] C   [5] F

## 深度解析

- **[1] D**：
  - **线索**：历史地位总结与衔接。空前总述"humans and dogs have lived and worked together"及"thousands of years"，空后描述"Today, dogs can be found in millions of homes...as companions"。D选项"In fact, no other animal has lived alongside humans for as long or as closely as the dog"（事实上，没有其他动物像狗一样与人类如此长久、如此紧密地生活在一起）用"In fact"强调狗与人类关系的独特性，"as long or as closely"呼应"thousands of years"与后文"millions of homes"，自然过渡到当今的普遍性。

- **[2] A**：
  - **线索**：驯化过程与因果递进。空前描述"wolves...came near human camps to find food"，空后"Over time, the wolves that were less fearful and more friendly stayed closer to humans"。A选项"Gradually, those that were calmer and more social began to form bonds with people"（渐渐地，那些更温顺、更合群的开始与人类建立联系）中"Gradually"与空后"Over time"呼应，"calmer and more social"与"less fearful and more friendly"同义，"form bonds"自然引出空后"stayed closer...eventually tamed"的驯化结果，逻辑连贯。

- **[3] E**：
  - **线索**：古今对比与承上启下。空前列举过去"guarded homes, hunted, pulled sleds"，空后"Today, some dogs are trained to guide...detect illnesses...comfort people"。E选项"Their roles have changed, but their value to humans has only grown"（它们的角色已改变，但对人类的价值却有增无减）概括古今转变，空后具体展开今日角色，形成总分关系，衔接自然。

- **[4] C**：
  - **线索**：情感层面转折与具体展开。空前设问"What makes the bond...so strong?"并提到"the way dogs respond to us"，空后描述"Dogs can read our emotions...loyalty and excitement...feel happier and less anxious"。C选项"The connection goes beyond practical help—it is also deeply emotional"（这种联系超越了实际帮助——它也是情感层面的）用"goes beyond practical help"承接前文工作犬角色，引出空后情感、共情、减压等描述，逻辑清晰。

- **[5] F**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后"Whether you have a dog or simply enjoy meeting them...earned their title as our best friends...love, support, and loyalty"。F选项"For many people, a dog is more than a pet; it is a member of the family"（对很多人来说，狗不只是宠物，更是家庭成员）概括人与狗的情感深度，与空后"best friends""love, support, loyalty"形成呼应，为全文总结定调。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **B. In return, people offered them food and shelter, and both sides benefited.**：提及人与狼的互利关系，与空[2]驯化过程相关。但空[2]前文为"wolves...came near...to find food"，空后为"wolves that were less fearful...stayed closer...eventually tamed"，逻辑链是"靠近→筛选温顺者→驯化"。B的"In return"暗示已完成某种交换，而空后仍在描述筛选与驯化的过程；且B强调"both sides benefited"的互利结果，空[2]需的是从"靠近"到"留下"的中间过渡。A的"form bonds"更准确衔接这一过程。属"时间逻辑错位"型干扰。
