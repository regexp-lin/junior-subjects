# 06-26 第一篇

**The Revolution of the Steam Engine**

Before the steam engine, most work depended on human labour or animal power. Factories, mines（矿井）, and farms could only produce as much as people and horses could manage. [1] All of this changed in the 18th century when inventors improved a simple idea: use steam to push a piston（活塞）back and forth, turning heat into motion. James Watt, a Scottish engineer, made key improvements that made the steam engine practical for factories and railways. His design used less coal and produced more power, which lowered costs and changed how goods were made and transported.

The steam engine changed industry and transport. [2] In factories, steam-powered machines could run looms（织布机）, pumps, and other equipment day and night without stopping. This made cloth, metal goods, and other products much cheaper and easier to produce. On land, steam locomotives（蒸汽机车）pulled trains across countries, carrying people and goods faster than ever. On water, steamships crossed oceans without relying on wind, making travel and trade more reliable. The world seemed to shrink as distances became easier to cover.

Steam power also shaped society in new ways. [3] Cities grew around factories because workers needed to live near their jobs. Railways connected towns and countryside, so people could move to find work or visit family. The demand for coal and iron（铁）rose sharply, creating mining towns and new jobs. At the same time, life became busier and noisier—factories ran around the clock, and trains brought both opportunity and pollution. People began to discuss workers' rights, safety rules, and the effects of industry on health and nature.

Not everything about the steam age was positive. [4] Child labour was common in many factories, and working conditions were often dangerous and unhealthy. Smoke from coal polluted the air in industrial cities. Rivers were sometimes filled with waste from factories. Over time, laws were passed to protect workers and limit pollution. The steam engine had started a revolution—but it also raised questions about how progress should be balanced with human welfare and the environment. These debates continue today as we face similar issues with new technologies.

[5] The steam engine turned heat into motion, and motion into a new world. It helped build the modern age of industry, transport, and global trade. Understanding this history reminds us that technology can change society quickly—and that we must think carefully about how we use it.

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前提出"The steam engine changed industry and transport"，空后分述"In factories...steam-powered machines run looms, pumps, equipment day and night...cloth, metal goods cheaper...On land steam locomotives pulled trains...faster...On water steamships crossed oceans without wind...travel and trade more reliable...world seemed to shrink"。B选项"In factories, steam-powered machines ran looms, pumps, and equipment; on land and water, trains and steamships made travel and trade faster and more reliable"（工厂中蒸汽机驱动织布机、泵等设备；陆地和水中，火车与轮船使交通和贸易更快更可靠）中"factories...looms, pumps, equipment...land and water...trains and steamships...faster and more reliable"与空后内容精准对应，逻辑紧密。
