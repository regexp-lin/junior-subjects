# 10-12 第二篇

**Ancient Civilizations: Lessons from the Past**

Long before modern cities and technology, great civilizations rose and fell around the world. The Egyptians built pyramids. The Chinese invented paper and printing. The Maya developed a complex calendar. These ancient societies left behind art, writing, buildings, and ideas that still influence us today. [1] By studying how they lived, how they solved problems, and why some thrived while others collapsed, we can better understand our own world and the challenges we face.

Archaeology—the study of ancient objects and sites—helps us piece together the past. Digs uncover tools, pottery, bones, and even entire cities buried under sand or jungle. Each find adds to our picture of how people lived, what they ate, and how they organized their societies. [2] New technologies, such as DNA analysis and satellite imaging, are helping archaeologists learn even more, revealing connections between cultures and showing how ideas and goods traveled across ancient trade routes.

Ancient civilizations also teach us about sustainability. Some societies, like the Romans, built aqueducts and roads that lasted for centuries. Others, like the Maya of some regions, may have struggled when their population grew faster than their resources. [3] Looking at what worked and what did not can help us think about how we manage water, land, and resources today, and whether our choices will benefit future generations.

Art and writing from the past give us a window into how people thought and felt. Poems, laws, and stories from ancient Greece, India, China, and elsewhere show that humans have long grappled with questions of justice, beauty, and meaning. [4] These texts remind us that the big questions we ask today—about right and wrong, about how to live well—are not new, and that we can learn from the wisdom and mistakes of those who came before us.

We do not need to copy the past, but we can learn from it. Ancient civilizations show both human creativity and human limits. [5] By honoring what they achieved and understanding what they lost, we can make wiser choices for our own time and for the generations that will follow.

## 选项

A. Learning from ancient civilizations is not just about facts and dates—it is about understanding patterns of human behavior, innovation, and collapse that still matter today.
B. Advances in science and technology are opening new doors for archaeologists, allowing them to answer old questions and to discover links between peoples and places that were once unknown.
C. The rise and fall of past societies can serve as a guide for our own—showing us which practices support long-term success and which can lead to decline when resources run short or the environment is damaged.
D. The ideas and values explored in ancient literature and art continue to shape how we think about ethics, society, and the human experience, and they connect us to a shared history of thought and reflection.
E. The past does not tell us exactly what to do, but it offers lessons, cautionary tales, and inspiration that can help us build a better future if we pay attention.
F. For example, the ruins of Pompeii in Italy give us a detailed snapshot of Roman daily life, frozen in time by a volcanic eruption nearly 2,000 years ago.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E
