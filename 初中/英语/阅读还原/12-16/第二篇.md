**The History of Radio: From Waves to Worldwide Reach**

Radio was one of the first ways to send information without wires. In the late 1800s, scientists like Marconi and Tesla experimented with radio waves. By the early 1900s, the first radio broadcasts reached listeners at home. People could hear news, music, and stories from far away. Radio quickly became a household necessity. It changed how people learned about the world and entertained themselves. [1]

The "Golden Age of Radio" lasted from the 1920s to the 1950s. Families gathered around the radio for news, sports, and drama shows. Radio was the main source of entertainment before television. During World War II, leaders used radio to speak directly to their nations. Radio connected people across countries and brought distant events into living rooms. [2]

Radio technology improved over time. FM radio offered better sound quality than AM. Transistors made radios smaller and portable. Today, radio exists alongside podcasts, streaming music, and internet broadcasts. Many people still listen to radio in the car or at work. Community and public radio stations provide local news and diverse programming. Radio has adapted rather than disappeared. [3]

Radio remains important in emergencies. When the power goes out or the internet fails, battery-powered radios can still receive information. In remote areas, radio is often the only way to get news and weather. Organizations use radio to reach people who lack internet access. In this sense, radio is more than nostalgia—it is a lifeline. [4]

The story of radio shows how a simple technology can have a lasting impact. It brought the world closer and continues to serve people in new ways. [5]

## 选项

A. Radio emerged as a revolutionary way to communicate across distances and quickly became part of daily life.
B. During this period, radio was the center of home entertainment and a powerful tool for information.
C. Despite new media, radio has evolved and still holds a place in modern life.
D. In times of crisis or in areas with limited connectivity, radio remains essential.
E. Radio's history demonstrates that old technologies can find new roles in a changing world.
F. For example, the BBC World Service has broadcast to global audiences since the 1930s.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍无线电为首批无线传信方式之一、19世纪末马可尼特斯拉等实验、20世纪初首次广播入户、可听新闻音乐故事、迅速成为家庭必需品、改变认知与娱乐方式，空后需总结革命性意义与普及。A的"emerged as a revolutionary way to communicate across distances and quickly became part of daily life"与前文对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说广播黄金时代约1920–1950、家庭围坐听新闻体育戏剧、电视出现前为主要娱乐、二战期间领袖用广播演说、连接各国、把远方带入客厅，空后需总结黄金时代的地位与功能。B的"During this period, radio was the center of home entertainment and a powerful tool for information"与前文呼应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说技术不断改进、FM音质优于AM、晶体管使收音机便携、如今与播客流媒体并存、车载或工作中仍听、社区与公共电台提供本地多元节目、适应而非消失，空后需总结在新媒体时代的延续。C的"Despite new media, radio has evolved and still holds a place in modern life"与前文对应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说广播在紧急情况仍重要、断电断网时电池收音机可收信息、偏远地区常为唯一信息源、组织用广播触达无网人群、不止怀旧更是生命线，空后需总结应急与偏远场景的意义。D的"In times of crisis or in areas with limited connectivity, radio remains essential"与前文呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说广播故事表明简单技术可有持久影响、拉近世界、继续以新方式服务人们，空后需总结技术延续与更新。E的"Radio's history demonstrates that old technologies can find new roles in a changing world"与前文对应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the BBC World Service...**：以For example开头，为具体广播机构案例。各空均为概括或过渡句；[2]易被误选因涉及广播机构，但该空需总结黄金时代广播的中心地位，而非补充某一机构的细节。
