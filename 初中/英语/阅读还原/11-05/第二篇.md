**Traditional Festivals: Keeping Culture Alive**

Every culture in the world has its own festivals and holidays. These special days bring families and communities together to celebrate, remember the past, or welcome the seasons. From the Chinese Spring Festival to Christmas, from Diwali to Thanksgiving, festivals are a way for people to share their values and pass traditions from one generation to the next. [1] Festivals serve as a bridge between the past and the present, helping people stay connected to their roots while celebrating with those they love.

Many festivals have deep historical roots. The Dragon Boat Festival in China honors the ancient poet Qu Yuan; Thanksgiving in the United States recalls the early settlers' harvest feast with Native Americans. These stories give meaning to the food we eat, the games we play, and the rituals we follow. When children learn why we celebrate, they understand more about their culture and identity. [2] Understanding the history behind festivals helps us appreciate their significance and keeps important stories alive for future generations.

Food plays a central role in most celebrations. Mooncakes during the Mid-Autumn Festival, turkey on Thanksgiving, and special sweets during festivals in India—these dishes are not just delicious; they carry cultural meaning. Families often spend hours preparing traditional meals together, and the recipes are passed down through the years. [3] Food connects us to our heritage in a powerful way, turning every meal into a moment of shared memory and belonging.

In today's globalized world, festivals face new challenges. Young people may live far from their hometowns, and busy schedules can make it hard to gather. Some worry that festivals are becoming too commercial, focused on shopping rather than meaning. Yet many communities are finding creative ways to keep traditions alive, from online family gatherings to school programs that teach children about their cultural heritage. [4] Adapting festivals to modern life while preserving their core spirit is a challenge that many cultures are working to address.

Looking ahead, festivals will continue to evolve, but their purpose remains the same: to bring people together, to honor the past, and to create memories for the future. Whether celebrated in a big city or a small village, whether traditional or newly adapted, festivals remind us of who we are and what we value. [5] As long as people care about their roots and each other, festivals will remain a vital part of human life, celebrating our shared humanity across differences.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍全球各地节日的普遍性——中国春节、圣诞、排灯节、感恩节，节日让人们团聚、分享价值观、传承传统，空后需概括节日的联结作用。A的"bridge between the past and the present"与"pass traditions from one generation to the next"呼应，"stay connected to their roots while celebrating with those they love"与"bring families and communities together"对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前举例端午节纪念屈原、感恩节追溯早期 settlers 与印第安人的丰收宴，说明故事赋予食物、游戏、仪式意义，孩子理解节日能更好认识文化，空后需总结历史理解的价值。B的"Understanding the history behind festivals"与前文"learn why we celebrate"呼应，"keeps important stories alive for future generations"与"pass traditions"对应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说食物在节日中的核心地位——月饼、火鸡、印度甜食，承载文化含义，家庭共同准备传统餐食、代代相传菜谱，空后需总结食物与传承的联结。C的"Food connects us to our heritage"与前文"carry cultural meaning"呼应，"shared memory and belonging"与"preparing traditional meals together"对应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说现代挑战——年轻人离乡、忙碌难聚、商业化担忧，同时提到社区通过线上聚会、学校教育等方式创新传承，空后需总结适应现代与保留本质的平衡。D的"Adapting festivals to modern life while preserving their core spirit"与前文挑战与创新呼应，"many cultures are working to address"与"communities are finding creative ways"对应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前展望节日未来——会演变但目的不变，联结人们、致敬过去、创造回忆，无论城市或乡村、传统或新形式，空后需总结节日对人类的持续意义。E的"As long as people care about their roots and each other"与"remind us of who we are"呼应，"celebrating our shared humanity across differences"是全文合理收尾。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For instance, during the Lantern Festival in China, families make and display colorful lanterns and solve riddles written on them.**：以For instance开头，为具体节日习俗案例（中国元宵节）。各空均为概括性或过渡句；[3]易被误选因涉及节日习俗，但该空需概括食物与传承的关系，而非元宵节做灯笼、猜灯谜的具体习俗。
