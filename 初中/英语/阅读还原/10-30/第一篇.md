# 10-30 第一篇

**Guide Dogs: Leading the Way to Independence**

For people who are blind or have serious vision loss, moving around independently can be difficult. Streets, stairs, and obstacles pose real dangers. Guide dogs—specially trained dogs that help their owners navigate safely—have changed the lives of many people with visual impairments. These loyal companions do more than lead the way; they offer companionship, confidence, and a greater sense of freedom. [1] Guide dogs are not just pets; they are working partners that undergo years of training to perform a vital role in the daily lives of their owners.

Training a guide dog is a long and careful process. Puppies are first raised by volunteer families who teach them basic obedience and social skills. Then they return to training centers, where professional trainers teach them to avoid obstacles, stop at curbs, and navigate busy streets and public transport. Only the dogs that pass strict tests are matched with an owner. [2] The training is demanding because a guide dog must be reliable in all situations, staying calm in crowds, traffic, and unexpected events to keep its owner safe.

A guide dog and its owner form a close bond. The owner learns to read the dog's movements and signals; the dog learns to respond to the owner's commands and habits. Together, they work as a team. Many owners say that their guide dog has given them more than mobility—it has given them the courage to try new things and live more fully. [3] The relationship between a guide dog and its owner is built on trust, communication, and mutual respect, and it often lasts for many years.

Guide dogs are welcomed in most public places, including shops, restaurants, and public transport. Laws in many countries protect the right of people with guide dogs to access these spaces. Still, some people may not understand that guide dogs are working and should not be patted or distracted while on duty. [4] Public awareness and respect for guide dogs help ensure that people with visual impairments can move through society with dignity and without unnecessary barriers.

Organizations that train and place guide dogs rely on donations and volunteers. Breeding, raising, training, and matching each dog with the right owner costs a lot of money and time. Many programs provide guide dogs free of charge to people who need them. [5] Support for guide dog programs is an investment in independence and equality, helping people with visual impairments participate fully in work, study, and community life.

## 选项

A. Guide dogs are not just pets; they are working partners that undergo years of training to perform a vital role in the daily lives of their owners.
B. The training is demanding because a guide dog must be reliable in all situations, staying calm in crowds, traffic, and unexpected events to keep its owner safe.
C. The relationship between a guide dog and its owner is built on trust, communication, and mutual respect, and it often lasts for many years.
D. Public awareness and respect for guide dogs help ensure that people with visual impairments can move through society with dignity and without unnecessary barriers.
E. Support for guide dog programs is an investment in independence and equality, helping people with visual impairments participate fully in work, study, and community life.
F. For example, Labrador Retrievers and Golden Retrievers are commonly used as guide dogs because of their calm nature and ability to learn complex tasks.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍导盲犬帮助视障人士安全出行、提供陪伴与自由，空后需概括导盲犬的身份与训练性质。A的"not just pets; they are working partners"与"loyal companions"及"do more than lead the way"呼应，"undergo years of training to perform a vital role"与空后训练流程自然衔接。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前描述训练流程——寄养家庭、专业中心、障碍回避、路缘停车、通过测试才匹配，空后需说明训练严格的原因。B的"The training is demanding because a guide dog must be reliable in all situations"与严格筛选呼应，"staying calm in crowds, traffic, and unexpected events"与"busy streets and public transport"衔接，逻辑完整。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说狗与主人形成紧密纽带、相互学习、作为团队工作、主人获得勇气与更充实生活，空后需总结二者关系的本质。C的"The relationship... built on trust, communication, and mutual respect"与"owner learns... dog learns... work as a team"对应，"often lasts for many years"与"close bond"呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说导盲犬可进入大多数公共场所、法律保护、但仍有人不知不应抚摸或打扰工作犬，空后需强调公众意识的作用。D的"Public awareness and respect for guide dogs"与"some people may not understand"形成对照，"help ensure... move through society with dignity and without unnecessary barriers"与法律保护及无障碍出行衔接。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说训练机构依赖捐赠与志愿者、成本高、很多免费提供导盲犬，空后需总结支持这些项目的意义。E的"Support for guide dog programs is an investment in independence and equality"与"free of charge"及"changed the lives"呼应，"participate fully in work, study, and community life"是全文收尾的合理升华。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, Labrador Retrievers and Golden Retrievers are commonly used as guide dogs because of their calm nature and ability to learn complex tasks.**：以For example开头，为具体犬种举例。各空均为概括或过渡句；[1]或[2]易被误选，但两空分别需导盲犬身份概括与训练严格原因，不需要犬种案例。
