# 03-26 第二篇

**The Changing Face of Libraries**

When you think of a library, you might imagine a quiet room full of wooden shelves and dusty books. That was true many years ago. [1] They still have plenty of books, but today's libraries offer much more than that.

For example, almost every library now provides free computers and Wi-Fi. [2] People who do not have internet at home can come to the library to study, apply for jobs, or stay in touch with family members who live far away. For many, the library is the only place where they can access the online world for free.

Some libraries have even created special rooms called "makerspaces" (创客空间). In these areas, you can find 3D printers, sewing machines, and other tools that were once only available in factories or workshops. [3] You might build a robot, design a piece of jewelry, or create a model for a school project—all with the library's equipment and help from trained staff. These spaces are especially popular with teenagers who love to experiment and create.

Libraries also host events that bring the community together. Many hold story time for young children, book clubs for adults, and English classes for new arrivals. Some even offer film nights, craft workshops, and local history talks. [4] This is one reason why libraries are often called the "living room" of a neighborhood.

[5] Whether you want to borrow a book, learn a new skill, or simply find a quiet place to think, your local library is there to welcome you. It has never been a better time to discover—or rediscover—what your library can do for you.

## 选项

A. As a result, most experts believe that printed books will disappear from libraries completely within the next decade.
B. In fact, they have become busy community centers that serve people in many different ways.
C. Students and job seekers often use these resources to complete homework or search for job opportunities.
D. These creative spaces allow people to turn their ideas into real objects.
E. Such activities help bring people together and make the library a lively part of the neighborhood.
F. The good news is that you do not need a lot of money or special skills to enjoy what libraries offer.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] B   [2] C   [3] D   [4] E   [5] F

## 深度解析

- **[1] B**：
  - **线索**：代词指代与今昔对比。空前描述"多年前图书馆是放满木架和旧书的安静房间"。B选项"In fact, they have become busy community centers that serve people in many different ways"（事实上，它们已成为服务多样需求的繁忙社区中心）中"They"指代前文的"libraries"。空后"They still have plenty of books, but today's libraries offer much more"与B选项形成衔接——B说明图书馆"已成为社区中心"，后文则具体展开"仍有大量书籍，但提供更多服务"。

- **[2] C**：
  - **线索**：代词指代与逻辑延伸。空前介绍图书馆提供免费电脑和 Wi-Fi。C选项"Students and job seekers often use these resources to complete homework or search for job opportunities"（学生和求职者常利用这些资源完成作业或寻找工作机会）中"these resources"明确指代前文的"free computers and Wi-Fi"。空后"People who do not have internet at home can come to the library to study, apply for jobs..."与C选项的"complete homework""search for job opportunities"形成同义复现与逻辑延伸。

- **[3] D**：
  - **线索**：代词指代与概念解释。空前详细描述makerspace内有3D打印机、缝纫机等设备。D选项"These creative spaces allow people to turn their ideas into real objects"（这些创意空间让人们能把想法变成实物）中"These creative spaces"指代前文的"makerspaces"。空后列举"build a robot, design jewelry, create a model"正是"turn ideas into real objects"的具体例证，形成总—分结构。

- **[4] E**：
  - **线索**：代词指代与主题升华。空前列举图书馆举办的活动：儿童故事会、成人读书会、英语课。E选项"Such activities help bring people together and make the library a lively part of the neighborhood"（这类活动有助于凝聚邻里，使图书馆成为社区活力的一部分）中"Such activities"指代前文列举的各类活动。空后将图书馆称为社区的"living room"与E选项的"lively part of the neighborhood"形成同义呼应。

- **[5] F**：
  - **线索**：总结过渡与段首引导。空[5]位于结尾段段首。前文已从书籍、数字资源、创客空间、社区活动多维度展现现代图书馆。F选项"The good news is that you do not need a lot of money or special skills to enjoy what libraries offer"（好消息是，享受图书馆提供的服务不需要太多金钱或特殊技能）用"The good news"引出全文收束。空后"Whether you want to borrow a book, learn a new skill, or simply find a quiet place"对应F选项——无论何种需求，都可免费、无门槛地在图书馆实现。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **A. As a result, most experts believe that printed books will disappear from libraries completely within the next decade.**：该选项含"As a result""experts""libraries""printed books"等话题词，且使用因果衔接。但其主张"印刷书籍将在十年内从图书馆完全消失"与第一段"They still have plenty of books"（图书馆仍有大量书籍）以及全文"libraries offer much more"（提供更多而非取代书籍）的立场相悖。全文强调图书馆在保留书籍基础上的功能拓展，而非书籍的消亡。属于"话题词复现+与原文事实矛盾"的干扰项。
