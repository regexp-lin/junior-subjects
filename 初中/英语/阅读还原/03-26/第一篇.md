# 03-26 第一篇

**Why Handwriting Still Matters in the Digital Age**

Today, most students complete their homework on computers or tablets. Typing has become the main way we communicate in school and at work. Yet research shows that the simple act of picking up a pen and writing on paper still offers benefits that typing cannot replace. [1]

First, handwriting helps improve your memory. When you take notes by hand, you cannot write as quickly as you type. [2] This forces you to think about what is important and put it in your own words, which helps the information stay in your brain longer. Students who take notes by hand often perform better on exams than those who only type.

What is more, writing by hand is good for your brain. [3] Forming each letter with your hand uses different parts of the brain than simply pressing keys. Some scientists believe this extra brain activity can even help young children learn to read and spell better.

You might wonder: should I stop using my computer? Not at all. [4] The key is to use both—for example, write your first draft by hand when preparing an essay, then type it on the computer to edit and share. Typing is still the best way to produce a neat final copy and send your work to others quickly.

[5] Whether you prefer a pen or a keyboard, remember that both tools can help you learn. The goal is to choose the right tool for the right task.

## 选项

A. Therefore, schools should require all students to give up their computers and use only paper and pens.
B. In fact, many successful people still keep a notebook nearby for quick ideas and plans.
C. You have to decide which ideas are worth writing down and which can be left out.
D. It connects your hand movements with the letters and words you are learning in a special way.
E. The good news is that you do not have to choose one over the other.
F. In the end, both the pen and the keyboard are simply tools—what matters is how you use them.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] B   [2] C   [3] D   [4] E   [5] F

## 深度解析

- **[1] B**：
  - **线索**：补充例证与代词指代。空前指出"研究表明拿起笔在纸上写字仍能提供打字无法替代的好处"。B选项"In fact, many successful people still keep a notebook nearby for quick ideas and plans"（事实上，许多成功人士仍会在手边放一个笔记本记录灵感和计划）用"In fact"引出具体例证，补充说明在数字时代手写依然被重视。句中未出现明确代词，但"many successful people"与上文"research shows"形成"研究结论→现实例证"的论证链。

- **[2] C**：
  - **线索**：因果逻辑与衔接词呼应。空前说明"手写笔记时无法写得像打字一样快"。C选项"You have to decide which ideas are worth writing down and which can be left out"（你不得不决定哪些想法值得写下来，哪些可以省略）解释了"写得慢"带来的必然结果——必须做出选择。空后"This forces you to think about what is important"中的"This"指代C选项描述的"做取舍"这一过程，逻辑紧密衔接。

- **[3] D**：
  - **线索**：代词指代与同义复现。空前总述"手写对大脑有益"。D选项"It connects your hand movements with the letters and words you are learning in a special way"（它以特殊的方式将你的手部动作与你正在学习的字母和单词联系起来）中"It"指代前文"writing by hand"。空后"Forming each letter with your hand uses different parts of the brain"是对D选项"connects...in a special way"的具体展开——说明手部动作与学习内容如何连接。

- **[4] E**：
  - **线索**：转折衔接与后文呼应。空前用"Not at all"否定了"是否应该停止用电脑"的担忧。E选项"The good news is that you do not have to choose one over the other"（好消息是你不必在两者之间二选一）用"The good news"承接否定后的积极回应。空后"The key is to use both"与E选项的"do not have to choose one"形成同义替换，明确传达"两者兼用"的主旨。

- **[5] F**：
  - **线索**：总结升华与段首过渡。空[5]位于结尾段段首。前文已论述手写与打字各有优势，并建议两者结合使用。F选项"In the end, both the pen and the keyboard are simply tools—what matters is how you use them"（归根结底，笔和键盘都只是工具——重要的是你如何运用它们）用"In the end"引出全文总结。空后"Whether you prefer...both tools can help you learn"与F选项的"both...are simply tools"形成呼应，强调工具中性、重在运用的观点。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **A. Therefore, schools should require all students to give up their computers and use only paper and pens.**：该选项含"Therefore""schools""students""computers""paper""pens"等话题高频词，且使用因果衔接词。但其主张"学校应要求学生放弃电脑、仅用纸笔"与第四段"The key is to use both"（关键是两者都用）以及第五段"both tools can help you learn"（两种工具都有助学习）的全文立场完全对立。属于"话题词复现+立场完全矛盾"的典型干扰项。
