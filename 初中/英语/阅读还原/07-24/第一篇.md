**Ocean Plastic Pollution: A Growing Crisis**

The world's oceans are facing a serious threat: plastic pollution. Every year, millions of tons of plastic waste enter the sea. This plastic harms marine life, pollutes beaches, and even enters our food chain. The problem has grown so large that scientists have found plastic in the deepest parts of the ocean. [1] Addressing this crisis requires both global cooperation and individual action. Let us look at the causes, effects, and possible solutions.

Where does all this plastic come from? [2] Most ocean plastic comes from land-based sources. Rain and rivers carry plastic bags, bottles, and packaging from streets and landfills into the sea. Fishing nets and gear lost at sea also add to the problem. In some regions, waste is dumped directly into the ocean. Once plastic enters the water, it is carried by currents to every corner of the world. No ocean is left untouched.

The impact on marine life is devastating. [3] Sea animals often mistake plastic for food and eat it, which blocks their stomachs and causes starvation. Dolphins and whales get tangled in fishing nets and plastic ropes. Tiny pieces of plastic, called microplastics, are eaten by small fish and move up the food chain to larger animals—and eventually to humans. Coral reefs are damaged when plastic covers them and blocks sunlight. The suffering of marine creatures is a direct result of our waste.

What can we do to help? [4] We can start by reducing our use of single-use plastics such as straws, bags, and bottles. Choosing reusable products and recycling properly makes a big difference. Supporting laws that limit plastic production and improve waste management is another important step. Scientists are also developing new materials that break down naturally. Every small action adds up to create real change.

Ocean plastic pollution is one of the greatest environmental challenges of our time. [5] By changing our habits and supporting better policies, we can protect the oceans for future generations. The sea gives us so much—food, oxygen, and beauty. It is time for us to give back. Together, we can turn the tide on plastic pollution and ensure our oceans remain healthy and thriving.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述塑料污染严重性（波及深海），空后总领"causes, effects, solutions"三部分，A项"解决它需要政府与普通人共同努力"承上启下，将问题严重性与解决路径衔接，与后文"global cooperation and individual action"对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：本段回答"Where does all this plastic come from?"，空后列举陆地来源：雨水、河流携带、渔网遗失、直接倾倒等，B项"大部分来自陆地上的人类活动"准确概括来源。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：本段主题为对海洋生物的影响，空后具体描述误食阻塞、缠绕、微塑料进入食物链、珊瑚受损等，C项"动物以多种方式被塑料伤害、中毒或致死"全面概括段意。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：本段为解决方案，空后列举减塑、重复使用、回收、支持立法、新材料研发等，D项"个人选择与政策调整能带来切实解决方案"概括措施维度，与段内论据一致。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：结尾段总结并号召，空前总述为时代重大环境挑战，空后强调"changing habits""supporting policies""give back""turn the tide"，E项"我们必须承担责任、共同行动以守护更清洁的海洋"呼应号召。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, some beaches organize cleanup events every weekend.**：以 For example 开头，虽与海洋环保相关，但仅为例证，且与 [4] 空后"individual choices""policy"的层次不同，无法作为段首概括句，易误选，为干扰项。
