**The Mystery of Human Memory**

Memory is how we store and recall past experiences, facts, and skills. Without it, we would not know who we are or learn from our mistakes. Scientists have studied memory for over a century, yet many questions remain unanswered. Our brains do not work like a video recorder; memories are reconstructed each time we recall them. [1]

Memory is not stored in a single place. Different types of memories involve different brain regions. Short-term memory, like remembering a phone number briefly, relies on the prefrontal cortex. Long-term memories are linked to the hippocampus and other areas. When we form new memories, connections between brain cells grow stronger. [2]

Forgetting is a normal part of memory. We forget things we do not use often, and some memories fade over time. Sleep plays a key role—during sleep, the brain sorts and strengthens important memories. Stress and strong emotions can affect what we remember and what we forget. Understanding why we forget helps us learn better. [3]

We can improve our memory with practice. Repeating information, connecting it to things we already know, and using images or stories can help. Getting enough sleep and staying physically active support a healthy brain. Avoid cramming before exams; spreading study over time works better. [4]

Memory shapes our identity and our future. It lets us build relationships, develop skills, and plan ahead. Even when our memories are imperfect, they help us make sense of the world. The more we learn about how memory works, the better we can use it to our advantage. [5]

A. In other words, memory is both powerful and imperfect, and scientists are still uncovering its secrets.
B. This organization helps explain why some memories last while others slip away.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍记忆的定义与作用、科学家研究百年仍有未解之谜、记忆非录像式存储而是每次 recalled 时重建，空后需总结记忆的复杂性与科研进展。A的"both powerful and imperfect"与前文"do not work like a video recorder"呼应，"scientists are still uncovering its secrets"与前文对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说记忆分布在不同脑区、短期记忆依赖前额叶、长期记忆与海马体相关、新记忆形成时脑细胞连接加强，空后需总结这种结构如何解释记忆留存。B的"This organization"指代前文脑区分工，"why some memories last while others slip away"与前文对应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说遗忘是正常现象、不用则忘、睡眠帮助整理和强化记忆、压力与情绪影响记忆，空后需总结记忆受多种因素影响。C的"These factors"指代前文，"memory is not fixed but influenced by many conditions"与前文呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前提出记忆改善方法——重复、联想、图像或故事、充足睡眠与运动、分散学习优于突击，空后需总结策略的可行性。D的"With the right strategies"与前文对应，"anyone can train their brain to remember more effectively"与前文呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说记忆塑造身份与未来、建立关系发展技能规划、即使不完美也有意义、越了解越能善用，空后需总结记忆的价值。E的"That is why"承接前文，"most fascinating and useful aspects of the human mind"与前文对应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the story method links new words to a vivid scene...**：以For example开头，为具体记忆技巧案例（故事法）。各空均为概括或过渡句；[4]易被误选因涉及记忆策略，但该空需总结多种策略的整体效果，而非补充某一技巧的细节。
