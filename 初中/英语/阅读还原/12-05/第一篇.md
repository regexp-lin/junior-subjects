**Fast Fashion and Its Environmental Impact**

Fast fashion refers to cheap clothing that is produced quickly to follow the latest trends. Brands release new collections every few weeks, encouraging people to buy more. This system has made fashion affordable and fun for many, but it comes at a high cost to the environment. Huge amounts of water, energy, and chemicals are used to make each piece of clothing. [1]

The production process creates serious pollution. Dyeing and treating fabrics releases harmful chemicals into rivers and groundwater. Many factories burn coal or use electricity from non-renewable sources. Cotton farming often requires large amounts of water and pesticides. These practices damage ecosystems and affect the health of people living nearby. [2]

Clothing waste is another major problem. People tend to throw away clothes they no longer want instead of donating or recycling them. In some countries, tons of unwanted clothing end up in landfills each year. Synthetic fabrics like polyester can take hundreds of years to break down. Even when donated, excess clothing may be shipped overseas and cause problems there. [3]

There are ways to reduce the impact. Buying fewer clothes and choosing quality over quantity helps. Second-hand shops and clothing swaps give clothes a second life. Washing clothes less often and in cold water saves energy. When buying new items, look for brands that use recycled or sustainable materials. [4]

Change starts with small steps. You do not have to give up fashion to care for the planet. Every choice matters—whether it is repairing a favorite jacket or choosing a thrift store over a new store. Together, consumers and companies can move toward a more sustainable fashion industry. [5]

## 选项

A. In short, fast fashion has made style accessible while creating serious environmental challenges that we cannot ignore.
B. As a result, the fashion industry leaves a heavy footprint on our planet from production to disposal.
C. For these reasons, we need to rethink how we produce, use, and discard our clothes.
D. Fortunately, simple changes in how we shop and care for clothes can make a real difference.
E. Small actions, when added together, can lead to big changes for both people and the environment.
F. For example, cotton T-shirts require about 2,700 litres of water each to produce from farm to factory.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍快速时尚的定义、低价快速追随潮流、使时尚可负担却带来环境代价、每件衣服消耗大量水能源和化学物质，空后需总结快速时尚的双面性。A的"made style accessible while creating serious environmental challenges"与前文对应，"we cannot ignore"与前文"high cost to the environment"呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说生产环节造成污染——印染排放化学物、工厂用煤或非可再生能源、棉花种植耗水耗农药、损害生态与周边居民健康，空后需总结生产链的环保足迹。B的"As a result"承接前文，"heavy footprint on our planet from production to disposal"与前文污染内容对应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说服装浪费严重——人们丢弃而非捐赠回收、大量衣物进填埋场、化纤难降解、捐赠过剩衣物也带来问题，空后需总结对生产、使用、丢弃环节的反思。C的"For these reasons"承接前文，"rethink how we produce, use, and discard our clothes"与前文对应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前提出减损方法——少买重质、二手店与交换、少洗冷洗、选择可持续品牌，空后需总结这些改变的积极效果。D的"Fortunately"与前文转折呼应，"simple changes"对应具体建议，"make a real difference"与前文呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说改变从小事开始、不必放弃时尚、每个选择都重要、消费者与公司可共建可持续产业，空后需总结个人行动的累积效果。E的"Small actions, when added together, can lead to big changes"与前文呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, cotton T-shirts require about 2,700 litres of water...**：以For example开头，为具体数据案例（棉T恤耗水量）。各空均为概括或过渡句；[2]易被误选因涉及生产耗水，但该空需总结生产链的整体环保足迹，而非单一产品的数据举例。
