**Urban Gardening: Bringing Nature to the City**

In busy cities, concrete and buildings often replace trees and grass. Yet more and more people are finding ways to grow plants in urban spaces. They plant vegetables on rooftops, herbs on balconies, and flowers in community gardens. Urban gardening turns unused land—empty lots, schoolyards, or even walls—into green, productive spaces. It brings nature closer to people who live far from the countryside. [1] Urban gardening is not only a way to grow food and flowers; it also helps city dwellers connect with nature, build community, and improve the quality of urban life.

Growing food in the city has many benefits. Fresh vegetables and herbs can be harvested right outside your door, reducing the need to buy produce that has traveled long distances. Gardening is a form of exercise that gets people outdoors and moving. It can also reduce stress and improve mood. Studies show that spending time around plants can lower blood pressure and increase feelings of well-being. [2] In these ways, urban gardening supports both physical health and mental wellness for people who take part in it.

Community gardens are special places where neighbors grow plants together. People share tools, seeds, and knowledge. Children learn where food comes from by watching plants grow. Elderly people often enjoy the social connections and sense of purpose that gardening provides. Community gardens can turn neglected areas into beautiful, safe spaces that bring people together. [3] Through shared gardening, urban residents create stronger bonds and a greater sense of belonging in their neighborhoods.

Urban gardening does face some challenges. Space is limited, so gardeners often use containers, vertical planters, or small raised beds. Polluted soil or air in some cities may require special care, such as growing in clean soil or choosing plants that tolerate urban conditions. Not everyone has access to sunlight or water. [4] Despite these limits, creative solutions and support from local governments or organizations can help more city residents enjoy the joys and benefits of gardening.

Cities around the world are encouraging urban gardening. Some offer free seeds or plots of land. Schools include gardening in their lessons. Businesses install green walls or rooftop gardens. As cities grow, finding space for nature becomes more important. [5] Urban gardening shows that even in the busiest places, we can make room for plants—and in doing so, make our cities greener, healthier, and more livable.

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍城市园艺现象——屋顶种菜、阳台种香草、社区花园、改造闲置地，空后需总结城市园艺的多重意义。A的"not only a way to grow food and flowers"与前文种植内容对应，"connect with nature, build community, and improve the quality of urban life"概括后文将展开的几大益处。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前列举城市种植食物的好处——新鲜蔬菜、锻炼、减压、改善情绪、降血压、提升幸福感，空后需总结对身心健康的支持。B的"In these ways"承接前文列举，"supports both physical health and mental wellness"概括本段健康与心理益处。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前描述社区花园——共享工具种子知识、儿童学习、老年人社交与意义感、美化与凝聚社区，空后需总结社区园艺的社会价值。C的"Through shared gardening"与前文集体种植对应，"create stronger bonds and a greater sense of belonging"概括社区联结与归属感。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前指出城市园艺的局限——空间有限、土壤空气污染、光照水源不足，空后需提出克服途径。D的"Despite these limits"承接前文挑战，"creative solutions and support from local governments or organizations"与前文问题形成呼应，给出可行方向。
