# 09-20 第一篇

**Standing Up Against School Bullying**

School should be a safe place where every student can learn and grow. Sadly, bullying affects many young people, harming their mental health and their ability to focus on studies. [1] Understanding and addressing bullying is essential for creating a school environment where all students feel safe and respected.

Bullying can take many forms. It might be physical, such as hitting or pushing. It can be verbal, like insults or threats. It can also happen online, through messages or social media. [2] No matter what form it takes, bullying causes real pain and can leave lasting effects on its victims.

Bullies often act out because they feel insecure or powerless in other parts of their lives. This does not excuse their behavior, but it helps us understand it. [3] Addressing the root causes of bullying, including the bullies' own needs, can lead to more effective and lasting solutions.

Bystanders play an important role. When others stand by and do nothing, bullies may feel encouraged. When students speak up or report bullying, the behavior often stops. [4] Encouraging students to be upstanders rather than bystanders can change the culture of a school and protect more students from harm.

Schools and families must work together. Clear rules, open communication, and support for both victims and those who need to change their behavior are all important. [5] A united effort from adults and students alike is the best way to prevent bullying and help everyone thrive at school.

## 选项

A. Tackling bullying seriously is the first step toward building a school where every student can thrive without fear.
B. Bullying in any form hurts those who experience it and can affect them for years to come.
C. Dealing with the reasons why some students bully others can help prevent bullying from happening again.
D. When more students choose to support victims instead of staying silent, bullying becomes harder to continue.
E. Preventing and reducing bullying requires cooperation between schools, families, and the whole school community.
F. For example, some schools have introduced anonymous reporting systems so students can report bullying without fear of retaliation.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述学校本应是安全场所，但欺凌影响学生心理健康与学习，空后强调理解与应对欺凌的重要性。A的"Tackling bullying seriously"与"Understanding and addressing bullying"呼应，"building a school where every student can thrive without fear"与"all students feel safe and respected"一致。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前列举欺凌的多种形式（身体、语言、网络），空后强调无论何种形式都会造成真实伤害和持久影响。B的"Bullying in any form"与"No matter what form it takes"呼应，"can affect them for years to come"与"lasting effects"一致。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前分析欺凌者可能因不安全感或无力感而施暴，空后强调从根源入手能带来更持久的解决。C的"Dealing with the reasons why some students bully"与"Addressing the root causes"、"the bullies' own needs"呼应，"prevent bullying from happening again"与"more effective and lasting solutions"一致。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说旁观者沉默会助长欺凌，发声或报告往往能制止，空后鼓励学生做"upstanders"而非 bystanders。D的"When more students choose to support victims instead of staying silent"与"speak up or report"呼应，"bullying becomes harder to continue"与"the behavior often stops"一致。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前列举学校与家庭需合作（规则、沟通、对受害者与施暴者的支持），空后总结各方联合是预防欺凌的最佳途径。E的"cooperation between schools, families, and the whole school community"与"A united effort from adults and students"呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, some schools have introduced anonymous reporting systems so students can report bullying without fear of retaliation.**：以For example开头，是具体措施案例；易误放[4]或[5]，但[4]需概括旁观者角色转变，[5]需多方合作的概括，非单一制度举例。
