**Preparing for Earthquakes**

Earthquakes can strike without warning. They can cause buildings to fall, roads to crack, and lives to be lost. While we cannot predict exactly when or where an earthquake will happen, we can reduce its impact by being prepared. [1] Learning about earthquakes and how to stay safe is something everyone living in earthquake-prone areas should do.

Before an earthquake, families and schools should have a plan. Know where to meet if you are separated. Have supplies like water, food, a flashlight, and a first-aid kit ready. [2] A clear plan and stored supplies can make a huge difference when an earthquake hits and normal services are disrupted.

During an earthquake, the right actions can save lives. If you are indoors, drop under a strong table, cover your head, and hold on. Stay away from windows and heavy objects. [3] Knowing and practicing these steps helps people react quickly and correctly when the ground starts to shake.

After an earthquake, be careful. There may be aftershocks. Check for gas leaks, broken wires, and damage before moving around. [4] Staying calm and following safety guidelines reduces the risk of further injury when the immediate danger has passed.

Communities can also prepare. Schools and offices can hold drills. Governments can strengthen building codes and improve early warning systems. [5] When individuals, schools, and governments all take preparedness seriously, communities become more resilient and fewer people get hurt when disaster strikes.

## 选项

A. Understanding earthquake risks and safety measures is vital for anyone who lives where earthquakes may occur.
B. Having a preparedness plan and emergency supplies ready before disaster strikes can save lives when help is delayed.
C. Regular practice of earthquake safety procedures ensures that people know what to do when every second counts.
D. Careful behavior after an earthquake helps avoid additional harm during a time when many dangers still exist.
E. A coordinated effort to prepare at every level of society makes communities stronger and better able to survive earthquakes.
F. For example, Japan conducts nationwide earthquake drills every year to prepare its citizens for possible disasters.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述地震突发性及危害，空后强调学习地震知识和安全措施的重要性。A的"Understanding earthquake risks and safety measures"与"Learning about earthquakes and how to stay safe"呼应，"vital for anyone who lives where earthquakes may occur"与"everyone living in earthquake-prone areas"一致。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说震前需有计划（集合点、物资储备），空后强调计划和物资在地震来袭、服务中断时的作用。B的"Having a preparedness plan and emergency supplies ready"与"A clear plan and stored supplies"直接对应，"when help is delayed"与"normal services are disrupted"呼应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前描述震中正确做法（趴下、掩护、抓牢、远离窗户），空后强调知晓并练习这些步骤有助于快速正确反应。C的"Regular practice of earthquake safety procedures"与"Knowing and practicing these steps"呼应，"when every second counts"与"react quickly and correctly"一致。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说震后需警惕余震、检查燃气电线损坏，空后强调保持冷静、遵守安全指南以减少二次伤害。D的"Careful behavior after an earthquake"概括震后谨慎，"helps avoid additional harm"与"reduces the risk of further injury"呼应，"many dangers still exist"与"aftershocks"、"gas leaks"一致。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前列举社区、学校、政府的准备措施（演练、建筑规范、预警系统），空后总结各方重视准备可使社区更坚韧。E的"A coordinated effort to prepare at every level"与"individuals, schools, and governments all take preparedness seriously"呼应，"stronger and better able to survive"与"more resilient"一致。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, Japan conducts nationwide earthquake drills every year to prepare its citizens for possible disasters.**：以For example开头，是具体国家案例；易误放[3]或[5]，但[3]需概括演练与反应的关系，[5]需多方协作的概括，非单一国家制度举例。
