**Why Cities Need More Trees and Parks**

More than half of the world's people now live in cities, and that number keeps growing. Urban life offers many benefits, but it also brings stress, noise, and concrete everywhere you look. [1] Studies show that people who live near parks or tree-lined streets tend to have lower stress levels and better overall health. Greenery does not just look nice—it actually helps our bodies and minds recover from the demands of daily life.

Trees and plants do more than calm us down. [2] They absorb carbon dioxide and other pollutants, release oxygen, and help cool the air through a process called evapotranspiration. On hot summer days, a shaded street can be several degrees cooler than one without trees. This means less need for air conditioning, lower energy costs, and a smaller carbon footprint for the whole city.

Not everyone has equal access to green spaces. [3] In many cities, wealthy neighborhoods often have more parks and gardens, while poorer areas may have few trees and little grass. This inequality can worsen health gaps between communities, since people without nearby nature miss out on its benefits. Some cities are now working to plant more trees in underserved areas and turn empty lots into small parks.

Creating and caring for urban green spaces takes effort and money. [4] Local governments, businesses, and community groups need to work together to fund projects, choose the right plants for each location, and keep parks safe and clean. When residents join in—by watering young trees, reporting problems, or volunteering in community gardens—the results are even better. Green cities are built by everyone, not just by officials.

[5] As cities grow, the choice to protect and expand green spaces becomes more important. Trees and parks are not a luxury; they are essential for healthy, livable cities. Whether you live in a skyscraper or a small flat, you deserve a place to breathe, play, and connect with nature. The future of our cities depends on the choices we make today.

## 深度解析

- **[1] B**：
  - **线索**：研究结论与主题引入。空前描述城市生活的压力与混凝土环境，空后"Studies show that people who live near parks or tree-lined streets tend to have lower stress levels and better overall health"。B选项"Research has found that access to nature in cities improves both physical and mental well-being"（研究发现，城市中接触自然能改善身心福祉）中"access to nature"与空后"live near parks or tree-lined streets"对应，"physical and mental well-being"与"lower stress...better overall health"形成同义呼应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] F**：
  - **线索**：功能列举与机制衔接。空前总述"Trees and plants do more than calm us down"，空后具体描述"They absorb carbon dioxide...release oxygen...help cool the air through evapotranspiration...shaded street can be several degrees cooler"。F选项"Big trees can reduce summer temperatures by providing shade and releasing moisture into the air"（大树通过遮阴和向空气中释放水分来降低夏季气温）与空后"cool the air""evapotranspiration""several degrees cooler"形成精准对应，"releasing moisture"即蒸发蒸腾作用的通俗表述，逻辑连贯。

- **[3] D**：
  - **线索**：问题转折与例证引入。空前总述"Not everyone has equal access to green spaces"，空后展开"wealthy neighborhoods...more parks...poorer areas may have few trees...inequality can worsen health gaps"。D选项"Unfortunately, green spaces are not distributed fairly across different neighborhoods"（遗憾的是，绿地在不同社区间的分布并不公平）中"not distributed fairly"与空后"wealthy...more...poorer...few"形成因果呼应，"Unfortunately"带出问题语气，自然引出不平等现状的具体描述。

- **[4] E**：
  - **线索**：解决方案与多方协作。空前指出"Creating and caring for urban green spaces takes effort and money"，空后列举"Local governments, businesses, and community groups need to work together...residents join in...watering...volunteering"。E选项"Making it happen requires cooperation between different parts of society"（实现这一点需要社会各方的合作）中"Making it happen"指代前文创建与维护绿地，"cooperation between different parts of society"与空后"work together""residents join in"完美对应。

- **[5] A**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与价值升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后"As cities grow...choice to protect and expand...essential for healthy, livable cities...deserve a place to breathe, play, connect with nature"。A选项"In short, urban greenery is key to building cities where people can thrive"（简言之，城市绿化是建设人们得以繁荣发展的城市的关键）用"In short"总结，"key to...cities where people can thrive"与空后"essential for healthy, livable cities"及"breathe, play, connect"形成升华，呼应全文主题。
