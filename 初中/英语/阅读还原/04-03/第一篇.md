**The Science Behind Fidget Spinners**

Fidget spinners became popular a few years ago, and you might still see students spinning them in class. Some teachers ban them as toys, but research suggests they may have real benefits. [1] Studies have found that small, repetitive movements can help some people, especially those who have trouble sitting still, to pay better attention. The spinning motion seems to give the brain a harmless way to release extra energy.

Not everyone benefits from fidgeting in the same way. [2] For some students, a spinner or a stress ball can reduce anxiety during tests or boring lessons. Others find that fidgeting actually distracts them from focusing. The key is to understand what works for you and to use fidget tools only when they help rather than when they disturb others.

The idea of "fidgeting to focus" is not new. [3] People have long known that tapping a pen, doodling, or gently rocking can help the mind stay alert. Fidget spinners are simply a modern version of these age-old habits. The difference is that spinners can be noisy or visually distracting to classmates, which is why many schools have rules about when and where they can be used.

If you think fidgeting might help you, talk to your teacher first. [4] Some teachers allow quiet fidget tools like stress balls or squishy toys that do not make noise. Explaining why you need them can help your teacher understand and support you. It is important to choose tools that help you without bothering anyone else.

[5] Remember that the goal is to stay focused and calm, not to play. If a fidget spinner or any other tool helps you learn better, use it wisely. If it does not, try other methods like taking short breaks or moving around between classes.

## 深度解析

- **[1] D**：
  - **线索**：因果衔接与语义呼应。空前指出研究显示fidget spinner可能有真正益处，空后具体描述"Studies have found that small, repetitive movements can help...pay better attention"。D选项"This is because gentle physical activity can improve concentration for certain learners"（这是因为温和的身体活动能提高某些学习者的专注力）用"This is because"承接前文"real benefits"，"gentle physical activity"与空后"small, repetitive movements"对应，"improve concentration"与"pay better attention"同义，逻辑连贯。

- **[2] F**：
  - **线索**：条件—结果与语义对应。空前指出"Not everyone benefits...in the same way"，空后"For some students, a spinner or a stress ball can reduce anxiety..."。F选项"In those cases, having a small object to hold or spin can calm nervous feelings"（在这种情况下，拥有一个小物件来拿或旋转可以平复紧张情绪）中"In those cases"指代前文"受益于fidgeting"的情形，"calm nervous feelings"与空后"reduce anxiety"完美呼应，空后是F的具体展开。

- **[3] A**：
  - **线索**：历史追溯与递进衔接。空前提出"'fidgeting to focus' is not new"，空后描述"People have long known that tapping a pen, doodling, or gently rocking can help..."。A选项"Long before spinners existed, people used simple movements to stay awake and concentrate"（早在指尖陀螺出现之前，人们就用简单动作保持清醒和专注）与空后"People have long known"形成呼应，"simple movements"对应"tapping...doodling...rocking"，"stay awake and concentrate"与"help the mind stay alert"同义。

- **[4] E**：
  - **线索**：转折与情境铺垫。空前建议"talk to your teacher first"，空后"Some teachers allow quiet fidget tools..."。E选项"However, teachers and students often disagree on whether they should be allowed in class"（然而，师生常对是否应在课堂使用产生分歧）用"However"引出"需与老师沟通"的背景原因——正因为存在分歧，故需先沟通。空后"Some teachers allow"则是分歧下的积极一面，表明沟通有可能得到理解与支持，逻辑顺畅。

- **[5] B**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与行动提醒。空[5]位于结尾段段首，前文已讨论fidget spinner的益处、个体差异、历史渊源、与老师沟通。B选项"Whether you use a spinner or not, keep your real purpose in mind"（无论你是否使用指尖陀螺，都要牢记真正目的）概括全文主旨，空后"Remember that the goal is to stay focused and calm, not to play"正是"real purpose"的具体阐述，"real purpose"与"the goal"对应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **C. In short, fidget spinners can be useful, but they are not a magic solution for everyone.**：含总结语"In short"及"useful""not...everyone"等关键词，与全文主旨相关。但C更适合放在全文最后一句作总结，而空[5]后还有"Remember that the goal...If a fidget spinner...use it wisely..."等内容，若填C会与空后"Remember""If...use it wisely"重复且阻断逻辑。空[5]需的是引出段内建议的过渡句，B的"keep your real purpose in mind"更自然引出后文"the goal is..."。属"总结位置不当"型干扰。
