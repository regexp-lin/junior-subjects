# 04-03 第二篇

**The Hidden Language of Micro-Expressions**

Have you ever noticed someone's face flash with anger or fear for just a split second before they smile? That brief, uncontrolled expression is called a micro-expression. [1] These expressions usually last less than half a second, and they often reveal emotions that people are trying to hide. Psychologists and police officers study them to better understand what others are really feeling.

Micro-expressions happen because it is difficult to control every tiny muscle in the face. [2] When we feel a strong emotion, our facial muscles react automatically before we can decide to hide it. For a moment, the true feeling shows on our face. Learning to notice these flashes can help you become more aware of others' emotions and improve your relationships.

Scientists have identified seven basic emotions that appear in micro-expressions all over the world. [3] These include anger, fear, sadness, disgust, surprise, happiness, and contempt. No matter where you were born, people tend to show these feelings in similar ways. This suggests that some emotional expressions are universal and not learned from culture alone.

Some people are naturally better at reading micro-expressions than others. [4] With practice, however, most people can improve their ability to spot these brief signals. Paying attention to the eyes, mouth, and brow—especially when someone's words do not match their expression—can give you clues about their real feelings. The key is to watch for sudden changes that disappear quickly.

[5] Being aware of micro-expressions is useful, but it should not be used to judge people too quickly. A brief frown might mean sadness, or it could simply be a reaction to bright light. Understanding body language takes time and context. Use what you learn to connect with others more kindly, not to assume you always know what they feel.

## 深度解析

- **[1] D**：
  - **线索**：定义补充与同义复现。空前介绍micro-expression概念（"That brief, uncontrolled expression"），空后描述"These expressions usually last less than half a second, and they often reveal emotions that people are trying to hide"。D选项"They last only a fraction of a second and can expose feelings that someone does not want to show"（它们只持续几分之一秒，能暴露某人不想展示的感受）与空后形成紧密呼应："a fraction of a second"≈"less than half a second"，"expose feelings...do not want to show"≈"reveal emotions...trying to hide"。D填[1]作为定义延伸，空后进一步展开，逻辑顺畅。

- **[2] A**：
  - **线索**：因果解释与语义衔接。空前提出"it is difficult to control every tiny muscle in the face"，空后"When we feel a strong emotion, our facial muscles react automatically before we can decide to hide it"。A选项"They appear because emotions trigger automatic facial movements before we can control them"（它们出现是因为情绪在我们能控制之前就触发了自动的面部动作）用"because"解释原因，"automatic facial movements"与空后"react automatically"对应，"before we can control"与空后"before we can decide to hide"呼应。A填[2]完美承接前文"difficult to control"，并自然引出空后的具体机制描述。

- **[3] F**：
  - **线索**：例证展开与主题衔接。空前总述"seven basic emotions...all over the world"，空后列举"anger, fear, sadness, disgust, surprise, happiness, and contempt"及"people tend to show these feelings in similar ways"。F选项"Researchers have found that the same seven emotions show up in similar facial patterns across different cultures"（研究发现相同的七种情绪在不同文化中以相似的面部模式呈现）与空后形成递进：F提出研究发现，空后给出七种情绪的具体名称及"similar ways""universal"的结论，逻辑连贯。

- **[4] E**：
  - **线索**：转折递进与语义呼应。空前指出"Some people are naturally better...than others"，空后"With practice, however, most people can improve their ability to spot these brief signals"。E选项"The good news is that this skill can be learned and improved"（好消息是这项技能是可以学习和提升的）中"this skill"指代读微表情的能力，"can be learned and improved"与空后"With practice...can improve"完美对应。空[4]需在"有些人天生更好"后给出积极转折，E正是此作用。

- **[5] B**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与辩证提醒。空[5]位于结尾段段首，前文已介绍微表情定义、成因、七种基本情绪、可学习性。B选项"In conclusion, micro-expressions are a powerful tool for understanding emotions, but they must be used with care"（总之，微表情是理解情绪的有力工具，但须谨慎使用）用"In conclusion"总结，空后"Being aware...is useful, but it should not be used to judge people too quickly"正是"must be used with care"的具体展开，且"useful...but"与B中"powerful...but"形成呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **C. As a result, training courses have been developed to help people recognize these fleeting expressions.**：含"recognize""expressions"等关键词，与空[4]前后"improve their ability to spot"有语义关联。但C讨论的是"training courses"（培训课程）的开发，而文中并未提及任何课程或培训项目，空[4]后强调的是"With practice"（通过练习）个人可提升，而非参加课程。C属"引入文中未涉概念"型干扰。
  - **A与[2]的区分**：A填[2]已用。若将A填[1]，则与空后"When we feel..."（[2]后）的衔接不如D自然——[1]空后直接描述时长和功能，D更贴合；[2]空后解释自动反应机制，A的"because...automatic...before we can control"与之完美匹配。
