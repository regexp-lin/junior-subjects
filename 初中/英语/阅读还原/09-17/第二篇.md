# 09-17 第二篇

**Why Forests Matter to Our Planet**

Forests cover about one-third of the Earth's land surface. They are home to millions of species, clean the air we breathe, and help control the climate. [1] Protecting and restoring forests is one of the most important things we can do for the health of our planet and future generations.

Trees absorb carbon dioxide from the air and release oxygen. This process helps reduce the amount of greenhouse gases that warm our planet. When forests are cut down, this natural balance is disturbed. [2] Forests act as the Earth's lungs, playing a vital role in regulating the atmosphere and fighting climate change.

Forests also provide homes for countless animals and plants. Many species cannot survive anywhere else. When we destroy forests, we lose not only trees but entire ecosystems. [3] Biodiversity in forests is essential for a healthy planet, and losing it affects everything from insects to large mammals.

People depend on forests too. Millions of people get food, medicine, and materials from forests. Forests also protect against floods and landslides by holding soil and water. [4] The benefits that forests provide to human communities are too valuable to lose through careless destruction.

What can we do? We can support laws that protect forests, choose products that do not come from illegal logging, and plant trees when we can. [5] Every action we take to safeguard forests helps ensure that these precious ecosystems continue to support life on Earth for years to come.

## 选项

A. The role of forests in sustaining life on Earth cannot be overstated, and their loss would have serious consequences.
B. Their ability to store carbon and produce oxygen makes them crucial in the fight against global warming.
C. Protecting the variety of life in forests is key to maintaining the balance of nature worldwide.
D. Forests offer services that human societies rely on, and these services must be preserved.
E. Individual and collective efforts to protect forests can make a lasting difference for the future.
F. For example, the Amazon rainforest produces about 20 percent of the world's oxygen and is often called the "lungs of the Earth."

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前概述森林的覆盖、物种栖息、净化空气和调节气候，空后强调保护与恢复森林的重要性。A的"The role of forests in sustaining life"概括前文，"their loss would have serious consequences"自然引出保护必要性。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说树木吸收二氧化碳释放氧气、森林砍伐破坏平衡，空后比喻森林是地球之肺。B的"Their ability to store carbon and produce oxygen"直接承接，"crucial in the fight against global warming"与"regulating the atmosphere and fighting climate change"一致。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说森林为动植物提供家园、破坏导致生态系统丧失，空后强调森林生物多样性的重要性。C的"Protecting the variety of life in forests"与"Biodiversity"呼应，"maintaining the balance of nature"与"healthy planet"逻辑一致。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前列举人类对森林的依赖（食物、药物、材料、防洪等），空后强调这些惠益不可因破坏而失去。D的"Forests offer services that human societies rely on"概括依赖关系，"these services must be preserved"与"too valuable to lose"呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前列举可采取的行动（支持法律、选择合法产品、植树），空后强调每一次保护行动都有长远意义。E的"Individual and collective efforts"概括行动主体，"lasting difference for the future"与"continue to support life for years to come"呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the Amazon rainforest produces about 20 percent of the world's oxygen and is often called the "lungs of the Earth."**：以For example开头，是具体地区数据；易误放[2]，但[2]需概括森林碳氧功能的普遍作用，非单一地区案例。
