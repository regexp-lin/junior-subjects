**Food Culture Around the World**

What people eat and how they eat it varies greatly from one culture to another. In some places, meals are a quick affair; in others, they can last for hours. Some cultures eat with chopsticks, others with forks and knives, and still others with their hands. These differences reflect the history, climate, and values of each society. [1] For this reason, learning about another culture's food can teach us as much about that culture as reading a history book or visiting a museum.

The ingredients used in cooking often depend on what is available locally. People who live near the ocean may eat more fish and seafood, while those in mountain areas might rely on grains and dairy. Spices and seasonings also differ widely, adding unique flavors to each region's dishes. [2] That is why a dish called "curry" in India may taste nothing like one with the same name in Thailand, even if both use similar spices.

Meal times and manners also vary. In some cultures, it is polite to leave a little food on your plate to show that the host has been generous. In others, finishing everything on your plate is a sign of respect. In some countries, the oldest person at the table is served first; in others, guests are given priority. [3] When traveling abroad, it is wise to observe how locals eat and to follow their lead, as this demonstrates respect and can lead to warmer welcomes.

Today, with increased travel and migration, food cultures influence one another more than ever. A dish that was once local may now be enjoyed in cities around the world. At the same time, some people worry that globalization may cause traditional cuisines to disappear. [4] How to keep treasured recipes and cooking methods alive while still welcoming new influences is a question that chefs, families, and governments are all trying to answer.

Learning about food from other cultures broadens our horizons and can bring people together. Trying new dishes can be an adventure, and sharing a meal is often a way to build friendship. [5] Whether we eat with chopsticks, forks, or our hands, the act of sharing food reminds us that we all need to eat, and that we can find common ground by breaking bread together.

## 选项

A. Food culture is more than a matter of taste—it is a window into how different people understand hospitality, family, and community across the globe.
B. As a result, the same basic ingredients can be transformed into completely different meals depending on where and how they are prepared.
C. Understanding these customs helps travelers avoid misunderstandings and shows respect for the local way of life when visiting a new place.
D. Balancing the sharing of food traditions with the need to preserve local cultures is a challenge that many communities are now facing.
E. In the end, food is a universal language that can bridge differences and remind us of our common humanity, no matter where we come from.
F. For example, sushi from Japan and pizza from Italy have become popular in countries far from their places of origin.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述各地饮食方式差异及其反映的历史与价值观，空后说"For this reason, learning about another culture's food can teach us..."。A的"more than a matter of taste"与前文"differences"呼应，"window into... hospitality, family, and community"与空后学习文化衔接。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说食材与调料因地域而异，空后说"That is why a dish called 'curry' in India may taste nothing like one in Thailand"。B的"the same basic ingredients can be transformed into completely different meals"与空后同名不同味呼应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前列举各地用餐礼仪差异，空后说"When traveling abroad, it is wise to observe how locals eat..."。C的"Understanding these customs helps travelers avoid misunderstandings"与空后"observe... follow their lead"衔接。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说全球化使传统饮食可能消失，空后说"How to keep treasured recipes... alive while welcoming new influences"。D的"Balancing the sharing... with the need to preserve"与空后问题直接对应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说了解异国饮食、分享餐食促进友谊，空后说"Whether we eat with chopsticks, forks, or our hands... we can find common ground by breaking bread together"。E的"food is a universal language that can bridge differences"与空后呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, sushi from Japan and pizza from Italy have become popular in countries far from their places of origin.**：以For example开头，属具体菜肴举例，各空均为概括或过渡句；易误放[4]，但[4]需的是共享与保护平衡的概括，非个例说明。
