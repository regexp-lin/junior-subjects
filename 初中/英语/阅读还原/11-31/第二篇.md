**Eco-Friendly Shopping: Small Choices, Big Impact**

Every time we buy something, we make a choice that affects the environment. From the materials used to make products to the way they are packed and shipped, our purchases have a footprint. Eco-friendly shopping means thinking about these impacts and choosing options that are kinder to the planet. It does not require a complete lifestyle change—even small steps can add up to a real difference. [1]

One of the easiest changes is to reduce single-use items. Bringing your own bags to the store avoids plastic bags that often end up in oceans or landfills. Using a reusable water bottle instead of buying bottled water cuts down on plastic waste. Carrying a reusable straw or cup when you go out can help too. [2]

Another important step is to buy products with less packaging. Some items come wrapped in layers of plastic, cardboard, and foam that are thrown away immediately. Choosing products with minimal or recyclable packaging reduces waste. Shopping at stores that offer bulk bins lets you buy exactly what you need without extra packaging. [3]

Considering where and how products are made also matters. Items that are produced locally travel shorter distances, which means less pollution from shipping. Products made from recycled or sustainable materials support a circular economy. Some companies clearly label their eco-friendly practices, making it easier for shoppers to choose. [4]

Eco-friendly shopping is not about being perfect. It is about being more aware and making better choices when possible. Every reusable bag, every product with less packaging, and every conscious purchase contributes to a healthier planet. [5]

## 选项

A. Eco-friendly shopping starts with awareness—understanding that our choices matter and that we can make a positive impact without giving up everything we enjoy.
B. These simple habits require little effort but can significantly reduce the amount of waste we create each day.
C. By paying attention to packaging, shoppers can reduce their environmental footprint and encourage companies to do better.
D. Thinking about the full journey of a product—from production to delivery—helps us make smarter, greener choices.
E. Together, these small actions add up to a larger movement toward a more sustainable way of living.
F. For example, some coffee shops now offer discounts to customers who bring their own cups.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前说每次购物影响环境、涉及材料包装运输、环保购物即考虑这些并选择更友善选项、无需彻底改变、小步可积累，空后需总结环保购物的起点与可行性。A的"starts with awareness"与"thinking about these impacts"呼应，"without giving up everything we enjoy"与"does not require a complete lifestyle change"对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前列举减少一次性用品——自带购物袋、自带水壶、自带吸管或杯子，空后需总结这些习惯的效应。B的"These simple habits"指代前文，"require little effort but can significantly reduce waste"与前文呼应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说选择少包装产品、过度包装被丢弃、选简约或可回收包装、散装购物减少包装，空后需总结关注包装的影响。C的"By paying attention to packaging"与前文对应，"reduce their environmental footprint and encourage companies to do better"概括双重效果。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说考虑产地与生产方式——本地产品运输短、回收或可持续材料、企业环保标识，空后需总结全链条思维。D的"full journey of a product—from production to delivery"与前文产地、材料、运输对应，"smarter, greener choices"概括本段主旨。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说环保购物不必完美、重在更清醒与尽量更好选择、每件小事都在贡献，空后需总结累积效应。E的"Together, these small actions"与前文每项选择对应，"larger movement toward a more sustainable way of living"是合理收尾。

- **干扰项分析**：
