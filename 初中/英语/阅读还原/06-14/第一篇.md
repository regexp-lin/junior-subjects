# 06-14 第一篇

**The International Space Station**

Floating about 400 kilometres above Earth, the International Space Station (ISS) is one of humanity's greatest engineering achievements. It is a huge laboratory and home where astronauts from different countries live and work together for months at a time. [1] In other words, the ISS is a symbol of global cooperation and scientific discovery in space. Since 2000, it has been continuously occupied, making it the longest-running human outpost（前哨）in orbit.

The ISS was built through international partnership. [2] The United States, Russia, Europe, Japan, and Canada all contributed parts and expertise. Modules（舱段）were launched into space over many years and connected bit by bit—a feat that required exact planning and teamwork across borders. Today, the station is roughly the size of a football field and includes living quarters, laboratories, and solar panels that provide power. Running and maintaining the ISS costs billions each year, but the scientific returns are considered worth the investment.

Life on the ISS is unlike life on Earth. [3] In microgravity（微重力）, there is no "up" or "down"—astronauts float through the station, and objects drift if not secured. Daily tasks like eating, sleeping, and exercising require special equipment and routines. Astronauts must exercise for about two hours each day to keep their muscles and bones strong, as the lack of gravity weakens the body over time. They also conduct experiments in medicine, biology, and physics that cannot be done on Earth, helping scientists understand how space affects living things and materials.

The ISS teaches us about space and ourselves. [4] Research done on the station has improved our knowledge of how the human body changes in space, how plants grow without gravity, and how materials behave in extreme conditions. This research supports future missions to the Moon and Mars. The ISS also shows that when nations work together, they can achieve things that no single country could do alone. It stands as proof that cooperation in science can rise above political differences.

[5] The International Space Station remains a beacon of human ambition and collaboration. As newer space stations and moon bases are planned, the lessons learned from the ISS will guide the next chapter of space exploration. For now, whenever you look up at the night sky, remember that somewhere above, people are living and working in space—and that achievement belongs to all of humanity.

## 选项

A. In other words, the ISS represents international cooperation and scientific progress in orbit.
B. That is because it was assembled through contributions from multiple nations over many years.
C. In fact, daily life in microgravity requires special routines and supports unique research.
D. These outcomes show that the ISS has advanced both science and international teamwork.
E. In short, the ISS is a landmark of human achievement that paves the way for future exploration.
F. For example, some astronauts have spent more than a year on the station in a single mission.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：引出国际空间站作为国际合作与科学探索的象征。空前描述"ISS is one of humanity's greatest engineering achievements...huge laboratory and home where astronauts from different countries live and work together for months"，空后说明"the ISS is a symbol of global cooperation and scientific discovery in space...Since 2000 continuously occupied...longest-running human outpost in orbit"。A选项"In other words, the ISS represents international cooperation and scientific progress in orbit"（换言之，国际空间站代表轨道上的国际合作与科学进步）中"In other words"概括空前，"international cooperation and scientific progress"与空后"global cooperation and scientific discovery"精准呼应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：ISS 由多国多年参与建造而成。空前提出"The ISS was built through international partnership"，空后详述"United States, Russia, Europe, Japan, Canada contributed parts and expertise...Modules launched over many years, connected bit by bit...exact planning and teamwork across borders...size of football field...living quarters, laboratories, solar panels...costs billions, scientific returns worth investment"。B选项"That is because it was assembled through contributions from multiple nations over many years"（那是因为它由多国多年贡献组装而成）中"That is because"解释合作建造，"contributions from multiple nations over many years"与空后五国贡献、多年发射、逐步连接完美对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：微重力下的日常与独特研究。空前提出"Life on the ISS is unlike life on Earth"，空后列举"In microgravity no up or down...astronauts float, objects drift...eating, sleeping, exercising require special equipment and routines...exercise two hours daily for muscles and bones...conduct experiments in medicine, biology, physics...cannot be done on Earth...how space affects living things and materials"。C选项"In fact, daily life in microgravity requires special routines and supports unique research"（事实上，微重力下的日常生活需要特殊惯例并支持独特研究）中"In fact"强化差异与科研，"special routines and unique research"与空后 special equipment、exercise routine、experiments 精准对应，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：ISS 在科学与国际合作方面的成果。空前提出"The ISS teaches us about space and ourselves"，空后描述"Research improved knowledge: human body in space, plants without gravity, materials in extreme conditions...supports future Moon and Mars missions...nations work together achieve what no single country could...proof cooperation in science rises above political differences"。D选项"These outcomes show that the ISS has advanced both science and international teamwork"（这些成果表明国际空间站既推进了科学也推进了国际合作）中"These outcomes"指代空后科研发现与多国协作，"advanced both science and international teamwork"与空后"improved knowledge...work together...cooperation rises above differences"完美对应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与 ISS 对未来探索的引领作用。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"ISS remains beacon of human ambition and collaboration...newer stations and moon bases planned...lessons from ISS will guide next chapter of space exploration...people living and working in space...achievement belongs to all humanity"。E选项"In short, the ISS is a landmark of human achievement that paves the way for future exploration"（简言之，国际空间站是人类成就的地标，为未来探索铺平道路）用"In short"总结全文，"landmark...paves the way for future exploration"与空后"beacon...guide next chapter"形成呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, some astronauts have spent more than a year on the station in a single mission.**：涉及宇航员单次驻留超一年，易被误填[3]或[5]。但F以"For example"引出驻留时长案例，而空[3]讨论的是微重力下的日常生活与科研内容，F的"more than a year"为时间长度，与"special routines...unique research"的日常与科研焦点不同；空[5]总结ISS的历史地位与未来意义，F的"single mission"为个体经历，与"beacon of ambition...paves the way"的宏观意义不符。F属"主题相关但论述层次错位"型干扰。
