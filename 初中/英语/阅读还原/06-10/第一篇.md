**The Benefits of Keeping a Diary**

Putting your thoughts and experiences down on paper might seem old-fashioned in the age of social media. Yet writing a diary—whether in a notebook or on a device—remains one of the most effective ways to understand yourself and manage your emotions. [1] In other words, diary writing helps you reflect on your day, clear your mind, and track your growth over time. It is a private space where you can be completely honest, without worrying about what others think.

Writing helps process emotions. [2] When you feel stressed, anxious, or confused, putting your feelings into words can make them easier to manage. Research shows that people who write about their worries often feel calmer afterwards. A diary gives you a safe place to express anger, sadness, or joy without judgment. You can explore why you feel a certain way and slowly gain insight into your own patterns. Many people find that writing before bed helps them fall asleep, as it clears their mind of the day's worries.

Diaries improve memory and clarity. [3] Recording what happened during the day—both the big events and the small details—helps fix them in your memory. Later, you can look back and see how you spent your time, what you learned, and how you changed. Some students use diaries to set goals and track their progress. Writing "I will finish my essay by Friday" and then checking it off creates a sense of achievement. A diary becomes a record of your personal journey, not just a list of events.

You do not need to write a lot. [4] Even a few sentences each day can make a difference. There is no right or wrong way to keep a diary—some people write long paragraphs, others prefer bullet points or drawings. The key is consistency. Writing regularly, even for just five minutes, builds a habit that becomes easier over time. You might start with something simple: "Today I felt grateful for..." or "One thing I learned today was...". Small entries add up to a valuable record of your life.

[5] A diary is a simple but powerful tool for self-care and self-understanding. It costs nothing, requires no special equipment, and can be started at any age. Whether you are dealing with exam stress, friendship problems, or simply want to remember your teenage years more clearly, a diary can help. Pick up a pen or open a document, and give it a try. Your future self will thank you for the stories you leave behind.

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：引出日记对自我反思、情绪疏导与个人成长的作用。空前描述"putting thoughts on paper might seem old-fashioned...writing a diary remains one of the most effective ways to understand yourself and manage emotions"，空后说明"diary writing helps you reflect on your day, clear your mind, track your growth...private space...be completely honest"。A选项"In other words, diary writing supports self-reflection, emotional relief, and personal growth"（换言之，写日记有助于自我反思、情绪疏导和个人成长）中"In other words"概括空前价值，"self-reflection, emotional relief, personal growth"与空后"reflect, clear mind, track growth"精准呼应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：把感受写下来能减轻压力、带来平静的原因。空前提出"Writing helps process emotions"，空后详述"When stressed, anxious, confused...putting feelings into words makes them easier to manage...Research: people who write about worries feel calmer...safe place to express...explore why...gain insight...writing before bed helps fall asleep...clears mind of worries"。B选项"That is because putting feelings into words can reduce stress and bring a sense of calm"（那是因为把感受诉诸文字可以减轻压力并带来平静）中"That is because"解释写作对情绪的作用，"reduce stress and bring calm"与空后 easier to manage、feel calmer、clears mind 完美对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：记录经历有助于记忆与长期学习。空前提出"Diaries improve memory and clarity"，空后列举"Recording what happened—big events and small details—helps fix in memory...look back...see how you spent time, what you learned, how you changed...students set goals, track progress...record of personal journey"。C选项"In fact, writing down your experiences helps you remember and learn from them over time"（事实上，把你的经历写下来有助于你记忆并在日后从中学习）中"In fact"强化记忆与学习作用，"remember and learn from them over time"与空后 fix in memory、look back、track progress 精准对应，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：日记可灵活书写、不必追求完美。空前提出"You do not need to write a lot"，空后描述"Even few sentences make difference...no right or wrong way...long paragraphs, bullet points, drawings...key is consistency...five minutes...builds habit...start with 'Today I felt grateful for...'...Small entries add up to valuable record"。D选项"These tips show that diary-keeping can be flexible and does not require perfection"（这些建议表明记日记可以灵活进行，不必追求完美）中"These tips"指代空后少写、多种形式、坚持、简单开头等，"flexible...does not require perfection"与空后"no right or wrong...few sentences...simple"完美对应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与习惯价值总结。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"simple but powerful tool for self-care and self-understanding...costs nothing, no special equipment, can start at any age...exam stress, friendship problems, remember teenage years...Pick up pen...give it a try...future self thank you"。E选项"In short, a diary is a worthwhile habit that anyone can start"（简言之，日记是任何人都可以开始的有价值的习惯）用"In short"总结全文，"worthwhile habit that anyone can start"与空后"simple...costs nothing...any age...give it a try"形成呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F.
