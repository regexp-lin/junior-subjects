**Learning to Accept Criticism**

Nobody likes to hear that they have made a mistake or that their work is not good enough. Criticism can hurt, and our first reaction is often to defend ourselves or feel upset. [1] Yet learning to accept feedback with an open mind is one of the most valuable skills we can develop, both in school and in life.

When someone criticizes us, our brain may treat it as an attack. We might feel angry, embarrassed, or want to argue back. [2] If we can pause and ask ourselves "What can I learn from this?", we turn criticism into a chance to improve instead of a reason to feel bad.

Criticism often comes from people who care about us—teachers, parents, or friends. They want to help us grow. [3] By listening carefully and thanking them for their honesty, we show that we respect their effort and are willing to change.

Some criticism is fair and helpful; some is not. Not every piece of feedback is worth following. [4] The key is to stay calm, think about whether the criticism makes sense, and then decide what to do. This balanced approach helps us grow without losing confidence.

Accepting criticism does not mean we are weak or that our work has no value. It means we are brave enough to face our weaknesses and strong enough to improve. [5] When we learn to see criticism as a gift rather than an attack, we take an important step toward becoming better people.

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述批评令人难受、想辩护，空后说"Yet learning to accept feedback with an open mind is one of the most valuable skills"。A用"However"转折，提出改变心态可使批评成为成长工具，与段末"valuable skills"相呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说大脑把批评当攻击、可能愤怒或想争辩，空后提出"pause and ask ourselves"。B的"Taking a moment to breathe and reflect"与"pause"同义，说明冷静反思使我们能"respond instead of react"，衔接紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说批评常来自关心我们的人、想帮助我们成长，空后无。C的"Showing appreciation"对应"thank them"，"strengthens our relationships"与"people who care"呼应，作为该段行为建议的总结。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前指出有的批评有用、有的没用，空后说"The key is to stay calm, think... and then decide"。D的"filter feedback wisely, keeping what helps... letting go of what does not"准确概括该段的筛选逻辑。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说把批评视为礼物而非攻击，空后无。E的"That shift in perspective"指代前文观念转变，"separates those who grow from those who stay stuck"升华主题，适合作结尾句。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, a teacher might point out errors in your essay so you can improve your writing.**：举例具体，与文中某段可能相关，但各空均为概括或过渡句，不需要具体例子；F作为干扰项易被误放在[3]空，但[3]空需的是对"倾听并感谢"的总结，非举例。
