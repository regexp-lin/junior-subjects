# 08-27 第一篇

**The Invention of Printing**

Long before the modern age, books were copied by hand, which made them rare and expensive. Only wealthy people and monasteries could afford them. Then, around the year 1040 in China, Bi Sheng invented movable type printing using clay characters. [1] This breakthrough laid the foundation for a revolution that would eventually change how knowledge spread across the world.

In Europe, Johannes Gutenberg improved the printing technique in the 1440s. He created metal movable type and an oil-based ink that worked well on paper. [2] Within decades, millions of books were printed, making knowledge available to ordinary people for the first time in history.

The printing press had a huge impact on society. Ideas could travel faster than ever before. Scientists shared discoveries, writers spread stories, and religious texts became widely readable. [3] Education no longer belonged only to the rich; schools and universities grew as more people learned to read and write.

Today we take printed books for granted, but the invention of printing remains one of the most important milestones in human history. It connected cultures, spread ideas, and helped build the modern world. [4] From newspapers to textbooks, the legacy of Bi Sheng and Gutenberg continues to shape our daily lives.

We live in a digital age where screens often replace paper, yet the principles of making information accessible remain the same. [5] Whether we read on paper or on a screen, the spirit of sharing knowledge that printing started continues to inspire us today.

## 选项

A. However, it was not until several centuries later that printing technology reached Europe and sparked a greater transformation.
B. His printing press could produce books much faster and more cheaply than hand copying.

E. The dream of making knowledge available to everyone began with those early printers.
F. For example, the first printed books in China included Buddhist texts and classical works.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前提到中国毕昇发明活字印刷，空后说" laid the foundation for a revolution that would eventually change..."，需要转折说明中国发明后，革命性变化在数世纪后才传到欧洲。A用"However"衔接，准确概括中国先行、欧洲后来接力的历史逻辑。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前描述古腾堡发明金属活字和油墨，空后说"Within decades, millions of books were printed"。B直接说明印刷机速度快、成本低，与"millions of books"形成因果对应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前列举印刷带来的社会变化（科学家、作家、宗教文本），空后说"Education no longer belonged only to the rich"。C的"literacy rates increased"与教育普及直接呼应，作为前文影响的总结。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前从报纸到教科书举例，空后无。D总结印刷技术演变但核心目的不变，承上启下，呼应"legacy continues"。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前对比纸张与屏幕，空后强调"the spirit of sharing knowledge"。E点出"making knowledge available to everyone"与早期印刷者的梦想，呼应结尾升华主题。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the first printed books in China included Buddhist texts and classical works.**：以For example开头，内容与中国印刷相关，但属于具体例证，与[1]空需的转折概括不符；且该空位于段末承上启下，需要过渡句而非举例。
