**Social Media Literacy: Being Smart Online**

Social media has changed how we connect, share, and learn. We use it to chat with friends, watch videos, and follow news from around the world. [1] However, not everything we see online is true or helpful. Fake news, advertisements（广告） that look like real posts, and unhealthy comparisons can affect our mood and choices. That is why learning to use social media wisely—called "social media literacy（素养）"—has become an important skill for people of all ages, especially teenagers.

What does it mean to be media literate? [2] It means asking questions before believing what we read or see. When you see a surprising post, ask yourself: Who made this? Why might they have shared it? Is there evidence（证据） to support the claim? Checking the source and comparing different reports can help us separate facts from opinions. In this way, we become more careful and thoughtful users instead of passive（被动的） consumers.

Social media can also influence how we feel about ourselves. [3] Many people only post their best moments—wins, trips, and happy photos—while hiding their struggles. This can make others feel that their own lives are not good enough. Being media literate helps us understand that what we see is often an edited（编辑过的） version of reality. We can enjoy others' posts without letting them damage our self-confidence（自信心） or make us feel less worthy.

How much time we spend on social media matters too. [4] Endless scrolling（滚动） can take away time from homework, exercise, and face-to-face conversation. Some studies suggest that too much screen time may affect sleep and mental health. Setting limits—for example, turning off notifications（通知） after a certain hour or choosing "screen-free" times—can help us stay in control. The goal is to use social media as a tool, not let it control us.

[5] In summary, social media literacy is not about avoiding the Internet but about using it with awareness and purpose. When we think critically, protect our well-being, and balance our online and offline lives, we can enjoy the benefits of social media while avoiding its pitfalls（陷阱）.
