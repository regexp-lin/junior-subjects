# 04-14 第二篇

**Snacks and Concentration: What to Eat While You Study**

Do you ever feel sleepy or easily distracted when studying in the afternoon? The food you eat may be part of the reason. [1] Your brain needs a steady supply of energy to stay focused, and the wrong snacks can cause sharp rises and sudden drops in blood sugar. When your blood sugar drops, you feel tired and find it hard to concentrate. Choosing the right snacks can help you stay alert and think more clearly during long study sessions.

So what makes a good study snack? Foods that release energy slowly are better than those that give a quick sugar rush. [2] Nuts, whole-grain crackers, cheese, and fruit like apples or bananas provide protein, fiber, and healthy fats. These nutrients help keep your blood sugar stable so your brain gets a steady flow of energy. A small handful of almonds or a yogurt can make a big difference when you have two more hours of homework ahead.

On the other hand, some snacks do more harm than good. Candy, chocolate bars, and sugary drinks taste great, but they cause your blood sugar to spike and then crash soon after. [3] This is why many students feel a "slump"（午后低迷）in the afternoon—they often reach for sweets when they are tired, which only makes the problem worse. Replacing sugary treats with healthier options can break this cycle.

Timing also matters. Eating a large meal right before studying can make you feel sleepy because your body is busy digesting. [4] A light snack every two or three hours works better than eating nothing for a long time and then having a big meal. This keeps your energy level even and avoids both hunger and that heavy, sleepy feeling. A good rule is to have a small snack before you start a difficult task, not when you are already struggling to focus.

[5] From choosing nuts over candy to timing your snacks wisely, small changes in what and when you eat can improve your concentration and make studying more effective. Your brain will thank you—and so will your grades.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] C   [3] D   [4] E   [5] B

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：食物与专注力的因果过渡。空前提出"feel sleepy or easily distracted...The food you eat may be part of the reason"，空后解释"Your brain needs a steady supply of energy...wrong snacks can cause sharp rises and sudden drops in blood sugar"。A选项"As a result, the best study snacks are those that give you lasting energy without big swings"（因此，最好的学习零食是能提供持久能量、避免大幅波动的）承接"食物是原因"这一结论，"lasting energy without big swings"与空后"steady supply...sharp rises and sudden drops"形成对比，先点明正确方向再引出空后对错误零食的详述，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] C**：
  - **线索**：好零食的标准与具体举例。空前提出"Foods that release energy slowly are better than those that give a quick sugar rush"，空后列举"Nuts, whole-grain crackers, cheese, and fruit...provide protein, fiber, and healthy fats"。C选项"The key is to choose snacks that digest slowly and keep your energy levels stable throughout the day"（关键在于选择消化慢、能全天保持能量稳定的零食）中"digest slowly"与空前"release energy slowly"同义对应，"keep energy levels stable"与空后"keep your blood sugar stable...steady flow of energy"呼应，自然引出具体食物举例，衔接自然。

- **[3] D**：
  - **线索**：糖类零食导致的生理反应。空前描述"Candy, chocolate bars, and sugary drinks...cause your blood sugar to spike and then crash soon after"，空后说明"This is why many students feel a 'slump' in the afternoon...Replacing sugary treats with healthier options can break this cycle"。D选项"When that happens, you may feel energetic for a few minutes, but then you become drowsy and lose focus"（当这种情况发生时，你可能先感到精力充沛几分钟，但随后就会困倦、失去专注力）中"When that happens"指代空前"blood sugar spike and crash"，"energetic for a few minutes...then drowsy and lose focus"与"spike...crash"完美对应，且自然引出空后"This is why...slump"，逻辑紧密。

- **[4] E**：
  - **线索**：进食频率与时机建议。空前提出"Eating a large meal right before studying can make you feel sleepy"，空后说明"A light snack every two or three hours works better...keeps your energy level even...have a small snack before you start a difficult task"。E选项"That is why experts suggest snacking little and often rather than waiting until you are very hungry"（因此专家建议少食多餐，而不是等到非常饿再吃）中"That is why"承接空前大餐致困与少量多餐更好的对比，"snacking little and often"与空后"light snack every two or three hours...before you start"呼应，"rather than waiting until very hungry"与"eating nothing for a long time"对应，衔接精准。

- **[5] B**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后列举"choosing nuts over candy...timing your snacks wisely"并总结"small changes...improve your concentration...Your brain will thank you—and so will your grades"。B选项"In short, paying attention to your snacks is a simple way to support your brain when it matters most"（简言之，注意零食选择是在关键时刻支持大脑的简单方法）用"In short"总结全文，"paying attention to your snacks"与空后"small changes in what and when you eat"呼应，"support your brain when it matters most"与"improve concentration...grades"形成升华，收束得当。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. Studies show that students who eat a balanced breakfast often perform better in morning classes.**：强调早餐与上午表现的关系，易被误填[1]。但全文主题为"下午学习时的零食选择"与"专注力"，F讨论的是早餐与上午课，与首段"feel sleepy...in the afternoon"及后文"afternoon slump"的语境不符；空[1]需承接"食物可能是原因"并引出大脑能量与血糖机制的讨论，F的"breakfast...morning"脱离下午学习场景。属"主题相关但场景错位"型干扰。
