# 11-11 第一篇

**Urban Greening: Bringing Nature Back to the City**

Cities are often seen as places of concrete and cars, but many are working to change that. Parks, rooftop gardens, and tree-lined streets can make urban areas cooler, cleaner, and more pleasant to live in. Green spaces provide places for people to relax, exercise, and connect with nature. They also help reduce air pollution and provide homes for birds and insects. [1]

There are many ways to add green to a city. Large public parks offer space for sports, picnics, and community events. Small pocket parks can fit into empty lots between buildings. Schools and hospitals are planting gardens on their grounds. Some buildings even have green roofs covered with plants that help absorb rainwater and keep buildings cooler in summer. [2]

Getting involved is easier than you might think. You can join a community gardening group or help plant trees in your neighborhood. Schools often need volunteers to maintain their gardens. If you have a balcony or windowsill, you can grow herbs or flowers in pots. Even small actions, like watering street trees during dry spells, make a difference. [3]

Green cities face some challenges. Space is limited, and land in cities is expensive. Some plants struggle with pollution or lack of sunlight between tall buildings. Cities must balance green projects with the need for housing, roads, and other buildings. [4]

The benefits of urban greening reach far into the future. Trees planted today will provide shade and cleaner air for decades. Green spaces help cities adapt to climate change by absorbing heat and managing rainfall. Children who grow up near parks often develop a lifelong love of nature. [5]

## 选项

A. Urban greening is not just about beauty—it improves health, quality of life, and the environment for everyone who calls the city home.
B. From large parks to tiny gardens, every patch of green contributes to a healthier, more livable city.
C. Everyone can play a part in urban greening, whether through community projects or simple steps at home.
D. Despite these difficulties, cities around the world are finding creative ways to grow greener, proving that change is possible with planning and commitment.
E. Investing in urban greening today creates a better world for the next generation, where cities and nature can thrive together.
F. For example, Singapore has set a goal to become a "City in a Garden" and has created over 350 parks since the 1960s.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述城市绿化带来的具体好处——公园、屋顶花园、林荫道使城市更凉爽清洁舒适，绿地供人 relax、运动、接触自然，减少污染、为鸟类昆虫提供家园；空后需总结城市绿化的综合价值。A的"not just about beauty"与前文美化呼应，"improves health, quality of life, and the environment"与前文 relax、cleaner air、homes for wildlife 等对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前列举多种绿化形式——大型公园、小型口袋公园、学校医院花园、绿色屋顶，空后需总结各种规模绿化共同的作用。B的"From large parks to tiny gardens"与前文大小形式对应，"every patch of green contributes to a healthier, more livable city"与前文各类绿化的作用呼应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说参与方式——参加社区园艺、帮忙植树、维护学校花园、阳台窗台种盆栽、为行道树浇水等；空后需总结人人可参与的主题。C的"Everyone can play a part"与前文各种参与途径呼应，"whether through community projects or simple steps at home"与"join a group"和"grow herbs in pots"对应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前提出挑战——土地有限昂贵、植物面临污染和光照不足、需平衡绿化与住房道路等建设；空后需转折指出仍有希望。D的"Despite these difficulties"与前文挑战呼应，"cities around the world are finding creative ways to grow greener"与"proving that change is possible"对应，是典型的让步后转折。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说长远益处——今日植树未来数十年提供阴凉与清洁空气、绿地助城市适应气候变化、儿童在公园旁长大易培养自然之爱；空后需总结投资的未来意义。E的"Investing in urban greening today"与前文长期效益呼应，"better world for the next generation"与"decades"和"children"对应，是合理收尾。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, Singapore has set a goal to become a "City in a Garden" and has created over 350 parks since the 1960s.**：以 For example 开头，为新加坡城市绿化的具体案例与数据。各空均为概括或过渡句；[2] 易被误选因涉及多种绿化形式，但该空需概括"各种绿地共同贡献"，而非某一国家的举例。
