# 11-11 第二篇

**Time Management: Making the Most of Every Day**

Students today have much to juggle—homework, tests, clubs, friends, and family time. Without a plan, it is easy to feel overwhelmed or to waste time without realizing it. Learning to manage your time well can reduce stress, improve your grades, and leave room for the things you enjoy. [1]

One simple tool is a daily schedule. Write down your tasks and when you will do them. Block out time for study, meals, rest, and activities. You might use a paper planner, a calendar app, or a simple to-do list. The key is to be realistic—do not pack too much into one day. Leave some free time for unexpected things or for relaxation. [2]

Some tasks are more important or urgent than others. Not everything has to be done right away. Learn to tell the difference between "must do today" and "can wait until tomorrow." Big projects are easier when you break them into smaller steps and do a little each day instead of leaving everything for the last moment. [3]

Distractions can steal your focus. Phones, social media, TV, and noise can make it hard to concentrate. Try studying in a quiet place, turning off notifications, or setting a timer to work for 25 minutes and then take a short break. Tell family or friends when you need focus time. [4]

Time management is not about doing more—it is about doing what matters. Sometimes the best use of time is to rest, play, or spend time with people you care about. A balanced life includes work, rest, and play. [5]

## 选项

A. Good time management is a skill that helps you achieve more while feeling less pressured, and it is something you can learn and improve at any age.
B. Creating a clear plan for your day helps you stay on track and makes it easier to see what needs to be done first.
C. Learning to prioritize and break down large tasks is a key part of managing your time effectively and avoiding last-minute panic.
D. Reducing distractions and protecting your focus time helps you get more done in less time, so you have more freedom for fun later.
E. In the end, good time management gives you control over your days, so you can accomplish your goals and still enjoy the moments that make life meaningful.
F. For instance, the Pomodoro Technique uses a timer to break work into 25-minute blocks with short breaks in between, and many students find it helps them stay focused.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述学生面临的多种任务及无计划时的压力与时间浪费，提到学会管理时间可减轻压力、提高成绩、留出娱乐空间；空后需总结时间管理的综合价值。A的"achieve more while feeling less pressured"与前文 reduce stress、improve grades 呼应，"something you can learn and improve at any age"与"Learning to manage"对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍日程表工具——写下任务与时间、为学习餐饮休息活动留出时间、可用纸质/电子工具、要切合实际并留缓冲；空后需总结制定计划的作用。B的"Creating a clear plan for your day"与前文 daily schedule、block out time 呼应，"stay on track"与"easier to see what needs to be done first"对应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说任务有轻重缓急、要区分"今天必做"与"可延后"、大项目应分解并每日推进而非拖到最后；空后需总结 prioritization 与分解任务的意义。C的"Learning to prioritize and break down large tasks"与前文对应，"avoiding last-minute panic"与"leaving everything for the last moment"呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前列举干扰源及对策——手机社交媒体电视噪音、安静环境、关通知、番茄钟、告知他人专注时间；空后需总结减少干扰、保护专注时间的益处。D的"Reducing distractions and protecting your focus time"与前文呼应，"get more done in less time"与"more freedom for fun later"与前文"room for the things you enjoy"对应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说时间管理不是做更多而是做重要的事、休息娱乐陪伴同样重要、平衡生活；空后需总结时间管理带来的最终收获。E的"good time management gives you control over your days"与前文"doing what matters"呼应，"accomplish your goals and still enjoy the moments"与"work, rest, and play"对应，是合理收尾。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For instance, the Pomodoro Technique uses a timer to break work into 25-minute blocks with short breaks in between, and many students find it helps them stay focused.**：以 For instance 开头，为番茄工作法的具体方法举例。各空均为概括或过渡句；[4] 易被误选因涉及专注与计时，但该空需总结"减少干扰、保护专注"的 overall 作用，而非某一具体技巧的举例。
