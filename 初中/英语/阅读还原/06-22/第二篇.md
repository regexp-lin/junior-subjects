# 06-22 第二篇

**City Lights at Night: Beauty and a Growing Problem**

When the sun goes down, cities light up. Bright signs, street lamps, and building lights make the night seem almost like day. For many people, a city at night is beautiful—the glowing skyline, the lit-up streets, the sense of energy. [1] However, all this light has a downside: it creates light pollution, which affects wildlife, human health, and our view of the stars. As cities grow, the problem is getting worse, but there are ways we can reduce it.

Light pollution affects many living things. [2] For example, birds that migrate at night can become confused by bright lights and fly into buildings. Sea turtles that hatch on beaches may crawl toward city lights instead of the ocean and never reach the water. Insects are attracted to street lamps and often die there, which disrupts the food chain. Even humans can be affected—too much artificial light at night can disrupt sleep and harm our health. Scientists are learning more about how light pollution impacts different species and what we can do to help.

Light pollution also blocks our view of the night sky. [3] In big cities, it is often hard to see stars, the moon, or the Milky Way. Many children grow up never having seen a truly dark sky full of stars. Astronomers need dark skies to study the universe, so they build telescopes in remote, dark places. For ordinary people, losing the night sky means losing a connection to nature and to the wonders of the cosmos（宇宙）. Some cities have started "dark sky" programmes to reduce unnecessary lighting and bring back the stars.

There are practical ways to reduce light pollution. [4] Using shields on street lamps can direct light downward instead of into the sky. Turning off or dimming lights when they are not needed—in empty office buildings or car parks at night—saves energy and reduces glow. Choosing warmer, softer lights instead of harsh blue-white ones can also help, as they are less disruptive to wildlife and human sleep. Cities can set rules for outdoor lighting, and individuals can turn off lights at home when they are not in use.

[5] By reducing light pollution, we protect wildlife, improve our health, and bring back the wonder of a starry night. The next time you look at a bright city skyline, think about the darkness we have lost—and what we can do to bring it back.

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：由城市夜景之美转折引入光污染问题。空前描述"When sun goes down, cities light up...bright signs, street lamps, building lights...night almost like day...many people find city at night beautiful...glowing skyline, lit-up streets, sense of energy"，空后说明"As cities grow, problem getting worse...ways we can reduce it"。A选项"However, all this light has a downside: it creates light pollution, which affects wildlife, human health, and our view of the stars"（然而，所有这些光都有其弊端：它造成光污染，影响野生动物、人类健康和我们观看星空）中"However"转折，"downside...light pollution...affects wildlife, health, view of stars"与空后"problem...reduce"形成逻辑衔接，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：光污染对生物的具体影响。空前提出"Light pollution affects many living things"，空后详述"Birds that migrate at night can become confused by bright lights and fly into buildings...Sea turtles that hatch may crawl toward city lights instead of ocean...Insects attracted to street lamps, often die, disrupt food chain...Even humans...too much artificial light disrupt sleep, harm health"。B选项"For example, migrating birds can be confused by bright lights, sea turtle babies may crawl the wrong way, and insects gather at lamps and die"（例如，迁徙的鸟可能被强光迷惑，小海龟可能爬错方向，昆虫会聚集在灯下并死亡）中"For example"引出具体案例，"birds confused...sea turtle wrong way...insects...lamps...die"与空后内容完美对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：光污染遮蔽夜空与观星。空前提出"Light pollution also blocks our view of the night sky"，空后描述"In big cities hard to see stars, moon, Milky Way...Many children grow up never having seen truly dark sky full of stars...Astronomers need dark skies...build telescopes in remote dark places...ordinary people losing night sky...losing connection to nature, wonders of cosmos...some cities dark sky programmes"。C选项"In big cities, stars and the Milky Way are often hard to see, and many people never experience a truly dark sky"（在大城市里，星星和银河往往难以看见，许多人从未体验过真正的黑暗夜空）中"big cities...stars, Milky Way hard to see...never experience truly dark sky"与空后精准对应，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：减少光污染的具体措施。空前提出"There are practical ways to reduce light pollution"，空后列举"Using shields on street lamps direct light downward...Turning off or dimming when not needed...empty office buildings, car parks...saves energy, reduces glow...Choosing warmer, softer lights instead of harsh blue-white...less disruptive to wildlife, human sleep...Cities set rules...individuals turn off at home when not in use"。D选项"Using shields, dimming unused lights, and choosing warmer lights can all help reduce the problem"（使用遮光罩、调暗闲置灯光、选择更温和的灯光都有助于减轻问题）中"shields...dimming unused lights...warmer lights...reduce"与空后措施完美对应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与呼吁明智用光。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后为"By reducing light pollution, we protect wildlife, improve our health, and bring back the wonder of a starry night. The next time you look at a bright city skyline, think about the darkness we have lost—and what we can do to bring it back."。E选项"City lights can be beautiful, but we need to use them wisely to reduce their harm"（城市灯光可以很美，但我们需要明智地使用它们以减少其危害）用"can be beautiful, but...use wisely"呼应开头美丽与问题的二元性，"reduce their harm"与空后"reducing light pollution...protect...improve...bring back"形成呼应，收束精准。

  - **F.
