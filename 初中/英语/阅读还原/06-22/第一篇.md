# 06-22 第一篇

**The Wonderful World of Mushrooms**

Mushrooms are neither plants nor animals—they belong to a group of living things called fungi（真菌）. You can find them in forests, on lawns, and even in some foods we eat every day. [1] In other words, mushrooms are a unique form of life that appears in many shapes, sizes, and colours, and they play important roles in nature and on our dinner tables. Learning about mushrooms can help us understand the hidden connections in the natural world.

Mushrooms grow in many different ways. [2] Some appear after rain in wet soil or on fallen trees; others grow in dark, damp places like caves or cellars. The part we see above ground is only the "fruit"—most of the fungus lives underground as a network of threads called mycelium（菌丝体）. This network can be huge: one fungus in a forest can stretch over several hectares. Mushrooms use this network to absorb water and nutrients from the soil or from dead plants and trees. They are nature's recyclers, breaking down dead matter and turning it into nutrients for other plants.

Mushrooms are important for both cooking and medicine. [3] Many kinds are edible and add flavour to soups, salads, and main dishes. Common edible mushrooms include button mushrooms, shiitake（香菇）, and oyster mushrooms. In traditional Chinese medicine, some mushrooms are used to support health and boost immunity. However, some wild mushrooms are poisonous and can cause serious harm. Never pick and eat wild mushrooms unless you are sure they are safe—it is better to buy them from a reliable shop or market.

Mushrooms also help the environment. [4] As they break down dead wood and leaves, they return nutrients to the soil and help new plants grow. Some mushrooms form partnerships with trees: the fungus helps the tree absorb water and minerals, while the tree provides the fungus with sugars. This kind of cooperation, called symbiosis（共生）, is common in forests. Without mushrooms and other fungi, forests would have a harder time staying healthy.

[5] From recycling dead matter to supporting forest health, they work quietly behind the scenes. The next time you see a mushroom in a park or on your plate, take a moment to appreciate this fascinating form of life.

## 选项

A. In other words, mushrooms are a unique form of life that appears in many shapes, sizes, and colours, and they play important roles in nature and on our dinner tables.
B. Some appear after rain in wet soil or on fallen trees; others grow in dark, damp places like caves or cellars.
C. Many kinds are edible and are used in cooking and traditional medicine, but some wild mushrooms are poisonous.
D. As they break down dead wood and leaves, they return nutrients to the soil and help forests stay healthy.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：概括蘑菇的独特性与多元角色。空前描述"Mushrooms are neither plants nor animals...belong to fungi...find in forests, lawns, foods we eat every day"，空后说明"Learning about mushrooms can help understand hidden connections in natural world"。A选项"In other words, mushrooms are a unique form of life that appears in many shapes, sizes, and colours, and they play important roles in nature and on our dinner tables"（换言之，蘑菇是一种独特的生命形式，以多种形状、大小和颜色出现，在自然界和我们的餐桌上都扮演重要角色）中"In other words"概括空前，"unique form of life...many shapes, sizes, colours...roles in nature and dinner tables"与空前描述精准呼应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：蘑菇生长的多样环境与方式。空前提出"Mushrooms grow in many different ways"，空后详述"Some appear after rain in wet soil or on fallen trees...others grow in dark, damp places like caves or cellars...part we see is only fruit...most lives underground as mycelium...network can be huge...stretch over several hectares..."。B选项"Some appear after rain in wet soil or on fallen trees; others grow in dark, damp places like caves or cellars"（有的在雨后出现在潮湿土壤或倒木上；有的生长在黑暗潮湿的地方，如洞穴或地窖）中"Some...after rain...wet soil...fallen trees; others...dark, damp...caves or cellars"与空后列举完美对应，直接承接"many different ways"，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：蘑菇在烹饪与医学中的用途及食用安全。空前提出"Mushrooms are important for both cooking and medicine"，空后列举"Many kinds edible...add flavour to soups, salads, main dishes...button mushrooms, shiitake, oyster mushrooms...traditional Chinese medicine...support health, boost immunity...However some wild mushrooms poisonous...cause serious harm...Never pick and eat wild mushrooms unless sure safe"。C选项"Many kinds are edible and are used in cooking and traditional medicine, but some wild mushrooms are poisonous"（许多种类可食用，用于烹饪和传统医学，但有些野生蘑菇有毒）中"edible...cooking...traditional medicine...but...some...poisonous"与空后内容精准对应，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：蘑菇的生态功能——分解、养分循环与共生。空前提出"Mushrooms also help the environment"，空后描述"Break down dead wood and leaves...return nutrients to soil...help new plants grow...form partnerships with trees...fungus helps tree absorb water and minerals...tree provides fungus with sugars...symbiosis...common in forests...Without mushrooms...forests would have harder time staying healthy"。D选项"As they break down dead wood and leaves, they return nutrients to the soil and help forests stay healthy"（当它们分解枯木和落叶时，将养分返还土壤并帮助森林保持健康）中"break down dead wood and leaves...return nutrients...help forests stay healthy"与空后表述完美对应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与蘑菇的价值总结。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后为"From recycling dead matter to supporting forest health, they work quietly behind the scenes. The next time you see a mushroom in a park or on your plate, take a moment to appreciate this fascinating form of life."。E选项"Mushrooms are more than just food—they are a key part of the natural world"（蘑菇不仅仅是食物——它们是自然界的关键组成部分）用"more than just food"呼应前文烹饪段落，"key part of natural world"与空后"recycling...supporting forest health...work quietly behind the scenes"形成呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, many people enjoy mushroom hunting as a weekend hobby in autumn.**：涉及人们秋季采蘑菇的爱好，易被误填[2]。但F以"For example"引出人类活动（采蘑菇爱好），而空[2]讨论的是蘑菇自身的生长环境和方式（雨后、土壤、倒木、洞穴等），F的"mushroom hunting...hobby"属于人类行为，与"grow in many different ways...appear after rain...grow in dark places"的生长论述焦点不同。F属"主题相关但论述层次错位"型干扰。
