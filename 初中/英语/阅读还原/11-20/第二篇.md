**Wind Energy: Harnessing the Power of the Wind**

Wind has been used by humans for thousands of years—to sail ships, turn windmills, and pump water. Today, wind is one of the fastest-growing sources of clean energy in the world. Wind turbines, tall structures with large rotating blades, capture the wind's energy and convert it into electricity. A single large turbine can power hundreds of homes. Wind farms—groups of turbines placed in windy areas—produce enough electricity for entire towns or cities. [1] Wind energy offers a renewable, pollution-free way to meet our growing electricity needs while reducing our reliance on fossil fuels.

Wind power has several advantages over other energy sources. It does not produce greenhouse gases or air pollution. Once a wind farm is built, the fuel—wind—is free and unlimited. Wind energy creates jobs in manufacturing, installation, and maintenance. Many rural areas with strong winds have seen new economic opportunities as wind farms are developed. [2] As a clean and abundant resource, wind power plays an important role in the transition to a more sustainable energy future.

However, wind energy also has some challenges. Wind does not blow constantly, so wind power cannot provide a steady supply of electricity around the clock. Engineers are working on better battery storage to save excess wind energy for use when the wind is calm. Some people worry about the noise from turbines or their effect on birds and bats. Researchers are designing quieter turbines and studying how to reduce harm to wildlife. [3] With continued research and innovation, these challenges can be addressed, making wind power an even more reliable and wildlife-friendly option.

Wind turbines work best in places where the wind blows often and strongly. Coastal areas, open plains, and mountain passes are common sites for wind farms. Offshore wind farms—built in the ocean—often have stronger and more consistent winds. Countries like Denmark and the United Kingdom get a large share of their electricity from offshore wind. [4] By choosing the right locations, we can maximize the amount of clean energy that wind power can provide.

The use of wind energy is expected to keep growing in the coming decades. Governments and companies are investing in larger, more efficient turbines and in new wind farm projects. As technology improves and costs drop, wind power becomes more competitive with traditional energy sources. [5] In the fight against climate change and air pollution, wind energy stands as one of our most promising tools for a cleaner, healthier planet.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍风能的古今应用、现代风力涡轮机、风电场及发电规模，空后需总结风能的价值。A的"renewable, pollution-free way"与前文清洁能源对应，"meet our growing electricity needs... reducing our reliance on fossil fuels"概括风能的能源替代意义。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前列举风能优势——无排放、燃料免费无限、创造就业、农村经济机遇，空后需总结风能的战略地位。B的"As a clean and abundant resource"与前文优势呼应，"plays an important role in the transition to a more sustainable energy future"点明风能的转型作用。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说明风能的挑战——间歇性、储能需求、噪音与对鸟类蝙蝠影响，及应对研究，空后需总结问题可解决的前景。C的"With continued research and innovation, these challenges can be addressed"承接前文问题与研发，"more reliable and wildlife-friendly"概括改善方向。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前描述风能选址——海岸、平原、山口、海上风电及丹麦英国案例，空后需总结选址的意义。D的"By choosing the right locations"与前文选址讨论对应，"maximize the amount of clean energy"概括选址对发电量的影响。
