# 04-09 第二篇

**The Invention of Braille**

Before the 1800s, blind people had very few ways to read or write. Some systems used raised letters that could be felt with the fingers, but they were difficult to learn and slow to use. [1] Everything changed in 1824, when a fifteen-year-old French boy named Louis Braille created a simple code based on patterns of dots. His invention opened the door to literacy for millions of blind people around the world. Today, braille is used in books, signs, elevator buttons, and even on food packaging.

How did Louis Braille get his idea? As a child, he became blind after an accident. [2] At his school for the blind in Paris, he learned about "night writing"—a code of raised dots that soldiers used to read messages in the dark without making a sound. Louis was inspired by this system, but he found it too complicated. He spent years simplifying it until he had a code that used only six dots arranged in different patterns. Each pattern stood for a letter, number, or common word.

The braille system is easy to understand once you see how it works. [3] Imagine a small rectangle divided into two columns of three dots each—like the number six on a domino. A dot can be raised or flat, so there are 64 possible combinations. Louis used these 64 patterns to represent the letters of the alphabet, punctuation marks, and numbers. With just six dots, a person could read and write any text.

At first, many people did not accept braille. [4] Some teachers thought that blind students should rely on listening instead of touching. Others believed that the dot system was too different from the printed letters that sighted people use. It was not until Louis had died that braille slowly spread to schools and libraries across Europe. Today, braille is recognized as one of the most important inventions for people with visual impairments.

[5] From a young boy's idea to a global system of literacy, braille has changed countless lives. Thanks to Louis Braille's creativity and determination, blind people can now read independently, study the same subjects as everyone else, and work in fields that once seemed impossible. His story reminds us that one person's innovation can make a huge difference for many.

- **[1] F**：
  - **线索**：历史转折与人物引入。空前描述盲人在19世纪前"very few ways to read or write...raised letters...difficult to learn and slow to use"，空后具体指出"Everything changed in 1824, when a fifteen-year-old French boy named Louis Braille created a simple code"。F选项"That breakthrough came from a teenager who refused to give up on the dream of reading"（这一突破来自一位不愿放弃阅读梦想的少年）中"That breakthrough"指代前文困境的突破，"teenager"与空后"fifteen-year-old...boy"对应，"refused to give up on the dream of reading"与"opened the door to literacy"呼应，自然引出Louis Braille及其发明，逻辑顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：灵感来源与具体展开。空前指出"As a child, he became blind...At his school for the blind in Paris, he learned about 'night writing'"。B选项"He discovered a military code that later inspired his own invention"（他发现了一种军用密码，后来启发了自己的发明）中"military code"与空后"night writing—a code of raised dots that soldiers used"完美对应，"inspired his own invention"与空后"Louis was inspired...spent years simplifying it"衔接，完成"发现→启发→改造"的逻辑链。

- **[3] E**：
  - **线索**：系统解释与结构说明。空前提出"The braille system is easy to understand once you see how it works"，空后具体描述"Imagine a small rectangle divided into two columns of three dots each—like the number six on a domino"。E选项"The key is the 'braille cell'—a unit of six dots in a 2×3 grid"（关键在于"盲文单元格"——一个由6个点组成的2×3网格单元）中"braille cell"为专业术语，与空后"small rectangle...two columns of three dots"结构完全对应，"six dots...2×3 grid"与"two columns of three dots each"及"64 possible combinations"（2^6=64）逻辑一致，衔接紧密。

  - **线索**：早期反对与具体理由。空前指出"At first, many people did not accept braille"，空后列举"Some teachers thought that blind students should rely on listening instead of touching. - **[5] A**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后"From a young boy's idea to a global system of literacy...Thanks to Louis Braille's creativity and determination...one person's innovation can make a huge difference"。A选项"In the end, braille stands as a lasting gift to the blind community"（总之，盲文成为送给盲人群体的一份持久礼物）用"In the end"总结全文，"lasting gift"与空后"changed countless lives...read independently...make a huge difference"形成升华，"blind community"与前文主题一致，收束得当。
