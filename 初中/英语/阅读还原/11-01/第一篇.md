# 11-01 第一篇

**Recycling: Turning Waste into Resources**

Every day, millions of tons of waste are produced around the world. Much of it ends up in landfills, where it takes years—sometimes centuries—to break down. Recycling offers a way to reduce waste by turning used materials into new products. Glass, paper, metal, and certain plastics can all be recycled, saving energy and natural resources in the process. [1] Recycling is not just about reducing trash; it is a practical way to protect the environment and use the earth's limited resources more wisely.

The recycling process begins when people sort their waste into different categories. Clean paper goes into one bin; glass and metal into another; certain plastics into a third. Collection trucks take these materials to recycling facilities, where they are cleaned, sorted further, and processed. Paper is turned back into pulp; metals are melted and reshaped; glass is crushed and remade. [2] Each material follows its own recycling path, but the goal is always the same: to give used items a second life instead of sending them to a landfill.

Recycling saves energy compared to making products from raw materials. For example, recycling aluminum cans uses about 95 percent less energy than producing new aluminum from ore. Recycling paper saves trees and uses less water. These savings add up quickly when millions of people participate. [3] The environmental benefits of recycling are significant, and they grow larger as more communities and businesses adopt recycling programs.

However, recycling has its challenges. Not all materials can be recycled easily. Contaminated items—food-stained paper or mixed plastics—often cannot be processed and end up in landfills anyway. Many people are confused about what can and cannot be recycled in their area. [4] Better education and clearer labeling can help people recycle correctly, reducing contamination and making the whole system more efficient.

Looking ahead, new technologies are making recycling more effective. Scientists are developing ways to recycle materials that were once considered impossible to reuse. Some companies are designing products with recycling in mind, using fewer mixed materials and simpler packaging. [5] As technology improves and awareness grows, recycling will play an even larger role in building a sustainable future where less is wasted and more is reused.

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍全球废弃物问题——数百万吨垃圾、填埋场、分解缓慢，引出回收利用——玻璃、纸、金属、塑料可回收、节省能源与资源，空后需概括回收的更大意义。A的"not just about reducing trash"与前文回收概念呼应，"protect the environment and use the earth's limited resources more wisely"与"saving energy and natural resources"对应，是对回收意义的提升概括。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前描述回收流程——分类投放、收集运输、清洗再分拣、纸变纸浆、金属熔化重塑、玻璃粉碎再造，空后需总结不同材料回收的共同目标。B的"Each material follows its own recycling path"与纸、金属、玻璃各自工序对应，"give used items a second life instead of sending them to a landfill"概括了回收的核心目的。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前举铝罐回收节能95%、纸回收保树省水、数百万人参与节约效果累积等具体数据，空后需总结环保收益。C的"The environmental benefits of recycling are significant"与前文节能数据呼应，"grow larger as more communities and businesses adopt recycling programs"与"millions of people participate"逻辑延伸。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说回收面临挑战——非所有材料可回收、污染物品无法处理、人们对可回收物困惑，空后需提出解决方案。D的"Better education and clearer labeling"针对"people are confused"提出对策，"reducing contamination and making the whole system more efficient"与"contaminated items"形成呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说新技术提升回收效率——科学家研发新回收方式、公司设计更易回收产品，空后需展望回收的未来前景。E的"As technology improves and awareness grows"承接前文科技进步，"recycling will play an even larger role in building a sustainable future"是全文收尾的合理展望。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For instance, Japan has one of the most detailed recycling systems in the world, with residents sorting waste into more than ten categories.**：以For instance开头，为具体国家回收案例（日本）。各空均为概括或过渡句；[2]易被误选因涉及分类，但该空需总结回收流程的共同目标，而非日本分类系统的具体案例。
