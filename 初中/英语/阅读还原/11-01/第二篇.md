# 11-01 第二篇

**The Power of Music in Our Lives**

Music is everywhere—in our homes, in shops, on the streets, and in our headphones. People listen to music when they are happy, sad, excited, or tired. It has been part of human culture for thousands of years, from ancient drums and flutes to today's digital streaming. But music is more than entertainment; it affects our emotions, our health, and even our brains. [1] Music has a unique ability to connect with us on a deep level, influencing how we feel and think in ways that few other things can.

Scientists have found that listening to music can change our mood. Upbeat songs can make us feel more energetic, while slow, gentle music can help us relax. Hospitals use music to help patients manage pain and anxiety. Music therapy is now a recognized treatment for conditions such as depression and PTSD. [2] The healing power of music is supported by growing scientific evidence, showing that it is not just a matter of personal taste but a real tool for improving well-being.

Music also helps with learning. Studies show that children who learn to play an instrument often do better in math, reading, and problem-solving. Playing music requires focus, coordination, and memory, all of which strengthen the brain. Even listening to background music while studying can improve concentration for some people. [3] Whether we play it or simply listen, music exercises our minds and can make learning more enjoyable and effective.

Beyond individual benefits, music brings people together. Concerts, festivals, and community choirs create shared experiences that build bonds between strangers. National anthems unite citizens; lullabies connect parents and children. In times of difficulty, people often turn to music for comfort and solidarity. [4] Music's power to unite people across age, language, and culture is one of its most remarkable qualities, reminding us of what we have in common.

As technology advances, the way we experience music continues to change. Streaming services give us access to millions of songs, and artificial intelligence can now compose original pieces. Yet the emotional core of music remains the same. [5] No matter how technology reshapes the music industry, the human need for melody, rhythm, and harmony will endure, keeping music at the heart of our shared experience.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前说音乐无处不在、伴随各种情绪、有数千年历史、影响情感健康和大脑，空后需概括音乐的独特影响力。A的"unique ability to connect with us on a deep level"与"more than entertainment"呼应，"influencing how we feel and think"与"affects our emotions... and even our brains"对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说音乐改变情绪、医院用于疼痛和焦虑管理、音乐治疗被认可用于抑郁和PTSD，空后需总结音乐的治愈力。B的"The healing power of music is supported by growing scientific evidence"与前文科学发现呼应，"not just a matter of personal taste but a real tool for improving well-being"与音乐治疗对应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说学习乐器的孩子数学阅读更好、演奏需专注协调记忆、背景音乐可提升注意力，空后需总结音乐对学习的作用。C的"Whether we play it or simply listen"与"playing music"和"listening to background music"两方面呼应，"exercises our minds and can make learning more enjoyable and effective"概括了学习益处。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说音乐将人们联系在一起——演唱会、节日、社区合唱、国歌、摇篮曲、困难时期寻求慰藉，空后需总结音乐的联结力。D的"Music's power to unite people across age, language, and culture"与前文多种联结场景呼应，"reminding us of what we have in common"是合理升华。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说技术改变音乐体验——流媒体、AI作曲、但情感核心不变，空后需展望音乐未来。E的"No matter how technology reshapes the music industry"承接前文技术发展，"the human need for melody, rhythm, and harmony will endure"与"emotional core remains the same"呼应，是全文收尾。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the Finnish education system includes music as a core subject from primary school onward, recognizing its role in child development.**：以For example开头，为具体国家教育案例（芬兰）。各空均为概括或过渡句；[3]易被误选因涉及学习，但该空需概括音乐对学习的总体益处，而非芬兰教育系统的具体案例。
