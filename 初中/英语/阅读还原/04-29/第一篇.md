# 04-29 第一篇

**When Winter Comes: How Animals Sleep Through the Cold**

When the weather gets cold and food becomes hard to find, many animals face a serious problem. [1] Some animals solve this by going into a deep sleep called hibernation（冬眠）. During hibernation, their body temperature drops and their heart beats much more slowly. They may not eat or move for weeks or even months. This helps them save energy when there is little or no food around.

Different animals prepare for hibernation in different ways. Bears eat a lot in autumn to build up fat. [2] The fat stored in their bodies will keep them alive through the long winter months. Squirrels collect nuts and seeds and hide them in safe places. Some frogs dig into the mud at the bottom of ponds. Each animal has its own way to survive the cold season.

What happens inside an animal's body during hibernation? Scientists have found that hibernating animals can slow down almost everything. [3] Their breathing becomes very slow, and they use much less energy than when they are active. A hibernating bear may breathe only once or twice a minute. A ground squirrel's body temperature can drop to just a few degrees above freezing. Without these changes, the animal would not have enough energy to last until spring.

Not all animals that sleep in winter are true hibernators. [4] Some animals, like raccoons（浣熊） and skunks（臭鼬）, wake up from time to time to look for food when the weather is a bit warmer. True hibernators, such as bears and ground squirrels, stay asleep for most of the winter. They wake up only when spring arrives and the weather becomes mild again.

[5] Hibernation is one of nature's most amazing survival strategies. It shows how animals have learned to live with the changing seasons. When we see snow covering the ground, we can remember that underneath the quiet winter world, many animals are sleeping safely, waiting for the return of spring and warm weather.

## 选项

A. In other words, they store energy in their bodies before winter comes so they can use it later.
B. That is why their bodies must change in special ways to save energy.
C. In short, hibernation helps animals survive the hardest time of the year.
D. Before winter arrives, these animals must get ready in one way or another.
E. However, some animals only sleep lightly and do not hibernate in the true sense.
F. For example, chipmunks（花栗鼠） wake up every few weeks to eat some of the food they have stored.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] D   [2] A   [3] B   [4] E   [5] C

## 深度解析

- **[1] D**：
  - **线索**：引出动物如何应对冬季缺食问题。空前描述"weather gets cold and food becomes hard to find...many animals face a serious problem"，空后列举"Some animals solve this by going into hibernation...body temperature drops...heart beats slowly...save energy"。D选项"Before winter arrives, these animals must get ready in one way or another"（冬季到来之前，这些动物必须以某种方式做好准备）中"Before winter arrives"承接空前"cold...food hard to find"，"get ready in one way or another"自然引出空后"Some animals solve this by..."的多种应对方式，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] A**：
  - **线索**：秋季储备与冬季消耗的逻辑。空前详述"Bears eat a lot in autumn to build up fat"，空后说明"The fat stored in their bodies will keep them alive through the long winter months"。A选项"In other words, they store energy in their bodies before winter comes so they can use it later"（换言之，它们在冬季到来前把能量储存在体内，以便之后使用）中"In other words"对上文"build up fat"进行概括，"store energy...before winter...use it later"与空后"fat stored...keep them alive through the long winter"完美呼应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] B**：
  - **线索**：冬眠时体内变化的原因与必要性。空前提出"What happens inside an animal's body during hibernation?"并说明"hibernating animals can slow down almost everything"，空后列举"breathing becomes very slow...use much less energy...breathe only once or twice a minute...body temperature can drop...Without these changes, would not have enough energy"。B选项"That is why their bodies must change in special ways to save energy"（因此它们的身体必须以特殊方式发生变化以节省能量）中"That is why"承接空前"slow down almost everything"，"change in special ways to save energy"与空后具体变化及"would not have enough energy"的后果形成因果呼应，衔接自然。

- **[4] E**：
  - **线索**：真冬眠与浅冬眠的区分。空前说明"Not all animals that sleep in winter are true hibernators"，空后举例"Some animals, like raccoons and skunks, wake up from time to time to look for food...True hibernators, such as bears and ground squirrels, stay asleep for most of the winter"。E选项"However, some animals only sleep lightly and do not hibernate in the true sense"（然而，有些动物只是轻度睡眠，并非真正意义上的冬眠）中"However"承接空前"Not all...true hibernators"，"sleep lightly...do not hibernate in the true sense"与空后"wake up from time to time"及"True hibernators...stay asleep"形成对比，衔接得当。

- **[5] C**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后列举"one of nature's most amazing survival strategies...animals have learned to live with the changing seasons...many animals are sleeping safely, waiting for spring"。C选项"In short, hibernation helps animals survive the hardest time of the year"（简言之，冬眠帮助动物度过一年中最艰难的时期）用"In short"总结全文，"survive the hardest time of the year"与空后"survival strategies...waiting for spring"形成呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, chipmunks wake up every few weeks to eat some of the food they have stored.**：涉及动物冬眠期间醒来进食，易被误填[4]。但F以"For example"引出花栗鼠的例证，而空[4]前后文已有"raccoons and skunks"及"bears and ground squirrels"的具体例子；且F侧重"stored food"（储粮型动物），与空后"wake up...to look for food"（外出觅食型）语义略有差异。若填入[4]，会与"True hibernators...stay asleep for most of the winter"产生冲突。F属"举例型但与段内已有例证重复、衔接层次不足"型干扰。
