# 08-03 第1篇

**3D Printing Technology**

3D printing, also known as additive manufacturing, has changed the way we create objects. Instead of cutting or carving material away like traditional methods, 3D printing builds objects layer by layer from the bottom up. A digital design is sent to the printer, which then uses materials such as plastic, metal, or even chocolate to create a physical object. [1] This technology has become more affordable over the years, allowing schools, hospitals, and small businesses to use it. Today, 3D printing is used in many fields, from making toys and jewelry to building airplane parts and medical implants.

In medicine, 3D printing has brought amazing advances. Doctors can now create detailed models of a patient's organs before surgery, helping them plan the operation more carefully. [2] Some hospitals even print custom implants and prosthetics that fit each patient perfectly. This personalized approach improves recovery and reduces the risk of complications. In the future, scientists hope to 3D print living tissue and organs for transplants.

Education is another area where 3D printing shines. Students can turn their ideas into real objects, learning about design and engineering in a hands-on way. [3] Teachers use 3D printers to create teaching aids such as models of molecules, historical artifacts, or parts of the human body. This makes abstract concepts easier to understand and helps students stay engaged in learning.

The environmental impact of 3D printing is still being studied. On one hand, it can reduce waste because we only use the material we need. [4] On the other hand, some 3D printing materials are hard to recycle. As the technology improves, researchers are working on eco-friendly materials and more efficient printing processes. Balancing innovation with sustainability remains a challenge.

[5] 3D printing will likely play an even bigger role in our lives in the coming years. As printers become faster, cheaper, and capable of using more materials, the possibilities seem endless.

## 选项

A. As a result, what was once a tool mainly for engineers is now available to more people.
B. These custom-made medical devices are changing how we treat injuries and disabilities.
C. Schools are finding that 3D printing sparks creativity and problem-solving skills.
D. Manufacturers and designers must consider both the benefits and the drawbacks.
E. From homes to factories, this technology promises to transform how we make things.
F. For example, architects use 3D printers to build scale models of buildings quickly.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍3D打印的基本原理和材料，空后说 “This technology has become more affordable...allowing schools, hospitals, and small businesses to use it”。选项 A 的 “what was once a tool mainly for engineers is now available to more people” 与 “more affordable” 和 “schools, hospitals, small businesses” 完美呼应，承上启下。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前提到 “custom implants and prosthetics that fit each patient perfectly”，空后说 “This personalized approach improves recovery”。选项 B 的 “These custom-made medical devices” 指代前文，“changing how we treat injuries and disabilities” 与 “improves recovery” 逻辑一致。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：本段主题为教育领域应用，空前说 “Teachers use 3D printers to create teaching aids”，空后强调 “makes abstract concepts easier to understand...helps students stay engaged”。选项 C 的 “sparks creativity and problem-solving skills” 概括教育价值，与后文 “engaged in learning” 衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：本段讨论环境影响，空前对比 “reduce waste” 与 “materials are hard to recycle”，空后说 “Balancing innovation with sustainability remains a challenge”。选项 D 的 “consider both the benefits and the drawbacks” 直接对应这一辩证关系，作段落总结恰当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：结尾段展望未来，空前说 “play an even bigger role...possibilities seem endless”。选项 E 的 “From homes to factories...transform how we make things” 总结应用范围之广，与全文对多领域应用的介绍形成呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, architects use 3D printers to build scale models of buildings quickly.**：虽与3D打印应用相关，但属具体行业举例。文中已按医学、教育、环境分段论述，插入 F 会打乱层次；且在医学或教育段插入建筑案例会导致主题偏移，易在 [2] 或 [3] 处误选。
