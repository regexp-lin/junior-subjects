**Gardening: Growing Plants and Well-Being**

Gardening is the practice of growing plants—flowers, vegetables, herbs, or trees—either in the ground or in containers. People garden for many reasons: to grow their own food, to beautify their space, to relax, or simply to connect with nature. Gardening can be done in a large backyard, on a small balcony, or even on a windowsill. You do not need a lot of space or experience to get started. A few pots, some soil, and seeds or seedlings are enough to begin. [1] Gardening is a flexible and rewarding activity that people of all ages and skill levels can enjoy, whether they have a big garden or just a few pots by the window.

Gardening involves basic skills that are easy to learn. You need to choose the right plants for your climate and light conditions. Watering, weeding, and feeding plants require some attention, but they become part of a peaceful routine. Learning when to plant, when to harvest, and how to deal with pests comes with practice. Many gardeners find that mistakes are part of the learning process—plants can be forgiving, and each season brings a new chance to improve. [2] With patience and a willingness to learn, anyone can develop the skills needed to grow healthy plants and enjoy the satisfaction of watching them thrive over time.

Gardening has proven benefits for physical and mental health. Digging, planting, and weeding provide gentle exercise and time outdoors in the fresh air and sunlight. Studies show that spending time in green spaces can reduce stress, improve mood, and even boost concentration. Growing your own food can also encourage healthier eating habits. For many people, gardening is a form of mindfulness—a way to slow down and focus on the present moment. [3] Gardening offers a natural path to better health and well-being, combining physical activity, stress relief, and a sense of accomplishment that benefits both body and mind.

Community gardens bring people together. In cities, where private outdoor space is limited, community gardens offer plots that people can rent or share. Neighbors meet, exchange tips, and sometimes grow food for local food banks. Schools use gardens to teach students about science, nutrition, and responsibility. [4] Gardening can be a social activity that builds community, teaches valuable lessons, and helps people share space and knowledge, especially in places where land is scarce or expensive.

As more people move to cities and face busy schedules, gardening reminds us of our connection to the natural world. Even a small green space—a few herbs on a windowsill or tomatoes on a balcony—can bring joy and a sense of care. [5] Gardening connects us to the rhythms of nature and to the simple pleasure of watching something grow, offering a peaceful escape from the rush of modern life and a reminder that we can create beauty and nurture life with our own hands.

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍园艺——种植花卉、蔬菜、香草或树木，可在地里或容器中，人们为自种食物、美化空间、放松或亲近自然而园艺，可在后院、阳台或窗台进行，无需大空间或经验，几盆土和种子或幼苗即可开始，空后需概括园艺作为灵活有益活动、适合各年龄与水平的特点。A的"flexible and rewarding activity"与前文"many reasons"和"do not need a lot of space"呼应，"people of all ages and skill levels can enjoy"与"whether they have a big garden or just a few pots"对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说园艺基本技能——根据气候与光照选植物，浇水除草施肥需关注但可成日常，播种收获与病虫害防治随练习掌握，许多园丁视错误为学习过程、植物有容错性、每季可改进，空后需总结耐心与学习意愿对掌握技能与享受植物生长的意义。B的"With patience and a willingness to learn"与前文"easy to learn"和"comes with practice"呼应，"develop the skills needed to grow healthy plants"与"basic skills"对应，"satisfaction of watching them thrive"与"each season brings a new chance"对应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说园艺对身心健康的益处——挖土种植除草提供轻度运动与户外日照，研究显示绿景可减压、改善情绪、提升专注，自种食物促健康饮食，对许多人园艺是正念形式，空后需总结园艺作为改善健康与幸福的自然途径。C的"natural path to better health and well-being"与前文"proven benefits"呼应，"physical activity, stress relief, and a sense of accomplishment"与"gentle exercise"、"reduce stress"、"mindfulness"对应，"benefits both body and mind"与"physical and mental health"对应。
