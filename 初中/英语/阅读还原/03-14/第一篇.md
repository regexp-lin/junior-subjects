**The Power of Positive Thinking**

Have you ever noticed that happy people seem to have all the luck in the world? It might look like magic, but science actually has an explanation for this. [1] In fact, positive thinking can completely change the way you experience life.

When you have a positive attitude, your brain naturally looks for solutions instead of complaining about problems. [2] If you fail a test, a negative mind might make you want to give up studying. However, a positive mind will encourage you to find out why you failed and try harder next time.

Additionally, positive thinking is incredibly beneficial for your physical health. When you are cheerful and hopeful, your body produces chemicals that reduce stress and make you feel relaxed. [3] This means that generally happy people tend to get sick less often and recover much faster when they do catch a cold.

So, how can we train our brains to be more positive? One effective method is keeping a "gratitude journal" (感恩日记). [4] Even thinking about a sunny day or a delicious meal can slowly teach your brain to focus on the brighter side of life.

[5] Although nobody can be perfectly happy all the time, choosing to look for the good in every situation is a habit worth developing.

# 03-14 第一篇

**The Power of Positive Thinking**

## 选项

A. Studies show that these chemicals can powerfully strengthen your immune system.
B. Every night before bed, write down three simple things that made you happy that day.
C. For example, consider how two different minds react to exactly the same bad situation.
D. Therefore, experts suggest that positive thinking depends entirely on having lots of money.
E. By making this simple choice, you can truly unlock a healthier and much happier future.
F. It is because the way we think directly affects how we feel and what we achieve.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] F   [2] C   [3] A   [4] B   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] F**：
  - **线索**：回答前文与解释原因。空前提出疑问“你有没有注意到快乐的人似乎拥有世界上所有的好运？”并说“科学对此有解释”。F选项“It is because the way we think directly affects how we feel and what we achieve（这是因为我们的思维方式直接影响了我们的感受和成就）”正是在给出这种“科学解释”。“the way we think”呼应了随后的中心词“positive thinking（积极思维）”。
- **[2] C**：
  - **线索**：举例说明与上下文呼应。空前指出“大脑自然地寻找解决方案而不是抱怨”。C选项“For example, consider how two different minds react to exactly the same bad situation（例如，想一想两种完全不同的大脑如何应对完全相同的不良情况）”非常确切地引出了后面的具体情境：一个是negative mind（消极心态），一个是positive mind（积极心态），两者都面对了“fail a test（考砸了）”这个相同的“bad situation”。逻辑过渡堪称完美。
- **[3] A**：
  - **线索**：词汇承接与因果递进。空前说身体产生减少压力和让人放松的chemicals（化学物质）。A选项“Studies show that these chemicals can powerfully strengthen your immune system”（研究表明，这些化学物质能强有力地增强您的免疫系统），其中代词“these chemicals”就是直接指代前一句释放的化学物质。正因为增强了免疫系统，也就顺理成章地引出了空后的结果：“This means... 快乐的人往往较少生病”（生病少即免疫系统强大的表现）。
- **[4] B**：
  - **线索**：同义解释与承上启下。空前提出的建议是“keeping a gratitude journal（写感恩日记）”。B选项“Every night before bed, write down three simple things that made you happy that day”（每天晚上睡觉前，写下当天让你快乐的三件简单的事情）正是对到底什么是“写感恩日记”、该怎么写的具体行为指导。这也自然顺承了后一句：即使是想想美好的食物或阳光（这些简单的快乐事物），也能教你的大脑聚焦光明面。
- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：段首总结与全文呼应。空[5]位于结论段的首句。E选项“By making this simple choice, you can truly unlock a healthier and much happier future”（通过做出这个简单的选择，您可以真正开启更健康、更幸福的未来）是对全文第二段（更幸福/心理）和第三段（更健康/生理）两大论点的高度总结。“this simple choice”呼应了前一段的做感恩记录等简单做法。也引出了空后“虽然没人能一直完美开心，但选择发现美好是个值得培养的习惯”的客观结语。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **D. Therefore, experts suggest that positive thinking depends entirely on having lots of money.**：该项包含了“Therefore（因此）”总结词和“positive thinking（积极思考）”这一全文高频中心词。然而，其表达的内涵“积极的心态完全依赖于拥有很多钱”，这是一种非常物质的观点。全文从头到尾都在强调积极思维可以通过改变认知或写感恩日记（主观努力）来获得，选项将这种美好的正念单纯归结于金钱，与原篇格调、主旨彻底相逆，属于逻辑谬误的干扰项。
