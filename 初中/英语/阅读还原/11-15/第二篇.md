**Waste Sorting: A Small Act with Big Impact**

The amount of waste we produce each year is growing, and landfills around the world are filling up. Much of what we throw away could be recycled or reused, but it often ends up mixed together, making it hard to process. Waste sorting—separating recyclables, kitchen waste, and other types of trash—is a simple step that can make a huge difference. [1]

Different places use different sorting systems. Some have two bins: recyclables and general waste. Others add a third for kitchen or organic waste. Colors and labels help: blue for paper, green for glass, yellow for plastic. It may seem confusing at first, but with a little practice, sorting becomes a habit. Schools and communities often provide guides or hold workshops to help people get started. [2]

Recycling saves energy and raw materials. Making new products from recycled materials often uses less energy than making them from scratch. For example, recycling aluminum saves about 95% of the energy needed to produce new aluminum. Paper recycling saves trees, and plastic recycling reduces the need for new petroleum. [3]

Kitchen waste can be turned into compost. Fruit peels, vegetable scraps, coffee grounds, and eggshells can break down into rich soil that helps plants grow. Composting at home or through community programs reduces the amount of waste sent to landfills. It also cuts down on greenhouse gases that come from rotting organic matter in landfills. [4]

Everyone has a role to play. Students can remind their families to sort. Schools can set up recycling bins in every classroom. Communities can organize clean-up events and share tips. Governments can improve collection systems and support recycling industries. [5]

## 选项

A. Proper waste sorting is the first step toward reducing pollution, saving resources, and building a more sustainable future for our planet.
B. Learning your local sorting rules and following them consistently turns a small daily habit into a meaningful contribution to the environment.
C. When we sort and recycle correctly, we give materials a second life and reduce the strain on our natural resources and the environment.
D. Composting organic waste closes the loop—from food to soil to food again—and is one of the most effective ways to make our waste work for the planet instead of against it.
E. Waste sorting works best when everyone participates—and when we all do our part, we move closer to a cleaner, healthier world for generations to come.
F. For example, Germany recycles about 68% of its municipal waste, one of the highest rates in the world, thanks to its strict sorting and recycling laws.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前说垃圾量增长、填埋场饱和、许多可回收物被混扔难处理、分类是简单而有效的步骤；空后需总结正确分类的第一步意义。A的"first step toward reducing pollution, saving resources, and building a more sustainable future"与前文 make a huge difference 呼应，与 landfill、recycle 对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说各地分类方式不同——两桶或三桶、颜色与标签、初看似复杂但可成习惯、学校社区有指南或工作坊；空后需总结学习并坚持本地规则的意义。B的"Learning your local sorting rules and following them consistently"与前文呼应，"small daily habit into a meaningful contribution"与"with a little practice, sorting becomes a habit"对应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说回收节约能源与原料——再生品耗能更少、举例铝纸塑料；空后需总结正确分类回收赋予材料二次生命、减轻环境负担。C的"When we sort and recycle correctly"与前文 recycling 呼应，"give materials a second life"与"saves"和"reduces"对应，"reduce the strain on our natural resources"与前文 raw materials、trees、petroleum 对应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说厨余可堆肥——果皮菜渣咖啡渣蛋壳分解成肥土、家庭或社区堆肥减少填埋与温室气体；空后需总结有机堆肥形成闭环、变废为宝。D的"Composting organic waste closes the loop—from food to soil to food again"与前文 break down、rich soil、helps plants grow 呼应，"make our waste work for the planet instead of against it"与前文 reduces、cuts down 对应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说人人有责——学生提醒家人、学校设回收桶、社区组织活动、政府完善系统；空后需总结人人参与、共同行动的意义。E的"Waste sorting works best when everyone participates"与前文 "Everyone has a role" 呼应，"we all do our part"与各主体对应，"cleaner, healthier world for generations to come"是合理收尾。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, Germany recycles about 68% of its municipal waste, one of the highest rates in the world, thanks to its strict sorting and recycling laws.**：以 For example 开头，为德国回收率的具体数据举例。各空均为概括或过渡句；[2] 或 [5] 易被误选因涉及分类与参与，但该空需概括性陈述而非某一国家的统计数据举例。
