# 11-15 第一篇

**Photography: Capturing Moments, Telling Stories**

Photography allows us to freeze a moment in time and keep it forever. With a camera or smartphone, we can capture birthdays, vacations, sunsets, and everyday life. Photos help us remember what we might otherwise forget and share experiences with people who were not there. [1]

You do not need expensive equipment to take good photos. Modern smartphones take sharp pictures, and many free apps help you edit and improve them. The basics matter more than the gear: pay attention to light, composition, and timing. A well-lit scene, a clear subject, and the right moment can make even a simple shot memorable. [2]

There are many genres of photography. Portrait photography captures people and their personalities. Landscape photography shows the beauty of nature. Street photography documents life in cities. Wildlife photography requires patience and skill to catch animals in their natural habitats. You can explore different styles and find what interests you most. [3]

Editing is part of the process. Cropping can improve composition. Adjusting brightness, contrast, and color can make an image pop. But it is important to stay honest—heavy editing that changes the truth of a scene can mislead viewers. Many photographers aim for natural-looking results that enhance rather than replace reality. [4]

Photography can also be a form of storytelling. A series of images can tell a story about a day, a journey, or a person. Photojournalists use images to report news and bring distant events closer to the public. Family albums preserve memories for future generations. [5]

## 选项

A. Photography is both an art and a tool—it lets us document our lives and express our view of the world in ways that words alone cannot.
B. Good photography is more about seeing and practice than about having the latest or most expensive camera.
C. From portraits to landscapes, photography offers countless ways to explore the world through a lens and share what you see with others.
D. Learning to edit well while staying true to your subject helps you create photos that are both beautiful and trustworthy.
E. Whether you photograph for fun, for art, or for history, every picture you take has the power to capture a moment and give it meaning that can last far beyond the click of the shutter.
F. For example, the first permanent photograph was taken in 1826 by Joseph Nicéphore Niépce, and it required an exposure time of several hours to capture a single image.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前说摄影定格瞬间、用相机或手机记录生日假期日常、助记忆与分享；空后需总结摄影作为艺术与工具的双重本质。A的"both an art and a tool"与前文 freeze、capture、remember、share 呼应，"document our lives and express our view"与"ways that words alone cannot"对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说不必昂贵器材——手机即可、免费 app 可编辑、光构图时机更重要、光线主体时机决定效果；空后需总结好照片更依赖观察与练习而非器材。B的"more about seeing and practice than about having the latest or most expensive camera"与前文"basics matter more than the gear"和"pay attention to"对应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前列举摄影类型——人像、风光、街拍、野生动物，可探索不同风格找兴趣；空后需总结摄影提供多种探索与分享的方式。C的"From portraits to landscapes"与前文 genres 对应，"explore the world through a lens and share what you see"与"find what interests you most"呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说后期——裁剪改善构图、调光对比色彩、需保持真实、避免过度修图误导、追求自然增强；空后需总结学会编辑且忠于主体的意义。D的"Learning to edit well while staying true to your subject"与前文呼应，"both beautiful and trustworthy"与"natural-looking"和"enhance rather than replace reality"对应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说摄影可叙事——组图讲故事、新闻摄影报道、家庭相册传承；空后需总结无论目的如何每张照片的力量。E的"Whether you photograph for fun, for art, or for history"与前文多种用途对应，"capture a moment and give it meaning that can last far beyond"与开篇 freeze、keep forever 呼应，是合理收尾。
