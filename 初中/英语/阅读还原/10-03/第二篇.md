# 10-03 第二篇

**The Ancient Silk Road**

Over two thousand years ago, a network of trade routes connected China with Central Asia, the Middle East, and Europe. This network, known as the Silk Road, allowed goods, ideas, and cultures to travel across vast distances. Silk, spices, tea, and countless other products were traded along these routes. [1] As a result, cities and towns along the way grew rich, and people from different lands learned about each other's customs and beliefs.

The Silk Road got its name from Chinese silk, which was one of the most valuable and sought-after goods in the ancient world. However, many other items were also traded, including jade, glass, and precious metals. Merchants traveled in caravans, often facing harsh deserts and high mountains. [2] Yet the journey was worth the risk, as traders could earn great wealth by bringing rare goods from one region to another.

Besides goods, the Silk Road also carried knowledge and ideas. Buddhism spread from India to China along these routes, while papermaking and other technologies traveled west. Different cultures influenced one another through contact and exchange. [3] Today, historians study these exchanges to understand how ideas and technologies spread across the ancient world.

The Silk Road declined over time as sea routes became safer and faster. Yet its legacy remains important. Today, many countries are working together to revive the spirit of the Silk Road through new trade and infrastructure projects. [4] These efforts show that the dream of connecting distant lands and fostering mutual prosperity is as relevant now as it was two thousand years ago.

Studying the Silk Road helps us understand how different peoples have always been linked through trade and cultural exchange. It reminds us that cooperation and openness can bring benefits to all. [5] In an increasingly globalized world, the lessons of the Silk Road—connection, exchange, and mutual respect—continue to guide us toward a more peaceful and prosperous future.

## 选项

A. The Silk Road was far more than a trade route—it was a bridge between civilizations that shaped the history and culture of many nations for centuries.
B. Despite the dangers, trade flourished because the profits were high and both sides benefited from exchanging goods they could not produce at home.
C. In this way, the Silk Road helped create a more interconnected world long before the age of globalization we know today.
D. The Belt and Road Initiative is one modern example of how the idea of connecting East and West continues to inspire cooperation and development across regions.
E. The story of the Silk Road is a reminder that, throughout history, the exchange of goods and ideas has often been the key to peace, prosperity, and progress among nations.
F. For example, Marco Polo traveled along the Silk Road in the 13th century and wrote about his experiences in China.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述丝绸之路促进商品与文化交流，空后说"As a result, cities and towns along the way grew rich, and people... learned about each other's customs and beliefs"。A的"bridge between civilizations that shaped the history and culture"与空后城镇繁荣、相互学习呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说商队面临沙漠与高山等危险，空后说"Yet the journey was worth the risk, as traders could earn great wealth"。B的"Despite the dangers, trade flourished because the profits were high"与空后"worth the risk"及"earn great wealth"直接对应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说丝绸之路传播佛教、造纸术等，空后说"Today, historians study these exchanges to understand how ideas and technologies spread"。C的"helped create a more interconnected world"概括前文，"long before globalization"与空后研究古代传播呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说多国正通过新项目复兴丝绸之路精神，空后说"These efforts show that the dream of connecting distant lands... is as relevant now"。D的"Belt and Road Initiative"为现代例证，"connecting East and West continues to inspire"与空后"dream of connecting distant lands"衔接。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说研究丝绸之路提醒我们合作与开放带来益处，空后说"In an increasingly globalized world, the lessons of the Silk Road... continue to guide us"。E的"exchange of goods and ideas has often been the key to peace, prosperity, and progress"与空后"lessons... connection, exchange, and mutual respect"呼应。
