**Animal Migration: Nature's Great Journeys**

Every year, millions of animals travel long distances across land, sea, and sky. Birds fly from cold regions to warmer ones; whales swim thousands of miles to breeding grounds; monarch butterflies journey from Canada to Mexico. These seasonal movements are called migration. Animals migrate to find food, escape harsh weather, or reach safe places to raise their young. Migration is one of the most amazing behaviors in the natural world. [1] Through migration, animals follow ancient routes that have been passed down through generations, ensuring their survival in a changing environment.

Scientists are still learning how animals know where to go. Some birds use the position of the sun and stars to navigate. Others seem to sense Earth's magnetic field, like a built-in compass. Salmon are thought to use the smell of their home river to find their way back. Young animals often migrate with experienced adults who have made the trip before. [2] No matter which method they use, migrating animals rely on a mix of instinct, learned behavior, and environmental clues to complete their journeys successfully.

Migration is full of dangers. Predators may attack along the way. Bad weather can blow birds off course or make seas too rough for fish. Human activities—such as building cities, roads, and dams—often block migration paths. Light pollution can confuse birds that travel at night. [3] Despite these challenges, many species continue to migrate each year because the benefits of reaching their destination outweigh the risks of the journey.

Protecting migration routes is important for conservation. When a key stopover site is destroyed, entire populations can suffer. Governments and organizations work to create wildlife corridors—protected areas that connect habitats—so animals can move safely. Some countries have signed agreements to protect migratory species that cross borders. [4] By safeguarding migration routes, we help ensure that future generations will still witness these extraordinary natural events.

Migration reminds us that the natural world is deeply connected. A bird that breeds in the Arctic may spend the winter in South America; a river in one country may be essential for fish that spawn there. Understanding and protecting migration helps us see how our actions affect wildlife far beyond our own neighborhoods. [5] In the end, protecting migratory animals means protecting the health of ecosystems across the entire planet.

## 选项

A. Through migration, animals follow ancient routes that have been passed down through generations, ensuring their survival in a changing environment.
B. No matter which method they use, migrating animals rely on a mix of instinct, learned behavior, and environmental clues to complete their journeys successfully.
C. Despite these challenges, many species continue to migrate each year because the benefits of reaching their destination outweigh the risks of the journey.
D. By safeguarding migration routes, we help ensure that future generations will still witness these extraordinary natural events.
E. In the end, protecting migratory animals means protecting the health of ecosystems across the entire planet.
F. For instance, the Arctic tern flies from the North Pole to the South Pole and back each year, covering about 70,000 kilometers in total.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍动物迁徙现象——鸟类、鲸鱼、帝王蝶等的长距离移动，以及迁徙目的（觅食、避寒、繁育），空后需升华迁徙的意义。A的"ancient routes that have been passed down through generations"与前文世代延续的迁徙行为对应，"ensuring their survival in a changing environment"概括迁徙的生态功能。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前列举动物导航方式——太阳星辰、地磁场、气味、跟随成体等，空后需总结多种方式的共同点。B的"No matter which method they use"与前文多种导航方式呼应，"a mix of instinct, learned behavior, and environmental clues"概括本能、学习和环境线索的三类因素。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前描述迁徙风险——天敌、恶劣天气、人类活动、光污染等，空后需解释动物仍坚持迁徙的原因。C的"Despite these challenges"承接前文困难，"the benefits of reaching their destination outweigh the risks"点明迁徙的动力所在。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说明保护迁徙路线的措施——野生动物廊道、跨国协议等，空后需总结保护的意义。D的"By safeguarding migration routes"与前文保护举措对应，"future generations will still witness these extraordinary natural events"展望保护带来的长远效果。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前强调迁徙体现的自然连通性——北极鸟类南下、跨国河流对鱼类的重要性，空后需升华保护 migratory animals 的全局意义。E的"protecting migratory animals means protecting the health of ecosystems across the entire planet"点明保护迁徙动物即保护全球生态。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For instance, the Arctic tern flies from the North Pole to the South Pole and back each year, covering about 70,000 kilometers in total.**：以For instance开头，为具体物种迁徙案例（北极燕鸥）。各空均为概括或过渡句；[1]易被误选因涉及鸟类迁徙，但该空需总结迁徙的世代传承与生存意义，而非单一物种的迁徙数据。
