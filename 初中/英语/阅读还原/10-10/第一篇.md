# 10-10 第一篇

**World Food Day: Ending Hunger Together**

World Food Day, celebrated on October 16 each year, reminds us that millions of people still go to bed hungry. While some countries have plenty of food, others face shortages, malnutrition, and food waste. The day was created to raise awareness and to encourage action toward a world where everyone has enough to eat. [1] Without enough nutritious food, children cannot grow well, students struggle to learn, and adults lack the energy to work and care for their families.

Hunger has many causes. Poverty, conflict, and climate change all play a role. In some regions, farmers lose their crops to droughts or floods. In others, wars destroy farmland and disrupt supply chains. [2] Understanding these causes helps governments, charities, and communities design better programs to provide food aid, support local farmers, and improve access to markets.

Food waste is another major issue. A large amount of food is thrown away each year—in homes, restaurants, and stores—while others have nothing to eat. Reducing waste can help feed more people without growing more crops. [3] Simple steps such as buying only what we need, storing food properly, and donating extra to food banks can cut waste and make a real difference in our communities.

Schools and families can take part in World Food Day by learning about where food comes from and how to reduce waste. They can also support food drives, volunteer at soup kitchens, or grow vegetables in school gardens. [4] When young people understand the problem and get involved, they are more likely to carry these habits and values into adulthood and to become part of the solution.

Ending hunger will require cooperation from everyone—governments, businesses, farmers, and citizens. World Food Day is a chance to reflect on what we eat, how we use it, and what we can do to help others. [5] Every small action, from saving food at home to supporting policies that feed the hungry, brings the world a step closer to a future where no one has to worry about their next meal.

## 选项

A. Food security is not just about having food on the table—it affects health, education, and the ability of people to build better lives for themselves and their communities.
B. Once we know why people go hungry, we can work on solutions such as improving farming methods, building stronger food systems, and making sure help reaches those who need it most.
C. Changing our habits at home and in our communities is one of the most direct ways we can contribute to feeding everyone and protecting the planet.
D. Educating the next generation about food, hunger, and sustainability ensures that the fight against hunger continues long after World Food Day is over.
E. Together, we can move toward a world where everyone has access to safe, healthy, and enough food—and where World Food Day celebrates progress, not just a dream.
F. For example, in Japan, many schools teach students to appreciate each grain of rice and to clean their plates as a way to reduce waste and respect farmers.
