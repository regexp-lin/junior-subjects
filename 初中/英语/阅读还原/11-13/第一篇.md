# 11-13 第一篇

**Curiosity and Invention: How Questions Change the World**

Curiosity drives human progress. When people ask "Why?" or "What if?" they open the door to new ideas and discoveries. Many of the world's greatest inventions—from the wheel to the smartphone—came from someone who wondered how to solve a problem or do something better. [1]

Inventors often face failure before success. Thomas Edison tried thousands of materials before finding the right one for the light bulb. The Wright brothers crashed many times before their first successful flight. These stories remind us that failure is part of the process. Each "wrong" attempt teaches something new and brings us closer to the answer. [2]

Today, anyone can be an inventor. You do not need a fancy lab or lots of money. Simple materials like cardboard, rubber bands, and recycled parts can lead to creative projects. Schools and community centers sometimes host invention clubs or maker spaces. Online platforms offer tutorials and ideas for DIY projects. [3]

Inventions often solve real problems. Some address health, like medical devices that save lives. Others help the environment, like machines that clean ocean plastic. Still others make daily life easier, like apps that help people manage their time. The best inventions often come from noticing a need and thinking about how to meet it. [4]

The spirit of invention is alive in every generation. Young people today are building robots, designing apps, and experimenting with new technologies. Schools encourage project-based learning and creative thinking. The challenges we face—climate change, disease, inequality—will need new ideas and new solutions. [5]

## 选项

A. Curiosity is the spark that has led to countless inventions and improvements, shaping the way we live today.
B. Learning from failure and refusing to give up are essential qualities for anyone who wants to turn an idea into reality.
C. With curiosity, creativity, and a willingness to try, anyone can explore the joy of invention, regardless of age or background.
D. Great inventions often begin with a simple observation: someone sees a problem and decides to find a solution.
E. The future belongs to the curious, the creative, and the persistent—those who dare to ask questions and keep trying until they find answers.
F. For example, the printing press invented by Johannes Gutenberg in the 15th century made it possible to produce books quickly and helped spread knowledge across Europe.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前说好奇心推动进步——问"为什么""如果怎样"带来新想法与发现，从轮子到智能手机的发明都源于好奇；空后需总结好奇心在发明中的核心作用。A的"Curiosity is the spark"与前文"Curiosity drives"呼应，"countless inventions and improvements"与"greatest inventions"对应，"shaping the way we live today"与前文"door to new ideas"对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前举例爱迪生与莱特兄弟——多次失败后成功，强调失败是过程的一部分、每次错误带来新知；空后需总结从失败中学习与坚持的意义。B的"Learning from failure and refusing to give up"与前文呼应，"essential qualities for anyone who wants to turn an idea into reality"与"inventors"和"success"对应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说人人可发明——无需昂贵实验室、简单材料即可、学校社区有发明俱乐部、网络有教程；空后需总结好奇、创造与尝试带来的普适可能。C的"With curiosity, creativity, and a willingness to try"与前文呼应，"anyone can explore the joy of invention"与"anyone can be an inventor"对应，"regardless of age or background"与"simple materials"对应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说发明解决实际问题——医疗、环保、日常便利，最佳发明常源于发现需求并思考如何满足；空后需总结观察与解决问题的起点。D的"Great inventions often begin with a simple observation"与前文"noticing a need"呼应，"someone sees a problem and decides to find a solution"与"real problems"和"how to meet it"对应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说发明精神代代相传——年轻人做机器人设计 app、学校鼓励项目学习、未来挑战需要新想法；空后需总结未来属于谁。E的"The future belongs to the curious, the creative, and the persistent"与前文"spirit of invention"和"new ideas"呼应，"dare to ask questions and keep trying"与开篇"ask Why"和"Learning from failure"对应，是合理收尾。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the printing press invented by Johannes Gutenberg in the 15th century made it possible to produce books quickly and helped spread knowledge across Europe.**：以 For example 开头，为印刷术的具体历史案例。各空均为概括或过渡句；[1] 易被误选因涉及发明影响，但该空需概括好奇心与发明的普遍关系，而非某一发明的具体历史举例。
