**Folk Tales: Stories Passed Down Through Time**

Folk tales are stories that have been told and retold for generations. They were usually passed down by word of mouth before being written down. Different cultures have their own folk tales, but many share common themes: good against evil, clever heroes, and lessons about life. These stories help people understand their history, values, and the world around them. [1]

Characters in folk tales are often simple but memorable. There might be a kind young person who helps others, a clever animal that outwits stronger enemies, or a greedy villain who gets what they deserve. Sometimes the hero is small or weak but wins through wisdom or courage. These characters teach us what to admire and what to avoid. [2]

Folk tales often include magical or impossible events. Animals may talk, objects may come to life, and ordinary people may receive special gifts from supernatural beings. These elements make the stories exciting and help listeners imagine different worlds. The magic is not random—it usually rewards the good and punishes the wrong. [3]

Today, folk tales are still enjoyed in many forms. They appear in books, films, and cartoons. Parents read them to children at bedtime. Writers and artists reuse the same stories in new ways. Each retelling may add local details or modern twists, but the core messages often remain the same. [4]

Folk tales connect the past with the present. They remind us of shared human experiences—hope, fear, love, and the desire for justice. By keeping these stories alive, we preserve the wisdom and imagination of generations before us and pass them on to the next. [5]

## 选项

A. In this way, folk tales serve as a bridge between cultures and between the past and the present.
B. Through these characters, folk tales show us the values and morals that societies hold dear.
C. Such elements make folk tales more than entertainment—they carry deeper meanings.
D. Thanks to this, folk tales continue to inspire and entertain people of all ages.
E. That is why folk tales remain an important part of our cultural heritage.
F. For example, the story of Cinderella appears in many cultures with different names and details.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍民间故事代代相传、口耳相传后成文字、不同文化各有特色但共享主题（善恶、聪明英雄、人生教训）、帮助理解历史价值观与世界，空后需总结民间故事的跨文化与跨时代桥梁作用。A的"bridge between cultures and between the past and the present"与前文对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说人物简单鲜明——善良助人的年轻人、聪明弱小的动物、贪婪恶棍、弱小英雄凭智慧勇气获胜，空后需总结人物所承载的价值与道德。B的"Through these characters"指代前文，"values and morals that societies hold dear"与前文对应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说民间故事常有奇幻元素——动物会说话、物品活过来、凡人获神赐、使故事刺激并激发想象、魔法常惩恶扬善，空后需总结奇幻元素的意义。C的"Such elements"指代前文，"more than entertainment—they carry deeper meanings"与前文呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说民间故事仍以多种形式存在——书籍电影动画、睡前亲子共读、作家艺术家再创作、重述可加本地或现代元素但核心不变，空后需总结持续的影响力。D的"Thanks to this"承接前文，"continue to inspire and entertain people of all ages"与前文对应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说民间故事连结古今、唤起共同人性（希望恐惧爱与正义）、传承前代智慧与想象，空后需总结文化传承的价值。E的"That is why folk tales remain an important part of our cultural heritage"与前文呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the story of Cinderella appears in many cultures...**：以For example开头，为具体故事案例（灰姑娘跨文化版本）。各空均为概括或过渡句；[1]易被误选因涉及跨文化，但该空需总结民间故事的桥梁功能，而非单一故事的跨文化举例。
