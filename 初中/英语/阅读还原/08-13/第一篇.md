# 08-13 第一篇

**The Development of Electric Vehicles**

Cars have changed the way we live, but they have also caused serious environmental problems. Air pollution from gasoline engines harms our health and the planet. For decades, scientists and engineers have looked for cleaner alternatives. [1] Electric vehicles, or EVs, have emerged as one of the most promising solutions to reduce emissions and create a more sustainable future for transportation.

The history of electric cars goes back more than 100 years. In the early 1900s, electric cars were actually more popular than gasoline cars in some cities. [2] However, the invention of the electric starter and cheap gasoline made gas cars easier to use and more affordable. Electric cars nearly disappeared for most of the 20th century. It was not until the 1990s that interest in electric vehicles began to grow again, driven by environmental concerns.

Modern electric cars use powerful batteries to store energy. These batteries have improved greatly in recent years. [3] They can now travel over 300 miles on a single charge, and charging times have been reduced significantly. Battery technology continues to advance, making electric cars more practical for everyday use. Many drivers find that an electric car meets all their travel needs.

Governments around the world are encouraging the switch to electric vehicles. Some countries offer tax breaks and subsidies to buyers. [4] Cities are building more charging stations to make it easier for drivers to power their cars. In some places, electric car owners can use special lanes or park for free. These policies aim to speed up the transition to cleaner transportation.

The future of electric vehicles looks bright. [5] As technology improves and prices drop, more people will be able to afford and choose electric cars. This will lead to cleaner air, less noise, and a healthier planet. The road ahead is long, but electric vehicles are clearly driving us toward a better tomorrow.

## 选项

A. Today's batteries are much more efficient than those of the past.
B. They represent a major shift in how we think about personal transportation.
C. Public support is also growing through various government programs.
D. The path forward depends on both innovation and wider adoption.
E. Several factors caused electric cars to lose their early advantage.
F. For example, Tesla became famous for making stylish and fast electric cars.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] B   [2] E   [3] A   [4] C   [5] D

## 深度解析

- **[1] B**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍燃油车污染背景及寻找清洁替代品，空后"Electric vehicles, or EVs, have emerged..."。B项"They represent a major shift in how we think about personal transportation"承上启下，既呼应"alternatives"，又为后文EV登场做概括性铺垫。

- **[2] E**：
  - **线索**：第二段讲电动车早期历史，空前"electric cars were actually more popular"，空后"electric starter and cheap gasoline made gas cars easier...Electric cars nearly disappeared"。E项"Several factors caused electric cars to lose their early advantage"完美承接"曾更流行"与"为何衰落"，引出后文具体原因。

- **[3] A**：
  - **线索**：第三段主题是现代电动车电池，空前"These batteries have improved greatly"，空后"They can now travel over 300 miles...charging times have been reduced"。A项"Today's batteries are much more efficient than those of the past"总结电池进步，与后文具体数据呼应。

- **[4] C**：
  - **线索**：第四段讲政府鼓励措施，空前"tax breaks and subsidies"，空后"Cities are building more charging stations...special lanes or park for free"。C项"Public support is also growing through various government programs"概括多种政策支持，与前后列举的举措一致。

- **[5] D**：
  - **线索**：结尾段"The future of electric vehicles looks bright"，空前总结前景，空后"As technology improves and prices drop...cleaner air, less noise"。D项"The path forward depends on both innovation and wider adoption"升华主题，指出未来依赖技术革新与普及，自然引出具体展望。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, Tesla became famous for making stylish and fast electric cars**：F为品牌举例，与"电动车发展历程/技术/政策"的论述层次不符。若放[2]会打断历史因果；放[3]与电池技术段落脱节；放[4]与政策支持无关，易造成误选。
