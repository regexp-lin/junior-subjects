# 08-13 第二篇

**Learning to Forgive**

Holding a grudge can feel like carrying a heavy bag everywhere you go. When someone hurts us, anger and bitterness can grow inside. These negative feelings often hurt us more than the person who wronged us. [1] Forgiving others is not about letting them off the hook—it is about freeing ourselves from the weight of resentment and moving forward with a lighter heart.

Forgiveness does not mean pretending that the hurt never happened. We can acknowledge that something wrong was done. [2] The key is to choose not to let that past event control our present feelings and actions. We may never forget what happened, but we can decide to stop replaying it in our minds. This choice gives us back our peace and energy for more positive things.

Some people worry that forgiving makes them seem weak. In fact, the opposite is true. [3] It takes great strength to let go of anger and choose peace instead. Forgiving someone who has hurt us deeply is one of the bravest things we can do. It does not mean we approve of what they did; it means we refuse to let their actions define our emotional life.

Forgiveness can improve our physical and mental health. Research shows that people who hold grudges often have higher stress levels and more health problems. [4] Those who practice forgiveness report better sleep, less anxiety, and stronger relationships. Letting go of anger can lower blood pressure and improve our overall well-being. The benefits extend to both our body and mind.

Forgiveness is a skill that can be learned and practiced. [5] Start small—perhaps forgive a friend for a small mistake or let go of a minor annoyance. With practice, forgiveness becomes easier. Remember that forgiving is a gift we give ourselves, not just the other person. It opens the door to a happier and more peaceful life.

## 选项

A. Science has found that forgiveness brings real health benefits.
B. Understanding this can help us see forgiveness in a new light.
C. We can release the past without denying that it happened.
D. Real courage lies in choosing forgiveness over revenge.
E. Like any skill, it grows stronger the more we use it.
F. For example, some cultures have traditions of public apology and reconciliation.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] B   [2] C   [3] D   [4] A   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] B**：
  - **线索**：空前描述怨恨的负面后果，空后"Forgiving others is not about letting them off the hook—it is about freeing ourselves"。B项"Understanding this can help us see forgiveness in a new light"承上启下，从前文"怨恨之重"过渡到对"原谅"的新理解，与后文"定义"呼应。

- **[2] C**：
  - **线索**：第二段"Forgiveness does not mean pretending that the hurt never happened"，空前承认伤害存在，空后"The key is to choose not to let that past event control..."。C项"We can release the past without denying that it happened"精准概括：不否认伤害，但可放下，与前后逻辑一致。

- **[3] D**：
  - **线索**：第三段驳斥"forgiving makes them seem weak"，空前"In fact, the opposite is true"，空后"It takes great strength to let go of anger...bravest things"。D项"Real courage lies in choosing forgiveness over revenge"点明原谅需要勇气，与后文"strength""bravest"呼应。

- **[4] A**：
  - **线索**：第四段"Forgiveness can improve our physical and mental health"，空前总起，空后"Research shows...Those who practice forgiveness report..."。A项"Science has found that forgiveness brings real health benefits"引出科学研究，与后文具体研究结果衔接。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：结尾段"Forgiveness is a skill that can be learned and practiced"，空前强调可练习，空后"Start small...With practice, forgiveness becomes easier"。E项"Like any skill, it grows stronger the more we use it"承上启下，从"可学"过渡到"越练越强"，引出具体建议。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, some cultures have traditions of public apology and reconciliation**：F为文化习俗举例，与"个人原谅的心理与健康"主题相关但层次不同。原文五处均为概念阐释或过渡，无插入跨文化比较的位置，易使考生混淆论证主线。
