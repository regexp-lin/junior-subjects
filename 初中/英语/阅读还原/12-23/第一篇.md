# 12-23 第一篇

**Surfing: Riding the Waves**

Surfing has grown from an ancient Polynesian practice into a popular sport and lifestyle enjoyed around the world. The basic idea is simple: use a board to ride waves as they roll toward the shore. But mastering the sport takes years of practice. Surfers must read the ocean—understanding wave patterns, currents, and wind—and time their moves perfectly. The thrill of catching a good wave and the connection with nature keep millions of people coming back to the water. [1]

Surfboards have evolved over time. Traditional wooden boards were heavy and hard to maneuver. Modern boards are lighter, made of foam and fiberglass, and come in many shapes for different styles of surfing. Longboards are stable and good for beginners; shortboards are nimble and suited for tricks. Wetsuits help surfers stay warm in cold water, while sunscreen and awareness of weather conditions protect against sun and storms. [2]

Surfing culture values respect for the ocean and for other surfers. There are unwritten rules: the person closest to the peak of the wave has priority, and beginners should not crowd experienced surfers on difficult breaks. Pollution and overdevelopment of coastlines threaten the clean waves and beaches that surfers depend on. Many surfers take part in beach cleanups and campaigns to protect marine environments. [3]

Learning to surf usually starts with practicing on the sand—"pop-ups" to stand on the board—and then moving to small, gentle waves in shallow water. Instructors can teach balance, paddling, and safety. Wiping out, or falling off the board, is part of the learning process. Every surfer falls many times before finding their balance. Patience and persistence are key. [4]

Whether you surf for competition, exercise, or the sheer joy of being on the water, the sport offers a unique blend of athletic skill, natural beauty, and community. The ocean is the real teacher—humble and endless. [5]

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍冲浪从波利尼西亚古老习俗发展为全球流行运动、基本概念简单、但精通需多年练习、需解读海洋、冲浪的刺激与自然联结吸引众人，空后需总结冲浪运动与海洋欣赏的结合。A的"combines the excitement of sport with a deep appreciation for the power and beauty of the sea"与前文呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说冲浪板演变、传统木板沉重难操控、现代板更轻、泡沫与玻璃纤维、多种形状适应不同风格、长板稳定适合新手、短板灵活适合技巧、潜服防晒注意天气，空后需总结装备的作用。B的"The right equipment makes learning easier and helps surfers stay safe and comfortable"与前文对应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说冲浪文化重视尊重海洋与他人、有不成立规矩、污染与过度开发威胁清洁海浪海滩、许多冲浪者参与净滩与保护海洋，空后需总结社区的保护努力。C的"For this reason"承接前文，"many in the surfing community work to protect the oceans and coastlines"与前文呼应。

- **[4] D**：

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说无论为竞赛健身或纯粹水上之乐、冲浪提供运动技能自然美与社区的独特组合、海洋才是真正的老师，空后需总结冲浪的本质。E的"less about conquering waves and more about learning from them"与前文呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
