**Children's Rights: Every Child Matters**

Children have the right to be safe, healthy, educated, and heard. This idea is at the heart of the United Nations Convention on the Rights of the Child, a treaty signed by almost every country in the world. The convention states that children should be protected from harm, have access to education and healthcare, and be able to express their views on matters that affect them. These rights apply to every child, regardless of where they live or who they are. [1]

Yet millions of children still face serious challenges. Some are forced to work instead of going to school. Others lack clean water, enough food, or a safe place to live. Conflict and disaster displace families, leaving children vulnerable. Bullying, both online and offline, harms many young people. Ensuring that every child can enjoy their rights requires action from governments, communities, families, and each of us. [2]

Schools play a key role. Education does more than teach reading and math—it helps children develop critical thinking, confidence, and the skills to protect themselves. Schools can also be places where children learn about their rights and how to stand up for them. When students feel safe and respected, they are more likely to speak up and participate. Teachers and parents can model respect and listen to children's opinions. [3]

Young people themselves are powerful advocates. Around the world, children and teenagers have led campaigns for climate action, against bullying, and for better education. When given the chance, they show that they can think deeply about issues and propose creative solutions. Supporting youth voice means creating spaces where children can share ideas and be taken seriously. [4]

Children's rights are not a luxury—they are the foundation for a fair and peaceful world. When we protect and empower the youngest among us, we build a better future for everyone. [5]

## 选项

A. The convention reflects a global agreement that children deserve dignity, protection, and opportunity.
B. Addressing these gaps demands cooperation at every level—from international aid to local community efforts.
C. In this way, schools become more than places of learning; they become environments where rights are practiced and respected.
D. These young activists remind us that children are not passive; they have voices that deserve to be heard and heeded.
E. Investing in children today is an investment in the world we will inherit tomorrow.
F. For example, Malala Yousafzai became a global symbol of children's right to education after surviving an attack for speaking out about girls' schooling in Pakistan.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前说儿童有权安全健康受教育被倾听、联合国儿童权利公约核心、条约规定保护免受伤害、获得教育医疗、可表达观点、权利适用于每个儿童，空后需总结公约的全球共识。A的"The convention reflects a global agreement that children deserve dignity, protection, and opportunity"与前文呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说仍有数百万儿童面临严重挑战、被迫工作无法上学、缺乏洁净水食物或安全住所、冲突灾害使家庭流离、网络与现实霸凌、保障权利需政府社区家庭与个人行动，空后需总结弥补差距所需的合作。B的"Addressing these gaps demands cooperation at every level"与前文对应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说学校起关键作用、教育不只教读写算、帮助发展批判思维自信与自护技能、学校可成为学习权利与维护权利之地、学生感到安全受尊重更愿发声参与、师长可示范尊重倾听，空后需总结学校作为实践权利的场所。C的"In this way"承接前文，"schools become more than places of learning; they become environments where rights are practiced"与前文呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说年轻人自身是有力倡导者、全球青少年领导气候行动反霸凌更好教育的运动、有机会时展现出深度思考与创造性方案、支持青年声音需创造可分享想法并被认真对待的空间，空后需总结青年行动者的启示。D的"These young activists remind us"指代前文，"children have voices that deserve to be heard and heeded"与前文对应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说儿童权利不是奢侈、是公平和平世界的基础、保护并赋能最年幼者即为所有人建设更好未来，空后需总结投资儿童的意义。E的"Investing in children today is an investment in the world we will inherit tomorrow"与前文呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, Malala Yousafzai became...**：以For example开头，为具体儿童权利倡导者案例。各空均为概括或过渡句；[4]易被误选因涉及青年活动家，但该空需总结青年作为行动者的启示，而非补充某人的具体故事细节。
