# 08-24 第一篇

**Passing Down Traditional Festivals**

Traditional festivals have shaped cultures for thousands of years. They bring families together, remind us of our history, and give meaning to the passing of seasons. Yet in the modern world, many young people know little about the stories and customs behind these celebrations. Commercial activities and fast-paced life often overshadow the deeper significance of festivals. [1] Keeping these traditions alive requires both understanding their roots and finding ways to make them relevant today. Only then can we pass them on to future generations.

Every festival has a story. The Mid-Autumn Festival in China celebrates family reunion and the harvest. Thanksgiving in America honors gratitude and sharing. Diwali in India symbolizes the victory of light over darkness. [2] These stories connect us to our ancestors and teach values that still matter. When we tell these stories to children, we help them understand who they are and where they come from. The meanings behind the festivals are as important as the food and fun.

Tradition does not mean doing everything exactly as our grandparents did. The world has changed, and so have our lives. [3] We can adapt customs to fit modern life while keeping their core meaning. For example, families who cannot gather in person can celebrate online. New foods can be added to old festivals as long as the spirit of sharing remains. Flexibility allows traditions to survive and thrive in a new era.

Schools and communities play an important role in preservation. [4] Classes about festival history, craft activities, and community celebrations help young people experience traditions firsthand. When children make lanterns for the Lantern Festival or learn songs for the Spring Festival, they form memories that last a lifetime. These experiences create a bridge between the past and the future.

[5] The responsibility to carry on traditions lies with all of us. Parents can share stories and involve children in holiday preparations. Young people can ask elders about their childhood memories of festivals. When we value our heritage and make it part of our daily life, we ensure that these precious traditions do not fade away. They remind us of our shared humanity and the wisdom of those who came before us.

## 选项

A. Preserving traditions demands understanding their origins and connecting them to present-day life.
B. The stories behind festivals link us to our heritage and pass on lasting values.
C. Traditions can be updated to suit modern life without losing their essential spirit.

E. Everyone has a part to play in keeping traditions alive for the generations to come.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前指出年轻人不了解节日背后的故事和习俗，商业与快节奏生活掩盖了深层意义；空后强调“只有这样才能传给后代”。A项“传承传统需要理解其根源并将之与当代生活联系起来”承上概括问题，启下点出传承条件，起承上启下作用。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前举例中秋、感恩节、排灯节等节日故事；空后讲“这些故事把我们与祖先相连，并传承仍有价值的观念”。B项“节日背后的故事将我们与 heritage 相连并传承持久价值”准确概括本段主旨，与前后一致。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前讲传统不等于完全照搬祖辈做法，世界和生活已改变；空后举例无法当面团聚可线上庆祝、可加入新食物但保留分享精神。C项“传统可以在保持核心意义的同时适应现代生活”概括段内“灵活适应”的主题。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前指出学校和社区在传承中的角色；空后写节日历史课、手工活动、社区庆祝活动，以及做灯笼、学歌曲等亲身经历。D项“教育和亲身经历帮助下一代学习和欣赏传统”概括本段内容。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前为第四段；空后具体写家长分享故事、让孩子参与准备，年轻人向长辈询问节日回忆。E项“每个人都有责任让传统延续给后代”作为结尾主题句，总结全文并呼应“我们所有人”的责任。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, making dumplings during the Spring Festival is a beloved custom in many Chinese families.**：以For example开头，属单一习俗举例。虽与传统节日相关，但停留在具体细节层面，无法承担[1]–[5]的概括或递进作用；易误选为[2]或[3]。
