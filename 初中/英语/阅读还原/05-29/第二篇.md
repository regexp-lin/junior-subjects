**School Lunch Nutrition: Why What Students Eat Matters**

Many students eat lunch at school every day. What they eat affects their energy, focus, and health. A balanced lunch helps students learn better and stay active in the afternoon. Yet not all school lunches are equally nutritious（有营养的）. [1] That is because schools play a key role in teaching healthy eating habits. When schools serve vegetables, whole grains（全谷物）, and lean protein（瘦肉蛋白）, students learn what a healthy meal looks like. Good school lunches can reduce obesity（肥胖）and improve concentration. Poor lunches—full of fried food and sugary drinks—can lead to tiredness and poor grades. In short, school lunch is not just about filling stomachs. It is about building healthy bodies and minds. Schools have a responsibility to offer nutritious choices.

What makes a nutritious school lunch? [2] In other words, it should include a mix of food groups. A balanced meal has vegetables and fruits for vitamins and fiber. It has whole grains like brown rice or whole-wheat bread for energy. It includes protein—such as chicken, fish, beans, or eggs—for muscle growth. It limits added sugar, salt, and unhealthy fats. Many countries have set standards for school meals. For example, some require a certain amount of vegetables per meal. Others ban sugary drinks and limit fried foods. The goal is to give students the nutrients they need without too much junk food. A good lunch supports both learning and long-term health.

Why do some students choose unhealthy options? [3] That is why taste, habit, and peer pressure all play a role. Many students grew up eating fast food and prefer familiar flavors. Some think healthy food tastes boring. Others choose what their friends are eating. Schools can help by making healthy food more appealing—for example, serving colorful salads, tasty grilled chicken, or fruit smoothies. Education about nutrition also matters. When students understand why certain foods are good for them, they are more likely to choose them. For this reason, school lunch programs should combine good food with good teaching. Changing habits takes time, but it is possible.

How can parents support healthy school lunches? [4] It is true that parents can pack nutritious lunches and talk to school leaders. If you pack your child's lunch, include fruits, vegetables, and protein. Avoid too many cookies, chips, or sugary drinks. Parents can also join school committees that decide lunch menus. By voicing their opinions, parents can push for healthier options in the cafeteria（食堂）. Some schools have started "farm to school" programs—bringing fresh local food into the kitchen. When parents and schools work together, students benefit. Good nutrition is a team effort. Every meal is a chance to support a child's growth.

[5] It affects learning, health, and habits for life. Whether schools improve their menus or parents pack better lunches, the goal is the same: give students the food they need to thrive. So the next time you sit down for school lunch, think about what you are eating—your body and brain will thank you. Remember: healthy eating today leads to a healthier future tomorrow.
