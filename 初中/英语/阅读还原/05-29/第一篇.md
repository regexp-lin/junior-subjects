**Trees and Urban Cooling: How Green Spaces Lower City Temperatures**

Cities are often hotter than the countryside. Buildings, roads, and cars trap heat and create "urban heat islands（城市热岛）"—areas that stay warm even at night. This can make summer uncomfortable and increase the use of air conditioners. Yet trees can help cool cities down. [1] That is because trees provide shade and release water through their leaves. When sunlight hits a tree, the leaves block some of the heat. Trees also absorb（吸收）heat and release moisture（水分）into the air through a process called transpiration（蒸腾作用）. This process cools the air around them. Studies show that a single tree can cool the air as much as 10 air conditioners running for 20 hours. In short, planting more trees is one of the best ways to make cities cooler and more livable. Green spaces are essential for urban comfort.

Where should cities plant trees? [2] In other words, trees work best along streets, in parks, and near buildings. Street trees shade sidewalks and reduce the heat absorbed by roads. Parks create large cool zones where people can relax. Trees near buildings can lower the need for air conditioning by shading windows and walls. Some cities have started "green roofs"（绿色屋顶）—planting grass or small trees on top of buildings. The key is to plant trees where people live, work, and travel. A well-planned tree can lower the temperature by several degrees in its immediate area. Urban planners are learning that every tree counts.

What kind of trees work best in cities? [3] That is why cities need trees that can survive pollution, drought（干旱）, and limited space. Not all trees grow well in urban conditions. Some trees have deep roots that can damage sidewalks or pipes. Others need a lot of water or cannot handle car exhaust（废气）. Cities often choose native species—trees that already grow naturally in the area. These trees are used to the local climate and need less care. For this reason, urban forestry（城市林业）requires careful planning. The right tree in the right place can cool a neighborhood for decades. Scientists and city planners work together to pick the best species for each location.

How can people help? [4] It is true that everyone can support urban trees. You can water young trees during dry summers. You can join community planting events. You can also protect existing trees by not damaging their bark（树皮）or roots. Schools often organize tree-planting days where students learn about the benefits of green spaces. When more people care about urban trees, cities become cooler and healthier. The future of our cities depends on the trees we plant today. Small actions add up to big change. Together, we can turn our cities into cooler, greener places to live.

[5] In conclusion, trees are powerful allies in the fight against urban heat. They provide shade, cool the air, and make cities more enjoyable. Whether you plant a tree, water one, or simply enjoy the shade, you are part of the solution. So the next time you walk under a tree on a hot day, remember: that tree is working hard to keep you cool. Let us plant more trees and build cooler, greener cities for everyone.

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：总领树木降温的原理与作用。空前描述"Cities are often hotter than the countryside...urban heat islands...Yet trees can help cool cities down"，空后说明"When sunlight hits a tree, the leaves block some of the heat...Trees also absorb heat and release moisture into the air through transpiration...a single tree can cool the air as much as 10 air conditioners"。A选项"That is because trees provide shade and release water through their leaves"（那是因为树木提供荫蔽并通过叶片释放水分）中"That is because"解释降温机制，"shade and release water"与空后block heat、release moisture、transpiration精准对应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：树木的最佳种植位置。空前设问"Where should cities plant trees?"，空后列举"Street trees shade sidewalks...Parks create large cool zones...Trees near buildings can lower the need for air conditioning...Some cities have started 'green roofs'...plant trees where people live, work, and travel"。B选项"In other words, trees work best along streets, in parks, and near buildings"（换言之，树木最适合种在街道旁、公园里和建筑附近）中"In other words"概括空后内容，"along streets, in parks, and near buildings"与空后Street trees、Parks、Trees near buildings完美呼应，衔接自然。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：适合城市生长的树木特点——耐污染、耐旱、适应用地有限。空前设问"What kind of trees work best in cities?"，空后详述"Not all trees grow well in urban conditions...Some trees have deep roots that can damage sidewalks...Others need a lot of water or cannot handle car exhaust...Cities often choose native species...urban forestry requires careful planning"。C选项"That is why cities need trees that can survive pollution, drought, and limited space"（因此城市需要能耐受污染、干旱和有限空间的树木）中"That is why"承接空前及空后结论，"survive pollution, drought, and limited space"与空后urban conditions、car exhaust、native species精准对应，逻辑紧密。

  - **线索**：普通人如何参与支持城市树木。空前设问"How can people help?"，空后说明"You can water young trees during dry summers...You can join community planting events...You can also protect existing trees...Schools often organize tree-planting days...When more people care about urban trees, cities become cooler and healthier"。D选项"It is true that everyone can support urban trees"（的确，人人都可以支持城市树木）中"It is true that"引出参与方式，"everyone can support"与空后water、join、protect完美呼应，衔接得当。
