# 05-23 第一篇

**Traditional Chinese Architecture: Beauty in Wood and Stone**

Traditional Chinese architecture has a history of thousands of years. From palaces and temples to ordinary homes, these buildings show a unique style that is different from the West. [1] In fact, they often use wood as the main material, with stone for foundations and bases. The roofs slope gently and curve upward at the edges—a design that is not only beautiful but also helps rain and snow slide off easily. That is why traditional Chinese buildings blend function with art. They were built to last, and many ancient structures still stand today in places like Beijing, Xi'an, and Suzhou.

What makes the structure special? [2] Traditional buildings use a system of wooden beams（梁）and brackets（斗拱）that fit together without nails. Craftsmen could take apart and rebuild whole buildings when needed. The brackets, called dougong（斗拱）, support the heavy roof and spread the weight evenly. In other words, the design is both strong and flexible. It allowed buildings to survive earthquakes better than some rigid structures. This clever system has fascinated engineers and architects around the world for centuries.

Symbolism plays an important role. [3] Colours, shapes, and patterns in traditional buildings often carry meaning. Red stands for luck and joy; yellow was reserved for the emperor. Dragon and phoenix（凤凰）patterns appear on important buildings. The number of rooms and the direction buildings face also follow traditional rules. For this reason, a walk through an ancient Chinese courtyard（庭院）is not just a look at old buildings—it is a journey into Chinese culture and beliefs. Every detail tells a story.

How are these buildings being protected today? [4] Many old buildings face damage from weather, pollution, and ageing. Experts work to repair them using traditional methods and materials. Some historic areas have been turned into museums or cultural sites for visitors to learn from. It is true that preserving traditional architecture takes effort and money. But it is worth it—these buildings are a treasure for future generations. They connect us to the past and teach us about the skills and wisdom of ancient craftsmen.

[5] In short, traditional Chinese architecture is a precious part of world heritage. It combines beauty, strength, and deep cultural meaning. Whether you visit the Forbidden City, a small temple, or a water-town house in the south, you will see the same attention to balance and harmony. Remember: these buildings were made by hand, with care and patience. So next time you see a traditional Chinese roof or courtyard, take a moment to admire the craft—and the centuries of wisdom behind it.

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：总领中国传统建筑的材料与造型特点。空前描述"Traditional Chinese architecture has a history of thousands of years...these buildings show a unique style"，空后说明"In fact, they often use wood as the main material, with stone for foundations...The roofs slope gently and curve upward at the edges—a design that is not only beautiful but also helps rain and snow slide off easily...traditional Chinese buildings blend function with art"。A选项"That is because traditional Chinese buildings mix practical design with artistic beauty"（那是因为中国传统建筑将实用设计与艺术美感相结合）中"That is because"解释建筑特色，"mix practical design with artistic beauty"与空后beautiful、helps rain slide off、blend function with art精准对应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：木结构体系的承重与灵活性。空前设问"What makes the structure special?"，空后列举"Traditional buildings use a system of wooden beams and brackets that fit together without nails...The brackets, called dougong, support the heavy roof and spread the weight evenly...the design is both strong and flexible...It allowed buildings to survive earthquakes better"。B选项"In other words, the wooden structure is both strong and adaptable"（换言之，木结构既坚固又灵活）中"In other words"概括空后内容，"strong and adaptable"与空后strong、flexible、survive earthquakes完美呼应，衔接自然。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：建筑中的象征意义。空前提出"Symbolism plays an important role"，空后详述"Colours, shapes, and patterns in traditional buildings often carry meaning...Red stands for luck and joy; yellow was reserved for the emperor...Dragon and phoenix patterns appear on important buildings...a walk through an ancient Chinese courtyard is not just a look at old buildings—it is a journey into Chinese culture and beliefs"。C选项"That is why every element in these buildings carries cultural meaning"（因此这些建筑中的每个元素都承载着文化含义）中"That is why"承接空前及空后结论，"every element carries cultural meaning"与空后colours、shapes、patterns、meaning、culture and beliefs精准对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：传统建筑的保护现状与挑战。空前设问"How are these buildings being protected today?"，空后详述"Many old buildings face damage from weather, pollution, and ageing...Experts work to repair them using traditional methods...Some historic areas have been turned into museums...preserving traditional architecture takes effort and money...it is worth it"。D选项"It is true that preserving traditional architecture requires ongoing effort"（的确，保护传统建筑需要持续努力）中"It is true that"加强衔接，"ongoing effort"与空后repair、museums、takes effort and money完美呼应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与世界遗产价值。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"traditional Chinese architecture is a precious part of world heritage...It combines beauty, strength, and deep cultural meaning...Whether you visit the Forbidden City...you will see the same attention to balance and harmony...these buildings were made by hand, with care and patience"。E选项"In conclusion, traditional Chinese architecture is a valuable world heritage"（总之，中国传统建筑是宝贵的世界遗产）用"In conclusion"总结全文，"valuable world heritage"与空后precious part of world heritage形成呼应，收束有力。
