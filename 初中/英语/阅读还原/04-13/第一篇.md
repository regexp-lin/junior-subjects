# 04-13 第一篇

**Earthquake Safety: What You Need to Know**

Earthquakes can happen at any time, often without warning. Knowing what to do before, during, and after an earthquake can make a big difference and may even save your life. [1] Panic makes it harder to think clearly and can lead to dangerous choices. By learning and practising simple safety steps, you can protect yourself and your family when the ground starts to shake.

Before an earthquake happens, preparation is key. Make sure your home has a disaster kit (应急包) with water, food, a flashlight, and a first-aid kit. [2] Decide with your family where you will meet if you are separated and how you will contact each other. Practice "Drop, Cover, and Hold On" with your family so everyone knows what to do. These steps take only a few minutes, but they can keep you safe when seconds count.

When an earthquake hits, do not run outside. Running can cause you to fall, and objects may fall from buildings. [3] Get under a strong desk or table, cover your head and neck with your arms, and hold on until the shaking stops. If there is no desk nearby, sit against an interior wall away from windows and mirrors. Stay low and protect your head. This position reduces the chance of being hurt by falling objects.

After the shaking stops, be careful. There may be aftershocks (余震)—smaller earthquakes that follow the main one. [4] Check yourself and others for injuries before you move. Look for hazards such as broken glass, fallen objects, or damaged electrical lines. If you are indoors and the building seems damaged, leave slowly and use stairs instead of elevators. Stay away from beaches if you live near the sea, as tsunamis (海啸) can follow large earthquakes.

[5] Earthquakes are a natural part of our planet's activity, but with the right knowledge and preparation, we can stay safe. Remember: prepare early, stay calm when it happens, and take care after it ends. Share these tips with your family and friends—safety is everyone's responsibility.

## 选项

A. Instead, stay where you are and find cover immediately.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] F   [2] E   [3] A   [4] B   [5] D

## 深度解析

- **[1] F**：
  - **线索**：开篇过渡与核心原则引出。空前介绍"knowing what to do before, during, and after an earthquake can make a big difference"，空后指出"Panic makes it harder...By learning and practising simple safety steps, you can protect yourself"。F选项"Whether at home or at school, the key is to stay calm and follow the safety steps you have learned"（无论在家还是在学校，关键是保持冷静并遵循所学安全步骤）中"stay calm"与空后"Panic makes it harder"形成对比衔接，"follow the safety steps you have learned"与空后"learning and practising simple safety steps"对应，自然引入核心原则。

- **[2] E**：
  - **线索**：震前准备与家庭计划。空前提出"Make sure your home has a disaster kit..."并指出需与家人共同准备，空后"Decide with your family where you will meet...Practice 'Drop, Cover, and Hold On' with your family"。E选项"Create a family emergency plan that everyone understands"（制定一份全家人都能理解的应急计划）中"family emergency plan"与空后"Decide with your family...Practice with your family"形成总分关系，"everyone understands"与"everyone knows what to do"呼应，衔接顺畅。

- **[3] A**：
  - **线索**：震时行动与就地避险。空前明确"do not run outside...Running can cause you to fall"，空后详细说明"Get under a strong desk or table, cover your head and neck...hold on until the shaking stops"。A选项"Instead, stay where you are and find cover immediately"（相反，留在原地并立即寻找掩护）中"Instead"直接回应"do not run"的否定， "stay where you are"与"run outside"形成对比，"find cover immediately"自然引出空后"Get under a desk...cover your head"的具体操作，逻辑紧密。

- **[4] B**：
  - **线索**：震后检查与安全排查。空前提到"be careful...There may be aftershocks"，空后列举"Check yourself and others for injuries...Look for hazards...broken glass, fallen objects...If the building seems damaged, leave slowly"。B选项"First, check that everyone is safe and look for dangers around you"（首先，确认大家是否安全并查看周围的危险）中"First"引出震后首要步骤，"check everyone is safe"与空后"Check yourself and others for injuries"对应，"look for dangers"与"Look for hazards"完美契合，衔接自然。

- **[5] D**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"prepare early, stay calm when it happens, and take care after it ends...Share these tips...safety is everyone's responsibility"。D选项"In brief, being prepared and knowing what to do can greatly reduce the risk during an earthquake"（简言之，做好准备并知道该做什么能大大降低地震中的风险）用"In brief"总结全文，"being prepared"与"prepare early"呼应，"knowing what to do"与"stay calm...take care"及"Share these tips"形成呼应，收束得当。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **C. Planning and practising together can help everyone feel ready.**：强调计划与演练，易被误填[2]。但空[2]空前已提出"Make sure your home has a disaster kit"，空后需引出"Decide with your family where you will meet...Practice 'Drop, Cover, and Hold On'"等具体计划内容；E的"Create a family emergency plan that everyone understands"直接给出行动指令并与空后"Decide...Practice...everyone knows what to do"紧密衔接，C的"Planning and practising"更偏概括，缺乏"family plan"等关键词。属"主题相关但过于笼统"型干扰。
