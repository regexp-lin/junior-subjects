**The Advantages of Public Transport**

In busy cities around the world, traffic jams and air pollution have become serious problems. Many people wonder whether driving a car is still the best way to travel. Public transport—buses, trains, subways, and trams—offers a different choice. [1] Choosing public transport brings benefits not only for individuals but also for the whole community. Here are some key reasons to consider leaving the car at home.

First, public transport is much kinder to the environment. [2] Cars produce harmful gases that pollute the air and contribute to climate change. A single bus can carry dozens of people, replacing many private cars on the road. Trains and subways run on electricity, which can come from clean sources. By taking the bus or train, each person reduces their carbon footprint. Cities with good public transport often have cleaner air and fewer traffic problems.

Public transport also saves money. [3] Owning a car is expensive: you pay for fuel, insurance, parking, and repairs. A monthly bus or train pass usually costs much less. Students and elderly people often get discounts. Over a year, a family can save thousands of dollars by using public transport. The money saved can be used for other important things like education or travel. For many people, public transport is the most affordable option.

Another advantage is less stress. [4] When you take the bus or train, you do not have to worry about finding parking or getting stuck in traffic. You can read, listen to music, or even take a nap during the journey. Studies show that people who commute by public transport report lower stress levels than drivers. Your travel time becomes useful time instead of wasted time. This can improve both your mood and your productivity.

Public transport is more than just a way to get from A to B. [5] It helps us build greener, healthier, and more connected communities. The next time you plan a trip, consider taking the bus or train. Small changes in our travel habits can lead to big improvements in our cities and our lives. Together, we can create a better future for everyone.

## 选项

A. These benefits extend to both personal life and society as a whole.
B. It significantly reduces air pollution and greenhouse gas emissions.
C. It offers a cost-effective alternative to car ownership.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前引出公共交通这一选择，空后总领全文"Here are some key reasons"，A项"这些益处惠及个人生活与社会整体"承上启下，点明选择公共交通的双重意义，与后文分述环保、省钱、减压等维度对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：本段主题为环保，空后具体说明汽车排放有害气体、巴士替代多辆私家车、减少碳足迹等，B项"显著减少空气污染和温室气体排放"精准概括段意。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：本段围绕"save money"，空后列举养车成本（燃油、保险、停车、维修）与公交月票对比，以及学生、老人折扣，C项"提供相对于拥有汽车而言更省钱的选择"准确对应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：本段讲"less stress"，空后描述不必找停车位、堵车，可阅读听音乐、通勤者压力更低，D项"让乘客有效利用通勤时间并减少焦虑"与段内论据一致。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：结尾段总结并号召行动，空前总述公共交通不止是出行工具，空后强调"greener, healthier, connected communities""better future"，E项"在建设可持续、宜居城市中发挥关键作用"呼应总结。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, some cities have built bike-sharing programs alongside buses.**：以 For example 开头，主题与公共交通相关，但 bike-sharing 属另一类出行方式，与文章按公交、省钱、减压的论述层次不符，且与各空前后衔接不紧密，为干扰项。
