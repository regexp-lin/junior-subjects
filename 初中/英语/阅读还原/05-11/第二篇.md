**The Value of Urban Parks: Green Spaces in the City**

In busy cities full of buildings and traffic, parks offer a breath of fresh air. [1] In fact, urban parks are not just nice to look at—they provide real benefits for both people and the environment. Parks give city dwellers a place to exercise, relax, and spend time with family and friends. Children can run and play on the grass, while adults enjoy walking paths and quiet benches. Trees and plants in parks help clean the air by absorbing pollution and producing oxygen. That is why cities around the world are investing in creating and protecting green spaces.

Parks promote physical health. [2] When people have easy access to a park, they are more likely to be physically active. Jogging, cycling, tai chi, and team sports all take place in urban parks. Regular exercise reduces the risk of heart disease, obesity, and other health problems. For families who live in small apartments, parks provide space that they may not have at home. In other words, parks act as outdoor gyms that are free for everyone to use.

Parks also support mental wellbeing. [3] Spending time in nature has been shown to reduce stress and improve mood. After a long day at work or school, a walk in the park can help people feel calmer and more refreshed. Some studies suggest that people who live near parks report higher levels of happiness and life satisfaction. For this reason, urban planners consider parks essential for the quality of life in cities.

Parks bring communities together. [4] Parks are neutral spaces where people from different backgrounds can meet. Community events such as concerts, festivals, and outdoor movie nights often take place in parks. Neighbors who might never have talked to each other can start a conversation while walking their dogs or watching their children play. However, not all neighborhoods have equal access to parks—some areas have lush（茂盛的） green spaces while others have almost none. Cities are working to fix this imbalance so that everyone can enjoy the benefits of nature.

[5] In short, urban parks are valuable assets that make cities more livable. They improve our health, lift our spirits, and strengthen our communities. So the next time you need a break from the noise and rush of city life, consider visiting a nearby park. You might be surprised by how much better you feel.

## 选项

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：总领城市公园的多重价值。空前描述"In busy cities full of buildings and traffic, parks offer a breath of fresh air"，空后列举"In fact, urban parks are not just nice to look at—they provide real benefits for both people and the environment...give city dwellers a place to exercise, relax, spend time...Children can run and play...adults enjoy walking paths and quiet benches...Trees and plants help clean the air...cities around the world are investing in creating and protecting green spaces"。A选项"That is because parks serve multiple important functions"（那是因为公园发挥多种重要作用）中"That is because"解释空前"breath of fresh air"的深层意义，"multiple important functions"与空后锻炼、放松、社交、净化空气等多重功能精准对应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：公园对身体健康的促进。空前提出"Parks promote physical health"，空后说明"When people have easy access to a park, they are more likely to be physically active...Jogging, cycling, tai chi, team sports...Regular exercise reduces the risk of heart disease, obesity...parks provide space...parks act as outdoor gyms that are free for everyone to use"。B选项"In other words, parks encourage people to stay active and healthy"（换言之，公园鼓励人们保持活跃和健康）中"In other words"概括空前"promote physical health"，"stay active and healthy"与空后"physically active"及"outdoor gyms"完美呼应，衔接自然。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：公园对心理健康的支持。空前提出"Parks also support mental wellbeing"，空后描述"Spending time in nature has been shown to reduce stress and improve mood...walk in the park can help people feel calmer and more refreshed...people who live near parks report higher levels of happiness...urban planners consider parks essential for the quality of life"。C选项"For this reason, parks are seen as important for mental health in cities"（因此公园被认为对城市心理健康很重要）中"For this reason"承接空前及空后研究结论，"important for mental health in cities"与空后reduce stress、improve mood、happiness、quality of life形成呼应，衔接得当。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：公园的社区社交功能。空前提出"Parks bring communities together"，空后列举"Parks are neutral spaces where people from different backgrounds can meet...Community events such as concerts, festivals, outdoor movie nights...Neighbors...can start a conversation while walking their dogs or watching their children play...not all neighborhoods have equal access...Cities are working to fix this imbalance"。D选项"It is true that parks also play a social role in bringing people together"（的确，公园在凝聚人们方面也发挥着社交作用）中"It is true that"加强衔接，"social role in bringing people together"与空后neutral spaces、community events、neighbors converse精准对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与行动呼吁。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"urban parks are valuable assets that make cities more livable...improve our health, lift our spirits, strengthen our communities...next time you need a break...consider visiting a nearby park...how much better you feel"。E选项"In conclusion, we should value and protect our urban green spaces"（总之，我们应该珍惜和保护城市绿地）用"In conclusion"总结全文，"value and protect"与空后"valuable assets"及"make cities more livable"形成呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, some parks have special areas for skateboarding and climbing.**：涉及公园内的特定运动设施，易被误填[2]。但F以"For example"引出滑板、攀岩等具体设施，而空[2]前后文讨论的是公园促进身体活动的总体作用（慢跑、骑行、太极、团队运动），空后的"parks act as outdoor gyms"强调的是公园作为免费户外健身空间的整体功能，并非某一类设施；F属于公园设施的具体类型举例，与空[2]的概括性论述层级不匹配。F属"主题相关但举例过于具体"型干扰。
