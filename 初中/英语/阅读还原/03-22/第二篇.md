# 03-22 第二篇

**How Volunteering Changes Your Life**

Many teenagers think volunteering is just about helping other people. While that is certainly true, volunteering actually gives back much more than you put in. [1] The benefits are so wide-ranging that experts now recommend it as one of the best activities for personal growth.

First, volunteering helps you develop important life skills. When you serve food at a homeless shelter or organize a charity event, you learn teamwork, communication, and leadership. [2] Employers and universities look very favorably upon candidates who have spent their free time helping their community.

Second, volunteering connects you with people you would never normally meet. You might work alongside retired teachers, college students, or people from completely different backgrounds. [3] These new perspectives can open your mind and help you see the world in fresh, exciting ways.

Perhaps most importantly, volunteering has a powerful effect on your mental health. Studies show that people who regularly help others feel happier and more satisfied with their own lives. [4] The simple act of making someone else smile can instantly lift your own spirits and reduce feelings of loneliness.

[5] Whether it is picking up trash in a park or reading stories to younger children, every small act of kindness makes the world a little bit better — and makes you a stronger person.

## 选项

A. This sense of purpose and connection is something that money simply cannot buy.
B. These experiences do not just look great on a resume — they genuinely shape your character.
C. Therefore, teenagers should avoid volunteering because it takes away study time.
D. In short, volunteering is one of the most rewarding things a young person can do.
E. It turns out that helping others is actually one of the best ways to help yourself.
F. Each conversation teaches you something new about life and the human experience.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] E   [2] B   [3] F   [4] A   [5] D

## 深度解析

- **[1] E**：
  - **线索**：语义递进与主题揭示。空前提出了志愿服务"gives back much more than you put in"（回报远多于付出）这一核心论断。E选项"It turns out that helping others is actually one of the best ways to help yourself"（事实证明，帮助别人实际上是帮助自己的最佳方式之一）是对这一论断的精辟总结——帮助别人同时也在帮助自己。空后"The benefits are so wide-ranging that experts now recommend it"（好处如此广泛，以至于专家推荐它）紧承E选项展开"好处到底有多广"的论述。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：升华概括与承上启下。空前列举了志愿服务培养的具体技能——团队合作、沟通和领导力。B选项"These experiences do not just look great on a resume — they genuinely shape your character"（这些经历不仅在简历上好看——它们真正塑造了你的品格）中"These experiences"指代前文的志愿经历。"not just...they genuinely"结构将价值从"表面好看"提升到"深层塑造品格"。空后一句"Employers and universities look very favorably..."则具体说明了简历层面的好处。

- **[3] F**：
  - **线索**：具体化描述与连贯递进。空前指出志愿服务让你接触到平时不会遇到的人——退休教师、大学生、不同背景的人。F选项"Each conversation teaches you something new about life and the human experience"（每一次对话都教会你关于生活和人类经历的新东西）是对"接触不同人群"这一行为的深入阐释——你会从与他们的交流中学到新东西。空后"These new perspectives can open your mind"中的"These new perspectives"正是对F选项"something new"的概括指代。

- **[4] A**：
  - **线索**：因果衔接与情感深化。空前提出了科学证据：经常帮助别人的人更快乐、对生活更满意。A选项"This sense of purpose and connection is something that money simply cannot buy"（这种目的感和归属感是金钱根本买不到的）中的"This sense of purpose and connection"概括了前文的幸福感和满足感，并用"money cannot buy"提升了其独特价值。空后"The simple act of making someone else smile can instantly lift your own spirits"进一步解释了这种幸福感的来源。

- **[5] D**：
  - **线索**：总结全篇与号召行动。空[5]位于结尾段段首。前文从技能培养（第二段）、社交拓展（第三段）和心理健康（第四段）三个层面论证了志愿服务的好处。D选项"In short, volunteering is one of the most rewarding things a young person can do"（简而言之，志愿服务是年轻人能做的最有意义的事之一）用"In short"精准收束全文。空后的具体建议"无论是捡垃圾还是读故事"则引导读者付诸行动。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **C. Therefore, teenagers should avoid volunteering because it takes away study time.**：该选项含有因果连词"Therefore（因此）"和"teenagers""volunteering"等文章关键词。但其主张"青少年应该避免志愿服务，因为浪费学习时间"与全文竭力倡导青少年参与志愿服务的正面立场截然相反。这是一个利用话题关键词和因果逻辑词制造迷惑、但核心立场完全对立的典型干扰项。
