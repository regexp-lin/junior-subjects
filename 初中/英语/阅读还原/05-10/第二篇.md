# 05-10 第二篇

**Exercise and Mental Health: Moving for a Happier Mind**

We all know that exercise is good for our bodies, but did you know it is also powerful for our minds? [1] In fact, regular physical activity can reduce feelings of anxiety and depression. When we exercise, our brains release chemicals called endorphins（内啡肽） that make us feel happy and relaxed. Even a short walk or a 20-minute bike ride can improve our mood. That is why many doctors recommend exercise as part of treatment for people who feel stressed or sad.

Exercise helps us cope with stress. [2] When we are stressed, our bodies produce a hormone called cortisol（皮质醇） that can make us feel tired and worried. Exercise helps to lower cortisol levels and gives us a healthy way to release tension. Instead of sitting and worrying, going for a run or playing a sport can clear our minds and help us think more clearly. In other words, moving our bodies is one of the best ways to calm our minds.

For students, exercise can improve academic performance. [3] Research shows that students who are physically active tend to have better focus and memory. Exercise increases blood flow to the brain, which helps us learn and remember new information. Students who play sports or exercise before school often find it easier to concentrate in class. For this reason, many schools have made physical education a required part of the daily schedule.

Some people find it hard to start exercising. [4] They may feel too tired, too busy, or unsure of what to do. The good news is that you do not need to run a marathon to get the benefits. Simple activities like dancing to your favorite music, climbing stairs instead of taking the elevator, or playing with friends in the park all count as exercise. The key is to find something you enjoy so that you will stick with it. However, if you have not exercised for a long time, it is wise to start slowly and increase gradually.

[5] In short, exercise is one of the most effective ways to support both physical and mental health. Whether you prefer team sports, yoga, or a daily walk, getting moving can make a real difference in how you feel. So the next time you feel down or stressed, try putting on your shoes and taking a step outside—your mind will thank you.

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：总领运动对心理的益处。空前描述"We all know that exercise is good for our bodies, but did you know it is also powerful for our minds?"，空后列举"In fact, regular physical activity can reduce feelings of anxiety and depression...our brains release chemicals called endorphins that make us feel happy and relaxed...short walk or 20-minute bike ride can improve our mood...doctors recommend exercise as part of treatment"。A选项"That is because exercise has direct effects on our brain chemistry and emotions"（那是因为运动对我们的大脑化学物质和情绪有直接影响）中"That is because"解释空前"powerful for our minds"，"direct effects on brain chemistry and emotions"与空后endorphins、reduce anxiety and depression、improve mood完美呼应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：运动如何帮助应对压力。空前提出"Exercise helps us cope with stress"，空后说明"When we are stressed, our bodies produce cortisol...Exercise helps to lower cortisol levels...gives us a healthy way to release tension...going for a run or playing a sport can clear our minds and help us think more clearly"。B选项"In other words, physical activity is an effective way to manage stress"（换言之，体育运动是应对压力的有效方式）中"In other words"概括空前"cope with stress"，"effective way to manage stress"与空后lower cortisol、release tension、clear our minds精准对应，衔接自然。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：运动对学生学业表现的帮助。空前提出"For students, exercise can improve academic performance"，空后列举"Research shows that students who are physically active tend to have better focus and memory...Exercise increases blood flow to the brain...Students who play sports or exercise before school often find it easier to concentrate...many schools have made physical education a required part of the daily schedule"。C选项"That is why schools are paying more attention to physical activity"（因此学校越来越重视体育活动）中"That is why"承接空前"improve academic performance"，"schools paying more attention to physical activity"与空后"schools have made physical education a required part"完美呼应，逻辑紧密。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：克服起步困难的建议。空前指出"Some people find it hard to start exercising...They may feel too tired, too busy, or unsure of what to do"，空后说明"The good news is that you do not need to run a marathon...Simple activities like dancing, climbing stairs, playing with friends all count...The key is to find something you enjoy...start slowly and increase gradually"。D选项"It is true that getting started can feel difficult, but small steps work"（的确，开始锻炼可能很难，但小步骤有效）中"It is true that"承认空前困难，"small steps work"与空后"Simple activities...do not need to run a marathon...start slowly"精准对应，衔接得当。

  - **线索**：全文收束与行动鼓励。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"exercise is one of the most effective ways to support both physical and mental health...Whether you prefer team sports, yoga, or a daily walk...getting moving can make a real difference...next time you feel down or stressed, try...taking a step outside"。E选项"In conclusion, we should not forget the mental benefits of staying active"（总之，我们不应忘记保持运动对心理的益处）用"In conclusion"总结全文，"mental benefits of staying active"与空后"physical and mental health"及"make a real difference in how you feel"形成呼应，收束有力。
