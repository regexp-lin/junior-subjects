**Protecting Coral Reefs: A Call for Action**

Coral reefs are often called the "rainforests of the sea" because they support about a quarter of all marine species. These colorful underwater ecosystems provide food, shelter, and breeding grounds for countless fish and other sea creatures. However, coral reefs around the world are facing serious threats. [1] Rising sea temperatures due to climate change cause corals to lose their vibrant colors and eventually die. Scientists and environmental groups are working together to find solutions before it is too late.

One major threat to coral reefs is ocean pollution. [2] When harmful chemicals and plastic waste flow into the ocean, they damage the delicate balance of reef ecosystems. Fertilizers from farms can cause harmful algae blooms that block sunlight from reaching the corals. Simple actions like reducing plastic use and proper waste disposal can make a real difference in protecting these precious habitats.

Another serious problem is overfishing. [3] Removing too many fish from reef areas disrupts the natural food chain and can lead to the growth of seaweed that competes with coral for space. Many countries have established marine protected areas where fishing is limited or banned, allowing fish populations to recover and reefs to heal.

Tourism also has both positive and negative effects on coral reefs. [4] While tourism brings money that can fund conservation efforts, careless snorkelers and boat anchors can break coral branches. Some resorts now educate visitors about reef etiquette and organize cleanup activities, turning tourists into protectors rather than destroyers.

[5] Every individual can contribute to coral reef protection. Choose reef-safe sunscreen, support sustainable seafood, and reduce your carbon footprint. Small changes in our daily lives add up to big benefits for the oceans we all depend on.
