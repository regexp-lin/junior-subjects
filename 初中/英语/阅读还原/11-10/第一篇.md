# 11-10 第一篇

**The Importance of Reading**

Reading opens doors to new worlds. Through books, we can travel to distant places, meet interesting characters, and explore ideas we have never considered before. Reading helps us do better in school—it improves our vocabulary, sharpens our thinking, and builds the skills we need for writing and comprehension. Yet in a world full of videos and games, many young people spend less time with books than earlier generations did. [1] Despite the distractions of modern life, reading remains one of the most valuable habits we can develop, both for academic success and for personal growth throughout our lives.

Reading starts with finding books that interest you. Fiction takes you into imagined worlds and lets you experience different lives; non-fiction teaches you about real topics—science, history, nature, and more. Graphic novels and magazines count too. The goal is to find something you enjoy so that reading feels like a pleasure, not a chore. Once you discover genres or authors you like, it becomes easier to read more. [2] When we read things we care about, we are more likely to stick with it and build a habit that lasts a lifetime, turning reading from a task into a joy.

Reading does not have to happen only at a desk. You can read on the bus, before bed, during a break, or on weekends. Setting aside even 15 or 20 minutes a day can make a difference. Some families make reading a shared activity—parents and children read together or discuss books at mealtime. Libraries offer free access to thousands of books, and many schools have reading programs that encourage students to set goals and track their progress. [3] Making reading a regular part of daily life, in whatever way works for you, is the key to reaping its long-term benefits.

Good readers tend to become better writers. When we read, we see how sentences are built, how stories flow, and how writers express ideas. We absorb new words and phrases naturally. Reading also trains our ability to concentrate—a skill that is increasingly rare in an age of constant notifications. [4] The benefits of reading extend far beyond the pages of a book, improving our communication skills, our focus, and our ability to understand complex ideas in school and beyond.

In the end, reading is about more than grades or skills. It is about curiosity, imagination, and connection. Books connect us to people we have never met and to ideas that have shaped human history. They help us understand ourselves and others better. [5] Cultivating a love of reading is one of the best gifts we can give ourselves, opening minds, expanding horizons, and enriching our lives in ways that last long after we close the book.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前说阅读的作用——打开新世界、旅行、遇见角色、探索想法，提升学业——词汇、思维、写作与理解，同时提到在视频游戏充斥下年轻人阅读时间减少，空后需强调阅读作为习惯的持续价值。A的"Despite the distractions of modern life"与前文"videos and games"和"spend less time"呼应，"most valuable habits... for academic success and for personal growth"与前文学业益处及开篇"opens doors"对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说如何开始阅读——找感兴趣的书，虚构与非虚构、漫画与杂志都可以，目标是让阅读像乐趣而非负担，找到喜欢的类型或作者后更易坚持，空后需总结兴趣与坚持的关系。B的"When we read things we care about"与前文"find something you enjoy"呼应，"stick with it and build a habit that lasts a lifetime"与"easier to read more"对应，"turning reading from a task into a joy"与"feels like a pleasure, not a chore"对应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说阅读可随时随地——公交、睡前、休息、周末，每天15–20分钟亦有帮助，家庭共读、图书馆免费借阅、学校阅读项目，空后需总结将阅读纳入日常的重要性。C的"Making reading a regular part of daily life"与前文各种场景与方式呼应，"in whatever way works for you"与"on the bus, before bed"等对应，"key to reaping its long-term benefits"与"make a difference"对应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说阅读对写作的促进作用——观察句法、叙事、表达，自然吸收词汇，提升专注力以应对信息干扰，空后需总结阅读超越书本的广泛益处。D的"The benefits of reading extend far beyond the pages of a book"与前文写作、词汇、专注呼应，"communication skills, our focus, and our ability to understand complex ideas"与"better writers"和"concentrate"对应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说阅读不止于成绩与技能——关乎好奇心、想象力与联结，书连接我们与陌生人及历史思想，帮助理解自我与他人，空后需总结培养阅读热爱的长远价值。E的"Cultivating a love of reading"与前文"curiosity, imagination, and connection"呼应，"opening minds, expanding horizons, and enriching our lives"与"opens doors to new worlds"和"understand ourselves and others"对应，是合理收尾。
