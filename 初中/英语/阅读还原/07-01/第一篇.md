# 07-01 第一篇

**The Power of Reading**

Reading is one of the most valuable habits a person can develop. Whether it is a novel, a newspaper, or an online article, reading opens doors to new ideas and worlds. [1] In other words, reading not only builds knowledge but also strengthens the mind in ways that few other activities can. From improving vocabulary to sharpening critical thinking, the benefits of reading are both wide and deep. Yet many young people today read less than ever before, often choosing screens over books. This article explores why reading matters and how you can make it part of your daily life.

Reading improves your language skills. [2] The more you read, the more words and sentence patterns you meet. Over time, these become part of your own vocabulary and writing style. Reading exposes you to correct grammar, varied sentence structures, and rich expressions that you might not hear in everyday conversation. Students who read regularly tend to write better essays, speak more clearly, and perform well in language exams. Even reading for just fifteen minutes a day can make a noticeable difference. The key is consistency—a little reading every day is better than a lot once in a while.

Reading develops your thinking ability. [3] When you read a story, you follow characters, predict what will happen next, and draw your own conclusions. When you read non-fiction, you evaluate evidence, compare viewpoints, and form opinions. These mental exercises strengthen your ability to think logically and creatively. Good readers often become good problem-solvers because they are used to processing information and making connections. Reading also teaches patience and focus—skills that are useful in every area of life. In a world full of distractions, the ability to concentrate on a text is a real advantage.

Reading broadens your view of the world. [4] Through books, you can travel to distant countries, experience different cultures, and understand people whose lives are very different from your own. Reading builds empathy—the ability to feel what others feel—because it lets you see the world through someone else's eyes. History books show you how the past shaped the present; science books reveal how nature works; stories help you understand human emotions. The more you read, the more you understand, and the more open-minded you become. Reading is a window to the world that never closes.

[5] Reading is a gift you give yourself. It costs little, takes no special equipment, and can be done anywhere. Start with topics you enjoy—sports, animals, adventure, science—and let your curiosity guide you. Set a small daily goal, like ten or fifteen minutes before bed. Over time, you will find that reading becomes a habit you look forward to. The benefits—better language, sharper thinking, and a wider view of the world—will stay with you for life. Pick up a book today and start the journey.
