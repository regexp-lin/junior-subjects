# 07-09 第一篇

**The Value of Friendship**

In our busy lives, we sometimes forget how important friendships are. We rush from class to class, finish homework, and scroll through our phones. [1] Yet true friendship is one of life's greatest gifts. Real friends stand by us during difficult times and share our joy in happy moments. They make our lives richer and more meaningful in ways we often take for granted.

Friends provide emotional support when we need it most. [2] When you fail an exam or have a fight with your parents, a friend is there to listen. They do not always have solutions, but their presence alone can make you feel less alone. Studies show that teenagers with close friends handle stress better than those who feel isolated. This support helps us grow stronger and more confident.

Friendships also teach us important life skills. [3] Through friendships, we learn to communicate, compromise, and understand different viewpoints. We practice being honest when we disagree and forgiving when someone hurts us. These skills prepare us for all kinds of relationships in the future—at work, in our families, and in our communities.

Good friendships require effort and care. [4] You cannot expect a friendship to grow without investing time in it. This means making time to talk, being there when your friend needs help, and showing that you care about their feelings. Small acts of kindness—a text when they are sad, celebrating their success—strengthen the bond between friends.

[5] In a world that often feels rushed and competitive, friendship reminds us of what truly matters. It teaches us loyalty, kindness, and the joy of connection. No matter how busy we get, it is worth making time for the people who make our lives brighter.

## 选项

A. Still, the benefits of having close friends cannot be overlooked.
B. Having someone to talk to during hard times can make all the difference.
C. The skills we develop in friendship extend far beyond our school years.
D. Like any meaningful relationship, friendship needs attention and investment.
E. All in all, friendship adds irreplaceable value to our lives.
F. For example, many people met their best friends in elementary school.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述忙碌生活中容易忽视友情，空后强调"true friendship is one of life's greatest gifts"并列举朋友的作用。A选项"Still, the benefits of having close friends cannot be overlooked"以"Still"转折，承接前文对友情的忽视，引出后文对友情价值的正面论述，起到承上启下作用。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：第二段主题是朋友提供情感支持，空后具体描述考试失败、与父母争吵时朋友倾听、陪伴的场景，并引用研究说明有朋友的人更能应对压力。B选项"Having someone to talk to during hard times can make all the difference"精准概括本段主旨：困难时期有人可倾诉至关重要。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：第三段论述友谊教会我们生活技能，空后列举沟通、妥协、理解不同观点、诚实、原谅等能力。C选项"The skills we develop in friendship extend far beyond our school years"概括本段：友谊中培养的技能延伸至未来的工作、家庭、社区等各类关系。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：第四段主题为友谊需要投入，空后具体说明要花时间交流、在需要时出现、关心对方感受，并举发短信、庆祝成功等小例子。D选项"Like any meaningful relationship, friendship needs attention and investment"准确概括：友谊像其他重要关系一样需要关注和投入。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：最后一段需总结全文，空前已展开论述，空后归纳友情在快节奏竞争世界中的意义——忠诚、善意、联结的快乐。E选项"All in all, friendship adds irreplaceable value to our lives"是总结句，用"All in all"收束全文，与后文"what truly matters"呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, many people met their best friends in elementary school.**：以"For example"开头，是举例型干扰。虽与友谊相关，但只是"何时结识好友"的单一事实，无法充当某段的主题概括句。若放入[2]或[4]处，与段落论述层次不符——这两段分别讲情感支持和投入付出，而非结识朋友的时机。
