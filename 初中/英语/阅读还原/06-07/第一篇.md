**How Fingerprints Help Solve Crimes**

No two people in the world have the same fingerprints. Even identical twins have different patterns on their fingers. [1] This uniqueness makes fingerprints one of the most reliable tools for identifying people. For over a century, police and scientists have used fingerprints to solve crimes, catch suspects, and protect innocent people from being wrongly accused.

Fingerprints form before birth. [2] The patterns—loops, whorls, and arches—are influenced by genes and by the random movements of a baby's hands in the womb（子宫）. Once formed, they do not change over a person's lifetime, except for temporary damage from cuts or burns. This stability makes them perfect for long-term identification. A fingerprint taken at age five will still match the same person at age eighty.

How do experts collect and compare fingerprints? [3] At a crime scene, investigators use special powder, brushes, or chemical methods to make latent（潜在的） prints visible. They photograph the prints and scan them into a database. Computers can then search millions of records in seconds to find a match. The ridges and valleys on each finger create a unique "map" that can be compared point by point. Even partial prints can sometimes lead to a match if enough detail is preserved.

Fingerprints are not only used in crime fighting. [4] Today, many smartphones use fingerprint sensors to unlock the device. Banks and offices use them for secure access. Airports in some countries use fingerprint scans as part of identity checks. The same feature that helps catch criminals also helps protect our personal data and secure our daily activities.

[5] From ancient clay seals to modern digital databases, fingerprints have remained a powerful way to confirm who we are. As technology improves, fingerprint identification becomes faster and more accurate. It is a simple but remarkable feature of the human body that continues to serve society in countless ways.

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：引出指纹的独特性与识别功能。空前描述"No two people have the same fingerprints...Even identical twins have different patterns"，空后说明"This uniqueness makes fingerprints one of the most reliable tools for identifying people...solve crimes, catch suspects, protect innocent"。A选项"In other words, each person's fingerprints are like a unique ID card that never changes"（换言之，每个人的指纹都像一张永不变更的独特身份证）中"In other words"概括空前独特性，"unique ID card that never changes"与空后"reliable tools for identifying"精准呼应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：指纹形成后保持稳定的原因。空前提出"Fingerprints form before birth"及"patterns—loops, whorls, arches—influenced by genes...random movements in womb"，空后详述"Once formed, they do not change over lifetime...except temporary damage...stability makes them perfect...fingerprint at age five matches at age eighty"。B选项"That is because the patterns are set early and remain stable throughout life"（那是因为图案在早期形成并在一生中保持稳定）中"That is because"解释稳定性，"set early and remain stable throughout life"与空后"form before birth...do not change...age five to eighty"完美对应，逻辑紧密。

  - **线索**：现代技术在指纹比对中的应用。空前提出"How do experts collect and compare fingerprints?"，空后列举"special powder, brushes, chemical methods...photograph...scan into database...Computers search millions of records in seconds...ridges and valleys...unique map...compared point by point...partial prints"。C选项"In fact, modern technology has made fingerprint matching faster and more efficient"（事实上，现代技术使指纹比对更快、更高效）中"In fact"强化技术手段，"faster and more efficient"与空后"search millions in seconds...partial prints"精准对应，衔接自然。

  - **线索**：全文收束与价值总结。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"From ancient clay seals to modern digital databases...powerful way to confirm who we are...technology improves...faster and more accurate...simple but remarkable feature...serves society in countless ways"。E选项"In short, fingerprints are a lasting and valuable tool for identification"（简言之，指纹是一种持久且有价值的身份识别工具）用"In short"总结全文，"lasting and valuable"与空后"ancient to modern...powerful...countless ways"形成呼应，收束精准。
