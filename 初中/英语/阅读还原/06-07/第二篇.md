# 06-07 第二篇

**The Culture of Kites**

Kites have been flying in the sky for over two thousand years. They were first invented in China, and from there they spread to other parts of Asia, Europe, and the rest of the world. [1] In other words, kites are more than toys—they carry deep cultural meaning and have served many purposes throughout history. Today, flying kites remains a popular pastime that connects people across generations and cultures.

In ancient China, kites had practical uses. [2] Soldiers used them to send signals and measure distances on the battlefield. Some kites were large enough to carry a person into the air to observe the enemy. Farmers used kites to scare birds away from their crops. Over time, kites also became part of festivals and celebrations. During the Qingming Festival（清明节）, people fly kites to remember ancestors and to wish for good luck. Letting go of the string is said to send bad luck away with the wind.

Kites come in countless shapes and styles. [3] Traditional Chinese kites often feature dragons, butterflies, or birds, each symbolizing different meanings—the dragon for power, the butterfly for joy. Japanese kites are known for their bold colours and warrior designs. In Thailand, large kites are used in competitive kite-fighting games. Making a kite by hand—cutting bamboo, painting paper, and tying the frame—is still a respected craft in many communities. The design and materials reflect the culture and creativity of the maker.

Flying kites offers benefits beyond fun. [4] It gets people outdoors and encourages physical activity. It requires patience and skill to read the wind and control the string. Families and friends often fly kites together, strengthening bonds. In some schools, kite-making is used to teach physics—students learn about lift, drag, and the forces that keep a kite in the air. A simple kite can connect science, art, and tradition in one activity.

[5] From ancient battlefields to modern parks, kites have stayed with us because they combine play, art, and meaning. Whether you fly a store-bought kite or build your own, you are taking part in a tradition that spans centuries. So next time the wind picks up, why not go outside and let a kite carry your hopes a little higher?

## 选项

A. In other words, kites have played many roles beyond simple entertainment.
B. That is because they were useful tools for communication, warfare, and farming.
C. In fact, kite design and style vary widely and often reflect local culture.
D. These benefits show that kite-flying is both healthy and educational.
E. In short, kites remain a living link between the past and the present.
F. For example, Benjamin Franklin used a kite to study electricity in the 18th century.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：引出风筝的多重角色与意义。空前描述"flying for over two thousand years...first invented in China...spread to Asia, Europe, world"，空后说明"more than toys—carry deep cultural meaning...served many purposes throughout history...popular pastime...connects people across generations and cultures"。A选项"In other words, kites have played many roles beyond simple entertainment"（换言之，风筝扮演了远超单纯娱乐的多种角色）中"In other words"概括空前历史与传播，"many roles beyond entertainment"与空后"cultural meaning...many purposes"精准呼应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：古代风筝的实际用途。空前提出"In ancient China, kites had practical uses"，空后列举"Soldiers used them to send signals and measure distances...large enough to carry person...observe enemy...Farmers used kites to scare birds...part of festivals...Qingming Festival"。B选项"That is because they were useful tools for communication, warfare, and farming"（那是因为它们是通讯、军事和农业的有用工具）中"That is because"解释实际用途，"communication, warfare, and farming"与空后信号、测距、观察敌人、驱鸟等完美对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：风筝设计与风格的多样性及其文化反映。空前提出"Kites come in countless shapes and styles"，空后详述"Traditional Chinese kites...dragons, butterflies, birds...Japanese kites...bold colours, warrior designs...Thailand...kite-fighting...Making by hand...respected craft...design and materials reflect culture and creativity"。C选项"In fact, kite design and style vary widely and often reflect local culture"（事实上，风筝的设计和风格差异很大，往往反映当地文化）中"In fact"强化多样性，"vary widely...reflect local culture"与空后中、日、泰等国不同风格精准对应，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：放风筝的健康与教育益处。空前提出"Flying kites offers benefits beyond fun"，空后描述"gets people outdoors...physical activity...patience and skill...read wind, control string...Families fly together...strengthening bonds...schools teach physics...lift, drag, forces...connect science, art, tradition"。D选项"These benefits show that kite-flying is both healthy and educational"（这些益处表明放风筝既健康又有教育意义）中"These benefits"指代空后户外、体力、耐心、家庭纽带、物理教学等，"healthy and educational"与空后内容完美对应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与古今联结。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"From ancient battlefields to modern parks...stayed with us...combine play, art, and meaning...tradition that spans centuries...let a kite carry your hopes higher"。E选项"In short, kites remain a living link between the past and the present"（简言之，风筝仍是连接过去与现在的生动纽带）用"In short"总结全文，"living link between past and present"与空后"ancient to modern...tradition spans centuries"形成呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, Benjamin Franklin used a kite to study electricity in the 18th century.**：涉及富兰克林用风筝研究电，易被误填[2]或[4]。但F以"For example"引出科学实验，而空[2]讨论的是中国古代风筝的军事、农业用途，与18世纪美国科学实验的时代和地域不符；空[4]讨论的是放风筝的户外活动、家庭纽带、物理教学等日常益处，富兰克林的实验属于高风险科学探索，与"benefits beyond fun"中的健康、教育侧重点不同。F属"主题相关但语境错位"型干扰。
