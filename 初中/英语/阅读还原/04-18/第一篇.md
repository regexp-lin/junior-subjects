# 04-18 第一篇

**Managing Your Emotions: A Skill for Life**

Everyone feels angry, sad, or worried sometimes. These emotions are normal and part of being human. [1] However, how we deal with them makes a big difference in our daily life. Some people shout or say mean things when they are upset. Others learn to take a deep breath and think before they act. Learning to manage emotions helps us get along better with family and friends, and it also makes us feel calmer and happier inside.

One helpful way to deal with strong feelings is to name them. [2] Saying "I feel angry" or "I feel disappointed" can help us understand what is going on inside. When we put our feelings into words, we begin to see them more clearly. This simple step often makes the emotion feel less powerful and easier to handle. Many students find that writing in a journal（日记） before bed helps them let go of worries from the day.

Another useful skill is to pause before reacting. When something makes us angry, our first impulse（冲动） may be to react right away. [3] But if we wait a few seconds, count to ten, or step away for a moment, we give ourselves time to choose a better response. This "pause" can prevent arguments and help us say what we really mean instead of saying something we might regret later. Teachers and parents often remind kids to "think before you speak" for this reason.

It is also important to find healthy ways to release（释放） stress. [4] Playing sports, listening to music, or talking to a trusted friend can all help us feel better. These activities do not make our problems go away, but they give our minds and bodies a chance to relax. When we feel calmer, we are often able to solve problems more easily. Staying up late or avoiding difficult feelings usually only makes things worse in the long run.

[5] In short, emotional management is not about hiding our feelings or pretending we never get upset. It is about understanding them, choosing how we respond, and taking care of ourselves in healthy ways. With practice, anyone can become better at handling emotions—and that is a skill that will help for the rest of your life.

## 选项

A. Emotions themselves are not good or bad; it is our actions that matter.
B. Experts suggest that labeling our feelings is the first step to controlling them.
C. A short break can help us avoid saying or doing things we will later wish we had not.
D. Regular exercise and hobbies can act as outlets for built-up tension.
E. In other words, managing emotions is a skill that grows with practice and patience.
F. To summarize, learning to manage emotions means learning to live better with ourselves and others.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] F

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：情绪本质与应对态度的总领。空前指出"Everyone feels angry, sad, or worried sometimes...These emotions are normal"，空后对比"how we deal with them makes a big difference...Some people shout...Others learn to take a deep breath"。A选项"Emotions themselves are not good or bad; it is our actions that matter"（情绪本身无好坏之分，重要的是我们的行动）中"emotions are not good or bad"与空前"normal"呼应，"our actions that matter"与空后"how we deal with them"精准对应，自然引出两种不同应对方式，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：命名情绪法与专家建议。空前提出"One helpful way...is to name them"，空后举例"Saying 'I feel angry' or 'I feel disappointed'...put our feelings into words...see them more clearly"。B选项"Experts suggest that labeling our feelings is the first step to controlling them"（专家建议，给我们的感受贴上标签是控制它们的第一步）中"labeling our feelings"与空后"name them...Saying 'I feel angry'"对应，"first step"与空前"one helpful way"呼应，衔接自然。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：暂停反应与避免冲动后果。空前描述"first impulse may be to react right away"，空后详述"wait a few seconds...count to ten...step away...choose a better response...prevent arguments...say what we really mean instead of...something we might regret later"。C选项"A short break can help us avoid saying or doing things we will later wish we had not"（短暂停顿能帮助我们避免说出或做出事后会后悔的事）中"short break"与空后"wait...step away"对应，"avoid saying or doing things we will later wish we had not"与空后"regret later"完美呼应，逻辑紧密。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：健康释放压力的方式。空前提出"find healthy ways to release stress"，空后列举"Playing sports, listening to music, or talking to a trusted friend...give our minds and bodies a chance to relax"。D选项"Regular exercise and hobbies can act as outlets for built-up tension"（规律运动和爱好可作为累积压力的出口）中"exercise and hobbies"与空后"sports...music...talking"对应，"outlets for built-up tension"与空前"release stress"呼应，衔接顺畅。

- **[5] F**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"not about hiding...about understanding...choosing how we respond...With practice, anyone can become better...skill that will help for the rest of your life"。F选项"To summarize, learning to manage emotions means learning to live better with ourselves and others"（总之，学会管理情绪意味着学会与自己及他人更好地相处）用"To summarize"总结全文，"live better with ourselves and others"与空后主题升华对应，收束得当。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **E. In other words, managing emotions is a skill that grows with practice and patience.**：与主题相关，易被误填[5]。但E用"In other words"表示换种说法、解释前文，通常用于同义重述而非全文总结；空[5]后已是全文最后结论，需段首总结句。F的"To summarize"为典型总结标记，"live better with ourselves and others"与空后"helps for the rest of your life"形成升华。E属"语义相关但功能错位"型干扰。
