# 05-13 第一篇

**Preventing School Bullying: Building a Safe Environment for Every Student**

Bullying in schools is a serious problem that affects millions of students every year. It can happen in the classroom, in the hallway, or even online. [1] In fact, bullying hurts not only the victims but also the bullies themselves and everyone who witnesses it. Students who are bullied may feel sad, afraid, or unable to focus on their studies. Bullies often carry their behavior into adulthood if no one helps them change. That is why schools and families must work together to stop bullying before it causes lasting harm.

Understanding what bullying looks like is the first step. [2] Bullying can be physical, such as pushing or hitting. It can also be verbal, like teasing or name-calling. Nowadays, cyberbullying（网络欺凌）—sending hurtful messages online—has become common. Sometimes bullying is hidden, such as leaving someone out on purpose or spreading rumors. In other words, bullying takes many forms, and we need to recognize all of them to prevent it.

Schools play a key role in prevention. [3] Many schools have set up anti-bullying rules and trained teachers to notice warning signs. Some schools use peer support programs where older students help younger ones. Students are encouraged to report bullying to a trusted adult instead of staying silent. When everyone knows the rules and what to do, bullying becomes harder to continue. For this reason, a clear policy and a caring environment can make a real difference.

What can students do to help? [4] If you see someone being bullied, do not join in or laugh. Stand up for the victim if it is safe to do so, or find an adult to help. If you are being bullied, remember that it is not your fault and you deserve respect. Talk to a parent, teacher, or friend you trust. Nobody should face bullying alone. So every student has a part to play in creating a bully-free school.

[5] In short, preventing school bullying requires effort from everyone—students, teachers, and parents. By learning to recognize bullying, following school rules, and supporting each other, we can create a safer and kinder place for all. Remember: one kind word or action can change someone's day, or even their life.

## 选项

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：引出欺凌的广泛负面影响。空前描述"Bullying in schools is a serious problem...It can happen in the classroom, in the hallway, or even online"，空后说明"In fact, bullying hurts not only the victims but also the bullies themselves and everyone who witnesses it...Students who are bullied may feel sad, afraid, or unable to focus...Bullies often carry their behavior into adulthood if no one helps them change...schools and families must work together to stop bullying before it causes lasting harm"。A选项"That is because the effects of bullying go far beyond the moment it happens"（那是因为欺凌的影响远超发生的那一刻）中"That is because"解释为何欺凌是严重问题，"effects go far beyond the moment"与空后受害者、欺凌者、旁观者三方受损及持久伤害精准对应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：总领欺凌的多种表现形式。空前提出"Understanding what bullying looks like is the first step"，空后列举"Bullying can be physical, such as pushing or hitting...can also be verbal, like teasing or name-calling...cyberbullying—sending hurtful messages online—has become common...Sometimes bullying is hidden, such as leaving someone out on purpose or spreading rumors...bullying takes many forms, and we need to recognize all of them"。B选项"In other words, we must learn to identify different types of bullying"（换言之，我们必须学会识别不同类型的欺凌）中"In other words"概括空前"understanding what bullying looks like"，"identify different types"与空后身体、言语、网络、隐蔽等多种形式完美呼应，衔接自然。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：学校在预防欺凌中的制度与措施。空前提出"Schools play a key role in prevention"，空后详述"Many schools have set up anti-bullying rules and trained teachers to notice warning signs...peer support programs...Students are encouraged to report bullying...When everyone knows the rules and what to do, bullying becomes harder to continue...a clear policy and a caring environment can make a real difference"。C选项"That is why schools need clear rules and programs to stop bullying"（因此学校需要明确的规则和项目来制止欺凌）中"That is why"承接空前"key role in prevention"，"clear rules and programs"与空后反欺凌规则、教师培训、同伴支持、举报机制等精准对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：学生个人的行动空间。空前设问"What can students do to help?"，空后分述"If you see someone being bullied, do not join in or laugh...Stand up for the victim or find an adult...If you are being bullied, remember it is not your fault...Talk to a parent, teacher, or friend...Nobody should face bullying alone...every student has a part to play"。D选项"It is true that each student can help stop bullying in their own way"（的确，每个学生都能以自己的方式帮助制止欺凌）中"It is true that"加强衔接，"in their own way"与空后旁观者挺身/求助、受害者倾诉等不同角色行动精准对应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与责任共担。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"preventing school bullying requires effort from everyone—students, teachers, and parents...By learning to recognize bullying, following school rules, and supporting each other, we can create a safer and kinder place...one kind word or action can change someone's day, or even their life"。E选项"In conclusion, we all share the responsibility to end bullying at school"（总之，我们都有责任终结校园欺凌）用"In conclusion"总结全文，"we all share the responsibility"与空后"everyone"及"everyone has a part"完美呼应，收束有力。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, some schools hold sports competitions to build team spirit.**：涉及学校活动如体育比赛，易被误填[3]。但F讨论的是通过体育活动培养团队精神，而空[3]前后文聚焦反欺凌的具体制度——规则制定、教师培训、同伴支持、举报机制等；F属于广义的校园氛围建设，与空[3]的针对性反欺凌政策与项目不匹配。F属"主题相关但论述对象错位"型干扰。
