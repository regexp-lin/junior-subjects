**Using Social Media the Right Way**

Social media lets us connect with friends, share news, and learn new things. But it can also waste time, spread wrong information, and sometimes hurt our feelings. [1] The key is to use it wisely—set limits, check facts, and protect your privacy. Like any tool, social media is neither good nor bad by itself; it depends on how we use it. This article offers simple tips to help you enjoy the benefits of social media while avoiding its downsides.

Manage your time and attention. [2] It is easy to scroll for hours without realising it. Set a daily limit—for example, 30 minutes or one hour—and use a timer or app to remind you. Turn off unnecessary notifications so your phone does not constantly interrupt you. Try to avoid checking social media right before bed; the blue light and endless content can affect your sleep. When you study or do homework, put your phone in another room. Small habits like these help you stay in control instead of being controlled by your phone.

Think before you post. [3] Once you share something online, it can be copied, saved, or spread—even if you delete it later. Ask yourself: Would I want my parents, teachers, or a future employer to see this? If the answer is no, do not post it. Do not share personal details like your home address or phone number. Be kind to others: avoid gossip, rumours, or mean comments. What you write can have a real impact on people's feelings and even their lives. A few seconds of thought can prevent years of regret.

Stay critical about what you see. [4] Not everything on social media is true. Fake news, edited photos, and misleading posts are common. Before you share or believe something, check where it came from—is it from a trusted source? Do a quick search to see if other reports say the same thing. Be especially careful with health advice, rumours about famous people, or posts that make you very angry or afraid—these are often designed to spread quickly. Learning to question and verify is a skill that will serve you well both online and offline.

[5] Social media is part of modern life, but it does not have to rule it. Use it to connect, create, and learn—but also to disconnect when you need rest. Balance screen time with real conversations, exercise, and time outdoors. The best way to use social media is to make sure it adds to your life instead of taking over. You are in charge of your time and your choices—remember that.
