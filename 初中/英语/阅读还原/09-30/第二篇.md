**Protecting Our Water Sources**

Fresh water is essential for life. We drink it, cook with it, and use it for farming and industry. Yet clean water is not unlimited. Rivers, lakes, and underground aquifers can be polluted by chemicals, waste, and runoff from farms and cities. Protecting water sources is a responsibility shared by everyone. [1] Keeping our water clean and available for future generations depends on how we treat it today.

Rivers and lakes are connected to the land around them. When rainwater runs off paved surfaces, it picks up oil, trash, and pollutants before flowing into streams. Farming can add fertilizers and pesticides to nearby water. Factories must carefully manage what they release. [2] What happens on land directly affects the quality of the water we depend on for drinking and living.

Wetlands act as natural filters. Marshes and swamps absorb excess nutrients, trap sediment, and slow down floods. Many communities have lost wetlands to development, and the loss often leads to poorer water quality downstream. [3] Restoring and protecting wetlands is one of the most effective ways to improve water quality and reduce flooding at the same time.

Individuals can help in many ways. Fixing leaky taps saves water. Avoiding litter and proper disposal of chemicals prevents pollution. Choosing products with less packaging reduces waste that can end up in rivers. [4] Small daily choices, when millions of people make them, add up to a significant difference for our water supply.

Water does not follow borders. A river may flow through several countries. Groundwater aquifers span regions. Protecting shared water sources requires cooperation between communities, regions, and nations. [5] The health of our water—and thus our health—is bound to the choices we make together as a society.

## 选项

A. The future of our water supply depends on the actions we take now.
B. Land use and water quality are closely linked in ways we cannot ignore.
C. Wetlands provide benefits that are hard to replace once they are gone.
D. Everyone can contribute to water protection through simple, everyday actions.
E. Safeguarding water is a shared task that crosses boundaries and requires collective effort.
F. For example, the Yangtze River is one of the longest rivers in Asia and supports millions of people.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前指出淡水不可或缺，水资源易受污染，空后说"Keeping our water clean and available for future generations depends on how we treat it today"。A的"The future of our water supply depends on the actions we take now"概括当下行动与未来供水的关系，与"depends on how we treat it today"完美呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前描述径流、农业、工业对河流湖泊的影响，空后强调"What happens on land directly affects the quality of the water we depend on"。B的"Land use and water quality are closely linked in ways we cannot ignore"概括土地利用与水质的关系，与"on land"和"quality"逻辑一致。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说明湿地的过滤、滞留与防洪作用及湿地丧失的后果，空后说"Restoring and protecting wetlands is one of the most effective ways to improve water quality and reduce flooding"。C的"Wetlands provide benefits that are hard to replace once they are gone"准确概括湿地的不可替代性，与"most effective ways"对应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前列举个人可采取的节水与防污行动，空后强调"Small daily choices, when millions of people make them, add up to a significant difference"。D的"Everyone can contribute to water protection through simple, everyday actions"概括个人日常行为对水资源保护的贡献，与"Small daily choices"呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前指出水域跨边界、跨区域，保护需多方合作，空后说"The health of our water—and thus our health—is bound to the choices we make together as a society"。E的"Safeguarding water is a shared task that crosses boundaries and requires collective effort"升华主题，与"does not follow borders"和"make together"完美对应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the Yangtze River is one of the longest rivers in Asia and supports millions of people.**：以For example开头，属具体举例；各空均为概括或过渡句，F为长江地理与人口的个案，不适用于任何空位，易误放[1]或[5]，但两空分别需水资源保护紧迫性的概括与集体行动的概括，非举例。
