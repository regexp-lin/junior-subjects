# 09-01 第二篇

**Urban Green Spaces**

Cities are growing larger and more crowded every year. As buildings rise higher and roads spread wider, natural spaces often disappear. Yet parks, gardens, and tree-lined streets are essential for the health and happiness of city residents. [1] Creating and protecting urban green spaces is one of the smartest investments a city can make for its people and its future.

Green spaces offer clear benefits for physical health. They provide places to exercise, play sports, and simply walk in fresh air. [2] Studies have shown that people who live near parks tend to be more active and have lower rates of heart disease and obesity.

Beyond physical health, green spaces also support mental wellbeing. Spending time in nature, even in a small city park, can reduce stress, improve mood, and help people feel more relaxed. [3] In our busy, screen-filled lives, having a quiet green place to escape to is increasingly valuable for maintaining balance and peace of mind.

Green spaces benefit the environment as well. Trees and plants absorb carbon dioxide, filter air pollution, and provide habitats for birds and insects. [4] By keeping green areas in cities, we help fight climate change and protect biodiversity right where most people live.

However, not all neighborhoods have equal access to green spaces. Wealthier areas often have more parks, while poorer communities may lack them. [5] Ensuring that every resident, regardless of income, has access to nearby green spaces is a matter of fairness and a step toward building healthier, more equal cities.

## 选项

A. Green spaces are not luxuries—they are necessities that improve the quality of life for everyone in a city.
B. Regular access to outdoor areas encourages healthy lifestyles and reduces the burden on healthcare systems.
C. The mental health benefits of nature are well documented and should not be overlooked in urban planning.
D. These environmental services make urban greenery a key part of any city's strategy against pollution and warming.
E. Addressing this inequality requires deliberate planning and investment in underserved communities.
F. For example, New York City's Central Park attracts over 42 million visitors each year.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述城市扩张导致自然空间消失，但绿地对健康和幸福至关重要，空后说"Creating and protecting urban green spaces is one of the smartest investments"。A的"Green spaces are not luxuries—they are necessities"与"essential"呼应，"improve the quality of life"概括投资的意义。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说绿地提供锻炼和新鲜空气场所，空后说住在公园附近的人更活跃、心脏病和肥胖率更低。B的"Regular access to outdoor areas encourages healthy lifestyles"承上，"reduces the burden on healthcare systems"引出健康数据的讨论。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说绿地减压、改善情绪，空后说"having a quiet green place to escape to is increasingly valuable"。C的"mental health benefits of nature are well documented"概括前文，"should not be overlooked in urban planning"引出规划层面的建议。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前列举树木和植物的环境功能（吸收CO2、过滤污染、提供栖息地），空后说"we help fight climate change and protect biodiversity"。D的"environmental services"概括前文列举，"key part of any city's strategy"与后文呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前指出绿地分配不均——富区多、穷区少，空后说"Ensuring that every resident... has access"。E的"Addressing this inequality"承接前文公平问题，"deliberate planning and investment in underserved communities"呼应解决方案。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, New York City's Central Park attracts over 42 million visitors each year.**：以For example开头，属具体城市案例举例，各空均为概括或过渡句；易误放[1]或[2]，但两空分别需绿地价值的概括与健康效益的过渡，非个案数据。
