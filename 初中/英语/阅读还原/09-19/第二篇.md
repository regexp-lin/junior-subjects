# 09-19 第二篇

**Mountain Climbing: Challenge and Reward**

Mountain climbing is one of the oldest and most challenging outdoor activities. People climb for many reasons: to test their limits, to enjoy nature, or simply to reach the top. [1] Whether for sport, adventure, or personal growth, mountain climbing offers unique rewards to those who dare to take on the challenge.

Climbing requires both physical and mental strength. You need strong muscles, good endurance, and the ability to keep going when you are tired. You also need focus, courage, and the ability to make smart decisions under pressure. [2] Success on the mountain depends on a combination of fitness, skill, and the right mindset.

Preparation is key. Climbers must choose the right equipment, plan their route, and check the weather. Rushing into a climb without preparation can lead to accidents. [3] Careful planning and proper preparation greatly increase the chances of a safe and successful climb.

The mountains also teach respect for nature. High altitudes, extreme weather, and thin air remind us that nature is powerful and unforgiving. [4] Understanding and respecting the mountain environment is essential for staying safe and protecting these fragile places for future climbers.

Climbing can also build character. Overcoming fear, facing setbacks, and working with a team all help climbers grow as people. [5] The lessons learned on the mountain often extend far beyond the climb itself, shaping how people approach challenges in everyday life.

## 选项

A. Mountain climbing appeals to people with different goals, but all share the thrill of pushing beyond their comfort zone.
B. A successful climb is the result of both physical readiness and mental discipline.
C. Taking time to prepare properly reduces risk and improves the likelihood of reaching your goal safely.
D. Climbers must recognize the power of the natural world and act responsibly to protect themselves and the environment.
E. The skills and attitudes developed through climbing can last a lifetime and influence many areas of life.
F. For example, Mount Everest is the world's highest peak and attracts thousands of climbers each year despite its dangers.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前列举人们攀爬的多种动机（挑战极限、享受自然、登顶），空后强调无论出于何种目的，登山都带来独特回报。A的"Mountain climbing appeals to people with different goals"与"People climb for many reasons"呼应，"thrill of pushing beyond their comfort zone"与"unique rewards"、"dare to take on the challenge"一致。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前分别讲体能需求（肌肉、耐力、坚持）和心理需求（专注、勇气、决策），空后总结山上成功依赖多种因素。B的"physical readiness and mental discipline"与"both physical and mental strength"直接对应，"A successful climb is the result of"概括本段逻辑。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说需选装备、规划路线、查天气，仓促易出事故，空后强调充分准备提高安全与成功几率。C的"Taking time to prepare properly"与"Careful planning and proper preparation"呼应，"reduces risk and improves the likelihood"与"greatly increase the chances of a safe and successful climb"一致。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说高海拔、恶劣天气、稀薄空气提醒自然威力，空后强调理解尊重山地环境对安全与保护的重要性。D的"recognize the power of the natural world"与"Understanding and respecting the mountain environment"呼应，"protect themselves and the environment"与"protecting these fragile places"一致。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说克服恐惧、面对挫折、团队协作帮助成长，空后强调山上学到的道理延伸到日常。E的"The skills and attitudes developed through climbing"与"The lessons learned on the mountain"呼应，"can last a lifetime and influence many areas of life"与"extend far beyond the climb itself"一致。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, Mount Everest is the world's highest peak and attracts thousands of climbers each year despite its dangers.**：以For example开头，是具体山峰案例；易误放[1]或[4]，但[1]需概括登山动机与回报，[4]需自然敬畏与责任的概括，非单一山峰介绍。
