# 07-07 第二篇

**The Responsibility of Owning a Pet**

Pets can bring great joy to our lives. A dog wagging its tail when you come home, a cat curling up beside you, or a fish swimming peacefully in a tank—these moments make many of us smile. [1] However, having a pet is a serious commitment that lasts for many years. Pets depend on us for food, shelter, healthcare, and love. Before bringing a pet home, you must be sure that you and your family can provide for its needs for its entire life. Owning a pet is rewarding, but it also comes with real responsibilities that should not be taken lightly.

[2] Every pet needs proper nutrition and a safe place to live. Dogs and cats need balanced meals—not just leftovers or scraps—to stay healthy. Different animals have different dietary needs, so it is important to learn what your pet should eat. Your pet also needs a clean, comfortable space: a bed for a dog, a litter box for a cat, or a suitable tank for a fish. Make sure your home is safe—remove things that could harm your pet, such as poisonous plants or small objects it might swallow. These basics show your pet that you care.

[3] Regular health care is essential for pets. Pets need vaccinations to prevent diseases and check-ups to catch problems early. When your pet is sick or injured, you must take it to a vet. This can be expensive, so it is important to plan for vet costs when you decide to get a pet. Some pets, like dogs, need regular grooming—bathing, brushing, and nail trimming. Dental care is also important for many animals. Keeping your pet healthy is part of being a responsible owner. Ignoring health needs can lead to suffering and shorter lives.

[4] Pets also need attention, exercise, and training. Dogs need daily walks and playtime to stay happy and fit. Cats need toys and interaction to prevent boredom. Leaving a pet alone for long hours every day can cause stress and behavior problems. Training helps pets learn rules and makes living together easier. A well-trained dog is safer around people and other animals. Investing time in your pet strengthens the bond between you and makes life better for both of you.

[5] Owning a pet is a long-term commitment that requires time, money, and dedication. Before getting a pet, research the animal's needs and make sure your lifestyle fits. Consider adopting from a shelter—many animals need loving homes. If you already have a pet, remember that it relies on you completely. Your pet cannot ask for help or choose another home. By being a responsible owner, you give your pet a good life and experience the joy that comes from caring for another living being. That is the true reward of pet ownership.

## 选项

A. Yet pet ownership is a long-term commitment that requires serious responsibility.
B. Providing proper food and a safe living environment is the foundation of pet care.
C. Regular vet visits and health care are essential for keeping your pet healthy.
D. Pets need daily attention, exercise, and training to stay happy and well-behaved.
E. Being a responsible pet owner means planning carefully and dedicating yourself fully.
F. For example, dogs typically live 10 to 15 years depending on their breed and size.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述养宠带来的快乐（狗摇尾、猫依偎、鱼游动），空后转折指出养宠是长期承诺——宠物依赖主人提供食物、住所、医疗和关爱，领养前需评估家庭能力。A选项"However, having a pet is a serious commitment that lasts for many years"完美承上启下：用"However"转折，承接前文的快乐，引出后文对"承诺与责任"的论述。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：第二段论述基本照护——营养均衡、安全住所、清洁空间、家居安全。B选项"Providing proper food and a safe living environment is the foundation of pet care"准确概括段落主题。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：第三段聚焦医疗照护——疫苗、体检、生病就医、费用规划、美容、牙科。C选项"Regular vet visits and health care are essential for keeping your pet healthy"精准总结段落主旨。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：第四段讨论关注、运动与训练——遛狗、玩耍、互动、训练、减少行为问题。D选项"Pets need daily attention, exercise, and training to stay happy and well-behaved"准确概括段落核心。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：末段总结全文，强调长期投入、领养前调研、领养替代购买、全心负责。E选项"Being a responsible pet owner means planning carefully and dedicating yourself fully"精准呼应段落的总结性主旨。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example...**：以"For example"开头，举狗通常活10–15年的例子。虽与"长期承诺"相关，但它是支撑第一段论点的具体论据（说明承诺之久），而非段落的概括句。若放入[1]会造成层次错位——F是例证层，正确选项A是论点/主题概括层。
