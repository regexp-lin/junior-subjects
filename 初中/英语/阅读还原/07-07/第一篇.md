# 07-07 第一篇

**Staying Safe Online: A Guide for Teenagers**

The internet opens up a world of information, entertainment, and connection. We use it for homework, social media, games, and staying in touch with friends. [1] However, the online world also has risks, and knowing how to protect yourself is essential. Strangers may try to contact you, fake websites can steal your information, and too much screen time can affect your health. Learning about online safety is not optional—it is a skill you need for life. The good news is that with some basic knowledge and good habits, you can enjoy the internet safely.

[2] Your passwords are the keys to your online accounts, so protect them carefully. Use strong passwords that mix letters, numbers, and symbols. Never share your passwords with anyone, including friends. Avoid using the same password for different websites—if one account is hacked, others could be at risk too. Consider using a password manager to help you remember different passwords securely. Turn on two-factor authentication when it is available—this adds an extra layer of protection. These steps may seem small, but they greatly reduce the chance of someone accessing your accounts.

[3] Be careful about what you share online. Once you post something, it can be copied, shared, or saved by others, and it is very hard to remove completely. Think before you post: would you be comfortable if your parents, teachers, or future employers saw this? Do not share personal details like your address, phone number, or school name with strangers. Be wary of people who ask for private information or want to move conversations to private apps. If something feels wrong, trust your instincts and stop the conversation.

[4] Not everything you see online is true. Fake news, scam messages, and misleading ads are common. Before believing or sharing something, check the source. Is it from a trusted website or account? Have other reliable sources reported the same thing? Be especially careful with messages that offer free gifts, ask for money, or urge you to act quickly. These are often tricks to steal money or information. When in doubt, ask an adult you trust. Critical thinking is one of your best defenses online.

[5] Staying safe online is a habit you build over time. Set limits on your screen time and take breaks to protect your eyes and mind. Keep your devices and apps updated so you get the latest security fixes. Talk to your parents or teachers if you see something that worries or confuses you. Remember that you have the right to feel safe online—if someone harasses or threatens you, report them and block them. The internet can be a wonderful place when we use it wisely and stay alert. Your safety is in your hands.

## 选项

A. Yet the internet has dangers too, and learning to stay safe online is crucial.
B. Protecting your passwords is one of the most important steps for online security.
C. Think carefully about what you share and who you share it with online.
D. Being able to judge what is real and what is not helps you avoid scams and misinformation.
E. Building safe online habits and seeking help when needed will keep you protected.
F. For example, many social media apps now offer privacy settings to control who sees your posts.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述网络的便利（信息、娱乐、社交、学习、联系），空后转折指出网络风险——陌生人、钓鱼网站、过度用眼，并强调学习网络安全的重要性。A选项"However, the online world also has risks, and knowing how to protect yourself is essential"完美承上启下：用"However"转折，承接前文对网络的正面描述，引出后文对风险的论述及"保护自己"的主题。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：第二段全段论述密码保护——强密码、勿分享、勿复用、密码管理器、双重认证。B选项"Protecting your passwords is one of the most important steps for online security"准确概括段落主题。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：第三段聚焦信息分享——发帖需谨慎、内容难彻底删除、勿透露地址电话、警惕陌生人、相信直觉。C选项"Think carefully about what you share and who you share it with online"精准总结段落主旨。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：第四段讨论辨别真假信息——假新闻、诈骗、误导广告、核实来源、警惕免费/要钱/催促类信息。D选项"Being able to judge what is real and what is not helps you avoid scams and misinformation"准确概括段落核心。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：末段总结全文，提出养成安全习惯——控制用网时间、更新设备、遇到问题求助、举报骚扰。E选项"Building safe online habits and seeking help when needed will keep you protected"精准呼应段落的总结性主旨。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example...**：以"For example"开头，举许多社交应用提供隐私设置的例子。虽与保护隐私、控制分享相关，但它是支撑第三段"谨慎分享"论点的具体论据，而非段落的概括句。若放入[3]会造成层次错位——F是例证层，正确选项C是论点/主题概括层。
