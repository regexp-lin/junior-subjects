**How to Set Goals**

Setting goals is a powerful way to turn dreams into reality. Whether you want to improve your grades, learn a new skill, or make healthier choices, having clear goals gives you direction and motivation. However, not all goals are equally effective. [1] Research shows that goals work best when they are specific, measurable, and achievable. Vague goals like "do better" or "try harder" are hard to track and often lead to disappointment. Instead, set goals that you can measure and reach step by step.

One useful method is the SMART framework. SMART stands for Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. For example, instead of saying "I want to read more," you might say "I will read one book per month for the next six months." [2] This kind of goal gives you a clear target and a deadline, making it easier to stay on track. You can check your progress regularly and adjust your plan if needed. Goals without deadlines often get postponed forever.

It is also important to break big goals into smaller steps. Climbing a mountain seems impossible until you focus on one step at a time. [3] Each small success builds your confidence and motivates you to keep going. If your goal is to run a 5 km race, start by running short distances and gradually increase. Celebrate small victories along the way—they add up to big achievements.

Another key is to write your goals down. People who write their goals on paper are more likely to achieve them than those who only think about them. [4] Writing makes goals feel more real and creates a sense of commitment. You can also share your goals with a friend or family member who can support you and hold you accountable. Having someone to encourage you makes the journey easier.

[5] With the right approach, goal-setting can change your life. Start small, stay consistent, and remember that every step forward is progress.

## 选项

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前对比有效目标与无效目标，空后说 “Vague goals...are hard to track...Instead, set goals that you can measure”。选项 A 的 “Well-defined goals are more likely to lead to success than vague wishes” 概括 “specific, measurable, achievable”，与后文 “Vague goals” 形成对立，逻辑清晰。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前举 SMART 例句 “I will read one book per month for the next six months”，空后说 “clear target and a deadline...check your progress...Goals without deadlines often get postponed”。选项 B 的 “Deadlines create urgency and help you prioritize” 直接对应 “deadline” 与 “stay on track”，衔接紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：本段讲分解大目标，空前用爬山类比 “one step at a time”，空后举例 “run short distances and gradually increase...Celebrate small victories”。选项 C 的 “Breaking down goals makes the impossible seem possible” 与 “Climbing a mountain seems impossible” 呼应，概括本段主旨。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说 “People who write their goals...are more likely to achieve them”，空后列举 “Writing makes goals feel more real...share your goals...hold you accountable”。选项 D 的 “This simple habit can significantly increase your chances of success” 中的 “This simple habit” 指代写下来，与 “write your goals down” 一致。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：结尾段总结全文，空前说 “With the right approach...Start small, stay consistent”。选项 E 的 “Goal-setting is a skill that anyone can learn and improve with practice” 呼应前文 SMART、分解、书写等方法，强调可习得性，作收尾恰当。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, athletes often visualize their goals to improve performance.**：属目标实现的另一种技巧（可视化），与文中 “write down” “SMART” “break down” 等方法并列。但各段已有清晰主题，F 插入会引入新概念且无后续展开，层次错位，易在 [3] 或 [4] 处被误选。
