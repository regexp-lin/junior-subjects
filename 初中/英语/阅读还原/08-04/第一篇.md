**The Ecological Value of Wetlands**

Wetlands are areas where water covers the soil or is present near the surface for at least part of the year. They include marshes, swamps, bogs, and riverbanks. For a long time, people viewed wetlands as useless land—places to drain and build on. [1] Scientists now understand that wetlands are among the most valuable ecosystems on Earth. They provide clean water, control floods, and support a huge variety of wildlife. Protecting wetlands is essential for both the environment and human well-being.

One of the most important roles of wetlands is filtering water. As water passes through wetland plants and soil, harmful chemicals and excess nutrients are removed. [2] This natural filtering system improves water quality for rivers, lakes, and even underground water sources. Many cities depend on wetlands to keep their drinking water clean. Without wetlands, we would need to build expensive water treatment plants, and some pollutants would still reach our water supply.

Wetlands also act as natural sponges during heavy rain and floods. They absorb excess water and release it slowly over time. [3] By doing so, they reduce flood damage to nearby towns and farms. In coastal areas, wetlands protect the shore from erosion and storm surges. When we destroy wetlands, we lose this protection, and floods become more severe. Restoring wetlands is one way to make communities more resilient to climate change.

Wetlands are home to countless species. Birds, fish, insects, and plants thrive in these rich environments. [4] Many species depend entirely on wetlands for their survival—they cannot live anywhere else. When wetlands disappear, these species lose their habitats and may become endangered or extinct. Protecting wetlands means protecting biodiversity and the delicate balance of nature.

[5] The value of wetlands goes far beyond what we can see. They support our health, our economy, and the survival of many species. It is time to change how we treat these precious ecosystems and give them the protection they deserve.

## 选项

A. Research has shown that wetlands offer benefits that we once ignored or underestimated.
B. In this way, wetlands work like a free and efficient water purification system.
C. This flood control function saves lives and property every year around the world.
D. Wetlands are critical for maintaining the diversity of life on our planet.
E. For these reasons, conservation efforts must focus on preserving and restoring wetlands.
F. For example, the Okavango Delta in Africa is one of the world's largest inland deltas.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述人们曾把湿地视为无用之地，空后列出湿地的多种价值。选项 A 的 “benefits that we once ignored or underestimated” 承接 “viewed wetlands as useless”，并用 “Research has shown” 引出后文科学家认识，转折自然。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前描述湿地过滤水质的过程，空后说 “improves water quality...Many cities depend on wetlands to keep their drinking water clean”。选项 B 的 “work like a free and efficient water purification system” 概括前文过滤功能，与 “water treatment plants” 形成对比，逻辑顺畅。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说湿地吸收洪水、减缓释放，空后强调 “reduce flood damage...protect the shore...When we destroy wetlands...floods become more severe”。选项 C 的 “This flood control function saves lives and property” 总结本段主题，与后文 “Restoring wetlands...resilient to climate change” 衔接。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：本段讲湿地与生物多样性，空前列举物种，空后说 “these species lose their habitats...endangered or extinct”。选项 D 的 “critical for maintaining the diversity of life” 与 “countless species” 和 “biodiversity” 完全对应，作段落概括恰当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：结尾段需总结湿地价值并呼吁保护。空前概括 “support our health, our economy, and the survival of many species”，空后说 “give them the protection they deserve”。选项 E 的 “conservation efforts must focus on preserving and restoring” 直接呼应 “protection”，收尾有力。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the Okavango Delta in Africa is one of the world's largest inland deltas.**：具体湿地案例，与任一空位的抽象论述层次不符。若放在 [4] 会与 “countless species” 的概括性表述冲突；放在 [1] 则打断 “scientists now understand” 与后文价值的逻辑链，易造成误选。
