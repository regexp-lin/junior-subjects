# 09-25 第一篇

**The Spirit of Entrepreneurship**

Starting a business is not easy. It takes courage, hard work, and the willingness to take risks. Yet many of the world's most successful companies began with a simple idea and a person who believed in it. Entrepreneurs see problems as opportunities and turn their dreams into reality. [1] This mindset—seeing what others miss and daring to act—is at the heart of innovation and progress.

Every entrepreneur faces setbacks. Products may fail, customers may say no, and money may run short. What separates those who succeed from those who quit is often how they respond to failure. [2] Learning from mistakes and trying again with new ideas is a skill that benefits not only business but life in general.

Entrepreneurship is not only about making money. Many young entrepreneurs start projects to solve social problems, protect the environment, or help their communities. They use business as a tool for good. [3] When profit and purpose go together, business can become a force for positive change in the world.

You do not need to wait until you are an adult to develop an entrepreneurial spirit. Selling homemade crafts, starting a school club, or finding creative solutions to everyday problems are all forms of entrepreneurship. [4] The habits you build now—thinking creatively, taking initiative, and working with others—will serve you no matter what path you choose later.

The future will bring new challenges and new needs. Someone will have to invent solutions, create jobs, and build the next generation of products and services. [5] Cultivating an entrepreneurial mindset today prepares you to be one of those who shape tomorrow.

## 选项

A. This way of thinking drives both personal success and wider social change.
B. Those who bounce back and adapt often find their way to success.
C. Combining business success with social impact can make entrepreneurship meaningful beyond profit.
D. Starting small and practicing these skills early builds a strong foundation for the future.
E. The people who will lead that future are those who start preparing today.
F. For example, Steve Jobs was fired from Apple but later returned to lead the company to great success.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前指出创业者将问题视为机会、敢于行动，空后说"This mindset—seeing what others miss and daring to act—is at the heart of innovation and progress"。A的"This way of thinking drives both personal success and wider social change"概括创业思维的双重作用，与"mindset"和"innovation and progress"完美呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前描述创业者面临的挫折及成功与放弃者的差异，空后强调"Learning from mistakes and trying again with new ideas is a skill that benefits not only business but life"。B的"Those who bounce back and adapt often find their way to success"概括从失败中恢复与适应的重要性，与"Learning from mistakes and trying again"逻辑一致。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前举例社会型创业（解决社会问题、环保、社区帮扶），空后说"When profit and purpose go together, business can become a force for positive change"。C的"Combining business success with social impact can make entrepreneurship meaningful beyond profit"准确概括商业与社会价值结合的意义，与"profit and purpose"对应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前列举青少年可实践的创业形式（手作、社团、创意解决问题），空后强调"The habits you build now... will serve you no matter what path you choose later"。D的"Starting small and practicing these skills early builds a strong foundation for the future"概括早期实践的习惯养成作用，与"habits you build now"呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前指出未来需要创新者、就业创造者与建设者，空后说"Cultivating an entrepreneurial mindset today prepares you to be one of those who shape tomorrow"。E的"The people who will lead that future are those who start preparing today"升华主题，与"prepare"和"shape tomorrow"完美对应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, Steve Jobs was fired from Apple but later returned to lead the company to great success.**：以For example开头，属具体举例；各空均为概括或过渡句，F为乔布斯个人经历个案，不适用于任何空位，易误放[2]，但该空需从失败中学习并再次尝试的概括句，非举例。
