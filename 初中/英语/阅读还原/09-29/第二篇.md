**Building Psychological Resilience**

Life does not always go as planned. Exams can be failed, friendships can break, and unexpected events can turn everything upside down. How we respond to setbacks often matters more than the setbacks themselves. Resilience is the ability to bounce back from difficulty and grow stronger because of it. [1] This inner strength helps us face challenges without losing hope or giving up on our goals.

Resilience is not something you are either born with or without. It can be learned and practiced. Like a muscle, it grows when you use it. Facing small challenges and recovering from them builds the confidence to handle bigger ones. [2] Over time, resilience becomes a habit that supports you through both everyday stress and major crises.

A positive mindset does not mean ignoring problems. It means believing that you can find solutions and that things can improve. People who focus on what they can control—their effort, their attitude, their choices—tend to cope better than those who feel helpless. [3] Shifting your focus from what you cannot change to what you can improves both your mood and your ability to act.

Support from others matters. Family, friends, teachers, and counselors can provide encouragement and practical help when times are tough. Asking for help is a sign of strength, not weakness. [4] No one has to face difficult times alone, and reaching out is often the first step toward recovery.

Resilience does not mean never feeling sad or stressed. It means experiencing those feelings and still moving forward. Setbacks can teach us lessons and make us wiser. [5] Building resilience is one of the best investments you can make in yourself, because it pays off in every area of your life.

## 选项

A. Resilience is a skill that helps you keep going when things get hard.
B. Practicing resilience in small ways prepares you for bigger challenges later.
C. Focusing on what you can control gives you power even when the situation is tough.
D. Getting support from people who care can make a huge difference during difficult times.
E. The ability to recover and grow from difficulty is valuable in school, work, and relationships.
F. For example, many successful people have failed multiple times before achieving their goals.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前指出人生难免不顺，应对方式比挫折本身更重要，空后说"This inner strength helps us face challenges without losing hope or giving up on our goals"。A的"Resilience is a skill that helps you keep going when things get hard"概括心理韧性的核心功能，与"inner strength"和"without losing hope"完美呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说明韧性可学习、可锻炼，空后强调"Over time, resilience becomes a habit that supports you through both everyday stress and major crises"。B的"Practicing resilience in small ways prepares you for bigger challenges later"概括从小事开始练习韧性的积累效应，与"becomes a habit"逻辑一致。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前讨论积极心态及关注可控因素，空后说"Shifting your focus from what you cannot change to what you can improves both your mood and your ability to act"。C的"Focusing on what you can control gives you power even when the situation is tough"准确概括聚焦可控因素的作用，与"what you can control"对应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前列举社会支持来源及求助的意义，空后强调"No one has to face difficult times alone, and reaching out is often the first step toward recovery"。D的"Getting support from people who care can make a huge difference during difficult times"概括他人支持的价值，与"reaching out"和"recovery"呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前指出韧性不等于无负面情绪，挫折可带来成长，空后说"Building resilience is one of the best investments you can make in yourself, because it pays off in every area of your life"。E的"The ability to recover and grow from difficulty is valuable in school, work, and relationships"升华主题，与"pays off"和"every area"完美对应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, many successful people have failed multiple times before achieving their goals.**：以For example开头，属具体举例；各空均为概括或过渡句，F为成功人士失败经历的个案，不适用于任何空位，易误放[2]或[5]，但两空分别需韧性练习的概括与韧性价值的概括，非举例。
