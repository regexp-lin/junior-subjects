# 09-29 第一篇

**The Dream of Space Travel**

For thousands of years, humans have looked up at the stars and wondered what lies beyond. Today, we have sent people to the Moon and robots to Mars. Private companies are building rockets, and nations are racing to return to the Moon and reach Mars. Space travel is no longer just science fiction. [1] What was once a dream is becoming reality, and the next decades could change how we see our place in the universe.

Space travel requires enormous effort. Rockets must overcome Earth's gravity, and every kilogram of weight costs fuel and money. Astronauts train for years and face risks that most people never encounter. Yet the rewards can be great. [2] The challenges of space inspire new technology, new jobs, and new ways of thinking that benefit life on Earth.

Space exploration has already given us much. Satellites enable GPS, weather forecasting, and global communication. Space telescopes have shown us distant galaxies and black holes. Research on the International Space Station helps us understand how the human body and materials behave in microgravity. [3] The benefits of space exploration extend far beyond the astronauts who fly—they touch the daily lives of billions.

Going deeper into space—to Mars and beyond—will require even more innovation. New propulsion systems, life-support technology, and ways to grow food in space are all being developed. Young people studying science and engineering today may design the ships that carry humans to other planets. [4] The next generation of explorers and engineers will shape humanity's future in space.

Space reminds us that we share one planet. Photos of Earth from space show a fragile blue marble in the darkness. That view has changed how many people think about protecting our world. [5] Exploring space can deepen our sense of responsibility for the only home we have—Earth.

## 选项

A. The age of human space exploration has arrived, and it is only the beginning.
B. Overcoming these difficulties leads to advances that improve life for everyone.
C. Space technology has become part of everyday life in ways we often take for granted.
D. The future of space travel depends on the talent and imagination of young people.
E. Looking at Earth from space can inspire a stronger commitment to protecting our planet.
F. For example, the Apollo 11 mission landed the first humans on the Moon in 1969.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前回顾人类对太空的向往及当今登月、探火、商业航天的发展，空后说"What was once a dream is becoming reality, and the next decades could change how we see our place in the universe"。A的"The age of human space exploration has arrived, and it is only the beginning"概括人类航天时代已到来的主题，与"dream is becoming reality"完美呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前描述太空旅行的难度与风险，空后强调"The challenges of space inspire new technology, new jobs, and new ways of thinking that benefit life on Earth"。B的"Overcoming these difficulties leads to advances that improve life for everyone"概括克服太空挑战带来的技术与社会进步，与"inspire"和"benefit life on Earth"逻辑一致。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前列举航天应用（卫星、GPS、气象、通信、太空望远镜、空间站研究），空后说"The benefits of space exploration extend far beyond the astronauts who fly—they touch the daily lives of billions"。C的"Space technology has become part of everyday life in ways we often take for granted"准确概括航天技术融入日常生活的程度，与"daily lives of billions"对应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前讨论深空探索所需创新及年轻人可能参与研发，空后强调"The next generation of explorers and engineers will shape humanity's future in space"。D的"The future of space travel depends on the talent and imagination of young people"概括青年对航天未来的作用，与"next generation"和"shape"呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前指出从太空看地球带来的视角转变及环保意识，空后说"Exploring space can deepen our sense of responsibility for the only home we have—Earth"。E的"Looking at Earth from space can inspire a stronger commitment to protecting our planet"升华主题，与"sense of responsibility"和"protecting our world"完美对应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the Apollo 11 mission landed the first humans on the Moon in 1969.**：以For example开头，属具体举例；各空均为概括或过渡句，F为阿波罗11号的个案，不适用于任何空位，易误放[1]，但该空需航天时代已到来的概括句，非举例。
