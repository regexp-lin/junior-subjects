# 03-28 第二篇

**The Mystery of Cat Purring**

If you have ever had a cat, you know the sound well—a soft, rumbling vibration (振动) that seems to come from deep inside. We usually think that cats purr when they are happy and relaxed. [1] Scientists have discovered that cats may also purr when they are in pain, hungry, or even when giving birth. This suggests that purring might serve more than one purpose.

One theory is that purring helps cats heal. The frequency of a cat's purr—between 25 and 150 Hertz—is similar to the frequencies used in human physical therapy to promote bone growth and reduce pain. [2] When a cat is injured, purring might be a way for it to heal itself naturally. This could explain why cats often purr when resting after an illness or injury.

Purring may also be a form of communication between kittens and their mothers. Very young kittens cannot see or hear at first, but they can feel vibrations. [3] The mother's purring may help guide the tiny kittens to her, so they can find warmth and milk. As kittens grow, they learn to purr back, creating a special bond with their mother.

Some researchers believe that purring has a calming effect on humans as well. Spending time with a purring cat can lower stress and slow your heart rate. [4] This is one reason why cats have become such popular pets—their presence alone can make us feel better. Some hospitals and nursing homes even invite therapy cats to comfort patients.

[5] Next time you hear your cat purring, remember that there is more to this sound than simple happiness. It may be healing, communicating, or simply sharing comfort with you.

## 选项

A. In short, purring remains one of the most fascinating and mysterious behaviors in the animal kingdom.
B. However, recent research shows that purring is not always a sign of contentment.
C. As a result, scientists now recommend that everyone should get a cat for medical reasons.
D. Purring creates vibrations that travel through their bodies, helping them find their mother even in the dark.
E. Research suggests that purring could be a natural healing mechanism that cats have developed over millions of years.
F. For this reason, many people find that simply sitting with a cat can improve their mood and reduce anxiety.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] B   [2] E   [3] D   [4] F   [5] A

## 深度解析

- **[1] B**：
  - **线索**：转折引入与语义衔接。空前指出人们通常认为猫满足放松时才打呼噜。B选项"However, recent research shows that purring is not always a sign of contentment"（然而，近期研究表明打呼噜并不总是满足的信号）用"However"转折，引出与常识不同的观点。空后"Scientists have discovered that cats may also purr when they are in pain, hungry, or even when giving birth"正是对B中"not always a sign of contentment"的具体例证，逻辑紧密。

- **[2] E**：
  - **线索**：科学论证与因果递进。空前给出呼噜频率（25–150 Hz）与人类理疗用于促骨骼生长、减痛频率相近的数据。E选项"Research suggests that purring could be a natural healing mechanism that cats have developed over millions of years"（研究表明打呼噜可能是猫在数百万年间进化出的天然愈合机制）用研究结论衔接数据与空后的"when injured, purring might heal itself naturally"，形成"频率相似→愈合机制→受伤时自愈"的推理链。

- **[3] D**：
  - **线索**：机制阐释与代词衔接。空前说幼崽最初看不见、听不见，但"can feel vibrations"。D选项"Purring creates vibrations that travel through their bodies, helping them find their mother even in the dark"（打呼噜产生振动传遍它们全身，帮助它们在黑暗中找到母亲）说明振动的作用。空后"The mother's purring may help guide the tiny kittens to her"中"mother's purring"与D的"Purring"呼应，"find their mother"与D中"helping them find their mother"完全对应。

- **[4] F**：
  - **线索**：因果衔接与同义呼应。空前描述与打呼噜的猫相处可降低压力、减缓心率。F选项"For this reason, many people find that simply sitting with a cat can improve their mood and reduce anxiety"（因此，许多人发现仅仅和猫坐在一起就能改善情绪、减轻焦虑）中"For this reason"指代前文的减压效果。"improve their mood and reduce anxiety"与空后"make us feel better"以及全文"calming effect"形成同义呼应。

- **[5] A**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与总结概括。空[5]位于结尾段段首，前文从多情境呼噜、愈合、亲子沟通、对人类抚慰四方面阐述。A选项"In short, purring remains one of the most fascinating and mysterious behaviors in the animal kingdom"（简而言之，打呼噜仍是动物王国中最迷人、最神秘的行为之一）用"In short"收束全文。"fascinating and mysterious"概括前文多用途、多机制的特点。空后"Next time you hear...remember that there is more to this sound"与A的"mysterious"自然衔接。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **C. As a result, scientists now recommend that everyone should get a cat for medical reasons.**：含"scientists""cat""medical"等关键词及因果连词"As a result"。但全文只客观介绍呼噜的可能功能与对人类情绪的舒缓作用，从未提出"所有人应为医疗目的养猫"的建议。该选项属"过度引申+绝对化"型干扰——将研究发现夸大为普遍医疗建议。
