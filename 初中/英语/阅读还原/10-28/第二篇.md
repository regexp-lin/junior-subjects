# 10-28 第二篇

**The Maritime Silk Road: Connecting Worlds by Sea**

Long before airplanes and the internet, ships carried goods and ideas across the oceans. The Maritime Silk Road was a network of sea routes that linked China with Southeast Asia, South Asia, the Middle East, and East Africa. For centuries, merchants sailed these waters, trading silk, spices, tea, and ceramics. [1] The Maritime Silk Road was more than a trade route; it was a bridge between cultures, helping people from different lands share goods, knowledge, and traditions.

The routes were not easy. Sailors faced storms, pirates, and the unknown. They relied on the monsoon winds—seasonal winds that blow in different directions at different times of year—to plan their journeys. Knowledge of stars, currents, and coasts was passed down from generation to generation. [2] Success on the Maritime Silk Road depended on skill, courage, and the willingness to learn from others, as sailors from different regions often shared navigation techniques and trade secrets.

The exchange went far beyond goods. Religions, languages, and technologies traveled along the same routes. Buddhism spread from India to China and Southeast Asia by sea. Arabic numerals and papermaking techniques moved between East and West. Cities along the coast grew into busy ports where people of many cultures met and mingled. [3] The Maritime Silk Road shaped not only economies but also the beliefs, arts, and sciences of the societies it connected.

In recent years, the idea of the Maritime Silk Road has been revived. Countries along the old routes are working together to build better ports, improve shipping, and promote trade and cooperation. The goal is to strengthen ties between nations and support shared development. [4] By building on the spirit of the ancient routes—openness, exchange, and mutual benefit—today's cooperation aims to create a more connected and prosperous world for all.

The story of the Maritime Silk Road reminds us that the sea has long been a place of connection, not division. In an age of global trade and travel, the lessons of the past—cooperation, respect for different cultures, and the courage to explore—remain as important as ever. [5] As we look to the future, the Maritime Silk Road stands as a symbol of what humanity can achieve when we work together across oceans and across time.

## 选项

A. The Maritime Silk Road was more than a trade route; it was a bridge between cultures, helping people from different lands share goods, knowledge, and traditions.
B. Success on the Maritime Silk Road depended on skill, courage, and the willingness to learn from others, as sailors from different regions often shared navigation techniques and trade secrets.
C. The Maritime Silk Road shaped not only economies but also the beliefs, arts, and sciences of the societies it connected.
D. By building on the spirit of the ancient routes—openness, exchange, and mutual benefit—today's cooperation aims to create a more connected and prosperous world for all.
E. As we look to the future, the Maritime Silk Road stands as a symbol of what humanity can achieve when we work together across oceans and across time.
F. For example, the port of Guangzhou in southern China was once a major hub where merchants from Arabia, India, and Southeast Asia gathered to trade.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍海上丝绸之路——连接中国与亚非多地的海路网络，贸易丝绸、香料等，空后需概括其超越贸易的文明意义。A的"more than a trade route; it was a bridge between cultures"与前文贸易呼应，"share goods, knowledge, and traditions"与"goods and ideas"对应，定义海上丝路本质。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说航行艰险——风暴、海盗、依靠季风与世代相传的航海知识，空后需总结成功的要素。B的"Success... depended on skill, courage, and the willingness to learn from others"与艰险环境呼应，"sailors from different regions often shared"与"passed down from generation to generation"及跨区域交流衔接。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说交流不限于货物——宗教、语言、技术传播，佛教、阿拉伯数字、造纸术，港口城市多元文化交融，空后需总结丝路的多维影响。C的"shaped not only economies but also the beliefs, arts, and sciences"与前文宗教、技术、文化城市形成完整概括。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说近年来海上丝路理念复兴，各国共建港口、航运与贸易合作，空后需概括当代合作的精神与目标。D的"building on the spirit of the ancient routes—openness, exchange, and mutual benefit"与古代丝路精神呼应，"create a more connected and prosperous world"与"strengthen ties... shared development"对应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说海上丝路故事提醒我们海洋是连接而非分裂，古今经验——合作、尊重文化、探索勇气——仍重要，空后需展望未来象征意义。E的"As we look to the future"与"lessons of the past"呼应，"symbol of what humanity can achieve when we work together"是全文收尾的合理升华。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the port of Guangzhou in southern China was once a major hub where merchants from Arabia, India, and Southeast Asia gathered to trade.**：以For example开头，为具体港口举例。各空均为概括或过渡句；[3]虽涉及港口城市，但需的是"经济、文化、科学多维影响"的概括，而非单一广州港案例。
