# 10-28 第一篇

**The Value of Writing Letters**

In an age of instant messages and email, many people have forgotten the art of writing letters. A quick text or emoji can say "hello" in seconds, but it rarely carries the same warmth and thoughtfulness as a handwritten note. Letter writing is slower, but it offers something that faster forms of communication often cannot. [1] Letters allow us to slow down, choose our words carefully, and express feelings and ideas in a way that creates a lasting connection between the writer and the reader.

When we write a letter, we often reflect more deeply on what we want to say. There is no expectation of an immediate reply, so we can take time to organize our thoughts, share stories, and ask meaningful questions. Letters can become keepsakes—treasured reminders of a friendship, a thank-you, or a moment in time. [2] The effort we put into writing shows the recipient that they matter, and the letter itself becomes a gift that can be read and reread for years to come.

Letters have played an important role throughout history. Scientists, writers, and leaders have used them to exchange ideas, record events, and build relationships across distance. Today, historians and biographers still study old letters to understand the past and the people who lived it. [3] Letters are not only personal; they can also be historical documents that future generations use to learn about our lives and our world.

Schools and teachers are rediscovering the value of letter writing. Some classes partner with students in other countries and exchange letters to practice language and learn about different cultures. Others encourage students to write thank-you notes to family members or community helpers. [4] Teaching young people to write letters helps them develop writing skills, empathy, and a habit of thoughtful communication that can serve them for life.

Even if we do not write letters every day, keeping the practice alive has benefits. A birthday card, a note of thanks, or a letter to a grandparent can brighten someone's day and strengthen a bond. [5] In a fast-paced world, the quiet act of writing a letter reminds us that some connections are worth the extra time and care, and that the best things in life are often worth waiting for.

## 选项

A. Letters allow us to slow down, choose our words carefully, and express feelings and ideas in a way that creates a lasting connection between the writer and the reader.
B. The effort we put into writing shows the recipient that they matter, and the letter itself becomes a gift that can be read and reread for years to come.
C. Letters are not only personal; they can also be historical documents that future generations use to learn about our lives and our world.
D. Teaching young people to write letters helps them develop writing skills, empathy, and a habit of thoughtful communication that can serve them for life.
E. In a fast-paced world, the quiet act of writing a letter reminds us that some connections are worth the extra time and care, and that the best things in life are often worth waiting for.
F. For example, many families keep letters from soldiers during wartime as precious memories of courage and love.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前对比即时通讯与写信——短信快但缺温度，写信慢却独特，空后需概括写信的核心价值。A的"slow down, choose our words carefully"与"slower"呼应，"express feelings... lasting connection"与"warmth and thoughtfulness"对应，完整概括写信的意义。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说写信时我们会更认真思考、不急于回复、信件可成纪念品，空后需说明写信的努力与信件作为礼物的价值。B的"The effort we put into writing shows the recipient that they matter"与"reflect more deeply"呼应，"gift that can be read and reread"与"keepsakes"逻辑一致。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说历史上书信的作用——科学家、作家、领袖通过信件交流，今人研究旧信了解历史，空后需概括信件的双重属性。C的"not only personal; they can also be historical documents"与前文私人交流与历史记录呼应，"future generations use to learn"与"historians and biographers study"衔接。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说学校和教师在重新重视写信——跨国笔友、写感谢信等，空后需总结写信教育对学生的益处。D的"Teaching young people to write letters helps them develop writing skills, empathy, and... thoughtful communication"与前文教学实践对应，"serve them for life"强调长期价值。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说不必每天写，但保留写信习惯有益，举例生日卡、感谢信、给祖父母的信，空后需总结写信在快节奏世界中的意义。E的"In a fast-paced world"与"instant messages"形成对比，"some connections are worth the extra time"与"brighten someone's day and strengthen a bond"呼应，收尾升华。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, many families keep letters from soldiers during wartime as precious memories of courage and love.**：以For example开头，为具体场景举例（战时士兵信件）。各空均为概括句；[3]虽涉及历史信件，但需的是"个人与历史双重属性"的概括，而非单一战时案例。
