# 04-01 第二篇

**Coral Reefs: Rainforests of the Sea**

Coral reefs are often called the "rainforests of the sea." Though they cover less than 1% of the ocean floor, they are home to about 25% of all marine species. [1] This makes them one of the most valuable ecosystems on our planet.

Corals are tiny animals called polyps (珊瑚虫) that live in groups. They build hard skeletons over time, and these form the reef structures we see. [2] In this way, reefs grow slowly but can span huge areas.

Coral reefs bring many benefits to people. They protect coastlines from storms and support fishing that feeds millions. [3] Tourism to see reefs also brings income to many coastal communities.

Sadly, coral reefs face serious threats. Rising ocean temperatures cause bleaching (白化) — when corals lose their color and may die. [4] Pollution and overfishing add to the problem, making it harder for reefs to recover.

[5] The good news is that we can all help protect them. Simple actions like using less plastic and learning about ocean life can make a difference.

## 选项

A. For this reason, scientists compare them to rainforests, which also hold a large share of Earth's biodiversity (生物多样性).
B. As new polyps grow on top of old ones, the reef continues to expand.
C. In addition, some medicines have been discovered from chemicals found in reef organisms (生物).
D. When this happens, the corals become weak and often cannot survive.
E. It may seem that these problems are too big for ordinary people to solve.
F. Many countries are now working together to create protected areas for coral reefs.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：因果衔接与标题呼应。空前指出珊瑚礁"覆盖不到1%的海底却容纳约25%的海洋物种"，空后总结"这使得它们成为地球上最有价值的生态系统之一"。A选项"For this reason, scientists compare them to rainforests, which also hold a large share of Earth's biodiversity"（因此，科学家将它们与雨林类比，雨林同样拥有地球生物多样性的一大部分）中"For this reason"承接前文数据，"compare them to rainforests"直接呼应标题"Rainforests of the Sea"，"hold a large share of Earth's biodiversity"与"25% of all marine species"形成同义呼应，自然引出空后的"valuable ecosystems"。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：过程描述与空后递进。空前说明珊瑚虫"逐步形成我们所见礁体"，空后"In this way, reefs grow slowly but can span huge areas"进一步说明礁体生长方式。B选项"As new polyps grow on top of old ones, the reef continues to expand"（随着新的珊瑚虫在旧的之上生长，礁体持续扩大）具体解释礁体如何形成与扩展，"expand"与空后"grow"、"span huge areas"语义连贯，构成完整的生长逻辑。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：并列列举与功能补充。空前已列举"保护海岸线"和"支撑渔业"，空后补充"观赏礁体的旅游业也为许多沿海社区带来收入"。C选项"In addition, some medicines have been discovered from chemicals found in reef organisms"（此外，一些药物是从礁体生物中发现的化学物质中研制出来的）用"In addition"引出另一类人文价值——药用，"medicines"与"organisms"（生物）均为考纲词汇，与前后列举的"渔业""旅游业"形成并列，共同说明珊瑚礁对人类的多重益处。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：指代衔接与因果链。空前描述"海洋温度上升导致白化——珊瑚失去颜色并可能死亡"，空后提到"污染和过度捕捞加剧问题"。D选项"When this happens, the corals become weak and often cannot survive"（当这种情况发生时，珊瑚会变弱且往往无法存活）中"When this happens"明确指代前文"bleaching"现象，"become weak...cannot survive"具体化白化的后果，与空后"making it harder for reefs to recover"形成因果链，逻辑顺畅。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：转折铺垫与收束过渡。空[5]位于结尾段段首，前文已述珊瑚礁面临的威胁。空后"The good news is that we can all help protect them"转而给出积极建议。E选项"It may seem that these problems are too big for ordinary people to solve"（似乎这些问题对普通人来说太大而无法解决）先提出读者的常见顾虑，空后"The good news"形成鲜明转折，引出"we can all help"的行动号召，符合"先抑后扬"的议论文收尾结构。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. Many countries are now working together to create protected areas for coral reefs.**：含"protected areas"、"coral reefs"等关键词，且与保护主题相关，易被误选入[4]或[5]。但填[4]时，空前刚说完"白化"现象，需承接白化后果的选项，F转向"各国合作建保护区"会打断"威胁—后果"的因果链；填[5]时，空后是"The good news is that we can all help"，强调个人可为，F强调国家合作，与"we can all help"的个体视角不一致。故F属"主题相关但逻辑角色错位"型干扰。
