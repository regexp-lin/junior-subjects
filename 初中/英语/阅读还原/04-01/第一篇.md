# 04-01 第一篇

**The Science of Laughter**

Have you ever wondered why we laugh? Laughter is a universal human experience that appears in every culture around the world. [1] Scientists believe that laughter first evolved as a way for early humans to strengthen social bonds and show that they meant no harm. Today, a good laugh can still bring people together and ease tension in a room.

Laughter is not just fun—it is also good for your health. When you laugh, your body releases endorphins, the natural "feel-good" chemicals that reduce stress and pain. [2] Your heart rate and blood pressure may drop, and your muscles relax. Some experts even suggest that laughing regularly can improve your immune system and help you fight off illness more effectively.

Researchers have found that we laugh much more often when we are with other people than when we are alone. [3] In fact, we are about 30 times more likely to laugh in social situations than when watching something funny by ourselves. This suggests that laughter plays a key role in building and maintaining our relationships with others.

Laughter can also be a powerful tool in difficult times. Many people use humor to cope with stress, sadness, or fear. [4] Finding something to laugh about, even in hard situations, can help us feel less alone and more hopeful. Of course, this does not mean we should ignore our real feelings, but a little humor can sometimes make the heavy moments a bit lighter.

[5] Whether you prefer silly jokes, funny movies, or simply spending time with people who make you smile, making room for laughter in your daily life is a simple way to feel better and connect with others.

## 选项

A. If you want to add more laughter to your life, there are many easy ways to do it.
B. Studies show that laughter has physical benefits that go beyond just feeling happy.
C. The reason may surprise you: laughter is mainly a social signal, not a reaction to jokes.
D. For thousands of years, humans have used laughter to communicate safety and friendship.
E. Humor can act as a buffer, helping us face challenges without feeling completely overwhelmed.
F. Interestingly, people who laugh together often report feeling closer to each other afterward.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] D   [2] B   [3] C   [4] E   [5] A

## 深度解析

- **[1] D**：
  - **线索**：历史追溯与同义呼应。空前指出"laughter is a universal human experience"，空后描述"Scientists believe that laughter first evolved...strengthen social bonds...show that they meant no harm"。D选项"For thousands of years, humans have used laughter to communicate safety and friendship"（数千年来，人类用笑声传达安全和友谊）与空后"evolved...strengthen social bonds...meant no harm"形成同义对应。"communicate safety"对应"meant no harm"，"friendship"对应"social bonds"，且"For thousands of years"与"first evolved"的时间维度衔接自然。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：段落过渡与语义递进。空前描述笑时释放内啡肽、减压镇痛，空后具体列举"heart rate and blood pressure may drop...muscles relax...improve your immune system"。B选项"Studies show that laughter has physical benefits that go beyond just feeling happy"（研究表明笑声带来的身体益处远不止感觉快乐）用"physical benefits"总领空后所列的心率、血压、肌肉、免疫等生理变化，"go beyond just feeling happy"承接前文内啡肽的"feel-good"，逻辑顺畅。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：承上启下与观点引出。空前给出数据"laugh much more often when we are with other people than when we are alone"，空后具体化"In fact, we are about 30 times more likely to laugh in social situations"。C选项"The reason may surprise you: laughter is mainly a social signal, not a reaction to jokes"（原因或许会令你惊讶：笑声主要是一种社交信号，而非对笑话的反应）用"The reason"解释前文现象，"social signal"自然引出空后的"in social situations"及"building and maintaining our relationships"。

- **[4] E**：
  - **线索**：功能定位与语义对应。空前描述"use humor to cope with stress, sadness, or fear"，空后"Finding something to laugh about...can help us feel less alone and more hopeful"。E选项"Humor can act as a buffer, helping us face challenges without feeling completely overwhelmed"（幽默可以充当缓冲，帮助我们在不完全被压垮的情况下面对挑战）中"act as a buffer"与"cope with"呼应，"face challenges"对应"stress, sadness, or fear"，"without feeling completely overwhelmed"与空后"feel less alone and more hopeful"形成同一语义场。

- **[5] A**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与行动建议。空[5]位于结尾段段首，前文已阐述笑声的演化、健康益处、社交功能及应对困难的作用。A选项"If you want to add more laughter to your life, there are many easy ways to do it"（若想让生活多一些笑声，有很多简单方法）用"If you want to"呼应读者，引出空后具体建议"Whether you prefer silly jokes, funny movies, or...spending time with people who make you smile"，"easy ways"与后文列举形成呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. Interestingly, people who laugh together often report feeling closer to each other afterward.**：含"laugh together""feel closer"等关键词，且与空[3]所在段落讨论笑声的社交功能有语义关联。但空[3]前文已有数据"30 times more likely to laugh in social situations"，空后需的是对现象的原因解释（为何社交时更易笑），而非另一条研究结论。F若填[3]会造成重复论证（都在讲笑带来亲近感），且无法解释"为什么社交时笑得更多"这一核心问题，属"语义相关但逻辑角色不当"型干扰。
