# 08-10 第一篇

**How Vaccines Work and Why They Matter**

Vaccines are one of the greatest achievements in medical history. They have saved millions of lives by preventing diseases that once killed or disabled countless people. Smallpox has been wiped out worldwide thanks to vaccination, and polio is close to extinction. [1] Vaccines train our immune system to recognize and fight harmful germs before we get sick. Understanding this simple idea helps explain why vaccination is so important for both individuals and communities.

The basic principle behind vaccines is often called "training" the immune system. [2] A vaccine contains weakened or inactive parts of a virus or bacteria. When injected, these parts cannot cause serious illness, but they prompt the body to produce antibodies. Later, if the real disease enters the body, these antibodies are ready to attack immediately. It is like practicing for a game before the actual match—you are prepared when it matters most.

Vaccination also protects those who cannot be vaccinated. [3] Some people—such as babies, the elderly, or those with weak immune systems—cannot safely receive certain vaccines. When enough people in a community are vaccinated, the disease has fewer places to spread. This "herd immunity" shields the most vulnerable. Getting vaccinated is not only about protecting yourself; it is an act of care for others.

Over the years, vaccines have become safer and more effective. [4] Before any vaccine is approved, it goes through years of testing. Scientists monitor for side effects and study how well it works. Serious reactions are rare, and the benefits of preventing disease far outweigh the risks for nearly everyone. Continuing research aims to develop vaccines for diseases we still cannot prevent.

[5] Vaccines have changed the world for the better, and their role will remain central in the fight against infectious diseases. Staying up to date with recommended vaccinations is one of the simplest and most powerful ways to protect your health and the health of your community. Science has given us these tools; using them wisely benefits us all.

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前列举疫苗成就（消灭天花、接近根除脊髓灰质炎），空后具体说明疫苗如何训练免疫系统。A项"这种'演练'使疫苗如此强大"中"practice run"后文即展开为train the immune system，承上（成就源于此机制）启下（具体原理），衔接自然。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：本段写疫苗含减弱或灭活成分、刺激产生抗体、遇真病原时能立即攻击。B项"身体学会在不面对真实危险的情况下自卫"与weakened/inactive、cannot cause serious illness、produce antibodies逻辑一致。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：段首点明保护无法接种者，后文写群体免疫、shield the vulnerable。C项"社区接种为脆弱人群筑起防护"准确概括本段。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：段中写批准前多年试验、监测副作用、评估效果、严重反应罕见。D项"严格测试确保获批疫苗可信"与段意相符。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：结尾段以[5]开头，后文强调疫苗改变世界、及时接种的重要性、善用科学工具。E项"公共卫生的未来取决于我们今日的选择"总领全段。
