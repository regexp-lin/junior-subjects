# 08-10 第二篇

**Developing Critical Thinking Skills**

Critical thinking is the ability to analyze information, question assumptions, and form well-reasoned judgments. It is not about criticizing everything or being negative; it is about thinking clearly and independently. In a world full of opinions, ads, and fake news, this skill has never been more important. [1] Critical thinking helps us separate facts from opinions and make better decisions in school, work, and daily life. Learning to think this way takes practice, but the benefits last a lifetime.

One way to develop critical thinking is to ask good questions. [2] Instead of accepting information at face value, ask: Who said this? What evidence supports it? Are there other viewpoints? Questions like these push you to dig deeper and avoid being misled. Teachers who encourage questioning in class help students become more independent thinkers. The habit of asking "why" and "how" is the foundation of critical thinking.

Another important practice is to consider multiple perspectives. [3] When you face a difficult issue, try to understand different sides before forming your own view. Reading articles from various sources, talking to people with different experiences, and playing "devil's advocate" can open your mind. Seeing an issue from several angles does not mean you cannot have strong opinions—it means your opinions will be better informed.

Be aware of your own biases. [4] We all tend to favor information that confirms what we already believe and ignore what challenges us. Recognizing this tendency is the first step toward overcoming it. Ask yourself: Am I agreeing with this because it is true, or because it feels comfortable? Critical thinkers regularly check their assumptions and stay open to changing their minds when the evidence shifts.

[5] Critical thinking is a skill that grows stronger with use. Start small—question one advertisement, fact-check one headline, or debate one topic with a friend. Over time, these habits become natural. In a world that often values quick answers, taking time to think deeply is a powerful advantage.
