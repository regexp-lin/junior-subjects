# 04-30 第一篇

**Knowing CPR: A Skill That Can Save Lives**

When someone's heart stops beating, every second counts. Cardiopulmonary resuscitation（心肺复苏）, or CPR, is a first-aid skill that can help save a life before medical help arrives. [1] Learning basic CPR takes only a few hours, and anyone can do it—even teenagers. You do not need to be a doctor or a nurse. The main idea is simple: push hard and fast on the person's chest to keep blood moving through the body until an ambulance（救护车） comes. Many lives have been saved because ordinary people knew what to do in those important first minutes.

How does CPR work? When the heart stops, blood stops flowing to the brain and other organs（器官）. [2] Without oxygen-rich blood, the brain can suffer serious damage within minutes. CPR keeps the blood moving by pressing on the chest. Each push acts like a heartbeat. Doing this correctly can double or even triple the chance of survival. That is why learning the right method matters. You should place your hands in the center of the chest and push down about 5 to 6 centimeters（厘米） deep, at a speed of 100 to 120 pushes per minute.

When should you use CPR? Use it only when a person is not breathing and has no pulse（脉搏）. [3] First, check if the person is responding—tap their shoulder and ask loudly, "Are you okay?" If there is no response, call 120 for help right away. While waiting for the ambulance, start CPR if you have been trained. Never practice CPR on a healthy person, as it can cause harm. Remember: your goal is to keep the person alive until professional help arrives.

Many schools and communities now offer free CPR training. [4] Students, parents, teachers, and office workers can all join these courses. In some places, learning CPR is even part of the school curriculum. The training usually includes practice on a special dummy（假人） so that learners can get a feel for the correct pressure and speed. After completing the course, you will feel more confident and ready to act if an emergency happens.

[5] In short, CPR is a life-saving skill that everyone can learn. You may never need to use it, but if you do, you could save a family member, a friend, or a stranger. Taking a few hours to learn CPR could make you a hero one day.

## 深度解析

- **[1] C**：
  - **线索**：引出 CPR 的易学性与适用人群。空前说明"CPR is a first-aid skill that can help save a life"，空后列举"Learning basic CPR takes only a few hours...anyone can do it—even teenagers...do not need to be a doctor or nurse...main idea is simple"。C 选项"In fact, anyone from teenagers to adults can learn it in a short time"（事实上，从青少年到成年人都可以在短时间内学会）中"anyone...teenagers to adults"与空后"anyone...even teenagers"精准对应，"learn it in a short time"与"takes only a few hours"呼应，自然引出下文，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] A**：
  - **线索**：解释 CPR 的原理——心脏骤停后为何需要立即施救。空前描述"When the heart stops, blood stops flowing to the brain and other organs"，空后说明"Without oxygen-rich blood, the brain can suffer serious damage within minutes...CPR keeps the blood moving"。A 选项"In other words, the brain and other organs will die quickly without a constant supply of blood"（换言之，没有持续的血液供应，大脑和其他器官会很快衰竭）中"In other words"对上文进行解释，"brain and other organs...without...blood"与空前"blood stops flowing to the brain and other organs"及空后"Without oxygen-rich blood, the brain can suffer serious damage"完美呼应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] B**：
  - **线索**：强调正确施救流程与及时行动的重要性。空前说明"Use it only when a person is not breathing and has no pulse"，空后详述"First, check if the person is responding...call 120...start CPR if trained...Never practice on a healthy person...goal is to keep the person alive until professional help arrives"。B 选项"That is why it is important to act quickly and correctly when someone needs CPR"（因此当有人需要 CPR 时，迅速且正确地行动很重要）中"That is why"承接空前使用条件，"act quickly and correctly"与空后检查、呼叫、施救的步骤呼应，衔接自然。

- **[4] E**：
  - **线索**：介绍 CPR 培训的开放性与参与对象。空前说明"Many schools and communities now offer free CPR training"，空后列举"Students, parents, teachers, and office workers can all join these courses...In some places, learning CPR is part of the school curriculum"。E 选项"These courses are open to people of all ages and backgrounds"（这些课程面向所有年龄和背景的人开放）中"These courses"指代 training，"people of all ages and backgrounds"与空后"Students, parents, teachers, and office workers"精准对应，衔接得当。

- **[5] D**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后列举"CPR is a life-saving skill that everyone can learn...may never need to use it, but if you do, you could save...Taking a few hours to learn CPR could make you a hero"。D 选项"In summary, this simple skill can mean the difference between life and death"（总之，这项简单技能可能意味着生与死的差别）用"In summary"总结全文，"simple skill...life and death"与空后"life-saving skill...save...hero"形成升华，收束精准。

  - **F. For example, some countries have made it a rule that certain jobs require CPR training.**：涉及 CPR 培训的强制性规定，易被误填[4]。但 F 聚焦"某些国家规定特定工作必须接受 CPR 培训"，而空[4]前后文讨论的是培训的开放性、参与对象（学生、家长、教师等）及学校课程，未提及强制性规定或特定职业；F 为举例拓展，与空后"Students, parents, teachers...can all join"的并列列举逻辑不符。F 属"主题相关但内容与上下文不匹配"型干扰。
