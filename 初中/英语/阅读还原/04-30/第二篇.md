# 04-30 第二篇

**Waste Sorting: Small Actions, Big Changes**

Every day, we throw away plastic bottles, food waste, paper, and many other things. Where does all this garbage go? In the past, most of it was buried or burned, which polluted the soil, air, and water. [1] Today, more and more cities are asking people to sort their waste before throwing it away. By separating recyclable materials from other trash, we can turn waste into useful resources again. Recycling（回收） helps reduce pollution and save energy. It is a simple habit that can make a real difference.

How do we sort waste? In China, waste is usually divided into four types: recyclables, kitchen waste, harmful waste, and other waste. [2] Recyclables include paper, plastic bottles, glass, and metal that can be made into new products. Kitchen waste refers to food scraps and leftovers that can be used to make fertilizer（肥料）. Harmful waste includes batteries, medicine, and chemicals that need special handling. Other waste is everything that does not fit the first three groups. Learning these categories takes a little time, but it becomes easy with practice.

Why is waste sorting so important? [3] When we mix all our garbage together, recyclable materials get dirty and hard to reuse. They may end up in landfills（垃圾填埋场） instead of recycling centers. On the other hand, when we sort correctly, more materials can be recycled and fewer new resources need to be taken from nature. For example, recycling one ton of paper can save about 17 trees. The benefits are clear: proper sorting leads to more recycling and less waste.

Waste sorting also needs everyone's effort. [4] Families can place different bins in the kitchen for different types of waste. Schools can teach students how to sort and organize recycling campaigns. Communities can set up clear signs and labels to help people sort correctly. When everyone does their part, the whole system works better. Some cities have even started to reward people who sort well or fine those who do not follow the rules. The goal is to build a habit that lasts a lifetime.

[5] From homes to schools to communities, waste sorting is a team effort that protects our planet. It may seem like a small thing, but when millions of people do it, the impact is huge. Let us start today and make every piece of trash count.

## 选项

A. In short, waste sorting is a simple step toward a cleaner and greener future.
B. That is why governments and communities are working together to make sorting easier for everyone.
C. Each type has its own purpose and should be placed in the right bin.
D. The reason is that mixed waste is much harder and more expensive to recycle.
E. Many people used to think that recycling was too troublesome to bother with.
F. For example, some families use different colored bags to help children learn how to sort.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] E   [2] C   [3] D   [4] B   [5] A

## 深度解析

- **[1] E**：
  - **线索**：引出垃圾分类的现状转变。空前描述"In the past, most waste was buried or burned...polluted the soil, air, and water"，空后说明"Today, more and more cities are asking people to sort their waste...By separating recyclables...we can turn waste into useful resources...Recycling helps reduce pollution"。E选项"Many people used to think that recycling was too troublesome to bother with"（许多人过去认为回收太麻烦而不愿去做）中"used to think...too troublesome"与空前"in the past...buried or burned"形成对比，"recycling"自然过渡到空后"sort...separating...recycling"，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] C**：
  - **线索**：四种垃圾分类及各自用途。空前说明"waste is usually divided into four types: recyclables, kitchen waste, harmful waste, other waste"，空后分别解释"Recyclables include...can be made into new products...Kitchen waste refers to...can be used to make fertilizer...Harmful waste includes...need special handling...Other waste is everything that does not fit"。C选项"Each type has its own purpose and should be placed in the right bin"（每种类型都有其用途，应放入正确的垃圾桶）中"Each type...own purpose...right bin"与空后四种类型的详细说明精准对应，衔接自然。

- **[3] D**：
  - **线索**：垃圾分类重要性的原因。空前提问"Why is waste sorting so important?"，空后详述"When we mix all our garbage together, recyclable materials get dirty and hard to reuse...end up in landfills instead of recycling centers...when we sort correctly, more materials can be recycled...fewer new resources need to be taken"。D选项"The reason is that mixed waste is much harder and more expensive to recycle"（原因在于混合垃圾回收起来更难、成本更高）直接回答问句，"mixed waste...harder and more expensive"与空后"mix...get dirty and hard to reuse...end up in landfills"完美呼应，逻辑紧密。

- **[4] B**：
  - **线索**：垃圾分类需要各方共同努力与支持。空前说明"Waste sorting also needs everyone's effort"，空后列举"Families can place different bins...Schools can teach students...Communities can set up clear signs...reward...fine...goal is to build a habit"。B选项"That is why governments and communities are working together to make sorting easier for everyone"（因此政府和社区正在共同努力，让每个人都能更容易地进行分类）中"That is why"承接空前"needs everyone's effort"，"working together...make sorting easier"与空后家庭、学校、社区的举措呼应，衔接得当。

- **[5] A**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后列举"From homes to schools to communities...team effort that protects our planet...small thing...when millions do it, impact is huge...make every piece of trash count"。A选项"In short, waste sorting is a simple step toward a cleaner and greener future"（简言之，垃圾分类是迈向更清洁、更绿色未来的简单一步）用"In short"总结全文，"simple step...cleaner and greener future"与空后"small thing...huge impact...protects our planet"形成呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, some families use different colored bags to help children learn how to sort.**：举例家庭分类做法，易被误填[2]或[4]。但空[2]讨论的是四种垃圾类型的定义与用途，F的"colored bags...help children learn"是具体方法，与空后"Recyclables include...Kitchen waste refers to..."的定义列举逻辑不符；空[4]讨论各方努力，空后已列举"Families can place different bins...Schools...Communities..."，F仅为家庭一例，且"help children learn"与"place bins...teach...set up signs"的并列结构相比过于细化。F属"内容相关但层级与衔接功能错位"型干扰。
