# 09-05 第一篇

**The Value of Handwriting**

In an age of keyboards and touchscreens, handwriting may seem like an outdated skill. Many people type faster than they write, and digital tools make it easy to create clean, readable text. Yet handwriting remains important, and researchers have found that it offers benefits that typing simply cannot match. [1] Far from being a relic of the past, handwriting is a powerful tool for learning, creativity, and personal expression.

Studies show that writing by hand helps us learn and remember better. When we write notes by hand, our brains process the information more deeply than when we type. [2] This is because handwriting forces us to slow down, think about what we are writing, and put ideas into our own words rather than simply copying them.

Handwriting also supports creativity. Many writers, artists, and inventors prefer to sketch ideas and draft plans by hand. [3] The physical act of putting pen to paper seems to unlock a different kind of thinking—one that is more free-flowing, personal, and imaginative.

For young children, learning to write by hand is especially important. It helps develop fine motor skills, hand-eye coordination, and letter recognition. [4] These early handwriting experiences lay the foundation for reading and writing abilities that children will build upon throughout their education.

Of course, typing skills are essential in the modern world, and technology has made communication faster and easier. But handwriting does not need to be replaced—it can work alongside digital tools. [5] Keeping handwriting alive in schools and daily life ensures that we do not lose an important skill that benefits the brain, supports learning, and connects us to centuries of human tradition.

## 选项

A. Handwriting is far more than just forming letters—it is a cognitive activity that strengthens how we think and learn.
B. Students who take handwritten notes tend to understand and recall material better than those who type their notes.
C. The freedom of a blank page and a pen allows the mind to wander and make unexpected connections.
D. Without these foundational skills, children may struggle with literacy tasks as they progress through school.
E. By valuing both handwriting and technology, we give ourselves the best of both worlds.
F. For example, many famous authors, including J.K. Rowling, wrote their first drafts entirely by hand.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前说手写有打字无法替代的好处，空后说手写是学习、创造力和个人表达的有力工具。A的"far more than just forming letters"承接手写的深层价值，"cognitive activity that strengthens how we think and learn"概括认知益处。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说手写笔记时大脑处理更深入，空后解释原因（放慢速度、思考、用自己的话）。B的"Students who take handwritten notes tend to understand and recall material better"承接前文研究发现，具体对比手写与打字的效果。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说许多作家、艺术家更喜欢手写构思，空后说笔触纸的动作似乎开启了不同的思维方式。C的"freedom of a blank page and a pen"承接手写场景，"allows the mind to wander and make unexpected connections"解释创造性思维的机制。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说手写帮助儿童发展精细运动、手眼协调和字母识别，空后说这些早期经验为读写能力奠定基础。D的"Without these foundational skills"承接前文能力列举，"children may struggle with literacy tasks"从反面强调重要性。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说手写可以与数字工具并行，空后说在学校和日常生活中保持手写很重要。E的"valuing both handwriting and technology"概括两者兼顾，"the best of both worlds"升华主题。

- **干扰项分析**：
