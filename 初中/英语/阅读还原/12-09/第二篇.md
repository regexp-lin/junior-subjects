**Robotics: Machines That Think and Move**

Robots are machines that can perform tasks automatically or with little human help. They range from simple devices that vacuum floors to complex machines that assemble cars or perform surgery. Robotics combines engineering, computer science, and mathematics to create machines that can sense, plan, and act. Today, robots are found in factories, hospitals, homes, and even space. [1]

Industrial robots have been used in manufacturing for decades. They weld, paint, and assemble products with speed and precision. Unlike humans, they can work around the clock without getting tired. This has made many factories more efficient but has also raised concerns about jobs. Some workers have been replaced by robots, while others have learned new skills to work alongside them. [2]

Robots are now entering everyday life. Home robots can clean floors, mow lawns, and even serve as companions for the elderly. In hospitals, robots assist surgeons and deliver supplies. Drones fly over fields to monitor crops or deliver packages. As technology improves, robots are becoming safer, cheaper, and more capable. [3]

Artificial intelligence (AI) is making robots smarter. They can recognize faces, understand speech, and learn from experience. Self-driving cars use AI to navigate roads. Some robots can adapt to new situations without being reprogrammed. With AI, robots are moving from simple, repetitive tasks to more complex and flexible roles. [4]

The rise of robots raises both excitement and concern. They can improve safety, health, and convenience. They may also change how we work and live. Society will need to adapt—through education, new jobs, and thoughtful policies—to make the most of robotics while addressing its challenges. [5]

## 选项

A. In short, robotics is a field that is reshaping how we live and work.
B. This shift has changed manufacturing forever, with both benefits and costs.
C. These applications show that robots are no longer limited to factories.
D. As a result, robots are becoming more intelligent and versatile than ever before.
E. How we respond to this change will define the future of work and daily life.
F. For example, the robot "Pepper" was designed to read human emotions and respond with empathy.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍机器人——自动或少量人力完成任务的机器、从扫地机到汽车组装和手术、结合工程计算机与数学、能感知规划行动、出现在工厂医院家庭和太空，空后需总结机器人与生活工作的关系。A的"In short"承接前文，"reshaping how we live and work"与前文对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说工业机器人用于制造数十年、焊接喷漆组装快而准、可全天工作、提高效率但引发就业担忧、部分工人被替代部分学会与机器人协作，空后需总结制造业的变革及其双面性。B的"This shift"指代前文，"changed manufacturing forever, with both benefits and costs"与前文呼应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说机器人进入日常生活——家用清洁割草陪伴老人、医院辅助手术与配送、无人机农田监测或快递、更安全便宜能力强，空后需总结机器人应用已超出工厂。C的"These applications"指代前文，"robots are no longer limited to factories"与前文对应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说AI使机器人更聪明——识别人脸理解语音、从经验学习、自动驾驶导航、可适应新情境无需重编程、从简单重复到复杂灵活，空后需总结智能与适应性提升。D的"As a result"承接前文，"more intelligent and versatile than ever before"与前文呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说机器人崛起带来兴奋与担忧、可提升安全健康便利、也可能改变工作与生活、社会需通过教育新岗位与政策去适应与应对，空后需总结应对方式对未来工作与生活的意义。E的"How we respond to this change will define the future of work and daily life"与前文对应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the robot "Pepper" was designed to read human emotions...**：以For example开头，为具体机器人案例（Pepper情感识别）。各空均为概括或过渡句；[4]易被误选因涉及智能，但该空需总结智能与适应性的整体提升，而非补充单一机器人的功能举例。
