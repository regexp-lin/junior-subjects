**Genetics and DNA: The Blueprint of Life**

DNA is often called the blueprint of life. It is a molecule found in almost every cell of every living thing. DNA carries the instructions that tell cells how to grow, develop, and function. Your eye colour, height, and many health traits are influenced by the genes stored in your DNA. Understanding DNA helps us understand why we look and behave the way we do. [1]

Genes are sections of DNA that hold specific information. You inherit half your genes from each parent, which is why you may look like your mother or father. Some traits, like blood type, are controlled by a single gene. Others, like height or intelligence, come from many genes working together. The mix of genes you receive is unique to you. [2]

Scientists have learned to read and even edit DNA. This has led to advances in medicine, such as finding genes linked to diseases and developing new treatments. In agriculture, DNA technology helps create crops that resist pests or grow in difficult conditions. Some questions about using this power—such as editing human embryos—remain highly debated. [3]

Genetics also explains similarities and differences between species. Humans share about 98% of their DNA with chimpanzees. Even plants and bacteria have DNA. By comparing DNA across species, scientists can trace how life has evolved over millions of years. This knowledge helps us see our place in the natural world. [4]

DNA research continues to open new doors. It can help solve crimes, identify ancient remains, and improve food production. At the same time, we must use this knowledge responsibly. The more we learn about genetics, the more important it becomes to balance science with ethics. [5]
