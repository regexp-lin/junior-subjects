# 07-04 第二篇

**Why Teamwork Matters**

Whether you are working on a school project or playing a sport, teamwork is a skill you will use throughout your life. [1] When people work together toward a shared goal, they can achieve much more than they could alone. Each person brings different strengths and ideas, and combining these leads to better results. Teamwork also helps build friendships and teaches us how to cooperate with others. Learning to work well in a team is one of the most valuable lessons you can learn in school and beyond.

[2] Good communication is the foundation of successful teamwork. Team members need to share ideas clearly and listen to each other. When someone has a question, they should feel comfortable asking it. When there is a problem, discussing it openly helps find solutions. Using tools like group chats or meeting regularly can keep everyone on the same page. Remember that communication is not just about talking—it is also about listening and understanding different points of view. Without clear communication, even the best team can fail.

[3] Every team needs different roles and skills. Some people are good at organizing, others at creating new ideas, and others at paying attention to details. When everyone contributes what they do best, the team becomes stronger. It is important to respect each member's role and avoid thinking that one job is more important than another. A project needs both the big-picture thinkers and the people who make sure every small task is done well. Celebrating each person's contribution helps the whole team feel valued.

[4] Conflicts are normal when people work together. People may disagree about ideas, ways of working, or how to divide tasks. The key is to handle conflicts in a positive way. Instead of arguing, try to understand why others think differently. Focus on the goal rather than on who is right or wrong. Sometimes a compromise—finding a solution that works for everyone—is the best approach. When conflicts are resolved well, the team often becomes stronger and more trusting.

[5] Teamwork skills will benefit you in countless situations. In school, group projects prepare you for future work. In sports, teamwork leads to victory. In your community, working with others can create positive change. Start practicing these skills now—listen to your teammates, share your ideas, and be willing to help when others need support. The ability to work well with others is one of the keys to success in life. Remember: together we are stronger.

## 选项

A. Working together allows people to achieve more and learn important life skills.
B. Clear and open communication is essential for any team to succeed.
C. A strong team makes use of each member's unique strengths and contributions.
D. Handling disagreements positively can actually strengthen a team.
E. The ability to work in a team will serve you well in many areas of life.
F. For example, a football team needs strikers, midfielders, and defenders to win games.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前提出团队合作的应用场景（学校项目、运动），空后具体阐述合作的好处——集体成就超越个人、取长补短、增进友谊、培养合作能力。A选项"When people work together toward a shared goal, they can achieve much more than they could alone"及后续概括完美承上启下，将"团队合作"具体化为"共同目标下的协作"，与后文"different strengths""better results"紧密衔接。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：第二段全段论述沟通的重要性——清晰表达、倾听、提问、讨论问题、工具辅助、听与说并重。B选项"Clear and open communication is essential for any team to succeed"准确概括段落主题。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：第三段聚焦角色分工——不同人擅长组织、创意或细节，各展所长、相互尊重、贡献得到认可。C选项"A strong team makes use of each member's unique strengths and contributions"精准总结段落主旨。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：第四段讨论冲突处理——冲突正常、积极应对、理解他人、关注目标、妥协、化解后团队更强大。D选项"Handling disagreements positively can actually strengthen a team"准确概括段落的辩证观点。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：末段总结全文，列举团队技能在多领域的应用（学业、运动、社区），鼓励当下练习。E选项"The ability to work in a team will serve you well in many areas of life"精准呼应段落的总结性主旨。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example...**：以"For example"开头，举足球需要前锋、中场、后卫的例子。虽与团队角色分工相关，但它是支撑第三段论点的具体论据，而非段落的概括句。若放入[3]会造成层次错位——F是例证层，正确选项C是论点/主题概括层。
