# 09-11 第二篇

**Mental Health Matters for Teens**

Being a teenager brings many changes—new schools, new friends, new pressures. Homework, exams, and social life can feel overwhelming at times. It is normal to have ups and downs, but when stress, sadness, or worry last too long, they may affect your mental health. [1] Taking care of your mind is just as important as taking care of your body, and knowing when to ask for help is a sign of strength, not weakness.

One way to support your mental health is to stay connected. Talking to family, friends, or teachers when you feel down can lighten the load. Sometimes just knowing that someone listens makes a big difference. [2] Sharing your feelings does not mean you are a burden; it often strengthens relationships and helps others understand how to support you.

Another important habit is to take breaks. Constant study without rest can lead to burnout and anxiety. Exercise, hobbies, and enough sleep all help your brain recover and stay balanced. [3] Building time for activities you enjoy into your daily routine can improve both your mood and your ability to focus when you return to work.

Social media can add extra pressure. Comparing yourself to others online often leads to feelings of not being good enough. It helps to limit screen time and to remember that what people post is often a highlight reel, not real life. [4] Being mindful of how you use technology can protect your mental health and free up time for things that truly matter.

If you or someone you know is struggling, professional help is available. School counselors, doctors, and mental health services are there to support you. [5] Reaching out for help when you need it is a courageous step, and it can lead to better coping skills and a brighter outlook on life.

## 选项

A. Recognizing that mental health deserves the same attention as physical health is the foundation of wellbeing.
B. Opening up to people you trust can relieve stress and remind you that you are not alone.
C. Taking care of yourself through rest and fun is not lazy—it is necessary for long-term success.
D. Learning to use social media in a healthy way can reduce stress and improve your sense of self-worth.
E. There is no shame in asking for support, and doing so often leads to real improvement.
F. For example, some schools now offer yoga or meditation classes to help students manage stress.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述青春期压力与情绪波动，空后强调"Taking care of your mind is just as important as taking care of your body... knowing when to ask for help is a sign of strength"。A的"Recognizing that mental health deserves the same attention as physical health is the foundation of wellbeing"与心理健康与身体同等重要的主题完美呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说与家人、朋友、老师倾诉可以减压，空后说"Sharing your feelings does not mean you are a burden... strengthens relationships"。B的"Opening up to people you trust can relieve stress and remind you that you are not alone"与倾诉、被倾听、减轻负担的逻辑一致，概括该段主旨。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前讨论休息、运动、爱好与睡眠的重要性，空后说"Building time for activities you enjoy... can improve both your mood and your ability to focus"。C的"Taking care of yourself through rest and fun is not lazy—it is necessary for long-term success"准确概括休息与兴趣活动的价值，与"improve mood and focus"呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前讨论社交媒体带来的比较压力与应对方法，空后说"Being mindful of how you use technology can protect your mental health"。D的"Learning to use social media in a healthy way can reduce stress and improve your sense of self-worth"与健康使用科技、保护心理健康的主题对应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍可求助的专业资源，空后说"Reaching out for help when you need it is a courageous step... leads to better coping skills and a brighter outlook"。E的"There is no shame in asking for support, and doing so often leads to real improvement"升华主题，与"courageous step"和"brighter outlook"完美对应，适合作全文收束。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, some schools now offer yoga or meditation classes to help students manage stress.**：以For example开头，属具体举例；各空均为概括或过渡句，F与减压方法相关但为个案，易误放[3]，但该空需休息与兴趣活动的概括，非举例。
