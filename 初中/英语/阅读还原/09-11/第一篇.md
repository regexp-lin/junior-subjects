# 09-11 第一篇

**Exploring Outer Space**

Humans have always looked up at the stars and wondered what lies beyond. Today, we have sent rockets to the Moon, rovers to Mars, and telescopes deep into space. Space exploration has taught us about our solar system, our place in the universe, and the possibilities of life elsewhere. [1] Pushing the boundaries of what we know inspires curiosity, drives innovation, and reminds us that we are part of something far greater than ourselves.

Early space missions focused on reaching the Moon and returning safely. Those achievements required thousands of scientists, engineers, and workers working together. The technology developed for space—from better computers to improved medical equipment—has found uses here on Earth. [2] The benefits of space exploration extend far beyond the stars, improving daily life in ways we often do not notice.

Today, Mars is a major target. Robotic rovers explore its surface, searching for signs of past water and life. Private companies are developing rockets to send humans to Mars within the next few decades. [3] Reaching another planet would be a historic achievement, opening the door to questions we have never been able to answer before.

Space is also full of dangers. Astronauts face radiation, isolation, and the risk of equipment failure. Long missions require careful planning and new technologies to keep crews safe. [4] Overcoming these challenges will be essential if humanity is to travel farther and stay longer in space.

Why do we explore space when there are so many problems on Earth? Some say the resources spent on space could help the poor or protect the environment. Others argue that space inspires young people to study science and that new discoveries help us all. [5] The debate will continue, but one thing is clear: the human desire to explore and learn will keep driving us toward the stars.

## 选项

A. Seeking answers to questions about the universe has always been a source of inspiration and progress for humanity.
B. Many everyday technologies we use today grew out of research done for space missions.
C. Success in this goal would mark a new chapter in human history and our understanding of the cosmos.
D. Solving these problems is a necessary step before we can dream of colonies on other worlds.
E. Whatever the answer, the spirit of exploration remains a powerful force in human culture and progress.
F. For example, the Apollo 11 mission landed the first humans on the Moon in July 1969.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述人类对星空的向往及太空探索成就，空后强调"Pushing the boundaries... inspires curiosity, drives innovation, and reminds us"。A的"Seeking answers to questions about the universe has always been a source of inspiration and progress"与探索动力和进步主题完美呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前描述早期登月任务及航天衍生技术，空后说"The benefits of space exploration extend far beyond the stars, improving daily life in ways we often do not notice"。B的"Many everyday technologies we use today grew out of research done for space missions"准确概括技术转移，与"improving daily life"呼应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前讨论火星探测与载人火星计划，空后说"Reaching another planet would be a historic achievement, opening the door to questions we have never been able to answer"。C的"Success in this goal would mark a new chapter in human history and our understanding of the cosmos"与"historic achievement"和"new questions"完美对应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前列举太空危险（辐射、隔绝、设备故障）及应对需求，空后说"Overcoming these challenges will be essential if humanity is to travel farther and stay longer"。D的"Solving these problems is a necessary step before we can dream of colonies on other worlds"与克服挑战、实现长远目标逻辑一致。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前呈现关于太空探索价值的正反辩论，空后无。E的"Whatever the answer, the spirit of exploration remains a powerful force in human culture and progress"升华主题，与"human desire to explore and learn will keep driving us"完美对应，适合作全文收束。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the Apollo 11 mission landed the first humans on the Moon in July 1969.**：以For example开头，属具体历史事件举例；各空均为概括或过渡句，F与登月相关但为个案，易误放[1]或[2]，但两空分别需探索意义概述与技术转移概括，非举例。
