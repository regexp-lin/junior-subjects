# 04-07 第一篇

**The Amazing Intelligence of Octopuses**

Octopuses are among the most intelligent animals in the ocean. Unlike most sea creatures, they can solve puzzles, use tools, and even escape from tanks. [1] Their brains are spread throughout their bodies, with two-thirds of their neurons in their arms. This means each arm can partly "think" on its own, helping the octopus feel, taste, and react to its surroundings in amazing ways.

Scientists have watched octopuses do things that seem almost human. In one experiment, an octopus learned to unscrew a jar to get food inside. [2] Another octopus was seen stacking rocks to block light from its tank, showing that it could plan ahead. These behaviors suggest that octopuses are not simply following instincts—they can learn, remember, and make decisions based on past experience.

Octopuses are also masters of disguise. They can change their color, texture, and even shape to blend in with rocks, seaweed, or coral. [3] Special cells called chromatophores in their skin expand and contract, creating patterns that match their environment. When an octopus is scared, it might turn pale; when it is angry, it can flash bright colors. This ability helps them hide from predators and sneak up on prey.

Despite their intelligence, octopuses live short lives—most species live only one to two years. [4] They do not take care of their young after the eggs hatch. Instead, the mother octopus guards her eggs until she dies, never leaving to eat. Scientists wonder why such smart animals evolved to live such brief lives. Some think their short lifespan may be related to their highly active brains and bodies, which use a lot of energy.

[5] Whether we meet them in an aquarium or read about them in books, octopuses continue to surprise us with their cleverness. As we learn more about these strange and wonderful creatures, we gain a deeper respect for the many forms that intelligence can take in the natural world.

## 选项

A. Octopuses can also change their skin texture to look like bumpy rocks or smooth sand.
B. In the wild, they have been observed using coconut shells as portable shelters.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] D   [2] B   [3] F   [4] E   [5] C

## 深度解析

- **[1] D**：
  - **线索**：因果解释与结构衔接。空前总述"intelligent...solve puzzles, use tools, escape"，空后具体描述"Their brains are spread throughout their bodies, with two-thirds of their neurons in their arms"。D选项"What makes octopuses so different is the way their nervous system is built"（章鱼之所以如此不同，在于其神经系统的构造方式）用"what makes...different"引出原因，空后"brains...spread...neurons in their arms"正是"the way their nervous system is built"的具体展开，逻辑衔接自然。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：例证递进与语义呼应。空前举例"unscrew a jar to get food"，空后"Another octopus was seen stacking rocks to block light...showing that it could plan ahead"。B选项"In the wild, they have been observed using coconut shells as portable shelters"（在野外，人们观察到它们用椰子壳当可携带的庇护所）提供另一个工具使用的实例，与空前"use tools"及空后"plan ahead"形成递进：用椰壳当庇护所同样体现规划和工具使用能力，属并列例证。

- **[3] F**：
  - **线索**：机制补充与逻辑递进。空前描述变色能力"change their color, texture, and even shape"，空后解释"Special cells called chromatophores...expand and contract, creating patterns"。F选项"This color change happens in less than a second, making it nearly impossible to spot a hiding octopus"（这种颜色变化在不到一秒内完成，使藏身的章鱼几乎无法被察觉）补充变色速度及效果，与空后chromatophore机制形成递进，"nearly impossible to spot"呼应前文"blend in"和"hide from predators"。

- **[4] E**：
  - **线索**：话题过渡与语义衔接。空前讨论"short lives...one to two years"，空后"They do not take care of their young after the eggs hatch. Instead, the mother octopus guards her eggs until she dies"。E选项"What's more, their approach to parenting is very different from that of mammals"（此外，它们的抚育方式与哺乳动物截然不同）用"What's more"递进引入新话题，"approach to parenting"与空后"do not take care of their young""mother guards her eggs"完美对应，自然引出繁殖与抚育行为的具体描述。

- **[5] C**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与情感升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后"Whether we meet them in an aquarium or read about them in books, octopuses continue to surprise us with their cleverness"。C选项"Yet their short lives do not stop them from doing incredible things"（然而短暂的生命并未阻止它们做出令人惊叹之事）中"incredible things"与空后"continue to surprise us...cleverness"形成呼应，将前文智慧、工具使用、变色、繁殖等特质凝聚为"不因寿命短而逊色"的总结，情感升华到位。
