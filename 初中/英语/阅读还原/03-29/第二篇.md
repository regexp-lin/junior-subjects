# 03-29 第二篇

**The Magic of Reading Aloud**

Many students read silently when they study. They move their eyes quickly across the page and finish a book in no time. However, reading aloud—speaking the words as you read them—offers benefits that silent reading cannot match. [1] It slows you down, which helps your brain process each word and sentence more carefully.

First, reading aloud improves your pronunciation and speaking skills. When you say the words, you practice how they sound. [2] You may discover words you have been mispronouncing for years. Correcting these mistakes can make you feel more confident when giving speeches or joining class discussions.

What is more, reading aloud strengthens your memory. The act of both seeing the words and hearing yourself say them creates a "double pathway" in your brain. [3] Information that travels through two senses often stays in your mind longer than information you only read silently.

Reading aloud is also a great way to share stories with others. Parents often read to young children at bedtime. [4] In a classroom, reading a paragraph aloud can help your classmates follow along and understand the material better. Even reading alone, you become your own listener—and sometimes that is all you need.

[5] Try it with a short article, a poem, or a few pages from your textbook. You might be surprised at how much more you remember—and how much more you enjoy the experience.

## 选项

A. As a result, teachers should ban silent reading and allow only reading aloud in all classrooms.
B. In other words, the more you use your voice when reading, the stronger your memory can become.
C. For this reason, reading aloud has been used for centuries as a powerful learning tool.
D. You might feel a bit silly at first, but the benefits are worth it.
E. The same idea works for older students too—hearing the text can aid understanding.
F. This process gives you a chance to notice mistakes in your pronunciation and correct them.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] C   [2] F   [3] B   [4] E   [5] D

## 深度解析

- **[1] C**：
  - **线索**：因果承接与逻辑过渡。空前指出"朗读提供静读无法比拟的益处"。C选项"For this reason, reading aloud has been used for centuries as a powerful learning tool"（因此，朗读几个世纪以来一直被用作强大的学习工具）中"For this reason"指代前文"offers benefits"。空后"It slows you down, which helps your brain process each word and sentence more carefully"具体展开朗读作为学习工具的第一个表现——放慢速度、加深处理。形成"总述益处→确立工具地位→分述具体好处"的清晰结构。

- **[2] F**：
  - **线索**：因果衔接与代词指代。空前描述"当你说出单词时，你就在练习它们的发音"。F选项"This process gives you a chance to notice mistakes in your pronunciation and correct them"（这个过程让你有机会发现发音错误并纠正）中"This process"指代前文"say the words"的练习过程。空后"You may discover words you have been mispronouncing for years"与F中"notice mistakes in your pronunciation"形成同义呼应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] B**：
  - **线索**：总结阐释与同义复现。空前解释朗读创造"double pathway"—既看见又听见。B选项"In other words, the more you use your voice when reading, the stronger your memory can become"（换句话说，阅读时你越多使用嗓音，记忆力就越强）用"In other words"总结前文"double pathway"对记忆的促进作用。"use your voice"与"hearing yourself say them"呼应；"stronger your memory"与空后"stays in your mind longer"同义衔接。

- **[4] E**：
  - **线索**：递进拓展与代词指代。空前举了"家长给孩子睡前朗读"的例子，说明朗读可用来与他人分享故事。E选项"The same idea works for older students too—hearing the text can aid understanding"（同样的方法对年龄较大的学生也有效——听文本有助于理解）中"The same idea"指代前文"reading to share/help others understand"。空后"In a classroom, reading a paragraph aloud can help your classmates follow along"与E中"older students""hearing the text can aid understanding"完美呼应——从幼儿延伸到课堂中的同学。

- **[5] D**：
  - **线索**：行动号召与心理鼓励。空[5]位于结尾段段首。D选项"You might feel a bit silly at first, but the benefits are worth it"（一开始你可能会觉得有点傻，但好处是值得的）回应可能存在的心理顾虑。空后"Try it with a short article, a poem, or a few pages"是具体的行动建议，与D中"feel a bit silly"形成呼应——正因为可能觉得尴尬，所以先用短材料试试。空后"surprised at how much more you remember...enjoy"对应D的"benefits are worth it"。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **A. As a result, teachers should ban silent reading and allow only reading aloud in all classrooms.**：该选项含"As a result""teachers""reading""classrooms"等关键词，但主张"教师应禁止默读、只允许朗读"与全文立场矛盾。文章强调朗读的额外益处，并未否定默读的价值；且"ban""only"等绝对化表述属于过度推断。属于"关键词+立场极端化"型干扰。
