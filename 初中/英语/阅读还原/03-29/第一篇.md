# 03-29 第一篇

**Why Kindness Matters More Than You Think**

We often think of kindness as something we do for others—holding the door for someone or helping a friend with homework. But did you know that being kind is also good for you? Research shows that acts of kindness can improve both your mood and your health. [1] When you help others, your brain releases chemicals that make you feel happy. This is sometimes called the "helper's high."

Kindness can strengthen your relationships. When you show care for your classmates or family members, they feel valued and are more likely to trust you. [2] Over time, these small acts build stronger connections that can last for years.

What is more, kindness is contagious (传染的). When someone is kind to you, you may want to pass that kindness on to someone else. [3] Before you know it, one simple action has touched many lives. A smile or a thank-you can start a chain of positive behavior.

Kindness also helps reduce stress. When you focus on helping others instead of worrying only about yourself, your problems may seem smaller. [4] Many people find that volunteering or simply being there for a friend makes them feel calmer and more at peace.

[5] You do not need to do anything big. Hold the door, say a kind word, or offer to share your lunch. These small steps can make a real difference—for others and for yourself.

## 选项

A. In other words, kindness creates a positive cycle that spreads from person to person.
B. Therefore, you should never show kindness because it will make you too tired to study.
C. In fact, studies suggest that people who often help others tend to feel less anxious and happier.
D. The best part is that you can start practicing kindness right away—no special skills required.
E. Scientists have found that being kind activates parts of the brain linked to pleasure and reward.
F. A single kind act can lead to another, and another, until kindness becomes a habit in your community.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] E   [2] C   [3] F   [4] A   [5] D

## 深度解析

- **[1] E**：
  - **线索**：科学佐证与因果衔接。空前指出"研究表明善意之举能改善情绪和健康"。E选项"Scientists have found that being kind activates parts of the brain linked to pleasure and reward"（科学家发现善良会激活大脑中与愉悦和奖励相关的区域）用科学研究解释其生理机制。空后"When you help others, your brain releases chemicals that make you feel happy"中的"releases chemicals"与E的"activates...pleasure and reward"形成同义呼应，共同解释"helper's high"。

- **[2] C**：
  - **线索**：总结归纳与同义衔接。空前描述"这些小小的善意举动会随着时间的推移建立更牢固的联系"。C选项"In fact, studies suggest that people who often help others tend to feel less anxious and happier"（事实上，研究表明经常帮助他人的人往往焦虑更少、更快乐）用研究结论概括善意对人际关系和自身情绪的双重益处。空后"Over time...build stronger connections"与C中"often help others"形成逻辑呼应。

- **[3] F**：
  - **线索**：具体阐释"传染性"与递进逻辑。空前指出"善良具有传染性"，当他人对你友善时，你会想把善意传递下去。F选项"A single kind act can lead to another, and another, until kindness becomes a habit in your community"（一次善举会引发另一次，再另一次，直到善良成为你社区的习惯）具体说明"传染"的连锁过程。空后"Before you know it, one simple action has touched many lives"与F中"a single kind act...lead to another"完美呼应。

- **[4] A**：
  - **线索**：总结升华与逻辑衔接。空前说明"专注于帮助他人时，你的问题似乎会变小"，并提及志愿服务、陪伴朋友能让人"feel calmer and more at peace"。A选项"In other words, kindness creates a positive cycle that spreads from person to person"（换句话说，善良创造了一个在人与人之间传播的积极循环）用"In other words"总结善意带来的心理效应。这种"positive cycle"既涵盖前文的减压、平静感，又呼应第三段的"传染性"，将个人受益与传递效应融为一体。

- **[5] D**：
  - **线索**：行动号召与段首过渡。空[5]位于结尾段段首。D选项"The best part is that you can start practicing kindness right away—no special skills required"（最棒的是你可以立即开始练习善良——不需要任何特殊技能）用"The best part"引出行动建议。空后"You do not need to do anything big"与D中"no special skills required""right away"形成呼应，鼓励读者从小事做起。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **B. Therefore, you should never show kindness because it will make you too tired to study.**：该选项含"Therefore""kindness""study"等话题词及因果连接词。但其主张"你绝不应表现善意，因为会让你太累无法学习"与全文大力倡导善良的立场完全对立。全文多处强调善良对身心有益（happy、health、calmer），从未提及"疲劳"。属于"关键词+立场完全相反"型干扰。
