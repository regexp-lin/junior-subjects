# 12-29 第一篇

**Textiles and Fashion History: Threads Through Time**

Cloth has been part of human life for thousands of years. Early people used animal skins and plant fibres to cover their bodies and stay warm. Over time, they learned to spin thread, weave fabric, and dye it with natural colours. Different cultures developed their own styles—silk in China, cotton in India, wool in Europe. Textiles tell the story of trade, technology, and human creativity. [1]

The Industrial Revolution changed how clothes were made. Machines could produce fabric and garments faster and cheaper than before. Factories replaced hand weaving in many places. Ready-made clothing became common, and fashion began to change more quickly with the seasons. This shift laid the groundwork for the modern fashion industry we know today. [2]

Fashion reflects the times. In the 1920s, women's skirts became shorter as society changed. In the 1960s, young people used clothing to express rebellion and new ideas. Today, sustainable fashion and second-hand clothing are growing as people worry about the environment. What we wear has always been linked to who we are and what we value. [3]

Museums and historians preserve garments from the past. Old clothes show how people lived, what materials were available, and how technology advanced. Designers still draw inspiration from historical costumes. Understanding fashion history helps us appreciate today's choices and imagine the future of what we wear. [4]

From ancient looms to smart fabrics, textiles continue to evolve. They protect us, express our identity, and connect us to cultures across the world. Whether we follow trends or dress for comfort, the clothes we choose are part of a long and rich human story. [5]

## 选项

A. In short, textiles have a long and global history, shaped by culture, trade, and human invention.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍布料历史悠久、早期用兽皮植物纤维、后发展纺线织布染色、各地文化形成不同织物（中国丝绸、印度棉、欧洲羊毛）、纺织品反映贸易技术与创意，空后需总结纺织品的历史与全球性。A的"long and global history, shaped by culture, trade, and human invention"与前文对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说工业革命改变制衣——机器加速量产、工厂替代手织、成衣普及、时尚随季节更快变化、奠定现代时尚产业基础，空后需总结19世纪在产销上的转折。B的"19th century marked a turning point in how clothing was produced and consumed"与前文呼应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前举例时尚反映时代——20年代裙摆变短、60年代青年用服饰表达叛逆、当今可持续与二手兴起、穿着始终与身份和价值观相关，空后需总结时尚是社会与个人价值的镜子。C的"more than style—it is a mirror of social change and personal values"与前文对应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说博物馆与历史学家保存旧衣、展示生活方式与材料技术、设计师从中汲取灵感、了解时装史有助于理解当下与想象未来，空后需总结这些遗存的意义。D的"These artefacts"指代保存的服装，"window into our collective past and future"与前文呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说从古老织机到智能布料持续演变、保护我们、表达身份、联结全球文化、无论追随时尚还是舒适、穿着选择都是人类漫长故事的一部分，空后需总结纺织与时尚的核心地位。E的"central to human life, past, present, and future"与前文对应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, denim was first used for work clothes...**：以For example开头，为具体面料历史案例（牛仔布）。各空均为概括或过渡句；[2]易被误选因涉及制衣与产业，但该空需总结19世纪产销方式的整体转折，而非单一面料的发展史。
