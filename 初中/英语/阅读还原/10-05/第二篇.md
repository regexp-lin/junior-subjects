# 10-05 第二篇

**Protecting the Rainforest**

Rainforests cover only about 6 percent of the Earth's surface, yet they are home to more than half of the world's plant and animal species. They produce much of the oxygen we breathe and absorb large amounts of carbon dioxide. Sadly, rainforests are disappearing at an alarming rate due to logging, farming, and development. [1] If this trend continues, we risk losing not only rare species but also a vital buffer against climate change that benefits the whole planet.

When trees are cut down, the animals that depend on them lose their homes. Many species cannot survive in smaller, fragmented forests. Some have already become extinct, and others are critically endangered. [2] When one species disappears, it can trigger a chain reaction that harms many others, including plants that depended on that animal to spread seeds or pollinate flowers.

Rainforests also matter for the climate. Trees store carbon; when they are burned or rot, that carbon is released into the atmosphere as greenhouse gases. Deforestation is a major contributor to climate change. [3] For this reason, international agreements and local efforts to curb deforestation have become key tools in the fight against rising temperatures.

What can be done? Governments can create and enforce laws to protect forests. Consumers can choose products that do not come from illegally logged wood. Companies can commit to sustainable practices. [4] When enough people and organizations take such steps, the combined effect can slow deforestation and give forests a chance to recover.

Protecting the rainforest is a global challenge that requires global cooperation. Rich and poor nations must work together, and businesses, scientists, and ordinary citizens all have a part to play. [5] Time is running out, but with cooperation and commitment, it is still possible to protect the rainforests that remain and to restore what has been lost.

## 选项

A. Protecting the rainforest is not only an environmental issue—it is a matter of survival for countless species and for the global climate that sustains us all.
B. The loss of biodiversity affects the entire ecosystem, as each species plays a role in keeping the forest healthy and balanced.
C. Slowing or stopping rainforest loss is therefore essential if we are to limit global warming and its effects on weather, sea levels, and human societies.
D. Each of these actions, from policy to personal choice, can contribute to a future in which rainforests continue to thrive instead of shrinking.
E. The decisions we make today about forests will determine what kind of world we leave for future generations—a world rich in biodiversity or one that has lost one of its greatest treasures.
F. For example, the Amazon rainforest alone stores an estimated 150 billion tons of carbon, making it one of the world's largest natural carbon sinks.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述雨林重要性及退化速度，空后说"If this trend continues, we risk losing not only rare species but also a vital buffer against climate change"。A的"matter of survival for countless species and for the global climate"与空后物种、气候缓冲完全对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说砍伐导致动物失所、物种灭绝或濒危，空后说"When one species disappears, it can trigger a chain reaction..."。B的"The loss of biodiversity affects the entire ecosystem"与空后"chain reaction"呼应，"each species plays a role"与后文传播种子、授粉对应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说森林破坏助长气候变化，空后说"For this reason, international agreements and local efforts to curb deforestation have become key tools"。C的"Slowing or stopping rainforest loss is therefore essential"与空后遏制砍伐努力衔接。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前列举政府、消费者、企业可采取的行动，空后说"When enough people and organizations take such steps, the combined effect can slow deforestation"。D的"Each of these actions... can contribute to a future"与空后"combined effect"呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前强调全球合作、多方参与，空后说"Time is running out, but with cooperation and commitment, it is still possible to protect..."。E的"The decisions we make today... will determine what kind of world we leave"与空后紧迫感及希望呼应。
