# 05-22 第一篇

**Introduction to Stargazing: Your First Steps into the Night Sky**

Looking up at the night sky can fill us with wonder. The stars, the moon, and sometimes even planets are there for anyone to see—you do not need expensive equipment to start. [1] In fact, all you need is a clear night, a place away from bright city lights, and a little patience. Your eyes will gradually adjust to the darkness, and you will begin to notice more and more stars. That is why stargazing is one of the oldest and most accessible hobbies in the world. People of all ages can enjoy it, and it costs almost nothing to begin.

What can you see with the naked eye? [2] You can recognise the Moon and its phases—full moon, half moon, and new moon. On clear nights, you may spot planets like Venus, Mars, or Jupiter, which often look brighter than most stars. You can also find simple patterns, or constellations（星座）, such as the Big Dipper（北斗七星）and Orion（猎户座）. In other words, even without a telescope, the night sky offers plenty to discover. Learning a few constellations helps you find your way around the sky, just like knowing landmarks helps you find your way on Earth.

A pair of binoculars（双筒望远镜）can make stargazing even more interesting. [3] With binoculars, you can see the craters on the Moon, the four large moons of Jupiter, and many more stars. They are cheaper than a telescope and easier to carry. Many beginners start with binoculars before buying a telescope. For this reason, binoculars are often the best first step for those who want to see more than the naked eye can show.

How can you get started? [4] You can use a star chart or a free app on your phone to identify what you see. Find a dark spot—a park, a rooftop, or a field away from streetlights. Dress warmly if it is cold, and bring a blanket or chair so you can sit comfortably. It is true that a little preparation makes stargazing more enjoyable. Once you know where to look, you will be amazed at how much is visible above you every night.

[5] In short, stargazing is a wonderful way to connect with nature and the universe. You do not need to be an expert—just step outside, look up, and let your curiosity guide you. Whether you watch the Moon, follow a planet, or simply enjoy the peace of a starry night, the sky is there for everyone. Remember: the universe has been there for billions of years, and it will still be there tomorrow. So why not start tonight?

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：总领天文观测的入门门槛。空前描述"Looking up at the night sky can fill us with wonder...you do not need expensive equipment to start"，空后说明"In fact, all you need is a clear night, a place away from bright city lights, and a little patience...stargazing is one of the oldest and most accessible hobbies...People of all ages can enjoy it, and it costs almost nothing to begin"。A选项"That is because stargazing can begin with very simple things"（那是因为天文观测可以从非常简单的事物开始）中"That is because"解释入门之易，"very simple things"与空后clear night、away from lights、patience、costs almost nothing精准对应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：肉眼可见的天体与星座。空前设问"What can you see with the naked eye?"，空后列举"You can recognise the Moon and its phases...you may spot planets like Venus, Mars, or Jupiter...You can also find simple patterns, or constellations, such as the Big Dipper and Orion...even without a telescope, the night sky offers plenty to discover"。B选项"In other words, the naked eye alone reveals a lot in the night sky"（换言之，仅凭肉眼就能在夜空中发现许多东西）中"In other words"概括空后内容，"naked eye alone reveals a lot"与空后Moon、planets、constellations、without a telescope完美呼应，衔接自然。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：双筒望远镜对初学者的价值。空前提出"A pair of binoculars can make stargazing even more interesting"，空后详述"With binoculars, you can see the craters on the Moon, the four large moons of Jupiter, and many more stars...They are cheaper than a telescope and easier to carry...Many beginners start with binoculars before buying a telescope...binoculars are often the best first step"。C选项"That is why binoculars are a great choice for beginners"（因此双筒望远镜是初学者的绝佳选择）中"That is why"承接空前及空后结论，"great choice for beginners"与空后cheaper、easier to carry、best first step精准对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：准备工作提升观测体验。空前设问"How can you get started?"，空后列举"You can use a star chart or a free app...Find a dark spot—a park, a rooftop, or a field away from streetlights...Dress warmly if it is cold, and bring a blanket or chair...Once you know where to look, you will be amazed"。D选项"It is true that a little preparation makes stargazing more enjoyable"（的确，一点准备就能让天文观测更加愉快）中"It is true that"加强衔接，"little preparation"与空后star chart、dark spot、dress warmly、blanket or chair完美呼应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与鼓励读者参与。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"stargazing is a wonderful way to connect with nature and the universe...You do not need to be an expert—just step outside, look up...Whether you watch the Moon, follow a planet, or simply enjoy the peace of a starry night, the sky is there for everyone...why not start tonight?"。E选项"In conclusion, stargazing is a rewarding hobby for everyone"（总之，天文观测是一项人人皆可受益的爱好）用"In conclusion"总结全文，"rewarding hobby for everyone"与空后wonderful way、sky is there for everyone形成呼应，收束有力。
