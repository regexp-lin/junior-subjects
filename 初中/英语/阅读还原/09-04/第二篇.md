# 09-04 第二篇

**Learning a Musical Instrument**

Learning to play a musical instrument is one of the most rewarding things a person can do. Whether it is the piano, guitar, violin, or drums, making music brings joy and a deep sense of accomplishment. Many people start learning an instrument as children, but it is never too late to begin. [1] The journey of learning music teaches much more than just how to play notes—it shapes the mind, the heart, and the character.

Playing an instrument strengthens the brain. Research shows that musicians develop stronger connections between different parts of the brain, especially areas related to memory, attention, and coordination. [2] This is why many educators encourage music education as a way to support overall academic performance in students.

Learning music also teaches discipline and patience. Progress is often slow, and students must practice regularly to improve. [3] The process of working through difficult pieces and gradually mastering them builds perseverance—a quality that is useful in every area of life.

Music has emotional benefits as well. Playing an instrument can help people express feelings they might struggle to put into words. [4] Whether someone is happy, sad, or stressed, music provides an outlet for emotions and can be a powerful source of comfort and healing.

Finally, music connects people. Playing in a band, orchestra, or even just jamming with friends creates a special bond. [5] Sharing music with others—whether performing on stage or singing together at home—is one of life's greatest pleasures and a reminder that art has the power to bring people together.

## 选项

A. Music is a lifelong companion that enriches every stage of our lives in ways that few other activities can.
B. These brain benefits can last a lifetime, making early music education an investment in long-term mental health.
C. Learning to accept mistakes and keep trying is one of the most important lessons music teaches.
D. Music becomes a personal language through which people communicate their deepest thoughts and emotions.
E. The shared experience of creating music together builds teamwork, trust, and lasting friendships.
F. For example, studies have found that children who play piano score higher on math tests.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前说学音乐任何时候开始都不晚，空后说音乐之旅教会的不仅是弹奏音符。A的"lifelong companion that enriches every stage of our lives"概括音乐的持久价值，"few other activities can"突出其独特性。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说音乐家大脑中记忆、注意力、协调等区域连接更强，空后说教育者鼓励音乐教育支持学业。B的"brain benefits can last a lifetime"概括前文研究发现，"investment in long-term mental health"引出教育意义。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说进步慢、需要坚持练习，空后说攻克难曲培养毅力。C的"Learning to accept mistakes and keep trying"承接练习中的困难，"most important lessons music teaches"概括该段主旨。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说乐器能帮助表达难以言说的情感，空后说无论开心、难过或压力大，音乐都提供情感出口。D的"personal language through which people communicate their deepest thoughts"概括音乐的情感表达功能。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说一起演奏创造特殊纽带，空后说分享音乐是人生最大乐趣之一。E的"shared experience of creating music together"承接合奏，"builds teamwork, trust, and lasting friendships"概括社交效益。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, studies have found that children who play piano score higher on math tests.**：以For example开头，属具体研究举例；易误放[2]，但[2]需的是大脑益处的概括总结，非单一研究数据。
