**Fire Safety: Knowledge That Can Save Lives**

Fires can happen anywhere—at home, at school, or in public places. They spread quickly and can cause serious harm. [1] That is why learning basic fire safety is important for everyone, young and old. Knowing how to prevent fires, what to do when they happen, and how to escape safely can make the difference between life and death. Fire drills（消防演习）at school are not just exercises; they prepare you for real emergencies. This article will help you understand the key points of fire safety.

Prevention is the first step. [2] Most home fires start in the kitchen—never leave cooking unattended, and keep things that burn away from the stove. Do not overload（过载）electrical sockets; too many plugs in one place can cause overheating and fire. Candles should be used with care and never left burning when you leave a room. Smoking indoors is dangerous; if someone in your home smokes, make sure cigarettes are fully put out. Check your smoke alarms regularly and change the batteries when needed. Small habits like these can prevent big disasters.

If a fire starts, act quickly and calmly. [3] First, shout "Fire!" to alert others. If there is smoke, stay low—smoke rises, so clean air is closer to the floor. Feel closed doors with the back of your hand before opening; if the door is hot, do not open it—find another way out. Use a wet towel over your nose and mouth to reduce smoke inhalation（吸入）. Never use the lift in a fire; always use the stairs. Have a meeting point outside so your family knows where to gather. Every second counts when escaping.

Sometimes you cannot escape. [4] If you are trapped in a room, close the door and block gaps under it with wet cloth to keep smoke out. Call the fire brigade（消防队）or your emergency number and tell them your exact location. Wave something bright at the window to attract rescuers. Stay near the window for fresh air if possible. Do not jump from a high floor unless firefighters are there to catch you. Staying put and signalling for help is often safer than trying to escape alone.

[5] Fire safety is not something to learn once and forget. Review these rules with your family and practise your escape plan. Check smoke alarms and fire extinguishers（灭火器）at home. When you stay in a hotel or visit a new building, notice where the fire exits are. The more you prepare, the safer you and your loved ones will be. Remember: prevention and preparation can save lives.
