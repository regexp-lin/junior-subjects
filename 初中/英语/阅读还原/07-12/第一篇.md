# 07-12 第一篇

**Privacy Protection in the Digital Age**

We live in an era of smart phones, social media, and smart home devices. We share photos, post updates, shop online, and use apps that know our preferences. Every click and search leaves a digital trace. [1] Protecting our privacy in this connected world requires awareness and action. Our personal data—names, locations, browsing habits, and more—is valuable to companies and sometimes to people with bad intentions. Understanding the risks and learning how to protect ourselves is essential for everyone.

Strong passwords are your first line of defense. [2] Use different passwords for different accounts, and make them long and complex. A password manager can help you remember them securely. Enable two-factor authentication when available—it adds an extra step that makes it much harder for others to access your accounts. These simple habits can prevent most unauthorized access to your personal information.

Be careful about what you share online. [3] Information you post on social media can be seen by more people than you expect. Photos and posts can be copied, shared, or used without your permission. Before posting, ask yourself: Would I be comfortable if a teacher, employer, or stranger saw this? Adjust your privacy settings to control who can view your content. Once something is online, it can be very difficult to remove completely.

Understand how apps and websites collect your data. [4] Many free apps earn money by selling or using your data for advertising. Read privacy policies—or at least skim the main points—before agreeing to terms. Turn off permissions that apps do not really need, such as access to your location or contacts when it is not necessary. Being selective about what you allow reduces how much of your life is tracked and stored.

[5] Privacy in the digital age is a skill we must all learn. It takes ongoing attention: strong passwords, mindful sharing, and informed choices about apps and websites. No single step makes you completely safe, but together these practices greatly reduce your exposure. Your data belongs to you—take steps to keep it that way.

## 选项

A. Staying safe online depends on knowing the risks and taking practical steps.
B. Secure passwords and extra verification steps help protect your accounts.
C. What you share on social media can spread further than you intend.

E. In the end, protecting privacy is a habit we need to develop over time.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述数字时代的生活、每一点击都留下痕迹，空后强调个人数据有价值、需认识风险并学习保护方法。A选项"Staying safe online depends on knowing the risks and taking practical steps"承接前文，概括后文"awareness and action"的内涵，承上启下。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：第二段主题是强密码，空后具体说明不同账户用不同密码、密码要长且复杂、使用密码管理器、开启双重认证。B选项"Secure passwords and extra verification steps help protect your accounts"精准概括：安全密码和额外验证步骤有助于保护账号。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：第三段讲谨慎分享，空后说明社交媒体内容可见范围超预期、可被复制滥用、发帖前自问、调整隐私设置、网络内容难以完全删除。C选项"What you share on social media can spread further than you intend"概括本段：社交媒体的分享可能比你预期的传播更广。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：第四段讨论了解数据收集，空后提到免费应用靠数据盈利、阅读隐私政策、关闭非必要权限、有选择地授权。D选项"Knowing how your data is collected and used helps you make better choices"准确概括：了解数据如何被收集和使用有助于做出更好选择。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：最后一段需总结升华，空前已分述密码、分享、数据收集，空后归纳为"ongoing attention""strong passwords, mindful sharing, informed choices""你的数据属于你"。E选项"In the end, protecting privacy is a habit we need to develop over time"用"In the end"收束，强调保护隐私是需要长期养成的习惯。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, many people use the same password for multiple websites.**：以"For example"开头，是举例型干扰。虽与密码相关，但只是"很多人多站同密码"的现象描述，若放入[2]处会与本段主旨矛盾——本段恰恰建议"不同账户用不同密码"。F作为反面例子可作为段内支撑，但不能作为段首主题概括句，且会削弱段落正面建议的力度。
