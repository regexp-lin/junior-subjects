# 07-20 第二篇

**Why First Aid Knowledge Matters**

In an emergency, the first few minutes can make the difference between life and death. When someone is injured, ill, or in danger, knowing what to do before professional help arrives can save lives. Yet many people lack basic first aid skills and feel helpless when faced with an emergency. [1] Learning these skills is easier than most people think, and the benefits extend far beyond crisis situations. Basic first aid includes knowing how to stop bleeding, perform CPR, and help someone who is choking. Many communities offer free or low-cost courses, and even a few hours of training can build essential skills.

[2] First aid training builds confidence so that people are more likely to act when an emergency occurs. Without training, people often freeze or panic, unsure of what to do. With knowledge and practice, they can stay calm and take the right steps. This confidence can mean the difference between waiting helplessly for help and providing immediate care that keeps a person alive until professionals arrive. The more people who are trained, the safer our communities become.

[3] First aid is not only for serious emergencies—it also helps with everyday injuries. Cuts, burns, sprains, and insect bites are common, and knowing how to treat them properly can prevent infection, reduce pain, and speed up healing. Parents, teachers, and caregivers especially benefit from these skills when caring for children. Simple actions like cleaning a wound correctly or applying the right kind of bandage can make a real difference.

[4] Young people can and should learn first aid too. Teens and even children can learn age-appropriate skills, such as calling for help, applying pressure to stop bleeding, or helping someone who is choking. Schools and youth organizations often offer first aid programs designed for young learners. When young people are trained, they can help at home, at school, or in their communities—and they carry these valuable skills for the rest of their lives.

[5] First aid knowledge is one of the most practical skills a person can have. It requires little time to learn but can have a lasting impact on the lives of others. Whether at home, at work, or in public, being prepared to help in an emergency makes us better neighbors and more responsible members of society. Everyone can benefit from learning first aid, and everyone can make a difference when it matters most.

## 选项

A. These skills are accessible to nearly everyone and cover the most common emergency situations.
B. Training gives people the confidence to respond effectively instead of standing by in panic.
C. Everyday situations often require basic first aid, and knowing what to do can improve outcomes.
D. Young learners can gain valuable skills that they will use throughout their lives.
E. Investing time in first aid training is one of the best choices we can make for ourselves and others.
F. For example, heart attacks often occur at home when a family member is present.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前指出许多人缺乏急救技能、遇险时感到无助，空后介绍止血、CPR、窒息处理等基础技能及培训可获得性。A 句 "These skills are accessible to nearly everyone and cover the most common emergency situations" 承接 "basic first aid" 与 "free or low-cost courses"，完成从问题到解决方案的过渡，承上启下。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：段落主题是急救培训如何提升在紧急情况下行动的自信。空后对比未培训者的茫然与培训后的冷静应对。B 句 "Training gives people the confidence to respond effectively instead of standing by in panic" 准确概括本段。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：段落强调急救不仅用于严重突发事件，也适用于日常小伤。空后列举割伤、烫伤、扭伤等及正确处理的益处。C 句 "Everyday situations often require basic first aid, and knowing what to do can improve outcomes" 精准对应本段主旨。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：段落讲青少年学习急救的可能与价值。空后提到适龄技能、学校与青年组织的培训项目。D 句 "Young learners can gain valuable skills that they will use throughout their lives" 与后文 "carry these valuable skills for the rest of their lives" 呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：结尾段总结急救知识的实用性与长远意义。E 句 "Investing time in first aid training is one of the best choices we can make for ourselves and others" 与前文 "practical skills"、"lasting impact" 自然衔接，作为总结句恰当。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example...**：虽与急救场景（心脏病发作、家人在场）相关，但仅为举例，应嵌入某段内部说明急救的重要性。作为独立选项，其概括层级不足，未与任一空位形成完整段落衔接，易因 "at home" 与第 3 段 "everyday" 或第 4 段家庭场景混淆而误选。
