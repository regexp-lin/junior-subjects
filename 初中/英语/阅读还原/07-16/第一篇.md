# 07-16 第一篇

**The Ecological Value of Forests**

Forests cover about one-third of the world's land area and are home to more than half of all land-based species. They are often called the "lungs of the Earth" because trees absorb carbon dioxide and release oxygen through photosynthesis. Forests also help regulate the water cycle, prevent soil erosion, and provide shelter for countless animals. When we lose forests, we lose these vital services. [1]

[2] Trees absorb large amounts of carbon dioxide from the atmosphere. This helps slow down climate change by reducing the concentration of greenhouse gases. Deforestation, however, does the opposite—it releases stored carbon back into the air. Protecting and replanting forests is one of the most effective ways to fight global warming.

[3] Forests act like giant sponges, soaking up rainfall and releasing it slowly into rivers and groundwater. When trees are cut down, water runs off quickly, causing floods in the wet season and droughts in the dry season. Forest roots hold soil in place, preventing it from washing away. In this way, forests protect both our water supply and our farmland.

[4] Millions of people depend on forests for their livelihoods. They gather food, collect medicinal plants, and harvest timber in sustainable ways. Forests also support tourism and recreation, bringing income to local communities. When forests are managed wisely, they can provide these benefits for generations.

[5] Forests are not just a resource to use—they are essential to life on Earth. Every tree we protect and every forest we restore contributes to a healthier planet. The time to act is now.

## 选项

A. This is why the protection of forests should be a global priority.
B. Forests play a crucial role in the fight against climate change.
C. When it comes to water and soil, forests are equally vital.
D. The economic value of forests to human society should not be overlooked.
E. The message is clear: we must value and protect our forests before it is too late.
F. For example, the Amazon rainforest is home to thousands of species found nowhere else.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：第一段介绍森林覆盖率、物种家园、"地球之肺"、调节水循环、防止水土流失、动物栖息地，并指出失去森林即失去这些服务。空格前强调森林功能的重要性。选项A "This is why the protection of forests should be a global priority" 承接因果关系，自然引出保护主题，承上启下。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：第二段讲树木吸收二氧化碳、减缓气候变化、砍伐释放碳、保护与 replanting 可应对全球变暖。选项B "Forests play a crucial role in the fight against climate change" 概括本段主题，与后文碳吸收、温室气体逻辑一致。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：第三段讲森林像海绵调节径流、保持水土、防洪抗旱。选项C "When it comes to water and soil, forests are equally vital" 概括水与土壤两方面作用，"equally vital"与第二段气候功能形成并列。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：第四段讲人类依赖森林：食物、药材、木材、旅游、社区收入、可持续管理。选项D "The economic value of forests to human society should not be overlooked" 点明本段从经济社会角度论述，与后文生计、收入呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：末段总结森林是生命必需品，每棵树每片林都利于地球健康，号召即刻行动。选项E "The message is clear: we must value and protect our forests before it is too late" 呼应"time to act"，收束有力。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the Amazon rainforest is home to thousands of species found nowhere else.**：与森林生物多样性相关，可支撑第一段。但第一段空格需承上启下引出后文分述，插具体地区案例会打断从"功能概述"到"分维度详述"的逻辑；且后文按气候、水土壤、经济分述，未再提物种。属主题相关但论述层次错位型干扰。
