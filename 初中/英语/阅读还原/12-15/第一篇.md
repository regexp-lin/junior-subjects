**Food Waste: A Problem We Can Solve**

Every year, about one-third of all food produced in the world is thrown away. That is enough to feed billions of people. Food waste happens at every step—on farms, in stores, in restaurants, and at home. When we waste food, we also waste the water, land, and energy used to produce it. Reducing food waste is one of the most effective ways to help the planet and fight hunger. [1]

At home, small changes can make a big difference. Plan your meals before you shop so you buy only what you need. Store food properly to keep it fresh longer. Use leftover vegetables in soups or stir-fries. Understand the difference between "use by" and "best before" dates—many foods are still safe to eat after the date. Learning to waste less takes practice, but it becomes a habit. [2]

Restaurants and supermarkets are also taking action. Some donate unsold food to charities. Others use apps to sell leftover food at lower prices. In some countries, laws encourage or require businesses to reduce waste. Technology can help track inventory and predict demand so less food goes bad. [3]

Schools and communities can raise awareness. Students learn about food waste in class and practice saving food in the canteen. Community fridges allow people to share food they cannot use. Campaigns remind people that every meal saved matters. When everyone works together, the impact grows. [4]

Food waste is a global problem, but each of us can be part of the solution. With awareness, planning, and small daily actions, we can waste less and do more good. [5]

## 选项

A. Food waste is a serious issue that harms both the environment and people in need, but we have the power to change it.
B. Simple habits at home can significantly cut the amount of food we throw away.
C. Businesses are finding new ways to reduce waste and put surplus food to better use.
D. Education and community efforts help spread the message and multiply the effect.
E. Together, individuals and communities can turn the tide on food waste.
F. For example, in France, a law requires supermarkets to donate unsold food to charities instead of throwing it away.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍全球约三分之一食物被浪费、可养活数十亿人、各环节均有浪费、浪费食物即浪费水土能源、减少浪费是助地球抗饥饿的有效途径，空后需总结问题的严重性与可改变性。A的"a serious issue that harms both the environment and people in need, but we have the power to change it"与前文对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说家庭小改变有大影响、计划购物、正确储存、利用剩菜、理解保质期、养成习惯，空后需总结家庭习惯的作用。B的"Simple habits at home can significantly cut the amount of food we throw away"与前文呼应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说餐厅超市也在行动、捐赠、用应用折价售卖、法律推动、技术追踪库存预测需求，空后需总结企业的应对方式。C的"Businesses are finding new ways to reduce waste and put surplus food to better use"与前文对应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说学校社区可提高意识、学生课堂学习与食堂实践、社区冰箱共享、宣传活动、众人合力效果放大，空后需总结教育与社区的作用。D的"Education and community efforts help spread the message and multiply the effect"与前文呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说食物浪费是全球问题、但每人可成为解决者、意识规划与日常小行动可减少浪费，空后需总结集体行动的力量。E的"Together, individuals and communities can turn the tide on food waste"与前文对应。
