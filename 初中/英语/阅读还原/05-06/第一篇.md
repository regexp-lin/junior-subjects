# 05-06 第一篇

**Volcanoes: Earth's Fiery Giants**

Deep beneath the surface of the Earth, temperatures are incredibly high. Rocks melt into a hot liquid called magma（岩浆）, and sometimes this magma finds its way to the surface through openings in the ground. [1] When magma reaches the surface, it is called lava（熔岩）. A volcano is simply a mountain or hill formed by layers of lava and ash（火山灰） that have built up over thousands or even millions of years.

There are about 1,500 active volcanoes around the world. [2] Most of them are found along the edges of tectonic plates（板块）, especially around the Pacific Ocean in an area known as the Ring of Fire（环太平洋火山带）. When two plates push against each other or pull apart, magma can rise to the surface. This is why volcanic eruptions（喷发） are common in countries like Japan, Indonesia, and Chile.

Volcanic eruptions can be extremely dangerous. [3] Hot lava can destroy everything in its path—forests, buildings, and entire towns. Volcanic ash can travel hundreds of kilometers through the air, making it hard to breathe and blocking sunlight. In 79 AD, the eruption of Mount Vesuvius（维苏威火山） buried the Roman city of Pompeii（庞贝古城） under meters of ash. Thousands of people died, and the city was lost for nearly 1,700 years.

However, volcanoes are not entirely harmful. [4] Volcanic soil is extremely rich in minerals（矿物质）, making it some of the best farmland in the world. Many famous wine regions（产酒区） and coffee-growing areas are located on volcanic slopes. Volcanoes also create new land—the Hawaiian Islands, for example, were formed entirely by underwater volcanic eruptions over millions of years.

[5] Volcanoes remind us of the incredible power hidden beneath our feet. They can destroy, but they can also create. Understanding them better helps us prepare for future eruptions and appreciate the forces that have shaped our planet.

## 选项

A. These eruptions are what we call volcanic eruptions.
B. They are not evenly spread across the globe, however.
C. The destruction caused by a major eruption can be catastrophic.
D. In fact, volcanoes have also brought many benefits to the land and the people living nearby.
E. In short, volcanoes are among the most powerful and complex natural forces on Earth.
F. For example, geothermal energy（地热能） from volcanoes is used to generate electricity in Iceland.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：引出火山喷发的概念。空前描述"Rocks melt into a hot liquid called magma, and sometimes this magma finds its way to the surface through openings in the ground"，空后说明"When magma reaches the surface, it is called lava...A volcano is simply a mountain or hill formed by layers of lava and ash"。A选项"These eruptions are what we call volcanic eruptions"（这些喷发就是我们所说的火山喷发）中"These eruptions"承接空前岩浆涌出地面的描述，"volcanic eruptions"自然引出空后对火山和熔岩的定义，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：火山的地理分布。空前提出"There are about 1,500 active volcanoes around the world"，空后说明"Most of them are found along the edges of tectonic plates...Ring of Fire...When two plates push against each other or pull apart"。B选项"They are not evenly spread across the globe, however"（然而，它们并非均匀分布在全球）中"however"转折，"not evenly spread"引出空后火山集中在板块边缘的地理分布规律，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：火山喷发的破坏力。空前提出"Volcanic eruptions can be extremely dangerous"，空后列举"Hot lava can destroy everything in its path...Volcanic ash can travel hundreds of kilometers...eruption of Mount Vesuvius buried...Pompeii"。C选项"The destruction caused by a major eruption can be catastrophic"（一次大型喷发造成的破坏可能是灾难性的）中"destruction...catastrophic"呼应空前"extremely dangerous"，自然引出空后熔岩、火山灰、庞贝等具体灾难案例，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：火山的益处。空前提出"However, volcanoes are not entirely harmful"，空后列举"Volcanic soil is extremely rich in minerals...best farmland...wine regions and coffee-growing areas...Hawaiian Islands were formed by volcanic eruptions"。D选项"In fact, volcanoes have also brought many benefits to the land and the people living nearby"（事实上，火山也给土地和附近居民带来了许多好处）中"In fact...many benefits"加强空前"not entirely harmful"，自然引出空后火山土壤肥沃、创造新陆地等具体好处，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"Volcanoes remind us of the incredible power...They can destroy, but they can also create...Understanding them...appreciate the forces that have shaped our planet"。E选项"In short, volcanoes are among the most powerful and complex natural forces on Earth"（简言之，火山是地球上最强大和复杂的自然力之一）用"In short"总结全文，"powerful and complex"与空后"destroy...create"的双重性呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, geothermal energy from volcanoes is used to generate electricity in Iceland.**：涉及冰岛地热能利用，易被误填[4]。但F以"For example"引出地热能发电的单一案例，而空[4]后已有"Volcanic soil...best farmland...wine regions...Hawaiian Islands"等多个并列好处的描述，F的"geothermal energy...electricity"属于工业利用范畴，与空[4]后农业和地理形成的举例主线不匹配。F属"好处类但举例层次与段落已有列举方向不一致"型干扰。
