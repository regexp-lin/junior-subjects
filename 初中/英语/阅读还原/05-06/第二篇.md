# 05-06 第二篇

**The History of Tea: From China to the World**

Tea is the second most consumed drink in the world, after water. Every day, billions of people start their morning with a warm cup of tea. [1] But few people know that this beloved drink has a history stretching back thousands of years, and that it all started in China.

According to legend, tea was discovered by accident around 2737 BC. [2] The Chinese Emperor Shen Nung was boiling water outdoors when some leaves from a nearby tea plant fell into his pot. He tasted the water and found it delicious and refreshing. Whether or not this story is true, we know that the Chinese were drinking tea at least 2,000 years ago. Tea quickly became an important part of Chinese culture and daily life.

For centuries, tea remained mainly a Chinese drink. [3] Then, during the Tang Dynasty（唐朝）, tea began to spread to Japan and Korea through trade and cultural exchange. Japanese monks（僧侣） who visited China brought tea seeds and brewing methods back to their homeland. In Japan, tea drinking developed into a beautiful ceremony（茶道） that is still practiced today. Gradually, tea reached other parts of Asia and the Middle East.

Tea arrived in Europe in the 1600s, and it quickly became fashionable among the wealthy. [4] The British, in particular, fell in love with tea. Afternoon tea, with its sandwiches and cakes, became a tradition that remains popular in Britain today. The demand for tea was so great that it drove trade between Europe and Asia for centuries, shaping history in ways that few other products have.

[5] Today, tea is grown in more than 50 countries and comes in hundreds of varieties—green, black, white, oolong（乌龙）, and more. From a simple leaf discovered by chance in ancient China, tea has become a drink that unites people across cultures and continents.

## 选项

A. It was during this period that the first book about tea, "The Classic of Tea"（《茶经》）, was written.
B. In fact, it was originally used as a medicine before becoming an everyday drink.
C. The story of how it was first discovered is a fascinating one.
D. At first, it was too expensive for ordinary people and was enjoyed mainly by royalty and the upper class.
E. In summary, the journey of tea from a local drink to a global favorite is a remarkable story.
F. For instance, India is now one of the world's largest tea producers, growing varieties like Darjeeling and Assam.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] B   [2] C   [3] A   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] B**：
  - **线索**：引出茶的悠久历史。空前描述"Every day, billions of people start their morning with a warm cup of tea"，空后说明"But few people know that this beloved drink has a history stretching back thousands of years...it all started in China"。B选项"In fact, it was originally used as a medicine before becoming an everyday drink"（事实上，茶最初是作为药物使用，后来才成为日常饮品）中"In fact"加深空前日常饮茶的认知，"originally used as a medicine...before becoming everyday drink"自然过渡到空后"history stretching back thousands of years...started in China"的历史叙述，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] C**：
  - **线索**：引出茶的发现传说。空前提出"According to legend, tea was discovered by accident around 2737 BC"，空后叙述"The Chinese Emperor Shen Nung was boiling water...leaves from a nearby tea plant fell into his pot...tasted the water and found it delicious"。C选项"The story of how it was first discovered is a fascinating one"（它最初被发现的故事令人着迷）中"story of how it was first discovered"承接空前"discovered by accident"，"fascinating"自然引出空后皇帝偶然品茶的生动叙述，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] A**：
  - **线索**：茶文化的早期发展。空前提出"For centuries, tea remained mainly a Chinese drink"，空后说明"Then, during the Tang Dynasty, tea began to spread to Japan and Korea through trade and cultural exchange"。A选项"It was during this period that the first book about tea, 'The Classic of Tea', was written"（正是在这一时期，第一本关于茶的书《茶经》被写成）中"during this period"承接空前"For centuries"的中国独有时期，"The Classic of Tea"展示茶文化的成熟，自然过渡到空后唐朝茶向日韩传播的叙述，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：茶在欧洲的传播。空前描述"Tea arrived in Europe in the 1600s, and it quickly became fashionable among the wealthy"，空后说明"The British, in particular, fell in love with tea...Afternoon tea...became a tradition...The demand for tea was so great that it drove trade"。D选项"At first, it was too expensive for ordinary people and was enjoyed mainly by royalty and the upper class"（起初，茶对于普通人来说太贵了，主要为皇室和上层阶级所享用）中"At first...too expensive...mainly by royalty and upper class"承接空前"fashionable among the wealthy"，解释了空后英国人对茶的狂热及茶贸易的历史影响，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"tea is grown in more than 50 countries...hundreds of varieties...From a simple leaf...a drink that unites people across cultures and continents"。E选项"In summary, the journey of tea from a local drink to a global favorite is a remarkable story"（总之，茶从一种地方饮品到全球宠儿的旅程是一个了不起的故事）用"In summary"总结全文，"from a local drink to a global favorite"与空后"50 countries...unites people across cultures and continents"精准呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For instance, India is now one of the world's largest tea producers, growing varieties like Darjeeling and Assam.**：涉及印度茶叶生产，易被误填[5]。但F以"For instance"引出印度的茶叶品种，而空[5]后已有"more than 50 countries...green, black, white, oolong"的全球视角描述，F仅聚焦印度一个国家，范围过窄；且空[5]需要总结性过渡句，而非具体国家的举例。F属"全球话题中的单一国家举例，与总结性功能不匹配"型干扰。
