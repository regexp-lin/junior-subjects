# 07-08 第二篇

**Waste Sorting and Recycling: A Simple Guide**

As cities grow larger, the amount of garbage we produce grows with them. Landfills are filling up fast, and burning waste pollutes the air. [1] The good news is that proper waste sorting can greatly reduce these problems. When we separate our trash correctly, we make it easier to recycle useful materials and reduce the amount of waste that ends up in landfills.

Understanding what goes where is the first step. [2] Paper, plastic bottles, glass, and metal should go into the recyclable bin. Food waste and garden waste belong in the compost bin. Things like batteries and old electronics need special handling because they contain harmful chemicals. Learning these basic rules helps everyone participate in recycling effectively.

Recycling saves natural resources and energy. [3] Making new paper from recycled paper uses much less water than making it from wood. Producing aluminum from recycled cans saves 95% of the energy needed to make new aluminum. These savings add up when millions of people recycle every day. Every bottle or can we recycle is a step toward a greener planet.

Changing habits takes time, but it gets easier with practice. [4] Start by placing different bins in your kitchen for different types of waste. Label them clearly so family members know where to put each item. Once sorting becomes part of your daily routine, you will hardly notice the extra effort. Many communities offer tips and workshops to help residents get started.

[5] Waste sorting is not just a personal choice—it is a collective responsibility. When each household does its part, the whole community benefits. cleaner streets, less pollution, and more materials given a second life. Together, we can build a more sustainable future.

## 选项

A. Fortunately, simple changes in how we handle trash can make a big difference.
B. Each type of waste has its own proper destination.
C. The environmental benefits of recycling are clear when we look at the numbers.
D. Practical steps at home can help you form good sorting habits.
E. In the end, effective recycling depends on everyone's participation.
F. For example, some cities have introduced colorful bins to make sorting easier.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述城市垃圾增多、填埋场饱和、焚烧污染等问题，空后出现"The good news is that proper waste sorting can greatly reduce these problems"。A选项"Fortunately, simple changes in how we handle trash can make a big difference"以"Fortunately"承接前文困境，自然引出后文的"good news"和垃圾分类的解决方案，承上启下。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：第二段主题是"Understanding what goes where"，空后具体说明纸张、塑料、玻璃、金属进可回收桶，厨余进堆肥桶，电池和电子产品需特殊处理。B选项"Each type of waste has its own proper destination"精准概括：每种垃圾都有对应的正确去向。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：第三段主题为回收节省资源和能源，空后给出具体数据：造再生纸省水、再生铝节省95%能源。C选项"The environmental benefits of recycling are clear when we look at the numbers"概括本段，与后文的数字论证紧密呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：第四段讲改变习惯、在家实践，空后具体步骤：厨房分设不同垃圾桶、贴标签、养成日常习惯。D选项"Practical steps at home can help you form good sorting habits"概括本段：居家的具体做法有助于养成分类习惯。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：最后一段需总结升华，空前已讲个人习惯，空后强调"each household""the whole community""Together"。E选项"In the end, effective recycling depends on everyone's participation"点明主题：有效回收依赖每个人的参与，与后文的集体责任呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, some cities have introduced colorful bins to make sorting easier.**：以"For example"开头，是举例型干扰。内容虽与垃圾分类相关（彩色垃圾桶），但只是具体案例，不能充当段落的主题概括句。若放在[4]处，会与本段已有的厨房分桶、贴标签等具体步骤混为一谈，且论述层次错位——本段重点是"在家实践形成习惯"，非"城市措施"。
