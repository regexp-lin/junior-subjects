# 07-08 第一篇

**Space Exploration: Why It Matters**

Humans have always looked at the stars with wonder. Since the first satellite was launched in 1957, space exploration has become one of humanity's greatest achievements. [1] Many people wonder if spending money on space programs is worthwhile when there are so many problems on Earth. However, history shows that space research has brought countless benefits to our daily lives.

Space technology has improved our lives in unexpected ways. [2] For instance, GPS systems that help us navigate came from space research. Memory foam used in mattresses was first developed for NASA astronauts. Water purification systems used in space stations are now helping provide clean water in remote areas. These inventions show how space exploration leads to practical solutions for everyday problems.

Beyond technology, space exploration inspires young people to study science. [3] When students watch astronauts land on the Moon or rovers explore Mars, they dream of becoming scientists or engineers. Schools report increased interest in STEM subjects after major space events. This inspiration creates the next generation of innovators who will solve future challenges.

Space exploration also helps us understand our own planet better. [4] Satellites monitor climate change, track hurricanes, and help farmers predict crop yields. By studying other planets, scientists learn more about Earth's atmosphere and environment. This knowledge is essential for protecting our home planet for future generations.

[5] The journey to the stars is not just about curiosity. It is about improving life on Earth, inspiring young minds, and preparing for humanity's future. As we continue to explore, we discover more reasons why space matters.

## 选项

A. Yet the value of space exploration goes far beyond scientific discovery.
B. Technology developed for space often finds uses in everyday life.
C. Educational programs linked to space missions encourage students to pursue STEM careers.
D. Earth observation from space provides crucial data for environmental protection.
E. In short, space exploration serves multiple important purposes for humanity.
F. For example, the International Space Station has been orbiting Earth since 1998.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍太空探索的历史成就，空后出现"Many people wonder if..."的转折——质疑太空探索是否值得。A选项"Yet the value of space exploration goes far beyond scientific discovery"恰好承接前文成就、回应后文质疑，用"Yet"实现转折，引出下文的"benefits"论述。置空处需承上启下。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：第二段主题句是"Space technology has improved our lives in unexpected ways"，空后列举GPS、记忆海绵、净水系统等具体例子。B选项"Technology developed for space often finds uses in everyday life"精准概括本段主旨：太空技术的地面应用，与后文事例一一对应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：第三段主题为太空探索激励年轻人学科学，空后描述学生观看登月、火星探测后的兴趣提升。C选项"Educational programs linked to space missions encourage students to pursue STEM careers"与段落主旨完全契合，概括了本段"inspiration→STEM interest"的逻辑。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：第四段论述从太空了解地球，空后提到卫星监测气候、追踪飓风、预测作物产量等。D选项"Earth observation from space provides crucial data for environmental protection"准确概括本段：太空观测为环境保护提供关键数据。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：最后一段需总结全文，空前已展开论述，空后归纳"improving life""inspiring young minds""preparing for future"。E选项"In short, space exploration serves multiple important purposes for humanity"是典型的总结句，用"In short"引出全文收束，与后文三点呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the International Space Station has been orbiting Earth since 1998.**：以"For example"开头，属于举例型干扰。虽与太空相关，但只是单一事实陈述，无法充当段落的主题概括句。若放入[2]处，会与本段已有的具体例子重复且层次混乱；若放其他空位，无法与前后文形成逻辑衔接。
