# 10-13 第一篇

**The Power of Gratitude**

Gratitude is the feeling of being thankful for what we have—our family, friends, health, and the small joys of daily life. It may seem simple, but research shows that practicing gratitude can improve our mood, strengthen our relationships, and even boost our physical health. [1] People who regularly take time to notice and appreciate the good in their lives tend to feel happier and less stressed than those who focus mainly on what they lack or what has gone wrong.

Gratitude does not mean ignoring problems. It means choosing to also notice what is going well. When we pause to think about things we are thankful for—a kind word from a friend, a sunny day, or a meal shared with family—we shift our attention away from worries and complaints. [2] This shift in focus can reduce anxiety, help us sleep better, and make it easier to cope with difficulties when they do arise.

Gratitude can strengthen relationships. When we thank others and show that we value their help, they feel seen and appreciated. Saying "thank you" or writing a short note of thanks can deepen friendships and family bonds. [3] Expressing gratitude reminds us that we are not alone—that we are supported by others—and it encourages us to give back and to support others when we can.

Schools and families can help young people build a gratitude habit. Some teachers ask students to write down three good things that happened each day. Some families share something they are grateful for at dinner. [4] These simple practices, when done regularly, can help children and teens develop a more positive outlook and a stronger sense of connection to the people and things that matter in their lives.

Gratitude is a skill that can be learned and practiced. It does not require special talent or wealth—anyone can start by noticing one small thing to be thankful for each day. [5] Over time, this habit can change how we see the world, making us more resilient, more connected to others, and more likely to find joy even in challenging times.

## 选项

A. Studies have found that people who practice gratitude regularly report higher levels of happiness, lower levels of depression, and better ability to handle stress compared to those who do not.
B. Focusing on what we have rather than what we lack can calm the mind, improve our emotional state, and build a mental reserve that helps us face hard times with more strength.
C. Gratitude helps us recognize and strengthen the bonds we have with others, and it often leads to a cycle of kindness—when we feel grateful, we are more likely to help others in return.
D. Building gratitude into daily routines—whether at home or at school—can shape the way young people view their lives and the world around them, with benefits that last well beyond childhood.
E. Like any habit, gratitude grows stronger with practice, and the more we notice and appreciate the good in our lives, the more we tend to find—creating a positive loop that can last a lifetime.
F. For example, keeping a gratitude journal and writing down three things you are thankful for each night before bed has been shown to improve sleep and mood in many people.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前说感恩可改善情绪、关系和健康，空后说"People who regularly take time to notice and appreciate the good in their lives tend to feel happier and less stressed"。A的"Studies have found that people who practice gratitude regularly report higher levels of happiness, lower levels of depression"与空后研究型表述及具体效果直接呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说感恩不是忽视问题而是关注好事，空后说"This shift in focus can reduce anxiety, help us sleep better, and make it easier to cope with difficulties"。B的"Focusing on what we have rather than what we lack can calm the mind, improve our emotional state... build a mental reserve"与空后注意力转移、抗压能力对应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说感恩可加强关系、感谢他人，空后说"Expressing gratitude reminds us that we are not alone—that we are supported by others—and it encourages us to give back"。C的"Gratitude helps us recognize and strengthen the bonds... often leads to a cycle of kindness... more likely to help others in return"与空后不孤单、回报他人衔接。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说学校与家庭可培养感恩习惯、举例日记与餐桌分享，空后说"These simple practices, when done regularly, can help children and teens develop a more positive outlook and a stronger sense of connection"。D的"Building gratitude into daily routines... can shape the way young people view their lives... benefits that last well beyond childhood"与空后regularly、young people、outlook呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说感恩可练习、从小事开始，空后说"Over time, this habit can change how we see the world, making us more resilient, more connected, and more likely to find joy"。E的"Like any habit, gratitude grows stronger with practice... creating a positive loop that can last a lifetime"与空后habit、change、over time对应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, keeping a gratitude journal and writing down three things you are thankful for each night...**：以For example开头，属具体做法举例，各空均为概括或过渡句；易误放[1]或[4]，但两空分别需研究结论概括与培养习惯的概括，非单一方法说明。
