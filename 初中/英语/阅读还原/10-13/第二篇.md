**Sustainable Fashion: Dressing for a Better World**

Fast fashion—cheap clothes that are made quickly and often thrown away after a few wears—has become a major source of pollution and waste. The fashion industry uses huge amounts of water, energy, and chemicals, and many garments end up in landfills. Sustainable fashion aims to change this by producing clothes in ways that are kinder to the planet and to the people who make them. [1] Choosing clothes that last longer, are made from eco-friendly materials, or are produced under fair conditions can reduce the environmental and social cost of what we wear.

One way to be more sustainable is to buy less and choose better. Instead of buying many cheap items that fall apart quickly, we can invest in fewer pieces that are well made and that we will wear for years. [2] Wearing clothes longer, repairing them when they tear, and passing them on to others when we no longer need them extends the life of each garment and cuts down on waste.

Another option is to support brands that use recycled materials, organic cotton, or other sustainable fabrics. Some companies are also working to reduce water and energy use in their factories and to ensure that workers are paid fairly. [3] By choosing these brands and asking questions about how clothes are made, consumers can push the industry toward more responsible practices and show that there is demand for better options.

Secondhand and vintage shopping is another way to reduce the impact of fashion. Buying used clothes means no new resources are used to make them, and it keeps garments out of landfills. Many people enjoy finding unique styles at thrift stores or online resale platforms. [4] Reusing and recycling clothes is not just good for the planet—it can also be a fun and creative way to express personal style while saving money.

Sustainable fashion is not about perfection. It is about making better choices when we can—buying less, wearing longer, choosing wisely, and supporting change. [5] Every small step toward more sustainable habits sends a message to the industry and helps build a fashion system that values people and the planet as much as profit.
