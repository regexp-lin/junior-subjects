# 03-11 第二篇

**The Lost Art of Writing Letters**

Nowadays, when we want to tell a friend something, we simply take out our phones and send a quick text message. It takes only a few seconds. [1] However, before the invention of the Internet, people communicated mainly by writing letters by hand. Sadly, this beautiful tradition is slowly disappearing.

Writing a physical letter is very different from typing an email. [2] You cannot simply press a "delete" button if you make a mistake. Therefore, people usually think much more carefully about what they want to say before putting pen to paper. 

Furthermore, handwritten letters carry a personal touch that digital messages completely lack. [3] A text message looks exactly the same no matter who sends it. But a handwritten letter is as unique as the person who wrote it, showing their personal style and effort.

Another wonderful thing about letters is that they can be kept forever. [4] You can hide them in a special box and read them again many years later. Reading an old letter from a grandparent or an old friend can bring back beautiful memories and warm feelings.

Finally, getting a real letter in the mail is highly exciting. In a mailbox full of boring bills and advertisements, a colorful envelope stands out like a treasure. [5] So, why not pick up a beautiful pen tonight and write a letter to someone you love?

## 选项

A. As a result, handwriting is no longer taught in many modern schools.
B. When you write by hand, it requires your full attention and patience.
C. This quick speed allows us to stay connected with everyone easily.
D. Unlike phone messages that are easily lost or deleted over time.
E. You can actually see the writer's feelings through their handwriting.
F. It shows that someone truly cares enough to spend their valuable time on you.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] C   [2] B   [3] E   [4] D   [5] F

## 深度解析

- **[1] C**：
  - **线索**：指代关系与逻辑关联。空前说发短信（text message）“takes only a few seconds”（只需要几秒钟）。C选项“This quick speed allows us to stay connected with everyone easily”（这种飞快的速度让我们很容易与大家保持联系）中的“This quick speed（这种速度）”就是指代前一句的“只有几秒钟”。随后，空后使用“However（然而）”发生了时代的转折，引入了互联网发明前人们手工写信的慢节奏方式。
- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：对比引入与语境提示。空前提出段落观点：手写信与打电子邮件大不相同。B选项“When you write by hand, it requires your full attention and patience”（当你用手写时，它需要你全神贯注和耐心）是对“手写信”这一行为的具体要求描述。而空后的句子“You cannot simply press a 'delete' button...”（你不能简单地按个删除键）正是对为什么写信需要“patience（耐心和谨慎）”的详细解释，引出了随后的结果“人们落笔前会仔细思考”。
- **[3] E**：
  - **线索**：词汇同现与逻辑解释。空前指出“handwritten letters carry a personal touch”（手写信带有一种个人色彩/人情味）。E选项“You can actually see the writer's feelings through their handwriting”（你实际上能通过他们的笔迹看到作者的感情）就是对这种“personal touch”的深入解释。随后空后拿千篇一律的数字短信来进行反面衬托，最后总结手写信展现了个人风格（personal style），前后逻辑一气呵成。
- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：对比逻辑与结构搭配。空前提到“Another wonderful thing about letters is that they can be kept forever”（信件的另一个奇妙之处在于它们可以被永久保存）。D选项“Unlike phone messages that are easily lost or deleted over time”（不像随着时间推移很容易丢失或删除的手机短信）运用了反衬手法。手机短信的“easily lost”与前文信件的“kept forever”形成鲜明对比，也为了后文“你可以把它们藏在特别的盒子里多年后重读”提供了对比语境。
- **[5] F**：
  - **线索**：语义升华与总结呼应。本段描述收到信的兴奋，把信件比作账单中的宝藏。F选项“It shows that someone truly cares enough to spend their valuable time on you”（它表明有人真的在乎你，愿意为你花费他们宝贵的时间）是对为什么收到信会让人Exciting（兴奋）、为什么觉得它是宝藏的情感升华和终极解释。这种温暖的总结也为文章结尾的号召“为什么不今晚拿起笔给你爱的人写封信呢？”烘托了极好的情绪氛围。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **A. As a result, handwriting is no longer taught in many modern schools.**：该选项中包含“handwriting”这个文章中出现探讨的核心词。虽然从常理上看，因为打字方便导致学校不教书法这一社会事实确实存在，但文章的主题是呼吁人们回归写信，感受写信带来的温暖、专注和独特记忆。该选项所表达的结论虽然带上了逻辑词“As a result”，但与第一段（甚至全篇）的怀旧和温情主题并不贴合，是一个偏离文章核心情感的干扰项。
