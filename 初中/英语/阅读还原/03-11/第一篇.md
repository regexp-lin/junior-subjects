# 03-11 第一篇

**The Reality of Space Tourism**

For many years, traveling to space was something only highly trained astronauts could do. [1] Today, however, "space tourism" is slowly becoming a reality. Several private companies are now offering normal people the chance to fly beyond Earth's atmosphere.

Taking a trip to space is certainly an exciting idea. Passengers get to experience weightlessness and see the beautiful curve of our blue planet. [2] For those who have dreamed of touching the stars since childhood, it is the ultimate adventure. 

However, space tourism still faces some serious challenges. The most obvious problem is the huge cost. [3] This means that currently, only the super-rich can afford to buy a ticket. For the average person, a trip to the moon remains just a fantasy.

Safety is another major concern for both companies and passengers. Unlike flying in an airplane, traveling in a rocket is still incredibly dangerous. [4] Even a tiny mistake in the machine's design or a small piece of space rock could lead to a terrible disaster.

Finally, we must consider the environmental impact of these flights. [5] If space tourism becomes as common as air travel, the pollution might cause serious harm to our atmosphere. Therefore, before we all pack our bags for Mars, we need to make sure that space trips are safe, affordable, and green.

## 选项

A. The spaceship travels at extremely high speeds through a completely unknown environment.
B. It is reported that a single seat on these short space flights costs millions of dollars.
C. These rockets burn a massive amount of fuel, releasing harmful gases into the air.
D. Therefore, space tourism has already become the most popular holiday choice.
E. At that time, it was widely believed that no ordinary person would ever go there.
F. It is an unbelievable view that cannot be perfectly captured by any camera.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] E   [2] F   [3] B   [4] A   [5] C

## 深度解析

- **[1] E**：
  - **线索**：时间对比与逻辑转折。空前提及“For many years, traveling to space was something only highly trained astronauts could do”（多年来，太空旅行只有受过高度训练的宇航员才能做）。E选项的“At that time”（在那个时候）完美指代了前面的“For many years”。并且“no ordinary person would ever go there”（普通人永远不会去那里）与前句“只有宇航员去”形成同义呼应。同时，它为随后的转折句“Today, however, 'space tourism' is slowly becoming a reality”（然而今天，太空旅游逐渐成为现实）做好了极佳的反面对比铺垫。
- **[2] F**：
  - **线索**：代词指代与词汇复现。空前提到了乘客能看到“the beautiful curve of our blue planet”（我们蓝色星球美丽的弧线/轮廓）。F选项“It is an unbelievable view that cannot be perfectly captured by any camera”（这是一种任何相机都无法完美捕捉的难以置信的景色）中，代词“It”和定语“an unbelievable view（难以置信的景色）”恰如其分地指代和形容了前面的“beautiful curve”。
- **[3] B**：
  - **线索**：举例说明与因果逻辑。空前指出太空旅游最明显的问题是“the huge cost（巨大的成本/花费）”。B选项“It is reported that a single seat on these short space flights costs millions of dollars”（据报道，这些短途太空飞行的单人座位要花费数百万美元）是用具体的金钱数目（数百万美元）来具象化前文的“huge cost”。并且，正是因为这几百万美元的成本，才顺势引出了空后的必然推论“This means that currently, only the super-rich can afford...”（这意味着目前只有超级富豪才能负担得起...）。
- **[4] A**：
  - **线索**：并列例证与语境衔接。空前提到“traveling in a rocket is still incredibly dangerous”（乘坐火箭旅行仍然极其危险）。A选项“The spaceship travels at extremely high speeds through a completely unknown environment”（宇宙飞船在一个完全未知的环境中以极高的速度运行）是对为何危险的客观条件描述。而空后的句子“Even a tiny mistake... or a small piece of space rock could lead to a terrible disaster”则是对这种极速未知环境后果的具体举证（哪怕一点小错误都会带来灾难）。
- **[5] C**：
  - **线索**：同义解释与概念呼应。段落的首句（空前）抛出了段落主旨“environmental impact of these flights（这些航班的环境影响）”。C选项“These rockets burn a massive amount of fuel, releasing harmful gases into the air”（这些火箭燃烧大量的燃料，向空气中释放有害气体）正是对“环境影响”的具体内容阐释。燃料燃烧和废气排放也完美衔接了空后一句话里的担忧“the pollution might cause serious harm to our atmosphere（污染可能对我们的大气层造成严重危害）”。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **D. Therefore, space tourism has already become the most popular holiday choice.**：该干扰项包含“space tourism”的高频话题词，以及结论性的连词“Therefore”，容易被错放到最后一段作为总结。但是文章的大基调是：太空旅游很贵、很危险、有环境污染隐患，普通人去月球目前“仍是一个幻想”。选项中“has already become the most popular holiday choice（已经成为最受欢迎的度假选择）”严重违背了文中的事实和逻辑推断。
