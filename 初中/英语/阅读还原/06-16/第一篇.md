# 06-16 第一篇

**The Invention of Paper and Papermaking**

Before paper was invented, people wrote on bamboo strips, silk, or animal skins. These materials were either heavy, expensive, or hard to produce in large quantities. Around 2,000 years ago in China, a man named Cai Lun improved the method of making paper using tree bark, hemp, and rags. [1] In other words, his invention made it possible to create light, cheap, and useful paper that could be produced in great amounts. This change allowed knowledge to spread more easily across the world.

The ancient papermaking process had several steps. Workers first soaked the raw materials in water to soften them, then beat them into a pulp（纸浆）. [2] Next, they poured the mixture into a flat frame with a fine bamboo screen and shook it so the water drained away, leaving a thin layer of wet fibres. The sheet was then pressed to remove more water and dried in the sun. This method produced strong and lasting paper that could hold ink well. Each step required skill and patience.

Paper soon became important in China and later spread to other parts of Asia. [3] By the 8th century, the secret of papermaking had reached the Arab world and then Europe through trade along the Silk Road. Before long, paper replaced parchment（羊皮纸）and other writing materials in many places. Books became cheaper and easier to make, and more people could learn to read and write. The spread of paper helped shape human history.

Today, paper is still widely used, though digital technology has reduced our need for it in some areas. [4] We use paper for books, newspapers, packaging, money, and countless everyday objects. Yet the making of paper also uses large amounts of water and wood, which raises environmental concerns. Many people now recycle paper and support "green" products made from recycled or sustainable materials. Balancing our need for paper with care for the environment remains a challenge for the future.

[5] The invention of paper was one of the greatest gifts to human civilisation. It changed how we store and share knowledge, supported the growth of education and culture, and connected people across distances. From ancient China to the modern world, paper has carried our thoughts, stories, and ideas for nearly two thousand years.

## 选项

A. In other words, Cai Lun's work made paper light, affordable, and easy to produce in large quantities.
B. Then they poured the pulp into a frame with a bamboo screen and let the water drain to form a thin sheet.
C. In fact, papermaking spread through trade routes and later replaced older writing materials in many regions.
D. These uses and impacts show that paper remains useful but also leads to environmental challenges.
E. In short, paper has greatly shaped human civilisation and continues to carry our ideas across time.
F. For example, bamboo was one of the main plants used in early Chinese papermaking.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：概括蔡伦造纸术革新带来的效果。空前描述"Before paper...wrote on bamboo, silk, animal skins...heavy, expensive, hard to produce...Around 2,000 years ago Cai Lun improved method using tree bark, hemp, rags"，空后说明"his invention made it possible to create light, cheap, useful paper...produced in great amounts...knowledge to spread more easily"。A选项"In other words, Cai Lun's work made paper light, affordable, and easy to produce in large quantities"（换言之，蔡伦的工作使纸张轻便、廉价且易于大批量生产）中"In other words"概括空前发明，"light, affordable, easy to produce...large quantities"与空后"light, cheap...great amounts"精准呼应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：造纸流程中的抄纸步骤。空前描述"Soaked raw materials...soften...beat into pulp"，空后详述" poured the mixture into flat frame with fine bamboo screen...shook so water drained away, leaving thin layer of wet fibres...pressed...dried in sun...strong, lasting paper"。B选项"Then they poured the pulp into a frame with a bamboo screen and let the water drain to form a thin sheet"（接着他们将纸浆倒入带竹筛的框内，让水流走后形成薄薄的纸层）中"Then"承接前步，"poured...frame...bamboo screen...water drain...thin sheet"与空后 poured、frame、screen、drained、thin layer 完美对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：造纸术的传播及取代旧书写材料。空前提出"Paper soon became important...spread to other parts of Asia"，空后列举"By 8th century...secret reached Arab world and Europe through Silk Road...paper replaced parchment...books cheaper...more people could learn to read...helped shape human history"。C选项"In fact, papermaking spread through trade routes and later replaced older writing materials in many regions"（事实上，造纸术通过贸易路线传播，后来在许多地区取代了更古老的书写材料）中"In fact"强化传播事实，"spread through trade routes...replaced older writing materials"与空后 Silk Road、replaced parchment 精准对应，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：纸的现代用途及环境问题。空前提出"Today, paper is still widely used...digital technology reduced need in some areas"，空后描述"Use paper for books, newspapers, packaging, money...countless everyday objects...making uses large amounts water and wood...environmental concerns...recycle...sustainable...Balancing need with care for environment...challenge"。D选项"These uses and impacts show that paper remains useful but also leads to environmental challenges"（这些用途和影响表明纸张仍然有用，但也带来环境挑战）中"These uses and impacts"指代空后各种用途与资源消耗，"remains useful...environmental challenges"与空后 useful、environmental concerns 完美对应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与纸对人类文明的意义。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"invention one of greatest gifts...changed how we store and share knowledge...supported education and culture...connected people...carried our thoughts, stories, ideas for nearly two thousand years"。E选项"In short, paper has greatly shaped human civilisation and continues to carry our ideas across time"（简言之，纸极大地塑造了人类文明，并持续承载着我们的思想跨越时代）用"In short"总结全文，"shaped human civilisation...carry our ideas across time"与空后"greatest gifts...carried our thoughts"形成呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, bamboo was one of the main plants used in early Chinese papermaking.**：涉及竹子作为造纸原料，易被误填[2]或[1]。但F以"For example"引出单一原料，而空[2]讨论的是抄纸工序（frame、screen、drain），F的"bamboo...main plants"属于原料选择，与"poured...screen...drain"的工艺流程焦点不同；空[1]讨论蔡伦发明的整体效果，F的原料细节与"light, cheap, large quantities"的概括层次不符。F属"主题相关但论述层次错位"型干扰。
