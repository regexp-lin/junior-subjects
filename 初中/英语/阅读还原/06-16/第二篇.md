**How the Eye Works**

The human eye is a wonderful organ that allows us to see the world in colour and detail. It works like a camera: light enters through the front, passes through several parts, and finally forms an image at the back. [1] In other words, the eye is a complex system that turns light into the pictures we "see" in our brain. Understanding how the eye works helps us appreciate the gift of sight and reminds us to take good care of our eyes.

When light enters the eye, it first passes through the cornea（角膜）—a clear, curved layer at the front that helps focus the light. [2] Then it travels through the pupil, the black circle in the middle of the eye, which grows larger in dim light and smaller in bright light to control how much light enters. Behind the pupil is the lens, which further focuses the light onto the retina（视网膜）at the back of the eye. The lens can change shape to focus on objects both near and far—a process called accommodation（调节）.

The retina is where the real "seeing" happens. [3] It contains millions of cells called rods and cones: rods help us see in low light and detect movement, while cones allow us to see colours and fine details in bright light. These cells turn light into electrical signals that travel along the optic nerve to the brain. The brain then interprets these signals and creates the image we perceive. Without the retina and its cells, we would not be able to see at all.

Many people have vision problems. [4] Some are short-sighted and cannot see distant objects clearly; others are long-sighted and have trouble seeing things up close. These conditions can often be corrected with glasses or contact lenses. Spending too much time on screens, reading in poor light, or not wearing protective glasses when needed can strain the eyes. Regular eye checks and good habits—such as resting the eyes and spending time outdoors—can help keep our vision healthy for life.

[5] Our eyes are precious and deserve our care. By understanding how they work and taking steps to protect them, we can enjoy clear sight for years to come. Remember to rest your eyes, wear sunglasses in bright sunshine, and see an eye doctor if you notice any changes in your vision.
