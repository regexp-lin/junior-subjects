**The Survival Crisis of Polar Bears**

Polar bears are among the largest land predators on Earth. They live in the Arctic, where snow and ice cover the land for most of the year. These powerful animals depend on sea ice to hunt seals, their main food source. [1] As the climate warms, the ice melts earlier each spring and forms later each autumn, leaving polar bears with less time and space to find food.

Scientists have been studying polar bears for decades. They track the animals, measure their weight, and observe their behavior. [2] The data shows that polar bear populations are declining in many regions, and some bears are thinner and weaker than they were in the past because of food shortages.

The loss of sea ice affects not only polar bears but the whole Arctic ecosystem. Seals, fish, and other creatures also suffer when their habitat changes. [3] What happens in the Arctic matters for the rest of the world, as melting ice contributes to rising sea levels and shifts in weather patterns.

Many countries and organizations are working to protect polar bears. They set up nature reserves, reduce pollution, and support research. [4] Still, the most important step is to slow down climate change by cutting greenhouse gas emissions worldwide.

Polar bears have become a symbol of the fight against climate change. When we see a bear standing on a small piece of ice, we are reminded of what we stand to lose. [5] Saving polar bears means saving the Arctic—and ultimately, taking care of our shared planet for future generations.

## 选项

A. The main threat they face today is climate change, which is destroying their frozen home.
B. Research findings have raised serious concerns about the future of these magnificent creatures.
C. Scientists warn that the entire Arctic food chain could be disrupted if temperatures keep rising.
D. These efforts help, but they cannot solve the problem alone.
E. Protecting them is not just about one species; it is about protecting our planet and ourselves.
F. For example, polar bears can swim long distances, but they cannot catch seals in open water.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述北极熊依赖海冰捕猎海豹，空后具体说明冰融得更早、形成更晚。A概括指出气候变化是主要威胁、正在破坏冰冻家园，与后文现象形成总—分关系。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说科学家追踪、测量、观察北极熊，空后给出具体结论（数量下降、变瘦变弱）。B的"Research findings"与"data"对应，"raised serious concerns"与"declining""thinner and weaker"呼应，作为研究结果的概括。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说海冰减少影响整个北极生态，空后说"What happens in the Arctic matters for the rest of the world"。C的"entire Arctic food chain could be disrupted"承接前文，又引出后文全球影响的逻辑，承上启下。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前列举保护措施，空后说"the most important step is to slow down climate change"。D的"These efforts help, but they cannot solve the problem alone"转折指出局限，自然引出后文更根本的解决方案。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说北极熊成为气候变化的象征，空后无。E的"Protecting them is not just about one species"升华主题，与"saving our shared planet"形成呼应，适合作全文收束。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, polar bears can swim long distances, but they cannot catch seals in open water.**：以For example开头，内容与北极熊捕食相关，但属具体细节举例，与各空需要的概括或过渡句不符；易误放[1]或[2]，但[1]需威胁总述，[2]需研究结论概括。
