**The Culture of Tea Around the World**

Tea is one of the most widely consumed drinks in the world, second only to water. From Asia to Europe, from ancient times to the present day, tea has played an important role in many cultures. It is more than a simple beverage—tea is often connected to ceremony, hospitality, and daily ritual. [1] The way people prepare and enjoy tea varies greatly from place to place. In China, tea has been cultivated for thousands of years and is central to traditions like gongfu cha, where small cups are used to appreciate the flavor and aroma of fine teas. In Japan, the tea ceremony emphasizes harmony, respect, and a moment of calm. In Britain, afternoon tea with scones and sandwiches has become a beloved custom.

[2] Tea has a long and rich history that spans continents. Legend says that tea was discovered in China nearly 5,000 years ago when leaves fell into an emperor's boiling water. Over time, tea spread along trade routes to Japan, the Middle East, and eventually Europe. The British East India Company played a major role in bringing tea to the West, and today tea is grown in many countries, including India, Sri Lanka, Kenya, and China. Each region has developed its own varieties and traditions.

[3] Different types of tea come from the same plant—Camellia sinensis—but are processed in different ways. Green tea is lightly processed to preserve its natural color and antioxidants. Black tea is fully oxidized, giving it a stronger flavor and dark color. Oolong tea falls between green and black. Herbal "teas" such as chamomile or mint are not made from the tea plant but from other plants, and are often enjoyed for their soothing effects. Understanding these differences helps people choose teas that suit their taste and health goals.

[4] Tea culture often goes beyond the drink itself. In many societies, offering tea to guests is a sign of welcome and respect. Sharing a cup of tea can be a way to strengthen relationships, conduct business, or simply take a break from a busy day. The act of slowing down to prepare and drink tea encourages mindfulness and connection. In a fast-paced world, the tea ritual reminds us to pause and appreciate the present moment.

[5] Tea continues to evolve while staying rooted in tradition. Modern tea culture includes everything from bubble tea and iced matcha to specialty tea shops and online communities of tea lovers. Yet the core values of tea—connection, hospitality, and mindful enjoyment—remain meaningful across time and borders. Whether you prefer a formal ceremony or a simple cup at home, tea offers a way to slow down, connect with others, and savor life's small pleasures.

## 选项

A. Tea culture differs from one region to another, yet each has its own meaningful traditions.
B. The history of tea is a story of trade, discovery, and cultural exchange across the globe.
C. The variety of teas available reflects different processing methods and ingredients.
D. Tea often serves as a bridge for social connection and a moment of mindful pause.
E. Tea remains both timeless and adaptable, blending old traditions with new forms of enjoyment.
F. For example, chai in India is typically brewed with milk, spices, and sugar.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前概括茶不仅是饮料，还与文化、待客、仪式相关，空后分别介绍中国功夫茶、日本茶道、英国下午茶。A 句 "Tea culture differs from one region to another, yet each has its own meaningful traditions" 承接 "varies greatly"，并自然引出后文对各地传统的具体描述，承上启下。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：段落讲述茶的历史传播。空后描述中国起源、丝绸之路传播、东印度公司、各国种植。B 句 "The history of tea is a story of trade, discovery, and cultural exchange across the globe" 准确概括本段主旨。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：段落介绍不同茶类的加工与特点。空后对比绿茶、红茶、乌龙茶及花草茶。C 句 "The variety of teas available reflects different processing methods and ingredients" 精准对应本段内容。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：段落讨论茶文化的社会与心理意义。空后提到待客、增进关系、商务、正念与当下。D 句 "Tea often serves as a bridge for social connection and a moment of mindful pause" 与前文完全呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：结尾段总结茶在传统与现代间的延续。空后提到珍珠茶、抹茶、茶店与网络社群，以及历久弥新的核心价值。E 句 "Tea remains both timeless and adaptable, blending old traditions with new forms of enjoyment" 与段落主旨自然衔接。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example...**：印度奶茶的冲泡方式虽体现 regional tradition，但属于具体举例，应嵌入第 1 段或第 2 段内部。作为独立选项，其层级为例证而非段落概括，易因 "India" 与第 2 段 "India" 相关而误选。
