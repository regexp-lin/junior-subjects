**City Life vs Country Life: Finding Your Place**

Cities and the countryside offer very different ways of living. In cities, people can find more jobs, schools, hospitals, and entertainment. Public transport makes it easy to get around, and there are often more chances to meet people and try new things. However, cities can be crowded, noisy, and expensive. Traffic and pollution are common problems. [1]

Country life tends to be quieter and slower. There is more space, fresher air, and often a stronger sense of community. People may know their neighbours well and help each other more. On the other hand, jobs and services can be limited. Young people often move to cities for work or study, and some villages face depopulation. Access to healthcare and shops may require longer trips. [2]

Each lifestyle has its own benefits and trade-offs. City dwellers might dream of peace and nature; country residents might miss the buzz of urban life. Some people choose to live in the suburbs—between city and country—to enjoy a mix of both. Others change their mind over time, moving from one to the other as their needs change. [3]

Governments and planners try to improve both environments. Cities work on green spaces, bike lanes, and cleaner air. Rural areas invest in broadband, transport links, and services to attract young people and businesses. The goal is to make city and country life more sustainable and attractive for everyone. [4]

Where you live shapes your daily experience, but it does not define who you are. Whether you prefer the energy of a city or the calm of the countryside, the best choice is the one that fits your values, work, and family. Understanding both worlds helps us appreciate what each has to offer. [5]

## 选项

A. In short, cities offer convenience and opportunity but often at the cost of space, peace, and affordability.
B. For these reasons, rural areas provide a different set of advantages and challenges for those who call them home.
C. That is why there is no single "best" place—only what works best for each person at each stage of life.
D. Thanks to these efforts, both urban and rural areas can become better places to live.
E. In other words, the choice of where to live is personal, and knowing both lifestyles enriches our view.
F. For example, Tokyo has one of the most developed metro systems in the world, carrying millions of passengers daily.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍城市优势——工作教育医疗娱乐多、公共交通便利、社交与尝试机会多，以及劣势——拥挤嘈杂昂贵、交通与污染，空后需总结城市生活的两面性。A的"convenience and opportunity"与"cost of space, peace, and affordability"与前文对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说乡村更安静缓慢、空间大空气好、社区感强、邻里互助，但也受限——工作与服务少、年轻人外迁、村落空心化、医疗购物需远行，空后需总结乡村的利弊。B的"different set of advantages and challenges"与前文呼应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说各有取舍——城市人向往田园、乡村人怀念都市、有人选郊区折中、有人随需求变化而搬迁，空后需总结无单一最佳选择。C的"no single 'best' place"与前文对应，"what works best for each person at each stage of life"与前文呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说政府与规划者努力——城市改善绿地自行车道空气质量、乡村投资宽带交通与服务以吸引年轻人与企业、追求可持续与吸引力，空后需总结这些努力的效果。D的"these efforts"指代前文，"both urban and rural areas can become better places to live"与前文对应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说居住地影响日常但不定义人、无论偏好城市活力还是乡村宁静、最佳选择取决于价值观工作与家庭、了解两种生活可丰富视野，空后需总结选择的个人性与双重视角的价值。E的"choice of where to live is personal"与前文呼应，"knowing both lifestyles enriches our view"与前文对应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, Tokyo has one of the most developed metro systems...**：以For example开头，为具体城市交通案例（东京地铁）。各空均为概括或过渡句；[1]易被误选因涉及公共交通，但该空需总结城市整体利弊，而非单一系统的数据举例。
