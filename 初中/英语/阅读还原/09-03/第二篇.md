# 09-03 第二篇

**Traditional Festivals Around the World**

Every culture has its own special festivals and celebrations. These events bring people together, honor traditions, and give communities a chance to celebrate their heritage. From colorful parades to family gatherings, festivals play an important role in keeping cultural identity alive. [1] Learning about festivals from different parts of the world helps us appreciate the rich diversity of human culture.

Many festivals are connected to the changing seasons or the harvest. In autumn, people in many countries celebrate the gathering of crops with feasts and thanksgiving. [2] These seasonal celebrations remind us of our connection to nature and the importance of being grateful for the food that sustains us.

Religious festivals are another important part of cultural life. Christmas, Eid, Diwali, and the Spring Festival are just a few examples of holidays that hold deep spiritual meaning for billions of people. [3] Although the beliefs behind these festivals differ, they often share common themes of family, generosity, and hope.

Festivals also serve as a way to pass traditions from one generation to the next. Children learn songs, dances, recipes, and stories through participating in celebrations with their families. [4] This transfer of cultural knowledge ensures that customs and values are not lost over time but continue to enrich the lives of future generations.

In our increasingly connected world, people have more opportunities to experience festivals from other cultures. Attending a cultural festival or learning about one online can open your eyes to new ideas and ways of life. [5] By celebrating both our own traditions and those of others, we build bridges of understanding and respect across cultures.

## 选项

A. Festivals are windows into the soul of a culture, reflecting its values, history, and way of life.
B. Harvest festivals, found in nearly every culture, are among the oldest celebrations in human history.
C. Despite their differences, these celebrations unite people through shared human experiences and emotions.
D. In this way, festivals act as living classrooms where culture is taught through experience rather than textbooks.
E. Embracing cultural exchange through festivals strengthens global connections and promotes peace.
F. For example, the Mid-Autumn Festival in China features mooncakes and lanterns to celebrate family reunion.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述节日的多种功能（团聚、传承、庆祝），空后说了解各地节日有助于欣赏文化多样性。A的"windows into the soul of a culture"概括节日的文化意义，"reflecting its values, history, and way of life"与"cultural identity"呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说秋天许多国家庆祝丰收，空后说季节性庆祝提醒我们与自然的联系。B的"Harvest festivals... among the oldest celebrations"对丰收节作历史概括，承上启下。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前列举圣诞、开斋节、排灯节、春节等宗教节日，空后说这些节日虽信仰不同但共享家庭、慷慨、希望主题。C的"Despite their differences"承接不同宗教，"unite people through shared human experiences"概括共性。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说孩子通过参与节日学习歌曲、舞蹈、食谱、故事，空后说文化知识的传递确保习俗不会丢失。D的"festivals act as living classrooms"概括前文学习过程，"taught through experience rather than textbooks"突出体验式传承。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说人们有更多机会体验他国节日，空后说庆祝自己和他人的传统可以建立理解与尊重的桥梁。E的"Embracing cultural exchange through festivals"承接跨文化体验，"strengthens global connections and promotes peace"升华主题。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the Mid-Autumn Festival in China features mooncakes and lanterns to celebrate family reunion.**：以For example开头，属具体节日举例；易误放[2]或[3]，但两空分别需丰收节历史概括和宗教节日共性概括，非具体节日描述。
