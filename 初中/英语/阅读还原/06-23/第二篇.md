**Sun Protection: Why It Matters for Your Skin**

The sun gives us light, warmth, and Vitamin D, but too much exposure can harm our skin. Ultraviolet（紫外线）, or UV, rays from the sun can cause sunburn, premature ageing, and even skin cancer. [1] In other words, enjoying the sun safely means understanding the risks and taking simple steps to protect ourselves. Whether you are at the beach, on a sports field, or just walking to school, sun protection should be part of your daily routine, especially in summer.

Sunscreen is one of the most effective ways to protect your skin. [2] Choose a sunscreen with an SPF of at least 30, which blocks most UVB rays. Apply it 15 to 30 minutes before going outside, and reapply every two hours or after swimming or sweating. Many people forget to put sunscreen on their ears, neck, and the backs of their hands—these areas get a lot of sun too. Sunscreen works best when used together with other steps, such as wearing a hat and staying in the shade during the strongest hours of the day, usually between 10 a.m. and 4 p.m.

Clothing and accessories also help block the sun. [3] Long-sleeved shirts, long trousers, and wide-brimmed hats can provide good protection. Dark or tightly woven fabrics block more UV rays than light, thin ones. Sunglasses that block both UVA and UVB rays protect your eyes and the delicate skin around them. Some clothes now carry a "UPF" (Ultraviolet Protection Factor) label—the higher the number, the better the protection. On hot days, choosing light-coloured, loose clothing can keep you cool while still offering some sun protection.

Children and teenagers need extra attention. [4] Young skin is more sensitive to UV damage, and sunburns in childhood can increase the risk of skin cancer later in life. Parents and schools can help by providing shade, encouraging the use of sunscreen, and teaching kids to stay out of the midday sun. Teens often spend more time outdoors for sports or activities—reminding them to apply sunscreen and wear protective clothing can become a healthy habit for life. Leading by example—when adults use sun protection, children are more likely to do the same.

[5] By using sunscreen, wearing the right clothes, and avoiding the strongest sun, you can enjoy outdoor activities while keeping your skin healthy for years to come. The next time you step outside on a sunny day, remember: a little protection goes a long way.
