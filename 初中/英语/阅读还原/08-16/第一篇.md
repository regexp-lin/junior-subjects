# 08-16 第一篇

**The Invention and Impact of the Compass**

Before the compass was invented, sailors had to rely on the sun and stars to find their direction at sea. On cloudy days or during long ocean voyages, they could easily get lost. The compass changed all of this. It was first developed in ancient China around the 2nd century BC, originally as a tool for fortune-telling. [1] Over time, people discovered that the same magnetic principle could be used for navigation, which would eventually transform exploration and trade across the world.

The earliest compasses were simple: a magnetized needle floating in water that would always point north. Later, the needle was placed on a pivot inside a box with direction markings. [2] This design made the compass portable and reliable enough for use on ships. Chinese sailors used compasses for sea travel by the 11th century. The technology spread along trade routes to the Middle East and Europe, where it revolutionized maritime navigation.

The compass made long ocean voyages possible. Sailors could now travel far from land without losing their way. [3] Explorers like Christopher Columbus and Zheng He relied on the compass to reach new lands. Trade between continents grew, connecting cultures and spreading ideas. The Age of Exploration—when Europeans sailed to the Americas, Africa, and Asia—would have been much more difficult without this simple but powerful tool.

Today, we have GPS and digital maps, but the compass has not been forgotten. [4] It remains useful when electronic devices fail or when batteries run out. Hikers, sailors, and military personnel still carry compasses as backup navigation tools. The basic principle—a magnetic needle pointing north—has not changed in over a thousand years. Some things that work well simply do not need to be replaced.

The compass is a reminder of how one invention can change the course of history. [5] It helped humans explore the globe, build trade networks, and understand the world better. From ancient China to modern classrooms, the compass continues to teach us about magnetism and the creative power of human ingenuity. Its impact reaches far beyond the small box that holds a spinning needle.

## 选项

A. Its original purpose was quite different from what we use it for today.
B. Improved designs soon made it practical for everyday use at sea.
C. This reliability opened a new chapter in human exploration.
D. Simple tools often prove their worth when technology fails.
E. Few inventions have had such a lasting impact on human history.
F. For example, the Vikings used the sun and stars to navigate to Iceland.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前"originally as a tool for fortune-telling"，空后"people discovered that the same magnetic principle could be used for navigation"。A项"Its original purpose was quite different from what we use it for today"承上启下，既呼应"fortune-telling"，又为后文"discovered...for navigation"做铺垫。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：第二段讲罗盘结构演变，空前"needle on a pivot inside a box with direction markings"，空后"This design made the compass portable and reliable...Chinese sailors used...by the 11th century"。B项"Improved designs soon made it practical for everyday use at sea"概括设计改进带来实用化，与后文"portable and reliable"衔接。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：第三段"The compass made long ocean voyages possible"，空前"sailors could now travel far from land"，空后"Explorers like Christopher Columbus and Zheng He relied on the compass...Trade between continents grew...Age of Exploration"。C项"This reliability opened a new chapter in human exploration"概括罗盘催生大航海时代，与后文举例与影响对应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：第四段"we have GPS and digital maps, but the compass has not been forgotten"，空前对比现代技术，空后"It remains useful when electronic devices fail...Hikers, sailors...still carry compasses as backup"。D项"Simple tools often prove their worth when technology fails"点明罗盘在技术失效时的价值，与后文完美衔接。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：结尾段"The compass is a reminder of how one invention can change the course of history"，空前总结历史意义，空后"It helped humans explore the globe, build trade networks...continues to teach us..."。E项"Few inventions have had such a lasting impact on human history"升华主题，与后文具体影响列举呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the Vikings used the sun and stars to navigate to Iceland**：F讲指南针发明前的导航方式，与"罗盘发明与影响"的主线相关但方向相反。若放[1]会与"fortune-telling→navigation"的转折冲突；放[3]会打断"罗盘促成探险"的逻辑；易造成误选。
