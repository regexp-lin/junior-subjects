# 07-29 第一篇

**The History of Paper**

Paper is one of the most important inventions in human history. Before paper, people wrote on bamboo, silk, or stone—materials that were either heavy or expensive. [1] This invention changed how knowledge was stored and shared across the world. Today we take paper for granted, but its creation was a revolution.

Paper was first made in China around 2,000 years ago. [2] A court official named Cai Lun improved the process by mixing plant fibers with water. He used materials like tree bark, hemp, and old rags. The mixture was pressed flat and dried to form thin sheets. This method spread along the Silk Road to other civilizations.

For centuries, papermaking remained a slow, handmade craft. [3] In the 19th century, machines were invented to produce paper on a large scale. Factories could make tons of paper every day. Books and newspapers became affordable for ordinary people. Literacy rates rose dramatically.

Paper has many uses beyond writing. [4] We use it for packaging, money, tissues, and even building materials. Different types of paper serve different purposes. Cardboard boxes protect goods during shipping, while tissues are made to be soft and absorbent.

In the digital age, some people wonder if paper will disappear. [5] Yet paper remains important for education, art, and legal documents. Recycling has made paper production more sustainable. The story of paper is far from over.

## 选项

A. It opened the door for education and the spread of ideas everywhere.
B. Ancient craftsmen developed techniques to turn plant materials into sheets.
C. Industrialization transformed paper from a luxury into an everyday necessity.
D. Its versatility has made it essential in countless aspects of modern life.
E. Paper continues to adapt and find its place alongside new technology.
F. For example, the ancient Egyptians wrote on papyrus made from reeds.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前对比纸张发明前的书写材料；空后强调发明的革命性意义。A 项“它为各地的教育和思想传播打开了大门”承接“改变知识的储存与传播方式”，与后文“革命”相呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前指出纸张约两千年前在中国发明；空后介绍蔡伦及植物纤维制纸法。B 项“古代工匠发展了将植物材料制成薄片的技术”概括造纸工艺，与后文具体描述形成总分关系。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说造纸曾长期是缓慢的手工技艺；空后叙述19世纪机械化生产及对书籍、报纸、识字率的影响。C 项“工业化使纸张从奢侈品变成日常必需品”概括该段主旨，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前提出纸张用途不止书写；空后列举包装、货币、纸巾、建材等。D 项“其多功能性使其在现代生活诸多方面不可或缺”总结主旨，与后文举例对应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前引出数字时代纸张是否会消失的疑问；空后说明纸张在教育、艺术、法律中的持续重要性及回收利用。E 项“纸张继续适应并在新技术旁找到自己的位置”总结本段主旨，与后文呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the ancient Egyptians wrote on papyrus...**：与早期书写材料相关，但 papyrus 非“纸”，且埃及不在中国造纸技术发展脉络中，仅为平行举例，与各空位逻辑不符。
