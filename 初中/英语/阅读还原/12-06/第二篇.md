# 12-06 第二篇

**Fire Safety: Protect Yourself and Others**

Fire can spread in minutes and cause serious injury or loss of life. Understanding basic fire safety helps you stay calm and act quickly in an emergency. Fires need three things to start: fuel, heat, and oxygen. Removing any one of these can stop a fire or slow its spread. [1]

Prevention is the best defence. Do not leave cooking unattended, and keep flammable items away from stoves and heaters. Check electrical cords for damage and avoid overloading sockets. Candles and matches should be used with care and never left burning unsupervised. A small habit can prevent a major disaster. [2]

Smoke alarms save lives. Install them on every floor and in or near sleeping areas. Test alarms monthly and replace batteries at least once a year. When an alarm sounds, treat it as a real emergency. Have an escape plan: know two ways out of each room and a meeting point outside. Practice the plan with your family. [3]

If a fire breaks out, stay low under smoke and cover your mouth with a wet cloth if possible. Feel doors before opening them—if they are hot, use another escape route. Never go back inside for belongings. Call emergency services once you are safe. Every second counts in a fire. [4]

Fire safety is everyone's responsibility. Schools and workplaces hold drills to prepare people for emergencies. Learning basic first aid can help if someone is injured. By being aware and prepared, you can protect yourself and help others when it matters most. [5]

## 选项

A. For this reason, knowing how fire works is the first step toward keeping yourself safe.
B. Most fires can be prevented by paying attention to everyday risks.
C. These simple steps can give you precious time to get out safely.
D. Staying calm and following these rules increases your chance of survival.
E. That is why fire safety education and preparation matter for us all.
F. For example, a kitchen towel too close to a hot stove can catch fire within seconds.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍火灾蔓延快、可能造成伤害与死亡、了解基础消防安全有助于冷静应对、火三角（燃料热氧）及移除其一可灭火，空后需总结了解火灾原理的重要性。A的"For this reason"承接前文，"knowing how fire works"与火三角对应，"first step toward keeping yourself safe"与前文呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说预防最重要——勿无人看管烹饪、易燃物远离炉灶、检查电线避免过载、蜡烛火柴谨慎使用，空后需总结日常风险的可防性。B的"Most fires can be prevented"与前文对应，"paying attention to everyday risks"与前文具体建议呼应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说烟雾报警器救命、每层及睡眠区安装、定期检测换电池、制定逃生计划、两条出路及户外集合点、与家人演练，空后需总结这些措施带来的逃生时间。C的"These simple steps"指代前文，"give you precious time to get out safely"与前文呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说火灾中要低姿避烟、湿布捂口、摸门再开、门热则换路线、不要折返取物、安全后报警，空后需总结冷静按规则行动的效果。D的"Staying calm and following these rules"与前文对应，"increases your chance of survival"与前文呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说消防安全人人有责、学校单位演练、学急救可助伤者、有意识有准备能保护自己帮助他人，空后需总结安全教育与准备的价值。E的"That is why"承接前文，"fire safety education and preparation matter for us all"与前文对应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, a kitchen towel too close to a hot stove can catch fire...**：以For example开头，为具体火灾案例（厨房毛巾起火）。各空均为概括或过渡句；[2]易被误选因涉及厨房风险，但该空需总结日常风险的整体可防性，而非单一物品起火的举例。
