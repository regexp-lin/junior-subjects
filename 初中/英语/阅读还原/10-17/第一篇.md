# 10-17 第一篇

**Sign Language and Deaf Culture**

Sign language is a complete language with its own grammar, vocabulary, and way of expressing ideas. It is used by millions of Deaf people around the world to communicate. Unlike spoken language, sign language uses hand shapes, facial expressions, and body movements. [1] Learning sign language helps hearing people connect with the Deaf community and understand their world better.

Each country often has its own sign language. American Sign Language (ASL) is different from British Sign Language (BSL), and both differ from Chinese Sign Language (CSL). These languages have developed over many years within their Deaf communities. [2] Sign language is not simply a word-for-word translation of spoken language—it has its own rules and ways of building sentences that make it unique and expressive.

Deaf culture is rich and full of traditions. Deaf people have their own history, jokes, poetry, and art forms that use signing. Many Deaf people see themselves as part of a cultural group, not as people with a disability. [3] This cultural view helps Deaf people feel proud of who they are and builds a strong sense of belonging within their community.

Schools and workplaces are slowly becoming more inclusive. More businesses now offer sign language interpreters for meetings and events. Some schools teach sign language to all students, not just those who are Deaf. [4] When everyone can communicate in different ways, society becomes more open and welcoming to people of all abilities.

The future of sign language looks bright. Technology such as video calls has made it easier for Deaf people to communicate at a distance. More apps and websites now include sign language content. [5] As more people learn and use sign language, the barriers between the hearing and Deaf worlds will continue to shrink, creating a more connected world for everyone.

## 选项

A. Sign language opens doors to friendship, understanding, and equal opportunity for Deaf and hearing people alike.
B. Just as English and Chinese have different grammar, sign languages around the world each have their own structure that reflects the culture and history of the people who use them.
C. Being part of Deaf culture means sharing experiences, supporting one another, and celebrating the beauty of visual communication.
D. Making sign language more common in public life is an important step toward building a society where everyone can participate fully.
E. As technology improves and awareness grows, we can look forward to a future where sign language is respected and used more widely.
F. For example, in Japan, there is Japanese Sign Language (JSL), which has its own signs and grammar that differ from both spoken Japanese and other sign languages.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍手语的特征和用途，空后说"Learning sign language helps hearing people connect with the Deaf community"。A的"opens doors to friendship, understanding, and equal opportunity for Deaf and hearing people alike"与空后"connect"、"understand their world better"完美呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前列举各国手语不同（ASL、BSL、CSL），空后说"Sign language is not simply a word-for-word translation... it has its own rules"。B的"sign languages around the world each have their own structure"承接空前各国手语差异，并引出空后"own rules and ways"。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍聋人文化（历史、笑话、诗歌、艺术），空后说"This cultural view helps Deaf people feel proud... builds a strong sense of belonging"。C的"Being part of Deaf culture means sharing experiences, supporting one another"与空后"sense of belonging"、"proud"呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说学校和工作场所提供手语翻译、教授手语，空后说"When everyone can communicate in different ways, society becomes more open"。D的"Making sign language more common... society where everyone can participate fully"与空后"open and welcoming"衔接。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说技术发展、更多app和网站包含手语内容，空后说"As more people learn and use sign language, the barriers... will continue to shrink"。E的"As technology improves and awareness grows... sign language is respected and used more widely"与空后展望未来形成呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, in Japan, there is Japanese Sign Language (JSL)...**：以For example开头，是具体国家案例。文中[1]和[2]空虽涉及各国手语，但[1]需概括手语对听障与健听人士的意义，[2]需概括性过渡句说明手语有其语法结构，而非补充具体例证，故F为干扰项。
