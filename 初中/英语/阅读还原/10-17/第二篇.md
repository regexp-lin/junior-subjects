# 10-17 第二篇

**The History of Flying Machines**

Human beings have dreamed of flying for thousands of years. Ancient stories tell of people who wished to fly like birds. It was not until the late 18th and early 19th centuries that real progress began. [1] The first successful flight with humans came from balloons, which proved that the sky was no longer out of reach.

Balloons could rise into the air, but they could not be steered easily. Inventors wanted to build machines that could fly in a chosen direction. In the late 1800s, gliders—aircraft without engines—showed that controlled flight was possible. [2] These early experiments laid the groundwork for the powered airplanes that would change the world in the 20th century.

On December 17, 1903, the Wright brothers made history. Their Flyer became the first powered airplane to achieve controlled, sustained flight. The flight lasted only 12 seconds and covered about 37 meters, but it opened a new era. [3] From that small beginning, aviation grew rapidly, leading to commercial flights, military aircraft, and eventually space travel.

The 20th century saw airplanes become faster, safer, and able to carry more people and cargo. Jet engines replaced propeller engines, making long-distance travel common. By the end of the century, millions of people flew each year. [4] What had once been a dangerous dream had become an everyday way to cross continents and oceans.

Today, flying is a normal part of life. We use airplanes for business, holidays, and visiting family. Drones fill the sky for photography, delivery, and research. New ideas such as electric planes and flying taxis are being tested. [5] The story of human flight is far from over—each new invention brings us closer to a world where the sky is truly open to all.

## 选项

A. Flying was no longer just a dream; it had become a reality, and the age of air travel had begun.
B. Learning from these gliders, engineers began to design aircraft with engines that could carry people through the air under their own power.
C. That first flight was short, but it proved that humans could build machines that would one day connect every corner of the globe.
D. The dream of the ancient inventors had come true: ordinary people could now travel through the air as easily as they traveled by train or ship.
E. The next chapter of flight will bring even more changes, and the possibilities seem as limitless as the sky itself.
F. For example, the Montgolfier brothers in France flew the first hot-air balloon in 1783, carrying passengers for the first time in history.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前说18世纪末19世纪初取得进展，空后说"The first successful flight with humans came from balloons... the sky was no longer out of reach"。A的"Flying was no longer just a dream; it had become a reality, and the age of air travel had begun"与空后气球成功载人、天空可及完美呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说滑翔机证明可控飞行可行，空后说"These early experiments laid the groundwork for the powered airplanes"。B的"Learning from these gliders, engineers began to design aircraft with engines"承接滑翔机实验，引出空后动力飞机的诞生。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说莱特兄弟1903年首飞，仅12秒37米，空后说"From that small beginning, aviation grew rapidly... commercial flights, military aircraft, space travel"。C的"That first flight was short, but it proved... would one day connect every corner of the globe"与空后航空快速发展呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说20世纪飞机更快更安全、喷气机普及、数百万人乘机，空后说"What had once been a dangerous dream had become an everyday way to cross continents"。D的"The dream of the ancient inventors had come true: ordinary people could now travel through the air"与空后形成总结。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说当今飞行常态、无人机、电动飞机和飞行出租车在测试，空后说"The story of human flight is far from over—each new invention brings us closer"。E的"The next chapter of flight will bring even more changes... possibilities seem as limitless as the sky"与空后展望未来呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the Montgolfier brothers in France flew the first hot-air balloon in 1783...**：以For example开头，是具体历史案例。空[1]需概括"飞行从梦想到实现"的过渡，而非补充气球发明的细节，故F为干扰项。
