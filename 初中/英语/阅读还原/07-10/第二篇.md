# 07-10 第二篇

**How to Deal with Exam Stress**

Exams are a normal part of student life, but they often bring anxiety and pressure. Many students lie awake at night worrying about grades, feel their stomach turn before a test, or panic when they see a difficult question. [1] The good news is that exam stress can be managed with the right approach. Understanding why we feel stressed and learning practical strategies can help you perform better and feel calmer during the exam period.

Preparation is the best way to reduce anxiety. [2] When you have studied well, you enter the exam room with confidence. Start reviewing early—cramming the night before only increases stress. Create a study schedule, break material into small chunks, and use practice tests to familiarize yourself with the format. Feeling prepared reduces the fear of the unknown, which is often the main source of exam stress.

Your body and mind are connected. [3] Physical habits greatly affect how you feel during exams. Get enough sleep—at least 7 to 8 hours before a big test. Exercise regularly to release tension and improve focus. Avoid too much caffeine and junk food, which can make you feel jittery or tired. A rested body supports a calmer mind when it is time to sit down and write.

During the exam itself, simple techniques can help. [4] If you feel overwhelmed, take a few deep breaths. Read each question carefully before answering. If you get stuck, move on and return to difficult questions later. Remember that one difficult question does not define your whole exam. Staying calm under pressure is a skill you can practice and improve over time.

[5] Exam stress is common, but it does not have to control you. With good preparation, healthy habits, and calm strategies during the test, you can turn pressure into positive energy. Believe in yourself, and remember that one exam is just one step in your learning journey.

## 选项

A. Fortunately, there are effective ways to cope with this pressure.
B. Being well prepared builds confidence and reduces fear.
C. Taking care of your physical health supports your mental state during exams.
D. Simple strategies during the exam can help you stay focused and calm.
E. In the end, you can learn to manage exam stress successfully.
F. For example, some students perform better under pressure than others.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述考试带来的焦虑、失眠、肠胃不适、 panic 等，空后出现"The good news is that exam stress can be managed with the right approach"。A选项"Fortunately, there are effective ways to cope with this pressure"以"Fortunately"承接前文压力，自然引出后文的"good news"和应对方法，承上启下。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：第二段主题是准备减轻焦虑，空后具体说明提前复习、制定计划、分块学习、做模拟题，强调准备充分能减少对未知的恐惧。B选项"Being well prepared builds confidence and reduces fear"精准概括：充分准备建立信心、减少恐惧。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：第三段讲身心相连，空后列举睡眠、运动、减少咖啡因和垃圾食品等身体习惯对考试心态的影响。C选项"Taking care of your physical health supports your mental state during exams"概括本段：照顾身体健康有助于考试期间的心理状态。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：第四段讲考试过程中的技巧，空后具体方法：深呼吸、仔细读题、卡住时先跳过、保持冷静。D选项"Simple strategies during the exam can help you stay focused and calm"准确概括：考试中的简单策略有助于保持专注和冷静。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：最后一段需总结升华，空前已分述准备、身体、考场策略，空后归纳"good preparation, healthy habits, calm strategies""turn pressure into positive energy"。E选项"In the end, you can learn to manage exam stress successfully"用"In the end"收束全文，强调可以成功应对考试压力。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, some students perform better under pressure than others.**：以"For example"开头，是举例型干扰。虽与考试压力相关，但只是关于"部分人在压力下表现更好"的个体差异，无法充当某段的主题概括句。若放入[1]或[5]处，与段落主旨（应对方法、总结建议）不符；本段需概括性或建议性句子，而非举例说明个体差异。
