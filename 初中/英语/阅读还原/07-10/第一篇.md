# 07-10 第一篇

**The Importance of Urban Greening**

Cities are growing rapidly around the world. Tall buildings, busy streets, and concrete surfaces have replaced many natural landscapes. [1] Yet bringing nature back into cities is not just about beauty. Urban green spaces—parks, trees, gardens, and green roofs—provide essential benefits for both people and the environment. They improve air quality, reduce summer heat, and create places where communities can gather and relax.

Green spaces help combat air pollution in crowded cities. [2] Trees and plants absorb harmful gases and release oxygen. They also trap dust and small particles that can damage our lungs. Studies show that neighborhoods with more trees have better air quality and fewer respiratory problems. Planting trees along streets and in parks is one of the most cost-effective ways to make cities healthier for everyone.

Urban heat is a growing problem as cities expand. [3] Concrete and asphalt absorb heat during the day and release it at night, making cities several degrees warmer than surrounding areas. Trees provide shade and cool the air through evaporation. Green roofs on buildings reduce heat absorption and lower energy use for air conditioning. A well-greened city can feel noticeably cooler and more comfortable in summer.

Green spaces also support mental health and social connection. [4] People who live near parks report lower stress levels and better overall well-being. Parks offer places for exercise, play, and casual meetings. They bring neighbors together and help build a sense of community. In dense cities, access to nature becomes a vital resource for quality of life.

[5] Urban greening is an investment that pays off in many ways. It makes cities cleaner, cooler, and more livable. As we plan for the future, creating more green spaces should be a priority in every city. The benefits reach every resident—from cleaner air to happier neighborhoods.

## 选项

A. The value of urban greenery, however, goes far beyond aesthetics.
B. Plants act as natural air filters, cleaning the air we breathe.
C. Greenery can significantly reduce the "heat island" effect in cities.
D. Access to green spaces improves both physical and mental well-being.
E. In summary, urban greening brings multiple benefits to city life.
F. For example, Central Park in New York attracts millions of visitors each year.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述城市扩张、建筑和水泥取代自然，空后列举城市绿地的具体作用：改善空气质量、降低夏季高温、提供聚集放松空间。A选项"The value of urban greenery, however, goes far beyond aesthetics"以"however"转折，承接前文对自然的取代，强调绿地价值不止于美观，与后文的功能性益处紧密衔接。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：第二段主题为绿地对空气污染的作用，空后具体说明树木吸收有害气体、释放氧气、吸附颗粒物，并引用研究。B选项"Plants act as natural air filters, cleaning the air we breathe"精准概括：植物充当天然空气过滤器。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：第三段讨论城市热岛问题，空后说明水泥沥青吸热、树木遮阴与蒸发降温、绿色屋顶减少吸热和空调能耗。C选项"Greenery can significantly reduce the 'heat island' effect in cities"准确概括本段：绿化能显著缓解城市热岛效应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：第四段讲绿地对心理健康和社交的作用，空后提到靠近公园的人压力更低、公园用于运动玩耍社交、增强社区感。D选项"Access to green spaces improves both physical and mental well-being"概括本段：接触绿地改善身心福祉。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：最后一段需总结全文，空前已分述各段，空后归纳"cleaner, cooler, more livable""benefits reach every resident"。E选项"In summary, urban greening brings multiple benefits to city life"用"In summary"收束，与后文的多重益处呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, Central Park in New York attracts millions of visitors each year.**：以"For example"开头，是举例型干扰。虽与城市公园相关，但仅是单个公园的参观数据，无法作为某段的主题概括句。若放入[4]处，本段重点是"心理健康与社交"，中央公园游客量是现象描述，非概括性论点，且论述层次错位。
