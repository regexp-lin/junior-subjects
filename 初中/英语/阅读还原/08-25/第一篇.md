# 08-25 第一篇

**Smartphones and Teenagers: A Double-Edged Sword**

Smartphones have become a central part of teenage life. Young people use them to communicate, learn, entertain themselves, and stay connected with friends. These devices offer powerful tools: instant information, educational apps, and creative platforms. At the same time, many parents and teachers worry about the negative effects of excessive screen time. [1] Smartphones bring both opportunities and risks, and the key is learning to use them wisely. Understanding this balance is essential for today's teens and their families.

One major concern is the impact on mental health. Studies show that teenagers who spend many hours on social media often report higher levels of anxiety, loneliness, and low self-esteem. Comparing themselves to others' carefully edited posts can make them feel inadequate. [2] Constant scrolling and notifications can disrupt sleep patterns and reduce face-to-face social interaction. The blue light from screens affects the brain's production of melatonin, making it harder to fall asleep. These factors together can harm both physical and emotional well-being.

On the positive side, smartphones can support learning. Students can access tutorials, language apps, and online courses anywhere. They can collaborate on projects, share notes, and reach teachers for help outside school hours. [3] Used as tools for education, phones can expand knowledge and improve academic performance. The difference lies in whether teens use their devices intentionally or let them control their attention.

Parents often struggle with how to set limits. Complete bans may lead to rebellion or leave teenagers unprepared for a digital world. [4] Clear rules—such as no phones at meals or in the bedroom—combined with open conversation, tend to work better. Discussing why limits matter and involving teens in creating family guidelines can reduce conflict. The goal is to build healthy habits, not to create a battleground.

[5] Smartphones are here to stay, so the focus should be on teaching responsible use rather than avoiding technology entirely. Schools can include digital literacy in the curriculum. Families can model balanced screen habits. When teenagers learn to manage their phone use, they gain skills that will serve them for life. The device itself is neutral; it is how we use it that defines its impact.

A. Achieving a balanced relationship with phones is essential for teenagers and their families.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述智能手机带来便利与工具，同时家长和老师担心过度使用；空后强调“理解这种平衡对当今青少年及其家庭至关重要”。A项“智能手机带来机会与风险，关键在于学会明智使用”承上概括双面性，启下引出平衡的重要性，起承上启下作用。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前讲社交媒体使用与焦虑、孤独、自卑相关，以及与他人精修帖子的比较；空后写持续刷屏和通知影响睡眠、减少面对面社交，蓝光影响褪黑素。B项“过度使用会损害睡眠、心理健康和现实社交”概括本段多重负面影响，逻辑一致。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前举例智能手机支持学习：教程、语言 app、在线课程、协作、联系老师等；空后强调“差别在于青少年是有意使用还是被设备控制注意力”。C项“作为教育工具使用时，手机能扩展知识、提升学业表现”概括正面用途及其条件。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前指出完全禁止可能引发反抗或让青少年缺乏数字素养；空后建议明确规则（如用餐和卧室禁手机）配合坦诚交流，并让青少年参与制定家庭规则。D项“设定合理边界并坦诚沟通，比严格管控更能带来好结果”概括本段建议及效果。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前为第四段；空后写手机将长期存在、应注重教授负责任使用、学校可纳入数字素养、家庭可示范健康习惯。E项“最佳做法是教青少年负责任地使用技术，而非完全回避”作为结尾主题句，总结全文导向。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, many teenagers use their phones to watch short videos for several hours each day.**：以For example开头，为具体使用行为举例。虽与手机使用相关，但仅描述现象，无法承担[1]–[5]的概括或递进功能；层次低于段落主旨，易误选为[2]或[4]。
