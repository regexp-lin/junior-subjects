# 05-08 第二篇

**Space Exploration: Reaching for the Stars**

Humans have always looked at the stars and wondered what lies beyond our planet. [1] In recent decades, we have made amazing progress in space exploration. We have sent astronauts to the moon, landed robots on Mars（火星）, and sent probes（探测器） to study distant planets and stars. Satellites now orbit Earth, helping us with weather forecasts, GPS navigation, and communication. Space exploration has changed the way we live and understand our place in the universe.

Why do we explore space? Some people ask this question because space missions cost a lot of money. [2] The answer is that space exploration brings benefits that reach far beyond science. Many inventions we use every day—such as memory foam, wireless headphones, and smartphone cameras—were first developed for space programs. Medical equipment and water purification systems have also improved thanks to space research. In other words, investing in space often leads to discoveries that improve life on Earth.

Space exploration also inspires young people to study science. [3] When students see astronauts floating in space or robots sending back pictures from Mars, they become curious about how it all works. This curiosity can lead them to choose careers in science, technology, engineering, or math. Many scientists and engineers today say they first became interested in their field after watching a space launch or reading about space missions as children. That is why space programs are important for educating the next generation.

Of course, space exploration faces challenges. [4] Space travel is dangerous, and astronauts must train for years before their missions. Building rockets and spacecraft requires huge amounts of money and teamwork. There are also concerns about space debris（太空碎片）—old satellites and rocket parts that float in orbit and could damage new spacecraft. However, countries around the world continue to work together to solve these problems and push the boundaries of what is possible.

[5] In short, space exploration is one of humanity's greatest adventures. It has given us new technology, inspired generations of scientists, and shown us that we can achieve amazing things when we work together. As we look to the future, the stars may be closer than we think.

## 选项

A. In fact, our achievements in space have grown at an incredible speed.
B. That is because the technology developed for space often finds uses on Earth.
C. For this reason, space programs play a key role in inspiring future scientists.
D. It is true that there are many difficulties to overcome.
E. In conclusion, the benefits of exploring space go far beyond the stars.
F. For example, private companies are now building their own rockets.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：总领太空探索的成就。空前描述"Humans have always looked at the stars and wondered what lies beyond our planet"，空后列举"In recent decades, we have made amazing progress...sent astronauts to the moon, landed robots on Mars, sent probes...Satellites...weather forecasts, GPS, communication"。A选项"In fact, our achievements in space have grown at an incredible speed"（事实上，我们在太空领域的成就以惊人的速度增长）中"In fact"加强衔接，"achievements...grown at incredible speed"与空后"amazing progress"及一系列具体成就完美呼应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：太空探索带来的实际应用。空前设问"Why do we explore space? Some people ask...because space missions cost a lot of money"，空后列举"The answer is that...Many inventions we use every day—memory foam, wireless headphones, smartphone cameras—were first developed for space programs...Medical equipment and water purification...improved thanks to space research"。B选项"That is because the technology developed for space often finds uses on Earth"（那是因为为太空开发的技术往往能在地球上找到用途）中"That is because"回答空前关于投入的质疑，"technology...finds uses on Earth"与空后具体发明及应用精准对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：太空探索对青少年的激励作用。空前提出"Space exploration also inspires young people to study science"，空后描述"When students see astronauts floating...robots sending back pictures from Mars, they become curious...choose careers in science, technology, engineering, or math...Many scientists...say they first became interested...after watching a space launch"。C选项"For this reason, space programs play a key role in inspiring future scientists"（因此，太空计划在激励未来科学家方面起着关键作用）中"For this reason"承接空前"inspires young people"，"inspiring future scientists"与空后"choose careers in science...became interested"完美呼应，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：太空探索面临的挑战。空前指出"Of course, space exploration faces challenges"，空后列举"Space travel is dangerous...astronauts must train for years...Building rockets requires huge amounts of money...concerns about space debris...However, countries continue to work together to solve these problems"。D选项"It is true that there are many difficulties to overcome"（的确，有许多困难需要克服）中"It is true that"与空前"Of course"呼应，承认挑战，"many difficulties to overcome"总领空后危险、成本、太空碎片等具体困难，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"space exploration is one of humanity's greatest adventures...given us new technology, inspired generations of scientists...shown us that we can achieve amazing things when we work together...stars may be closer than we think"。E选项"In conclusion, the benefits of exploring space go far beyond the stars"（总之，探索太空的好处远超星空本身）用"In conclusion"总结全文，"benefits...go far beyond the stars"与空后技术、激励、合作等多重收益形成呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, private companies are now building their own rockets.**：涉及私营公司造火箭，易被误填[4]。但F以"For example"引出私营太空公司的例子，而空[4]前后文讨论的是太空探索面临的挑战（危险、成本、太空碎片），空后"However, countries around the world continue to work together"强调的是国际合作解决问题，并非私营公司参与；F属于太空产业的新趋势，与空[4]"challenges"主题不匹配。F属"主题相关但讨论维度错位"型干扰。
