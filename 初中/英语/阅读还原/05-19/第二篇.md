# 05-19 第二篇

**The Ancient Silk Road: A Bridge Between East and West**

More than 2,000 years ago, a network of trade routes connected China with Central Asia, the Middle East, and Europe. This network, known as the Silk Road, was not a single road but many paths that carried goods, ideas, and people across continents. [1] In fact, the Silk Road got its name from silk, one of China's most valuable exports at that time. But traders also carried spices（香料）, tea, porcelain（瓷器）, glass, and precious stones. Ideas about religion, science, and art travelled along these routes too. That is why the Silk Road was much more than a trade route—it was a bridge between cultures.

How did the Silk Road begin? [2] During the Han Dynasty（ dynasty 朝代）, a Chinese official named Zhang Qian was sent to find allies in the West. His journey opened the way for trade between China and other regions. Merchants（商人）from different lands met at markets along the route. Camels and horses carried goods across deserts and mountains. Rest stops, or caravanserais（商队客栈）, provided shelter for tired travellers. In other words, the Silk Road grew from exploration and the desire of people to exchange what they had for what they needed.

The journey was long and dangerous. [3] Traders faced deserts with little water, high mountain passes with snow, and sometimes attacks by bandits（土匪）. Yet the rewards could be great. Those who made it could sell their goods for high prices in distant markets. Cultural exchange also happened: Buddhism came to China from India along the Silk Road, and Chinese inventions like paper and gunpowder spread westward. For this reason, the Silk Road shaped not only trade but also the history and culture of many nations.

The Silk Road is still important today. [4] China's Belt and Road Initiative（一带一路倡议）aims to build new connections between countries, inspired by the spirit of the ancient Silk Road. Modern trains, ships, and planes have replaced camels, but the idea remains the same: trade and cooperation can bring people together and create shared prosperity. So the legacy of the Silk Road continues to influence how nations work together in the 21st century.

[5] In short, the Ancient Silk Road was one of the most important links in human history. It brought East and West closer, spread goods and ideas, and showed that different cultures can learn from each other. Today, as we face global challenges like climate change and disease, the spirit of the Silk Road—connecting, sharing, and cooperating—reminds us that we are all part of one world. Remember: the roads we build today may shape the world of tomorrow.

## 选项

A. That is because the Silk Road was named after silk but carried far more than goods.
B. In other words, the Silk Road grew from trade and the meeting of people from different lands.

D. It is true that the ideas of the Silk Road still guide international cooperation today.
E. In conclusion, the Silk Road remains a symbol of connection and exchange between peoples.
F. For example, Marco Polo travelled from Italy to China along the Silk Road.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

  - **线索**：总领丝绸之路得名与内涵。空前描述"More than 2,000 years ago, a network of trade routes connected China with Central Asia, the Middle East, and Europe...This network, known as the Silk Road, was not a single road but many paths that carried goods, ideas, and people across continents"，空后说明"In fact, the Silk Road got its name from silk, one of China's most valuable exports at that time...But traders also carried spices, tea, porcelain, glass, and precious stones...Ideas about religion, science, and art travelled along these routes too...the Silk Road was much more than a trade route—it was a bridge between cultures"。A选项"That is because the Silk Road was named after silk but carried far more than goods"（那是因为丝绸之路以丝绸命名但运载的远不止货物）中"That is because"解释丝路得名与内涵，"named after silk but carried far more than goods"与空后silk、spices/tea/porcelain、ideas精准对应，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：丝绸之路的起源与形成。空前设问"How did the Silk Road begin?"，空后详述"During the Han Dynasty, a Chinese official named Zhang Qian was sent to find allies in the West...His journey opened the way for trade between China and other regions...Merchants from different lands met at markets along the route...Camels and horses carried goods across deserts and mountains...Rest stops, or caravanserais, provided shelter...the Silk Road grew from exploration and the desire of people to exchange what they had for what they needed"。B选项"In other words, the Silk Road grew from trade and the meeting of people from different lands"（换言之，丝绸之路源于贸易和来自不同地区人们的相遇）中"In other words"概括空后内容，"grew from trade...meeting of people from different lands"与空后Zhang Qian、merchants、exchange完美呼应，衔接自然。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：丝绸之路对商业与文化的双重影响。空前提出"The journey was long and dangerous"，空后详述"Traders faced deserts...high mountain passes...sometimes attacks by bandits...Yet the rewards could be great...sell their goods for high prices in distant markets...Cultural exchange also happened: Buddhism came to China from India...Chinese inventions like paper and gunpowder spread westward...the Silk Road shaped not only trade but also the history and culture of many nations"。C选项"That is why the Silk Road changed both commerce and culture across many regions"（因此丝绸之路改变了多个地区的商业和文化）中"That is why"承接空前及空后结论，"changed both commerce and culture"与空后trade、cultural exchange、Buddhism、paper and gunpowder精准对应，逻辑紧密。
