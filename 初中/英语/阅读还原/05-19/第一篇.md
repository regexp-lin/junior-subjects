# 05-19 第一篇

**First Aid: Simple Skills That Can Save Lives**

Accidents can happen anywhere—at home, at school, or on the street. When someone gets hurt, the first few minutes are often the most important. Knowing basic first aid can help you stay calm and do the right thing until professional help arrives. [1] In fact, first aid is the immediate care we give to an injured or sick person before a doctor or ambulance arrives. It does not replace medical treatment, but it can prevent a situation from getting worse and may even save a life. That is why learning first aid should be part of everyone's education.

What are the most important first aid skills? [2] One of the key skills is CPR—cardiopulmonary resuscitation（心肺复苏）—which can help restart a person's heart and breathing when they have stopped. Another essential skill is stopping serious bleeding by pressing firmly on the wound with a clean cloth. For burns, we should cool the area with running water for several minutes. For choking, we can use the Heimlich manoeuvre（海姆立克急救法）to help push the object out. In other words, knowing a few basic steps can make a big difference in an emergency.

Staying calm is also crucial. [3] When we panic, we may forget what to do or make wrong decisions. Take a deep breath, check if the scene is safe, and then call for help if needed. In China, the emergency number is 120. Tell the operator clearly where you are and what has happened. While waiting, follow the first aid steps you have learned. For this reason, practising first aid in classes or workshops helps us feel more confident when a real emergency happens.

Never do more than you are trained to do. [4] First aid is about keeping the person stable, not trying to "fix" them. Do not move someone who may have a broken bone or neck injury, as moving them could cause more harm. Do not give food or water to someone who is unconscious. If you are not sure what to do, it is better to stay with the person, keep them calm, and wait for professional help. So knowing our limits is as important as knowing what we can do.

[5] In short, first aid is a life skill that everyone can learn. You do not need to be a doctor to help in an emergency. By taking a first aid course and practising the steps, you can be ready to act when someone needs you. Remember: in those first few minutes, you might be the only one who can help. The skills you learn today could save a life tomorrow.
