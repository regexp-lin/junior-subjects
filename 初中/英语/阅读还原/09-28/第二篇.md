**Sustainable Fishing and Our Oceans**

Fish is an important source of food for billions of people around the world. However, decades of overfishing have put many fish populations in serious danger. When too many fish are taken from the ocean too quickly, populations cannot recover, and entire marine ecosystems suffer. [1] Finding a balance between meeting human needs and protecting ocean life is one of the biggest challenges of our time.

Overfishing happens when fishing boats catch more fish than the population can replace through natural reproduction. Modern fishing technology—huge nets, sonar, and factory ships—has made it possible to catch enormous quantities of fish. [2] While this efficiency has increased food supply, it has also pushed many species to the brink of collapse.

The effects of overfishing go beyond just fewer fish. When one species is removed from the food chain, it affects all the creatures that depend on it. [3] This ripple effect can destabilize entire ecosystems, leading to the loss of other marine species and the degradation of ocean habitats.

Sustainable fishing practices offer a way forward. Setting catch limits, creating marine protected areas, and using selective fishing gear that avoids catching unwanted species all help. [4] When fisheries are managed responsibly, fish populations can recover and continue to provide food for generations to come.

Consumers also have a role to play. Choosing seafood from sustainable sources, looking for eco-labels, and reducing fish waste are simple actions that support responsible fishing. [5] By making informed choices and supporting sustainable practices, we can enjoy the benefits of the ocean while ensuring its health and abundance for the future.

## 选项

A. The future of our oceans depends on our ability to harvest their resources wisely and responsibly.
B. However, this technological advantage has come at a great cost to the health of marine ecosystems.
C. The interconnected nature of ocean life means that overfishing one species can have far-reaching consequences.
D. Science-based management of fisheries has proven effective in restoring depleted fish stocks in many regions.
E. Individual choices, when multiplied by millions of consumers, can drive meaningful change in the fishing industry.
F. For example, the population of Atlantic bluefin tuna dropped by over 80 percent before strict quotas were introduced.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前说过度捕捞使鱼类种群和海洋生态系统受损，空后说找到人类需求与海洋保护的平衡是最大挑战。A的"future of our oceans depends on our ability to harvest wisely"概括核心矛盾，与"balance"呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说现代捕鱼技术可大量捕获鱼类，空后说虽然增加了食物供应但推动物种濒临崩溃。B的"this technological advantage has come at a great cost"承接技术效率，"cost to marine ecosystems"引出负面后果。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说移除一个物种会影响整个食物链，空后说这种连锁反应可破坏整个生态系统。C的"interconnected nature of ocean life"解释生态关联，"far-reaching consequences"概括影响的广泛性。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前列举可持续捕捞措施（限额、保护区、选择性渔具），空后说负责任管理可使鱼类恢复。D的"Science-based management"承接前文措施，"proven effective in restoring depleted fish stocks"提供成功证据。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前列举消费者可采取的行动（选择可持续来源、看标签、减少浪费），空后说做出知情选择可享受海洋益处。E的"Individual choices, when multiplied by millions"承接消费者行动，"drive meaningful change"概括集体效果。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the population of Atlantic bluefin tuna dropped by over 80 percent before strict quotas were introduced.**：以For example开头，属具体物种数据；易误放[2]或[4]，但两空分别需技术代价的概括和管理成效的概括，非个案数据。
