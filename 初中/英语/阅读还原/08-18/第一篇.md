# 08-18 第一篇

**The Development of Robot Technology**

Robots have come a long way from the simple machines in old science fiction movies. Today, they work in factories, hospitals, and even our homes. [1] As technology improves, robots are becoming smarter, safer, and more helpful in our daily lives. Understanding this progress helps us prepare for the future.

In factories, robots have changed how we make things. They can assemble cars, pack goods, and perform tasks that are repetitive or dangerous for humans. [2] This has led to faster production and fewer workplace injuries. Many factories now use robots working alongside human workers, each doing what they do best.

Robots are also making a difference in healthcare. Surgical robots help doctors perform operations with extreme precision. They can make smaller cuts, which means patients heal faster and face less risk of infection. [3] Some robots even assist elderly people with daily tasks like reminding them to take medicine.

At home, robot vacuums and smart assistants have become common. [4] These devices learn our habits and adjust their behavior to fit our needs. As artificial intelligence improves, home robots will likely do even more to simplify our routines. The line between helper and companion is slowly blurring.

The future of robots is both exciting and uncertain. [5] We must think about how to use robots wisely, train people for new kinds of jobs, and make sure technology benefits everyone. Schools are already teaching students about robots and coding so they can be part of this changing world.

## 选项

A. This growth raises important questions about how humans and machines will work together.
B. Robots never get tired, so they can work around the clock without breaks.
C. Recovery time is often shortened, and patients can return to normal life sooner.
D. This partnership between humans and machines helps us get more done at home.
E. Society needs to plan for these changes and ensure no one is left behind.
F. For example, robot pets can provide comfort to people who feel lonely.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍机器人在工厂、医院、家庭的应用，空后说"随着技术发展机器人更智能、更安全、更有用"。选项A"这一发展引发了关于人类与机器如何协作的重要问题"承接机器人应用扩展的事实，自然引出后文对人类与机器关系的思考，为全文奠定讨论框架。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前列举机器人可完成重复、危险任务，空后提到"更快生产、更少工伤"。选项B"机器人不知疲倦，能全天候连续工作"解释了何以实现"更快生产"——效率提升的直接原因，与"人类做危险工作易受伤"形成对比，支撑"更少工伤"的逻辑。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前描述手术机器人可做更小切口、加快康复、降低感染风险，空后提到辅助老年人的机器人。选项C"康复时间 often shortened，患者能更快回归正常生活"是对前文"patients heal faster"的进一步概括与延伸，与"face less risk of infection"形成完整的术后恢复益处表述，自然过渡到"其他医疗辅助机器人"。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍家用机器人如扫地机器人和智能助手已普及，空后说"这些设备学习我们的习惯并调整行为"。选项D"这种人与机器的协作帮助我们在家中完成更多事务"承接"家用机器人普及"的事实，概括人机协作对家庭效率的提升，与后文"学习习惯、适应需求"形成逻辑链。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前总结机器人未来既令人兴奋又充满不确定性，空后列举需思考的问题及学校教育。选项E"社会需要为这些变化做好规划，确保没有人被落下"与前文"important questions"呼应，与后文"培训新工种、技术惠及所有人"及"学校教授编程"形成递进，完美收尾。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, robot pets can provide comfort to people who feel lonely**：以For example开头的典型干扰项。与第四段家用机器人、辅助老年人相关，容易被误选为[4]。但F聚焦"情感陪伴"这一细分场景，而[4]处需要的是对家用机器人整体价值的概括；且F的"robot pets"与"扫地机器人、智能助手"的论述层次不同，属于举例型干扰。
