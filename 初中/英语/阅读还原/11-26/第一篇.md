**Coffee Culture: More Than Just a Drink**

Coffee is one of the most popular drinks in the world. Millions of people start their day with a cup of coffee to feel more awake and focused. But coffee is more than a simple drink—it has become part of daily life, social gatherings, and even art. Coffee shops are places where people meet friends, work on laptops, or simply take a break. In many cultures, offering someone a cup of coffee is a sign of welcome and friendship. [1] Coffee has grown from a simple beverage into a cultural phenomenon that connects people, shapes daily routines, and creates spaces for work and social life around the world.

The history of coffee goes back hundreds of years. It is said to have first been used in Ethiopia, then spread through the Middle East, Europe, and eventually the rest of the world. Today, coffee is grown in tropical regions such as Brazil, Colombia, Vietnam, and parts of Africa. The way people drink coffee varies widely—espresso in Italy, filter coffee in the United States, tea-like coffee in Turkey, and sweet iced coffee in many Asian countries. [2] Coffee's journey from its origins to today shows how a single plant has traveled across continents and cultures, adapting to local tastes and traditions wherever it went.

Making good coffee involves both science and skill. The quality of the beans, the roasting process, the grind size, and the brewing method all affect the final taste. Baristas train for years to master techniques such as pulling the perfect espresso shot or creating latte art. At home, people use everything from simple drip machines to fancy pour-over setups. [3] Whether in a café or at home, making coffee well requires attention to detail and a willingness to learn, turning a daily habit into a craft that many people enjoy and take pride in.

The coffee industry also has a darker side. Many coffee farmers in developing countries work long hours for low pay. Climate change threatens coffee-growing regions with drought and disease. Some companies are working to promote fair trade—paying farmers a fair price—and sustainable farming. As consumers, we can choose brands that support these efforts. [4] Being aware of where our coffee comes from and how it is produced can help us make choices that support farmers and the environment, rather than only focusing on taste and convenience.

Coffee will likely remain a central part of global culture. New brewing methods, single-origin beans, and specialty shops continue to attract coffee lovers. At the same time, the conversation about fairness and sustainability in the coffee supply chain is growing. [5] As we enjoy our daily cup, we can appreciate both the rich tradition behind coffee and the role we play in shaping its future—through the choices we make and the values we support.

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍咖啡——全球最流行饮品之一，数百万人以咖啡提神开启一天，但咖啡不止是饮料，已成为日常生活、社交甚至艺术的一部分，咖啡馆是相聚、办公、休息之所，在许多文化中请人喝咖啡是欢迎与友谊的表示，空后需概括咖啡从简单饮品演变为连接人、塑造日常、创造工作与社交空间的文化现象。A的"grown from a simple beverage into a cultural phenomenon"与前文"more than a simple drink"呼应，"connects people, shapes daily routines, and creates spaces"与前文各项社会功能对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说咖啡历史——可追溯数百年，相传起源于埃塞俄比亚，经中东、欧洲传遍世界，今在巴西、哥伦比亚、越南、非洲等地种植，饮用方式各异——意大利浓缩、美国过滤、土耳其茶式、亚洲甜冰咖等，空后需总结咖啡从起源至今跨越大陆与文化的传播与在地适应。B的"Coffee's journey from its origins to today"与前文"history"和"spread"呼应，"traveled across continents and cultures, adapting to local tastes"与各地饮法对应。

- **[3] C**：
