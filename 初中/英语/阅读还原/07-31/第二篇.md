# 07-31 第二篇

**The Connection Between Reading and Writing**

Reading and writing are two sides of the same coin. Good writers are almost always avid readers. [1] The more you read, the better you understand how language works. Stories, articles, and essays teach you structure, style, and the power of words. Reading feeds your imagination and gives you ideas to write about.

Reading exposes you to different voices and styles. [2] Each author has a unique way of telling a story or making an argument. Notice how they open a paragraph, build suspense, or conclude a section. You can borrow techniques without copying—this is how writers learn. Analyzing what you read improves your own writing.

Writing, in turn, deepens your reading. [3] When you write, you pay closer attention to how sentences are built. You notice word choice, punctuation, and paragraph organization. This awareness makes you a more critical reader. You start to see the craft behind the words, not just the message.

Practice both skills regularly. [4] Set aside time each day for reading—even 20 minutes helps. Write something every day, even if it is just a journal entry or a short paragraph. Consistency matters more than length. The two skills reinforce each other over time.

Teachers and parents can support this connection. [5] Encourage children to discuss what they read and write. Ask them why they liked a certain book or how they chose their words. Conversations about reading and writing build confidence and critical thinking. These habits last a lifetime.

## 选项

A. Reading builds the foundation that makes strong writing possible.
B. Studying how others write helps you develop your own voice.
C. The act of writing sharpens your ability to understand what you read.
D. Making both activities part of your routine leads to steady improvement.
E. Guiding young people to think and talk about both skills strengthens them.
F. For example, many famous authors kept detailed journals throughout their lives.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前强调阅读与写作相辅相成，好作家多是好读者；空后说明阅读越多越懂语言、各类文本教会结构风格与用词。A 项“阅读打下使写作变强的基础”承接阅读对写作的支撑作用，与后文自然衔接。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前提出阅读让你接触不同声音和风格；空后建议留意作者如何开头、制造悬念、收尾，并说明借鉴技巧而非抄袭。B 项“研究他人如何写作有助于形成自己的声音”概括该段主旨，与后文分析式学习对应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前提出写作反过来加深阅读；空后具体说明写作时更关注句式、用词、标点、段落，使阅读更批判。C 项“写作这一行为提升了你理解所读内容的能力”概括写作对阅读的反馈作用，与后文呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前建议定期练习两种能力；空后给出每日阅读 20 分钟、每日写作（日记或段落）等具体方法。D 项“把两种活动纳入日常会带来稳步进步”概括该段主旨，与后文建议对应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前提出老师和家长可支持这种联系；空后建议鼓励孩子讨论所读所写、提问其偏好与选词理由。E 项“引导青少年思考并讨论这两种能力能使其更强”总结该段主旨，与后文呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, many famous authors kept detailed journals...**：虽与写作习惯相关，但仅为个别例子，未概括阅读与写作的互促关系，也未对应任何空位的承上启下逻辑，易被误选。
