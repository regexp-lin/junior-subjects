**The Dangers of E-Waste**

Electronic devices have become part of our daily lives. Phones, computers, and TVs are constantly being replaced with newer models. [1] The old devices we throw away create a growing environmental problem. This "e-waste" is one of the fastest-growing waste streams in the world.

E-waste contains harmful materials. [2] Lead, mercury, and cadmium are common in old electronics. When devices are dumped in landfills or burned, these toxins can leak into the soil and water. Workers who dismantle e-waste without proper protection face serious health risks. The pollution affects both people and wildlife.

Recycling e-waste is not always done safely. [3] Some countries ship their e-waste to poorer nations. There, it is often processed by hand in informal workshops. Children and adults breathe toxic fumes and touch dangerous chemicals. Proper recycling requires special facilities and trained workers.

Consumers can make a difference. [4] Before buying a new device, ask if you really need it. Repair or upgrade your old ones when possible. When you must dispose of electronics, find a certified e-waste recycler. Many stores and manufacturers offer take-back programs.

Solving the e-waste problem needs global effort. [5] Governments must pass laws to ensure safe disposal and recycling. Companies should design products that last longer and are easier to repair. As consumers, we can choose brands that care about the environment. Together, we can reduce the harm caused by e-waste.

## 选项

A. Discarded electronics are piling up and damaging our planet.
B. These chemicals pose serious threats to human health and the environment.
C. In many places, e-waste handling lacks safety standards and protections.
D. Simple choices can reduce the amount of e-waste we produce.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍电子设备更新频繁、旧设备被淘汰；空后说明被丢弃设备造成的环境问题及 e-waste 增速。A 项“被丢弃的电子设备正在堆积并危害地球”承接“丢弃旧设备”并点明问题，与后文 e-waste 论述衔接。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前提出 e-waste 含有有害物质；空后列举铅、汞、镉及进入土壤水源、危害拆解工人和野生动物的后果。B 项“这些化学物质对人类健康和环境构成严重威胁”概括危害，与后文具体描述对应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前指出 e-waste 回收并非总是安全；空后描述转运至贫困国家、非正规作坊手工处理、接触毒烟毒物等。C 项“许多地方的 e-waste 处理缺乏安全标准与保护”概括该段主旨，与后文形成总分关系。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前提出消费者可以发挥作用；空后给出具体建议（理性购买、维修升级、选择认证回收、参与回收计划）。D 项“简单的选择就能减少我们产生的 e-waste”概括消费者行为的影响，与后文呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说解决 e-waste 需全球努力；空后分别说明政府立法、企业设计、消费者选择。E 项“改变需要政府、企业和个人共同行动”总结多方责任，与后文并列结构对应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, a smartphone contains over 60 different elements**：与电子设备成分相关，但未涉及危害、回收或解决方案，仅为信息性举例，与各空位逻辑层次不符。
