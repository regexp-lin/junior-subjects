# 08-14 第一篇

**Protecting World Heritage**

Around the world, there are places of extraordinary value—ancient temples, natural wonders, and historic cities. These sites tell the story of human civilization and the beauty of our planet. In 1972, UNESCO created a list to recognize and protect such places. [1] The World Heritage List now includes over 1,100 sites that are considered important to all humanity and worthy of special care for future generations.

World Heritage sites face many threats. Climate change is causing rising seas that could flood coastal sites. War and conflict have destroyed ancient treasures. [2] Too many tourists can also damage fragile buildings and ecosystems. Some sites have had to limit visitor numbers or close certain areas to prevent further harm. Balancing the desire to share these treasures with the need to protect them is an ongoing challenge.

Countries that have World Heritage sites must work to protect them. They create management plans and pass laws to guard against development that could harm the sites. [3] International cooperation plays a crucial role when sites cross borders or when expertise is needed from abroad. UNESCO provides guidance and can offer emergency assistance when sites are in danger. Sometimes sites are placed on a "danger list" to draw attention to their plight.

Young people can contribute to heritage protection in many ways. Learning about World Heritage sites helps us understand their value. [4] We can support conservation by visiting responsibly, following rules, and spreading awareness. Avoiding littering, staying on marked paths, and respecting local customs all make a difference. Every small action helps preserve these treasures for others to enjoy.

World Heritage belongs to all of us. [5] By caring for these sites today, we ensure that they remain for our children and grandchildren to experience. Protection requires the efforts of governments, organizations, and ordinary citizens alike. Together, we can safeguard the cultural and natural wonders that make our world unique.

## 选项

A. This global effort has grown into a powerful tool for conservation.
B. Local governments carry the main responsibility for their upkeep.
C. Overuse by visitors creates another serious problem for these fragile places.
D. Everyone has a part to play in keeping these places safe.
E. The goal is to pass these treasures on to future generations.
F. For example, the Great Wall of China attracts millions of visitors each year.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] C   [3] B   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍UNESCO 1972年创立名录，空后"The World Heritage List now includes over 1,100 sites..."。A项"This global effort has grown into a powerful tool for conservation"承上启下，既总结前文全球举措，又为后文名录规模与意义做铺垫。

- **[2] C**：
  - **线索**：第二段讲世界遗产面临的威胁，空前提到climate change和war，空后"Too many tourists can also damage...limit visitor numbers"。C项"Overuse by visitors creates another serious problem for these fragile places"引出游客过度问题，与后文"limit visitor numbers"完美衔接。

- **[3] B**：
  - **线索**：第三段讲保护责任，空前"Countries...must work to protect them...management plans and pass laws"，空后"International cooperation plays a crucial role...UNESCO provides guidance"。B项"Local governments carry the main responsibility for their upkeep"强调本国政府主责，与后文"国际合作、UNESCO支援"形成主次补充关系。注意：B中"Local"在语境下可理解为"所在国"，与"International"形成对照。

  （若B与后文"International cooperation"有张力，可考虑：空前"create management plans and pass laws"强调国家行动，空后"International cooperation...when sites cross borders"，B强调本国职责，后文讲跨境时的国际合作，逻辑成立。）

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：第四段"We can contribute...Learning about...helps"，空前讲年轻人可参与，空后"We can support conservation by visiting responsibly...spreading awareness"。D项"Everyone has a part to play in keeping these places safe"概括人人有责，与后文具体行动列举呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：结尾段"World Heritage belongs to all of us"，空前强调共有，空后"By caring for these sites today, we ensure that they remain for our children and grandchildren"。E项"The goal is to pass these treasures on to future generations"点明保护目标，与后文"for our children"呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the Great Wall of China attracts millions of visitors each year**：F为具体遗产举例，与"威胁—保护—责任"的论证层次不符。若放[2]会打断威胁类型的列举；放[4]与个人行动建议无关；易造成误选。
