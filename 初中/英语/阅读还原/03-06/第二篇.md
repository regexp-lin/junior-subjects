# 03-06 第二篇

**Understanding Body Language Across Cultures**

When we think about communication, we usually think about words. [1] Surprisingly, researchers believe that more than half of our message is delivered through body language. Therefore, understanding these silent signals is just as important as learning a foreign language.

However, body language is not exactly the same everywhere. [2] A gesture that is friendly in one country might be deeply offensive in another. Take the "thumbs up" sign for example. In many Western countries, it means "good job" or "everything is fine." [3] But in some parts of the Middle East, it is actually seen as a terrible insult.

Eye contact is another interesting example of cultural differences. In America and Europe, looking people in the eye shows honesty and confidence. [4] On the contrary, in some Asian countries, looking directly into an older person's eyes is sometimes considered impolite. People there may lower their eyes to show respect.

To avoid misunderstandings when travelling abroad, it is always a good idea to do some research first. [5] By being aware of cultural differences, you can communicate more smoothly and make friends anywhere in the world.

## 选项

A. Instead, it varies greatly from culture to culture.
B. However, human beings also communicate without saying anything at all.
C. This simple hand movement is widely used to show agreement.
D. Therefore, people around the world share exactly the same non-verbal signals.
E. For instance, when talking to a boss, you are expected to look at them directly.
F. Learning about the local customs can save you from an embarrassing situation.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] B   [2] A   [3] C   [4] E   [5] F

## 深度解析

- **[1] B**：
  - **线索**：逻辑转折与语境呼应。空前指出“When we think about communication, we usually think about words”（想到交流，我们通常会想到言语文字）。B选项由“However”（然而）引出转折，“human beings also communicate without saying anything at all”（人类在什么都不说的情况下也能交流），将话题从“有声语言”过渡到了“无声的动作”，并完美引出了后一句“more than half of our message is delivered through body language”（一半以上的信息是通过肢体语言传达的）。
- **[2] A**：
  - **线索**：同义解释与逻辑递进。空前句说“body language is not exactly the same everywhere”（肢体语言并非到处都完全相同）。A选项“Instead, it varies greatly from culture to culture”（相反，它在不同文化之间差异很大）进一步解释了为何不同。前句的“not the same”与后句的“varies greatly”形成语义的承接，并且为后来要举的文化差异例子（如竖大拇指）作了铺垫。
- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：代词指代与词汇复现。空前举例提到了西方国家的“thumbs up”（竖大拇指），并解释了它的意思。C选项中的主语“This simple hand movement”（这个简单的手部动作）完美指代了前面的“thumbs up sign”。同时也作为转折连词前的一个正面陈述，与空后的转折句“But in some parts of the Middle East, it is actually seen as a terrible insult”（但在中东部分地区，这却被视为一种极大的侮辱）形成强烈的对比。
- **[4] E**：
  - **线索**：举例说明与逻辑关系。空前介绍了欧美关于眼神交流的习惯：“looking people in the eye shows honesty and confidence”（看着别人的眼睛展现出诚实和自信）。E选项“For instance, when talking to a boss, you are expected to look at them directly”（例如，和老板说话时，你被期望直视他们）正是对欧美这一文化习惯的具体举例支撑。随后紧跟的句首连词“On the contrary”（相反）恰到好处地将视线转向了亚洲国家的截然相反的做法。
- **[5] F**：
  - **线索**：词汇同现与结论总结。这段的主旨是避免出国时的误解。空前建议“do some research first”（先做一些研究）。F选项的“Learning about the local customs”（了解当地风俗）正是“do some research”的具体内容，且“save you from an embarrassing situation”（帮助你免于尴尬的处境）照应了前句的“avoid misunderstandings（避免误解）”，前后逻辑连贯，也为全文结尾“能让你更顺畅交流、交各种朋友”作好升华铺垫。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **D. Therefore, people around the world share exactly the same non-verbal signals.**：虽然这一项包含“Therefore”这个看似有总结性质的词，以及“non-verbal signals”（非语言信号，即肢体语言的同义词），但它的句意是“因此，全世界的人拥有完全相同的非语言信号”。这与文本第二段的中心思想“body language is not exactly the same everywhere”（肢体语言并非哪里都一样）完完全全地背道而驰，从逻辑上可以立刻排除。这就是利用核心词汇但不符合全篇核心基调的典型干扰项。
