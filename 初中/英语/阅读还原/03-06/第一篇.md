# 03-06 第一篇

**The Importance of Good Sleep for Teenagers**

Sleep is as important to our health as eating right and exercising. [1] However, studies show that many teenagers are not getting enough sleep. In fact, most teenagers need 8 to 10 hours of sleep every night to function at their best.

Lack of sleep can strongly affect a teenager's life in several ways. For one thing, it makes it hard to pay attention in class. [2] As a result, their grades will likely drop over time. Besides, tired teenagers often experience more mood swings. [3] This happens because their brains lack the rest needed to control emotions properly. 

So, how can teenagers improve their sleep quality? One effective method is to keep a regular sleep schedule. [4] Doing this helps set the body's internal clock, making it easier to fall asleep and wake up naturally. 

Another good habit is to turn off screens at least an hour before bed. [5] The blue light from phones and computers tricks the brain into thinking it is still daytime. Instead of looking at a screen, teenagers can try reading a book or listening to soft music to relax before sleeping.

## 选项

A. Going to bed and waking up at the same time every day is highly recommended.
B. They might become easily angry or upset over very small things.
C. This means you must put your smart phone away and turn off the TV.
D. It keeps your body strong, your mind sharp, and your energy levels high.
E. Sleepy students often find it difficult to understand and remember new information.
F. Therefore, looking at your screen in bed can help you fall asleep faster.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] D   [2] E   [3] B   [4] A   [5] C

## 深度解析

- **[1] D**：
  - **线索**：代词指代与语义递进。空前指出“Sleep is as important to our health as eating right and exercising”（睡眠和饮食、运动一样对健康重要）。D选项中的“It keeps your body strong...”（它使你的身体保持强壮……）中的“It”完美指代“Sleep”，并且具体说明了睡眠对健康的重要作用，与前文衔接紧密。
- **[2] E**：
  - **线索**：同义替换与因果逻辑。空前提到了“it makes it hard to pay attention in class”（它使得在课堂上很难集中注意力）。E选项中的“find it difficult to understand and remember new information”（发现很难理解和记住新信息）是对“难集中注意力”的具体表现描述，并且自然地引出了后文的必然结果“As a result, their grades will likely drop”（结果，他们的成绩可能会下降）。
- **[3] B**：
  - **线索**：词汇复现与代词指代。空前指出“tired teenagers often experience more mood swings”（疲惫的青少年经常经历更多情绪波动）。B选项中的“become easily angry or upset”（很容易生气或心烦意乱）正是对“mood swings（情绪波动）”的具体解释。代词“They”完美指代前一句复数名词“tired teenagers”。紧随其后的“This happens because”（这发生的原因是）也是对B选项所描述现象的解释。
- **[4] A**：
  - **线索**：词义匹配与代词指代。空前建议“keep a regular sleep schedule”（保持规律的睡眠作息）。A选项“Going to bed and waking up at the same time every day”（每天同一时间睡觉和起床）是对“规律作息”的具体做法说明。同时，A选项的内容完美对应了空后一句的“Doing this helps set the body's internal clock”（这样做有助于设定身体的生物钟），“Doing this”指代A中的行为。
- **[5] C**：
  - **线索**：同义转换与语境衔接。空前的建议是“turn off screens at least an hour before bed”（睡前一小时关闭屏幕）。C选项“This means you must put your smart phone away and turn off the TV”（这意味着你必须收起智能手机并关掉电视）具体说明了“关闭屏幕”包含了哪些实际行动。空后紧接着解释了手机和电脑屏幕带来危害（蓝光欺骗大脑），整段逻辑环环相扣。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. Therefore, looking at your screen in bed can help you fall asleep faster.**：该选项包含文章中出现的话题词汇“screen”和“fall asleep”，并在结构上使用了逻辑词“Therefore”，看起来像是要给出建议。然而，其语义（在床上看屏幕能帮你更快入睡）与文章主旨（睡前关掉屏幕）及其前后句的科学事实（蓝光会欺骗大脑让人睡不着）完全相悖，是一个典型的“貌合神离”的干扰项。
