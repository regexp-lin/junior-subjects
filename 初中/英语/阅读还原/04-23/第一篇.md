# 04-23 第一篇

**Garbage Sorting: A Small Act with Big Impact**

Every day, millions of tons of waste are produced around the world. If not handled properly, this waste can pollute our air, water, and soil. [1] In many countries, garbage sorting has become a daily habit for families, schools, and offices. People separate their trash into different bins—recyclables（可回收物）, kitchen waste, and harmful waste—before throwing it away. This simple change at home can lead to a cleaner environment for everyone.

Why does garbage sorting matter so much? When we mix all kinds of waste together, it becomes difficult and expensive to process. [2] Recyclable materials like paper, plastic, and glass can be made into new products instead of ending up in landfills（垃圾填埋场）. Kitchen waste can be turned into compost（堆肥）or energy. By sorting first, we help recycling plants work more efficiently and reduce the amount of garbage that must be buried or burned.

Some people find sorting troublesome at first. They may wonder which bin a used milk box or old battery should go into. [3] Fortunately, many cities now provide clear guides, colorful posters, and even apps to help citizens learn the rules. Schools also play a role—students often take what they learn in class home and remind their parents to sort correctly. Over time, sorting becomes as natural as brushing teeth.

Of course, challenges remain. Not every community has enough recycling bins, and some people still throw everything in one bag out of habit. [4] Yet the trend is changing. More and more people are joining in, and the results are visible: cleaner streets, less pollution, and a growing sense of responsibility. Small actions, when repeated by millions, can truly make a difference.

[5] Garbage sorting is not just about obeying rules—it is about caring for our planet and the future. When we teach our children to sort, we pass on values that will shape how they treat the earth. So the next time you hold a piece of trash, remember: where it goes matters.

## 选项

A. In short, garbage sorting is a habit that connects daily life to environmental protection.
B. That is why learning the rules and getting support from schools and communities can help.
C. As a result, governments and environmental groups have encouraged people to sort their waste.
D. Different types of waste need different ways of treatment to be useful again.
E. Some research shows that countries with strict sorting laws produce less waste per person.
F. Change does not happen overnight, but every effort counts.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] C   [2] D   [3] B   [4] F   [5] A

## 深度解析

- **[1] C**：
  - **线索**：引出垃圾分类的背景与普及。空前描述"millions of tons of waste...pollute our air, water, and soil"，空后说明"In many countries, garbage sorting has become a daily habit...separate their trash into different bins"。C选项"As a result, governments and environmental groups have encouraged people to sort their waste"（因此，政府和环保组织一直鼓励人们进行垃圾分类）中"As a result"承接空前污染问题，"encouraged people to sort"自然引出空后"garbage sorting has become a daily habit"，逻辑顺畅。

- **[2] D**：
  - **线索**：解释垃圾分类的重要性与各类垃圾的利用方式。空前提出"when we mix all kinds of waste together, it becomes difficult and expensive to process"，空后具体说明"Recyclable materials...can be made into new products...Kitchen waste can be turned into compost or energy"。D选项"Different types of waste need different ways of treatment to be useful again"（不同类型的垃圾需要不同的处理方式才能再次利用）总结空后各类垃圾的处理方式，与"separate...different bins"和"recyclables...kitchen waste"形成呼应，衔接精准。

- **[3] B**：
  - **线索**：解决"如何学会分类"的困难。空前描述"Some people find sorting troublesome...wonder which bin a used milk box or old battery should go into"，空后说明"many cities now provide clear guides, colorful posters, and even apps...Schools also play a role"。B选项"That is why learning the rules and getting support from schools and communities can help"（因此，学习规则并获得学校和社区的支持会有所帮助）中"That is why"承接空前的困惑，"learning the rules...support from schools and communities"与空后"guides...posters...apps...Schools"完美对应，逻辑紧密。

- **[4] F**：
  - **线索**：面对挑战时的态度与积极变化。空前描述"challenges remain...Not every community has enough recycling bins...some people still throw everything in one bag"，空后说明"Yet the trend is changing...More and more people are joining in...results are visible...Small actions, when repeated by millions"。F选项"Change does not happen overnight, but every effort counts"（改变不会一夜发生，但每一次努力都算数）承接空前挑战，与空后"Yet the trend is changing...every effort counts"呼应，强调坚持与希望，衔接自然。

- **[5] A**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后列举"not just about obeying rules...caring for our planet...teach our children to sort...where it goes matters"。A选项"In short, garbage sorting is a habit that connects daily life to environmental protection"（简言之，垃圾分类是一种将日常生活与环境保护联系起来的习惯）用"In short"总结全文，"connects daily life to environmental protection"与空后"caring for our planet...values...treat the earth"形成呼应，收束得当。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **E. Some research shows that countries with strict sorting laws produce less waste per person.**：强调严格法规与人均垃圾量的关系，易被误填[1]或[4]。但E讨论的是"countries...strict laws...less waste per person"，偏重国际比较与数据；空[1]需引出"garbage sorting has become a daily habit"的普及原因，E的"strict laws"与空后"daily habit...separate trash"衔接不够自然；空[4]讨论的是社区挑战与人们改变的趋势，E的"countries...laws"与"communities...people joining in"焦点不同。属"主题相关但角度与衔接错位"型干扰。
