# 04-23 第二篇

**E-books vs Paper Books: Which One Do You Prefer?**

Reading is one of the best ways to learn and relax. For centuries, people have enjoyed turning pages of paper books. Today, however, we have another choice: e-books. You can store thousands of titles on a single device and read anywhere, anytime. [1] Still, many readers say they prefer the feel of paper—the smell of a new book, the sound of turning pages. Each format has its own fans, and the debate continues.

E-books offer clear advantages. They save space—you no longer need shelves full of books at home. They are often cheaper than paper books, and you can get them instantly without visiting a bookstore. [2] For students, e-books can be especially helpful. They can highlight text, take notes, and search for words with a tap. When doing homework or preparing for exams, these tools can save a lot of time.

Paper books, on the other hand, have their own strengths. They do not need batteries or screens, so you can read for hours without worrying about power. [3] Some studies suggest that reading on paper helps people remember and understand better than reading on screens. Paper books also reduce eye strain（眼疲劳）after long reading, which many screen users complain about.

So which should you choose? The answer depends on your needs and habits. If you travel often or like to read in dim light, an e-reader may suit you better. [4] If you enjoy collecting books or sharing them with friends and family, paper might be your choice. There is no single "best" way to read—what matters is that you keep reading.

[5] Whether you pick up a paperback or open an app on your phone, the important thing is that you are reading. Both formats can lead you to new ideas, different worlds, and a lifelong love of books. So go ahead—choose what works for you and enjoy the journey.

## 选项

A. In other words, the format you choose is less important than the act of reading itself.
B. Paper books have been popular for hundreds of years and will likely remain so.
C. Research has found that people who read regularly tend to have larger vocabularies.
D. If you value focus and comfort during long reading sessions, paper may work better.
E. E-readers and tablets have made it possible to carry a whole library in your pocket.
F. These advantages have helped e-books become popular among students and young readers.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] E   [2] F   [3] D   [4] B   [5] A

## 深度解析

- **[1] E**：
  - **线索**：引出电子书的便利性与两种选择的对比。空前描述"we have another choice: e-books...store thousands of titles on a single device...read anywhere, anytime"，空后说明"Still, many readers say they prefer the feel of paper"。E选项"E-readers and tablets have made it possible to carry a whole library in your pocket"（电子阅读器和平板电脑使得把整座图书馆装进口袋成为可能）补充说明电子书的便携优势，与空前"store thousands...single device"呼应，空后"Still"自然转折到纸书偏好，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] F**：
  - **线索**：电子书优势与学生应用的过渡。空前列举"save space...cheaper...get them instantly"，空后说明"For students, e-books can be especially helpful...highlight...take notes...search"。F选项"These advantages have helped e-books become popular among students and young readers"（这些优势使电子书在学生和年轻读者中流行起来）中"These advantages"指代空前各项优点，"popular among students and young readers"自然引出空后"For students, e-books can be especially helpful"，衔接精准。

- **[3] D**：
  - **线索**：纸书的优势——专注、舒适与记忆。空前描述"They do not need batteries or screens...read for hours without worrying about power"，空后说明"Some studies suggest that reading on paper helps people remember and understand better...reduce eye strain"。D选项"If you value focus and comfort during long reading sessions, paper may work better"（如果你重视长时间阅读时的专注和舒适，纸书可能更合适）中"focus and comfort...long reading sessions"与空前"read for hours"及空后"remember...understand better...reduce eye strain"形成呼应，衔接自然。

- **[4] B**：
  - **线索**：纸书的另一优势——收藏与分享。空前提出"If you travel often or like to read in dim light, an e-reader may suit you better"，空后说明"If you enjoy collecting books or sharing them with friends and family, paper might be your choice"。B选项"Paper books have been popular for hundreds of years and will likely remain so"（纸书已流行数百年，很可能将继续流行）承上启下，与空后"collecting...sharing...paper might be your choice"呼应，强调纸书的持久魅力与收藏价值，衔接得当。

- **[5] A**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后列举"Whether you pick up a paperback or open an app...the important thing is that you are reading...Both formats can lead you to new ideas...lifelong love of books"。A选项"In other words, the format you choose is less important than the act of reading itself"（换言之，你选择的载体不如阅读行为本身重要）用"In other words"总结全文，"format...less important than the act of reading"与空后"important thing is that you are reading...Both formats"完美对应，收束有力。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **C. Research has found that people who read regularly tend to have larger vocabularies.**：强调经常阅读与词汇量的关系，易被误填[2]或[3]。但C讨论的是"read regularly...larger vocabularies"，偏重阅读习惯与语言能力；空[2]需承接电子书优势并过渡到学生应用，C的"vocabularies"与"highlight...take notes...search"焦点不同；空[3]讨论的是纸书与记忆、理解、护眼的关系，C的"vocabularies"与"remember...understand...eye strain"角度不吻合。属"主题相关但论点与衔接错位"型干扰。
