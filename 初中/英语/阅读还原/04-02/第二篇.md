**The Amazing World of Hummingbirds**

Hummingbirds are among the smallest birds in the world, yet they are known for their incredible abilities. There are over 300 species of hummingbirds, and most of them live in the Americas. [1] Their wings can beat up to 80 times per second, which creates the humming sound that gives these birds their name. This rapid wing movement allows them to hover in mid-air, fly backward, and even upside down—something few other birds can do.

One reason hummingbirds need so much energy is their extremely fast metabolism. Their hearts beat about 1,200 times per minute when they are active. [2] To survive, they must eat almost constantly, visiting hundreds of flowers each day to drink nectar. A hummingbird might consume up to twice its body weight in food every day.

Hummingbirds have a special relationship with flowers. As they feed on nectar, they also help pollinate the plants. [3] The long, slender beak of a hummingbird fits perfectly into tube-shaped flowers, while the bird's tongue can extend to reach the sweet liquid inside. In return, many flowers have evolved bright red or orange colors, which hummingbirds can see clearly, while bees often struggle with these colors.

Migration is another remarkable aspect of hummingbird life. Some species travel thousands of kilometers each year. [4] It makes a non-stop flight of about 800 kilometers across the Gulf of Mexico—a journey that takes roughly 20 hours. This is one of the most demanding migrations in the bird world.

[5] Whether you plant flowers that attract them or simply observe them in nature, these tiny birds never fail to inspire wonder. Protecting their habitats and the plants they depend on helps ensure that future generations can enjoy their beauty.
