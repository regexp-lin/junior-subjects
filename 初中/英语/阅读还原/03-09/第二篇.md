# 03-09 第二篇

**Embracing Changes in Life**

Change is a natural and unavoidable part of our lives. From moving to a new school to making new friends, we face changes almost every single day. [1] As a result, these unpredictable new situations can be quite scary and stressful. However, learning to accept and even welcome change is a very important life skill.

Why do we often fear change? The main reason is that human beings love comfort. [2] When something changes, we are forced to leave this comfortable area and face the unknown. This feeling of uncertainty easily makes us nervous and worried about the future.

Despite these fears, change often brings wonderful opportunities for personal growth. [3] Imagine if you only ever ate the same food or played the same game every single day. Life would become extremely boring! Changes push us to try new activities, discover hidden talents, and step out of our comfort zones.

So, how can we become better at handling change? First, try to focus on the positive side of the situation. [4] Instead of worrying about what you might lose, think about what you could gain. Second, be patient with yourself and give yourself time to adapt to the new environment. [5] Remember that every expert was once a beginner. By keeping an open mind, you can turn any scary change into a wonderful adventure.

## 选项

A. It allows us to learn new things and become better versions of ourselves.
B. For example, moving to a new city means you can explore exciting new places.
C. Therefore, it is always the safest choice to avoid changes and taking risks.
D. We naturally prefer familiar daily routines where we know what to expect.
E. You should not expect to feel completely comfortable on the very first day.
F. While some changes are exciting, others can make us feel deeply uncomfortable.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] F   [2] D   [3] A   [4] B   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] F**：
  - **线索**：语义递进与逻辑铺垫。空前提到生活中每天都面临着变化（如转学、交友）。F选项“While some changes are exciting, others can make us feel deeply uncomfortable”（虽然有些改变令人兴奋，但其他一些会让我们感到深深的不适）对变化作出了具有正反两面性的让步评价。选项末尾的“deeply uncomfortable”完美地铺垫了空后的“As a result, these unpredictable new situations can be quite scary and stressful”（结果，这些不可预测的新情况可能相当可怕和充满压力）。
- **[2] D**：
  - **线索**：因果解释与词汇呼应。空前提出了一个人类共性：“human beings love comfort”（人类热爱安逸/舒适）。D选项“We naturally prefer familiar daily routines where we know what to expect”（我们天生偏好熟悉且可预见的日常运作）是对“热爱安逸”这一天性的具象化解释。不仅如此，D项中的“familiar daily routines”（熟悉的日常）正是后一句“leave this comfortable area”（离开这个舒适区）所指代的具体范畴。
- **[3] A**：
  - **线索**：代词指代与概念阐述。空前点出本段的核心观点：“change often brings wonderful opportunities for personal growth”（改变常常带来个人成长的美妙机会）。A选项中的代词“It”指代“change”，且句中的“learn new things and become better versions of ourselves”（学习新事物，成为更好的自己）是对“personal growth（个人成长）”的详细拆解。选项放入此处起到理论阐释的作用，并引出随后“假如你只吃同一种食物”的反面例证假设。
- **[4] B**：
  - **线索**：举例说明与语境对照。空前提出应对改变的首个策略：“focus on the positive side of the situation”（关注情况的积极面）。B选项“For example, moving to a new city means you can explore exciting new places”（例如，搬去一个新城市意味着你可以去探索令人兴奋的新地点）正是用个例展示了何为“关注积极面”。同时，这正好呼应了紧接着其后的一句“与其去担心你会失去什么，不如想想你会得到什么”。
- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：同义连贯与劝慰语气。空前提出的第二个策略是：“be patient with yourself and give yourself time to adapt...”（对自己要有耐心，给自己时间去适应...）。E选项“You should not expect to feel completely comfortable on the very first day”（你不应该期望在第一天就觉得完全舒适）是在用实用的心理预期去践行“宽慰和耐心”，逻辑上一脉相承。同时与后一句名言式的“Remember that every expert was once a beginner”（记住，每一个专家都曾是个初学者）在语义氛围上融为一体。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **C. Therefore, it is always the safest choice to avoid changes and taking risks.**：该干扰项包含“Therefore”（因此）这样的总结连接词和“safest choice”（最安全的选择）“avoid changes”（避免改变）的词眼。因为第二段提及了人类因为害怕未知而讨厌改变，考生容易产生共鸣，顺着人性避险的思路误选。然而，通读全文，作者的核心观点（主旨）是“改变带来机遇，应该抱着积极开放的心态接受它”，而该选项的躲避观念与全文基调彻底相悖。
