**Why More People Choose to Ride Bicycles**

In many cities around the world, bicycles are becoming an increasingly popular way to get around. Traffic jams make car travel stressful and slow, while air pollution from vehicles harms both our health and the environment. [1] Riding a bike offers a clean, healthy, and often faster way to travel short and medium distances in urban areas.

Bicycles are good for our bodies. Unlike driving, cycling gets our hearts pumping and muscles working. Regular bike riders often report feeling more energetic and sleeping better at night. [2] Physical activity through cycling helps people stay fit and reduces the risk of many health problems caused by a sedentary lifestyle.

Cycling is also kind to the planet. Cars and buses burn fuel and release greenhouse gases that contribute to climate change. Bicycles, however, run on human power alone. [3] Every trip made by bike instead of by car means less pollution and fewer emissions, which benefits the air we breathe and the planet we share.

Cities are starting to support this trend. New bike lanes, bike-sharing programs, and secure parking racks make it easier and safer to choose two wheels over four. [4] These improvements show that when communities invest in cycling infrastructure, more people feel encouraged to leave their cars at home.

The future of urban transport may look quite different from today. As cities grow more crowded, the advantages of bicycles become harder to ignore. [5] For those who can, choosing to ride a bike is not only a personal choice but also a contribution to a greener and healthier community.

## 选项

A. Bicycles provide a practical solution that benefits both individuals and the environment in crowded cities.
B. Making exercise part of your daily commute is an effective way to maintain good health.
C. Switching to bicycles for short trips can significantly lower a city's carbon footprint.
D. Better facilities for cyclists lead to higher rates of bicycle use among the public.
E. Embracing bicycle culture helps build more sustainable and livable cities for everyone.
F. For example, Amsterdam has over 800,000 bicycles, more than the number of people living in the city.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述交通拥堵和汽车尾气污染问题，空后说骑自行车提供了清洁、健康且往往更快的出行方式。A的"practical solution"和"benefits both individuals and the environment"概括了骑自行车的综合优势，与上下文形成因果衔接。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前列举骑车的身体好处（心脏、肌肉、精力、睡眠），空后强调体育锻炼减少久坐导致的健康风险。B的"Making exercise part of your daily commute"将骑车与日常通勤结合，"maintain good health"承接健康主题。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前对比汽车排放与自行车零排放，空后说每次骑车代替开车都意味着更少污染。C的"Switching to bicycles for short trips"与"Every trip made by bike"呼应，"lower a city's carbon footprint"概括环保效应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前列举城市支持措施（自行车道、共享单车、停车架），空后说这些改进鼓励更多人少开车。D的"Better facilities for cyclists"承接基础设施，"higher rates of bicycle use"与"more people feel encouraged"逻辑一致。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前总结自行车优势难以忽视，空后呼吁人们选择骑车作为对社区的贡献。E的"Embracing bicycle culture"概括选择骑车的行动，"sustainable and livable cities"升华主题，与"greener and healthier community"呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, Amsterdam has over 800,000 bicycles, more than the number of people living in the city.**：以For example开头，是具体城市案例；易误放[1]或[4]，但[1]需概括性优势陈述，[4]需设施与使用率的因果概括，非单一城市数据。
