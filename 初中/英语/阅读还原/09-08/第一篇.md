**Water Conservation at Home**

Water is one of Earth's most precious resources, yet many of us take it for granted. We turn on the tap and expect clean water to flow, often without thinking about where it comes from or how much we use. With growing populations and changing climates, water shortages are becoming more common in many parts of the world. [1] Saving water at home is something everyone can do, and even small changes can make a big difference.

One of the easiest ways to save water is to fix leaks. A dripping faucet can waste thousands of liters of water each year. [2] Checking pipes, toilets, and faucets regularly and repairing them quickly is a simple but effective way to prevent unnecessary water loss.

Changing daily habits also helps. Taking shorter showers, turning off the tap while brushing teeth, and using a bowl instead of running water to wash vegetables are all practical steps. [3] These small adjustments may seem minor, but when practiced by millions of people, they add up to a significant reduction in water use.

In the garden, smart watering practices can save large amounts of water. Watering plants in the early morning or late evening reduces evaporation. [4] Choosing drought-resistant plants and using mulch to keep soil moist are other strategies that help gardens thrive while using less water.

Governments and communities also have a role to play. Building better water infrastructure, setting fair pricing, and educating the public about conservation all contribute to a more sustainable water future. [5] When individuals, families, and governments work together, we can ensure that clean water remains available for everyone, now and in the years to come.

## 选项

A. Learning to value and conserve water is essential for meeting the challenges of a world with limited freshwater supplies.
B. Even a small leak that seems harmless can result in a surprisingly large amount of wasted water over time.
C. When everyone adopts water-saving habits, the collective impact on water resources is enormous.
D. These gardening techniques not only conserve water but also promote healthier and more resilient plants.
E. A combined effort at every level of society is the key to protecting this vital resource for future generations.
F. For example, Cape Town in South Africa nearly ran out of water in 2018 due to severe drought.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前说水资源短缺日益严重，空后说每个人都能在家节水。A的"Learning to value and conserve water is essential"概括节水的紧迫性，"challenges of a world with limited freshwater supplies"与"water shortages"呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说一个漏水龙头每年浪费数千升水，空后说定期检查和修理是有效的防止浪费方法。B的"Even a small leak that seems harmless"承接漏水话题，"surprisingly large amount of wasted water"强调危害。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前列举日常节水习惯（短淋浴、刷牙关水等），空后说这些小调整看似微不足道但百万人实践效果显著。C的"When everyone adopts water-saving habits"承接个人行动，"collective impact on water resources is enormous"概括集体效果。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说选择耐旱植物和覆盖层保持土壤湿润，空后无直接后续。D的"These gardening techniques not only conserve water"承接园艺方法，"promote healthier and more resilient plants"补充额外好处。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前列举政府和社区的作用，空后说各方合作确保水资源可用。E的"A combined effort at every level of society"概括多方协作，"protecting this vital resource for future generations"升华主题。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, Cape Town in South Africa nearly ran out of water in 2018 due to severe drought.**：以For example开头，属具体城市案例；易误放[1]，但[1]需的是节水重要性的概括，非个案数据。
