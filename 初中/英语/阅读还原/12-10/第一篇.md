# 12-10 第一篇

**Rainforests: The Lungs of the Earth**

Rainforests are dense forests that receive a lot of rainfall each year. They are home to more than half of the world's plant and animal species, though they cover only about 6% of Earth's land. The Amazon in South America, the Congo in Africa, and the forests of Southeast Asia are among the largest. These ecosystems are rich in life and essential to our planet. [1]

Rainforests play a crucial role in the climate. Their trees absorb huge amounts of carbon dioxide from the air and release oxygen. In this way, they help regulate global temperatures and clean the air we breathe. They also influence rainfall patterns far beyond their borders. When rainforests are cut down, this balance is disturbed. [2]

Deforestation is a major threat. Large areas are cleared for farming, logging, and mining. Every year, millions of hectares disappear. This destroys wildlife habitat, releases stored carbon, and can lead to soil erosion and flooding. Indigenous communities who have lived in the forests for generations lose their homes and traditions. [3]

Conservation efforts are underway. Some countries have created protected areas and national parks. International agreements aim to reduce deforestation and support sustainable practices. Consumers can help by choosing products that do not contribute to forest loss, such as certified wood and sustainable palm oil. [4]

Rainforests are a treasure we cannot afford to lose. They support countless species, stabilize the climate, and provide resources and knowledge we have only begun to explore. Protecting them is not just an environmental issue—it is a matter of survival for people and wildlife alike. [5]

## 选项

A. For these reasons, rainforests are among the most important ecosystems on Earth.
B. This role makes rainforests vital to the health of our planet and its atmosphere.
C. As a result, rainforest loss has serious consequences for nature and people.
D. These actions offer hope that rainforests can be preserved for future generations.
E. That is why saving rainforests should be a global priority for us all.
F. For example, the poison dart frog uses bright colours to warn predators that it is toxic.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍雨林——年降雨量大的密林、占陆地面积极少却拥有半数以上物种、亚马孙刚果东南亚等为最大、生态系统丰富且重要，空后需总结雨林在全球生态中的核心地位。A的"For these reasons"承接前文，"among the most important ecosystems on Earth"与前文对应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说雨林在气候中的角色——树木吸收大量二氧化碳释放氧气、调节全球气温与净化空气、影响远距离降雨、砍伐破坏平衡，空后需总结雨林对地球与大气健康的重要性。B的"This role"指代前文，"vital to the health of our planet and its atmosphere"与前文呼应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说森林砍伐为主要威胁、为农耕伐木采矿而清林、每年数百万公顷消失、破坏栖息地释放碳、导致水土流失与洪涝、原住民失去家园与传统，空后需总结雨林消失的后果。C的"As a result"承接前文，"serious consequences for nature and people"与前文对应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说保护行动进行中——设立保护区与国家公园、国际协议减排与可持续实践、消费者可选用认证木材与可持续棕榈油等，空后需总结这些行动的意义。D的"These actions"指代前文，"offer hope that rainforests can be preserved for future generations"与前文呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说雨林不可失去、支撑无数物种与气候稳定、提供有待探索的资源与知识、保护雨林不仅是环境问题更是人与野生动物的生存问题，空后需总结全球层面的优先保护。E的"That is why saving rainforests should be a global priority for us all"与前文对应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the poison dart frog uses bright colours to warn predators...**：以For example开头，为雨林物种案例（箭毒蛙警戒色）。各空均为概括或过渡句；[1]易被误选因涉及雨林物种，但该空需总结雨林的全球生态地位，而非补充单一物种的举例。
