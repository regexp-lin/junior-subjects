**Snacks and Nutrition: Smart Choices Matter**

Snacks are small meals or bites eaten between main meals. They can help keep your energy up and satisfy hunger when you are busy or on the go. However, not all snacks are equal. Some provide useful nutrients and sustained energy; others are high in sugar, salt, or fat and offer little nutrition. The key is to choose wisely. [1]

Many popular snacks are highly processed. Chips, candy, and sugary drinks often contain lots of added sugar, salt, and unhealthy fats. Eating these regularly can lead to weight gain, tooth decay, and other health problems. They tend to give a quick burst of energy that quickly fades, leaving you hungry again soon. Reading labels helps you see what is really inside. [2]

Healthy snacks can fuel your body and brain. Fruits, vegetables, nuts, yoghurt, and whole-grain crackers are good choices. They provide vitamins, fibre, and protein that help you stay focused and full longer. A small handful of nuts or an apple with peanut butter can be a satisfying snack. Preparing snacks at home gives you more control over ingredients. [3]

Timing matters as well. Snacking when you are truly hungry is better than eating out of boredom. Avoid grazing all day, which can lead to overeating. Plan snacks around your schedule—for example, before exercise or between classes. A balanced approach helps you maintain steady energy without excess calories. [4]

Snacks do not have to be boring or restrictive. With a little planning, you can enjoy tasty and nutritious options that support your health and daily activities. Small changes in what and when you snack can make a big difference over time. [5]

- **[2] B**：

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说健康零食可提供能量与营养——水果蔬菜坚果酸奶全麦饼干、含维生素纤维蛋白质、助专注与饱腹、坚果或苹果配花生酱等、自制便于控制成分，空后需总结健康选项的益处。C的"These options"指代前文，"give your body what it needs without the drawbacks of processed foods"与前文对应。
