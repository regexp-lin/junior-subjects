# 05-05 第一篇

**The Secrets of the Ocean Floor**

The ocean covers more than 70 percent of the Earth's surface, yet we know surprisingly little about what lies beneath the waves. [1] In fact, scientists say we have explored less than 5 percent of the ocean floor. The deep sea remains one of the last great frontiers（前沿领域） on our planet, full of mysteries waiting to be discovered.

The deepest part of the ocean is the Mariana Trench（马里亚纳海沟）, which reaches nearly 11,000 meters below sea level. [2] At that depth, the water pressure is more than 1,000 times the pressure at the surface. It is completely dark, and the temperature is just a few degrees above freezing. For a long time, scientists believed that nothing could survive in such extreme conditions. However, they have since discovered strange creatures living in the deepest waters.

Some of these deep-sea animals look like they belong in a science fiction movie. [3] The anglerfish（鮟鱇鱼）, for example, has a glowing light on its head to attract prey in the darkness. Giant tube worms（管虫） can grow up to two meters long and live near underwater volcanoes（海底火山） called hydrothermal vents（热液喷口）. These creatures survive by feeding on chemicals from the hot water, not sunlight. Each new discovery teaches scientists something new about how life can adapt to harsh environments.

Exploring the deep sea is extremely difficult and expensive. [4] Submarines（潜水器） must be built to withstand enormous pressure, and missions can cost millions of dollars. Remotely operated vehicles（遥控潜水器）, or ROVs, are now used to explore places too dangerous for humans. These robots can take photographs, collect samples, and map the ocean floor. As technology improves, scientists hope to uncover even more secrets hidden deep beneath the sea.

[5] The ocean floor is a world of wonder that we are only beginning to understand. Every expedition brings new surprises, reminding us that our planet still has much to teach us.

## 选项

A. The conditions at the bottom of the ocean are truly extreme.
B. Many of these creatures have developed unusual features to survive in total darkness.
C. Most people think we know a lot about our planet, but the truth is quite different.
D. That is why only a handful of people have ever visited the deepest parts of the ocean.
E. To put it simply, the deep sea is Earth's last unexplored wilderness.
F. For instance, some deep-sea fish can produce their own light through a process called bioluminescence（生物发光）.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] C   [2] A   [3] B   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] C**：
  - **线索**：引出人类对海洋的了解有限。空前描述"The ocean covers more than 70 percent of the Earth's surface, yet we know surprisingly little about what lies beneath"，空后说明"scientists say we have explored less than 5 percent of the ocean floor...one of the last great frontiers"。C选项"Most people think we know a lot about our planet, but the truth is quite different"（大多数人以为我们很了解我们的星球，但事实大不相同）中"think we know a lot...truth is quite different"与空前"know surprisingly little"及空后"explored less than 5 percent"形成对比，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] A**：
  - **线索**：描述深海极端条件。空前说明"the Mariana Trench reaches nearly 11,000 meters below sea level"，空后具体列举"water pressure is more than 1,000 times...completely dark...temperature is just a few degrees above freezing...nothing could survive in such extreme conditions"。A选项"The conditions at the bottom of the ocean are truly extreme"（海底的条件确实极端）中"conditions...truly extreme"总领空后压力、黑暗、低温等具体极端条件的描述，衔接自然。

- **[3] B**：
  - **线索**：深海生物的特殊适应特征。空前描述"Some of these deep-sea animals look like they belong in a science fiction movie"，空后举例"The anglerfish has a glowing light on its head to attract prey in the darkness...Giant tube worms...live near underwater volcanoes...survive by feeding on chemicals"。B选项"Many of these creatures have developed unusual features to survive in total darkness"（这些生物中的许多都进化出了在完全黑暗中生存的特殊特征）中"unusual features...total darkness"承接空前"science fiction"般的外貌，自然引出空后发光鱼、管虫等具体特征的描述，逻辑紧密。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：深海探索的困难与高成本。空前提出"Exploring the deep sea is extremely difficult and expensive"，空后说明"Submarines must be built to withstand enormous pressure...missions can cost millions of dollars...ROVs are now used to explore places too dangerous for humans"。D选项"That is why only a handful of people have ever visited the deepest parts of the ocean"（这就是为什么只有少数人到过海洋最深处）中"That is why...only a handful"承接空前"difficult and expensive"，"visited the deepest parts"自然过渡到空后潜水器和遥控装置的技术替代方案，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"a world of wonder that we are only beginning to understand...Every expedition brings new surprises...our planet still has much to teach us"。E选项"To put it simply, the deep sea is Earth's last unexplored wilderness"（简而言之，深海是地球上最后的未探索荒野）用"To put it simply"总结全文，"last unexplored wilderness"与空后"only beginning to understand"及"much to teach us"形成呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For instance, some deep-sea fish can produce their own light through a process called bioluminescence.**：涉及深海鱼的生物发光，易被误填[3]。但F以"For instance"引出生物发光的例子，而空[3]后已有"anglerfish has a glowing light"的具体例子，F与之信息重复；空[3]需要的是总领"特殊特征"的过渡句，而非另一个并列例子。F属"内容高度相关但与已有例证重复"型干扰。
