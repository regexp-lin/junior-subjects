# 05-05 第二篇

**Learning a Musical Instrument: Why It Matters**

Many parents encourage their children to learn a musical instrument, and there are good reasons for this. Playing music is not just a hobby—it can shape a young person's brain and character in powerful ways. [1] Research shows that children who play instruments tend to do better in school, have stronger social skills, and develop more self-discipline than those who do not. The benefits last well into adulthood.

One of the biggest advantages of learning an instrument is the effect on the brain. [2] When you play music, many parts of your brain are active at the same time. Your eyes read the notes, your fingers move on the keys or strings, your ears listen to the sound, and your brain coordinates（协调） everything together. Scientists have found that this kind of multi-tasking strengthens the brain's connections and can improve memory, attention, and problem-solving skills.

Playing music also teaches important life skills. [3] Learning a new piece of music takes time and practice. Students must set goals, manage their time, and push through frustration（挫折感） when things do not go well. These are the same skills needed to succeed in school, work, and personal relationships. Many successful people credit their musical training for teaching them how to work hard and never give up.

Music can also bring people together. [4] Playing in a band, orchestra（管弦乐队）, or choir（合唱团） requires teamwork and communication. Musicians must listen carefully to each other and adjust their playing to create a harmonious（和谐的） sound. This experience helps young people learn to cooperate and respect others' contributions. Many students say that their closest friendships were formed through making music together.

[5] Whether you choose the piano, the violin, the guitar, or the drums, learning an instrument is one of the most rewarding things you can do. It enriches your mind, builds your character, and connects you with others. The music you make today will echo in your life for years to come.

## 选项

A. In fact, learning music has been compared to a full workout for the brain.
B. Patience and perseverance are among the most important of these.
C. In other words, the skills gained from musical training extend far beyond the practice room.
D. This is because making music with others is naturally a social experience.
E. All in all, picking up an instrument is an investment that pays off in many ways.
F. For example, studies show that children who learn piano score higher on math tests.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] C   [2] A   [3] B   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] C**：
  - **线索**：引出音乐学习的广泛好处。空前描述"Playing music is not just a hobby—it can shape a young person's brain and character in powerful ways"，空后列举"children who play instruments tend to do better in school, have stronger social skills, and develop more self-discipline"。C选项"In other words, the skills gained from musical training extend far beyond the practice room"（换言之，音乐训练获得的技能远超练习室的范围）中"In other words"概括空前"not just a hobby"，"extend far beyond the practice room"自然引出空后学业、社交、自律等多方面好处，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] A**：
  - **线索**：音乐对大脑的锻炼作用。空前提出"One of the biggest advantages of learning an instrument is the effect on the brain"，空后具体描述"many parts of your brain are active at the same time...eyes read the notes, fingers move, ears listen...strengthen the brain's connections"。A选项"In fact, learning music has been compared to a full workout for the brain"（事实上，学音乐被比作大脑的全面锻炼）中"In fact"加强空前论点，"full workout for the brain"与空后多个脑区同时活跃的描述完美呼应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] B**：
  - **线索**：音乐培养的具体生活技能。空前提出"Playing music also teaches important life skills"，空后列举"Learning a new piece takes time and practice...set goals, manage their time, and push through frustration"。B选项"Patience and perseverance are among the most important of these"（耐心和毅力是其中最重要的）中"these"指代空前"important life skills"，"patience and perseverance"与空后"takes time and practice...push through frustration"精准对应，衔接自然。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：音乐的社交功能。空前提出"Music can also bring people together"，空后描述"Playing in a band, orchestra, or choir requires teamwork and communication...listen carefully to each other...learn to cooperate and respect"。D选项"This is because making music with others is naturally a social experience"（这是因为与他人一起创作音乐天生就是一种社交体验）中"This is because"解释空前"bring people together"的原因，"social experience"与空后"teamwork and communication...cooperate and respect"呼应，衔接得当。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与升华。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后总结"learning an instrument is one of the most rewarding things...enriches your mind, builds your character, and connects you with others"。E选项"All in all, picking up an instrument is an investment that pays off in many ways"（总之，学乐器是一项在许多方面都能获得回报的投资）用"All in all"总结全文，"investment that pays off"与空后"rewarding...enriches...builds...connects"形成呼应，收束精准。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, studies show that children who learn piano score higher on math tests.**：涉及学琴与数学成绩的关系，易被误填[2]。但F以"For example"引出钢琴与数学成绩的单一研究结论，而空[2]后已有"eyes read notes, fingers move, ears listen...strengthen brain's connections"的详细大脑机制描述；F的"math tests"过于具体，与空[2]段落讨论的大脑全面锻炼相比范围过窄。F属"内容相关但举例过于细化、与段落展开逻辑不匹配"型干扰。
