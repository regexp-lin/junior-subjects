# 03-07 第二篇

**How Exercise Benefits the Brain**

We all know that exercise is great for building strong muscles and keeping our hearts healthy. [1] In fact, working out regularly can make you smarter, happier, and even more creative. Let's find out how this works.

When you exercise, your heart beats faster, pumping more blood up to your head. [2] As a result, your memory improves rapidly, making it much easier to learn and remember new information at school.

Furthermore, physical activity is a powerful tool to fight stress. [3] When a person feels worried or sad, a simple run or swim can quickly improve their mood. This miracle happens because exercise causes the brain to release special chemicals called "endorphins" (内啡肽). [4] As these chemicals flow through your body, pain and sadness seem to magically disappear.

Finally, getting enough exercise can also powerfully boost your creativity. Have you ever noticed that your best ideas often come to you during a long walk? [5] Therefore, next time you are stuck on a difficult math problem or an essay, try taking a break to move your body!

## 选项

A. The reason is that moving your body helps your brain think in new ways.
B. However, many people forget that it is also amazing for our brains.
C. This extra blood carries oxygen and nutrients to help your brain cells grow.
D. They are widely known as the body's natural "feel-good" medicine.
E. For example, active students generally feel much less nervous before tests.
F. Therefore, working out too much can sometimes damage your muscles.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] B   [2] C   [3] E   [4] D   [5] A

## 深度解析

- **[1] B**：
  - **线索**：逻辑转折与话题引入。空前提到大家都知道运动对肌肉和心脏好。B选项用“However”（然而）形成转折，“many people forget that it is also amazing for our brains”（很多人忘记了它对我们的大脑也大有裨益），代词“it”指代“exercise”。这一句完美地将话题从“身体”转移到了“大脑”，并与空后一句“In fact, working out regularly can make you smarter”（事实上，锻炼能让你变聪明）在逻辑上紧密相连，引出全文核心。
- **[2] C**：
  - **线索**：词汇同现与因果逻辑。空前提到了锻炼时心脏跳动加快，“pumping more blood up to your head”（将更多血液泵入大脑）。C选项紧接着说明“This extra blood carries oxygen and nutrients to help your brain cells grow”（这多余的血液携带氧气和营养来帮助脑细胞生长）。“This extra blood”完美指代前一句增加的血流。正因为脑细胞得到了营养，所以自然引出了空后的结果“As a result, your memory improves rapidly”（结果，你的记忆力迅速提高）。
- **[3] E**：
  - **线索**：举例说明与上下文呼应。空前提出段落主旨“physical activity is a powerful tool to fight stress”（体力活动是对抗压力的有力工具）。E选项“For example, active students generally feel much less nervous before tests”（例如，活跃的学生在考试前通常不那么紧张）正是对这一主旨的绝佳例证（少紧张=对抗压力）。随后，空后一句泛指“When a person feels worried... a simple run... can improve their mood”，在逻辑上层层递进。
- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：代词指代与同义替换。空前解释了运动能改善情绪的原因是因为大脑释放了被称为“内啡肽”的特殊化学物质。D选项“They are widely known as the body's natural 'feel-good' medicine”（它们被广泛认为是身体天然的“感觉良好”药物）中，“They”完美指代“endorphins / special chemicals”，而“feel-good medicine”是对内啡肽作用的形象替换。空后再进一步描述这些化学物质流经身体产生的效果。
- **[5] A**：
  - **线索**：因果解释与句意连贯。空前提出了一个有趣的现象：“Have you ever noticed that your best ideas often come to you during a long walk?”（你有没有注意到你最好的点子通常是在长时间散步时出现的？）。A选项“The reason is that moving your body helps your brain think in new ways”（原因在于活动身体能帮助大脑以新的方式思考）正好回答了前一句的疑问。同时也为文末总结的实质性建议“Therefore, next time you are stuck... try taking a break to move your body!”（下次卡住时，休息一下去活动身体！）提供了理论支撑。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. Therefore, working out too much can sometimes damage your muscles.**：该选项结构上包含总结词“Therefore”，话题上包含“working out”和“muscles”。但这句表达的主题是“锻炼过度有时会损伤肌肉”，这涉及的是运动可能带来的弊端（身体层面的负面影响）。而整篇文章都在极力赞扬运动对大脑的益处（智力、情绪、创造力层面的积极影响）。这种语义方向上的南辕北辙使其成为极易识别的干扰项。
