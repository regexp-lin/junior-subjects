**The Importance of Waste Sorting**

Every day, we throw away tons of garbage. Much of it ends up in landfills or the ocean, harming the environment and wildlife. Sorting our waste is a simple habit that can make a huge difference. [1] Learning to separate recyclable materials from other waste is the first step toward a cleaner and more sustainable future.

Different types of waste need different treatment. Paper, plastic, glass, and metal can often be recycled and turned into new products. Food waste can be turned into compost for gardens. [2] When we sort our trash correctly, we make it possible for materials to be reused instead of buried or burned.

Recycling saves resources and energy. Making new products from recycled materials uses less water, less energy, and fewer raw materials than making them from scratch. [3] The more we recycle, the less we need to take from nature, which helps protect forests, mines, and water sources.

Waste sorting also reduces pollution. When recyclables are mixed with other waste, they often end up in landfills or incinerators, releasing harmful chemicals. [4] Proper sorting keeps dangerous materials out of the wrong places and allows safe and clean recycling processes to work effectively.

Everyone has a role to play. Families can set up separate bins at home. Schools and offices can provide clear labels and instructions. [5] When sorting becomes a normal part of daily life for more people, the benefits for our environment and society grow with each passing year.

## 选项

A. Adopting the habit of waste sorting is essential for reducing environmental damage and building a circular economy.
B. Correct waste sorting is what makes recycling and reuse possible on a large scale.
C. Recycling reduces our need for new resources and lessens our impact on the natural world.
D. Good waste sorting practices ensure that materials go to the right facilities and cause less harm.
E. As more people make waste sorting part of their routine, the positive effects multiply over time.
F. For example, Japan has one of the strictest waste sorting systems in the world, with different pickup days for different types of garbage.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述垃圾泛滥、环境受害，空后强调学习分类是可回收物分离的第一步。A的"Adopting the habit of waste sorting"与"Learning to separate"呼应，"reducing environmental damage"承接前文问题，"circular economy"与"sustainable future"一致。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前列举不同废弃物需不同处理（纸张塑料可回收、厨余可堆肥），空后强调正确分类使材料得以再利用。B的"Correct waste sorting"与"sort our trash correctly"呼应，"makes recycling and reuse possible"与"materials to be reused instead of buried or burned"逻辑一致。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说回收可节省资源能源，空后强调回收越多对自然索取越少。C的"Recycling reduces our need for new resources"直接承接，"lessens our impact on the natural world"与"protect forests, mines, and water sources"呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说混合垃圾导致填埋焚烧释放有害物，空后强调正确分类让危险物质去到合适场所。D的"Good waste sorting practices"概括做法，"materials go to the right facilities"与"keeps dangerous materials out of the wrong places"一致，"cause less harm"与"safe and clean recycling"呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前列举家庭、学校、办公室可做的分类举措，空后说分类成为日常生活一部分后效益逐年增长。E的"As more people make waste sorting part of their routine"与"sorting becomes a normal part of daily life"呼应，"positive effects multiply"与"benefits grow"一致。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, Japan has one of the strictest waste sorting systems in the world, with different pickup days for different types of garbage.**：以For example开头，是具体国家案例；易误放[2]或[5]，但[2]需分类与回收的概括关系，[5]需众人参与与效益累积的概括，非单一国家制度举例。
