# 08-15 第二篇

**Cooperation and Competition**

In school, at work, and in sports, we often face a choice between working with others and competing against them. Some people believe that life is all about winning and that only the strongest succeed. Others argue that cooperation leads to better outcomes for everyone. [1] The truth is that both cooperation and competition play important roles, and the key is knowing when to use each approach wisely.

Competition can push us to do our best. When we compete, we often practice harder and think more creatively. [2] Sports and academic contests can motivate students to improve their skills and discover their potential. Healthy competition teaches us to handle pressure and learn from losses. However, competition becomes harmful when winning becomes the only goal and we forget to respect others. The best competitors know that fair play matters more than the final score.

Cooperation brings people together to achieve goals that no one could reach alone. Team projects at school show how different skills and ideas can combine for better results. [3] Working with others helps us develop communication skills and learn to appreciate different perspectives. We also build trust and lasting friendships through shared efforts. Many of the world's greatest achievements—from space exploration to medical breakthroughs—required teams of people working together.

In real life, cooperation and competition often work together. A basketball team competes against other teams, but the players must cooperate with each other to win. [4] Similarly, companies compete for customers while their employees collaborate on projects. Finding the right balance depends on the situation. Sometimes we need to focus on beating the challenge; other times we need to join forces with others.

Understanding both cooperation and competition helps us succeed. [5] We can strive to do our personal best while also supporting and learning from those around us. Neither approach is always right—the wisest people know when to compete and when to cooperate. Developing both skills prepares us for the complex challenges of school, work, and life.

## 选项

A. A balanced view recognizes the value of both forces.
B. It can inspire people to reach higher and achieve more.
C. Collaboration builds skills that last a lifetime.
D. The two forces often exist side by side in daily life.
E. The goal is to use each approach at the right time.
F. For example, the Olympic Games bring athletes from many countries to compete.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前对比"只讲竞争"与"只讲合作"两种观点，空后"The truth is that both...play important roles...knowing when to use each"。A项"A balanced view recognizes the value of both forces"承上驳斥极端观点，引出"两者皆有价值"的平衡立场，与后文呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：第二段主题是竞争的正向作用，空前"Competition can push us to do our best...practice harder and think more creatively"，空后"Sports and academic contests can motivate students...discover their potential"。B项"It can inspire people to reach higher and achieve more"概括竞争的激励作用，与后文"motivate""potential"衔接。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：第三段讲合作，空前"different skills and ideas can combine for better results"，空后"Working with others helps us develop communication skills...appreciate different perspectives...build trust and lasting friendships"。C项"Collaboration builds skills that last a lifetime"概括合作带来的长期能力，与后文呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：第四段"In real life, cooperation and competition often work together"，空前举篮球队、公司为例，空后"Similarly, companies compete...while their employees collaborate...Finding the right balance..."。D项"The two forces often exist side by side in daily life"概括二者并存，与前后举例一致。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：结尾段"Understanding both...helps us succeed"，空前总结，空后"We can strive to do our personal best while also supporting...Neither approach is always right—the wisest people know when to compete and when to cooperate"。E项"The goal is to use each approach at the right time"点明核心要旨，与后文"when to compete and when to cooperate"完美对应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the Olympic Games bring athletes from many countries to compete**：F为奥运竞争举例，与"竞争与合作平衡"的论证层次不完全匹配。若放[2]会与"sports and academic contests"重复且打断逻辑；放[4]则奥运同时体现合作（国家/团队）与竞争，但与"companies""basketball"等日常例子层次不同，易造成误选。
