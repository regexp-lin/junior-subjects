# 08-15 第一篇

**The Threat of Microplastics**

Plastic has become a part of almost everything we use—from bottles and bags to clothes and cosmetics. When plastic breaks down, it does not disappear. It turns into tiny pieces called microplastics that are smaller than a grain of rice. These particles have been found in oceans, rivers, soil, and even in the air we breathe. [1] Scientists are only beginning to understand how widespread microplastics are and what effects they may have on human health and the environment.

Microplastics enter the environment in many ways. They come from larger plastic items that break apart over time. [2] They also come from products designed to contain tiny plastic beads, such as some facial scrubs and toothpastes. Synthetic clothing releases microplastic fibers when washed. Car tires wear down and leave plastic particles on roads, which eventually wash into waterways. The sources of microplastics are more numerous than many people realize.

Once in the environment, microplastics are very difficult to remove. They are too small to filter out easily and can travel long distances. [3] Marine animals often mistake microplastics for food and eat them, which can harm their digestive systems. The plastic then moves up the food chain—fish eat the smaller creatures, and we eat the fish. This means microplastics may be finding their way onto our dinner plates.

Research on the health effects of microplastics is still evolving. Studies have found microplastics in human blood, lungs, and even placentas. [4] The full impact on our bodies is not yet clear, but scientists are concerned about possible links to inflammation and other health issues. More research is needed to understand the long-term risks. In the meantime, reducing plastic use is a sensible precaution.

The fight against microplastics requires action at many levels. [5] Governments can ban single-use plastics and require better wastewater treatment. Companies can design products with less plastic and develop biodegradable alternatives. Individuals can choose reusable bags, avoid products with microbeads, and reduce overall plastic consumption. Everyone has a role to play in tackling this growing problem.

## 选项

A. The scale of the problem is larger than most people imagine.
B. Many everyday products contribute to this pollution in surprising ways.
C. Wildlife is already suffering from the presence of plastic in ecosystems.
D. While many questions remain, early findings have raised concerns.
E. Solutions will require efforts from all sectors of society.
F. For example, a single load of laundry can release thousands of microfibers.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前列举微塑料无处不在（oceans, rivers, soil, air），空后"Scientists are only beginning to understand how widespread...and what effects..."。A项"The scale of the problem is larger than most people imagine"承上概括问题规模，与后文"widespread"及"刚起步的研究"衔接。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：第二段讲微塑料来源，空前"larger plastic items that break apart"，空后"They also come from products designed to contain tiny plastic beads...Synthetic clothing...Car tires..."。B项"Many everyday products contribute to this pollution in surprising ways"概括多种日常产品来源，与后文列举呼应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：第三段讲微塑料难以清除及传播，空前"travel long distances"，空后"Marine animals often mistake microplastics for food...harm their digestive systems...moves up the food chain"。C项"Wildlife is already suffering from the presence of plastic in ecosystems"概括对野生动物的危害，与后文食物链逻辑一致。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：第四段"Research on the health effects...is still evolving"，空前"found microplastics in human blood, lungs, placentas"，空后"The full impact...is not yet clear...scientists are concerned...More research is needed"。D项"While many questions remain, early findings have raised concerns"准确概括"有发现但未定论"的研究状态。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：结尾段"The fight against microplastics requires action at many levels"，空前总起，空后"Governments can...Companies can...Individuals can..."。E项"Solutions will require efforts from all sectors of society"概括多方参与，与后文政府、企业、个人三段式对应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, a single load of laundry can release thousands of microfibers**：F为洗衣释放微纤维的具体例子，与[2]段"Synthetic clothing releases..."主题相关。但原文[2]处需概括句"Many everyday products..."而非举例；若插入F会打断"产品类型列举"的逻辑，易使考生误将F当作[2]答案。
