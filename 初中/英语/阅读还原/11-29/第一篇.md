**Tea Culture: A Tradition That Crosses Borders**

Tea is one of the most popular drinks in the world, enjoyed by billions of people every day. It originated in China thousands of years ago and later spread to Japan, India, Europe, and beyond. Different cultures have developed their own ways of preparing and serving tea, each with its own meaning and rituals. Whether it is a simple cup of black tea in Britain or a formal tea ceremony in Japan, tea connects people across borders and generations. [1]

The history of tea began in ancient China. According to legend, Emperor Shen Nong discovered tea when leaves fell into his boiling water around 2737 BC. Tea was first used as a medicine before it became a daily drink. Later, Buddhist monks brought tea to Japan, where it became part of Zen practices. In the 17th century, British traders brought tea to Europe, and it quickly became a favorite. [2]

Today, tea is grown in many countries, including China, India, Kenya, and Sri Lanka. The type of tea—green, black, white, or oolong—depends on how the leaves are processed. Green tea keeps more of its original nutrients because the leaves are steamed or pan-fired soon after picking. Black tea is fully oxidized, giving it a stronger taste. Each variety offers different flavors and health benefits. [3]

Tea ceremonies and customs vary around the world. In China, serving tea to elders is a sign of respect. In Japan, the tea ceremony emphasizes harmony, respect, and tranquility. In Britain, afternoon tea with scones and sandwiches is a social tradition. These rituals show how a simple drink can carry deep cultural meanings. [4]

Modern research has found that tea contains antioxidants and other compounds that may support health. Regular tea drinkers may have better heart health and a lower risk of some diseases. However, experts suggest drinking tea without too much sugar or milk to get the most benefit. [5]

## 选项

B. From its roots in China to its spread across the globe, tea has a rich and fascinating history that continues to influence how we live today.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍茶是全球流行饮品、源自中国、传播至多国、不同文化有不同喝法、连接各地人与世代，空后需总结茶的文化连接作用。A的"not just a drink; it is a bridge between cultures"与"connects people across borders and generations"呼应，"bringing people together and preserving traditions"概括前文多元茶文化。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前按时间线叙述茶的历史——神农传说、从药到饮品、传入日本、近代传入英国，空后需总结历史意义。B的"From its roots in China to its spread across the globe"与前文时间线索对应，"rich and fascinating history"概括本段内容。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍茶的产地、绿茶/红茶/白茶/乌龙茶的加工差异与各自特点，空后需过渡总结。C的"Understanding these differences"指代前文不同品种的差异，"helps people choose the right tea for their taste and health goals"与前文"different flavors and health benefits"衔接。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前列举中国敬茶、日本茶道、英国下午茶等习俗，空后需总结习俗的文化意义。D的"Whether simple or formal"对应各国不同仪式，"connecting with others and honoring the past"概括茶习俗的文化内涵。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说茶叶含抗氧化物质、可能有益健康、建议少糖少奶，空后需收尾总结。E的"long history and growing scientific support"承接全文，"comfort and potential health benefits"与前文健康研究呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, in Morocco, mint tea is served with great care...**：以For example开头，为具体国家茶俗案例（摩洛哥薄荷茶）。各空均为概括或过渡句；[4]易被误选因涉及习俗，但该空需总结各国茶俗的共同文化意义，而非补充某一国的具体案例。
