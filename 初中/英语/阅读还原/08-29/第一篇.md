**The Future of Wind Power**

Wind has been used by humans for thousands of years—to sail ships, turn windmills, and grind grain. Today, we use wind in a much bigger way: to generate electricity. Wind turbines, those tall structures with spinning blades, can be seen in many countries. [1] Unlike fossil fuels, wind energy produces no pollution and will never run out, making it one of the most promising sources of clean power for the future.

In recent decades, wind power has grown rapidly. More and more countries are building wind farms both on land and at sea. [2] As technology improves, wind turbines have become more efficient and cheaper to build, which helps explain why wind energy is now one of the fastest-growing energy sources in the world.

Wind power has its challenges, too. The wind does not blow all the time, so we need other energy sources when it is calm. [3] Scientists and engineers are working on better ways to store wind energy in batteries, so we can use it even when the wind is not blowing.

Many people support wind power because it fights climate change and creates jobs. Others worry about noise, birds, or how turbines change the view of the landscape. [4] Finding a balance between these different views is important as we move toward a cleaner energy future.

The future of wind power looks bright. Nations have set goals to use more renewable energy, and wind is a key part of those plans. [5] From small turbines for homes to giant offshore wind farms, wind energy will continue to play a vital role in powering our world in a sustainable way.

## 选项

A. This makes wind power both environmentally friendly and economically attractive for the long term.
B. Advances in design and materials have made turbines quieter, stronger, and more productive.
C. Solutions like energy storage systems are being developed to make wind power more reliable.
D. Governments and communities must work together to address these concerns while supporting green energy.
E. As we look ahead, wind power is set to become an even more important part of our energy mix.
F. For example, Denmark gets more than half of its electricity from wind on some windy days.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述风力发电现状，空后强调"Unlike fossil fuels, wind energy produces no pollution and will never run out"。A的"environmentally friendly and economically attractive"概括无污染与可持续性，与后文"promising"呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说风电快速发展、各国建陆上和海上风场，空后说"technology improves... more efficient and cheaper"。B的"Advances in design and materials"对应技术进步，"quieter, stronger, more productive"与"efficient"一致，概括技术发展带来的变化。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前指出风不常吹、需要其他能源，空后说科学家在研究储能。C的"Solutions like energy storage... to make wind power more reliable"准确概括该段主旨，与"store wind energy in batteries"形成对应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前对比支持者与担忧者观点，空后说"Finding a balance... is important"。D的"work together to address these concerns while supporting green energy"正是平衡两方的表述，与"balance"呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说各国设定可再生能源目标、风能是关键，空后举例小型与大型应用。E的"set to become an even more important part"与"key part"呼应，适合作该段及全文的总结升华。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, Denmark gets more than half of its electricity from wind on some windy days.**：以For example开头，属具体国家举例，与文中某段可能相关，但各空均需概括或过渡句；易误放[2]，但[2]需的是技术进步的概括，非国别案例。
