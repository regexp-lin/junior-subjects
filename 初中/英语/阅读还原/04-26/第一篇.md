# 04-26 第一篇

**The Silk Road: A Bridge Between East and West**

More than two thousand years ago, China began producing beautiful silk. People in other countries loved this soft and shiny（光亮的） cloth, but they did not know how to make it. [1] Chinese traders（商人） started to bring silk to places far away, and step by step, a long path across Asia was formed. This path later became known as the Silk Road. It was not a single road but a network（网络） of routes connecting China with Central Asia, the Middle East, and even Europe.

The Silk Road was useful for more than silk alone. [2] Spices（香料）, tea, paper, and many other goods were also carried along these routes. Ideas, religions（宗教）, and knowledge traveled the same way. For example, Buddhism（佛教） spread from India to China through the Silk Road. Scientists and artists from different countries learned from each other. In this way, the Silk Road helped different cultures grow and share what they knew.

Traveling on the Silk Road was never easy. [3] Traders had to cross deserts, climb mountains, and face strong winds. The journey could take months or even years. Bandits（强盗） sometimes attacked travelers, and sickness could strike at any time. Yet people still chose to go because the rewards（回报） were great. Those who succeeded could earn a lot of money and gain respect from their communities.

The Silk Road did not stay busy forever. [4] After the 15th century, ocean shipping（海运） became cheaper and faster. Ships could carry heavier loads and reach more ports. Slowly, fewer and fewer traders used the old land routes. Some parts of the Silk Road became quiet, while others were forgotten. Still, its history has never been lost.

[5] Today, the idea of the Silk Road has come back. Many countries along the ancient routes are working together again to build roads, railways, and trade links. They hope to bring back the spirit of connection and exchange that made the old Silk Road so important. In this sense, the Silk Road is not just history—it is a model for how different peoples can cooperate and grow together.

## 选项

A. In other words, the Silk Road was a path not only for goods but also for culture and knowledge.
B. That is why the ancient Silk Road has inspired a modern project to reconnect Asia and Europe.
C. At first, only silk was traded, but soon other products joined the journey.
D. The journey was full of danger and hardship, yet brave traders never gave up.
E. The Silk Road changed the world by linking East and West in ways that had never happened before.
F. As a result, land trade along the Silk Road gradually declined and lost its importance.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] E   [2] A   [3] D   [4] F   [5] B

## 深度解析

- **[1] E**：
  - **线索**：引出丝绸之路的形成与历史意义。空前描述"China began producing silk...People in other countries loved it...did not know how to make it"，空后详述"Chinese traders started to bring silk...a long path across Asia was formed...became known as the Silk Road...a network of routes connecting China with...Europe"。E选项"The Silk Road changed the world by linking East and West in ways that had never happened before"（丝绸之路以前所未有的方式将东西方联系起来，从而改变了世界）中"linking East and West"与空后"connecting China with Central Asia, the Middle East, and even Europe"精准对应，"changed the world"自然承接空前"loved silk...did not know how to make it"所暗示的需求与机遇，衔接顺畅。

- **[2] A**：
  - **线索**：丝绸之路不只运送丝绸，还传播文化与知识。空前说明"The Silk Road was useful for more than silk alone"，空后列举"Spices, tea, paper...Ideas, religions, and knowledge traveled the same way...Buddhism spread...Scientists and artists learned from each other...helped different cultures grow"。A选项"In other words, the Silk Road was a path not only for goods but also for culture and knowledge"（换言之，丝绸之路不仅是一条商品之路，也是文化与知识之路）中"In other words"对上文"more than silk alone"进行概括，"not only for goods but also for culture and knowledge"与空后"spices, tea, paper...ideas, religions, knowledge...cultures grow"完美呼应，逻辑紧密。

- **[3] D**：
  - **线索**：丝绸之路旅行的艰辛与商人的毅力。空前描述"Traveling on the Silk Road was never easy"，空后详述"cross deserts, climb mountains, face strong winds...months or even years...Bandits sometimes attacked...sickness could strike...Yet people still chose to go because the rewards were great"。D选项"The journey was full of danger and hardship, yet brave traders never gave up"（旅途充满危险与艰辛，但勇敢的商人从未放弃）中"danger and hardship"与空后"deserts, mountains, bandits, sickness"对应，"never gave up"与空后"Yet people still chose to go"形成呼应，衔接自然。

- **[4] F**：
  - **线索**：丝绸之路衰落的原因。空前说明"The Silk Road did not stay busy forever...After the 15th century, ocean shipping became cheaper and faster...Ships could carry heavier loads and reach more ports"，空后描述"Slowly, fewer and fewer traders used the old land routes...Some parts became quiet, while others were forgotten"。F选项"As a result, land trade along the Silk Road gradually declined and lost its importance"（因此，沿丝绸之路的陆路贸易逐渐衰落并失去其重要性）中"As a result"承接空前"ocean shipping became cheaper and faster"，"gradually declined and lost its importance"与空后"fewer traders...became quiet...forgotten"精准对应，衔接得当。

- **[5] B**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与现代"一带一路"呼应。空[5]位于结尾段段首，空后列举"Today, the idea of the Silk Road has come back...countries are working together again...build roads, railways, and trade links...bring back the spirit of connection...a model for how different peoples can cooperate"。B选项"That is why the ancient Silk Road has inspired a modern project to reconnect Asia and Europe"（因此古丝绸之路激发了 reconnect 亚欧的现代项目）中"That is why"承接上文历史，"inspired a modern project to reconnect"与空后"idea has come back...working together again...build roads, railways"完美呼应，收束升华。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **C. At first, only silk was traded, but soon other products joined the journey.**：易被误填[2]。但C以"At first, only silk"开头，暗示时间顺序"先丝绸后其他"，而空[2]空前已说"useful for more than silk alone"，空后直接列举"Spices, tea, paper...Ideas, religions"，不需"at first...soon"的时间过渡；A的"In other words...not only for goods but also for culture and knowledge"是概括性过渡，更贴合。C属"语义相关但逻辑衔接词与功能错位"型干扰。
