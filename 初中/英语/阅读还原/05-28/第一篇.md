# 05-28 第一篇

**Teen Volunteer Service: Giving Back to the Community**

Many teenagers spend their free time on phones, games, or social media. Yet more and more young people are choosing to volunteer. They help at food banks, teach kids to read, clean up parks, or visit the elderly. [1] That is because volunteering helps teens develop skills and feel good about themselves. When you give your time to others, you learn responsibility, teamwork, and compassion（同情）. You also discover new interests and make friends who care about the community. Studies show that teens who volunteer do better in school and feel happier. In short, volunteer service is a powerful way for young people to grow and contribute. It benefits both the community and the volunteer.

Where can teens volunteer? [2] In other words, there are many places that welcome young helpers. Schools often need tutors for younger students. Hospitals and nursing homes（养老院）look for people to chat with patients or read to them. Animal shelters need help feeding and walking dogs. Environmental groups organize clean-up days at beaches or parks. Libraries welcome volunteers to sort books and help with events. You can also check websites or ask your school counselor for opportunities. The key is to find something that matches your interests and schedule. Once you start, you will see how much your help matters.

What do teens gain from volunteering? [3] That is why volunteering builds confidence and leadership. When you complete a task, help someone in need, or lead a small project, you feel capable and useful. Many volunteers say they learned to speak in front of others, solve problems, and work with different kinds of people. These skills are valuable for future jobs and college applications. Employers and universities like to see that you have given back to your community. For this reason, volunteering is not only kind—it is also smart. It prepares you for the future while helping others today.

How can teens get started? [4] It is true that the first step is to find a cause you care about. Do you love animals? Try an animal shelter. Do you enjoy teaching? Look for tutoring programs. Do you care about the environment? Join a local clean-up group. Start with a few hours a week—you do not need to do everything at once. Ask friends to volunteer with you if that makes it easier. Remember: every small act of service counts. The best way to begin is simply to begin. Many organizations are happy to train new volunteers.

[5] In conclusion, teen volunteer service is a rewarding experience. It builds character, develops skills, and strengthens communities. Whether you help at a food bank or plant trees in a park, your efforts make a difference. So the next time you have a few free hours, consider volunteering—you might be surprised how much you gain from giving. Remember: the best way to feel good about yourself is to do good for others.
