# 10-07 第一篇

**The Rise of Mobile Payment**

Not long ago, paying for something meant carrying cash or a wallet full of cards. Today, in many countries, people simply hold their phone near a reader or scan a code to complete a purchase. Mobile payment has grown rapidly, especially in China, where apps like Alipay and WeChat Pay are used by hundreds of millions of people. [1] As a result, the traditional wallet is becoming less necessary, and many young people now rarely carry cash at all.

One reason for its success is convenience. There is no need to carry cash or worry about losing a card. Payments can be made in seconds, and many apps also offer discounts, loyalty points, and other rewards. [2] Shops and restaurants that accept mobile payment often attract more customers, especially younger ones who prefer not to carry cash.

Mobile payment has also helped bring banking services to people who previously had little or no access to them. In rural areas and in countries with few bank branches, a smartphone can act as a wallet. [3] Farmers can now receive payments for crops directly on their phones, and workers can send money home to their families without traveling long distances to a bank.

However, there are concerns. Some people worry about security—what if their phone is lost or stolen? Others fear that paying with a phone may make it easier to overspend because it feels less real than handing over cash. [4] Companies are responding with features like spending limits, alerts, and biometric locks to keep accounts secure and help users manage their money wisely.

The trend toward mobile payment is likely to continue. As more businesses accept it and more people adopt it, cash may become less common. [5] Learning how to use mobile payment safely—and when to choose other methods—will help each of us navigate this changing landscape with confidence.

A. Mobile payment is not just convenient—it has changed how people shop, travel, and even send money to family and friends, making daily life easier in countless ways.
B. For merchants, mobile payment reduces the need to handle cash and can lower the risk of theft, while also providing useful data about customer spending habits.

E. Whether we prefer cash, cards, or phones, the way we pay is evolving, and understanding these changes helps us make the most of the new options while staying secure and in control of our money.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述移动支付普及及使用场景，空后说"As a result, the traditional wallet is becoming less necessary"。A的"not just convenient—it has changed how people shop, travel, and send money"与空后现金、钱包式微呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说便利、优惠与积分，空后说"Shops and restaurants that accept mobile payment often attract more customers"。B的"For merchants... reduces the need to handle cash... useful data about customer spending"与空后商户获益衔接。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说智能手机在缺乏银行地区充当钱包，空后说"Farmers can now receive payments... workers can send money home"。C的"This has opened new opportunities... save money, pay bills, and run small businesses"与空后农民、工人例证呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前列举安全与过度消费担忧，空后说"Companies are responding with features like spending limits, alerts, and biometric locks"。D的"Addressing these concerns through strong security measures"与空后企业应对衔接。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说移动支付趋势将持续，空后说"Learning how to use mobile payment safely... will help each of us navigate this changing landscape"。E的"understanding these changes helps us make the most of the new options while staying secure"与空后呼应。
