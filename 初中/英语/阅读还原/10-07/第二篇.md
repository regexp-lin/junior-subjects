# 10-07 第二篇

**Rescuing Endangered Animals**

Many animals around the world are in danger of extinction. Habitat loss, hunting, and pollution have pushed countless species to the brink. Fortunately, conservationists, governments, and ordinary citizens are working to save them. Rescue centers, wildlife sanctuaries, and breeding programs have helped bring some species back from the edge. [1] Each species saved is a thread preserved in the web of life—a web that supports not only wildlife but also human health, agriculture, and the stability of our climate.

Rescue centers take in injured, sick, or orphaned wildlife. Staff and volunteers nurse animals back to health and, when possible, prepare them for release back into the wild. For species that cannot be released, sanctuaries offer a safe home for life. [2] Releasing an animal back into the wild is a complex process that may take months or years, as the animal must learn to find food, avoid predators, and adapt to its natural environment again.

Breeding programs play a key role for species with very small populations. By carefully matching animals and raising young in protected environments, scientists can help increase numbers. Some species, such as the giant panda, have seen their populations grow thanks to such programs. [3] The ultimate goal is not to keep animals in captivity forever but to build up numbers until they can be safely returned to protected habitats in the wild.

Protecting habitats is equally important. Animals cannot survive if their homes are destroyed. Laws that ban logging in certain areas, create national parks, or restrict hunting can make a real difference. [4] Without safe places to live, breed, and find food, even the best rescue and breeding efforts may not be enough to save a species from extinction.

Everyone can contribute. Donating to conservation groups, avoiding products made from endangered species, and supporting laws that protect wildlife are all ways to help. [5] Every small action adds up, and together we can ensure that the next generation inherits a world that is still rich in wildlife and wonder.

## 选项

A. Saving endangered animals is not only about protecting individual species—it is about preserving the balance of ecosystems and the planet's biodiversity for future generations.
B. These efforts require patience, funding, and expertise, but they have given many animals a second chance at survival when they would otherwise have had none.
C. Success in breeding can eventually lead to reintroduction into the wild, restoring species to areas where they had once disappeared.
D. When habitats are preserved and restored, endangered species have a fighting chance to recover and thrive in the long term.
E. The fate of endangered animals depends on the choices we make today—whether to protect or to destroy, to care or to ignore—and those choices will echo for generations to come.
F. For example, the California condor was down to just 22 birds in the 1980s but has since been brought back from the brink through a captive breeding program.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述救助中心、庇护所、繁育项目的努力，空后说"Each species saved is a thread preserved in the web of life"。A的"preserving the balance of ecosystems and the planet's biodiversity"与空后"web of life... supports not only wildlife but also human health"呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说救治后尽可能放归野外、无法放归的提供终身庇护，空后说"Releasing an animal back into the wild is a complex process... must learn to find food, avoid predators"。B的"These efforts require patience, funding, and expertise... given many animals a second chance"与空后放归复杂性衔接。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说繁育项目助大熊猫等种群恢复，空后说"The ultimate goal is not to keep animals in captivity forever but to build up numbers until they can be safely returned"。C的"Success in breeding can eventually lead to reintroduction"与空后"returned to protected habitats"直接对应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前强调保护栖息地、立法的作用，空后说"Without safe places to live, breed, and find food... rescue and breeding efforts may not be enough"。D的"When habitats are preserved and restored... have a fighting chance to recover"与空后"Without safe places"形成对比呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说个人可采取的贡献方式，空后说"Every small action adds up... we can ensure that the next generation inherits a world... rich in wildlife"。E的"The fate of endangered animals depends on the choices we make today"与空后"Every small action"及"next generation"衔接。

  - **F. For example, the California condor was down to just 22 birds...**：以For example开头，属具体物种案例，各空均为概括或过渡句；易误放[3]，但[3]需的是繁育成功与野化放归的概括，非单一物种数据。
