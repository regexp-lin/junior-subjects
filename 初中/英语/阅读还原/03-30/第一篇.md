# 03-30 第一篇

**The Science of Dreams**

Have you ever woken up from a strange dream and wondered what it meant? People have been curious about dreams for thousands of years. [1] Modern science suggests that dreams may help our brains process emotions, sort through memories, and even prepare us for future challenges. While we sleep, our minds are still hard at work.

During the night, we go through different stages of sleep. The stage when most vivid dreams happen is called REM sleep, which stands for Rapid Eye Movement. [2] Your eyes move quickly under your eyelids even though they are closed. Scientists think this is when the brain is most active, creating the stories and images we experience as dreams.

Dreams often mix things we did during the day with older memories. This may be the brain's way of organizing information. [3] Some researchers believe that dreams help us practice dealing with difficult situations in a safe environment. For example, if you are worried about a test, you might dream about it and wake up feeling more prepared.

Not everyone remembers their dreams. [4] Writing down your dreams as soon as you wake up can improve your dream recall over time. The more you pay attention to your dreams, the easier it becomes to remember them.

[5] Whether you remember your dreams or not, they are a normal and healthy part of sleep. Understanding a little about how dreams work can help you appreciate this fascinating part of being human.

## 选项

A. In fact, you can learn to remember more of your dreams with a simple habit.
B. In short, dreams are a normal and fascinating part of sleep that scientists are still learning about.
C. Today, we have some answers, though many questions remain.
D. During this stage, your body is relaxed, but your brain is busy.
E. As a result, everyone should keep a dream journal to become more creative.
F. Dreams may help us work through worries and feelings we have not fully dealt with in our waking life.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] C   [2] D   [3] F   [4] A   [5] B

## 深度解析

- **[1] C**：
  - **线索**：承上启下与话题引入。空前指出人类对梦好奇数千年。C选项"Today, we have some answers, though many questions remain"（今天我们有了一些答案，尽管很多问题仍未解开）用"Today"承接历史，"some answers"自然引出空后"Modern science suggests..."的具体科学发现，形成"古人对梦好奇→今有答案→科学解释"的递进逻辑。

- **[2] D**：
  - **线索**：段落内解释与生理描述。空前介绍REM睡眠阶段。D选项"During this stage, your body is relaxed, but your brain is busy"（在此阶段，身体放松但大脑忙碌）准确描述REM特征。空后"Your eyes move quickly..."补充眼部活动细节，与D中"brain is busy"呼应——大脑活跃时眼睛才会快速转动。逻辑连贯。

- **[3] F**：
  - **线索**：同义复述与功能解释。空前说梦可能帮助我们在安全环境中练习应对困难情境，举例"worried about a test"。F选项"Dreams may help us work through worries and feelings we have not fully dealt with in our waking life"（梦可能帮助我们处理清醒时尚未完全应对的担忧与情绪）中"work through worries"与前文"practice dealing with difficult situations"同义，"worries"与"worried about a test"直接对应。空后举例是F的延伸。

- **[4] A**：
  - **线索**：问题—解决与习惯建议。空前提出"Not everyone remembers their dreams"。A选项"In fact, you can learn to remember more of your dreams with a simple habit"（事实上，你可以通过一个简单习惯学会记住更多梦）用"In fact"转折，给出可行方法。空后"Writing down your dreams...can improve your dream recall"正是"simple habit"的具体内容，衔接紧密。

- **[5] B**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与总结呼应。空[5]位于结尾段段首，前文已从科学解释、REM睡眠、情绪处理、梦的回忆等多方面介绍梦。B选项"In short, dreams are a normal and fascinating part of sleep that scientists are still learning about"（简言之，梦是睡眠中正常而迷人的一部分，科学家们仍在不断探索）用"In short"总结全文。"normal and fascinating"与空后"normal and healthy part of sleep""fascinating part of being human"形成同义呼应；"scientists are still learning about"与全文"many questions remain"基调一致，收束得体。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **E. As a result, everyone should keep a dream journal to become more creative.**：含"dream journal"与第四段"Writing down your dreams"相关，但全文只说写梦可提高回忆（dream recall），从未提及"become more creative"。"everyone should"过于绝对，且"As a result"暗示前文有因果铺垫，但[5]位于全文结尾，前文无此结论性因果。属"偷换概念+绝对化+逻辑错位"型干扰。
