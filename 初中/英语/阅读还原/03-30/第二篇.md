# 03-30 第二篇

**The Power of Kindness**

When someone holds the door open for you or says a warm "thank you," it can make your day a little brighter. Kindness is a simple act, but it has a powerful effect on both the giver and the receiver. [1] Studies show that when people perform acts of kindness, their brains release chemicals that make them feel happy and connected to others. Being kind is good for your health in more ways than one.

Kindness does not have to be something big. Small actions matter—helping a classmate with a difficult question, sharing your lunch, or simply smiling at someone who looks sad. [2] These simple gestures can turn a person's bad day into a better one. You never know when a small act might mean the world to someone else.

Sometimes we forget to be kind to ourselves. We are quick to help others but hard on ourselves when we make mistakes. [3] Treating yourself with the same patience and understanding you show to friends can improve your mood and reduce stress. Self-kindness is not selfish; it helps you be a better person for everyone around you.

Kindness can also spread from one person to another. When you are kind to someone, they are more likely to be kind to someone else. [4] This creates a chain of positivity that can reach far beyond what you see. One good deed can inspire many more.

[5] Start with something small today—compliment a friend, help at home, or write a kind note to someone who helped you. You might be surprised by how good it makes you feel, and the world could use a little more kindness.

## 选项

A. In fact, kindness often works like a ripple in water—one action leads to another.
B. Therefore, schools should add a kindness class to the curriculum and grade students on it.
C. The good news is that kindness benefits both sides.
D. What seems small to you may be exactly what another person needed.
E. If you want to make a difference, you do not have to wait for a special occasion.
F. Remember that you deserve compassion too, especially when things do not go as planned.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] C   [2] D   [3] F   [4] A   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] C**：
  - **线索**：总领衔接与同义呼应。空前介绍善意的双重效果——对给予者与接收者都有益。C选项"The good news is that kindness benefits both sides"（好消息是善意对双方都有益）直接概括空前的"powerful effect on both the giver and the receiver"。空后"Studies show...their brains release chemicals...feel happy"从科学角度展开说明"benefits"的具体表现，逻辑顺畅。

- **[2] D**：
  - **线索**：举例总结与递进强调。空前列举小事——帮同学、分享午餐、对悲伤者微笑。D选项"What seems small to you may be exactly what another person needed"（对你来说的小事，可能正是别人所需要的）紧扣"small actions matter"主题。空后"turn a person's bad day into a better one""mean the world to someone"正是"exactly what another person needed"的生动体现。

- **[3] F**：
  - **线索**：主题转换与自我善意。空前指出我们常对他人仁慈却对自己苛刻。F选项"Remember that you deserve compassion too, especially when things do not go as planned"（记住你也值得同情，尤其当事情不如预期时）直接回应"forget to be kind to ourselves"。空后"Treating yourself with the same patience...you show to friends"与F中"compassion""when things do not go as planned"（即犯错、不顺时）完美对应。

- **[4] A**：
  - **线索**：比喻说明与连锁效应。空前描述善意从一人传向另一人。A选项"In fact, kindness often works like a ripple in water—one action leads to another"（事实上，善意常像水中的涟漪——一个举动引发另一个）用"ripple"比喻生动阐释传播机制。空后"This creates a chain of positivity""One good deed can inspire many more"与A中"one action leads to another"同义呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：全文收束与行动号召。空[5]位于结尾段段首，前文已论证善意的健康益处、小事意义、自我善意与连锁效应。E选项"If you want to make a difference, you do not have to wait for a special occasion"（若想带来改变，不必等待特殊场合）破除"要等大机会"的顾虑。空后"Start with something small today—compliment a friend, help at home..."正是"do not have to wait"的实操体现，衔接自然。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **B. Therefore, schools should add a kindness class to the curriculum and grade students on it.**：含"kindness""schools""students"等词，但全文从个人角度倡导善意，从未讨论学校课程改革。"add a class""grade students"属"过度引申+偷换主语"型干扰——将个人选择偷换为教育政策，且"grade"与善意自愿本质相悖。
