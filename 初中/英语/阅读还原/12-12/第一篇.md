**The Silk Road: An Ancient Bridge Between East and West**

The Silk Road was not a single road but a network of trade routes that connected China with the Mediterranean world for over a thousand years. Silk, spices, tea, and other goods traveled along these paths. Ideas, religions, and technologies also moved between cultures. The name "Silk Road" comes from the valuable silk that China exported to the West. This exchange shaped civilizations on both ends of the route. [1]

The journey was long and dangerous. Merchants faced deserts, mountains, and harsh weather. They often traveled in caravans for safety. Camels were the main animals used to carry heavy loads across dry regions. Bandits sometimes attacked travelers, so protection was important. Despite these challenges, trade continued for centuries. [2]

Trade was not the only thing that spread. Buddhism traveled from India through Central Asia to China. Paper and printing techniques moved westward. The compass helped sailors on the seas beyond the land routes. Languages and customs influenced one another. The Silk Road was a channel for cultural exchange as much as for goods. [3]

Today, the Silk Road still matters. China's "Belt and Road" initiative seeks to revive connections between Asia and Europe. Ancient cities along the route attract tourists interested in history. Scholars study old texts and artifacts to understand past trade and culture. The spirit of exchange lives on in modern forms. [4]

The Silk Road reminds us that people have always sought to connect across great distances. Trade brought wealth, but it also brought understanding between different peoples. The routes that once carried silk and spices now carry ideas and cooperation. [5]

## 选项

A. The Silk Road was more than a trade route—it linked distant lands and allowed goods, ideas, and cultures to flow between East and West.
B. Travelers had to overcome many difficulties, yet they kept the routes open and active.
C. In this way, the Silk Road became a path not only for merchants but for the spread of knowledge and beliefs.
D. Its legacy continues to shape how we think about global connection and cultural exchange.
E. This history shows that human societies have long valued trade and cultural exchange across borders.
F. For example, the city of Dunhuang in western China was an important stop on the Silk Road and is famous for its Mogao Caves.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍丝绸之路是贸易路线网、连接中国与地中海、丝绸香料茶叶等流通、思想宗教技术传播、名称由来、交流塑造文明，空后需总结丝绸之路的综合性质。A的"more than a trade route—it linked distant lands and allowed goods, ideas, and cultures to flow between East and West"与前文多重作用呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说旅途漫长危险、商人面对沙漠高山恶劣天气、结队而行、骆驼驮货、匪徒威胁、尽管如此贸易持续数百年，空后需总结克服困难与坚持。B的"had to overcome many difficulties, yet they kept the routes open and active"与前文对应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说不止商品传播、佛教东传、纸张印刷西传、指南针助航海、语言习俗互相影响、丝绸之路是文化通道，空后需总结文化与知识的传播。C的"In this way"承接前文，"path not only for merchants but for the spread of knowledge and beliefs"与前文呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说丝绸之路今日仍重要、一带一路复兴亚欧连接、古城吸引游客、学者研究历史、交流精神延续，空后需总结当代遗产。D的"Its legacy continues to shape how we think about global connection and cultural exchange"与前文对应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说丝绸之路提醒人们自古追求远距离连接、贸易带来财富与理解、昔日运送丝绸香料的路如今运送思想与合作，空后需总结历史意义。E的"This history shows that human societies have long valued trade and cultural exchange across borders"与前文呼应。
