# 12-02 第一篇

**Tree Planting: Growing a Greener Future**

Trees are often called the lungs of the Earth because they absorb carbon dioxide and release oxygen. They provide shade, reduce soil erosion, and offer homes for wildlife. Yet forests around the world are shrinking due to logging, fires, and land clearing. Tree planting is one of the most direct ways for people to help restore nature and fight climate change. Every tree planted is a step toward a healthier planet. [1]

Planting a tree may seem simple, but doing it correctly matters. The right time to plant depends on the climate—spring and autumn are usually best. Choosing native species helps the trees survive and support local ecosystems. The hole should be deep enough for the roots, and the soil should be kept moist after planting. A little care in the beginning leads to a strong, long-lived tree. [2]

Schools, communities, and organizations often hold tree-planting events. These events bring people together and teach both children and adults about the importance of trees. Participants learn how to plant, what species suit their area, and why forests matter. [3]

The benefits of tree planting reach far beyond the immediate area. Trees cool cities in summer by providing shade and releasing water vapor. They absorb stormwater and reduce flooding. In rural areas, tree belts protect farmland from wind and help keep soil in place. [4]

Governments and NGOs have launched large-scale tree-planting programs. Some countries aim to plant billions of trees over the next decade. While big projects matter, individual action counts too—planting a tree in your yard, joining a local event, or supporting tree-planting charities. [5]

## 选项

A. Tree planting is a powerful and hopeful act that anyone can take to protect the environment and leave a positive legacy for future generations.
B. With proper planning and care, each planted tree has a better chance to grow and contribute to the ecosystem for decades.
C. Through these activities, tree planting becomes not only an environmental action but also a social and educational experience.
D. In both cities and countryside, trees provide services that make our lives safer, healthier, and more sustainable.
E. Whether through large programs or small personal efforts, every tree planted helps build a greener, more resilient world.
F. For example, the Miyawaki method uses dense planting of native species to create mini-forests that grow quickly.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：

- **[2] B**：

- **[3] C**：
