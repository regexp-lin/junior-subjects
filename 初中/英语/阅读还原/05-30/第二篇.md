# 05-30 第二篇

**Family Reading Time: Sharing Books Together**

Many families are busy. Parents work, children have homework, and everyone spends time on screens. Yet setting aside time to read together can strengthen family bonds and improve children's skills. Family reading time means parents and children sit down with a book—either one person reads aloud or everyone reads quietly in the same room. [1] That is because shared reading creates connection and builds literacy. When parents read to young children, they help develop vocabulary and imagination. When older kids read with parents, they can discuss ideas and learn together. Studies show that children who are read to at home do better in school. In short, family reading is not just about books—it is about time, attention, and love. It is one of the best gifts parents can give their children.

When is the best time for family reading? [2] In other words, any regular time can work—before bed, after dinner, or on weekend mornings. The key is to make it a habit. Many families read together for 15 to 20 minutes each night. Some choose a book that everyone enjoys and take turns reading chapters. Others let each person read their own book but share what they liked afterward. The time does not need to be long—what matters is that it is consistent. When reading becomes part of the daily routine, children begin to look forward to it. A regular reading time signals that books and family both matter. Small habits lead to big results.

What kind of books should families read? [3] That is why the best books are those that interest everyone. For young children, picture books with bright colors and simple stories work well. For older kids, try adventure stories, mysteries, or non-fiction about topics they enjoy. Parents can let children pick books sometimes—this makes them more eager to read. It is also fine to reread favorite books. The goal is to create positive feelings about reading, not to finish a certain number of books. For this reason, family reading should feel like fun, not like school. When children associate reading with joy, they become lifelong readers. The right book can open doors.

How can busy families find time to read? [4] It is true that even 10 minutes a day makes a difference. Parents can read during breakfast or on the way to school if they drive. Families can swap screen time for reading time—for example, read for 20 minutes before turning on the TV. Some families keep a "reading basket" with books in the living room so that picking up a book is easy. The key is to treat reading as a priority, not an extra. When parents model reading—by reading their own books—children notice. Kids learn that reading is a normal, enjoyable part of life. Small steps add up to a reading culture at home.

[5] It builds bonds, boosts skills, and creates memories. Whether you read picture books with a toddler or novels with a teen, the time together matters. So the next time you have a free moment, reach for a book and invite your family to join you. Remember: the best stories are the ones we share with the people we love.
