**The Educational Value of Housework**

Many parents wonder whether children should do housework. Some think that kids should focus only on schoolwork and that chores take away precious study time. However, research suggests that children who help at home often perform better academically and develop important life skills. [1] Doing chores teaches responsibility, teamwork, and practical abilities that textbooks cannot provide. These benefits last far beyond childhood.

One of the most valuable lessons from housework is time management. When children have regular chores, they must learn to balance homework, play, and household tasks. [2] This practice helps them plan their day and understand that time is limited. They discover how to set priorities and avoid procrastination. These skills are essential for success in high school, college, and later in their careers.

Chores also build a sense of belonging. When everyone in the family contributes, the home runs more smoothly. [3] Children feel that they are part of a team and that their efforts matter. This boosts their self-esteem and teaches them that cooperation is important. It also reduces the burden on parents and creates a fairer division of labor at home.

Some parents worry that chores might be too hard or boring for their kids. [4] The key is to assign age-appropriate tasks and to offer praise and encouragement. Young children can fold clothes or set the table; teenagers can cook meals or manage the family budget. Starting small and gradually increasing responsibility helps children grow without feeling overwhelmed.

[5] Housework is not a punishment—it is preparation for life. The habits formed in childhood often shape who we become as adults. By involving children in chores, parents give them tools for independence and success. Whether they live alone someday or share a home with others, the ability to cook, clean, and organize will serve them well.

## 选项

A. Chores develop skills and character traits that formal education alone cannot offer.
B. Children learn to organize their time and recognize the importance of planning ahead.
C. Contributing to the household gives children a sense of purpose and strengthens family bonds.
D. Choosing the right tasks and supporting children emotionally makes chores beneficial rather than burdensome.
E. The skills gained from doing chores prepare children for a successful and independent adulthood.
F. For example, washing dishes can teach children about cleanliness and the importance of hygiene.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前指出做家务的孩子学业更好、生活技能更强；空后强调“这些好处远不止于童年”。A项“家务培养责任、合作和课本无法提供的实践能力”承上概括家务的教育意义，启下引出长期受益，衔接自然。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前讲家务让孩子学会平衡学习、玩耍和家庭任务；空后写“他们学会安排日程、理解时间有限、分清主次、减少拖延”。B项“孩子学会安排时间并认识到提前规划的重要性”准确概括本段核心，与前后一致。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前讲家庭成员共同贡献使家庭运转顺畅；空后写孩子感到自己是团队一员、付出有意义，从而提升自尊、学会合作。C项“为家庭做贡献让孩子有目标感并增强家庭纽带”概括归属感与团队意识，逻辑吻合。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前提到家长担心家务太难或无聊；空后建议分配合适年龄的任务、给予表扬鼓励，并举不同年龄段例子。D项“选择合适任务并在情感上支持孩子，能使家务有益而非负担”概括解决顾虑的方法。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前为第四段；空后强调家务是人生准备、童年习惯影响成年、做家务赋予独立与成功的能力。E项“家务中获得的能力为孩子成年后的成功与独立做好准备”作为结尾主题句，总结全文。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, washing dishes can teach children about cleanliness and the importance of hygiene.**：以For example开头，为单一任务举例。虽与家务教育相关，但仅涉及洗碗这一细节，无法承担[1]–[5]的概括功能；层次低于段落主题句，易误选为[1]或[4]。
