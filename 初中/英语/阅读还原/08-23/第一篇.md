**Polar Ice Melting: A Global Warning**

The polar regions are often called the "refrigerators" of our planet. They hold massive amounts of ice that help regulate Earth's temperature and weather patterns. Over the past few decades, scientists have observed an alarming trend: ice in the Arctic and Antarctica is melting at an unprecedented rate. [1] This loss of ice affects not only the poles but also people and wildlife across the globe. Understanding why it happens and what we can do is urgent for our future.

The main cause of polar ice melting is global warming. Human activities, especially burning fossil fuels, release large amounts of greenhouse gases into the atmosphere. [2] These gases trap heat from the sun and cause the average temperature of the Earth to rise. As the air and ocean warm, ice that has existed for thousands of years begins to break apart and melt. The process is faster in the Arctic, but Antarctica's ice sheets are also at risk.

When polar ice melts, sea levels rise. Coastal cities and island nations face the threat of flooding. [3] Millions of people may have to leave their homes as shorelines disappear. Wildlife such as polar bears and penguins lose their habitats and struggle to find food. Fisheries are affected as ocean currents and temperatures change. The impacts reach far beyond the frozen regions themselves.

Scientists warn that some changes may already be irreversible. [4] Even if we stop emitting greenhouse gases today, it would take decades for the climate to stabilize. However, slowing down emissions can still prevent the worst outcomes. Every year we delay action makes the problem harder to solve. The window for meaningful change is narrowing, but it is not yet closed.

[5] We all have a role to play in protecting our planet. Reducing energy use, choosing sustainable transport, and supporting policies that limit carbon emissions can make a difference. Young people can raise awareness in their communities and push leaders to act. The fate of the polar ice is connected to the choices we make today.

  - **线索**：空前讲燃烧化石燃料释放温室气体；空后说“随着大气和海洋变暖，存在数千年的冰开始破裂融化”。B项“这些气体通过阻挡热量散逸而使地球升温”准确解释温室效应机制，与前后逻辑一致。
