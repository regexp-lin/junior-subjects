**The Intelligence of Crows**

When people think of smart animals, they usually picture dolphins, monkeys, or dogs. [1] However, scientists have discovered that a common black bird is actually one of the smartest creatures on Earth. Crows (乌鸦), often seen in cities and parks, possess (拥有) surprisingly high levels of intelligence.

One of the most impressive abilities of crows is their skill in making and using tools. While many animals use objects they find, crows can actually change them. [2] For example, a crow might bend a straight piece of wire into a hook to pull an insect out of a deep hole. This shows advanced problem-solving skills.

Even more amazingly, crows have excellent memories, especially when it comes to human faces. [3] If a person treats a crow badly, the bird will remember that face for years. The crow might even warn its friends, gathering a large group to scold the unfriendly human whenever they appear.

Crows also understand basic rules of physics. In Japan, some clever crows have learned how to safely crack open hard nuts by using cars. [4] Then, they wait perfectly on the sidewalk until the traffic light turns red to safely collect their tasty meal.

[5] Their amazing tool-making, powerful memory, and clear understanding of their environment prove that brain size is not the only thing that matters.

E. Therefore, it is no surprise that scientists often use them to send messages.

# 03-16 第二篇

**The Intelligence of Crows**

When people think of smart animals, they usually picture dolphins, monkeys, or dogs. [1] However, scientists have discovered that a common black bird is actually one of the smartest creatures on Earth. Crows (乌鸦), often seen in cities and parks, possess (拥有) surprisingly high levels of intelligence.

One of the most impressive abilities of crows is their skill in making and using tools. While many animals use objects they find, crows can actually change them. [2] For example, a crow might bend a straight piece of wire into a hook to pull an insect out of a deep hole. This shows advanced problem-solving skills.

Even more amazingly, crows have excellent memories, especially when it comes to human faces. [3] If a person treats a crow badly, the bird will remember that face for years. The crow might even warn its friends, gathering a large group to scold the unfriendly human whenever they appear.

Crows also understand basic rules of physics. In Japan, some clever crows have learned how to safely crack open hard nuts by using cars. [4] Then, they wait perfectly on the sidewalk until the traffic light turns red to safely collect their tasty meal.

[5] Their amazing tool-making, powerful memory, and clear understanding of their environment prove that brain size is not the only thing that matters.

## 选项

A. They drop the nuts onto a busy road and let the passing cars crush them.
B. Rarely does anyone consider a bird to be among the most intelligent.
C. In short, we should definitely show more respect to these remarkable birds.
D. Research shows they never forget a person who has either helped or harmed them.
E. Therefore, people in Japan usually avoid driving cars when birds are walking.
F. If a tool is not quite right for the job, they will reshape it until it works perfectly.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] B   [2] F   [3] D   [4] A   [5] C

## 深度解析

- **[1] B**：
  - **线索**：对比转折与语境递进。空前说人们想到聪明动物就想到海豚、猴子或狗。B选项“Rarely does anyone consider a bird to be among the most intelligent”（极少有人会认为鸟类是最聪明的之一）作为一个倒装句，刚好承接了前句的常规认知（没有提到鸟）。紧随其后的“However”（然而），自然地引出了作者的核心反转论点：科学家发现一种普通的黑鸟（鸟类的具体代表乌鸦）实际上是地球上最聪明的生物之一。
- **[2] F**：
  - **线索**：同义解释与具体举例。空前强调了乌鸦能“change them（改变物体/工具）”。F选项“If a tool is not quite right for the job, they will reshape it until it works perfectly”（如果工具不太适合这项工作，它们会重塑它直到完美运作）正是对“改变（change）”这一核心动作的深入阐释，“reshape（重塑）”是极好的同义替换。随后空后的“For example（例如）”具体描述了乌鸦把直铁丝弄弯成钩子的事实，这是对F选项的完美实证。
- **[3] D**：
  - **线索**：主旨呼应与细节铺垫。本段的首句提出了主旨：“乌鸦拥有出色的记忆力，尤其是关于人脸的（human faces）”。D选项“Research shows they never forget a person who has either helped or harmed them”（研究表明它们永远不会忘记帮助过或伤害过它们的人）既承接了前面“对人记忆极好”的论断，又精准提出了“帮助或伤害过（helped or harmed）”这两个对比面。而空后紧咬着“If a person treats a crow badly（如果有人对乌鸦不好/伤害它们）”进行了具体的恶劣后果描写，严丝合缝。
- **[4] A**：
  - **线索**：动作顺承与因果逻辑。空前说到日本的乌鸦学会了巧妙地利用汽车来砸开坚硬的坚果（by using cars）。但具体怎么用的？A选项“They drop the nuts onto a busy road and let the passing cars crush them”（它们把坚果扔到繁忙的路上，让过往的汽车压碎它们）详细陈述了这一惊人操作的分解动作。这直接为接下来的空后一句铺路：“Then（然后），它们在人行道上完美等待，直到红灯亮起，再安全地收集它们美味的食物”。
- **[5] C**：
  - **线索**：总结全文与情感升华。空[5]位于文章结束段段首。C选项“In short, we should definitely show more respect to these remarkable birds”（简而言之，我们绝对应该对这些非凡的鸟类表现出更多的尊重）给出了总结性的态度建议（多尊重乌鸦）。之所以要尊重它，紧接着的空后一句进行了全篇论点的大汇总：“它们惊人的工具制造、强大的记忆力和对环境清晰的理解（分别对应文章2、3、4段）证明了大脑的大小并不是唯一重要的东西”。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **E. Therefore, people in Japan usually avoid driving cars when birds are walking.**：包含高频词汇“Therefore（因此）”、“Japan（日本）”、“cars（汽车）”、“birds（鸟）”。如果考生只盯着第四段的词汇找，可能会选它。但它的句意是“因此，当鸟儿在走时，日本人通常避免开汽车”。这不仅违背常识，也完全背离了文章“夸赞乌鸦非常聪明，懂得利用红绿灯和汽车（而不是让人躲乌鸦）”的主线逻辑。这属于移花接木式的干扰。
