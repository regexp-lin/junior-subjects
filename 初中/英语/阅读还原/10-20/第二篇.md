# 10-20 第二篇

**The Value of Folk Tales**

Folk tales are stories that have been passed down from one generation to the next, often by word of mouth. They appear in every culture—heroes who defeat monsters, clever animals who outsmart humans, and lessons about being brave, kind, or honest. Many of these stories are hundreds or thousands of years old. [1] Folk tales are more than old stories; they carry the wisdom, values, and traditions of the people who created them, and they remain important today.

One reason folk tales matter is that they teach morals. Through the adventures of characters, children learn about right and wrong. A story might show why lying leads to trouble, or why helping others brings rewards. [2] These lessons are often wrapped in exciting plots and memorable characters, so children absorb the message without feeling they are being lectured.

Folk tales also connect people to their past. They tell us how our ancestors lived, what they feared, and what they hoped for. They preserve languages, customs, and beliefs that might otherwise be forgotten. [3] When grandparents share folk tales with their grandchildren, they pass on not just stories but a sense of identity and belonging to a shared history.

In a world of TV and the internet, folk tales might seem old-fashioned. Yet they still have power. Writers and filmmakers draw on folk tales for new books and movies. Psychologists study how these stories shape the way we think. [4] Folk tales have proven their ability to adapt and stay relevant, no matter how much the world changes.

We should not take folk tales for granted. Some languages and cultures are disappearing, and with them, unique stories. Efforts to record, translate, and share folk tales help keep them alive. [5] By preserving and passing on these stories, we ensure that future generations can enjoy their magic, learn from their wisdom, and stay connected to the rich traditions of the past.

## 选项

A. Folk tales are a bridge between the past and the present, and they continue to shape how we understand ourselves and the world.
B. Stories use the power of narrative to teach important lessons in a way that is both entertaining and easy to remember.
C. Folk tales are a living link to our roots—they help us understand where we come from and who we are.
D. From books to films to psychology, folk tales remain a source of inspiration and insight for people everywhere.
E. Protecting folk tales is a way of protecting our cultural heritage, so that the wisdom of the past is not lost.
F. For example, the story of Cinderella exists in hundreds of versions around the world, from China to Europe to Africa, each with its own local details and characters.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍民间故事的定义和普遍性，空后说"Folk tales are more than old stories; they carry the wisdom, values, and traditions of the people who created them, and they remain important today"。A的"Folk tales are a bridge between the past and the present, and they continue to shape how we understand ourselves and the world"与空后wisdom、values、traditions、remain important呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说民间故事通过角色冒险传递是非观，空后说"These lessons are often wrapped in exciting plots and memorable characters, so children absorb the message without feeling they are being lectured"。B的"Stories use the power of narrative to teach important lessons in a way that is both entertaining and easy to remember"与空后"wrapped in... plots and characters"、"absorb without being lectured"呼应。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说民间故事连接人们与过去、保留语言习俗，空后说"When grandparents share folk tales with their grandchildren, they pass on not just stories but a sense of identity and belonging to a shared history"。C的"Folk tales are a living link to our roots—they help us understand where we come from and who we are"与空后identity、belonging、shared history衔接。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说在电视网络时代民间故事看似过时，但作家电影人仍从中取材、心理学家研究其影响，空后说"Folk tales have proven their ability to adapt and stay relevant, no matter how much the world changes"。D的"From books to films to psychology, folk tales remain a source of inspiration and insight for people everywhere"与空后adapt、stay relevant呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说部分语言文化在消失、需记录翻译分享以保护民间故事，空后说"By preserving and passing on these stories, we ensure that future generations can enjoy their magic, learn from their wisdom, and stay connected to the rich traditions"。E的"Protecting folk tales is a way of protecting our cultural heritage, so that the wisdom of the past is not lost"与空后preserving、future generations、wisdom呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the story of Cinderella exists in hundreds of versions around the world...**：以For example开头，是具体故事举例。各空均需概括性或过渡性句子，F为举例型干扰项。
