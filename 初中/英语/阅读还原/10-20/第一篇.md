# 10-20 第一篇

**3D Printing Technology**

3D printing is a way of making objects by building them layer by layer, instead of cutting them from a block of material. A computer sends instructions to the printer, which then creates the object from plastic, metal, or other materials. The technology has grown quickly over the past decade. [1] 3D printing is changing how we design, make, and repair things—from toys and tools to medical devices and even parts of buildings.

In medicine, 3D printing has brought important advances. Doctors can now print models of a patient's organs to plan surgery. Some hospitals use 3D printers to create custom prosthetics—artificial arms or legs—that fit each patient perfectly. Researchers are even testing 3D-printed tissue and organs for future use. [2] What once seemed like science fiction is now becoming a reality in hospitals and laboratories around the world.

Schools and businesses are also using 3D printers. Students can design and print their own projects, learning about engineering and creativity. Small companies can make prototypes—test versions of products—without expensive machinery. [3] 3D printing makes it easier and cheaper to turn ideas into real objects, which helps both education and innovation.

There are challenges, too. 3D printing can be slow, and some materials are expensive. Not everything can be printed yet. Some people worry about safety—for example, could someone print a weapon? [4] Like any new technology, 3D printing has both benefits and risks, and society will need to find the right balance as it becomes more common.

The future of 3D printing looks promising. Printers are getting faster and cheaper. New materials are being developed. One day, we might print food, houses, or even replacement body parts. [5] As the technology improves, 3D printing could transform how we live, work, and take care of our health in ways we cannot fully imagine today.

## 选项

A. Today, 3D printing is used in many fields, and its applications keep expanding as the technology improves.
B. Medical 3D printing has moved from an experimental idea to a practical tool that saves lives and improves patient care.
C. The ability to quickly turn a digital design into a physical object lowers the cost of trying new ideas and encourages more people to create and invent.
D. These questions remind us that new technologies bring new responsibilities, and we must use 3D printing wisely and safely.
E. The possibilities seem endless, and the next ten years may bring changes we can hardly predict.
F. For example, NASA has used 3D printers on the International Space Station to make tools and parts that would be too expensive to ship from Earth.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍3D打印原理和近年发展，空后说"3D printing is changing how we design, make, and repair things—from toys and tools to medical devices and even parts of buildings"。A的"Today, 3D printing is used in many fields, and its applications keep expanding"与空后各领域应用、变革设计制造方式呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前举医学应用（器官模型、定制假肢、组织器官研究），空后说"What once seemed like science fiction is now becoming a reality in hospitals and laboratories"。B的"Medical 3D printing has moved from an experimental idea to a practical tool that saves lives"与空后"science fiction→reality"完美衔接。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前列举挑战（速度慢、材料贵、安全担忧如打印武器），空后说"Like any new technology, 3D printing has both benefits and risks, and society will need to find the right balance"。D的"These questions remind us that new technologies bring new responsibilities, and we must use 3D printing wisely and safely"与空后risks、balance、responsibility呼应。
