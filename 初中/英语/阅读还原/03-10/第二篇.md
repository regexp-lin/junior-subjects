# 03-10 第二篇

**Why We Need to Make Mistakes**

Nobody likes making mistakes. Whenever we get a bad grade on a test or say the wrong thing to a friend, we often feel embarrassed and disappointed. [1] However, scientists and psychologists (心理学家) tell us that making mistakes is actually a necessary part of human life. 

First, mistakes are our best teachers. Think about how a baby learns to walk. [2] But they don't just stay on the floor; they figure out what went wrong and try again. Similarly, when we fail at a math problem, we can examine our errors to understand the math rules much better. [3]

Second, making mistakes teaches us to be humble (谦逊的). When we are perfect at everything, it is easy to become too proud and stop listening to others. [4] This helps us build stronger relationships, because nobody likes to be around someone who thinks they know everything.

Finally, without mistakes, there would be no amazing discoveries in the world. Many famous inventions, like the microwave and the pacemaker (心脏起搏器), were actually created by accident when scientists were looking for something else. [5] If they had been afraid of getting things wrong, modern technology would not exist today. Therefore, the next time you mess up, simply smile and learn from it!

## 选项

A. They trip and fall down hundreds of times before taking their first steps.
B. On the contrary, realizing our own flaws makes us more open to advice.
C. It proves that failure is the only reason why people succeed in life.
D. We try our best to hide them from others so we do not look foolish.
E. In a word, true learning happens when we correct the things we did wrong.
F. Their "failures" turned out to be some of the greatest gifts to humans.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] D   [2] A   [3] E   [4] B   [5] F

## 深度解析

- **[1] D**：
  - **线索**：指代关系与逻辑延续。空前提到了犯错时我们常感到尴尬或失望（feel embarrassed and disappointed）。D选项“We try our best to hide them from others so we do not look foolish”（我们尽最大努力向别人隐瞒它们以免看起来很愚蠢）中，“them”指代前文的“mistakes”，且“hide them... not look foolish”是对前文“尴尬”这种心理的直接行为反应。空后一句以“However（然而）”转折，引出心理学家认为犯错是必要的反调，前后承接完美。
- **[2] A**：
  - **线索**：举例印证与语境照应。空前一句提出了关于婴儿学步的例子：“Think about how a baby learns to walk”（想想婴儿是如何学走路的）。A选项“They trip and fall down hundreds of times before taking their first steps”（在迈出第一步之前他们会绊倒并摔跤数百次）正是对婴儿学步的具体状态描述。同时，A项的“fall down”也为紧接着的一句“But they don't just stay on the floor（但他们绝不会一直呆在地板上）”提供了发生的情境前提。
- **[3] E**：
  - **线索**：段落总结与逻辑提炼。空前讲述了通过检查数学题的错误能更好地理解数学规则。E选项“In a word, true learning happens when we correct the things we did wrong”（总之，当我们纠正做错的事情时，真正的学习就发生了）使用了总结词“In a word”，完美地对第二段的主旨“mistakes are our best teachers（错误是我们最好的老师）”以及后面的具体例子进行了升华和归纳。
- **[4] B**：
  - **线索**：对比转折与词汇呼应。空前描述的是一种反面情况：“当我们在各方面都很完美时，很容易变得骄傲并不再听别人的”。B选项用“On the contrary（相反）”引出正面影响：“realizing our own flaws makes us more open to advice”（意识到我们自己的缺陷让我们愿意接受建议）。这里的“flaws（缺陷/错误）”呼应前文的犯错，而“open to advice”恰好是“stop listening to others”的反义，这也顺理成章地引出了空后“这（虚心）有助于我们建立更强的人际关系”。
- **[5] F**：
  - **线索**：同义替换与总结连贯。空前提到了许多著名的发明其实是科学家在寻找其它东西时意外创造的（如微波炉等）。F选项“Their 'failures' turned out to be some of the greatest gifts to humans”（他们的“失败”结果成了给人类最伟大的礼物之一）中，“Their”指代前文的“scientists”，“failures”加了引号，对应科学家“looking for something else（也就是最初目标的偏离/失败）”。“greatest gifts”也是对前文“famous inventions”的高度赞扬替换。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **C. It proves that failure is the only reason why people succeed in life.**：这一项极具迷惑性，因为它包含了“failure（失败）”和“succeed（成功）”这等看似吻合文章主题的高频词汇。但是，“the only reason（唯一的原因）”这种说法太过绝对和极端。文章阐述的是犯错有很多好处，能帮助我们学习、让人谦逊和带来意外发现，但从未说犯错/失败是成功的唯一原因（努力、天赋等同样重要）。基于过于绝对化的逻辑谬误，可断然排除。
