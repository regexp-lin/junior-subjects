# 09-15 第二篇

**Learning from Failure**

Nobody likes to fail. When we get a low grade, lose a game, or make a mistake, we often feel embarrassed, discouraged, or angry. Yet failure is a part of life—every successful person has faced setbacks along the way. [1] Understanding that failure can be a teacher, not just a punishment, is the first step toward turning setbacks into stepping stones for future success.

Failure shows us what does not work. When a plan fails, we learn which approach to avoid next time. Scientists often run hundreds of experiments before finding the right answer; each "failed" experiment narrows the search. [2] Viewing failure as useful feedback, rather than as proof that we are not good enough, changes how we approach challenges and keeps us from giving up too soon.

Failure also builds resilience. Each time we bounce back from a disappointment, we become stronger and more confident. People who have overcome difficulties often report that they learned more from their failures than from their easy wins. [3] The ability to recover from setbacks and try again is a skill that grows with practice and serves us well throughout life.

It is important to reflect on why we failed. Did we prepare enough? Did we miss something? Honest reflection helps us improve instead of repeating the same mistakes. [4] Taking time to understand what went wrong—without blaming ourselves too harshly—can turn a painful experience into a valuable lesson.

Failure is uncomfortable, but it does not have to define us. What defines us is how we respond: whether we give up or get back up, whether we hide from our mistakes or learn from them. [5] Embracing failure as part of the journey, not as the end of it, is what separates those who grow from those who stay stuck.

## 选项

A. Seeing failure as an opportunity to learn, rather than as a final judgment, opens the door to growth and improvement.
B. Treating mistakes as information rather than as personal attacks on our ability can fuel persistence and progress.
C. The strength we gain from overcoming failure often becomes one of our greatest assets in future endeavors.
D. Analyzing our failures honestly can reveal what we need to change and how we can do better next time.
E. How we choose to respond to failure—with courage and curiosity instead of fear and avoidance—shapes who we become.
F. For example, Thomas Edison tried thousands of materials before finding the right one for the light bulb filament.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前描述失败带来的负面情绪及成功者也曾失败，空后强调"Understanding that failure can be a teacher, not just a punishment, is the first step toward turning setbacks into stepping stones"。A的"Seeing failure as an opportunity to learn, rather than as a final judgment, opens the door to growth and improvement"概括对失败的积极认知，与"teacher"和"stepping stones"完美呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前讨论从失败中学到什么无效、科学家试错过程，空后说"Viewing failure as useful feedback, rather than as proof that we are not good enough, changes how we approach challenges"。B的"Treating mistakes as information rather than as personal attacks on our ability can fuel persistence and progress"与"useful feedback"和"keeps us from giving up"逻辑一致，概括该段主旨。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前讨论失败如何增强韧性、从失败中收获更多，空后说"The ability to recover from setbacks and try again is a skill that grows with practice and serves us well throughout life"。C的"The strength we gain from overcoming failure often becomes one of our greatest assets in future endeavors"准确概括韧性与未来收益的关系，与"grows with practice"呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前强调反思失败原因的重要性，空后说"Taking time to understand what went wrong—without blaming ourselves too harshly—can turn a painful experience into a valuable lesson"。D的"Analyzing our failures honestly can reveal what we need to change and how we can do better next time"与反思、改进的逻辑对应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前对比不同应对方式，空后强调"Embracing failure as part of the journey, not as the end of it, is what separates those who grow from those who stay stuck"。E的"How we choose to respond to failure—with courage and curiosity instead of fear and avoidance—shapes who we become"升华主题，与"how we respond"和"separates those who grow"完美对应，适合作全文收束。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, Thomas Edison tried thousands of materials before finding the right one for the light bulb filament.**：以For example开头，属具体历史人物举例；各空均为概括或过渡句，F与从失败中学习相关但为个案，易误放[2]，但该空需把失败视为反馈的概括性论述，非举例。
