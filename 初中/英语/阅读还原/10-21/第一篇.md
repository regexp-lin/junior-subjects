# 10-21 第一篇

**Shared Spaces in the City**

Cities are full of buildings, roads, and cars. As more people move to cities, space becomes scarce and expensive. One solution is to create shared spaces—places that many people can use instead of everyone having their own. Parks, libraries, and community centers are classic examples. [1] Shared spaces make cities more livable by giving people room to relax, meet others, and enjoy activities without needing to own or pay for private facilities.

Parks are perhaps the most familiar shared spaces. They offer green grass, trees, and fresh air in the middle of concrete and traffic. People can jog, children can play, and families can picnic together. [2] Parks provide something that is hard to find in crowded cities: a place where people can breathe, move freely, and feel a connection to nature.

Libraries have long been shared spaces for learning and reading. Anyone with a library card can borrow books, use computers, or attend free events. In recent years, some cities have added "tool libraries" where people can borrow equipment like drills or sewing machines. [3] Shared resources like these reduce waste and cost, and they give more people access to things they might not be able to afford on their own.

Some cities are experimenting with new kinds of shared spaces. "Pocket parks" are small green areas built on empty lots. "Shared streets" give equal space to walkers, cyclists, and cars. Community gardens let people grow food together. [4] These creative ideas show that even in dense cities, there are ways to create places that belong to everyone and improve quality of life.

Shared spaces require care and investment. They need to be safe, clean, and well-maintained. Cities must balance the needs of different users—quiet readers and noisy children, drivers and pedestrians. [5] When cities invest in shared spaces and manage them well, everyone benefits: neighborhoods become stronger, people feel more connected, and urban life becomes more enjoyable for all.

## 选项

A. Shared spaces bring people together and offer benefits that private spaces cannot provide—especially in crowded, fast-paced city life.
B. In a world of apartments and offices, parks offer a valuable escape—a chance to step outside and enjoy the outdoors.
C. Sharing instead of owning can save money, reduce clutter, and help build a more sustainable and fair society.
D. Creative urban design can turn overlooked corners of the city into welcoming, useful places for the community.
E. The success of shared spaces depends on good planning and care, and the rewards are shared by the whole community.
F. For example, the High Line in New York City is a park built on an old railway line above the streets, attracting millions of visitors each year.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍城市共享空间概念和传统类型（公园、图书馆、社区中心），空后说"Shared spaces make cities more livable by giving people room to relax, meet others, and enjoy activities without needing to own or pay for private facilities"。A的"Shared spaces bring people together and offer benefits that private spaces cannot provide—especially in crowded, fast-paced city life"与空后more livable、relax、meet、without owning呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说公园提供绿地、跑步、玩耍、野餐，空后说"Parks provide something that is hard to find in crowded cities: a place where people can breathe, move freely, and feel a connection to nature"。B的"In a world of apartments and offices, parks offer a valuable escape—a chance to step outside and enjoy the outdoors"与空后hard to find、breathe、nature衔接。注：正文"Lighter"应为"People"的笔误，但不影响选题。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说图书馆、工具图书馆等共享资源，空后说"Shared resources like these reduce waste and cost, and they give more people access to things they might not be able to afford on their own"。C的"Sharing instead of owning can save money, reduce clutter, and help build a more sustainable and fair society"与空后reduce waste and cost、access呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前举袖珍公园、共享街道、社区花园等新尝试，空后说"These creative ideas show that even in dense cities, there are ways to create places that belong to everyone and improve quality of life"。D的"Creative urban design can turn overlooked corners of the city into welcoming, useful places for the community"与空后creative ideas、dense cities、belong to everyone呼应。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说共享空间需维护、需平衡不同使用者需求，空后说"When cities invest in shared spaces and manage them well, everyone benefits: neighborhoods become stronger, people feel more connected"。E的"The success of shared spaces depends on good planning and care, and the rewards are shared by the whole community"与空后invest、manage、everyone benefits呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the High Line in New York City is a park built on an old railway line...**：以For example开头，是具体地点案例。各空均需概括性或过渡性句子，F为举例型干扰项。
