# 10-21 第二篇

**The Extinction of Dinosaurs**

Dinosaurs ruled the Earth for about 165 million years. They were the largest and most diverse land animals of their time. Then, about 66 million years ago, they disappeared. The extinction of the dinosaurs is one of the greatest mysteries in Earth's history. [1] Scientists have spent decades trying to understand what caused these powerful creatures to vanish—and the answer has changed how we think about life on Earth.

For a long time, people had many different ideas. Some thought dinosaurs died from diseases. Others suggested that they could not adapt to a changing climate. [2] In the 1980s, scientists found strong evidence that changed everything: a giant asteroid had hit the Earth, causing widespread destruction and long-term changes to the environment.

The asteroid strike would have been catastrophic. It crashed into what is now Mexico, creating a huge crater. The impact sent dust and debris high into the atmosphere, blocking sunlight for months or years. Without enough light, plants died, and the food chain collapsed. [3] The asteroid did not kill every dinosaur at once—instead, it triggered a chain of events that made the world uninhabitable for large reptiles and many other species.

Not all life disappeared. Some small mammals, birds, and reptiles survived. These animals were able to hide in burrows, eat seeds, or adapt to the dark, cold world. Over millions of years, mammals evolved and spread. [4] The extinction of the dinosaurs opened the door for mammals to become the dominant animals on land—and eventually, for humans to evolve.

Studying the dinosaur extinction helps us understand how life on Earth can change suddenly. It also reminds us that asteroids still exist and could pose a threat. [5] By learning from the past, we can better protect the future—whether that means tracking near-Earth asteroids or understanding how climate change might affect life today.

## 选项

A. The story of the dinosaurs' disappearance is not just about the past—it has important lessons for the present and future.
B. A new theory emerged that would eventually be supported by rocks, fossils, and evidence from around the world.
C. The real damage came from the long-lasting effects: darkness, cold, and the collapse of ecosystems that dinosaurs depended on.
D. In a world without giant dinosaurs, smaller animals had new opportunities to grow, diversify, and eventually take over.
E. Understanding mass extinctions helps us see how fragile life can be—and how important it is to protect our planet today.
F. For example, the Chicxulub crater in Mexico is over 150 kilometers wide and is thought to be the site where the dinosaur-killing asteroid landed.

## 答案键（Answer Key）

[1] A   [2] B   [3] C   [4] D   [5] E

## 深度解析

- **[1] A**：
  - **线索**：空前介绍恐龙统治地球及突然灭绝，空后说"Scientists have spent decades trying to understand what caused these powerful creatures to vanish—and the answer has changed how we think about life on Earth"。A的"The story of the dinosaurs' disappearance is not just about the past—it has important lessons for the present and future"与空后"changed how we think"、科学意义呼应。

- **[2] B**：
  - **线索**：空前说曾有疾病、气候等多种假说，空后说"In the 1980s, scientists found strong evidence that changed everything: a giant asteroid had hit the Earth"。B的"A new theory emerged that would eventually be supported by rocks, fossils, and evidence from around the world"与空后evidence、asteroid theory衔接。

- **[3] C**：
  - **线索**：空前说撞击导致尘埃遮蔽阳光、植物死亡、食物链崩溃，空后说"The asteroid did not kill every dinosaur at once—instead, it triggered a chain of events that made the world uninhabitable"。C的"The real damage came from the long-lasting effects: darkness, cold, and the collapse of ecosystems"与空后chain of events、uninhabitable呼应。

- **[4] D**：
  - **线索**：空前说小型哺乳动物、鸟类、爬行动物幸存并演化，空后说"The extinction of the dinosaurs opened the door for mammals to become the dominant animals on land—and eventually, for humans to evolve"。D的"In a world without giant dinosaurs, smaller animals had new opportunities to grow, diversify, and eventually take over"与空后mammals dominant、humans evolve完美衔接。

- **[5] E**：
  - **线索**：空前说研究恐龙灭绝可理解突变、小行星仍具威胁，空后说"By learning from the past, we can better protect the future—whether that means tracking near-Earth asteroids or understanding how climate change might affect life today"。E的"Understanding mass extinctions helps us see how fragile life can be—and how important it is to protect our planet today"与空后learn、protect、asteroids、climate呼应。

- **干扰项分析**：
  - **F. For example, the Chicxulub crater in Mexico is over 150 kilometers wide...**：以For example开头，是具体地点案例。各空均需概括性或过渡性句子，F为举例型干扰项。
