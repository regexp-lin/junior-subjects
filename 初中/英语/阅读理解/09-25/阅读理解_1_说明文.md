# 阅读理解（说明文）

① Whales are the largest animals on Earth. They live in the ocean but they are not fish — they are mammals (哺乳动物), just like us. They breathe air, give birth to live babies, and feed their young with milk. Understanding how whales live helps us protect these amazing creatures.

② Whales spend their whole lives in water. They have a thick layer of fat called blubber (鲸脂) under their skin. Blubber keeps them warm in cold seas. Whales breathe through a hole on top of their head called a blowhole (呼吸孔). When they surface, they blow out old air and take in fresh air. You can sometimes see a spray of water when they breathe. Different kinds of whales can hold their breath for different lengths of time — some for a few minutes, others for over an hour.

③ Whales eat different things depending on their type. Some whales, like the blue whale, eat tiny creatures called krill (磷虾) — they can swallow millions of them each day. Others, like killer whales (虎鲸), eat fish, seals, or even other whales. Whales often travel in groups called pods (鲸群). In a pod, they hunt together, care for their young, and protect each other. Mother whales are very gentle with their babies. They nurse them and teach them how to survive.

④ Whales migrate (迁徙) — they travel long distances every year. In summer, they go to cold waters where there is plenty of food. In winter, they move to warmer waters to give birth. Some whales sing. Their songs can travel for hundreds of kilometres underwater. Scientists think whales use these songs to communicate and find mates.

⑤ Sadly, whales face many dangers. Pollution, noise from ships, and climate change affect their lives. Some species are in danger of dying out. Many countries now protect whales and limit hunting. By learning about whales, we can help ensure they continue to swim in our oceans for years to come.

**注释：** mammal 哺乳动物；blubber 鲸脂；blowhole 呼吸孔；krill 磷虾；pod 鲸群；migrate 迁徙

**Questions:**

1. Why are whales considered mammals?
   A. Because they are big.
   B. Because they breathe air, give birth to live babies, and feed their young with milk.
   C. Because they live in water.
   D. Because they eat fish.

2. What is blubber used for?
   A. To help whales swim faster.
   B. To keep whales warm in cold seas.
   C. To attract fish.
   D. To help whales breathe.

3. How do blue whales get their food?
   A. They hunt seals.
   B. They eat tiny creatures called krill.
   C. They eat other whales.
   D. They eat plants only.

4. Why do whales migrate?
   A. To play with other animals.
   B. To find food in cold waters in summer and move to warmer waters to give birth in winter.
   C. To escape from ships.
   D. They never migrate.

5. What dangers do whales face according to the passage?
   A. Only hunting.
   B. Pollution, noise from ships, and climate change; some species may die out.
   C. Too much food.
   D. No dangers at all.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "they are mammals, just like us. They breathe air, give birth to live babies, and feed their young with milk"

**【解析】** 鲸鱼呼吸空气、胎生、哺乳，因此属于哺乳动物。A、C、D 项非哺乳动物特征。故选 B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "They have a thick layer of fat called blubber... Blubber keeps them warm in cold seas"

**【解析】** 鲸脂是皮下脂肪层，用于在寒冷海水中保暖。A、C、D 项与原文不符。故选 B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "Some whales, like the blue whale, eat tiny creatures called krill"

**【解析】** 蓝鲸以磷虾等微小生物为食。A、C、D 项与原文不符。故选 B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "In summer, they go to cold waters where there is plenty of food. In winter, they move to warmer waters to give birth"

**【解析】** 鲸鱼夏季迁往寒冷水域觅食，冬季迁往温暖水域繁殖。A、C、D 项与原文不符。故选 B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "Pollution, noise from ships, and climate change affect their lives. Some species are in danger of dying out"

**【解析】** 鲸鱼面临污染、船舶噪音、气候变化等威胁，部分种类濒临灭绝。A、C、D 项与原文不符。故选 B。
