# 阅读理解（说明文）

① Antarctica (南极洲) is the coldest, windiest, and driest continent (大陆) on Earth. It is located at the South Pole and is almost completely covered by thick ice all year round. In some places, the ice is more than 4,000 meters thick! Because of the extreme (极端的) cold weather, there are no permanent (永久的) human residents in Antarctica.

② However, this frozen world is not empty. It is home to many amazing animals that have adapted (适应) to the harsh environment. The most famous animal in Antarctica is the penguin. Unlike flying birds, penguins are excellent swimmers. They dive deep into the freezing ocean to catch fish and krill (磷虾). Emperor penguins, the largest of all, can live through the dark, cold winter by huddling (挤在一起) together to keep warm.

③ Another important animal is the seal (海豹). Seals have a thick layer of fat called blubber under their skin. This blubber acts like a warm blanket, protecting them from the icy water. They spend most of their time hunting in the ocean but come up to the ice to rest and give birth to their babies.

④ In recent years, scientists from all over the world have visited Antarctica. They go there during the short summer months to study the ice, the animals, and the changing climate (气候). The ice in Antarctica holds important clues about the Earth's past climate.

⑤ To protect this unique environment, countries around the world signed a special agreement. It says that Antarctica should only be used for peace and science. No country owns Antarctica, and activities like mining (采矿) are strictly forbidden.

**注释：** extreme 极端的；permanent 永久的；adapt 适应；blubber 鲸脂(用来保暖的动物脂肪)；climate 气候；forbid 禁止

### 选择题

1. Where is Antarctica located according to paragraph ①?
A. At the North Pole.
B. Near the equator (赤道).
C. In the middle of the Atlantic Ocean.
D. At the South Pole.

2. Why are there no permanent human residents in Antarctica?
A. Because the continent is too small.
B. Because it is completely covered by water.
C. Because the weather is extremely cold, windy, and dry.
D. Because wild bears attack people there.

3. How do Emperor penguins keep themselves warm in the dark, cold winter?
A. By building large ice houses.
B. By flying south to warmer places.
C. By swimming fast in the ocean all day.
D. By huddling (挤在一起) together.

4. What is the thick layer of fat under a seal's skin called?
A. A warm blanket.
B. Blubber.
C. Krill.
D. Emperor.

5. What does the special agreement about Antarctica say in the last paragraph?
A. That Antarctica is owned by the richest country.
B. That people can do mining if they find gold.
C. That Antarctica should only be used for peace and science.
D. That scientists must not visit Antarctica anymore.

---

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段第2句 "It is located at the South Pole..."

**【解析】** 原文中清楚无误地说明了南极洲（Antarctica）的地理位置是南极（the South Pole）。这与D项内容完全符合。A项说在北极、B项说在赤道旁、C项说位于大西洋中央都与客观地理常识和文本信息悖绝。故选D。

**2. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解判断

**【定位】** 第①段最后一句 "Because of the extreme cold weather, there are no permanent human residents in Antarctica."

**【解析】** 由于该地天气极为寒冷极端（extreme cold weather），以及南极洲被大风、厚冰覆盖和干燥异常（首句），因此该大陆没有人类永久定居（no permanent human residents）。A项说太小、B项说被水覆盖、D项说野熊袭击皆是纯属捏造的无端猜想。故选C。

**3. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段最后一句 "Emperor penguins... can live through the dark, cold winter by huddling (挤在一起) together to keep warm."

**【解析】** 遇到漫长黑暗冷冰的极地寒冬，帝企鹅们是怎么熬过来的？在提到最大体型的帝企鹅时指明了它们是通过相互挤在一起（huddling together）来取暖的。A造冰屋、B向南飞（本身就在最南），或者一直在海里游来增加热量都不在其避冬描述之列。故选D。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段第2句 "Seals have a thick layer of fat called blubber under their skin."

**【解析】** 第三段海豹部分提及：海豹皮下厚厚的用于御寒的那层脂肪叫什么？叫“blubber（鲸脂皮下脂肪）”。文中说这层脂肪扮演了天然大毯子（blanket）的作用，而名字本身就是"blubber"。故选B。

**5. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第⑤段第2句 "It says that Antarctica should only be used for peace and science."

**【解析】** 根据最后一段《南极条约》（special agreement）的内容陈述，“只应当被用于和平及科学事业”。任何矿产开采（mining）以及国别归属统统是被严格禁止（strictly forbidden）及不被承认的。C项（仅作和平与科学使用）完美承接了文章的法理。故选C。
