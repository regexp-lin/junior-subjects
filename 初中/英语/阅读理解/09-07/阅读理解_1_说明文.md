# 阅读理解（说明文）

① China's Four Great Inventions are the compass, gunpowder, paper, and printing. They were invented in ancient China hundreds or even thousands of years ago. These inventions have had a huge impact on the whole world and changed human history.

② The compass was first used in China during the Song Dynasty, around a thousand years ago. It was made of a magnetic needle placed on a smooth surface. The needle always pointed north, which helped sailors find their way at sea. Before the compass, sailors often got lost in bad weather. With this tool, they could travel to faraway places more safely. Later, the compass spread to Europe and other parts of the world.

③ Gunpowder was invented by Chinese scientists by accident. At first, it was used to make fireworks for festivals. Later, people found that it could be used in war. However, today we mainly remember gunpowder for the beautiful fireworks we see during New Year and other celebrations.

④ Paper was invented in China about two thousand years ago. Before paper, people wrote on bamboo or silk, which were heavy or expensive. Paper was lighter and cheaper. It made it easier to store and share knowledge. The invention of paper changed the way people learned and communicated.

⑤ Printing was another great step. In the past, books had to be copied by hand, which took a long time. With printing, many copies of a book could be made quickly. This helped ideas and knowledge spread to more people. Together, these four inventions show the wisdom of ancient China and its influence on our world today.

**注释：** compass 指南针；gunpowder 火药；magnetic 磁性的

**Questions:**

1. What are China's Four Great Inventions?
   A. The phone, the computer, the car, and the plane.
   B. The compass, gunpowder, paper, and printing.
   C. The clock, the telescope, the microscope, and the camera.
   D. The wheel, the sail, the engine, and the battery.

2. When was the compass first used in China?
   A. About five hundred years ago.
   B. About a thousand years ago during the Song Dynasty.
   C. About two thousand years ago.
   D. About three thousand years ago.

3. What was gunpowder first used for?
   A. Making weapons for war.
   B. Making fireworks for festivals.
   C. Making medicine.
   D. Making paper.

4. Why was the invention of paper important?
   A. Because paper was heavier than bamboo.
   B. Because paper was lighter and cheaper, making it easier to store and share knowledge.
   C. Because paper could be used as money.
   D. Because paper was invented in Europe first.

5. What can we learn from the last paragraph about printing?
   A. Printing made it slower to copy books.
   B. Printing helped ideas and knowledge reach more people quickly.
   C. Printing was only used in China.
   D. Printing replaced paper completely.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段第1句 "China's Four Great Inventions are the compass, gunpowder, paper, and printing."

**【解析】** 原文明确列出四大发明：指南针、火药、造纸术和印刷术。A、C、D项所列发明均与原文不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段第1句 "The compass was first used in China during the Song Dynasty, around a thousand years ago."

**【解析】** 原文说明指南针在中国北宋时期首次使用，距今约一千年。A、C、D项时间均错误。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段第2句 "At first, it was used to make fireworks for festivals."

**【解析】** 原文明确说明火药最初用于制作节日烟花。A项"制作战争武器"是后来的用途；C、D项原文未提及。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "Paper was lighter and cheaper. It made it easier to store and share knowledge."

**【解析】** 造纸术的意义在于纸更轻便、更便宜，便于储存和传播知识。A项"更重"与原文相反；C、D项原文未涉及。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "With printing, many copies of a book could be made quickly. This helped ideas and knowledge spread to more people."

**【解析】** 印刷术使书籍能快速大量复制，从而让思想和知识传播给更多人。B项正确。A项"更慢"与原文相反；C、D项无依据。故选B。
