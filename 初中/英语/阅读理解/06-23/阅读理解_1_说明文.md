# 阅读理解（说明文）

## How Our Eyes Help Us See

① Our eyes are wonderful. They help us see the world around us. But do you know how they work? When light comes from an object, it passes through the air and enters our eyes. The front part of the eye is clear like glass. It lets the light in and bends it so that it goes to the right place inside the eye.

② Behind this clear part is something called the lens. The lens is very important. It focuses the light and forms a clear picture, just like a camera does. When you look at something far away, the lens becomes thinner. When you look at something close, it becomes thicker. This helps you see things at different distances.

③ The focused light then reaches the back of the eye. There is a special layer called the retina. The retina has millions of tiny cells that are sensitive to light. These cells change the light into signals. The signals travel along the optic nerve to your brain. Your brain receives these signals and turns them into the images you "see." That is why we say we see with our eyes, but we understand with our brain.

④ To keep your eyes healthy, you need to take good care of them. Rest your eyes when you read or use a computer for a long time. Look at something far away every twenty minutes. Eat foods rich in vitamin A, such as carrots. Remember never to read in dim light, because it can make your eyes tired and may harm your vision over time.

⑤ Our eyes work day and night to help us experience the world. Understanding how they work can help us appreciate this gift and protect it well.

**注释：** lens 晶状体；retina 视网膜；optic nerve 视神经；sensitive 敏感的

### 选择题

1. What does the front part of the eye do according to paragraph ①?
   A. It lets the light in and bends it to the right place.
   B. It blocks all light from entering.
   C. It stores the light for later use.
   D. It sends light to other people's eyes.

2. What happens to the lens when you look at something close?
   A. It becomes thinner.
   B. It disappears.
   C. It becomes thicker.
   D. It stops working.

3. Where does the light go after it is focused by the lens?
   A. To the retina at the back of the eye.
   B. To the front of the eye.
   C. Directly to the brain.
   D. Out of the eye.

4. According to the passage, which of the following is good for your eyes?
   A. Reading in dim light for hours.
   B. Using a computer without resting.
   C. Eating less fruit and vegetables.
   D. Looking at something far away every twenty minutes when using eyes for long.

5. Why does the writer say "we see with our eyes, but we understand with our brain"?
   A. Because the eyes are more important than the brain.
   B. Because the brain receives signals and turns them into images.
   C. Because we can see without our brain.
   D. Because the eyes send signals to other parts of the body.

---

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "The front part of the eye is clear like glass. It lets the light in and bends it so that it goes to the right place inside the eye."

**【解析】** 眼睛前部像玻璃一样透明，让光线进入并将其折射到眼球内的正确位置。A 项正确。B 项阻挡光线、C 项储存光线、D 项将光送至他人眼睛均与原文不符。故选 A。

**2. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "When you look at something close, it becomes thicker."

**【解析】** 看近处物体时，晶状体变厚。C 项正确。A 项变薄是看远处时；B、D 项与原文不符。故选 C。

**3. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "The focused light then reaches the back of the eye. There is a special layer called the retina."

**【解析】** 聚焦后的光线到达眼球后部的视网膜。A 项正确。B 项前部、C 项直接到大脑、D 项离开眼睛均错误。故选 A。

**4. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第④段 "Rest your eyes when you read or use a computer for a long time. Look at something far away every twenty minutes."

**【解析】** 长时间用眼后应休息，每二十分钟看远处物体，对眼睛有益。D 项正确。A、B 项有害；C 项与文中 "Eat foods rich in vitamin A" 矛盾。故选 D。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断 / 词义理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "Your brain receives these signals and turns them into the images you 'see.'"

**【解析】** 大脑接收视网膜传来的信号，并将其转换为"看见"的图像。因此"用眼睛看、用大脑理解"是指大脑在成像中的关键作用。B 项正确。A、C、D 项均曲解原意。故选 B。
