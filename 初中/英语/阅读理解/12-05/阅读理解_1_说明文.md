# 阅读理解（说明文）

**The History and Culture of Kites**

① Kites have a long history in China. It is said that kites were first made more than 2,000 years ago. In the beginning, people used bamboo and silk to make them. Kites were used to send messages in wartime. Later, they became popular as toys for both children and adults.

② How does a kite fly? A kite needs wind to stay in the air. When the wind blows, it pushes against the kite. The string holds the kite at an angle, so the wind creates "lift." Lift is the upward force that makes the kite rise. If there is no wind or the string is broken, the kite will fall.

③ Different places in China have their own kite styles. Weifang in Shandong is famous as the "kite capital" of the world. Every spring, the Weifang International Kite Festival draws people from many countries. Visitors can see kites in the shapes of dragons, birds, flowers, and even cartoon characters.

④ Making a kite by hand is not very difficult. You need paper or cloth, bamboo sticks, string, and glue. First, tie the bamboo sticks into a frame. Then paste the paper or cloth onto the frame. Add a tail to keep the kite balanced. Finally, tie the flying string and your kite is ready.

⑤ Today, kites are still loved by people of all ages. Flying kites is a good way to spend time outdoors with family and friends. It also helps us understand Chinese culture and the wisdom of ancient people.

**Questions:**

1. What were kites first used for in ancient China?
   A. Only as toys for children.
   B. To send messages in wartime.
   C. To catch birds.
   D. To measure the wind.

2. What makes a kite stay in the air?
   A. The string only.
   B. The tail only.
   C. Wind creates lift when it pushes against the kite at an angle.
   D. The weight of the kite.

3. Which city is called the "kite capital" of the world?
   A. Beijing.
   B. Shanghai.
   C. Weifang.
   D. Hangzhou.

4. What do you need to make a kite by hand?
   A. Only paper.
   B. Paper or cloth, bamboo sticks, string, and glue.
   C. Metal and plastic.
   D. Wood and stone.

5. Why do people still love flying kites today?
   A. Because it is very cheap.
   B. Because it helps us understand Chinese culture and is a good outdoor activity.
   C. Because it is easy to make.
   D. Because it can send messages.

---

**Answers and Explanations:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "Kites were used to send messages in wartime."

**【解析】** 原文说风筝最初用于战争时期传递信息。A项仅作儿童玩具是后来的用途；C、D项文中未提及。故选B。

**2. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "When the wind blows, it pushes against the kite... the wind creates 'lift.' Lift is the upward force that makes the kite rise."

**【解析】** 风筝靠风力推动产生升力而停留空中。A项仅靠线、B项仅靠尾巴、D项靠重量均不完整或错误。故选C。

**3. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "Weifang in Shandong is famous as the 'kite capital' of the world."

**【解析】** 潍坊被誉为世界风筝之都。A、B、D项城市均非原文所述。故选C。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "You need paper or cloth, bamboo sticks, string, and glue."

**【解析】** 手工制作风筝需要纸或布、竹条、线和胶水。A项仅纸不完整；C项金属塑料、D项木头石头均与原文不符。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "Flying kites is a good way to spend time outdoors with family and friends. It also helps us understand Chinese culture and the wisdom of ancient people."

**【解析】** 放风筝是户外活动好方式，也有助于理解中国文化。A项便宜、C项容易制作、D项可传信均非主要原因。故选B。
