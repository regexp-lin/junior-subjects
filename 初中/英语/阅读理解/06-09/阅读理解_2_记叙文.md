# 阅读理解（记叙文）

① Last year, Li Ming joined the school writing competition. He was sure his first draft (初稿) was good enough to win. He wrote a story about a kind old man who helped his neighbors. After finishing the last sentence, he closed his notebook and handed it in proudly. A week later, when the results came out, Li Ming's name was not on the winner list.

② His English teacher, Ms. Wang, asked him to stay after class. She gave back his paper and said, "Your ideas are wonderful, but good writing needs revision (修改). Even famous writers change their work many times before they are satisfied." Li Ming had never thought about rewriting his story. He always believed that the first try was the best.

③ Ms. Wang taught him how to revise. First, read the whole piece again and find parts that are unclear. Second, add more details to make the story come alive. Third, check the grammar and choose better words. Li Ming went home and tried. He read his story again and found several weak parts. He added descriptions of the old man's smile and the sound of rain outside the window. He also changed some simple words into richer ones.

④ The next month, another writing competition was held. Li Ming entered again. This time, he wrote a first draft, then revised it twice before handing it in. His story about a lost dog finding its way home won second place. He was very happy, but he knew the prize was not the most important thing. What mattered was that he had learned a valuable lesson: good writing is rewriting.

⑤ Today, Li Ming still uses what Ms. Wang taught him. Whether he writes for homework or for fun, he always leaves time to revise. He has learned that the first draft is just the beginning — the real magic happens when we improve our work step by step.

**注释：** draft 初稿；revision 修改；satisfied 满意的；description 描述；valuable 宝贵的

---

### 选择题

1. What happened to Li Ming in the first writing competition?
   A. He won second place.
   B. He refused to hand in his paper.
   C. His teacher praised his work.
   D. He did not win.

2. According to paragraph ③, what is the third step of revision that Ms. Wang taught?
   A. Read the whole piece again.
   B. Add more details to make the story come alive.
   C. Check the grammar and choose better words.
   D. Find parts that are unclear.

3. What did Li Ming add to his story when he revised it?
   A. A new character.
   B. Descriptions of the old man's smile and the sound of rain.
   C. A different ending.
   D. More grammar exercises.

4. What can we infer from paragraph ④ about Li Ming?
   A. He values the lesson he learned more than the prize.
   B. He only cares about winning prizes.
   C. He will never enter a writing competition again.
   D. He thinks his first draft was perfect.

5. What is the main message of this passage?
   A. Writing competitions are very important for students.
   B. Teachers should always help students with their writing.
   C. Revision is important for improving one's writing.
   D. The first draft is always the best.

---

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段最后一句 "A week later, when the results came out, Li Ming's name was not on the winner list."

**【解析】** 原文明确说明一周后结果公布，李明的名字不在获奖名单上，即他未能获奖。A 项"他获得二等奖"是第二次比赛的结果；B 项"他拒绝交稿"、C 项"老师表扬他的作品"文中均未提及。故选 D。

**2. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "First, read the whole piece again and find parts that are unclear. Second, add more details to make the story come alive. Third, check the grammar and choose better words."

**【解析】** 原文列出修改的三个步骤：第一步是通读并找不清之处；第二步是添加更多细节使故事生动；第三步是检查语法并选用更好的词。题目问第三步，故为 C。A 和 D 对应第一步；B 对应第二步。故选 C。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "He added descriptions of the old man's smile and the sound of rain outside the window."

**【解析】** 原文清楚写出李明修改时添加了对老人微笑和窗外雨声的描述。A 项"新角色"、C 项"不同结局"、D 项"更多语法练习"均与原文不符。故选 B。

**4. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第④段 "He was very happy, but he knew the prize was not the most important thing. What mattered was that he had learned a valuable lesson: good writing is rewriting."

**【解析】** 原文说李明很高兴，但他知道奖品不是最重要的，重要的是他学到了宝贵的一课：好作品是改出来的。由此可推知他更看重学到的教训而非奖项，即 A 项。B 项"只关心获奖"与原文矛盾；C 项"再也不会参加比赛"过度推断；D 项"认为初稿完美"与他对修改的认同相反。故选 A。

**5. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 主旨大意

**【定位】** 全文脉络：第①段李明初稿未获奖；第②—③段老师教其修改方法并实践；第④—⑤段通过修改获奖并领悟修改的重要性。

**【解析】** 全文围绕李明从忽视修改到学会修改、从而提升写作的过程展开，主旨是"修改对提高写作至关重要"。A 项"写作比赛很重要"以偏概全；B 项"老师应帮助学生写作"是辅助信息；D 项"初稿总是最好的"与全文主旨相反。故选 C。
