> **04-29 第2篇**

# 阅读理解（记叙文）

① In a small village surrounded by mountains, there lived an old man named Arthur. Arthur was not rich, but he had something everyone in the village wanted: a beautiful apple orchard (果园). Every autumn, his trees produced the sweetest, reddest apples you could ever imagine. 

② However, Arthur was known as a very grumpy (脾气暴躁的) man. He built a high wooden fence around his orchard. He never shared his apples with anyone, not even the village children who looked at the red apples with hungry eyes. "Go away! These are my apples!" he would shout if a child came near his fence.

③ One stormy night, a strong wind blew through the village. It rained heavily, and thunder crashed in the sky. The next morning, when Arthur woke up and looked out of his window, his heart sank. The storm had broken down a large part of his fence, and many of his beautiful apple trees were blown over. His precious apples were scattered (散落) everywhere in the mud. 

④ Arthur sat on the ground and cried. He was too old and weak to clean up the mess by himself. Just then, he heard footsteps. A group of villagers, including the children he used to shout at, were walking towards him. They didn't come to laugh at him or steal his fallen apples. Instead, they brought shovels (铁锹), ropes, and baskets. 

⑤ Without saying a word, the villagers began to work. The strong men pulled the trees back up, the women tied them tightly, and the children carefully picked up the apples from the mud and washed them clean. By the evening, the orchard looked almost normal again.

⑥ Arthur looked at the tired but smiling villagers. A warm feeling replaced his usual anger. He picked up the biggest basket of clean apples and handed it to the leader of the children. "Thank you," Arthur said softly, wiping a tear from his eye. "Please, take these. They taste much better when shared." From that day on, Arthur's fence stayed open.

**注释：** orchard 果园；grumpy 脾气暴躁的；scatter 散落；shovel 铁锹

---

### 选择题

1. What did Arthur have that everyone in the village wanted?
A. A large box of gold coins.
B. A very fast and strong horse.
C. A beautiful apple orchard with the sweetest apples.
D. A high wooden fence around his house.

2. How did Arthur usually treat the village children before the storm?
A. He often invited them into his orchard to play.
B. He would shout at them and tell them to go away.
C. He gave them a few green apples every day.
D. He taught them how to plant apple trees.

3. What happened to Arthur's orchard during the stormy night?
A. Thieves broke in and stole all his precious apples.
B. The high wooden fence grew taller because of the rain.
C. A new kind of sweet apple started growing on the trees.
D. Much of the fence was broken, trees were blown over, and apples scattered in the mud.

4. Why did the villagers come to Arthur's orchard the next morning?
A. To help him clean up the mess and save his trees.
B. To laugh at his terrible situation.
C. To steal the fallen apples for themselves.
D. To ask him to pay for the damage to their houses.

5. What changed in Arthur at the end of the story?
A. He decided to cut down all his apple trees.
B. He became even grumpier and built a much stronger fence.
C. He learned to share his apples and kept his fence open.
D. He moved to another village to live alone.

---

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段第2-3句 "...he had something everyone in the village wanted: a beautiful apple orchard. Every autumn, his trees produced the sweetest, reddest apples..."

**【解析】** 第一段提及亚瑟虽然不富裕，却拥有一座令全村人向往的美丽苹果园，里面产最甜最红的苹果。A选金块、B选快马、D选高墙均不正确。故选C。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "...He never shared his apples... 'Go away! These are my apples!' he would shout if a child came near his fence."

**【解析】** 暴风雨前，亚瑟是个出了名的脾气暴躁的人。他从不分享，只要有孩子靠近栅栏，他就会对他们大喊大叫（shout at them），让他们滚开（tell them to go away）。因此B选项描述最为准确。A、C、D项的友好举动不符合其之前的暴脾气设定。故选B。

**3. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段第3-4句 "The storm had broken down a large part of his fence, and many of his beautiful apple trees were blown over. His precious apples were scattered (散落) everywhere in the mud."

**【解析】** 那场暴风雨造成的结果是：大片栅栏被毁，许多苹果树被吹倒，宝贵的苹果散落一泥潭。D项就是对此事实的直接转述。盗贼偷菜（A）、栅栏长高（B）、长出新苹果（C）均是凭空捏造。故选D。

**4. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 细节推断理解

**【定位】** 第④段末及第⑤段首 "They didn't come to laugh at him or steal his fallen apples. Instead, they brought shovels... Without saying a word, the villagers began to work... pulled the trees back up... washed them clean."

**【解析】** 第二天村民们带着铁锹、绳子和篮子过来干什么？文中明确说他们不是来嘲笑（B错）也不是来偷苹果的（C错），而是默默开始干活，把树拉起、洗净苹果。他们在帮亚瑟收拾残局、拯救果树（help him clean up the mess and save his trees）。故选A。

**5. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 主旨意图推断

**【定位】** 故事结尾第⑥段 "'Please, take these. They taste much better when shared.' From that day on, Arthur's fence stayed open."

**【解析】** 结尾升华了主题，暴脾气的护食老头被村民的善意感化，他不仅把最大一篮干净苹果送给领头孩子并由衷感谢，更明白了“分享起来味道更好”的道理。从此他家果园的栅栏再没关上过。C选项（学会分享并敞开栅栏）完美契合这个改变。A砍树、B更暴躁建高墙、D搬家独居都违背了这温馨的结局。故选C。
