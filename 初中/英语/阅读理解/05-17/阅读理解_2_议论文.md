# 阅读理解（议论文）

① Should middle school students use social media (社交媒体) like WeChat, Douyin or TikTok? This is a question that many parents and teachers ask. Some say yes; some say no. Let's think about both sides of the argument.

② Those who support students using social media believe it has many benefits (好处). First, social media helps students stay in touch with friends and family. They can share news, photos and ideas easily. Second, students can learn new things through educational (教育的) videos and posts online. Some teachers even use social media to give homework and answer questions. Third, social media can help students feel less lonely when they have no one to talk to at home.

③ However, there are also strong reasons against students spending too much time on social media. One problem is that it can affect (影响) their study. Many students spend hours each day watching short videos or chatting with friends instead of doing homework or reading books. Another problem is that social media can be harmful to young people's health. Staring at a screen for too long hurts the eyes, and some students stay up late because they cannot stop scrolling (滚动浏览). What is more, not everything on social media is true or safe. Students may see information that is wrong or even meet strangers online.

④ So what is the best answer? Most experts (专家) suggest that the key is balance (平衡). Students do not have to avoid social media completely, but they should use it in a wise way. Parents and teachers can help by setting rules — for example, no phones during meal time or homework time, and limiting screen time to one or two hours a day. Students should also learn to tell the difference between real and fake (虚假的) information online.

⑤ In the end, social media is like a tool. It can be useful when we use it correctly, but it can also cause problems when we use it too much or in the wrong way. The question is not really "should students use it or not" — it is "how can they use it safely and wisely?"

**注释：** social media 社交媒体；benefit 好处；educational 教育的；affect 影响；scroll 滚动浏览；expert 专家；balance 平衡；fake 虚假的

---

### 选择题

1. According to paragraph ②, what is one benefit of social media for students?
   A. It helps them avoid homework.
   B. It makes them study longer each day.
   C. It helps them stay in touch with friends and family.
   D. It helps them meet strangers safely.

2. What problem does paragraph ③ mention about social media?
   A. It is too expensive for students.
   B. Students may see wrong information or meet strangers online.
   C. It is only useful for teachers.
   D. It makes students read more books.

3. What do most experts suggest about students and social media?
   A. Students should never use social media.
   B. Students should use social media for at least five hours a day.
   C. The key is balance — use it in a wise way with rules.
   D. Parents should not set any rules for their children.

4. What can we infer from paragraph ④?
   A. All information on social media is true and safe.
   B. Setting rules like "no phones during meal time" can help students use social media better.
   C. Students should use social media without any limits.
   D. Teachers never use social media to teach.

5. What is the writer's main point in this passage?
   A. Social media is always bad and students should stop using it.
   B. Social media is always good and students should use it more.
   C. The real question is how to use social media safely and wisely, not whether to use it or not.
   D. Only parents can decide if students should use social media.

---

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段第2句 "First, social media helps students stay in touch with friends and family."

**【解析】** 原文明确列举第一个好处：帮助学生与亲友保持联系。A 项"逃避作业"与支持方观点矛盾；B 项"每天学习更久"文中未提及；D 项"安全结识陌生人"恰恰是第③段反对方提到的风险之一。故选 C。

**2. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段最后一句 "What is more, not everything on social media is true or safe. Students may see information that is wrong or even meet strangers online."

**【解析】** 原文指出社交媒体的内容并非都真实安全，学生可能看到错误信息甚至在网上遇到陌生人。A 项"太贵"、B 项"只对老师有用"、C 项"让学生多读书"均未在反对理由中提及。故选 D。

**3. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段第2句 "Most experts suggest that the key is balance. Students do not have to avoid social media completely, but they should use it in a wise way."

**【解析】** 专家认为关键在于平衡，不必完全禁用，但应明智使用。A 项"绝不用"与"do not have to avoid completely"矛盾；B 项"每天至少五小时"与后文"limiting to one or two hours"矛盾；D 项"家长不应设规则"与"Parents and teachers can help by setting rules"矛盾。故选 C。

**4. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第④段 "Parents and teachers can help by setting rules — for example, no phones during meal time or homework time, and limiting screen time to one or two hours a day."

**【解析】** 原文举例说明可通过"用餐和写作业时不用手机"等规则帮助孩子，由此可推知这类规则有助于学生更好使用社交媒体。A 项"所有信息都真实安全"与第③段矛盾；B 项"无限制使用"与"limiting screen time"矛盾；C 项"老师从不用社交媒体教学"与第②段"Some teachers use social media to give homework"矛盾。故选 D。

**5. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 主旨大意

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "The question is not really 'should students use it or not' — it is 'how can they use it safely and wisely?'"

**【解析】** 作者在结尾点明核心观点：关键不在于用不用，而在于如何安全、明智地使用。A 项"总是有害应停用"、B 项"总是有益应用更多"均属极端，与作者辩证态度不符；C 项"只有家长能决定"过于片面，文中强调家长、老师与学生共同参与。故选 D。
