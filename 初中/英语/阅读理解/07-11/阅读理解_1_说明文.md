# 阅读理解（说明文）

# Strange Festivals Around the World

① People all over the world celebrate different festivals. Some festivals are famous, like Christmas and the Spring Festival. But have you ever heard of festivals where people throw tomatoes at each other, or run with bulls in the street? Let's take a look at some of the strangest festivals from around the world.

② In Spain, there is a festival called La Tomatina (西红柿节). It takes place in a small town called Buñol on the last Wednesday of August every year. During this festival, thousands of people gather in the streets and throw tomatoes at each other for about one hour. By the end, the whole town is covered in red tomato juice. Nobody really knows why it started, but it has become one of the most popular festivals in Spain.

③ Another famous festival is the Running of the Bulls in Pamplona, Spain. Every July, brave people run in front of bulls through the narrow streets of the city. The festival lasts for nine days. Although it can be dangerous, many tourists from all over the world come to watch or even take part in this exciting event.

④ In Thailand, people celebrate the Monkey Buffet Festival (猴子 buffet 节) every year. On this day, local people prepare a large feast with fruits and vegetables for over 2,000 monkeys that live nearby. The monkeys are considered sacred (神圣的) in the local culture, so people treat them with great respect. This unusual festival has attracted many visitors and photographers from different countries.

⑤ These strange festivals show us that culture can be fun and surprising. Each festival has its own story and meaning, and they help people understand different ways of life around the world. Would you like to visit one of these festivals one day?

**注释：** gather 聚集；feast 盛宴；sacred 神圣的；buffet 自助餐

### 选择题

1. When does La Tomatina take place?
   A. On the last Wednesday of August.
   B. On the first Wednesday of August.
   C. On the last Friday of August.
   D. In the middle of September.

2. What happens during the Running of the Bulls?
   A. People watch bulls fight in an arena.
   B. People run in front of bulls through city streets.
   C. People feed bulls with special food.
   D. People paint pictures of bulls.

3. How many monkeys are fed during the Monkey Buffet Festival?
   A. About 200.
   B. About 500.
   C. Over 2,000.
   D. About 1,000.

4. What can we infer from paragraph ④ about the Monkey Buffet Festival?
   A. Monkeys cause problems for local people.
   B. The festival is only for photographers.
   C. Monkeys are afraid of the food.
   D. Local people respect monkeys in their culture.

5. What is the main purpose of this passage?
   A. To introduce some unusual festivals from different countries.
   B. To explain how to travel to Spain and Thailand.
   C. To compare Spanish and Thai culture.
   D. To warn people about dangerous festivals.

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段第2句 "It takes place in a small town called Buñol on the last Wednesday of August every year."

**【解析】** 原文明确说明 La Tomatina 在每年八月的最后一个星期三举行。A 项正确。B 项"八月的第一个星期三"、C 项"八月的最后一个星期五"和 D 项"九月中旬"均与原文不符。故选 A。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段第2句 "Every July, brave people run in front of bulls through the narrow streets of the city."

**【解析】** 原文清楚描述了奔牛节的景象：人们七月在城市的狭窄街道上在公牛前面奔跑。A 项"在竞技场观牛斗"、C 项"喂牛"和 D 项"画牛"均与原文不符。故选 B。

**3. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "people prepare a large feast with fruits and vegetables for over 2,000 monkeys"

**【解析】** 原文明确提到为超过 2000 只猴子准备盛宴。C 项正确。A、B、D 项的数字与原文不符。故选 C。

**4. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第④段 "The monkeys are considered sacred (神圣的) in the local culture, so people treat them with great respect."

**【解析】** 原文指出猴子在当地文化中被视为神圣的，因此人们非常尊重它们。D 项正确。A 项"猴子给当地人制造麻烦"与原文矛盾；B 项"节日只面向摄影师"过于片面；C 项"猴子害怕食物"文中未提及。故选 D。

**5. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 主旨大意/写作意图

**【定位】** 全文：第①段引出奇特节日，第②③④段分别介绍西班牙西红柿节、奔牛节和泰国猴子 buffet 节，第⑤段总结这些节日展示文化多样性。

**【解析】** 全文旨在介绍世界各地的一些奇特节日，A 项"介绍不同国家的一些奇特节日"准确概括了文章主旨。B 项"解释如何前往西班牙和泰国"、C 项"比较西班牙和泰国文化"和 D 项"警示人们危险节日"均偏离主旨。故选 A。
