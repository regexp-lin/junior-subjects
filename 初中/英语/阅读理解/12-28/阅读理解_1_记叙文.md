> **12-28 第1篇**

# 阅读理解（记叙文）

**My Month as an Exchange Student**

① Last term, I took part in a student exchange programme. I went to a school in Australia for one month. I stayed with a local family. At first, I was nervous. My English was not perfect. What if nobody understood me? What if I made mistakes? But my host family was very kind. They smiled and said, "Take your time. We will help you."

② The school life was different from mine in China. In Australia, students choose many of their own subjects. They have art, music, and sport every week. In class, teachers often ask questions and expect students to discuss. We sat in circles or groups, not in rows. There was no morning reading aloud. Instead, we had "homeroom" — a short meeting where the teacher talked about news and plans for the day. I found it interesting but sometimes confusing.

③ I made friends with a girl named Emma. She showed me around the school. We had lunch together. She taught me some Australian slang. I taught her a few Chinese words. One day, we cooked dumplings in her kitchen. Her family had never tried real Chinese dumplings. They loved them! I felt proud. I was not just a visitor. I was a bridge between two cultures.

④ The hardest part was saying goodbye. On my last day, Emma gave me a small koala toy. She said, "Keep in touch." My host mother hugged me and said, "You will always be welcome here." I cried a little. I had learned so much — not only English, but also how people live, think, and care for each other in another country.

⑤ Now I am back in China. I still chat with Emma online. That month changed me. I am more confident in English. I understand that the world is big and full of different ways of life. I hope more students can have such an experience one day.

**注释：** host 寄宿家庭；homeroom 班会；slang 俚语；hug 拥抱

---

**Questions:**

1. How long did the writer stay in Australia?
   A. One week.
   B. One month.
   C. One term.
   D. One year.

2. What made the writer nervous at first?
   A. The food.
   B. Her English might not be good enough; she might make mistakes.
   C. The weather.
   D. The school building.

3. How was the Australian school different from the writer's school in China?
   A. Students did not study at all.
   B. Students chose their own subjects; classes had more discussion; no morning reading aloud.
   C. There were no teachers.
   D. School was only in the morning.

4. What did the writer and Emma do together?
   A. They only had lunch.
   B. Emma showed her around, they had lunch, learned each other's language, and cooked dumplings.
   C. They played sports only.
   D. They did homework every day.

5. What did the writer learn from the experience?
   A. Australian food is the best.
   B. She became more confident in English; the world has many different ways of life.
   C. Chinese schools are better.
   D. Exchange programmes are useless.

---

**Answers and Explanations:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "I went to a school in Australia for one month."

**【解析】** 原文明确说在澳大利亚待了一个月。A、C、D项时间均不正确。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "At first, I was nervous. My English was not perfect. What if nobody understood me? What if I made mistakes?"

**【解析】** 作者担心英语不够好、别人听不懂、会出错。A项食物、C项天气、D项校舍均非紧张原因。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "In Australia, students choose many of their own subjects... In class, teachers often ask questions and expect students to discuss... There was no morning reading aloud."

**【解析】** 澳洲学校学生可选课、课堂偏讨论、没有晨读。A项完全不学、C项没有老师、D项只在上午均与原文不符。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "She showed me around the school. We had lunch together. She taught me some Australian slang. I taught her a few Chinese words. One day, we cooked dumplings in her kitchen."

**【解析】** 她们一起逛校园、吃午饭、互教语言、一起做饺子。A、C、D项过于片面。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "I am more confident in English. I understand that the world is big and full of different ways of life."

**【解析】** 作者在英语上更自信，并意识到世界很大、生活方式多样。A、C、D项均未准确概括这一收获。故选B。
