# 阅读理解（说明文）

① In recent years, new energy vehicles (NEVs) have developed quickly around the world. Unlike traditional cars that use petrol or diesel, NEVs run on electricity or other clean energy. This change is helping to protect our environment and reduce air pollution in cities.

② Why are NEVs becoming popular? First, they produce no tailpipe emissions. When a petrol car runs, it releases harmful gases into the air. Electric cars do not. They are much cleaner. Second, electricity is often cheaper than petrol, so drivers can save money over time. Third, many governments now offer support to people who buy NEVs, such as tax cuts or subsidies (补贴). These policies encourage more people to choose green cars.

③ How do electric cars work? They use large batteries to store power. You charge the battery at home or at a charging station, similar to charging a phone. A fully charged battery can usually power the car for 200 to 500 kilometres. The range depends on the model and driving conditions. Some newer models can go even farther, and charging time has become shorter with better technology.

④ China is one of the largest markets for NEVs. In 2023, more than one third of new cars sold in China were electric or plug-in hybrids. Big cities like Beijing and Shanghai have built many charging stations to make it easier for drivers. Some schools and companies also have charging points in their parking lots.

⑤ The future of NEVs looks bright. More brands are making electric cars, and prices are slowly going down. As battery technology improves, cars will travel farther and charge faster. One day, perhaps most cars on the road will be electric — and our cities will be cleaner and quieter.

**注释：** subsidy 补贴；tailpipe 排气管；hybrid 混合动力车

---

**Questions:**

1. What is the main difference between NEVs and traditional cars?
   A. NEVs are always faster.
   B. NEVs use electricity or clean energy instead of petrol or diesel.
   C. NEVs cost more to buy.
   D. NEVs can only run in cities.

2. According to Paragraph ②, which is NOT a reason why NEVs are popular?
   A. They produce no tailpipe emissions.
   B. Drivers can save money because electricity is often cheaper.
   C. Governments offer support like tax cuts.
   D. They can fly for short distances.

3. How do you "fuel" an electric car?
   A. By filling it with petrol at a gas station.
   B. By charging the battery at home or at a charging station.
   C. By using solar panels only.
   D. By adding water to the engine.

4. What can we infer about China and NEVs?
   A. China does not like electric cars.
   B. China has a large market and many charging stations for NEVs.
   C. Only Beijing has charging stations.
   D. China stopped making petrol cars in 2023.

5. What is the writer's attitude toward the future of NEVs?
   A. Worried and doubtful.
   B. Hopeful and positive.
   C. Angry and disappointed.
   D. Sad and regretful.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "Unlike traditional cars that use petrol or diesel, NEVs run on electricity or other clean energy"

**【解析】** 原文明确指出新能源汽车与传统汽车的区别在于使用电力或清洁能源而非汽油或柴油。B项准确。A、C、D项原文未提及。故选B。

**2. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 细节理解（逆向/排除）

**【定位】** 第②段列举原因：无尾气排放、电费更便宜、政府补贴支持

**【解析】** A、B、C项均为原文提到的原因。D项"能短途飞行"纯属虚构，与NEVs无关。故选D。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "You charge the battery at home or at a charging station, similar to charging a phone"

**【解析】** 电动车通过在家或充电站给电池充电来"加油"。B项正确。A项为燃油车方式；C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第④段 "China is one of the largest markets for NEVs... more than one third of new cars sold... Beijing and Shanghai have built many charging stations... schools and companies also have charging points"

**【解析】** 可推知中国是NEV大市场，充电设施（充电站、充电桩）较多。B项正确。A项与文意相反；C项以偏概全；D项原文未提及。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断/写作意图

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "The future of NEVs looks bright... our cities will be cleaner and quieter"

**【解析】** 作者认为NEV前景光明，城市将更清洁安静，态度积极乐观。B项正确。A、C、D项均与作者态度不符。故选B。
