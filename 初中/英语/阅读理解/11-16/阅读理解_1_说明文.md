# 阅读理解（说明文）

**Why Do We Dream?**

① Every night when we sleep, our brain does not stop working. Most people dream for about two hours each night, though we often forget our dreams by morning. Scientists have studied dreams for a long time, but they still do not fully understand why we dream.

② One popular theory says that dreams help our brain process information. During the day, we see, hear, and learn many things. At night, our brain sorts through this information. It keeps what is important and throws away what is not. This is why students sometimes understand a difficult problem better after a good night's sleep.

③ Another theory suggests that dreams help us deal with emotions. If you feel worried or scared during the day, you might dream about those feelings at night. Some scientists believe this is the brain's way of practising how to handle difficult situations. It is like a safe space where you can face your fears without real danger.

④ There are also interesting facts about dreams. Most dreams happen during a stage of sleep called REM (Rapid Eye Movement). During REM sleep, our eyes move quickly under our closed eyelids, and our brain is almost as active as when we are awake. People who are woken up during REM sleep can usually remember their dreams clearly.

⑤ Although we still have much to learn about dreams, one thing is clear: they are an important part of how our brain works. So next time you have a strange dream, do not worry — your brain is just doing its job!

**注释：** theory 理论；emotion 情感；eyelid 眼皮

**Questions:**

1. How long do most people dream each night?
   A. About thirty minutes.
   B. About two hours.
   C. About five hours.
   D. The whole night.

2. According to Paragraph 2, what does the brain do with information at night?
   A. It forgets everything.
   B. It keeps all the information.
   C. It sorts through information, keeping what is important.
   D. It creates new information.

3. What does the third theory say about dreams?
   A. Dreams make us feel more worried.
   B. Dreams help us deal with emotions in a safe way.
   C. Dreams cause fear and danger.
   D. Dreams have nothing to do with feelings.

4. What happens during REM sleep?
   A. The brain stops working completely.
   B. Eyes move quickly and the brain is very active.
   C. People cannot dream at all.
   D. People always wake up.

5. What is the best title for this passage?
   A. How to Stop Dreaming
   B. The Danger of Dreams
   C. Why Do We Dream?
   D. Dreams and Nightmares

---

**Answers and Explanations:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "Most people dream for about two hours each night"

**【解析】** 原文明确指出大多数人每晚做梦约两个小时。A项"约30分钟"与原文不符；C项"约5小时"无依据；D项"整晚"夸大了事实。故选B。

**2. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "At night, our brain sorts through this information. It keeps what is important and throws away what is not."

**【解析】** 原文说大脑会整理信息，保留重要的、丢弃不重要的。A项"忘记一切"与原文矛盾；B项"保留所有信息"以偏概全；D项"创造新信息"原文未提及。故选C。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "Some scientists believe this is the brain's way of practising how to handle difficult situations. It is like a safe space where you can face your fears without real danger."

**【解析】** 原文说做梦是大脑练习处理困难情境的方式，像一个安全空间。A项"让人更担忧"与原文相反，梦帮助处理情绪；C项"造成恐惧和危险"曲解原文，原文说是没有真正危险的；D项"与感情无关"与原文矛盾。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "During REM sleep, our eyes move quickly under our closed eyelids, and our brain is almost as active as when we are awake."

**【解析】** 原文描述REM睡眠阶段眼球快速运动，大脑活跃程度接近清醒时。A项"大脑完全停止工作"与原文完全相反；C项"完全不做梦"与原文矛盾，大多数梦发生在REM阶段；D项"人总会醒来"原文未说。故选B。

**5. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 主旨大意

**【定位】** 全文围绕"为什么我们会做梦"展开，第①段提出问题，第②③④段分别给出不同理论解释

**【解析】** 文章主要探讨人类做梦的原因和相关科学理论，C项最能概括主旨。A项"如何停止做梦"文中未讨论；B项"梦的危险"与文章积极的基调不符；D项"梦和噩梦"过于笼统，文章重点是探讨做梦的原因。故选C。
