# 阅读理解（议论文）

① Should students be allowed to use mobile phones in class? This question has caused heated debate (争论) among teachers, parents and students. Some people argue that phones can be useful learning tools, while others believe they do more harm than good in the classroom. Let us look at both sides of the argument.

② Those who support phone use in class point out several benefits. First, smartphones can help students look up new words, take photos of the blackboard, and record the teacher's explanations for later review. In this digital age, many educational apps (应用) can make learning more interesting and effective. Second, parents argue that phones allow them to contact their children quickly in case of emergency (紧急情况). For these reasons, some schools have tried a "bring your own device" policy, allowing students to use phones for learning tasks only.

③ However, many teachers and experts strongly oppose (反对) phones in the classroom. They say that phones are a major distraction (干扰). Even when students are supposed to be taking notes, they may secretly check WeChat, play games, or watch videos. This not only affects their own learning but also disturbs classmates. Studies have shown that students who use phones during class often score lower on tests. Moreover, looking at screens for too long can harm young people's eyesight and sleep.

④ Another concern is fairness. Not every student can afford a smartphone. If phones become a required tool for class, students from poor families may feel left behind. Schools must think about how to treat all students equally.

⑤ In my opinion, the best approach is to have clear rules. Phones should be turned off and kept in bags during lessons. Students can use them only during breaks or for special projects when the teacher allows. This way, we keep the classroom focused while still making use of technology when it truly helps. Balance is the key — we should embrace technology, but not at the cost of concentration and good learning habits.

**注释：** debate 争论；emergency 紧急情况；oppose 反对；distraction 干扰

---

### 选择题

1. According to paragraph ②, which of the following is a benefit of using phones in class?
   A. Students can look up words and record the teacher's explanations for review.
   B. Students can play games during the lesson.
   C. Students can chat with friends on WeChat.
   D. Students can watch videos for fun.

2. Why do some teachers oppose phones in the classroom?
   A. Because phones are too expensive for students.
   B. Because phones distract students and affect their learning and test scores.
   C. Because phones can help parents contact children.
   D. Because phones make learning more interesting.

3. What does the word "distraction" in paragraph ③ most probably mean?
   A. Something that helps you focus.
   B. A kind of learning tool.
   C. A type of educational app.
   D. Something that takes your attention away from what you should do.

4. What can we infer from paragraph ④?
   A. All students have smartphones.
   B. Phones from poor families work less well.
   C. Rich students learn better than poor students.
   D. Schools should consider fairness — not every student can afford a phone.

5. What is the writer's suggestion about phone use in class?
   A. Students should never bring phones to school.
   B. Students should use phones freely in every lesson.
   C. Phones should be off and in bags during lessons; use them only during breaks or when the teacher allows.
   D. Only teachers should use phones in the classroom.

---

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "smartphones can help students look up new words, take photos of the blackboard, and record the teacher's explanations for later review"

**【解析】** 原文列举了查词、拍板书、录音便于复习等好处。B 项玩游戏、C 项聊天、D 项看视频娱乐均为反对理由中的干扰行为，非支持方所说的益处。故选 A。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "phones are a major distraction... affects their own learning but also disturbs classmates... students who use phones during class often score lower on tests"

**【解析】** 反对者认为手机会分散注意力、影响学习、打扰他人、导致成绩下降。A 项"太贵"在第④段涉及公平性，非反对主因；C 项是支持方理由；D 项与反对观点矛盾。故选 B。

**3. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 词义猜测

**【定位】** 第③段 "phones are a major distraction" 及 "they may secretly check WeChat, play games, or watch videos"

**【解析】** 语境表明手机让学生注意力从听课转向聊天、游戏、视频，distraction 意为"令人分心的事物"。A 项"帮助集中"与语境相反；B、C 项均不符合。故选 D。

**4. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第④段 "Not every student can afford a smartphone... students from poor families may feel left behind... Schools must think about how to treat all students equally"

**【解析】** 作者指出并非所有学生都能买得起智能手机，学校需考虑公平对待所有学生。A 项"所有学生都有"与原文矛盾；B 项"穷人家的手机质量差"、C 项"富裕学生学得更好"均为过度推断。故选 D。

**5. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 写作意图/主旨大意

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "Phones should be turned off and kept in bags during lessons. Students can use them only during breaks or for special projects when the teacher allows... Balance is the key"

**【解析】** 作者建议上课时关机放包内，仅限课间或教师允许的特殊项目使用，强调平衡。A 项"永不带手机"过于极端；B 项"自由使用"与作者建议相反；D 项"仅教师用"文中未提。故选 C。
