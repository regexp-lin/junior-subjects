# 阅读理解（说明文）

**The Winter Solstice: An Important Day in China**

① The Winter Solstice, or Dongzhi in Chinese, is one of the 24 solar terms in China. It usually falls on December 21 or 22. On this day, the Northern Hemisphere has the shortest daytime and the longest night of the year. After the Winter Solstice, the days become longer and longer. In ancient China, people believed that from this day on, positive energy would grow stronger.

② In northern China, people eat dumplings on the Winter Solstice. There is an old saying: "Eat dumplings on the Winter Solstice, or your ears will freeze." Long ago, a famous doctor helped the poor by making dumplings that looked like ears. He hoped eating them would keep people's ears warm in the cold winter. Today, families still make dumplings together on this day. It is a time for reunion and warmth.

③ In southern China, people eat tangyuan, or sweet rice balls. Tangyuan are round, which means "reunion" and "wholeness" in Chinese culture. Families sit together, make tangyuan, and boil them in sweet soup. Eating tangyuan on the Winter Solstice brings the hope that the family will stay together and everything will go well.

④ Many families also worship their ancestors on this day. They prepare food, light candles, and show respect to those who have passed away. This custom helps people remember their roots and feel connected to their family history.

⑤ The Winter Solstice is not just about food. It is a day that connects the past and the present. It reminds us of the importance of family, tradition, and the changing of seasons.

**注释：** solstice 至日；hemisphere 半球；ancestor 祖先；worship 祭祀

**Questions:**

1. When does the Winter Solstice usually come?
   A. December 1 or 2.
   B. December 21 or 22.
   C. January 1.
   D. November 21 or 22.

2. Why do people in northern China eat dumplings on the Winter Solstice?
   A. Because dumplings are cheap.
   B. Because an old story says eating dumplings can keep ears warm in winter.
   C. Because there is no other food.
   D. Because the government asks them to.

3. What do people in southern China eat on the Winter Solstice?
   A. Dumplings.
   B. Tangyuan.
   C. Rice.
   D. Noodles.

4. What does the round shape of tangyuan mean?
   A. Cold weather.
   B. Reunion and wholeness.
   C. Long nights.
   D. Good luck only.

5. What do many families do to remember their ancestors on this day?
   A. Go travelling.
   B. Prepare food, light candles, and show respect.
   C. Play games.
   D. Buy new clothes.

---

**Answers and Explanations:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "It usually falls on December 21 or 22."

**【解析】** 原文明确说冬至通常在12月21日或22日。A、C、D项日期均不正确。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "There is an old saying: 'Eat dumplings on the Winter Solstice, or your ears will freeze.' Long ago, a famous doctor... hoped eating them would keep people's ears warm"

**【解析】** 俗语和故事说明吃饺子是为了在寒冬保持耳朵温暖。A项便宜、C项没有别的食物、D项政府要求均非原因。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "In southern China, people eat tangyuan, or sweet rice balls."

**【解析】** 南方人在冬至吃汤圆（甜米团）。A项饺子是北方的；C项米饭、D项面条原文未提及。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "Tangyuan are round, which means 'reunion' and 'wholeness' in Chinese culture."

**【解析】** 汤圆圆形在中国文化中象征团圆和圆满。A项寒冷、C项长夜、D项仅好运均不完整。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "They prepare food, light candles, and show respect to those who have passed away."

**【解析】** 祭祀祖先时人们准备食物、点蜡烛、表达敬意。A项去旅行、C项玩游戏、D项买新衣均与祭祀无关。故选B。
