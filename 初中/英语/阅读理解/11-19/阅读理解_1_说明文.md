# 阅读理解（说明文）

**Waste Sorting: Why It Matters**

① Every day, people in China produce millions of tonnes of rubbish. In the past, most of it was simply buried underground or burned. But these methods cause serious pollution. To solve this problem, many cities in China have started waste sorting programmes. Shanghai was one of the first, and now Guangzhou, Shenzhen, and Beijing have followed.

② Waste sorting means dividing rubbish into different groups before throwing it away. In most Chinese cities, waste is sorted into four types: recyclable waste (paper, plastic bottles, glass), kitchen waste (leftover food, fruit peels), hazardous waste (batteries, medicine, light bulbs), and other waste (tissues, broken ceramics). Each type goes into a different coloured bin.

③ Why is sorting so important? First, it helps us recycle useful materials. A plastic bottle can be turned into new products instead of polluting the ocean. Second, kitchen waste can be made into fertiliser for farming. Third, hazardous waste needs special treatment. If batteries are mixed with ordinary rubbish, harmful chemicals may leak into the soil and water.

④ However, waste sorting is not easy. Many people find it confusing. Which bin does a milk carton go into? What about a used tissue? Some cities have created apps and guides to help people sort correctly. Schools also teach students about waste sorting through classes and activities.

⑤ Waste sorting may seem like a small thing, but it has a big impact on our planet. If everyone does their part, we can reduce pollution, save resources, and build a cleaner future for the next generation.

**注释：** hazardous 有害的；fertiliser 肥料；ceramics 陶瓷

**Questions:**

1. Which city was one of the first in China to start waste sorting?
   A. Guangzhou.
   B. Shenzhen.
   C. Shanghai.
   D. Beijing.

2. Into how many types is waste usually sorted in Chinese cities?
   A. Two.
   B. Three.
   C. Four.
   D. Five.

3. What can kitchen waste be turned into?
   A. Plastic bottles.
   B. Light bulbs.
   C. Fertiliser.
   D. Glass.

4. What may happen if batteries are mixed with ordinary rubbish?
   A. The rubbish will become lighter.
   B. Harmful chemicals may leak into the soil and water.
   C. The batteries will be recycled automatically.
   D. Nothing bad will happen.

5. What is the writer's attitude towards waste sorting?
   A. It is a waste of time.
   B. It is too difficult to do.
   C. It is important and everyone should do their part.
   D. Only scientists need to care about it.

---

**Answers and Explanations:**

**1. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "Shanghai was one of the first, and now Guangzhou, Shenzhen, and Beijing have followed."

**【解析】** 原文明确说上海是最早开始的城市之一。A项广州、B项深圳、D项北京是后来跟进的城市。故选C。

**2. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "In most Chinese cities, waste is sorted into four types: recyclable waste... kitchen waste... hazardous waste... and other waste."

**【解析】** 原文明确列出四类：可回收物、厨余垃圾、有害垃圾和其他垃圾。A项两类、B项三类、D项五类均不正确。故选C。

**3. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "kitchen waste can be made into fertiliser for farming."

**【解析】** 原文说厨余垃圾可以制成农业肥料。A项塑料瓶是可回收物的例子；B项灯泡属于有害垃圾；D项玻璃属于可回收物。故选C。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "If batteries are mixed with ordinary rubbish, harmful chemicals may leak into the soil and water."

**【解析】** 原文说如果电池和普通垃圾混在一起，有害化学物质可能渗入土壤和水中。A项"垃圾变轻"毫无依据；C项"自动回收"属于无中生有；D项"什么坏事都不会发生"与原文相反。故选B。

**5. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "Waste sorting may seem like a small thing, but it has a big impact on our planet. If everyone does their part, we can reduce pollution, save resources, and build a cleaner future."

**【解析】** 作者在结尾强调垃圾分类影响重大，呼吁每个人都尽自己的一份力，态度是积极支持的。A项"浪费时间"与作者态度相反；B项"太难做"不是作者的结论；D项"只有科学家需要关心"与"每个人都应参与"矛盾。故选C。
