# 阅读理解（记叙文）

**The Race I Will Never Forget**

① Our school held its annual sports meeting last month. I signed up for the 800-metre race. I had been practising every morning for three weeks. I wanted to win, not just for myself, but for our class.

② On the day of the race, the sun was shining brightly. Twelve runners stood at the starting line. I felt my heart beating fast. When the whistle blew, everyone rushed forward. I started at a steady pace, staying in the middle of the group. My coach had told me, "Don't run too fast at the beginning. Save your energy for the last 200 metres."

③ By the time we reached the halfway point, I was in fifth place. My legs felt heavy, and my chest was burning. I wanted to slow down. But then I heard my classmates shouting, "Come on! You can do it!" Their voices gave me strength. I pushed harder and moved up to third place.

④ In the final 200 metres, I gave everything I had. I passed one more runner and finished in second place. I did not win the gold medal, but when I crossed the finish line, my classmates ran to me and lifted me up. They were cheering as if I had won first place.

⑤ That moment taught me something important: winning is not always about being first. It is about trying your best and having people who believe in you. I will never forget that race — not because of the result, but because of the feeling.

---

**Questions:**

1. How long did the writer practise before the race?
   A. One week.
   B. Two weeks.
   C. Three weeks.
   D. One month.

2. What advice did the coach give the writer?
   A. Run as fast as possible from the start.
   B. Stay at the back of the group.
   C. Don't run too fast at the beginning; save energy for the last part.
   D. Give up if you feel tired.

3. What happened at the halfway point?
   A. The writer was in first place.
   B. The writer wanted to slow down but was encouraged by classmates.
   C. The writer stopped running.
   D. The writer fell down.

4. What place did the writer finish in?
   A. First.
   B. Second.
   C. Third.
   D. Last.

5. What lesson did the writer learn from the race?
   A. Winning the gold medal is the only thing that matters.
   B. You should never enter a race.
   C. Trying your best and having support from others are more important than being first.
   D. Practising is a waste of time.

---

**Answers and Explanations:**

**1. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "I had been practising every morning for three weeks."

**【解析】** 原文明确说作者练了三周。A项一周、B项两周、D项一个月均与原文不符。故选C。

**2. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "My coach had told me, 'Don't run too fast at the beginning. Save your energy for the last 200 metres.'"

**【解析】** 教练建议开始不要跑太快，把体力留给最后200米。A项"一开始就尽全力"与教练建议相反；B项"留在队伍后面"原文未说；D项"累了就放弃"与教练鼓励性的建议不符。故选C。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "My legs felt heavy, and my chest was burning. I wanted to slow down. But then I heard my classmates shouting, 'Come on! You can do it!'"

**【解析】** 跑到一半时作者很累想减速，但同学们的加油声给了他力量。A项"第一名"不对，他是第五名；C项"停止跑步"他没有停；D项"摔倒"原文未提及。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "I passed one more runner and finished in second place."

**【解析】** 原文明确说作者又超过一个人，最终以第二名完赛。A项第一、C项第三、D项最后一名均不正确。故选B。

**5. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "winning is not always about being first. It is about trying your best and having people who believe in you."

**【解析】** 作者总结道赢不一定是第一名，而是尽自己最大努力并拥有相信你的人。A项"只有金牌重要"与作者感悟相反；B项"不该参赛"无依据；D项"练习浪费时间"与文章矛盾。故选C。
