# 阅读理解（说明文）

**Bees and Pollination: A Hidden Partnership**

① When you bite into an apple or enjoy a bowl of strawberries, you might not think about where they come from. But did you know that about one-third of the food we eat depends on bees? Bees play a vital role in growing fruits, vegetables, and nuts through a process called pollination.

② Pollination happens when pollen is moved from one part of a flower to another. This allows the plant to produce seeds and fruit. While some plants rely on wind for pollination, many depend on insects — and bees are the best at this job. When a bee lands on a flower to drink nectar, tiny grains of pollen stick to its body. As the bee flies to the next flower, it carries the pollen along and drops some of it, completing the pollination.

③ Bees are incredibly hardworking. A single honeybee can visit up to 5,000 flowers in one day. A colony of bees may have 50,000 to 60,000 members, and they work together in an organized way. The queen bee lays eggs. Worker bees collect food, build the hive, and protect the colony. Each bee has a clear role.

④ Sadly, bee populations around the world are declining. Pesticides used in farming can poison bees. Loss of natural habitats means fewer wildflowers for bees to feed on. Climate change also affects the timing of flowers blooming, which can leave bees without food. Scientists warn that if bees disappear, many crops will fail, and food prices will rise sharply.

⑤ We can all help protect bees. Planting flowers in gardens and balconies gives bees more food sources. Avoiding harmful pesticides is important too. Some cities have started "bee-friendly" programmes, encouraging people to keep small beehives on rooftops. Every small effort counts when it comes to protecting these tiny but important creatures.

**注释：** pollination 授粉；pollen 花粉；nectar 花蜜；pesticide 杀虫剂；habitat 栖息地

**Questions:**

1. About how much of our food depends on bees?
   A. One-fifth.
   B. One-quarter.
   C. One-third.
   D. One-half.

2. What happens during pollination?
   A. Bees build new hives.
   B. Pollen is moved from one flower to another, helping plants produce seeds and fruit.
   C. Flowers change colour.
   D. Bees make honey from water.

3. How many flowers can a single honeybee visit in one day?
   A. Up to 500.
   B. Up to 2,000.
   C. Up to 5,000.
   D. Up to 10,000.

4. What is one reason bee populations are declining?
   A. Bees are eating too much.
   B. Pesticides used in farming can poison bees.
   C. There are too many flowers.
   D. Bees prefer to live in cities.

5. What does the writer suggest we do to help bees?
   A. Use more pesticides.
   B. Cut down more trees.
   C. Plant flowers and avoid harmful pesticides.
   D. Keep bees away from gardens.

---

**Answers and Explanations:**

**1. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "about one-third of the food we eat depends on bees"

**【解析】** 原文说我们吃的食物约三分之一依赖蜜蜂。A项五分之一、B项四分之一、D项一半均与原文不符。故选C。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "Pollination happens when pollen is moved from one part of a flower to another. This allows the plant to produce seeds and fruit."

**【解析】** 授粉是花粉从一朵花转移到另一朵花，帮助植物产生种子和果实。A项建蜂巢是蜜蜂的其他行为；C项花变色和D项用水制蜜均无依据。故选B。

**3. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "A single honeybee can visit up to 5,000 flowers in one day."

**【解析】** 原文说一只蜜蜂一天可以拜访多达5000朵花。A项500、B项2000、D项10000均不正确。故选C。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "Pesticides used in farming can poison bees."

**【解析】** 原文指出农药会毒害蜜蜂。A项"蜜蜂吃太多"无依据；C项"花太多"与"fewer wildflowers"矛盾；D项"蜜蜂喜欢城市"不是数量下降原因。故选B。

**5. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "Planting flowers in gardens and balconies gives bees more food sources. Avoiding harmful pesticides is important too."

**【解析】** 作者建议种花并避免使用有害农药来帮助蜜蜂。A项"使用更多农药"与建议相反；B项"砍更多树"会减少栖息地；D项"让蜜蜂远离花园"与保护蜜蜂的目的矛盾。故选C。
