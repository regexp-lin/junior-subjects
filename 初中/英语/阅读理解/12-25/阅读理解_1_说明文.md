# 阅读理解（说明文）

**Robots: From Factories to Our Homes**

① Robots are machines that can do tasks automatically. They do not get tired or bored. They can work 24 hours a day. In the past, robots were mainly used in factories. They welded car parts, packed goods, and did dangerous jobs. Today, robots have entered our daily life in many new ways.

② In hospitals, robots help doctors with surgeries. They can make very small cuts that human hands cannot. This helps patients recover faster. In schools, some robots teach children to code. They answer questions and make learning fun. In restaurants, robots can deliver food from the kitchen to the table. In some hotels, robots greet guests and carry their bags.

③ At home, we see more and more robots too. Cleaning robots move around the floor and sweep or mop. They avoid furniture and can find their way back to charge. Some families have robot vacuum cleaners. They set a time, and the robot cleans the room when everyone is out. Pet robots can play with dogs or cats when the owners are busy. They help reduce loneliness for both pets and people.

④ How do robots "think"? They use sensors to collect information. Cameras help them "see." Microphones help them "hear." A computer inside processes the data and sends commands to the motors. The robot then moves, picks up objects, or speaks. Advances in artificial intelligence (AI) help robots learn from experience. They get better over time.

⑤ Robots will continue to change our world. They may do more housework, teach more subjects, and even care for the elderly. But robots cannot replace human warmth and creativity. The best future is one where humans and robots work together.

**注释：** weld 焊接；surgery 手术；vacuum 真空吸尘器；sensor 传感器；artificial intelligence 人工智能

---

**Questions:**

1. Where were robots mainly used in the past?
   A. In hospitals.
   B. In schools.
   C. In factories.
   D. At home.

2. How can robots help in hospitals?
   A. By cooking meals.
   B. By helping doctors with surgeries and making small cuts.
   C. By cleaning the floor.
   D. By greeting guests.

3. What can cleaning robots do at home?
   A. Cook food.
   B. Move around, sweep or mop, and avoid furniture.
   C. Teach children.
   D. Play music.

4. How do robots "see" and "hear"?
   A. With human eyes and ears.
   B. With sensors: cameras and microphones.
   C. By reading books.
   D. By asking people.

5. What does the writer think about the future of robots?
   A. Robots will replace humans completely.
   B. Humans and robots will work together; robots cannot replace human warmth.
   C. Robots will disappear soon.
   D. Only factories will use robots.

---

**Answers and Explanations:**

**1. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "In the past, robots were mainly used in factories."

**【解析】** 过去机器人主要用在工厂。A项医院、B项学校、D项家庭是近年才增多的用途。故选C。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "In hospitals, robots help doctors with surgeries. They can make very small cuts that human hands cannot."

**【解析】** 医院里机器人协助手术，能做人工难以完成的微小切口。A项做饭、C项拖地、D项迎宾均非医院用途。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "Cleaning robots move around the floor and sweep or mop. They avoid furniture and can find their way back to charge."

**【解析】** 清扫机器人能在地面移动、扫地或拖地，并避障、自动回充。A项做饭、C项教学、D项播放音乐均非其功能。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "They use sensors to collect information. Cameras help them 'see.' Microphones help them 'hear.'"

**【解析】** 机器人通过传感器获取信息，用摄像头"看"，用麦克风"听"。A项人眼人耳、C项读书、D项问人均与原文不符。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "But robots cannot replace human warmth and creativity. The best future is one where humans and robots work together."

**【解析】** 机器人无法取代人的温情与创造力，人机协作才是最佳未来。A项完全取代、C项很快消失、D项仅工厂使用均与原文矛盾。故选B。
