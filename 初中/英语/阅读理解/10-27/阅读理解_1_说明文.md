# 阅读理解（说明文）

**The World's Longest Rivers**

① Rivers are important to life on Earth. They provide water for drinking, farming, and industry. They have also shaped human history. Many great civilisations grew along river banks. Among all the rivers in the world, which one is the longest? The answer might surprise you.

② The Nile in Africa is often said to be the longest river. It flows through eleven countries, from Lake Victoria in the east to the Mediterranean Sea in the north. For a long time, it was believed to be about 6,650 kilometres long. However, measuring a river is not easy. Scientists disagree on where a river truly begins and ends. Some say we should measure from the farthest source; others use different rules.

③ The Amazon in South America is another giant. It carries more water than any other river. Some recent studies suggest that if we measure from a certain source in Peru, the Amazon could be longer than the Nile. But this is still debated. What we know for sure is that both rivers are enormous. They support millions of people and countless species of plants and animals.

④ Asia has its own great rivers. The Yangtze in China is the longest river in Asia and the third longest in the world. It runs about 6,300 kilometres from the Tibetan Plateau to the East China Sea. The Yellow River, also in China, is often called the "cradle of Chinese civilisation" because it was the birthplace of ancient Chinese culture.

⑤ No matter which river holds the title of "longest," each one is unique and vital. Rivers connect mountains to seas, forests to cities, and people to each other. Protecting these rivers is one of the most important tasks for our future.

**注释：** Mediterranean 地中海的；source 源头；plateau 高原

**Questions:**

1. According to Paragraph ②, why is it hard to decide which river is the longest?
   A. Because all rivers are the same length.
   B. Because scientists disagree on where a river begins and ends.
   C. Because no one has ever measured rivers.
   D. Because rivers change every day.

2. What is special about the Amazon?
   A. It is in Africa.
   B. It carries more water than any other river.
   C. It is the shortest river.
   D. It has no fish.

3. Which river is called the "cradle of Chinese civilisation"?
   A. The Nile.
   B. The Amazon.
   C. The Yangtze.
   D. The Yellow River.

4. How long is the Yangtze River approximately?
   A. 6,000 km.
   B. About 6,300 km.
   C. 7,000 km.
   D. 5,000 km.

5. What can we infer from the last paragraph?
   A. Rivers are not important.
   B. All rivers are valuable and need to be protected.
   C. We should only care about the longest river.
   D. Rivers will never be damaged.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第②段 "measuring a river is not easy. Scientists disagree on where a river truly begins and ends"
**【解析】** 测量困难在于科学家对河流起止点存在分歧。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第③段 "The Amazon... carries more water than any other river"
**【解析】** 亚马孙河的水量超过其他任何河流。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】D**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第④段 "The Yellow River... is often called the 'cradle of Chinese civilisation'"
**【解析】** 黄河被称为"中华文明的摇篮"。D项正确。A、B、C项与原文不符。故选D。

**4. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第④段 "It runs about 6,300 kilometres"
**【解析】** 长江约6300公里。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 推理判断
**【定位】** 第⑤段 "each one is unique and vital... Protecting these rivers is one of the most important tasks"
**【解析】** 结尾强调每条河都重要，保护河流是未来重要任务。B项正确。A、C、D项与文意不符。故选B。
