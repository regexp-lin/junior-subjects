> **08-18 第2篇**

# 阅读理解（记叙文）

① It was the day of the school sports meeting. Chen Yue, a thin girl from Class 2, stood at the starting line of the 800-meter race. She was not a fast runner, but she had signed up anyway because no one else in her class wanted to run such a long distance.

② When the gun went off, all the runners dashed forward. Chen Yue fell behind quickly. By the time she reached the first turn, most of the other girls were already far ahead. Her legs felt heavy, and she wanted to stop. The sun was burning, and sweat (汗水) ran down her face. She thought about giving up.

③ Then she heard a voice from the side of the track. "Come on, Chen Yue! You can do it!" It was her classmate Liu Fang, running alongside the track and cheering for her. Soon, more students from Class 2 joined in. "Keep going! We believe in you!" they shouted. Their voices gave Chen Yue new energy. She told herself, "I don't have to be the fastest. I just have to finish."

④ The last 200 meters were the hardest. Her chest hurt, and her legs felt like they were made of stone. But she did not stop. Step by step, she moved forward. When she finally crossed the finish line, she was the last one to finish. But her whole class was waiting for her, clapping and cheering as if she had won first place. Liu Fang ran over and hugged her. "You did it! You're amazing!"

⑤ Chen Yue did not win any medal (奖牌) that day, but she won something more important — the respect of her classmates and confidence in herself. She learned that courage is not about being the best; it is about not giving up when things get hard. That 800-meter race became one of her proudest memories.

**注释：** dash 冲刺；sweat 汗水；track 跑道；chest 胸膛；medal 奖牌；courage 勇气

---

**Questions:**

1. Why did Chen Yue sign up for the 800-meter race?
   A. Because she was the fastest runner in her class.
   B. Because no one else in her class wanted to run.
   C. Because her teacher asked her to.
   D. Because she wanted to win a medal.

2. How did Chen Yue feel during the first part of the race?
   A. Excited and happy.
   B. Relaxed and confident.
   C. Tired and wanting to give up.
   D. Angry and disappointed.

3. What helped Chen Yue keep running?
   A. The prize at the finish line.
   B. The cheering from her classmates.
   C. The cool weather.
   D. The coach's advice.

4. What happened when Chen Yue crossed the finish line?
   A. No one noticed her.
   B. The teacher was angry because she was last.
   C. Her classmates clapped and cheered for her.
   D. She received a gold medal.

5. What does the story mainly tell us?
   A. Running is the most important sport in school.
   B. Only winners deserve respect.
   C. Courage means not giving up when things are difficult.
   D. Students should not sign up for races they cannot win.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段最后一句 "she had signed up anyway because no one else in her class wanted to run such a long distance."

**【解析】** 原文明确说明陈悦报名是因为班里没有其他人愿意跑这么远。A项"她是班里最快的"与原文"She was not a fast runner"矛盾；C项"老师让她跑"属于无中生有；D项"想赢奖牌"原文未提及此动机。故选B。

**2. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第②段 "Her legs felt heavy, and she wanted to stop...She thought about giving up."

**【解析】** 原文描述她腿沉重、想停下来、想放弃，可推断她感到疲惫并想放弃。A项"兴奋快乐"与原文描述相反；B项"放松自信"与原文矛盾；D项"愤怒失望"原文无此情绪。故选C。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "Their voices gave Chen Yue new energy."

**【解析】** 原文明确说同学们的加油声给了她新的力量。A项"终点线的奖品"原文未提及；C项"凉爽的天气"与原文"The sun was burning"矛盾；D项"教练的建议"原文未提及。故选B。

**4. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "her whole class was waiting for her, clapping and cheering as if she had won first place."

**【解析】** 原文描述全班同学等着她、鼓掌欢呼。A项"没人注意到她"与原文相反；B项"老师生气"属于无中生有；D项"获得金牌"与第⑤段"did not win any medal"矛盾。故选C。

**5. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 主旨大意

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "courage is not about being the best; it is about not giving up when things get hard."

**【解析】** 文章通过陈悦坚持跑完比赛的故事，告诉读者勇气是在困难面前不放弃。A项"跑步是学校最重要的运动"以偏概全；B项"只有赢家才值得尊重"与文意相反；D项"不该报名参加赢不了的比赛"与文章传递的正能量相悖。故选C。
