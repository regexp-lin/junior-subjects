# 阅读理解（议论文）

① Many people say that keeping a diary is a good habit. Some write every day; others write only when something special happens. Should we write a diary every day? Let's look at both sides of the argument.

② Those who support daily diary writing believe it has many benefits. First, writing every day helps improve your language skills. The more you write, the better you become at expressing your thoughts. Second, a diary can be a friend. When you feel happy, sad, or worried, writing it down can make you feel better. It is like talking to someone who always listens. Third, a diary helps you remember the past. Years later, you can look back and see how you have changed. Finally, writing a diary teaches you to reflect (反思) on your day. You may ask yourself: What did I learn today? What could I do better? Such questions help you grow.

③ However, some people disagree. They say that writing every day can become a chore (琐事). If you force yourself to write when you have nothing to say, the diary may become boring and you may give up. Besides, not everyone has time to write every day. Students have a lot of homework, and some days are so busy that finding time for a diary is difficult. They suggest that writing two or three times a week is enough. Quality matters more than quantity.

④ I think the best approach is to find what works for you. If you enjoy writing every day and it does not feel like a burden, go ahead. If you prefer writing when something meaningful happens, that is fine too. The important thing is to keep the habit over time. A diary is a tool to help you, not a task to stress you out.

⑤ Whether you write every day or once a week, the key is to be honest with yourself and to enjoy the process. A diary should be your own space — use it in the way that feels right for you.

**注释：** reflect 反思；chore 琐事；burden 负担

**Questions:**

1. According to Paragraph ②, how does daily diary writing help improve your skills?
   A. It makes you read more books.
   B. The more you write, the better you become at expressing your thoughts.
   C. It helps you make more friends.
   D. It teaches you to draw pictures.

2. Why do some people think writing a diary every day is not always good?
   A. Because diaries cost too much money.
   B. Because it can become a chore and students may not have time every day.
   C. Because teachers don't allow it.
   D. Because diaries are too heavy to carry.

3. What does the word "it" in Paragraph ② refer to? ("writing it down can make you feel better")
   A. The diary book.
   B. Your feelings (happy, sad, or worried).
   C. Your homework.
   D. The pen.

4. What is the writer's opinion about diary writing?
   A. Everyone must write a diary every day.
   B. Writing a diary is a waste of time.
   C. Find what works for you; the key is to keep the habit and enjoy it.
   D. Only adults should keep diaries.

5. Which of the following would the writer most likely agree with?
   A. A diary should stress you out.
   B. Quality of writing matters more than writing every day for some people.
   C. Students should never write diaries.
   D. Diaries must be kept secret from everyone.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "The more you write, the better you become at expressing your thoughts"

**【解析】** 原文说明写得越多，表达思想的能力就越好。A项读书、C项交友、D项画画均未提及。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "writing every day can become a chore" 和 "not everyone has time to write every day. Students have a lot of homework"

**【解析】** 反对者认为每天写可能变成负担，且学生作业多、未必每天有时间。A、C、D项原文均未提及。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 词义猜测

**【定位】** 第②段 "When you feel happy, sad, or worried, writing it down can make you feel better"

**【解析】** 根据上下文，"it"指代前文提到的感受（开心、难过或担心）。A、C、D项在语境中不合理。故选B。

**4. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第④段 "the best approach is to find what works for you... The important thing is to keep the habit over time... A diary is a tool to help you, not a task to stress you out"

**【解析】** 作者认为应找到适合自己的方式，关键是长期坚持并享受过程。A项"必须每天写"过于绝对；B项与作者观点矛盾；D项无中生有。故选C。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第③段 "They suggest that writing two or three times a week is enough. Quality matters more than quantity" 及第④段作者认同"find what works for you"

**【解析】** 作者认同因人而异，部分人可能更适合重视质量而非每天写。A项与"not a task to stress you out"矛盾；C、D项原文未涉及。故选B。
