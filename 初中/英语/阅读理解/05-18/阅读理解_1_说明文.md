# 阅读理解（说明文）

① Ants are small insects, but they are among the most successful animals on Earth. One reason for their success is their amazing social structure. Ants live in large groups called colonies (蚁群). Each colony works like a team where every ant has a clear job. Scientists have studied ants for many years and found that their teamwork is one of the best in the animal world.

② In an ant colony, there is usually one queen. Her main job is to lay eggs so the colony can grow. Most of the other ants are workers. Worker ants are all female. They build the nest, find food, and take care of the young ants. Some ants become soldiers. They protect the colony from enemies. Although each ant does different work, they all work for the good of the whole group.

③ Ants communicate with each other in special ways. They use chemicals called pheromones (信息素) to leave messages. When an ant finds food, it leaves a trail of pheromones on the ground. Other ants can follow this trail to the food. In this way, hundreds of ants can work together to carry a large piece of food back to the nest. No single ant could do this alone, but as a team they succeed.

④ Another example of ant teamwork is how they build their homes. Some ants work underground to dig tunnels. Others carry small pieces of dirt or sand to the surface. They do not have a boss telling them what to do — each ant knows its role and does it well. When heavy rain comes, worker ants quickly work together to block the entrances and protect the colony.

⑤ Ants teach us an important lesson: when people work together and each person does their part, great things can happen. Teamwork makes both ant colonies and human groups stronger. Next time you see ants marching in a line, remember that they are showing us the power of cooperation.

**注释：** colony 蚁群；pheromone 信息素；tunnel 隧道

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段第2-3句 "One reason for their success is their amazing social structure. Ants live in large groups called colonies. Each colony works like a team where every ant has a clear job."

**【解析】** 原文明确指出蚂蚁成功的原因之一是其出色的社会结构和团队协作。A 项"体型最大"原文未提及；C 项"寿命更长"和 D 项"飞得快跑得快"均与文章无关。故选 B。

**2. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "Her main job is to lay eggs so the colony can grow."

**【解析】** 原文清楚说明蚁后的主要工作是产卵以使蚁群壮大。A 项"筑巢和觅食"、B 项"保护蚁群"和 D 项"照顾幼蚁"均为工蚁的职责，非蚁后职责。故选 C。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "When an ant finds food, it leaves a trail of pheromones on the ground. Other ants can follow this trail to the food."

**【解析】** 原文描述蚂蚁通过在地上留下信息素痕迹，其他蚂蚁可循迹找到食物。A 项"大声呼叫"、C 项"等蚁后来帮忙"和 D 项"把食物掰成小块"均未在文中出现。故选 B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第④段 "They do not have a boss telling them what to do — each ant knows its role and does it well."

**【解析】** 原文说明蚂蚁没有老板指挥，每只蚂蚁清楚自己的角色并做好分内之事，可推断它们了解自身职责且无需他人吩咐即可工作。A 项"需要老板每天指挥"与原文"do not have a boss"直接矛盾；C 项"只在下雨时工作"和 D 项"从不在地下工作"均与第④段内容不符。故选 B。

**5. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 主旨大意

**【定位】** 全文：第①段引入蚂蚁社会结构，第②③④段分别介绍分工、沟通与协作实例，第⑤段总结团队合作的重要性。

**【解析】** 全文围绕蚂蚁的社会结构、分工协作及团队精神展开，C 项准确概括主旨。A 项"比较蚂蚁与其他昆虫"、B 项"介绍蚂蚁研究史"和 D 项"教读者养蚂蚁"文中均未涉及。故选 C。
