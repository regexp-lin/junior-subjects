> **12-04 第2篇**

# 阅读理解（记叙文）

**My First Speech Contest**

① Last spring, our English teacher asked who wanted to join the school speech contest. I was shy and never spoke in front of many people, but something made me put up my hand. I chose the topic "My Favourite Book" and started to prepare.

② I worked on my speech for three weeks. I wrote it, changed it many times, and practised in front of the mirror. My mother listened to me every night and gave me advice. "Speak slowly," she said. "Look at the audience, not at your paper." I tried my best, but I was still very nervous.

③ The day of the contest came. When I walked onto the stage, my hands were shaking. I took a deep breath and began to speak. For the first minute, my voice sounded strange. Then I looked at my classmates in the front row. They were smiling and nodding. I felt a little better and went on.

④ I finished my speech. The judge said, "Well done!" I did not win first place, but I got third. More importantly, I learned that I could do something I had never thought I could do before.

⑤ Now I am not so afraid of speaking in public. I sometimes help new students prepare for their first speech. If you are afraid to try something new, remember: the first step is always the hardest, but it is worth it.

---

**Questions:**

1. What made the writer join the speech contest?
   A. His mother told him to.
   B. He wanted to win a prize.
   C. Something made him put up his hand, though he was shy.
   D. His teacher chose him.

2. How did the writer prepare for the contest?
   A. He practised once.
   B. He worked on it for three weeks, wrote, rewrote, and practised in front of the mirror.
   C. He only read the speech.
   D. He asked his father for help.

3. How did the writer feel when he first went on stage?
   A. Happy and relaxed.
   B. His hands were shaking and he was very nervous.
   C. Full of confidence.
   D. A little sleepy.

4. What place did the writer get in the contest?
   A. First place.
   B. Second place.
   C. Third place.
   D. He did not get any place.

5. What did the writer learn from the experience?
   A. He learned that winning is the most important thing.
   B. He learned that he could do something he had never thought he could do before.
   C. He learned that he should never try again.
   D. He learned that his mother was right about everything.

---

**Answers and Explanations:**

**1. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "I was shy and never spoke in front of many people, but something made me put up my hand."

**【解析】** 原文说作者虽害羞，但有某种力量促使他举手参加。A项母亲要求、B项想获奖、D项老师选中均无依据。故选C。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "I worked on my speech for three weeks. I wrote it, changed it many times, and practised in front of the mirror."

**【解析】** 原文描述准备三周、反复修改、对着镜子练习。A项只练一次、C项只读、D项找父亲帮忙均不完整。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "When I walked onto the stage, my hands were shaking... I was still very nervous."

**【解析】** 原文明确说他上台时手在发抖、非常紧张。A项开心放松、C项充满自信、D项有点困均与原文矛盾。故选B。

**4. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "I did not win first place, but I got third."

**【解析】** 原文说未获第一，但得了第三名。A项第一、B项第二、D项未获奖均错误。故选C。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第④段 "I learned that I could do something I had never thought I could do before."

**【解析】** 作者领悟到自己能完成以前不敢想的事。A项获奖最重要、C项不应再尝试、D项母亲全对均非文章主旨。故选B。
