# 阅读理解（议论文）

**E-books or Paper Books: Which Is Better?**

① In the past, if you wanted to read a book, you had to go to a bookshop or a library. Today, you can download thousands of books onto a single device. E-books have become very popular, especially among young people. But are e-books really better than paper books? Let us look at both sides.

② E-books have clear advantages. First, they are convenient. You can carry hundreds of books on one tablet or phone. This is perfect for travelling or commuting. Second, e-books are often cheaper than paper books, and many classic novels are even free online. Third, e-books are environmentally friendly. Making paper books requires cutting down trees, using water, and producing ink. E-books need none of these.

③ On the other hand, paper books have their own charm. Many readers enjoy the feeling of holding a real book, turning pages, and even smelling the paper. Studies show that people remember information better when they read from paper. Paper books also do not need electricity or a screen, so they are easier on the eyes. For young students who already spend a lot of time on screens, reading a paper book gives their eyes a break.

④ There is also a practical concern. If your phone or tablet runs out of battery, you cannot read your e-book. A paper book never needs charging. Besides, some people find it easier to focus when reading paper books, because a phone may distract them with messages and apps.

⑤ In the end, both e-books and paper books have their place. Perhaps the best approach is to use both: e-books for convenience when you are on the go, and paper books for deeper, more focused reading at home.

**注释：** device 设备；commuting 通勤

---

**Questions:**

1. What is one advantage of e-books mentioned in the passage?
   A. They smell nice.
   B. They are convenient to carry.
   C. They help you sleep better.
   D. They last forever.

2. Why does the writer say e-books are environmentally friendly?
   A. They use a lot of paper.
   B. Making them does not require cutting trees, using water, or producing ink.
   C. They are made from recycled materials.
   D. They use less electricity than paper books.

3. According to the passage, what is one benefit of paper books?
   A. They are always free.
   B. You can carry thousands of them easily.
   C. People may remember information better when reading from paper.
   D. They can be downloaded quickly.

4. What practical problem do e-books have?
   A. They are too heavy.
   B. They cost more than paper books.
   C. You cannot read them if the battery runs out.
   D. They can only be read at home.

5. What is the writer's conclusion?
   A. E-books should replace all paper books.
   B. Paper books are always better than e-books.
   C. Both have their advantages and can be used in different situations.
   D. Neither e-books nor paper books are useful.

---

**Answers and Explanations:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "First, they are convenient. You can carry hundreds of books on one tablet or phone."

**【解析】** 原文列出电子书的第一个优势是方便携带。A项"闻起来香"是纸质书的特点；C项"帮助睡眠"原文未提及；D项"永久保存"原文未说。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "Making paper books requires cutting down trees, using water, and producing ink. E-books need none of these."

**【解析】** 原文说制作纸质书需要砍树、用水和生产墨水，而电子书不需要这些。A项"使用大量纸张"描述的恰恰是纸质书，不是电子书；C项"回收材料制成"无依据；D项"比纸质书用更少电"原文未做此比较。故选B。

**3. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "Studies show that people remember information better when they read from paper."

**【解析】** 原文引用研究表明纸质阅读有助于更好地记住信息。A项"总是免费"不对，电子书才有免费的经典小说；B项"轻松携带千本"是电子书的特点；D项"快速下载"也是电子书功能。故选C。

**4. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "If your phone or tablet runs out of battery, you cannot read your e-book."

**【解析】** 原文指出电子书的实际问题是设备没电就无法阅读。A项"太重"与电子书轻便的特点相反；B项"比纸质书贵"与原文第②段"often cheaper"矛盾；D项"只能在家看"原文未说，反而强调了其便携性。故选C。

**5. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 主旨大意

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "both e-books and paper books have their place. Perhaps the best approach is to use both"

**【解析】** 作者最终认为两者各有优势，建议在不同情境下结合使用。A项"电子书应取代纸质书"过于绝对；B项"纸质书总是更好"也是片面的；D项"两者都没用"与全文讨论的基调完全矛盾。故选C。
