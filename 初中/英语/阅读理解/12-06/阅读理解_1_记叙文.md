> **12-06 第1篇**

# 阅读理解（记叙文）

**Learning to Swim**

① I was afraid of water when I was young. I never went into the deep part of the swimming pool. Last summer, my father said, "It's time to learn. I'll teach you." I was nervous but agreed to try.

② On the first day, we went to the local swimming pool. My father stood in the water and asked me to hold the edge and practise kicking my legs. I held on tightly and kicked. The water splashed and I laughed. "Not bad!" my father said. For the next few days, we did the same thing. I began to feel less afraid.

③ Then my father taught me to float. "Relax. Let the water hold you," he said. I was scared at first. I held his hands and lay back. The water supported me. When I opened my eyes, I saw the blue sky. It felt amazing. After a week, I could float by myself for a few seconds.

④ The next step was putting my face in the water and blowing bubbles. I did not like it at first because I was afraid of water going up my nose. My father showed me how to breathe out slowly under water. I tried again and again. Finally, I could do it. Then we practised the arm strokes. "One, two, breathe. One, two, breathe," my father said. I copied his moves.

⑤ By the end of the summer, I could swim from one side of the pool to the other. I was so proud. My father smiled and said, "You did it!" Now I love swimming. I go to the pool every weekend. Learning to swim taught me that with practice and patience, we can overcome our fears.

**注释：** splash 溅起；float 漂浮；stroke 划水

---

**Questions:**

1. Why did the writer never go into the deep part of the pool before?
   A. The pool was too cold.
   B. He was afraid of water.
   C. His father did not allow him.
   D. He did not have a swimsuit.

2. What did the writer do on the first day of the lesson?
   A. He learned to float at once.
   B. He held the edge and practised kicking his legs.
   C. He swam across the pool.
   D. He blew bubbles under water.

3. How did the writer feel when he could float by himself?
   A. Bored.
   B. Scared.
   C. It felt amazing.
   D. Tired.

4. What was the hard part when learning to blow bubbles?
   A. The water was too cold.
   B. He was afraid of water going up his nose.
   C. His father was too strict.
   D. The pool was too crowded.

5. What did the writer learn from the experience?
   A. Swimming is easy.
   B. With practice and patience, we can overcome our fears.
   C. Fathers always know best.
   D. Summer is the best time to swim.

---

**Answers and Explanations:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "I was afraid of water when I was young. I never went into the deep part of the swimming pool."

**【解析】** 原文说作者小时候怕水，因此从不去深水区。A项水太冷、C项父亲不许、D项没泳衣均无依据。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "My father stood in the water and asked me to hold the edge and practise kicking my legs."

**【解析】** 第一天父亲让作者扶住池边练习踢腿。A项直接学漂浮、C项游过池子、D项水下吹泡泡均为后续内容。故选B。

**3. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "When I opened my eyes, I saw the blue sky. It felt amazing."

**【解析】** 能自己漂浮时作者感到"amazing"（奇妙）。A项无聊、B项害怕、D项疲倦均与感受不符。故选C。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "I did not like it at first because I was afraid of water going up my nose."

**【解析】** 吹泡泡困难在于担心水会进鼻子。A、C、D项均非原文所述原因。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "Learning to swim taught me that with practice and patience, we can overcome our fears."

**【解析】** 作者领悟到：练习和耐心能战胜恐惧。A项游泳简单、C项父亲最对、D项夏天最佳均非文章主旨。故选B。
