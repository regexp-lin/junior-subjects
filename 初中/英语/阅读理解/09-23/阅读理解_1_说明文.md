# 阅读理解（说明文）

① A volcano is a mountain that can erupt (喷发) — it can shoot out hot rock, ash, and gas from deep inside the Earth. Volcanoes are both powerful and fascinating. How do they form? The answer lies beneath our feet.

② Deep inside the Earth, it is extremely hot. The center, or core (地核), is as hot as the surface of the sun. The heat melts rocks and turns them into magma (岩浆) — a thick, liquid rock. Magma is lighter than the solid rock around it, so it rises slowly toward the surface. When magma collects in a large pool under the ground, it creates pressure. If the pressure becomes too great, the magma pushes through cracks (裂缝) in the Earth's crust (地壳) and explodes outward. That is a volcanic eruption.

③ During an eruption, magma that reaches the surface is called lava (熔岩). Lava can flow slowly down the sides of the volcano, or it can be thrown high into the air as ash and rock. Ash can travel long distances and block out the sun. Volcanic gas, such as sulfur dioxide (二氧化硫), can be harmful to breathe. Eruptions can also cause mudflows (泥石流) when hot material mixes with melted snow or rain.

④ Volcanoes are found mainly along the edges of tectonic plates (板块) — the large pieces that make up the Earth's surface. When two plates meet, one may slide under the other. The rock that goes deeper melts and becomes magma, which then rises and can form a volcano. This is why we see many volcanoes in places like Japan, Indonesia, and the Pacific coast of the Americas.

⑤ Although volcanoes can be dangerous, they also bring benefits. Volcanic soil is very fertile (肥沃的) — good for farming. Many people live near volcanoes because the land is rich. Scientists study volcanoes to predict eruptions and keep people safe. Understanding how volcanoes form helps us respect the power of nature.

**注释：** erupt 喷发；core 地核；magma 岩浆；crack 裂缝；crust 地壳；lava 熔岩；mudflow 泥石流；fertile 肥沃的

**Questions:**

1. What is magma?
   A. Cold water under the ground.
   B. Melted rock — thick, liquid rock that forms deep inside the Earth.
   C. Ash from a volcano.
   D. A type of gas.

2. Why does magma rise toward the surface?
   A. Because it is heavier than solid rock.
   B. Because it is lighter than the solid rock around it.
   C. Because the surface is cold.
   D. Because water pushes it up.

3. What happens during a volcanic eruption when magma reaches the surface?
   A. It stays underground.
   B. It becomes lava and can flow or be thrown as ash and rock.
   C. It turns into water.
   D. It freezes immediately.

4. Where are volcanoes mainly found?
   A. Only in the middle of oceans.
   B. Along the edges of tectonic plates where plates meet.
   C. Only in deserts.
   D. Only in cold countries.

5. What benefit do volcanoes bring according to the passage?
   A. They make the weather colder.
   B. Volcanic soil is fertile and good for farming.
   C. They stop earthquakes.
   D. They provide drinking water.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "The heat melts rocks and turns them into magma — a thick, liquid rock"

**【解析】** 岩浆是地球深处熔融的岩石，呈黏稠液态。A、C、D 项与原文不符。故选 B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "Magma is lighter than the solid rock around it, so it rises slowly toward the surface"

**【解析】** 岩浆比周围固体岩石轻，因此向地表上升。A、C、D 项与原文矛盾。故选 B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "magma that reaches the surface is called lava... Lava can flow slowly... or it can be thrown high into the air as ash and rock"

**【解析】** 岩浆到达地表后称为熔岩，可缓慢流动或喷发为火山灰和岩石。A、C、D 项与原文不符。故选 B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "Volcanoes are found mainly along the edges of tectonic plates... When two plates meet..."

**【解析】** 火山主要分布在板块边缘、板块交汇处。A、C、D 项与原文不符。故选 B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "Volcanic soil is very fertile — good for farming"

**【解析】** 火山土肥沃，利于耕种。A、C、D 项与原文不符。故选 B。
