# 阅读理解（记叙文）

**Learning to Make Zongzi with Grandma**

① The Dragon Boat Festival was coming. My grandma said she would teach me how to make zongzi. I was excited because I had never made them before. I only knew how to eat them!

② Early in the morning, Grandma prepared everything: glutinous rice, red bean paste, bamboo leaves, and strings. She washed the leaves and soaked the rice in water. "First, fold two leaves into a cone shape," she said. I tried, but my cone had a hole at the bottom. Grandma smiled and showed me again. "Hold the leaves like this. See? No gap." I tried several times and finally got it right.

③ Then we filled the cone with rice and red bean paste. "Not too full, or it will burst when we tie it," Grandma said. She wrapped the leaves and tied the zongzi with string. Her hands moved quickly and smoothly. Mine were slow and clumsy. My first zongzi looked like a messy ball. Grandma did not mind. "Practice makes perfect," she said.

④ We made twenty zongzi and boiled them in a big pot. The smell filled the kitchen. When they were ready, we tasted them. My zongzi fell apart a bit, but it still tasted good. Grandma said, "You did well for the first time. Next year you will do better."

⑤ That day I learned more than how to make zongzi. I learned that skills take time to learn, and that spending time with grandma is one of the best gifts. I will always remember that morning in the kitchen.

**注释：** glutinous rice 糯米；cone 锥形；clumsy 笨拙的

---

**Questions:**

1. Why was the writer excited at the beginning?
   A. Because the Dragon Boat Festival was over.
   B. Because Grandma would teach him/her to make zongzi and he/she had never made them before.
   C. Because he/she did not like zongzi.
   D. Because Grandma was leaving.

2. What problem did the writer have when folding the leaves?
   A. The leaves were too small.
   B. The cone had a hole at the bottom at first.
   C. There was no rice.
   D. Grandma was too strict.

3. Why should the cone not be filled "too full"?
   A. Because rice is expensive.
   B. Because it might burst when tied.
   C. Because Grandma said so only.
   D. Because red bean paste tastes better with less rice.

4. What did Grandma say when the writer's first zongzi looked messy?
   A. "You failed."
   B. "Practice makes perfect."
   C. "Never try again."
   D. "You are too slow."

5. What can we infer from the last paragraph?
   A. The writer will never make zongzi again.
   B. The writer values both the skill learned and the time spent with Grandma.
   C. Zongzi is not important.
   D. Grandma will not teach again.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第①段 "Grandma said she would teach me how to make zongzi. I was excited because I had never made them before"
**【解析】** 奶奶要教包粽子，作者很兴奋，因为从未做过。B项准确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第②段 "I tried, but my cone had a hole at the bottom"
**【解析】** 作者最初折的锥形底部有洞。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第③段 "Not too full, or it will burst when we tie it"
**【解析】** 不能装太满，否则捆扎时容易爆开。B项准确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第③段 "Grandma did not mind. 'Practice makes perfect,' she said"
**【解析】** 奶奶不介意作者包得不好，说"熟能生巧"。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 推理判断
**【定位】** 第⑤段 "I learned that skills take time to learn, and that spending time with grandma is one of the best gifts"
**【解析】** 作者体会到技能需要时间积累，与奶奶相处的时光同样珍贵。B项正确。A、C、D项与文意相反。故选B。
