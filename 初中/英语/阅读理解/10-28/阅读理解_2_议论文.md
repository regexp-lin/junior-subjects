# 阅读理解（议论文）

**Do Good Grades Mean Success?**

① In many schools, high grades are seen as the key to success. Parents and teachers often tell students, "Study hard and get good marks." But does doing well in exams really mean you will succeed in life? Not everyone thinks so.

② Those who believe good grades lead to success have reasons. Good grades can help you get into a better school or university. They may also help you win scholarships. Employers often look at your grades when you apply for a job. So, in a way, good grades open doors. They show that you can learn and work hard. Many successful people did well at school.

③ However, others argue that grades are not everything. Some of the most successful people in business, art, or sports did not have top grades. What mattered more was their passion, creativity, and ability to solve problems. Bill Gates left Harvard. Steve Jobs dropped out of college. They succeeded because they followed their interests and took risks. Besides, too much focus on grades can make students stressed and afraid of failure. They may stop trying new things.

④ In my view, grades are useful but not the only measure of success. They can show how well you understand your subjects. But success in life also depends on skills like communication, teamwork, and persistence. A person with average grades but strong soft skills may do better than someone with top grades but poor social skills. Schools should help students develop both academic ability and life skills.

⑤ So, do good grades mean success? They can help, but they are not the whole picture. Real success comes from a mix of knowledge, skills, character, and a bit of luck. Keep studying, but do not forget to grow in other ways too.

**注释：** scholarship 奖学金；persistence 坚持；academic 学业的

**Questions:**

   A. Because grades are easy to get.

2. Who are mentioned as examples of success without top grades?
   A. Teachers only.
   B. Bill Gates and Steve Jobs.

   D. No one.

3. What does the writer think about grades?
   A. Grades are the only important thing.
   B. Grades are useful but success also needs other skills like communication and teamwork.
   C. Grades are useless.

4. What does "soft skills" mean in Paragraph ④?
   A. Skills that are easy to learn.

5. What can we infer from the last paragraph?

   C. We should stop studying.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第②段 "help you get into a better school... win scholarships... Employers often look at your grades"
**【解析】** 好成绩有助于升学、奖学金及求职。B项准确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第③段 "Bill Gates left Harvard. Steve Jobs dropped out of college"
**【解析】** 文中以比尔·盖茨和史蒂夫·乔布斯为例。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第④段 "grades are useful but not the only measure... also depends on skills like communication, teamwork"
**【解析】** 作者认为成绩有用，但成功还需沟通、合作等能力。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 词义猜测
**【定位】** 第④段 "soft skills" 与 "communication, teamwork" 并列
**【解析】** 上下文指沟通、合作等非学业类能力。B项准确。A、C、D项与文意不符。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 推理判断
**【定位】** 第⑤段 "Real success comes from a mix of knowledge, skills, character, and a bit of luck"
**【解析】** 结尾指出真正成功来自知识、技能、品格和运气的综合，而非仅靠成绩。B项正确。A、C、D项与文意不符。故选B。
