> **12-10 第1篇**

# 阅读理解（记叙文）

**My First Time as a Debate Team Member**

① In Grade 8, our school set up a debate team. Our English teacher asked if anyone wanted to join. I liked talking and enjoyed arguing about topics with my friends, so I signed up. I had no idea how different a real debate would be.

② The coach taught us the rules. A debate has two sides: "for" and "against." Each side presents their views and then rebuts the other side. We had to speak clearly, use facts, and keep calm. "Never shout or get angry," the coach said. "A good debater uses logic, not emotion." I practised with my teammates every Tuesday and Thursday after school.

③ Our first match was against another class. The topic was "Should students have more homework?" I was on the "against" side. My heart was beating fast when I stood up to speak. I had written my points on a card, but my hand shook and I dropped it. I picked it up, took a breath, and started. My voice sounded strange at first. Then I looked at my teammates. They nodded at me. I finished my speech and sat down. We did not win, but the coach said, "Good try. You will do better next time."

④ Over the next few months, I took part in more debates. I learned to listen carefully to the other side and find weaknesses in their arguments. I learned to stay calm when someone disagreed with me. I also made great friends in the team. We helped each other prepare and celebrated together after each match.

⑤ Now I am in Grade 9. The debate team has taught me more than how to argue. It taught me to think critically, speak confidently, and respect different opinions. I am grateful for that first nervous experience. It led me to grow.

**注释：** rebut 反驳；logic 逻辑；critical 批判性的

---

**Questions:**

1. Why did the writer join the debate team?
   A. His parents made him.
   B. He liked talking and enjoyed arguing about topics with friends.
   C. He wanted to avoid other homework.
   D. The coach chose him.

2. What did the coach say a good debater should do?
   A. Shout and get angry.
   B. Use logic, not emotion, and keep calm.
   C. Never disagree with others.
   D. Always win.

3. What happened when the writer first spoke in a match?
   A. He won the match easily.
   B. His heart was beating fast, his hand shook, he dropped his card, but he finished his speech.
   C. He forgot all his words.
   D. He walked off the stage.

4. What did the writer learn from the debate team over time?
   A. How to win every argument.
   B. To think critically, speak confidently, and respect different opinions.
   C. How to avoid speaking in public.
   D. That debating is boring.

5. How does the writer feel about his first nervous experience now?
   A. He regrets it.
   B. He is grateful because it led him to grow.
   C. He has forgotten it.
   D. He never wants to debate again.

---

**Answers and Explanations:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "I liked talking and enjoyed arguing about topics with my friends, so I signed up."

**【解析】** 作者喜欢交谈、爱和朋友辩论话题，因此报名。A项父母要求、C项逃避作业、D项教练选中均无依据。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "'Never shout or get angry,' the coach said. 'A good debater uses logic, not emotion.'"

**【解析】** 教练要求不喊叫、不动怒，用逻辑而非情绪。A项与要求相反；C、D项非教练原话。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "My heart was beating fast when I stood up to speak... my hand shook and I dropped it. I picked it up, took a breath, and started... I finished my speech."

**【解析】** 首次发言时心跳快、手抖、掉了卡片，但捡起后深吸一口气并完成发言。A项轻松获胜、C项全忘、D项离场均与原文不符。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "The debate team has taught me more than how to argue. It taught me to think critically, speak confidently, and respect different opinions."

**【解析】** 辩论队教会作者批判性思维、自信表达和尊重不同观点。A、C、D项与所学内容矛盾。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "I am grateful for that first nervous experience. It led me to grow."

**【解析】** 作者感激那次紧张的初次经历，认为它促成了成长。A项后悔、C项遗忘、D项再也不辩论均与态度相反。故选B。
