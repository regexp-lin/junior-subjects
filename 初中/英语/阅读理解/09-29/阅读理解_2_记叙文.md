# 阅读理解（记叙文）

① Last summer, I spent two weeks at my grandparents' house in the countryside. At first, I was bored. There was no WiFi, no shopping mall, and my friends were far away in the city. "What can I do here?" I asked my grandma. She smiled and said, "Your grandpa will teach you to fish. It is more fun than you think."

② The next morning, Grandpa took me to a small lake near the village. The water was clear and quiet. Grandpa showed me how to tie a hook (鱼钩) to the line, put bait (鱼饵) on it, and throw the line into the water. "Now wait," he said. "Fishing needs patience. You cannot rush." I sat on the bank and waited. Five minutes passed. Ten minutes. Nothing. I felt restless. "Maybe there are no fish here," I said. Grandpa shook his head. "The fish are there. You just need to wait and watch."

③ I tried to calm down. I looked at the water, the trees, and the sky. For the first time in a long while, I was not looking at a screen. I listened to the birds. I felt the wind on my face. Then — a pull! My line moved. "Slowly, slowly," Grandpa said. I pulled the line carefully. A small silver fish came out of the water. I was so excited that I almost fell into the lake! Grandpa laughed. "Well done! Your first fish."

④ Over the next few days, I went fishing every morning. Sometimes I caught nothing. Sometimes I caught two or three fish. Grandma cooked them for dinner. "Nothing tastes better than fish you catch yourself," she said. I began to understand what she meant. It was not just the fish — it was the whole experience: waking up early, walking to the lake with Grandpa, sitting quietly, and feeling connected to nature.

⑤ When I went back to the city, I missed the countryside. I had learned to slow down and enjoy simple things. Fishing taught me patience. And I will always remember that summer — the lake, the fish, and the quiet mornings with Grandpa.

**注释：** hook 鱼钩；bait 鱼饵；restless 焦躁的

---

**Questions:**

1. Where did the writer spend his summer?
   A. At a seaside hotel.
   B. At his grandparents' house in the countryside.
   C. At school.
   D. In a big city.

2. How did the writer feel at first in the countryside?
   A. Very excited.
   B. Bored because there was no WiFi or mall.
   C. Afraid.
   D. Sick.

3. What did Grandpa say about fishing?
   A. Fishing is easy and fast.
   B. Fishing needs patience; you cannot rush.
   C. There are no fish in the lake.
   D. You must fish at night.

4. What did the writer learn from fishing?
   A. Fish are stupid.
   B. To slow down, enjoy simple things, and have patience.
   C. The countryside is boring.
   D. Grandma is a good cook.

5. Why does the writer say "Nothing tastes better than fish you catch yourself"?
   A. Because Grandma said it.
   B. Because the whole experience — catching it yourself, being in nature — makes it special.
   C. Because country fish are bigger.
   D. Because he was very hungry.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "I spent two weeks at my grandparents' house in the countryside"

**【解析】** 原文明确说作者在乡下祖父母家度过暑假。A、C、D 项与原文不符。故选 B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "At first, I was bored. There was no WiFi, no shopping mall"

**【解析】** 起初感到无聊，因为没有网络和商场。A、C、D 项与原文情感不符。故选 B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "'Fishing needs patience. You cannot rush,' he said"

**【解析】** 爷爷强调钓鱼需要耐心，不能急躁。A、C、D 项与爷爷的话不符。故选 B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "I had learned to slow down and enjoy simple things. Fishing taught me patience"

**【解析】** 作者学会放慢节奏、享受简单事物，并培养了耐心。A、C、D 项与所学不符。故选 B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第④段 "It was not just the fish — it was the whole experience: waking up early... sitting quietly, and feeling connected to nature"

**【解析】** 作者领悟到美味的不仅是鱼，更是亲手捕捞、亲近自然的全过程。A、C、D 项未触及深层含义。故选 B。
