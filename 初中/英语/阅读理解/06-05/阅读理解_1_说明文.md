# 阅读理解（说明文）

① 3D printing is a technology that can turn digital designs into real objects. Unlike traditional ways of making things, which often cut or shape materials, 3D printing builds objects layer by layer. The printer reads a computer file and then puts down thin layers of material one on top of another until the object is complete. This process is also called additive manufacturing (增材制造).

② How does 3D printing work? First, you need a 3D model on a computer. You can design it yourself or download it from the Internet. The software then cuts the model into many thin layers. The printer heats the material — usually plastic — and squeezes it out through a small nozzle (喷头). It moves back and forth, laying down one thin layer at a time. When one layer is finished, the printer moves up a little and starts the next layer. Repeating this thousands of times, it builds the full object.

③ 3D printing is used in many fields today. In medicine, doctors use it to make models of body parts before surgery. In education, students can print out models of planets or ancient buildings for their projects. Some people even use 3D printers at home to make toys, phone cases, or tools. Car makers and airplane companies also use 3D printing to make certain parts quickly and cheaply.

④ One advantage of 3D printing is that it can create complex shapes that are hard or impossible to make in traditional ways. Another advantage is that you can make a single object without building expensive molds (模具). This is useful when you need just one or a few copies of something. However, 3D printing is still slower than mass production for large numbers of items.

⑤ From hospitals to schools to factories, 3D printing is changing how we make things. As the technology improves, it will play a bigger role in our daily lives in the future.

**注释：** additive manufacturing 增材制造；nozzle 喷头；mold 模具

### 选择题

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "Unlike traditional ways of making things, which often cut or shape materials, 3D printing builds objects layer by layer."

**【解析】** 原文明确对比：传统方式切割或塑形材料，3D 打印则一层一层构建物体，A 项正确。B 项"材料更贵"、C 项"比所有传统方法都快"均未提及；D 项"传统方式不能做复杂形状"非原文对比重点。故选 A。

**2. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "First, you need a 3D model on a computer... The software then cuts the model into many thin layers... The printer heats the material... laying down one thin layer at a time... it builds the full object."

**【解析】** 原文描述顺序为：先有 3D 模型 → 软件将其切分为多层 → 打印机逐层铺设材料，C 项正确。A 项顺序颠倒；B 项"无需准备直接打印"与原文矛盾；D 项"先做模具"与 3D 打印原理不符。故选 C。

**3. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "In medicine... make models of body parts... In education... print out models... at home to make toys, phone cases, or tools... Car makers and airplane companies... make certain parts."

**【解析】** 文中提到医学模型、教育模型、家庭玩具和手机壳、汽车和飞机零部件，未提及建造完整房屋，C 项正确。A、B、D 均在文中有所体现。故选 C。

**4. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第④段 "it can create complex shapes that are hard or impossible to make in traditional ways... you can make a single object without building expensive molds... useful when you need just one or a few copies."

**【解析】** 原文说明 3D 打印可制作复杂形状、无需昂贵模具，适合少量生产，C 项正确。A 项"总比传统便宜"过于绝对；B 项"大批量也好"与 "slower than mass production for large numbers" 矛盾；D 项"无缺点"与 "However" 后的内容矛盾。故选 C。

**5. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 主旨大意

**【定位】** 全文：第①段 3D 打印概念，第②段工作原理，第③段应用领域，第④段优缺点，第⑤段展望。

**【解析】** 文章介绍 3D 打印的原理（逐层构建、工作流程）及在医学、教育、家庭、工业等领域的应用，D 项准确概括主旨。A 项侧重比较、B 项劝购、C 项侧重设计方法，均非全文主旨。故选 D。
