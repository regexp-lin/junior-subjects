# 阅读理解（说明文）

**China's Bridge-Building Achievements**

① China has a long history of building bridges. Ancient Chinese built stone bridges that are still standing today. In modern times, China has become a world leader in bridge engineering. Many of the longest and highest bridges in the world are in China.

② The Danyang-Kunshan Grand Bridge is the longest bridge in the world. It is part of the Beijing-Shanghai high-speed railway. It runs for about 165 kilometres. Building such a long bridge required new technology and years of hard work. Engineers had to deal with different types of soil, rivers, and weather conditions along the way.

③ China also has some of the highest bridges. The Beipanjiang Bridge stands more than 565 metres above the river. It connects two sides of a deep valley. Before the bridge was built, people had to drive for hours to get from one side to the other. Now the journey takes just a few minutes. Bridges like this have changed the lives of people in remote areas.

④ Building these bridges was not easy. Engineers faced many challenges: strong winds, earthquakes, and difficult terrain. They used new materials and designs to make the bridges strong and safe. Chinese engineers have shared their experience with other countries too. They have helped build bridges in Africa, Asia, and elsewhere.

⑤ China's bridge achievements show how engineering can connect people and improve lives. From ancient stone bridges to modern high-speed rail bridges, Chinese bridge-building continues to reach new heights.

**注释：** terrain 地形；remote 偏远的

**Questions:**

1. According to Paragraph ②, what is special about the Danyang-Kunshan Grand Bridge?
   A. It is the oldest bridge.
   B. It is the longest bridge in the world.
   C. It is in Africa.
   D. It is the shortest bridge.

2. How high is the Beipanjiang Bridge above the river?
   A. 165 metres.
   B. About 565 metres.
   C. 100 metres.
   D. 50 metres.

3. What benefit did the Beipanjiang Bridge bring to local people?
   A. It made the journey much shorter.
   B. It closed the valley.
   C. It made travel harder.
   D. It had no effect.

4. What challenges did engineers face when building these bridges?
   A. Only easy weather.
   B. Strong winds, earthquakes, and difficult terrain.
   C. No challenges.
   D. Too much money.

5. What can we infer from the passage?
   A. China has stopped building bridges.
   B. China's bridge engineering has developed greatly and benefits many people.
   C. All bridges in China are ancient.
   D. No other countries have long bridges.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B****【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第②段 "The Danyang-Kunshan Grand Bridge is the longest bridge in the world"
**【解析】** 丹昆特大桥为世界最长桥梁。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第③段 "The Beipanjiang Bridge stands more than 565 metres above the river"
**【解析】** 北盘江大桥高出江面约565米。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】A**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第③段 "the journey takes just a few minutes"
**【解析】** 过桥后行程缩短至几分钟。A项正确。B、C、D项与原文不符。故选A。

**4. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第④段 "strong winds, earthquakes, and difficult terrain"
**【解析】** 工程师需应对强风、地震和复杂地形。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 推理判断
**【定位】** 全文，尤其第⑤段
**【解析】** 中国桥梁工程发展迅速，造福民众。B项正确。A、C、D项与文意不符。故选B。
