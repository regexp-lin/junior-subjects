# 阅读理解（说明文）

## **The Silk Road: An Ancient Trade Route**

①The Silk Road was not a single road, but a network of trade routes that connected China with the rest of the world more than 2,000 years ago. It started in China's ancient capital Chang'an (today's Xi'an) and stretched all the way to countries in Asia, the Middle East, and even Europe. For centuries, merchants traveled along these routes, carrying goods and ideas between different cultures.

②Why was it called the Silk Road? China was the first country to make silk. This beautiful cloth was highly valued in other lands. Chinese merchants sold silk to foreign traders. In return, they brought back horses, glass, spices, and gems. Silk was one of the most important products traded, so the route got its name from it.

③The Silk Road did more than move goods. It also exchanged culture and knowledge. Buddhism came to China from India through the Silk Road. Paper-making and printing spread from China to the West. New foods like grapes and carrots entered China. Different peoples learned from each other and their lives became richer.

④Traveling the Silk Road was hard and dangerous. The routes crossed deserts, mountains, and rough land. Merchants faced bad weather, bandits, and illness. Yet they kept going because the trade was profitable. Their courage and hard work helped build the world we know today.

⑤Today, the idea of the Silk Road is still alive. China's "Belt and Road" initiative aims to build new connections between countries. Just like the ancient merchants, we continue to share goods, technology, and friendship across borders. The spirit of the Silk Road— openness and exchange—remains important in our modern world.

## 题目

1. What was the Silk Road?
   A. A single road from Beijing to Europe.
   B. A network of trade routes connecting China with other parts of the world.
   C. A modern highway in China.
   D. A river for shipping goods.

2. Why did the route get the name "Silk Road"?
   A. Because the road was made of silk.
   B. Because silk was one of the most important products traded on it.
   C. Because only silk was traded.
   D. Because merchants wore silk clothes.

3. According to paragraph 3, what besides goods was exchanged on the Silk Road?
   A. Only money.
   B. Culture, knowledge, and new foods.
   C. Nothing else.
   D. Only weapons.

4. What difficulties did merchants face on the Silk Road?
   A. Too many rest stops.
   B. Deserts, mountains, bad weather, bandits, and illness.
   C. Too much silk to carry.
   D. No customers.

5. What does the "Belt and Road" initiative have in common with the ancient Silk Road?
   A. They use the same old routes.
   B. They both aim to build connections and share between countries.
   C. They only trade silk.
   D. They have nothing in common.

---

## 答案与解析

**1. 答案：B**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第①段

【解析】
- 第①段写：“The Silk Road was not a single road, but a network of trade routes that connected China with the rest of the world.”
- A“单一道路”与“not a single road”矛盾。C“现代公路”、D“河流”未提及。故答案为 B。

**2. 答案：B**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第②段

【解析】
- 第②段写：“Silk was one of the most important products traded, so the route got its name from it.”
- A“路是丝绸做的”、C“只交易丝绸”、D“商人穿丝绸”均不准确。故答案为 B。

**3. 答案：B**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第③段

【解析】
- 第③段说明：除货物外，还交流文化、知识；佛教传入中国，造纸术和印刷术西传，葡萄、胡萝卜等新食物传入。
- A、C、D 与原文不符。故答案为 B。

**4. 答案：B**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第④段

【解析】
- 第④段写：路线穿越沙漠、山区和崎岖地带；商人面临恶劣天气、强盗和疾病（“deserts, mountains... bad weather, bandits, and illness”）。
- A、C、D 未在文中出现。故答案为 B。

**5. 答案：B**

【题型】推理判断题

【定位】第⑤段

【解析】
- 第⑤段说：当今“一带一路”旨在建立国家间新联系，与古代商人一样继续分享货物、技术和友谊。
- A“使用相同旧路线”与“build new connections”不符。C“只交易丝绸”错误。D 与全文主旨矛盾。故答案为 B。
