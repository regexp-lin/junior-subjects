# 阅读理解（记叙文）

## **How I Fell in Love with Reading**

①I used to hate reading. When my mother gave me a book, I would put it under my bed and forget about it. I thought books were boring. I preferred watching cartoons or playing video games. My mother never got angry. She just kept putting new books on my shelf and reading herself every night.

②One rainy afternoon, there was nothing to do. The power was out, so no TV, no computer. I was bored. I picked up a book from my shelf—it was a story about a boy who found a magic lamp. I turned the first page just to pass the time. Before I knew it, I had read five chapters. The boy in the story was on an adventure, and I felt like I was there with him. When my mother called me for dinner, I was surprised. Had two hours passed? It felt like only twenty minutes.

③After that day, I started reading more. I found that books could take me to different worlds—ancient Egypt, outer space, or a small village in the mountains. I did not need to leave my room. I could travel anywhere through words. I also learned new things. Some books taught me about science; others made me think about life.

④I built a habit: read for thirty minutes before bed every night. At first I had to force myself. Soon it became something I looked forward to. Now I cannot sleep without reading a few pages. My mother smiles when she sees me with a book. She says, "I knew you would find it one day."

⑤If you think you do not like reading, maybe you just have not found the right book yet. Try different types—adventure, mystery, or science. Give it a chance. You might discover a whole new world, just like I did.

---

**生词注释**
- chapter /ˈtʃæptə/ n. 章
- adventure /ədˈventʃə/ n. 冒险
- mystery /ˈmɪstri/ n. 推理，悬疑

## 题目

1. What did the writer think of reading at first?
   A. He loved it.
   B. He thought books were boring and preferred cartoons and games.
   C. He read every night with his mother.
   D. He only read science books.

2. What changed the writer's mind about reading?
   A. His teacher forced him to read.
   B. A rainy day with no power—he tried a book and got hooked.
   C. His mother got angry and punished him.
   D. He finished all his video games.

3. What did the writer discover about books?
   A. They are only for rainy days.
   B. They can take him to different worlds and teach him new things.
   C. They are cheaper than video games.
   D. They must be read in thirty minutes.

4. What habit did the writer build?
   A. Watch TV before bed.
   B. Read for thirty minutes before bed every night.
   C. Play games before bed.
   D. Only read on weekends.

5. What does the writer suggest to those who think they do not like reading?
   A. Give up and never try again.
   B. Try different types of books; you might find one you like.
   C. Only read what teachers assign.
   D. Reading is not important.

---

## 答案与解析

**1. 答案：B**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第①段

【解析】
- 第①段写：“I used to hate reading... I thought books were boring. I preferred watching cartoons or playing video games.”
- A 与“hate reading”矛盾。C“每晚和母亲一起读”是母亲独自读。D 未提及。故答案为 B。

**2. 答案：B**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第②段

【解析】
- 第②段说明：一个停电的雨天无事可做，他拿起一本书打发时间，结果读得入迷，连读五章。
- A、C、D 未在文中出现。故答案为 B。

**3. 答案：B**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第③段

【解析】
- 第③段写：发现书籍能带他去不同世界，还能学到新知识（“books could take me to different worlds... I also learned new things”）。
- A“只在雨天读”与后文习惯矛盾。C、D 未提及。故答案为 B。

**4. 答案：B**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第④段

【解析】
- 第④段写：“I built a habit: read for thirty minutes before bed every night.”
- A、C、D 与原文不符。故答案为 B。

**5. 答案：B**

【题型】推理/建议题

【定位】第⑤段

【解析】
- 第⑤段建议：也许只是还没碰到合适的书；可以尝试不同类型，给自己一个机会。
- A、C、D 与“Try different types... Give it a chance”矛盾。故答案为 B。
