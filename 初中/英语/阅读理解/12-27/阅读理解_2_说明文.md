# 阅读理解（说明文）

**Public Transport in Big Cities**

① In big cities like Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou, millions of people need to travel every day. How do they get around? Many choose public transport: buses, subways, and trains. Public transport is cheap, convenient, and better for the environment. Let us see how it works.

② **The subway.** The subway, or metro, runs underground. It is fast and rarely gets stuck in traffic. In rush hour, trains come every few minutes. You buy a ticket or use a transport card. You swipe the card at the gate, get on the train, and swipe again when you get off. The fare depends on how far you travel. In most cities, students can get a discount with a student card.

③ **Buses.** Buses run on the roads. They can reach almost every part of the city. The fare is usually low: one or two yuan for a short trip. You pay when you get on, or swipe your card. Some buses have special lanes. They do not mix with cars, so they move faster. In some cities, you can see the bus location on your phone. You know when the next bus will arrive.

④ **Shared bikes.** In recent years, shared bikes have become popular. You find a bike, scan the QR code with your phone, and ride. When you arrive, you park the bike and lock it. You pay by the minute, often less than one yuan for a short ride. Shared bikes are good for short trips: from home to the subway station, or from the bus stop to school. They also help reduce traffic and pollution.

⑤ Public transport makes city life easier. It saves money, cuts down on car emissions, and helps reduce traffic jams. Next time you go out, try the bus, subway, or a shared bike. You might find it better than sitting in a car!

**注释：** metro 地铁；swipe 刷（卡）；fare 票价；QR code 二维码

**Questions:**

1. Why do many people in big cities choose public transport?
   A. Because they cannot drive.
   B. Because it is cheap, convenient, and better for the environment.
   C. Because it is slower than cars.
   D. Because there are no cars.

2. How do you pay for the subway?
   A. Only with cash.
   B. By buying a ticket or using a transport card.
   C. With a credit card only.
   D. You do not need to pay.

3. How can students save money on subway fares?
   A. By walking.
   B. By using a student card for a discount.
   C. By taking the bus instead.
   D. By travelling at night.

4. What is one advantage of shared bikes?
   A. They are free.
   B. They are good for short trips and help reduce traffic and pollution.
   C. They can go very fast.
   D. They do not need a phone.

5. What does the writer suggest at the end?
   A. Never use public transport.
   B. Try the bus, subway, or shared bike when going out.
   C. Always drive a car.
   D. Stay at home.

---

**Answers and Explanations:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "Public transport is cheap, convenient, and better for the environment."

**【解析】** 公共交通便宜、方便且更环保。A项不会开车、C项比车慢、D项没车均非主要原因。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "You buy a ticket or use a transport card. You swipe the card at the gate"

**【解析】** 乘地铁可买票或用交通卡刷卡。A项仅现金、C项仅信用卡、D项无需付费均与原文不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "In most cities, students can get a discount with a student card."

**【解析】** 多数城市持学生卡可享票价优惠。A、C、D项均非学生优惠方式。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "Shared bikes are good for short trips... They also help reduce traffic and pollution."

**【解析】** 共享单车适合短途出行，并能减少拥堵和污染。A项免费与"pay by the minute"矛盾；C项很快、D项不需手机均非主要优势。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "Next time you go out, try the bus, subway, or a shared bike. You might find it better than sitting in a car!"

**【解析】** 作者建议下次出行试试公交、地铁或共享单车。A、C、D项均与建议相反。故选B。
