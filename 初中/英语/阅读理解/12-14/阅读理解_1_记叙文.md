# 阅读理解（记叙文）

## **My First 3D Printing Project**

①Last term, our school bought a 3D printer for the tech club. I had never seen one before. When our teacher showed us how it worked, I was amazed. The machine could make real objects from plastic, layer by layer. I decided to join the club and try it myself.

②Our first project was to design a name tag. I used a simple program on the computer to draw the letters of my name. It took me three hours to get it right. When I finally sent the file to the printer, I sat and watched. The printer nozzle moved back and forth, adding thin layers of plastic. Two hours later, my name tag was done. It was not perfect—one letter was a bit crooked—but I loved it because I had made it myself.

③The next project was harder. We had to design a pencil holder. I wanted to make one in the shape of a small house. I drew the design, but when I printed it, the roof fell off. The teacher said the walls were too thin. I went back, made the walls thicker, and tried again. The second time it worked. I still use that pencil holder on my desk today.

④Through 3D printing, I learned that making things takes time and patience. Every mistake taught me something new. I also learned to work with others. When my classmate had problems with her design, I helped her. When I was stuck, she helped me too.

⑤Now I dream of becoming an engineer one day. 3D printing opened a door for me. I see that technology is not just about using phones or playing games. It is about creating useful things with your own hands and imagination.

---

**生词注释**
- layer /ˈleɪə/ n. 层
- nozzle /ˈnɒzl/ n. 喷头
- crooked /ˈkrʊkɪd/ adj. 弯曲的，歪的

## 题目

1. Why did the writer join the tech club?
   A. His parents asked him to.
   B. He was amazed by the 3D printer and wanted to try it.
   C. He wanted to play computer games.
   D. The teacher forced him to join.

2. What was wrong with the writer's first name tag?
   A. The letters were too small.
   B. One letter was a bit crooked.
   C. The plastic color was wrong.
   D. It took too long to print.

3. Why did the roof of the pencil holder fall off the first time?
   A. The design was too simple.
   B. The walls were too thin.
   C. The printer was broken.
   D. The writer used the wrong program.

4. What did the writer learn from 3D printing?
   A. Technology is only for playing games.
   B. Making things takes time, patience, and teamwork.
   C. It is easy to design things on the first try.
   D. 3D printers never make mistakes.

5. What does the writer want to be when he grows up?
   A. A teacher.
   B. A game designer.
   C. An engineer.
   D. A printer repairer.

---

## 答案与解析

**1. 答案：B**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第①段

【解析】
- 第①段写：看到老师演示 3D 打印机后感到惊奇，决定加入社团亲自尝试（“I was amazed... I decided to join the club and try it myself”）。
- A、C、D 均未在文中提及。故答案为 B。

**2. 答案：B**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第②段

【解析】
- 第②段说明：成品不完美，“one letter was a bit crooked”（有一个字母有点歪）。
- A、C、D 未在文中出现。故答案为 B。

**3. 答案：B**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第③段

【解析】
- 第③段写：第一次打印时屋顶掉了，老师解释“the walls were too thin”（墙壁太薄）。
- A、C、D 与原文不符。故答案为 B。

**4. 答案：B**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第④段及第⑤段

【解析】
- 第④段写：明白做东西需要时间和耐心，也学会了与他人合作。第⑤段补充：技术不仅是玩游戏，更是用手和想象力创造有用之物。
- A 与第⑤段“not just about... playing games”矛盾。C“第一次就成功”与文中多次尝试不符。D 与“every mistake taught me”矛盾。故答案为 B。

**5. 答案：C**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第⑤段

【解析】
- 第⑤段首句明确写：“Now I dream of becoming an engineer one day”（梦想有一天成为工程师）。
- A、B、D 均未提及。故答案为 C。
