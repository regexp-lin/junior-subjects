# 阅读理解（说明文）

**Sleep and Learning: Why Rest Matters**

① Do you sometimes stay up late to study for an exam? Many students think that more study time means better grades. But scientists have found that sleep is just as important as study time. In fact, without enough sleep, your brain cannot learn or remember well. Let us understand why.

② When you sleep, your brain does not stop working. It is busy sorting and storing information from the day. Think of it like saving files on a computer. During sleep, especially in the "deep sleep" stage, your brain moves information from short-term memory to long-term memory. This is why you often remember things better after a good night's sleep. If you skip sleep, the information may never be stored properly.

③ Sleep also helps you concentrate. When you are tired, it is hard to pay attention in class. You may read the same sentence three times without understanding it. Studies show that students who sleep 8 to 9 hours do better in exams than those who sleep only 5 or 6 hours. Teenagers need about 8 to 10 hours of sleep each night because their bodies and brains are still growing.

④ Lack of sleep can cause more problems. You may feel moody, lose patience easily, and even get sick more often. Your immune system needs rest to work well. Some students drink coffee or energy drinks to stay awake. But these cannot replace sleep. They may help for a short time, but your brain still needs real rest.

⑤ So what can you do? Try to go to bed and wake up at roughly the same time every day, even on weekends. Avoid screens (phones, tablets) for at least an hour before bed — the blue light can make it harder to fall asleep. Do not study in bed; keep your bed for sleep only. A good sleep routine will help you study better, not worse.

**注释：** short-term 短期的；long-term 长期的；immune 免疫的；routine 习惯

---

**Questions:**

1. What happens to your brain when you sleep?
   A. It stops completely.
   B. It sorts and stores information; it moves information to long-term memory.
   C. It forgets everything.
   D. It only rests.

2. Why might you remember things better after a good night's sleep?
   A. Because you dream more.
   B. Because during sleep the brain stores information properly in long-term memory.
   C. Because you eat more.
   D. Because you exercise.

3. How much sleep do teenagers need each night?
   A. 5 to 6 hours.
   B. 4 hours.
   C. 8 to 10 hours.
   D. 12 hours.

4. What can lack of sleep cause?
   A. Better grades.
   B. Feeling moody, losing patience easily, and getting sick more often.
   C. More energy.
   D. Better concentration.

5. What does the passage suggest before bed?
   A. Study in bed.
   B. Avoid screens for at least an hour before bed.
   C. Drink coffee.
   D. Exercise heavily.

---

**Answers and Explanations:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "When you sleep, your brain does not stop working. It is busy sorting and storing information... moves information from short-term memory to long-term memory."

**【解析】** 睡眠时大脑在整理、存储信息，并将信息从短期记忆转移到长期记忆。A项完全停止、C项忘记一切、D项仅休息均与原文矛盾。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第②段 "your brain moves information from short-term memory to long-term memory... If you skip sleep, the information may never be stored properly."

**【解析】** 睡眠时大脑把信息存入长期记忆，睡得好则记忆更牢。A项做梦更多、C项吃得更多、D项运动均非主要原因。故选B。

**3. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "Teenagers need about 8 to 10 hours of sleep each night"

**【解析】** 青少年每晚需要约8到10小时睡眠。A项5-6小时不足；B项4小时、D项12小时均与原文不符。故选C。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "You may feel moody, lose patience easily, and even get sick more often."

**【解析】** 睡眠不足可能导致情绪低落、易烦躁、更容易生病。A项成绩更好、C项更多精力、D项更专注均与缺乏睡眠的后果矛盾。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "Avoid screens (phones, tablets) for at least an hour before bed"

**【解析】** 建议睡前至少一小时避免使用电子屏幕。A项在床上学习与"keep your bed for sleep only"矛盾；C项喝咖啡、D项剧烈运动均会妨碍睡眠。故选B。
