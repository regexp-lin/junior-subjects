> **12-01 第1篇**

# 阅读理解（记叙文）

**Overcoming My Exam Fear**

① I used to be very afraid of exams. Every time a test came, my heart would beat fast, my hands would sweat, and I could not think clearly. I would stay up late the night before, trying to memorise everything. But the next day, I often forgot what I had learnt. My grades were not bad, but I knew I could do better if I stayed calm.

② Things changed when I talked to our school counsellor, Mr Wang. He asked me what I was afraid of. "What if I fail?" I said. "What if my parents are disappointed?" He listened and then said, "Exams are just a way to see what you know. They are not a test of your worth as a person." Those words stayed with me.

③ Mr Wang gave me some practical tips. First, prepare early. Do not leave everything to the last night. Review a little every day for a week before the exam. Second, get enough sleep. A tired brain cannot work well. Third, breathe slowly before the exam. Take three deep breaths when you feel nervous. Fourth, read each question carefully. Do not rush. If you do not know an answer, skip it and come back later.

④ I followed his advice. Before the mid-term exam, I started reviewing two weeks earlier. I went to bed by 10 p.m. every night. On the morning of the exam, I did some breathing exercises. When I opened the paper, I still felt a little nervous. But I remembered Mr Wang's words: "Exams are not a test of your worth." I took my time. I finished the paper and even had time to check my answers.

⑤ I got my best grade that term. More importantly, I learned that fear could be managed. It does not have to control you. With the right preparation and a calm mind, exams become less scary. Now I still feel a bit nervous before tests, but I know I can handle it.

---

**Questions:**

1. What happened to the writer when exams came?
   A. He felt very relaxed.
   B. His heart beat fast, his hands sweated, and he could not think clearly.
   C. He always got top grades.
   D. He refused to take exams.

2. What did Mr Wang say about exams?
   A. Exams decide everything in life.
   B. Exams are just a way to see what you know; they are not a test of your worth as a person.
   C. You should never worry about exams.
   D. Only smart people pass exams.

3. Which tip did Mr Wang give about preparation?
   A. Memorise everything the night before.
   B. Review a little every day for a week before the exam.
   C. Do not prepare at all.
   D. Only study the easiest parts.

4. What did the writer do on the morning of the exam?
   A. He stayed in bed longer.
   B. He did some breathing exercises.
   C. He ate a big breakfast only.
   D. He watched TV.

5. What did the writer learn from this experience?
   A. Exams are meaningless.
   B. Fear can be managed; with the right preparation and a calm mind, exams become less scary.
   C. Counsellors cannot help.
   D. He should avoid all exams.

---

**Answers and Explanations:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "my heart would beat fast, my hands would sweat, and I could not think clearly."

**【解析】** 考试来时作者心跳加快、手出汗、无法清晰思考。A项非常放松、C项总是拿高分、D项拒绝考试均与描述不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "Exams are just a way to see what you know. They are not a test of your worth as a person."

**【解析】** 王老师说考试只是检验你所知的方式，不是对你个人价值的评判。A项决定一切过于绝对；C项从不担心、D项只有聪明人能过均非原意。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "Prepare early. Do not leave everything to the last night. Review a little every day for a week before the exam."

**【解析】** 建议提前准备，考前一周每天复习一点。A项考前一夜死记与建议相反；C项不准备、D项只复习最简单部分均非建议。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "On the morning of the exam, I did some breathing exercises."

**【解析】** 考试当天早上做了呼吸练习。A项多睡、C项只吃丰盛早餐、D项看电视均非原文所述。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 主旨大意

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "I learned that fear could be managed... With the right preparation and a calm mind, exams become less scary."

**【解析】** 作者领悟到恐惧可以应对，正确准备和冷静心态能让考试不再那么可怕。A项考试无意义、C项咨询师帮不了、D项应逃避考试均与主旨矛盾。故选B。
