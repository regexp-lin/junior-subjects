## A Lesson from the Chess Board

①Li Ming was a student who always gave quick answers in class. He believed being fast was the most important thing. But one day, everything changed when he joined the school chess club.

②In his first game, Li Ming moved his pieces quickly. He thought he would win easily. However, his opponent took time to think before every move. Fifteen minutes later, Li Ming lost. He felt surprised and a little angry. "Why did I lose? I moved faster than he did!" he asked his teacher.

③The teacher smiled. "Chess is not about speed," he said. "It is about thinking. Every move matters. If you act without thinking, you may make mistakes. Take your time. Look at the whole board. Ask yourself: What will happen if I move here?"

④Li Ming decided to try again. This time, he sat quietly before each move. He looked at where his pieces were. He imagined what his opponent might do next. Slowly, he began to see patterns. He won his next game, and then another. More importantly, he felt different inside—calmer and more confident.

⑤Later, Li Ming found that this habit helped him in other subjects too. When he faced a difficult math problem, he did not rush. He read the question carefully and thought step by step. His grades improved. The chess board had taught him something valuable: thinking carefully before acting can lead to better results in life.

**1.** What did Li Ming believe was most important before he joined the chess club?

A. Being fast when answering.
B. Working hard every day.
C. Making friends with others.
D. Winning every game.

**2.** Why did Li Ming lose his first chess game?

A. His opponent cheated.
B. The teacher helped his opponent.
C. He acted quickly without thinking enough.
D. He moved too slowly.

**3.** What advice did the teacher give Li Ming?

A. Move as fast as possible.
B. Give up playing chess.
C. Think carefully before each move and consider the results.
D. Practice only when he felt like it.

**4.** What can we infer from Paragraph 4?

A. Li Ming never lost a game again.
B. Thinking before acting made Li Ming feel more at peace and sure of himself.
C. Chess is the only subject that needs thinking.
D. Li Ming stopped going to the chess club.

**5.** What does the writer want to tell us through this story?

A. Teachers should not let students play games at school.
B. Speed is more important than thinking.
C. Thinking carefully before acting helps us do better in many areas of life.
D. Chess is the best sport for students.

---

**【答案】1. A  2. C  3. C  4. B  5. C**

**【1题解析】**
- **题型**：细节理解题
- **定位**：第①段 "He believed being fast was the most important thing."
- **解析**：李明最初认为速度最重要，故答案选A。

**【2题解析】**
- **题型**：细节理解题
- **定位**：第②段 "Li Ming moved his pieces quickly... Fifteen minutes later, Li Ming lost."
- **解析**：李明快速落子、不经思考就行动，导致失败，故答案选C。

**【3题解析】**
- **题型**：细节理解题
- **定位**：第③段 "Take your time. Look at the whole board. Ask yourself: What will happen if I move here?"
- **解析**：老师建议每次走棋前先思考，设想后果，故答案选C。

**【4题解析】**
- **题型**：推理判断题
- **定位**：第④段 "More importantly, he felt different inside—calmer and more confident."
- **解析**：可推理出先思考再行动让李明内心更平静、更自信，故答案选B。

**【5题解析】**
- **题型**：主旨大意题
- **定位**：全文，尤其是第⑤段 "thinking carefully before acting can lead to better results in life"
- **解析**：文章通过李明在下棋中学会思考的故事，传达"先思考再行动有助于在生活中取得更好结果"的主旨，故答案选C。
