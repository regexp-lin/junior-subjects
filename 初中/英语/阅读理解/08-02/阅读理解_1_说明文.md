## Giant Pandas: A Story of Hope

①Giant pandas are one of the most loved animals in the world. They live mainly in the mountains of Sichuan, Shaanxi and Gansu in China. For many years, giant pandas faced serious problems. Their home forests were cut down. People built roads and cities. The pandas had less and less space to live.

②In 2016, the good news came. The International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN) changed the giant panda from "endangered" to "vulnerable." This meant the number of pandas in the wild was slowly going up. Scientists believe there are now over 1,800 giant pandas living in the wild.

③How did this happen? The Chinese government and many organizations worked together. They set up more than 60 panda reserves. In these protected areas, pandas can live safely. People are not allowed to cut down trees or build there. Scientists also study pandas to understand how to help them better.

④Another important step is breeding programmes. In places like Chengdu Panda Base, experts help pandas have babies. When the baby pandas are strong enough, some of them are sent back to the wild. This helps increase the panda population.

⑤However, the work is not finished. Climate change may affect the bamboo forests that pandas need. We must continue to protect their homes. Every small action we take can help—saving energy, reducing waste, and caring for our environment. Together, we can give giant pandas a brighter future.

---

**Notes**
- **IUCN**: International Union for Conservation of Nature 世界自然保护联盟
- **endangered**: 濒危的
- **vulnerable**: 易危的
- **reserve**: 保护区
- **breeding**: 繁殖

A. In the mountains of Sichuan, Shaanxi and Gansu.

A. They allow people to keep pandas as pets.
B. They let scientists sell bamboo to zoos.
C. They protect pandas by keeping their homes safe from cutting and building.
D. They are built for tourists to take photos.

**4.** What can we infer from Paragraph 4?

A. All baby pandas stay in zoos forever.
B. Chengdu Panda Base is the only place that helps pandas.
C. Breeding programmes only work in the wild.
D. Some pandas raised in bases may return to nature to help the population grow.

**5.** What is the writer's main purpose in writing this passage?

A. To introduce panda protection progress and call on people to care for the environment.
B. To tell funny stories about pandas.
C. To explain how pandas eat bamboo.
D. To compare pandas in China with those in other countries.

---

**【答案】1. A  2. B  3. C  4. D  5. A**

**【1题解析】**
- **题型**：细节理解题
- **定位**：第①段 "They live mainly in the mountains of Sichuan, Shaanxi and Gansu in China."
- **解析**：原文直接说明大熊猫主要生活在四川、陕西和甘肃的山区，故答案选A。

**【2题解析】**
- **题型**：细节理解题
- **定位**：第②段 "The International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN) changed the giant panda from 'endangered' to 'vulnerable.'"
- **解析**：2016年IUCN将大熊猫从"濒危"调整为"易危"，故答案选B。

**【3题解析】**
- **题型**：细节理解题
- **定位**：第③段 "In these protected areas, pandas can live safely. People are not allowed to cut down trees or build there."
- **解析**：保护区的作用是禁止砍树和建设，保护熊猫家园安全，故答案选C。

**【4题解析】**
- **题型**：推理判断题
- **定位**：第④段 "When the baby pandas are strong enough, some of them are sent back to the wild. This helps increase the panda population."
- **解析**：可推理出基地繁育的幼年熊猫有一部分会被放归野外，以增加野外种群数量，故答案选D。

**【5题解析】**
- **题型**：写作意图题
- **定位**：全文，尤其是第⑤段 "We must continue to protect their homes... Together, we can give giant pandas a brighter future."
- **解析**：文章既介绍大熊猫保护进展，又在文末呼吁人们保护环境、采取行动，故答案选A。
