# 阅读理解（记叙文）

**Getting Back Up After Failure**

① Last term, I failed my maths exam. I had never got such a low score before. When I saw the red "52" on the paper, I felt like the world had ended. I could not stop thinking, "I am so stupid. I will never do well."

② For a whole week, I did not want to talk to anyone. I avoided my friends and stayed in my room. My parents tried to cheer me up, but I just turned away. I thought I would never be good at maths.

③ One evening, my grandpa came to visit. He sat beside me and said, "I failed my first driving test three times. Did you know that?" I looked up, surprised. He smiled and said, "Everyone fails sometimes. What matters is whether you give up or try again." He told me that failure is not the end — it is a chance to learn.

④ His words stayed with me. The next day, I opened my maths book again. I found a study group at school and asked for help. I practised every day. It was hard at first, but slowly I understood more. When the next exam came, I was nervous, but I did my best. I got 78. It was not perfect, but it was progress.

⑤ Now I know that failure hurts, but it does not define us. What defines us is how we respond. I am still working on my maths, and I am not afraid of failing again. Because every failure teaches me something new.

**注释：** define 定义；respond 回应

---

**Questions:**

1. What happened to the writer in the maths exam last term?
   A. He got the highest score.
   B. He did not take the exam.
   C. He failed and got 52.
   D. He got 78.

2. How did the writer feel during the week after the exam?
   A. Happy and confident.
   B. He avoided friends and stayed in his room, feeling down.
   C. He went travelling.
   D. He studied maths day and night.

3. What did Grandpa share with the writer?
   A. He had never failed anything.
   B. Maths is useless.
   C. He failed his first driving test three times, and failure is a chance to learn.
   D. The writer should give up maths.

4. What did the writer do after talking to Grandpa?
   A. He joined a study group, practised daily, and improved.
   B. He gave up maths completely.
   C. He only read the maths book once.
   D. He asked his parents to hire a tutor.

5. What is the main idea of the passage?
   A. Failure means we are stupid.
   B. Failure hurts, but how we respond to it defines us; we can learn and grow from it.
   C. Grandpa's advice is always right.
   D. Maths is the most important subject.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】C**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第①段 "Last term, I failed my maths exam... the red '52' on the paper"
**【解析】** 作者上学期数学考试失利，得分52。C项准确。A、B、D项与原文不符。故选C。

**2. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第②段 "For a whole week, I did not want to talk to anyone. I avoided my friends and stayed in my room."
**【解析】** 作者一周内不愿与人交流，回避朋友，待在房间里。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文矛盾。故选B。

**3. 【答案】C**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第③段 "I failed my first driving test three times... failure is not the end — it is a chance to learn"
**【解析】** 爷爷讲述自己驾照考试三次失败的经历，并强调失败是学习的机会。C项准确。A、B、D项与原文不符。故选C。

**4. 【答案】A**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第④段 "I found a study group at school and asked for help. I practised every day... I got 78"
**【解析】** 作者加入学习小组、每日练习，下次考试进步到78分。A项正确。B、C、D项与原文不符。故选A。

**5. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 主旨大意
**【定位】** 全文，尤其第⑤段 "failure hurts, but it does not define us. What defines us is how we respond"
**【解析】** 全文讲述从考试失败到重新振作的过程，主旨是失败不定义我们，我们如何面对失败才重要。B项概括准确。A、C、D项过于片面。故选B。
