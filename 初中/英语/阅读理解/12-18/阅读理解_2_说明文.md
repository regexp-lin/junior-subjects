# 阅读理解（说明文）

## **Chinese Kung Fu: More Than Fighting**

①When people hear "Chinese Kung Fu," they often think of fight scenes in movies. But Kung Fu is much more than that. It is an ancient practice that combines physical exercise, mental focus, and traditional culture. For over 2,000 years, Kung Fu has been part of Chinese life. Today, millions of people around the world practice it.

②Kung Fu includes many different styles. Some are slow and soft, like Tai Chi. You may have seen elderly people doing Tai Chi in the park in the morning. It helps balance and relaxation. Other styles are fast and powerful, like Shaolin Kung Fu. Monks at the Shaolin Temple have practiced it for centuries. Each style has its own history and moves.

③Practicing Kung Fu has many benefits. It strengthens the body—your muscles, bones, and heart. It also trains the mind. You must concentrate when you move. This improves focus and patience. Many students say Kung Fu helps them do better in school because they can concentrate more easily. Kung Fu also teaches respect. Students bow to their teachers and training partners. They learn discipline and self-control.

④Kung Fu is not about hurting others. The real goal is to improve yourself—body and mind. In the past, some people learned Kung Fu to protect themselves or their villages. Today, most people practice it for health and personal growth. It is a way to understand Chinese culture and history.

⑤You do not need to be strong or flexible to start. Kung Fu is for everyone. Many schools offer classes for beginners. If you are interested, find a good teacher and give it a try. You might discover a hobby that lasts a lifetime.

## 题目

1. What is the passage mainly about?
   A. Kung Fu movies.
   B. Chinese Kung Fu as a practice of body, mind, and culture—not just fighting.
   C. How to win a Kung Fu competition.
   D. The history of the Shaolin Temple only.

2. According to paragraph 2, what is Tai Chi like?
   A. Fast and powerful.
   B. Slow and soft, good for balance and relaxation.
   C. Only for young people.
   D. The same as Shaolin Kung Fu.

3. What benefits does Kung Fu bring to students?
   A. It only makes them stronger.
   B. It strengthens the body, trains the mind, and teaches respect and discipline.
   C. It helps them fight better.
   D. It has no benefits for study.

4. What is the real goal of Kung Fu according to the passage?
   A. To hurt others.
   B. To win competitions.
   C. To improve yourself—body and mind.
   D. To become a movie star.

5. Who can practice Kung Fu?
   A. Only strong and flexible people.
   B. Everyone; you do not need to be strong or flexible to start.
   C. Only boys.
   D. Only people in China.

---

## 答案与解析

**1. 答案：B**

【题型】主旨大意题

【定位】第①段及全文

【解析】
- 第①段指出 Kung Fu 不仅是打斗，而是结合身体锻炼、心理专注和传统文化的古老实践；后文从流派、益处、真正目标等方面展开。
- A“功夫电影”仅为引入。C“如何获胜”、D“仅少林寺历史”过于狭隘。故答案为 B。

**2. 答案：B**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第②段

【解析】
- 第②段写：“Some are slow and soft, like Tai Chi... It helps balance and relaxation.”
- A 描述的是 Shaolin Kung Fu。C、D 与原文不符。故答案为 B。

**3. 答案：B**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第③段

【解析】
- 第③段写：强身健体、训练专注、培养尊重与纪律（“strengthens the body... trains the mind... teaches respect... discipline and self-control”）。
- A、C 不全面。D 与“helps them do better in school”矛盾。故答案为 B。

**4. 答案：C**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第④段

【解析】
- 第④段写：“Kung Fu is not about hurting others. The real goal is to improve yourself—body and mind.”
- A、B、D 与原文不符。故答案为 C。

**5. 答案：B**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第⑤段

【解析】
- 第⑤段写：“You do not need to be strong or flexible to start. Kung Fu is for everyone.”
- A、C、D 与原文矛盾。故答案为 B。
