# 阅读理解（记叙文）

## **A Lesson I Learned on the Road**

①It was a sunny Saturday. I was riding my bike to the bookshop. I was in a hurry because my favourite writer was having a signing event at 2 p.m. I looked at my watch—I had only fifteen minutes left. I pedalled faster. The traffic light was red, but no cars were coming. I thought, "I can cross. It will save time."

②The moment I rode into the crossroads, a car turned the corner. It was so fast. I braked hard. My bike slid. I fell on the road. The car stopped just in front of me. My heart was beating like a drum. The driver got out. He was pale. "Are you OK?" he asked. I could not speak. My knee was bleeding. My hands were shaking.

③A kind lady helped me up. She called my mother. The driver said he was sorry. He had not seen me because I crossed on red. I wanted to say it was my fault, but the words stuck in my throat. My mother arrived and took me to the hospital. Luckily, I only had a few cuts. Nothing was broken.

④That night, I lay in bed and thought about what happened. I had broken the traffic rules. I had put myself in danger. I had scared the driver and worried my mother. All because I wanted to save a few minutes. It was not worth it. From that day on, I promised myself: I would never run a red light again, no matter how late I was.

⑤Now I always wait for the green light. I wear a helmet when I cycle. I look both ways before crossing. These small habits keep me safe. I share my story with my classmates too. Road safety is not boring—it saves lives. Mine could have been lost that day. I will never forget that lesson.

---

- brake /breɪk/ v. 刹车

## 题目

1. Why was the writer riding his bike so fast?
   A. He was late for a book signing event.
   B. He wanted to exercise.
   C. His mother asked him to hurry.
   D. The bookshop was closing.

2. What happened when the writer crossed the red light?
   A. He got to the bookshop on time.
   B. A car almost hit him; he fell and got hurt.
   C. A police officer stopped him.
   D. Nothing happened.

3. Whose fault was the accident?
   A. Only the driver's.
   B. Only the writer's.
   C. The writer crossed on red—it was mainly his fault.
   D. The traffic light's.

4. What did the writer learn from the accident?
   A. Bikes are dangerous.
   B. Never run a red light; road safety saves lives.
   C. Cars should not drive fast.
   D. He should never go to the bookshop again.

5. What does the writer do now to stay safe?
   A. He never rides a bike.
   B. He waits for green lights, wears a helmet, and looks both ways.
   C. He only rides on empty roads.
   D. He asks his mother to drive him everywhere.

---

## 答案与解析

**1. 答案：A**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第①段

【解析】
- 第①段写：骑车去书店，因喜欢的作家下午 2 点有签售会，时间紧迫（“my favourite writer was having a signing event at 2 p.m... I had only fifteen minutes left”）。
- B、C、D 未提及。故答案为 A。

**2. 答案：B**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第②段

【解析】
- 第②段写：闯红灯时一辆车拐弯驶来，他急刹车后滑倒，汽车在面前停下；膝盖流血、双手发抖。
- A、C、D 与原文不符。故答案为 B。

**3. 答案：C**

【题型】推理判断题

【定位】第②段、第④段

【解析】
- 第②段司机说“he had not seen me because I crossed on red”；第④段作者反思“I had broken the traffic rules... It was my fault”。
- A 与“I crossed on red”矛盾。B“only”过于绝对，司机也可能有责任，但主要责任在作者。D 不合常理。故答案为 C。

**4. 答案：B**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第④段、第⑤段

【解析】
- 第④段写：承诺不再闯红灯；第⑤段写：道路安全能救命，自己那天差点丧命。
- A、C、D 与原文不符。故答案为 B。

**5. 答案：B**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第⑤段

【解析】
- 第⑤段写：“I always wait for the green light. I wear a helmet when I cycle. I look both ways before crossing.”
- A、C、D 与原文不符。故答案为 B。
