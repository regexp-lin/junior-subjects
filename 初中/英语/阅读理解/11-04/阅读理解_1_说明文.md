# 阅读理解（说明文）

**Chopsticks: Culture and History**

① Chopsticks are one of the oldest eating tools in the world. They have been used in China for more than 3,000 years. Today, chopsticks are not only used in China but also in Japan, Korea, Vietnam, and other Asian countries. They are a symbol of Asian food culture.

② The earliest chopsticks were probably made of bamboo or wood. They were used for cooking, not for eating. People used them to pick up food from hot pots. Over time, chopsticks became the main tool for eating meals. In ancient China, using chopsticks was a sign of good manners. Nobles and scholars were expected to use them correctly. Today, learning to use chopsticks is still part of growing up in many Asian families.

③ Chopsticks come in different shapes and materials. Chinese chopsticks are usually long and have blunt tips. Japanese chopsticks are shorter and pointed. Korean chopsticks are often flat and made of metal. In China, chopsticks can be made of bamboo, wood, plastic, or even silver. Some families keep beautiful chopsticks for special occasions like festivals.

④ There are certain rules when using chopsticks. Do not stick chopsticks upright in a bowl of rice. In some cultures, this reminds people of funerals. Do not point at others with chopsticks. Do not use chopsticks to dig through food. These customs show respect for others and for the food we eat.

⑤ Chopsticks are more than just tools. They carry history, culture, and the spirit of sharing. When families eat together, they share dishes from the same plates. Chopsticks connect people around the table. In this way, they represent harmony and togetherness in Asian culture.

**注释：** blunt 钝的；harmony 和谐

**Questions:**

1. How long have chopsticks been used in China?

   A. Eating rice only.

   C. Fighting.
   D. Drawing.

   A. They are longer.
   B. They are often flat and made of metal.
   C. They are always wooden.
   D. They cannot be used for eating.

4. Why should we not stick chopsticks upright in a bowl of rice?
   A. Because it is too difficult.
   B. Because it reminds people of funerals in some cultures.
   C. Because the rice will get cold.
   D. Because it looks ugly.

5. What can we infer from the passage?
   A. Chopsticks are only used in China.
   B. Chopsticks represent more than eating — they stand for culture, sharing, and togetherness.
   C. No one uses chopsticks anymore.
   D. Chopsticks were invented in Japan.

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第①段 "They have been used in China for more than 3,000 years"
**【解析】** 筷子在中国已有逾三千年历史。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第②段 "The earliest chopsticks were probably made of bamboo or wood. They were used for cooking, not for eating... to pick up food from hot pots"
**【解析】** 最早用于烹饪、从热锅中取食。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第③段 "Korean chopsticks are often flat and made of metal"
**【解析】** 韩国筷子多为扁平且由金属制成。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第④段 "Do not stick chopsticks upright in a bowl of rice... this reminds people of funerals"
**【解析】** 在部分文化中，将筷子竖直插在饭里会让人联想到葬礼。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 推理判断
**【定位】** 第⑤段 "Chopsticks are more than just tools. They carry history, culture... represent harmony and togetherness"
**【解析】** 筷子不仅是餐具，还承载历史和文化，象征和谐与团聚。B项正确。A、C、D项与文意不符。故选B。
