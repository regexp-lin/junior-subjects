> **03-14 第2篇**

# 阅读理解 第2篇（议论文）

## 阅读下面短文，从每题所给的 A、B、C、D 四个选项中，选出最佳选项。

---

① Should students be allowed to use mobile phones at school? This has been a hot topic among teachers, parents, and students for many years. Some people think phones should be completely banned at school, while others believe students should be allowed to use them. Let's look at both sides.

② Those who are against phones at school have good reasons. First, phones can be a big distraction. Students may spend time playing games, watching videos, or chatting with friends online instead of paying attention in class. Second, phones can lead to cyberbullying. Some students use social media to say mean things about their classmates, which can hurt feelings and cause serious problems.

③ There is also a fairness issue. Not all families can afford expensive phones. If some students bring the latest smartphones while others don't have one at all, it may make some students feel left out or embarrassed. This is not good for building a friendly school environment.

④ On the other hand, supporters say phones can be useful tools for learning. Students can use educational apps, look up information quickly, and even take notes in class. In an emergency, a phone allows students to contact their parents right away. Also, learning to use technology responsibly is an important skill for the future.

⑤ In my opinion, the best solution is to find a middle ground. Schools could allow students to bring phones but require them to keep their phones turned off or in their bags during class time. After school, students can use their phones freely. This way, phones won't disturb learning, but students can still use them when needed.

---

**注释：** ban 禁止；distraction 分心/干扰；cyberbullying 网络霸凌；emergency 紧急情况

---

### 题目

1. What is one reason some people are against phones at school according to Paragraph ②?
   A. Phones are too heavy for students to carry.
   B. Phones can be a distraction in class.
   C. Phones are too expensive for schools to buy.
   D. Phones make teachers feel nervous.

2. What is the "fairness issue" mentioned in Paragraph ③?
   A. Some teachers give higher grades to students with phones.
   B. Girls are allowed to use phones but boys are not.
   C. Not all families can afford expensive phones.
   D. Students with phones have to share them with others.

3. According to Paragraph ④, how can phones be useful at school?
   A. Students can use educational apps for learning.
   B. Students can play games during class breaks.
   C. Students can make phone calls in class.
   D. Students can sell their old phones to classmates.

4. The underlined phrase "a middle ground" in Paragraph ⑤ probably means ________.
   A. a large playground in the school
   B. the ground floor of a building
   C. a piece of land between two schools
   D. a solution that satisfies both sides

5. What is the writer's suggestion about phones at school?
   A. Students should never bring phones to school.
   B. Phones should be kept off during class but allowed after school.
   C. Students should be allowed to use phones anytime.
   D. Schools should give every student a free phone.

---

### 答案与解析

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段第2句 "First, phones can be a big distraction."

**【解析】** 原文列出了反对在学校使用手机的理由，其中首先提到手机可能造成很大的干扰——学生可能会花时间玩游戏、看视频或在线聊天，而不是在课堂上认真听讲。B 项"手机可能在课堂上造成干扰"与原文完全对应。A 项"手机太重学生不方便携带"原文未提及重量问题；C 项"手机对学校来说太贵"偷换了主语——原文第③段谈的是家庭负担，不是学校购买；D 项"手机让老师感到紧张"属于无中生有。故选 B。

---

**2. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段第1-2句 "There is also a fairness issue. Not all families can afford expensive phones."

**【解析】** 原文明确指出公平问题在于"不是所有家庭都能负担得起昂贵的手机"。如果一些学生带最新的智能手机而其他人没有，可能会让部分学生感到被排斥或尴尬。C 项与原文完全吻合。A 项"有些老师给有手机的学生更高的分数"原文未提及成绩评分问题；B 项"允许女生用但不允许男生用"属于无中生有；D 项"有手机的学生必须和别人共享"也未在原文中出现。故选 C。

---

**3. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段第2句 "Students can use educational apps, look up information quickly, and even take notes in class."

**【解析】** 原文列出了支持者认为手机有用的方面：使用教育类应用程序（educational apps）、快速查找信息、在课堂上做笔记。A 项"学生可以使用教育类应用来学习"与原文直接对应。B 项"学生可以在课间玩游戏"——玩游戏是第②段提到的负面行为，不是第④段支持者的理由；C 项"学生可以在课堂上打电话"——虽然原文提到在紧急情况下可以联系父母，但"在课堂上打电话"扭曲了原文含义；D 项"学生可以把旧手机卖给同学"完全无中生有。故选 A。

---

**4. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 词义猜测

**【定位】** 第⑤段第1句 "In my opinion, the best solution is to find a middle ground."

**【解析】** 根据上下文，前几段分别展示了反对方和支持方的理由，而第⑤段作者提出了自己的观点——"最好的解决方案是找到一个 middle ground"。随后作者具体描述了这个方案：允许带手机但上课时关机或放在书包里，放学后自由使用。这个方案既照顾了反对方的担忧（上课不受干扰），又满足了支持方的需求（学生可以使用手机）。因此"middle ground"指的是"让双方都满意的折中方案"。D 项最准确。A 项"学校的大操场"将"ground"误解为操场的字面意义；B 项"建筑物的底层"同样是对"ground"的字面误读；C 项"两所学校之间的一块地"也是字面理解。故选 D。

**【技巧点拨】** 词义猜测题遇到短语时，不可拆分单词进行字面翻译。要结合上下文语境理解整体含义。本题中"middle"（中间的）+"ground"（立场/基础）= 折中立场/中间方案。

---

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 主旨大意

**【定位】** 第⑤段第2-3句 "Schools could allow students to bring phones but require them to keep their phones turned off or in their bags during class time. After school, students can use their phones freely."

**【解析】** 作者在最后一段明确提出了自己的建议：学校可以允许学生带手机，但要求上课期间关机或放在书包里；放学后学生可以自由使用手机。B 项"上课时手机应该关闭，放学后可以使用"最准确地概括了作者的建议。A 项"学生不应该带手机上学"过于绝对——作者建议允许带手机，只是上课时限制使用；C 项"学生应该被允许随时使用手机"忽略了上课时关机的限制条件；D 项"学校应该给每个学生一部免费手机"属于无中生有。故选 B。

**【技巧点拨】** 议论文的写作建议通常出现在最后一段。本文作者采用"两方论证 + 提出折中方案"的写作结构，最终建议是带有条件的允许——注意把握条件限定词"but require"和"during class time"。
