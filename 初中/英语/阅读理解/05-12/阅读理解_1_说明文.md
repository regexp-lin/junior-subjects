# 阅读理解（说明文）

① Have you ever stood on a beach and watched the water slowly move closer to your feet, and then move away again? This rising and falling of the sea is called the tide (潮汐). Tides happen every day along the coasts of the world, and they follow a regular pattern.

② Tides are mainly caused by the pull of the Moon's gravity (引力) on the Earth. Although the Moon is about 384,000 kilometers away, its gravity is strong enough to pull the ocean water toward it. The side of the Earth closest to the Moon has the strongest pull, so the water there rises and creates a high tide. At the same time, on the opposite side of the Earth, another high tide happens because of the spinning force of the Earth. The areas between these two high points experience low tides.

③ Most coastal areas have two high tides and two low tides each day. The time between one high tide and the next is about twelve hours and twenty-five minutes. This means that the tide schedule shifts a little bit every day. Fishermen and sailors have learned to follow this schedule carefully because it affects their work greatly.

④ The Sun also plays a role in creating tides, but its effect is smaller than the Moon's because the Sun is much farther away. However, when the Sun, the Moon, and the Earth line up — during a full moon or a new moon — their combined gravity produces extra-large tides called spring tides (大潮). When the Sun and Moon pull in different directions, the tides become smaller. These are called neap tides (小潮).

⑤ Tides are not just interesting science — they are important for life near the sea. Many sea animals, such as crabs and starfish, depend on the changing tides to find food. Tidal pools (潮汐池) left behind by low tides are home to small fish, shrimp, and colorful sea plants. Understanding tides also helps people stay safe at the beach, because swimming during a strong tide can be dangerous.

**注释：** tide 潮汐；gravity 引力；spinning 旋转的；coastal 沿海的；sailor 水手；spring tide 大潮；neap tide 小潮；crab 螃蟹；starfish 海星；shrimp 虾

### 选择题

1. According to paragraph ②, what mainly causes the tides?
   A. The heat from the Sun.
   B. The wind blowing over the ocean.
   C. The pull of the Moon's gravity on the Earth.
   D. The movement of fish under the water.

2. How often does a high tide happen in most coastal areas?
   A. Once a day.
   B. Twice a day.
   C. Three times a day.
   D. Four times a day.

3. What are "spring tides" according to paragraph ④?
   A. Tides that only happen in the spring season.
   B. Extra-large tides when the Sun, Moon, and Earth line up.
   C. Very small tides caused by the Sun alone.
   D. Tides that happen only at night during a full moon.

4. What can we infer from paragraph ③ about fishermen?
   A. They do not care about the tide schedule at all.
   B. They only go fishing during high tides.
   C. They pay close attention to tide times for their work.
   D. They prefer to work on days with no tides.

5. What is the best title for this passage?
   A. The Moon and Its Light
   B. How Tides Work and Why They Matter
   C. Dangerous Animals in the Sea

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段第1句 "Tides are mainly caused by the pull of the Moon's gravity on the Earth."

**【解析】** 原文明确说明潮汐主要由月球引力对地球的拉力造成。A 项"太阳的热量"文中未提及与潮汐成因的关系；B 项"风吹过海面"属于无中生有；D 项"鱼在水下的运动"更是毫无根据。故选 C。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段第1句 "Most coastal areas have two high tides and two low tides each day."

**【解析】** 原文清楚指出大多数沿海地区每天有两次涨潮和两次退潮。A 项"一天一次"与原文不符；C 项"一天三次"和 D 项"一天四次"均与原文矛盾。故选 B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "when the Sun, the Moon, and the Earth line up — during a full moon or a new moon — their combined gravity produces extra-large tides called spring tides"

**【解析】** 原文解释大潮（spring tides）是太阳、月球和地球连成一线时，引力叠加产生的特大潮汐。A 项把 spring 误解为"春天"，属于偷换概念；C 项说是"仅由太阳引起的小潮"与原文相反；D 项限定"只在满月夜间发生"过于片面。故选 B。

**4. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第③段最后一句 "Fishermen and sailors have learned to follow this schedule carefully because it affects their work greatly."

**【解析】** 原文说渔民和水手学会了仔细遵循潮汐时间表，因为潮汐对他们的工作影响很大，由此可以推断渔民非常关注潮汐时间。A 项"完全不关心"与原文直接矛盾；B 项"只在涨潮时捕鱼"属于过度推断，原文未提及；D 项"更喜欢没有潮汐的日子"属于无中生有。故选 C。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 主旨大意

**【定位】** 全文各段：第①段引入潮汐概念，第②段解释成因，第③段说明规律，第④段介绍大潮小潮，第⑤段阐述潮汐的重要性。

**【解析】** 全文围绕潮汐的成因、规律和重要性展开，B 项"潮汐如何运作以及为什么重要"最能概括全文主旨。A 项"月亮和它的光"以偏概全，只涉及月球部分内容；C 项"海里的危险动物"文中未涉及；D 项"海滩上的一天"过于宽泛，未体现科普性质。故选 B。
