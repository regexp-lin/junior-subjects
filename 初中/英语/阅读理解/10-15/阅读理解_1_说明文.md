# 阅读理解（说明文）

**Human Exploration of Mars**

① Mars is the fourth planet from the sun. It is often called the "Red Planet" because of its reddish colour. For many years, humans have dreamed of visiting Mars. Today, scientists and space agencies around the world are working hard to make this dream come true.

② Why do we want to go to Mars? First, we want to learn more about the solar system. Mars may have had water and perhaps even life in the past. By studying Mars, we can understand how planets form and change. Second, Mars might become a second home for humans in the future. Earth has limited resources. If we can live on Mars one day, it could help us ensure the long-term survival of our species. Third, exploring Mars pushes technology forward. The challenges of space travel have led to many inventions that we use on Earth today.

③ So far, we have sent robots to Mars. Rovers like Curiosity and Perseverance can drive on the Martian surface. They take photos, collect soil samples, and send data back to Earth. These robots have found evidence of ancient rivers and lakes on Mars. They have also detected signs that could mean past microbial life. But robots cannot do everything. Humans can think, adapt, and make decisions on the spot. That is why sending people to Mars is the next big step.

④ However, the journey to Mars is very difficult. It takes about seven months to get there. Astronauts would face dangers such as radiation, loneliness, and the risk of equipment failure. They would need enough food, water, and air for the whole trip. Scientists are still working on solutions to these problems.

⑤ Despite the challenges, the dream of landing humans on Mars has never been stronger. Several countries and private companies have plans to send people to Mars in the 2030s or 2040s. Perhaps in our lifetime, we will see the first humans walking on the Red Planet.

**注释：** rover 探测车；microbial 微生物的

**Questions:**

1. Why is Mars called the "Red Planet"?
   A. Because it is the hottest planet.
   B. Because of its reddish colour.
   C. Because it is the smallest planet.
   D. Because it has no water.

2. According to Paragraph ②, which is a reason for exploring Mars?
   A. Mars has more gold than Earth.
   B. We want to learn more about the solar system and Mars might become a second home for humans.
   C. Mars is very close to Earth.
   D. There are no challenges in space travel.

3. What have the Mars rovers done?
   A. They have built houses on Mars.
   B. They have taken photos, collected soil samples, and found evidence of ancient water.
   C. They have brought astronauts to Mars.
   D. They have found people living on Mars.

4. Why do we want to send humans to Mars instead of only robots?
   A. Because robots are too expensive.
   B. Because humans can think, adapt, and make decisions on the spot.
   C. Because robots refuse to go.
   D. Because humans need a holiday.

5. What can we infer from the last paragraph?
   A. No one is interested in Mars anymore.
   B. The dream of sending humans to Mars is strong, and it may happen in the coming decades.
   C. Mars exploration has been cancelled.
   D. Only one country plans to explore Mars.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第①段 "It is often called the 'Red Planet' because of its reddish colour."
**【解析】** 火星因其偏红的颜色而得名"红色星球"。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第②段 "we want to learn more about the solar system... Mars might become a second home for humans in the future"
**【解析】** 探索火星的原因包括：了解更多太阳系知识、火星可能成为人类第二家园。B项准确。A、C、D项原文未提及。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第③段 "They take photos, collect soil samples, and send data back... found evidence of ancient rivers and lakes"
**【解析】** 火星车拍照、采集土壤、传回数据，并发现古代河流湖泊的痕迹。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第③段 "Humans can think, adapt, and make decisions on the spot. That is why sending people to Mars is the next big step."
**【解析】** 人类可以思考、适应并在现场做决定，因此派出宇航员是下一步目标。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 推理判断
**【定位】** 第⑤段 "the dream of landing humans on Mars has never been stronger... we will see the first humans walking on the Red Planet"
**【解析】** 人类登陆火星的愿望更强烈，多国计划在2030–2040年代实现，可推知B项。A、C、D项与文意相反。故选B。
