# 阅读理解（记叙文）

**Helping My Brother Overcome His Fear of the Dark**

① My little brother Tom is seven years old. He is brave during the day, but at night he is afraid of the dark. He would not go to the bathroom alone. He always wanted the light on when he slept. "There might be monsters under my bed," he said. I wanted to help him.

② I read that many children are afraid of the dark. It is normal. The dark can make our imagination run wild. We cannot see clearly, so we imagine things that are not there. I decided to show Tom that his room was safe.

③ One night, I took a torch and said, "Let us be detectives. We will search your room and find out what is really there." Tom liked the idea. We looked under the bed — just some toys and a pair of slippers. We looked inside the wardrobe — only clothes. We checked the corner — nothing. "See?" I said. "There are no monsters. Your room is safe." Tom nodded slowly. He seemed a little less scared.

④ Over the next few nights, I sat with Tom until he fell asleep. I left a small night light on at first. Then we made it dimmer and dimmer. "You can close your eyes and think of something happy," I told him. "Like our trip to the park or the ice cream we had." He tried it and soon fell asleep with a smile.

⑤ After two weeks, Tom could go to the bathroom by himself. He did not need the big light on anymore. One night he said, "Brother, thank you. The dark is not so scary now." I felt proud. Sometimes the best way to overcome fear is not to fight it alone, but to have someone by your side.

**注释：** torch 手电筒；wardrobe 衣柜；dimmer 更暗的

---

**Questions:**

1. What was Tom afraid of?
   A. Dogs.
   B. The dark.
   C. School.
   D. Ice cream.

2. Why did the writer suggest being "detectives"?
   A. To play a game.
   B. To help Tom see that his room was safe and there were no monsters.
   C. To find lost things.
   D. To scare Tom more.

3. What did they find under Tom's bed?
   A. Monsters.
   B. Toys and a pair of slippers.
   C. Money.
   D. A torch.

4. How did the writer help Tom get used to the dark?
   A. By turning off all the lights at once.
   B. By sitting with him and slowly making the light dimmer over time.
   C. By locking him in the dark room.
   D. By buying him a monster toy.

5. What did the writer learn from this experience?
   A. Children should always sleep alone.
   B. The best way to overcome fear is to have someone by your side.
   C. The dark is always scary.
   D. Brothers never help each other.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第①段 "at night he is afraid of the dark"
**【解析】** 汤姆害怕黑暗。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 推理判断
**【定位】** 第③段 "Let us be detectives. We will search your room and find out what is really there... See? There are no monsters. Your room is safe."
**【解析】** 扮演侦探是为了和汤姆一起检查房间，发现没有怪物，证明房间安全。B项正确。A、C、D项偏离目的。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第③段 "We looked under the bed — just some toys and a pair of slippers."
**【解析】** 床下只有玩具和拖鞋。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第④段 "I sat with Tom until he fell asleep. I left a small night light on at first. Then we made it dimmer and dimmer."
**【解析】** 作者陪弟弟入睡，先保留小夜灯，再逐渐调暗。B项准确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 推理判断
**【定位】** 第⑤段 "Sometimes the best way to overcome fear is not to fight it alone, but to have someone by your side."
**【解析】** 作者体会到克服恐惧的最好方法是有他人陪伴，而不是独自面对。B项正确。A、C、D项与文意不符。故选B。
