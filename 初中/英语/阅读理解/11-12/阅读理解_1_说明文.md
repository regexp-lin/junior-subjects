# 阅读理解（说明文）

**Urban Heat Island Effect**

① Have you noticed that cities are often hotter than the countryside? On a summer evening, the temperature in the city centre can be 3 to 5 degrees higher than in nearby villages. This is called the "urban heat island effect." What causes it?

② First, cities have many buildings, roads, and pavements made of concrete and asphalt. These materials absorb heat during the day and release it at night. In the countryside, plants and soil absorb less heat and cool down faster. Second, cities have fewer trees and green spaces. Trees provide shade and release water through their leaves, which cools the air. Without enough green areas, cities stay hot. Third, cars, factories, and air conditioners produce a lot of heat. This "waste heat" adds to the temperature.

③ The urban heat island effect can cause problems. In summer, it makes cities uncomfortable. It increases the use of air conditioners, which use more electricity and produce more heat. It can also affect people's health, especially the elderly. Heavy rain can fall over hot cities, leading to floods. And at night, when the countryside cools down, cities often stay warm, so people may have trouble sleeping.

④ What can we do? Plant more trees and create more parks. Use light-coloured materials for buildings and roads — they reflect heat instead of absorbing it. Design buildings that allow wind to flow through. Use less air conditioning and more natural ways to stay cool. Every small change helps.

⑤ Understanding the urban heat island effect helps us build smarter, cooler cities for the future.

**注释：** concrete 混凝土；asphalt 沥青；reflect 反射

**Questions:**

1. What is the urban heat island effect?
   A. Cities are colder than the countryside.
   B. Cities are often 3 to 5 degrees hotter than the countryside.
   C. Cities have no heat.
   D. Only villages have heat.

2. According to Paragraph ②, why do concrete and asphalt make cities hotter?
   A. They are cheap.
   B. They absorb heat during the day and release it at night.
   C. They are white.
   D. They grow trees.

3. How do trees help cool the air?
   A. They block all light.
   B. They provide shade and release water through leaves.
   C. They produce heat.
   D. They remove all buildings.

4. What problem can the heat island effect cause?
   A. Cities become too cold.
   B. It increases AC use, affects health, and can lead to floods.
   C. Everyone moves to the countryside.
   D. Trees grow too fast.

5. What can we infer from Paragraph ④?
   A. Nothing can be done about the heat island effect.
   B. Simple changes like planting trees and using light-coloured materials can help reduce the effect.
   C. We should build more roads.
   D. Air conditioners are the only solution.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第①段 "the temperature in the city centre can be 3 to 5 degrees higher than in nearby villages. This is called the 'urban heat island effect'"
**【解析】** 城市热岛效应指城市中心气温可比周边乡村高3至5度。B项准确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第②段 "These materials absorb heat during the day and release it at night"
**【解析】** 混凝土和沥青白天吸热、夜间释放热量。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第②段 "Trees provide shade and release water through their leaves, which cools the air"
**【解析】** 树木提供荫凉，并通过叶片释放水分使空气降温。B项准确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第③段 "increases the use of air conditioners... affect people's health... leading to floods"
**【解析】** 热岛效应导致空调使用增加、影响健康、可能引发洪水。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 推理判断
**【定位】** 第④段 "Plant more trees... Use light-coloured materials... Every small change helps"
**【解析】** 植树、使用浅色材料等简单措施有助于减轻热岛效应。B项正确。A、C、D项与文意不符。故选B。
