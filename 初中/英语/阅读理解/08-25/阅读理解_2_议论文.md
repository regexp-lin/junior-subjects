# 阅读理解（议论文）

① Online games have become very popular among teenagers. Many students play games after school to relax and have fun. However, people often argue about whether online games are good or bad for students.

② Those who think online games are helpful point out several benefits. First, some games improve problem-solving skills. Players need to think quickly and make decisions. Second, many games are played in teams, so they can help students learn to work with others. Third, games can be a way to reduce stress. After a long day of study, playing for a short time can help students feel better. Fourth, some games teach history or science in a fun way.

③ However, others worry about the negative effects. Too much gaming can affect study time. Some students stay up late playing and feel tired in class. Gaming addiction (成瘾) is a real problem — when students cannot stop playing, their grades may drop. Besides, sitting in front of a screen for hours is bad for eyesight and health. Some games also have violence (暴力) which may not be suitable for young people.

④ So what can we do? Experts suggest setting time limits. For example, students could play for no more than one hour on weekdays and two hours on weekends. Parents should know what games their children play and talk with them about balance. Schools can hold activities like sports and clubs to give students other ways to have fun.

⑤ In my view, online games are neither all good nor all bad. They can be useful if we use them wisely. The important thing is to keep a balance: enjoy games as a hobby, but never let them take the place of study, sleep, or real-life friendships. Self-control is the key.

**注释：** addiction 成瘾；violence 暴力

---

**Questions:**

1. According to Paragraph ②, which is NOT a benefit of online games?
   A. Improving problem-solving skills.
   B. Learning to work in teams.
   C. Helping reduce stress.
   D. Making students stay up late.

2. What problem may gaming addiction cause?
   A. Students may become healthier.
   B. Students' grades may drop.
   C. Students may eat more.
   D. Students may exercise more.

3. What do experts suggest about game time?
   A. Students should never play games.
   B. Students could play for no more than one hour on weekdays.
   C. Students can play as long as they want.
   D. Only parents can play games.

4. What does the writer think about online games?
   A. They are always bad.
   B. They are always good.
   C. They can be useful if used wisely; balance is important.
   D. They should be banned in all schools.

5. What does "balance" in the last paragraph mean in this context?
   A. Standing on one foot.
   B. Keeping a healthy mix of gaming, study, sleep, and friendships.
   C. Saving money.
   D. Eating healthy food.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段列举的好处：problem-solving, teamwork, reduce stress, teach in fun way

**【解析】** A、B、C项均为原文提及的游戏益处。D项"让学生熬夜"是第③段提到的负面影响，非益处。故选D。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "when students cannot stop playing, their grades may drop"

**【解析】** 原文明确说游戏成瘾可能导致成绩下降。A、C、D项与成瘾的负面影响不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "students could play for no more than one hour on weekdays and two hours on weekends"

**【解析】** 专家建议平日不超过一小时，周末不超过两小时。A、C、D项均与原文不符。故选B。

**4. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "online games are neither all good nor all bad. They can be useful if we use them wisely... keep a balance"

**【解析】** 作者认为游戏并非全好或全坏，明智使用则有益，关键在于保持平衡。A、B过于绝对，D项原文未提及。故选C。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 词义猜测

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "keep a balance: enjoy games as a hobby, but never let them take the place of study, sleep, or real-life friendships"

**【解析】** 根据上下文，"balance"指在游戏、学习、睡眠和真实友谊之间保持适度，不偏废。A、C、D项与语境不符。故选B。
