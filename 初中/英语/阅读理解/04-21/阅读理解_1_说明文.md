# 阅读理解（说明文）

① Today, bicycles are everywhere. We ride them to school, to the park, or just for fun. They are a cheap, green, and healthy way to travel. But have you ever wondered what the first bicycle looked like? The history of the bicycle is quite interesting and goes back more than 200 years.

② The very first "bicycle" was invented in Germany in 1817 by a man named Karl Drais. However, his machine was very different from modern bikes. It had two wheels and a wooden frame (车架), but it did not have any pedals (脚踏板)! To move forward, the rider had to push his feet against the ground, just like a child's balance bike today. People called it the "running machine".

③ About 50 years later, in the 1860s, a French inventor added pedals to the front wheel. This new design was called the "velocipede" (骨摇车). It was a big step forward, but the ride was very uncomfortable (不舒服的) because the wheels were made of hard wood and iron (铁). Every time it hit a stone, the whole bike would shake heavily.

④ To make bikes go faster, inventors in the 1870s created the "Penny-Farthing". This strange bike had a huge front wheel and a very small back wheel. It could go very fast, but it was also dangerous. If the rider had to stop suddenly, he could easily fall over the handlebars (车把) and hurt his head!

⑤ Finally, in the 1880s, the "safety bicycle" was born. It had two wheels of the same size, a metal chain (链条), and rubber tires (橡胶轮胎) filled with air. This design made riding much safer and more comfortable. It looked very much like the bicycles we ride today. Thanks to these clever inventors, the bicycle has become one of the most popular vehicles in the world.

**注释：** pedal 脚踏板；uncomfortable 不舒服的；iron 铁；handlebar 车把；chain 链条；tire 轮胎

### 选择题

1. How did people move the first "bicycle" invented in 1817?
A. By pushing the pedals very fast.
B. By pulling a long metal chain.
C. By pushing their feet against the ground.
D. By using a small engine (发动机).

2. Why was the ride of the "velocipede" very uncomfortable in the 1860s?
A. Because it was too heavy for children to carry.
B. Because it only had one small wheel.
C. Because it had no pedals at all.
D. Because the wheels were made of hard wood and iron.

3. Form paragraph ④, what was the danger of the "Penny-Farthing" bike?
A. The wheels would easily catch fire.
B. The rider could easily fall over the handlebars if he stopped suddenly.
C. The chain would break and hurt the rider's legs.
D. The huge back wheel could crush (压碎) things behind it.

4. Which of the following is NOT a feature (特征) of the "safety bicycle" in the 1880s?
A. Two wheels of the same size.
B. A metal chain.
C. Wheels made of hard wood.
D. Rubber tires filled with air.

5. What would be the best title for this passage?
A. The Dangerous "Penny-Farthing"
B. How to Ride a Modern Bicycle Safely
C. Karl Drais: A Great German Inventor
D. The Interesting History of the Bicycle

---

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段中 "To move forward, the rider had to push his feet against the ground..."

**【解析】** 原文中明言骑这种1817年的原始自行车是要骑手双脚蹬地（push his feet against the ground）才能前进的，就像今天的儿童平衡车一样。那是没有脚踏板、链条或者发动机的（A、B、D皆错）。故选C。

**2. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段第2句 "...but the ride was very uncomfortable (不舒服的) because the wheels were made of hard wood and iron (铁)."

**【解析】** 原文中解答了乘坐不舒服的原因：因为车轮是由硬木头和铁（hard wood and iron）制作的。A（太重拿不动）、B（只有一个小轮子）、C（没有踏板在六十年代已经有踏板了）都是错误信息。故选D。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解判断

**【定位】** 第④段最后一句 "If the rider had to stop suddenly, he could easily fall over the handlebars (车把) and hurt his head!"

**【解析】** 在第四段介绍“Penny-Farthing”（一种前轮巨大后轮极小的高高古怪自行车）时，作者直言它的危险在于：如果必须急停时，骑手极其容易从车把上翻过去而摔伤头部（fall over the handlebars）。故选B符合原文。

**4. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解匹配

**【定位】** 第⑤段第2句 "It had two wheels of the same size, a metal chain (链条), and rubber tires (橡胶轮胎) filled with air."

**【解析】** 原文中“安全自行车”（safety bicycle）有同样大小的双轮（A）、金属链条（B）、充气橡胶轮胎（D）。而“硬木轮”（C项：Wheels made of hard wood）是第③段讲述60年代发明的车型的老式特点。题目问的是不属于安全自行车的选项，故选C。

**5. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 主篇主旨

**【定位】** 首句导语及后文内容的时间线索展开。

**【解析】** 这是一篇很分明的历史沿革说明文，从1817的跑动型机器，到1860的加了踏板的骨摇车，到1870的高轮车，定型于1880年代的安全自行车。因此，The Interesting History of the Bicycle（自行车有趣的进化史）涵盖了整文中心思想，其余各项均太过片面。故选D。
