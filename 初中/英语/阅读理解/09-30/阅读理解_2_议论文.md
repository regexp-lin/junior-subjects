# 阅读理解（议论文）

① Competition is everywhere in school life. Students compete for high marks, for places in sports teams, for roles in plays, and for awards. Is competition good or bad for students? People hold different views.

② Those who believe competition is good say that it pushes students to work harder. When students know they will be compared with others, they try their best to improve. Competition can help students discover their strengths and weaknesses. It teaches them to set goals and work toward them. In real life, adults face competition too — for jobs, for promotions (晋升), for success. Learning to compete fairly at school prepares students for the future. Besides, healthy competition can be fun. Winning a race or a debate (辩论) can bring joy and build confidence. Losing can teach students to accept failure and try again.

③ However, those who think competition is harmful have their reasons. First, too much competition can cause stress and anxiety (焦虑). Some students worry so much about grades and rankings that they cannot enjoy learning. Second, competition can damage relationships. Friends may become rivals (对手) and stop helping each other. In some cases, students might cheat to win, which harms their character. Third, not everyone is good at competing. Some students are creative or kind, but they may not do well in exams or sports. If we only value competition, we may make these students feel worthless. Education should help every student grow, not just the "winners."

④ In my view, competition itself is neither good nor bad. It depends on how we use it. A little competition can motivate (激励) students, but too much can harm them. Schools should balance competition with cooperation (合作). For example, besides individual (个人的) tests, there can be group projects where students work as a team. Besides, we should teach students that winning is not the only goal. Doing your best and improving yourself matter more than beating others. We should also care about students who feel stressed and help them deal with pressure.

⑤ The key is to make competition healthy: fair, respectful, and not the only measure of success.

**注释：** promotion 晋升；debate 辩论；anxiety 焦虑；rival 对手；motivate 激励；cooperation 合作；individual 个人的

**Questions:**

1. According to Paragraph ②, why do some people think competition is good?
   A. Because it makes school easier.
   B. Because it pushes students to work harder, helps them discover strengths and weaknesses, and prepares them for real life; healthy competition can be fun.
   C. Because teachers like it.
   D. Because there is no other way to learn.

2. What harm does competition sometimes cause, according to opponents?
   A. Students become too rich.
   B. Stress and anxiety; damaged relationships; some may cheat; some students may feel worthless.
   C. Schools close down.
   D. Parents get angry.

3. What does the writer suggest about competition?
   A. Ban all competition.
   B. Balance competition with cooperation; teach that doing your best matters more than beating others; care about stressed students.
   C. Make competition the only measure of success.
   D. Only have group work, no tests.

4. The word "motivate" in Paragraph ④ probably means _____.
   A. stop
   B. encourage or push someone to act
   C. punish
   D. forget

5. What is the writer's main message?
   A. Competition is always bad.
   B. Competition can be good or bad depending on how we use it; the key is to make it healthy — fair, respectful, and not the only measure of success.
   C. Everyone must compete.
   D. Cooperation is useless.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "it pushes students to work harder... helps students discover their strengths and weaknesses... prepares students for the future... healthy competition can be fun"

**【解析】** 支持者认为竞争能督促努力、发现优劣、为未来做准备，健康竞争也有乐趣。A、C、D 项非主要理由。故选 B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "too much competition can cause stress and anxiety... can damage relationships... students might cheat... some students may feel worthless"

**【解析】** 反对者指出过度竞争导致压力焦虑、损害关系、可能作弊、部分学生感到无价值。A、C、D 项未提及。故选 B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第④段 "balance competition with cooperation... there can be group projects... teach students that winning is not the only goal... care about students who feel stressed"

**【解析】** 作者建议平衡竞争与合作、开展小组项目、强调尽力比战胜他人更重要、关注压力大的学生。A、C、D 项与建议矛盾。故选 B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 词义猜测

**【定位】** 第④段 "A little competition can motivate students" — 与 "too much can harm them" 对照

**【解析】** 适量竞争可激励学生。motivate 意为"激励、促使"。A、C、D 项含义不符。故选 B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 主旨大意

**【定位】** 第④、⑤段 "competition itself is neither good nor bad. It depends on how we use it... The key is to make competition healthy: fair, respectful, and not the only measure of success"

**【解析】** 作者认为竞争好坏取决于使用方式，关键在于使之健康、公平、尊重且非唯一成功标准。A、C、D 项与作者立场不符。故选 B。
