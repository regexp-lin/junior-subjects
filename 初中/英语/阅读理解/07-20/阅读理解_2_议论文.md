# 阅读理解（议论文）

① **Should Schools Reduce the Number of Exams?**

② In recent years, many students, parents and teachers have discussed a hot topic: should schools cut down the number of exams? Some argue that fewer exams would reduce stress (压力) and give students more time to learn. Others believe that regular exams are necessary to check progress. Let us think about both sides.

③ **Those who support reducing exams** say that students today face too much pressure. They have exams in almost every subject, and the results can make them worried or even anxious (焦虑的). Less testing might give students more time to understand and enjoy what they learn instead of just memorizing for tests. It could also help them develop other skills, such as teamwork and creativity (创造力), which are hard to measure in exams.

④ **Those who disagree** point out that exams serve important purposes. First, exams help teachers and students see what has been learned and what still needs work. Without regular tests, it is harder to know if students are making progress. Second, exams prepare students for future challenges (挑战)—for example, the high school entrance exam. Third, some students study better when they know a test is coming; exams can motivate (激励) them to review and consolidate (巩固) their knowledge.

⑤ **A balanced view:** There is no simple "yes" or "no" answer. The key may be finding the right balance. Perhaps schools could reduce some repetitive (重复的) small tests and keep important ones. They could also try different ways of assessment (评估), such as projects or presentations, instead of only written exams. In this way, students might feel less stressed while still having clear goals and useful feedback (反馈) on their learning.

**注释：** stress 压力；anxious 焦虑的；creativity 创造力；challenge 挑战；motivate 激励；consolidate 巩固；repetitive 重复的；assessment 评估；feedback 反馈

---

### 选择题

1. According to the passage, why do some people support reducing exams?
   A. Because students face too much pressure and exams make them worried; fewer tests could give more time to learn and develop other skills.
   B. Because exams are too expensive for schools.
   C. Because teachers do not like marking exam papers.
   D. Because exams are against the law.

2. What do those who disagree think exams can do?
   A. Exams can replace all homework.
   B. Exams help teachers and students see progress, prepare for future challenges, and motivate students to study.
   C. Exams are the only way to teach students.
   D. Exams make students too relaxed.

3. What does the writer suggest as a balanced solution?
   A. Cancel all exams forever.
   B. Have more exams than before.
   C. Let students decide whether to take exams or not.
   D. Reduce some repetitive small tests, keep important ones, and try different assessment methods like projects or presentations.

4. What can we infer from the passage?
   A. The writer believes that only one side of the argument is correct.
   B. All schools have already reduced exams.
   C. The writer thinks there is no single right answer and a balanced approach may work best.
   D. Exams have no value at all.

5. What is the main purpose of the passage?
   A. To tell readers that exams are bad.
   B. To persuade schools to keep all exams.
   C. To describe how to design exam questions.
   D. To present different opinions on whether to reduce exams and suggest a balanced approach.

---

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "students today face too much pressure... results can make them worried or even anxious... Less testing might give students more time to understand and enjoy what they learn... help them develop other skills, such as teamwork and creativity."

**【解析】** 支持者认为学生压力大、考试结果让人焦虑；减少考试可让学习时间更充足、并发展团队合作和创造力等技能。A项准确概括。B项费用、C项教师不愿批改、D项违法均未提及。故选A。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "exams help teachers and students see what has been learned... exams prepare students for future challenges... exams can motivate them to review and consolidate their knowledge."

**【解析】** 反对者指出考试有助于检查学习进度、为未来挑战做准备、激励学生复习巩固知识。B项正确。A项取代作业、C项唯一教学方式、D项让学生过于放松均与原文不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "Perhaps schools could reduce some repetitive small tests and keep important ones. They could also try different ways of assessment, such as projects or presentations."

**【解析】** 作者建议减少重复性小考、保留重要考试，并尝试项目或展示等多元评估方式。D项正确。A项取消所有考试、B项增加考试、C项由学生决定均与原文不符。故选D。

**4. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "There is no simple 'yes' or 'no' answer. The key may be finding the right balance."

**【解析】** 作者明确指出没有简单的对错答案，关键在于找到平衡。可推知作者持辩证态度，认为平衡方案可能最佳。C项正确。A项仅一方正确与作者态度矛盾；B项所有学校已减少、D项考试无价值均未提及。故选C。

**5. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 主旨/写作意图

**【定位】** 全文

**【解析】** 文章呈现支持与反对减少考试的双方观点，最后提出平衡建议，主旨是呈现不同看法并提出折中方案。D项准确概括。A项考试有害、B项坚持全部考试均过于片面；C项设计考题与主题不符。故选D。
