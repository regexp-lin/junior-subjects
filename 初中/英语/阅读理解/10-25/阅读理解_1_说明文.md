# 阅读理解（说明文）

**How Bats Use Echolation to "See" in the Dark**

① Bats are famous for flying at night. Unlike most animals, they do not rely on their eyes to find their way. Instead, they use a special ability called echolocation. This helps them hunt for insects and avoid obstacles even in complete darkness.

② How does echolocation work? Bats make high-pitched sounds that humans cannot hear. These sounds travel through the air and hit objects such as trees, walls, or insects. When the sound hits something, it bounces back as an echo. The bat's ears catch this echo. By listening to the echo, the bat can tell how far the object is, how big it is, and in which direction it lies. It is like the bat is "seeing" with its ears.

③ Different types of bats use echolocation in slightly different ways. Some bats send out short calls and listen for the echo. Others use continuous calls. The size and shape of a bat's ears also help. Large ears can catch faint echoes better. Some bats can even tell the difference between a tasty moth and a leaf just by the echo.

④ Scientists have studied bat echolocation for years. They have found that it is very accurate. A bat can detect an insect the size of a mosquito from several metres away. This skill is so useful that humans have copied it. Ships use sonar to find objects underwater. Doctors use ultrasound to see inside the human body. Both technologies work on the same principle as bat echolocation.

⑤ Bats have lived on Earth for millions of years. Their ability to "see" in the dark has helped them survive and become one of the most successful groups of mammals. Next time you see a bat flying at dusk, remember that it is using one of nature's most clever inventions.

**注释：** echolocation 回声定位；obstacle 障碍物；sonar 声呐

**Questions:**

1. Why can bats fly and hunt at night?
   A. Because they have very large eyes.
   B. Because they use echolocation instead of relying on eyes.
   C. Because the night is always bright.
   D. Because they never need to see.

2. What happens when a bat's sound hits an object?
   A. The sound disappears.
   B. The sound bounces back as an echo, and the bat hears it.
   C. The object makes a loud noise.
   D. The bat stops flying.

3. According to Paragraph ④, what have humans learned from bats?
   A. How to fly.
   B. Sonar and ultrasound, which work like echolocation.
   C. How to hunt insects.
   D. How to build bat houses.

4. What can we infer about bat ears?
   A. They are useless.
   B. They play an important role in catching echoes.
   C. Bats cannot hear well.
   D. All bats have the same ear shape.

5. What is the main purpose of this passage?
   A. To tell people to be afraid of bats.
   B. To explain how bats use echolocation to navigate and hunt in the dark.
   C. To encourage people to keep bats as pets.
   D. To compare bats with other mammals.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第①段 "they do not rely on their eyes to find their way. Instead, they use a special ability called echolocation"
**【解析】** 蝙蝠不依赖眼睛，而是使用回声定位。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第②段 "When the sound hits something, it bounces back as an echo. The bat's ears catch this echo"
**【解析】** 声波碰到物体后以回声反射，蝙蝠用耳朵接收。B项准确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第④段 "humans have copied it. Ships use sonar... Doctors use ultrasound... Both technologies work on the same principle as bat echolocation"
**【解析】** 人类仿照蝙蝠发明了声呐和超声技术。B项正确。A、C、D项未在文中提及。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 推理判断
**【定位】** 第②段 "The bat's ears catch this echo"；第③段 "Large ears can catch faint echoes better"
**【解析】** 文中强调耳朵接收回声的作用，大耳朵能更好捕获微弱回声，可推知耳朵对回声定位很重要。B项正确。A、C、D项与文意不符。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 主旨大意
**【定位】** 全文围绕蝙蝠如何用回声定位在黑暗中导航和捕猎
**【解析】** 文章主要介绍蝙蝠利用回声定位在黑暗中觅食和避开障碍的原理。B项准确概括主旨。A、C、D项偏离主题。故选B。
