# 阅读理解（议论文）

① **Should Students Choose Their Own Seats?**

② In many classrooms, teachers decide where students sit. But some people think that students should be allowed to choose their own seats. This topic has caused a lot of discussion. Let us look at both sides.

③ **Those who support student-chosen seats** believe that when students sit with friends or people they feel comfortable with, they may participate (参加) more in class. They feel less nervous and are more willing to raise their hands and answer questions. Besides, choosing their own seats can teach students to be responsible (有责任心的). If they choose to sit with someone who distracts (使分心) them, they must face the result—perhaps lower grades. This helps them learn to make good decisions.

④ **Those who are against it** argue that some students will always choose to sit with their close friends. They may talk too much during class instead of listening. Teachers also need to consider students who cannot see the blackboard clearly or who have hearing problems. These students may need special seats. Moreover, letting students choose might cause arguments. Who gets the "best" seat? This could lead to unfairness (不公平) and even affect the classroom atmosphere (氛围).

⑤ **In conclusion,** there is no perfect answer. Some teachers use a mixed way: students can suggest where they want to sit, but the teacher makes the final decision. This balance might work better. The most important thing is that every student can learn well and feel comfortable in the classroom.

**注释：** participate 参加；responsible 有责任心的；distract 使分心；unfairness 不公平；atmosphere 氛围

---

### 选择题

1. According to the passage, what is one advantage of letting students choose their own seats?
   A. Teachers will have less work to do.
   B. Students may participate more because they feel comfortable with their seatmates.
   C. There will be no arguments in class.
   D. All students will get better grades immediately.

2. What might happen if a student chooses to sit with someone who distracts them?
   A. The teacher will change the seat for them.
   B. They may get lower grades and learn to make better decisions.
   C. Their parents will be called to school.
   D. They will be asked to leave the classroom.

3. Why do some people oppose student-chosen seats?
   A. Because it costs too much money.
   B. Because students may talk too much with friends, and some need special seats; it may also cause arguments.
   C. Because teachers do not like students to sit together.
   D. Because there are not enough seats in the classroom.

4. What does the passage suggest as a possible solution?
   A. Let students do whatever they want.
   B. Let teachers decide everything without listening to students.
   C. Use a mixed way: students suggest, teachers make the final decision.
   D. Change seats every day so nobody gets the best seat.

5. What is the writer's main purpose in writing this passage?
   A. To tell students they must choose their own seats.
   B. To present different opinions and show there is no perfect answer; the key is students learning well.
   C. To blame teachers for controlling seats.
   D. To explain why students should never sit with friends.

---

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "when students sit with friends or people they feel comfortable with, they may participate (参加) more in class. They feel less nervous and are more willing to raise their hands"

**【解析】** 文中指出支持者认为学生与朋友或相处舒适的人同坐时，会更积极参与课堂、减少紧张、更愿意举手。B项正确。A项教师工作量、C项无争执、D项成绩立即提高均非此处论述重点。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "If they choose to sit with someone who distracts (使分心) them, they must face the result—perhaps lower grades. This helps them learn to make good decisions."

**【解析】** 文中明确说明若选择与令人分心的人同坐，学生需面对结果（如成绩下降），从而学会做出更好决定。B项正确。A项教师换座、C项叫家长、D项赶出教室均未提及。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "some students will always choose to sit with their close friends. They may talk too much... students who cannot see the blackboard clearly or who have hearing problems... may need special seats... might cause arguments... lead to unfairness"

**【解析】** 反对理由包括：与好友同坐易闲聊、视听力困难学生需特殊座位、可能引发争执和不公。B项全面概括。A项费用、C项教师不愿学生同坐、D项座位不足均未提及。故选B。

**4. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "Some teachers use a mixed way: students can suggest where they want to sit, but the teacher makes the final decision. This balance might work better."

**【解析】** 作者提出混合方式：学生建议、教师最终决定，这种平衡可能更有效。C项正确。A项完全放任、B项完全不听取、D项每天换座均与原文建议不符。故选C。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 主旨理解 / 写作意图

**【定位】** 全文结构：第②段引出争议，第③④段分述正反观点，第⑤段总结 "there is no perfect answer... The most important thing is that every student can learn well and feel comfortable"

**【解析】** 文章呈现双方观点，指出无完美答案，强调关键是每位学生都能学好且感到舒适。B项准确概括写作意图。A项强制学生自选、C项指责教师、D项禁止与朋友同坐均与作者立场不符。故选B。
