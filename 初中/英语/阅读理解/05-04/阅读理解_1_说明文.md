# 阅读理解（说明文）

① When you look up at the night sky, you can see the glowing (发光的) moon. It looks like a bright silver plate. But did you know that the moon does not actually produce its own light? 

② Unlike the sun, which is a giant ball of burning gas, the moon is basically a large, cold rock floating in space. It is completely dark on its own. The reason we can see the moon shining at night is because it acts like a giant mirror. It reflects (反射) the light from the sun down to Earth. This is called "moonlight."

③ Because the moon orbits (绕轨道运行) around the Earth, the sun's light hits it from different angles (角度) throughout the month. This causes the moon to change its shape in our sky. These different shapes are called the phases (盈亏/相位) of the moon.

④ When the moon is exactly between the Earth and the sun, the side of the moon facing us is dark. We cannot see it. This is called a New Moon. As the moon continues its journey, we start to see a small, curved slice of light, which is a Crescent (新月) Moon. When the Earth is between the sun and the moon, the sun shines fully on the moon's face, creating a bright, round Full Moon.

⑤ The moon has no air, no wind, and no rain. Because there is no weather to change the surface, the footprints left by American astronauts in 1969 are still there today! The next time you walk under the moonlight, remember that you are actually looking at reflected sunlight from a cold, silent rock.

**注释：** glowing 发光的；reflect 反射；orbit 绕轨道运行；angle 角度；phase 盈亏/相位；crescent 新月

### 选择题

1. Where does the moon get its light from?
A. It produces its own light from burning gas.
B. It reflects the light from the Earth.
C. It reflects the light from the sun.
D. It has millions of glowing rocks on its surface.

2. Why does the moon change its shape in our sky according to paragraph ③?
A. Because large pieces of the moon fall off every week.
B. Because clouds cover different parts of it.
C. Because it grows bigger in the summer and shrinks in the winter.
D. Because it orbits the Earth and the sun's light hits it from different angles.

3. When can we see a Full Moon?
A. When the moon is exactly between the Earth and the sun.
B. When the Earth is between the sun and the moon.
C. When it is raining heavily at night.
D. When the moon stops moving in space.

4. What is the phase called when we cannot see the moon because its dark side faces us?
A. A Full Moon.
B. A Crescent Moon.
C. A New Moon.
D. A Silver Moon.

5. Why are the footprints left by astronauts in 1969 still on the moon today?
A. Because they used special glue to make the footprints stay.
B. Because there is no air, wind, or rain to wash them away.
C. Because aliens are protecting them.
D. Because the moon's surface is made of hard steel.

---

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段第3自然句 "The reason we can see the moon shining... is because it acts like a giant mirror. It reflects (反射) the light from the sun down to Earth."

**【解析】** 月球的光是从哪里来的呢？第二段明确指出，月球自己不会发光，它像一面巨大的镜子，反射来自太阳的光（It reflects the light from the sun）。这完美对应C选项。A自身发光、B反射地球的光、D自身有发光石头都不正确。故选C。

**2. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段第1-2句 "Because the moon orbits (绕轨道运行) around the Earth, the sun's light hits it from different angles... This causes the moon to change its shape in our sky."

**【解析】** 我们看到月亮形状发生变化（相位的变化）的原因是什么？书上第三段直接给出了因为月亮绕地球运行，这使得太阳光从不同的角度照射到它上面。所以D选项完全符合文意。A、B、C都属捏造的无厘头原因。故选D。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段最后一句 "When the Earth is between the sun and the moon, the sun shines fully on the moon's face, creating a bright, round Full Moon."

**【解析】** 满月（Full Moon）是怎么形成的？根据第四段描述，当地球位于太阳和月亮之间（the Earth is between the sun and the moon）时，太阳完全照亮了月球的正面，从而形成了又圆又亮的满月。这与B选项一致。A项是新月（New Moon）的条件。故选B。

**4. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解判断

**【定位】** 第④段第1-2句 "When the moon is exactly between the Earth and the sun, the side of the moon facing us is dark. We cannot see it. This is called a New Moon."

**【解析】** 当月球面向我们的一面是黑暗的，我们在地球上看不见它时，这被称为新月（New Moon）。题目询问“我们看不见、暗面朝向我们”时被称作什么相位，那自然是C选项（A New Moon）。A是满月、B是蛾眉月/新月形状、D银月为干扰项。故选C。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第⑤段第1-2句 "The moon has no air, no wind, and no rain. Because there is no weather to change the surface, the footprints left by American astronauts in 1969 are still there today!"

**【解析】** 宇航员1969年留下的脚印为何至今还在？最后一段解释由于月球上没有空气、大风和降雨，那里没有能改变地表的天气。正因为没有风雨侵蚀，脚印保留至今。选项B准确重述了这个科学事实。A胶水、C外星人保护、D钢铁表面均是胡编乱造。故选B。
