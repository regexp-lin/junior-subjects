# 阅读理解（议论文）

① In some schools, students are asked to rate (评分) their teachers — to give scores or feedback (反馈) after each term. Supporters say it helps improve teaching. Opponents say it is not fair or useful. Should students be allowed to rate their teachers? Let us look at both sides.

② Those who support student ratings argue that students sit in class every day. They know which teachers explain clearly, which classes are interesting, and which teachers care about their progress. This feedback can help teachers improve. If many students say a lesson is too fast or too boring, the teacher can try to change. Also, when teachers know that students will rate them, they may work harder to prepare better lessons. In this way, student ratings can raise the quality of teaching. Finally, giving feedback teaches students to think critically (批判性地) and express their views in a respectful (尊重的) way.

③ However, those who oppose student ratings have concerns. First, some students may give low scores to teachers who give them low marks or strict homework. The rating may reflect (反映) the student's mood, not the teacher's real ability. Second, young students may not have enough experience to judge (判断) teaching methods. They might prefer a teacher who gives less homework, but that does not mean the teacher is better. Third, if teachers worry too much about their ratings, they might try to please students instead of teaching what students need. For example, they might give easier exams or fewer assignments. That would harm real learning.

④ In my view, student feedback can be helpful, but it should be used wisely. Schools could ask students not only for scores, but also for specific (具体的) suggestions — for example, "The teacher speaks too quickly" or "More examples would help." This kind of feedback is more useful than a simple number. Ratings could be one part of teacher evaluation (评价), but not the only part. Principals and other teachers should also observe classes and give their views. In this way, we get a fuller picture.

⑤ The goal is to support teachers and help students learn better. When we use student feedback in the right way, everyone can benefit.

**注释：** rate 评分；feedback 反馈；critically 批判性地；respectful 尊重的；reflect 反映；judge 判断；specific 具体的；evaluation 评价

---

**Questions:**

1. According to Paragraph ②, why do supporters think student ratings are useful?
   A. Because students have nothing else to do.
   B. Because students know which teachers explain clearly and care about progress; feedback can help teachers improve and raise teaching quality.
   C. Because ratings are always correct.
   D. Because teachers like low scores.

2. What concern do opponents have about student ratings?
   A. Ratings take too long to complete.
   B. Students may give low scores to strict teachers out of mood; young students may not judge teaching well; teachers might please students instead of teaching properly.
   C. Ratings cost too much money.
   D. Parents do not like ratings.

3. What does the writer suggest about how to use student feedback?
   A. Use only scores and ignore suggestions.
   B. Ask for specific suggestions, not just scores; use ratings as one part of evaluation, together with observations by principals and other teachers.
   C. Let students decide teacher salaries.
   D. Never listen to student feedback.

4. The word "benefit" in Paragraph ⑤ ("everyone can benefit") probably means _____.
   A. lose
   B. gain or profit from
   C. forget
   D. complain

5. What is the writer's main opinion?
   A. Student ratings should be banned.
   B. Student feedback can be helpful when used wisely; it should be combined with other forms of evaluation.
   C. Only students should evaluate teachers.
   D. Teachers should never be evaluated.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "They know which teachers explain clearly... This feedback can help teachers improve... student ratings can raise the quality of teaching"

**【解析】** 支持者认为学生最了解课堂，反馈有助于教师改进、提升教学质量。A、C、D 项非主要理由。故选 B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "some students may give low scores to teachers who give them low marks or strict homework... young students may not have enough experience to judge... teachers might try to please students instead of teaching what students need"

**【解析】** 反对者担心评分受情绪影响、学生判断力不足、教师为讨好学生而降低要求。A、C、D 项未提及。故选 B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第④段 "ask students not only for scores, but also for specific suggestions... Ratings could be one part of teacher evaluation... Principals and other teachers should also observe classes"

**【解析】** 作者建议收集具体建议、将评分作为评价之一、结合校长和同行观察。A、C、D 项与建议矛盾。故选 B。

**4. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 词义猜测

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "When we use student feedback in the right way, everyone can benefit" — 正确使用反馈的积极结果

**【解析】** 在正确使用反馈时，大家都能从中获益。benefit 意为"获益、得益"。A、C、D 项含义相反。故选 B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 主旨大意

**【定位】** 第④、⑤段 "student feedback can be helpful, but it should be used wisely... combined with other evaluation... everyone can benefit"

**【解析】** 作者认为反馈有价值，但须审慎使用、与其他评价相结合。A、C、D 项与作者立场不符。故选 B。
