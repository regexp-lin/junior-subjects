# 阅读理解（议论文）

① Should students take part in school management and decision-making? This question has become popular in many schools. Some schools have started to let students vote on certain rules or join student councils (学生会). But is this a good idea? Let's think about it.

② Those who support student participation believe it has many advantages. First, students are the main users of the school. They know best what they need — for example, better lunch choices, more club activities, or changes to the library opening hours. Their ideas can help make the school a better place. Second, when students take part in decisions, they learn to be responsible (负责任的) and think carefully. This prepares them for future life. Third, it can make students feel valued. When their voices are heard, they may care more about the school and follow rules more willingly (心甘情愿地).

③ However, some people hold the opposite view. They worry that students are too young to make important choices. They may not understand the full results of their decisions. Students might vote for shorter school days without realizing this could affect their exam results. Besides, schools need experienced adults to manage complex (复杂的) issues such as safety and money. Too much student control might lead to confusion (混乱). Also, some students might not take it seriously and could be influenced by their friends instead of thinking for themselves.

④ In my opinion, the answer is not "all or nothing." Students can take part in some areas — like choosing lunch menus, planning school activities, or suggesting improvements to the campus. But serious matters, such as safety rules or how money is used, should still be decided by teachers and school leaders. In this way, students can learn and grow, while the school stays well-managed.

⑤ In short, letting students join in some decisions is a good step. It builds their confidence and teaches them how to work with others. But it needs clear rules and good guidance. Only in this way can we make school a place where both learning and democracy (民主) can grow.

**注释：** council 委员会；responsible 负责任的；willingly 心甘情愿地；complex 复杂的；confusion 混乱；democracy 民主

### 选择题

1. According to paragraph ②, why do supporters think students should join in school decisions?
   A. Because students know their own needs and can help improve the school.
   B. Because students have more free time than teachers.
   C. Because it can reduce the teachers' work.
   D. Because all students want to be school leaders.

2. What do those against student participation worry about?
   A. Students are smarter than teachers.
   B. Teachers will lose their jobs.
   C. Schools will have too much money.
   D. Students are too young and may not understand the results of their choices.

3. What does the writer think students could take part in?
   A. Choosing lunch menus and planning school activities.
   B. Deciding safety rules and how money is used.
   C. Hiring new teachers.
   D. Setting exam dates and results.

4. What does the word "valued" in paragraph ② most probably mean?
   A. Expensive.
   B. Old.
   C. Boring.
   D. Important and respected.

5. What is the writer's main opinion?
   A. Students should control everything at school.
   B. Students should never take part in any decisions.
   C. Students can join in some decisions, but serious matters need adult management.
   D. Schools should close down and let students study at home.

---

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "Students are the main users of the school. They know best what they need... Their ideas can help make the school a better place."

**【解析】** 支持者认为学生是学校主要使用者，最了解自身需求，其建议有助于改善学校。B 项"学生比老师有更多空闲"、C 项"减轻老师工作量"、D 项"所有学生都想当领导"均非支持理由。故选 A。

**2. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "They worry that students are too young to make important choices. They may not understand the full results of their decisions."

**【解析】** 反对者担忧学生年龄太小，无法理解自身决定可能带来的全部后果。A 项"学生比老师聪明"、B 项"老师失业"、C 项"学校钱太多"均未在文中作为反对理由出现。故选 D。

**3. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "Students can take part in some areas — like choosing lunch menus, planning school activities, or suggesting improvements to the campus."

**【解析】** 作者主张学生可参与的部分包括选择午餐菜单、策划学校活动、对校园改进提建议等。B 项"决定安全规则和资金使用"、C 项"聘请新教师"、D 项"设定考试时间和成绩"均为应由成人负责的严肃事项。故选 A。

**4. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 词义猜测

**【定位】** 第②段 "When their voices are heard, they may care more about the school... feel valued"

**【解析】** 语境中学生声音被倾听时会"感到 valued"，即感到自己的意见重要、被尊重。A 项"昂贵的"、B 项"年老的"、C 项"无聊的"均不符语境。故选 D。

**5. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 主旨大意

**【定位】** 第④段 "In my opinion, the answer is not 'all or nothing.' Students can take part in some areas... But serious matters... should still be decided by teachers and school leaders."

**【解析】** 作者观点是折中：学生可参与部分决策，但诸如安全、经费等严肃事务仍应由教师和校方决定。A 项"学生应控制一切"、B 项"学生完全不能参与"、D 项"学校关闭在家学习"均与作者立场矛盾。故选 C。
