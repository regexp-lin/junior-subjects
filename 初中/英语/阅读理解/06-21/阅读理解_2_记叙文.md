# 阅读理解（记叙文）

① **Learning to Be Independent on a School Camp**

② Last summer, my class went on a three-day camping trip to the countryside. I had never slept away from home before, and I was both excited and nervous. My mother packed everything for me — clothes, a toothbrush, and even a small bag of snacks. "Don't worry," she said. "You will be fine."

③ On the first night, I realized I had forgotten to bring a flashlight. It was dark outside, and I could not find my way to the toilet. I had to ask a classmate for help. I felt a little embarrassed. That night, I promised myself I would try to do things on my own. The next morning, I woke up early and folded my sleeping bag by myself. I also helped our teacher cook breakfast over the fire. It was hard at first, but I felt proud when I finished.

④ By the third day, I had learned to pack my own bag, tie my shoes properly, and even help other students who were having trouble. When we returned home, my mother was surprised. "You look different," she said. "You seem more confident." I told her about the flashlight, the breakfast, and the new friends I had made. She smiled and said, "Sometimes we learn more outside the classroom than inside it."

⑤ That camping trip taught me an important lesson: being independent does not mean you never need help. It means you try your best first, and when you really cannot do something alone, you are not afraid to ask. I still keep in touch with the friends I made on that trip, and we often talk about the fun we had and the things we learned together.

**注释：** flashlight 手电筒；fold 折叠

---

### 选择题

1. How did the writer feel before the camping trip?
   A. Both excited and nervous.
   B. Only excited.
   C. Only nervous.
   D. Bored and tired.

2. What happened on the first night?
   A. The writer got lost in the forest.
   B. The writer forgot a flashlight and had to ask a classmate for help.
   C. The writer did not sleep at all.
   D. The writer went home early.

3. What did the writer do on the second morning to become more independent?
   A. Slept longer than others.
   B. Called his or her mother for help.
   C. Folded the sleeping bag alone and helped cook breakfast.
   D. Stayed in the tent all day.

4. What can we infer from the mother's words "You seem more confident"?
   A. The writer looked taller after the trip.
   B. The writer had gained weight.
   C. The writer had bought new clothes.
   D. The camping trip had a positive effect on the writer's growth.

5. What does the writer think "being independent" means?
   A. Trying your best first, and not being afraid to ask when you really need help.
   B. Never asking anyone for help.
   C. Doing everything perfectly the first time.
   D. Always living away from home.

---

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "I was both excited and nervous."

**【解析】** 原文直接说明作者在露营前既兴奋又紧张。A 项正确。B 项仅兴奋、C 项仅紧张、D 项无聊疲惫均与原文不符。故选 A。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "On the first night, I realized I had forgotten to bring a flashlight. It was dark outside... I had to ask a classmate for help."

**【解析】** 第一晚作者忘带手电筒，室外很黑，不得不向同学求助。B 项正确。A 项在森林迷路、C 项整夜未眠、D 项提早回家原文均未提及。故选 B。

**3. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "The next morning, I woke up early and folded my sleeping bag by myself. I also helped our teacher cook breakfast over the fire."

**【解析】** 第二天早上作者早起自己叠睡袋，并帮老师生火做早饭。C 项正确。A 项睡得久、B 项打电话给妈妈、D 项整天待在帐篷与原文矛盾。故选 C。

**4. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第④段 "When we returned home, my mother was surprised. 'You look different,' she said. 'You seem more confident.'"

**【解析】** 母亲注意到作者更有自信，说明露营经历对作者的成长有积极作用。D 项正确。A 项变高、B 项增重、C 项买新衣服均非母亲话中之意。故选 D。

**5. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 主旨理解 / 作者观点

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "being independent does not mean you never need help. It means you try your best first, and when you really cannot do something alone, you are not afraid to ask."

**【解析】** 作者认为独立是先尽力尝试，真正无法独自完成时不怕求助。A 项准确概括。B 项永不求助、C 项首次就完美、D 项总离家住均与作者观点不符。故选 A。
