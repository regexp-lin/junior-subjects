# 阅读理解（议论文）

**Teacher-Led Lessons vs Student Discussion in Class**

① How should a good class be run? Some say the teacher should teach and students should listen. Others say students should discuss and the teacher should guide. Let us look at both sides.

② Supporters of teacher-led lessons argue that the teacher has more knowledge. A clear lecture helps students learn the basics quickly. There is no wasted time. Everyone follows the same pace. For difficult subjects like maths or science, a good explanation from the teacher is very important. Students can ask questions, but the teacher controls the flow. This works well when there is a lot to cover in limited time.

③ Supporters of student discussion say that when students talk and think together, they understand better. They remember what they discover themselves. Discussion also develops critical thinking and teamwork. Students become more active and confident. They learn to express their ideas and listen to others. The teacher's role is to ask good questions and guide, not to do all the talking.

④ However, each way has its limits. Teacher-led lessons can be boring. Some students may not pay attention. Pure discussion can be slow. Some students may not join in. If the topic is very new or hard, too much discussion without explanation can confuse students.

⑤ In my view, the best approach depends on the subject and the goal. For new or difficult content, teacher explanation is essential. For review, debate, or projects, student discussion is valuable. A good class often mixes both: the teacher explains first, then students discuss and apply. Balance is the key.

**注释：** lecture 讲座；critical thinking 批判性思维

**Questions:**

1. According to Paragraph ②, why do some people prefer teacher-led lessons?
   A. Students talk too much.
   B. The teacher has more knowledge; a clear lecture helps students learn basics quickly.
   C. Discussion is always better.
   D. Classes should be silent.

2. What advantage of student discussion is mentioned in Paragraph ③?
   A. It saves the teacher's energy.
   B. It develops critical thinking and teamwork; students remember what they discover.
   C. It makes classes shorter.
   D. It requires no preparation.

3. What problem can pure discussion cause?
   A. Students become too quiet.
   B. It can be slow; for hard topics, it may confuse students without explanation.
   C. Teachers work too hard.
   D. No one learns anything.

4. What does the writer suggest in the last paragraph?
   A. Use only teacher-led lessons.
   B. Use only student discussion.
   C. Mix both: explain first, then discuss and apply; balance is key.
   D. Let students teach the class.

5. What does "essential" mean in Paragraph ⑤?
   A. Useless.
   B. Very important or necessary.
   C. Optional.
   D. Boring.

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第②段 "the teacher has more knowledge. A clear lecture helps students learn the basics quickly"
**【解析】** 支持者认为教师更有知识，清晰的讲授有助于学生快速掌握基础。B项准确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第③段 "develops critical thinking and teamwork... They remember what they discover themselves"
**【解析】** 讨论能培养批判性思维和团队协作，学生对自己发现的内容记忆更牢。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第④段 "Pure discussion can be slow... too much discussion without explanation can confuse students"
**【解析】** 纯讨论可能进度慢，对难点缺乏讲解可能造成困惑。B项准确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**4. 【答案】C**
**【题型】** 推理判断
**【定位】** 第⑤段 "the best approach depends on the subject and the goal... A good class often mixes both... Balance is the key"
**【解析】** 作者建议根据学科和目标混合使用两种方式，先讲解再讨论应用，关键在于平衡。C项正确。A、B、D项与作者观点不符。故选C。

**5. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 词义猜测
**【定位】** 第⑤段 "For new or difficult content, teacher explanation is essential"
**【解析】** 上下文指对新内容或难点，教师讲解不可或缺。essential 意为"非常重要的、必要的"。B项准确。A、C、D项与文意不符。故选B。
