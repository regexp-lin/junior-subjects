# 阅读理解（议论文）

① **Should City Children Experience Life in the Countryside?**

② In recent years, more and more parents and teachers have asked: should city children go to the countryside to experience life? Some schools organize trips to villages so that students can see how farmers work, live with local families, and try activities like planting and feeding animals. This topic has caused a lot of discussion.

③ **Those who support the idea** believe that city children live in a very different world. They spend most of their time indoors, using computers and phones. Many of them have never seen where their food comes from. By going to the countryside, they can learn to appreciate (欣赏) nature, understand the hard work of farmers, and develop a sense of responsibility. They may also learn practical skills like cooking with fresh vegetables and taking care of animals. Supporters say these experiences can make children more independent and help them build good values.

④ **Those who disagree** argue that such trips can be expensive and not all families can afford them. Some parents worry about safety—what if children get hurt or feel uncomfortable in a new place? Besides, a short trip of a few days may not really change a child's habits. Critics (批评者) also point out that some "rural experience" programs are more like tourism than real learning. They suggest that schools could teach about farming and nature in the classroom instead, which would be cheaper and safer.

⑤ **My view:** I think city children can benefit from spending some time in the countryside, but it should be done properly. Schools and parents need to choose safe and meaningful programs. The focus should be on learning and growth, not just having fun. Whether through a school trip or a family visit, experiencing life beyond the city can open children's eyes and help them understand the world better.

**注释：** appreciate 欣赏；critic 批评者

---

### 选择题

1. According to supporters, what can city children learn from going to the countryside?
   A. To appreciate nature, understand farmers' hard work, develop responsibility, and learn practical skills like cooking and caring for animals.
   B. Only how to use computers better.
   C. How to make more money.
   D. Nothing useful at all.

2. What do those who disagree say about the rural experience trips?
   A. All families can easily afford them.
   B. The trips are always completely safe.
   C. Children always learn a lot from every trip.
   D. They can be expensive, raise safety concerns, and short trips may not really change habits; some programs are more like tourism.

3. What do critics suggest as an alternative?
   A. Letting children stay at home all the time.
   B. Teaching about farming and nature in the classroom, which would be cheaper and safer.
   C. Sending children abroad instead.
   D. Stopping all school trips.

4. What is the writer's view on the topic?
   A. City children should never go to the countryside.
   B. All rural experience programs are meaningless.
   C. Children can benefit from countryside experiences if programs are safe and meaningful, with a focus on learning and growth.
   D. Only rich families should take such trips.

5. What can we infer from the passage?
   A. The writer thinks short trips can never have any effect on children.
   B. The writer believes experiencing life beyond the city can help children understand the world better.
   C. All schools have already stopped organizing such trips.
   D. The writer thinks classroom learning is useless.

---

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "they can learn to appreciate nature, understand the hard work of farmers, and develop a sense of responsibility... learn practical skills like cooking with fresh vegetables and taking care of animals"

**【解析】** 支持者认为农村体验可使孩子欣赏自然、理解农民辛劳、培养责任感，并学到烹饪、照料动物等实用技能。A项全面概括。B、C、D项均与原文观点不符。故选A。

**2. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "such trips can be expensive and not all families can afford them... Some parents worry about safety... a short trip of a few days may not really change a child's habits... some 'rural experience' programs are more like tourism than real learning"

**【解析】** 反对者指出：此类活动可能昂贵、并非所有家庭负担得起；家长担忧安全；几天的短期旅行未必改变习惯；部分项目更像旅游而非真正学习。D项准确概括。A、B、C项均与反对者观点矛盾。故选D。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "They suggest that schools could teach about farming and nature in the classroom instead, which would be cheaper and safer"

**【解析】** 批评者建议学校在教室里教授农业和自然知识，这样更便宜、更安全。B项正确。A项一直待在家、C项出国、D项取消所有旅行均与原文不符。故选B。

**4. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 推理判断/主旨

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "I think city children can benefit from spending some time in the countryside, but it should be done properly... choose safe and meaningful programs. The focus should be on learning and growth"

**【解析】** 作者认为城市孩子可从农村体验中受益，但需选择安全、有意义的项目，重点在于学习与成长。C项准确概括作者观点。A、B项与作者支持态度矛盾；D项原文未提及。故选C。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "experiencing life beyond the city can open children's eyes and help them understand the world better"

**【解析】** 作者指出体验城市之外的生活能开阔孩子眼界、帮助他们更好地理解世界。可推知作者相信此类经历对孩子的认知有积极影响。B项正确。A项与"can benefit"矛盾；C、D项均无依据。故选B。
