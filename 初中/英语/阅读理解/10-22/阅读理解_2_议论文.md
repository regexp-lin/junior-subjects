# 阅读理解（议论文）

**Should Schools Offer Labour Classes?**

① In China, labour education has become more important. Many schools now have labour classes where students learn to cook, clean, grow plants, or repair things. But some parents ask: "Is this really necessary? Shouldn't children focus on their studies?" Let us look at both sides.

② Supporters say labour classes teach practical skills. Children learn how to take care of themselves. They understand that housework and cooking are part of life. Some students never sweep the floor or wash a dish at home. Labour classes help them become more independent. Besides, doing hands-on work can make learning fun. Studies show that students who do practical activities often perform better in other subjects too.

③ However, some people worry about the time. Students already have much homework and many exams. Adding labour classes may increase their burden. Parents also ask: "Who will teach these classes? Are the teachers trained?" Some schools do not have enough space or equipment for cooking or gardening. Without proper support, labour classes may become a formality rather than real learning.

④ In my view, labour education is valuable, but it needs to be done well. Schools should not treat it as extra homework. The classes should be interesting and useful. Parents can help at home too — let children do small chores like setting the table or folding clothes. Skills learned young often stay for life.

⑤ Whether at school or at home, learning to work with our hands is important. It prepares us for adult life and helps us value the work others do. The goal is not to turn every student into a chef, but to help them become capable and responsible people.

**注释：** formality 形式；chore 家务活

**Questions:**

1. According to Paragraph ②, what can labour classes help students become?
   A. Richer.
   B. More independent and practical.
   C. Better at exams only.
   D. Too tired to study.

2. Which is a concern mentioned in Paragraph ③?
   A. Labour classes are too easy.
   B. Students already have much homework; adding labour classes may increase their burden.
   C. All parents support labour classes.
   D. There is too much equipment.

3. What does the writer suggest parents do?
   A. Do all the housework themselves.
   B. Let children do small chores at home like setting the table or folding clothes.
   C. Never let children help at home.
   D. Only focus on exam results.

4. What is the goal of labour education according to the passage?
   A. To turn every student into a chef.
   B. To help students become capable and responsible people.
   C. To replace all other subjects.
   D. To make students work in factories.

5. What can we infer from the last paragraph?
   A. Labour education is useless.
   B. Labour skills prepare us for life and help us value others' work; it is about being capable and responsible.
   C. Schools should cancel all labour classes.
   D. Only cooking matters.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第②段 "Labour classes help them become more independent" 及 "teach practical skills"
**【解析】** 劳动课教授实用技能，帮助学生更独立。B项准确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第③段 "Students already have much homework and many exams. Adding labour classes may increase their burden"
**【解析】** 学生课业已重，增设劳动课可能加重负担。B项正确。A、C、D项与第三段担忧不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第④段 "Parents can help at home too — let children do small chores like setting the table or folding clothes"
**【解析】** 作者建议家长让子女在家做些小家务，如摆碗筷、叠衣服。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第⑤段 "The goal is not to turn every student into a chef, but to help them become capable and responsible people"
**【解析】** 劳动教育的目标是培养有能力、有责任感的人。B项准确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 推理判断
