# 阅读理解（说明文）

① The Silk Road was one of the most important trade routes (贸易路线) in history. It connected China with countries in Central Asia, the Middle East, and Europe. For over 1,500 years, goods, ideas, and cultures moved along this road. Today, the Silk Road still reminds us of the ancient link between East and West.

② The Silk Road was not a single road. It was a network (网络) of paths that crossed deserts, mountains, and grasslands. Traders traveled on foot or by camel. The journey was long and dangerous. They faced extreme heat, cold, sandstorms, and sometimes robbers. Yet many people risked their lives because the trade was very profitable (有利可图的). Silk from China was highly valued in Rome. Glass, gems (宝石), and spices from the West were popular in China.

③ Silk was China's most famous export. For a long time, only China knew how to make silk. The secret was closely guarded. Silk was light, soft, and beautiful. Rulers and rich people in other lands paid high prices for it. In return, China received horses, wool, gold, and other goods. Besides silk, Chinese merchants also sold tea, paper, and porcelain (瓷器) to the West.

④ The Silk Road was not only about goods. Ideas and technologies also traveled. Buddhism came to China from India through the Silk Road. Paper-making and gunpowder (火药) spread from China to other parts of the world. Different cultures met, exchanged knowledge, and learned from each other.

⑤ Today, the name "Silk Road" is still used. China's Belt and Road Initiative (倡议) aims to build new connections between countries, inspired by the spirit of the ancient Silk Road. The old road taught us that trade and cooperation can bring people together.

**注释：** route 路线；network 网络；profitable 有利可图的；gem 宝石；porcelain 瓷器；gunpowder 火药

---

**Questions:**

1. What was the Silk Road?
   A. A road made of silk.
   B. A network of trade paths connecting China with Central Asia, the Middle East, and Europe.
   C. A road only in China.
   D. A road built in modern times.

2. Why was the journey along the Silk Road dangerous?
   A. There were too many people.
   B. Traders faced extreme heat, cold, sandstorms, and sometimes robbers.
   C. The road was too short.
   D. No one wanted to trade.

3. Why was silk so valuable in ancient times?
   A. Because it was heavy.
   B. Because only China knew how to make it, and it was light, soft, and beautiful.
   C. Because it was cheap.
   D. Because everyone could make it.

4. Besides goods, what else traveled along the Silk Road?
   A. Only silk.
   B. Ideas and technologies, such as Buddhism, paper-making, and gunpowder.
   C. Nothing else.
   D. Only food.

5. What can we learn from the last paragraph?
   A. The Silk Road no longer exists.
   B. The spirit of the ancient Silk Road — trade and cooperation bringing people together — still inspires us today.
   C. China never traded with other countries.
   D. The Silk Road was only for goods.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "It connected China with countries in Central Asia, the Middle East, and Europe" 及第②段 "It was a network of paths"

**【解析】** 丝绸之路是连接中国与中亚、中东、欧洲的贸易路线网络。A、C、D 项与原文不符。故选 B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "They faced extreme heat, cold, sandstorms, and sometimes robbers"

**【解析】** 商队面临酷暑、严寒、沙尘暴，有时还有强盗。A、C、D 项与原文不符。故选 B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "only China knew how to make silk... Silk was light, soft, and beautiful. Rulers and rich people in other lands paid high prices for it"

**【解析】** 丝绸因中国独有、质轻柔软美观而价值高昂。A、C、D 项与原文矛盾。故选 B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "Ideas and technologies also traveled. Buddhism came to China from India... Paper-making and gunpowder spread from China"

**【解析】** 除了货物，佛教、造纸术、火药等思想与技术也沿丝绸之路传播。A、C、D 项与原文不符。故选 B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "China's Belt and Road Initiative... inspired by the spirit of the ancient Silk Road... trade and cooperation can bring people together"

**【解析】** 古代丝绸之路的精神——贸易与合作将人们联结——至今仍激励着当代倡议。A、C、D 项与段落含义不符。故选 B。
