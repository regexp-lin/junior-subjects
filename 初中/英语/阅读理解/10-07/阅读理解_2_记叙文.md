> **10-07 第2篇**

# 阅读理解（记叙文）

① When I was ten years old, I spent a summer at my grandparents' house in the countryside. My grandfather was a quiet man who loved Chinese chess. He had a worn wooden board and a set of pieces that he kept in a wooden box. One day, he asked me, "Would you like to learn?" I had never played Chinese chess before. I said yes, not knowing that it would become one of my favourite hobbies.

② At first, Grandfather did not teach me many rules. He simply said, "Let's play. You will learn as we go." I made many mistakes. I moved pieces in wrong ways. I did not understand why some moves were good and others were bad. But Grandfather never got angry. He would smile and say, "Think again. What will happen if I move here?" He pointed to the board and helped me see the consequences of each move. Slowly, I began to understand.

③ The best part of our lessons was not the chess itself, but the time we spent together. We usually played in the late afternoon, under a big tree in the yard. Grandmother would bring us tea and snacks. Sometimes we did not speak for a long time — we just looked at the board and thought. Grandfather taught me that good chess is about patience. "Don't rush," he said. "Take your time. Think before you move."

④ By the end of the summer, I could play a full game without too many silly mistakes. I still lost most of the time, but I enjoyed every game. On my last day there, Grandfather gave me a small chess set. "Practice at home," he said. "When you come back next year, we will play again." I promised I would.

⑤ That was five years ago. I still play Chinese chess. Every time I sit down at the board, I remember my grandfather's smile and his gentle voice: "Think before you move." He did not just teach me a game — he taught me to be patient, to plan ahead, and to learn from my mistakes. I am grateful for that summer.

**注释：** worn 用旧的；consequence 后果；patience 耐心

---

**Questions:**

1. Where did the writer learn Chinese chess?
   A. At school.
   B. At his/her grandparents' house in the countryside.
   C. In a chess club.
   D. On the Internet.

2. How did Grandfather teach the writer to play?
   A. By giving a long lecture first.
   B. By playing together and explaining as they went; he helped the writer see the consequences of moves.
   C. By asking the writer to read a book.
   D. By getting angry when the writer made mistakes.

3. What did Grandfather say about good chess?
   A. You must always win.
   B. Good chess is about patience — take your time and think before you move.
   C. You should move as fast as possible.
   D. Only adults can play well.

4. What did Grandfather give the writer at the end of the summer?
   A. A new bicycle.
   B. A book about chess.
   C. A small chess set to practice at home.
   D. Nothing.

5. What did the writer learn from Grandfather besides chess?
   A. How to cook.
   B. To be patient, plan ahead, and learn from mistakes.
   C. How to grow vegetables.
   D. How to drive a car.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "I spent a summer at my grandparents' house in the countryside"

**【解析】** 作者在外公外婆的乡间家中度过暑假并学棋。B项正确。A、C、D项与地点不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "He simply said, 'Let's play. You will learn as we go.'... He would smile and say, 'Think again'... He pointed to the board and helped me see the consequences of each move"

**【解析】** 外公边下边教，通过提问和指盘帮助作者理解每步的后果。B项准确。A、C、D项与教学方式不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "Grandfather taught me that good chess is about patience. 'Don't rush,' he said. 'Take your time. Think before you move.'"

**【解析】** 外公强调下棋需耐心，不要急，想好再走。B项正确。A、C、D项与教导不符。故选B。

**4. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "Grandfather gave me a small chess set. 'Practice at home,' he said"

**【解析】** 外公送了一副小棋，让作者回家练习。C项正确。A、B、D项与礼物不符。故选C。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "He did not just teach me a game — he taught me to be patient, to plan ahead, and to learn from my mistakes"

**【解析】** 外公不仅教棋，更教作者耐心、提前规划和从错误中学习。B项正确。A、C、D项与所得教益不符。故选B。
