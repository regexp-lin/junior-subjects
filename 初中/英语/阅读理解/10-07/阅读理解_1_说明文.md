# 阅读理解（说明文）

① Deserts cover about one third of the Earth's land. They are dry, hot (or cold) places where very little rain falls. Have you ever wondered how deserts are formed? The answer involves climate, geography, and time.

② The main reason deserts form is lack of rain. When an area gets very little rainfall — usually less than 250 mm per year — plants find it hard to grow. Without plants, the soil is not held together. The wind can easily blow the soil away. Over thousands of years, the land becomes bare and turns into desert. Some deserts form in the middle of continents, far from the ocean. Moisture (水分) from the sea rarely reaches these places, so they stay dry. Examples include the Gobi Desert in Asia and parts of central Australia.

③ Mountains can also create deserts. When moist air from the ocean rises over a mountain range, it cools down and drops its rain on one side of the mountain. This side is green and wet. But the air that passes over to the other side has lost most of its moisture. That side gets very little rain and may become a desert. This is called a "rain shadow." The Atacama Desert in South America is one of the driest places on Earth because of the Andes Mountains blocking rain.

④ Human activities can also turn land into desert. This process is called desertification (沙漠化). When people cut down too many trees, overgraze (过度放牧) livestock, or use too much water for farming, the soil loses its ability to hold water. Plants die, and the land becomes dry and barren. This is happening in some parts of Africa and China. Governments and organisations are working to plant trees and protect the land to slow down desertification.

⑤ Deserts are not empty wastelands. Many plants and animals have adapted to life there. Cacti store water in their stems. Camels can go for days without water. Understanding how deserts form helps us protect the environment and use land wisely.

**注释：** moisture 水分；desertification 沙漠化；overgraze 过度放牧；barren 贫瘠的；cacti 仙人掌（复数）

**Questions:**

1. What is the main reason deserts form?
   A. Too much rain.
   B. Lack of rain — usually less than 250 mm per year.
   C. Too many plants.
   D. The ocean is too close.

2. Why do some deserts form in the middle of continents?
   A. Because the ocean is there.
   B. Because moisture from the sea rarely reaches these places, so they stay dry.
   C. Because it is always cold there.
   D. Because people live there.

3. What is a "rain shadow"?
   A. A place that gets a lot of rain.
   B. The wet side of a mountain.
   C. The dry side of a mountain where little rain falls because the air has lost its moisture.
   D. A type of cloud.

4. What can cause desertification?
   A. Planting many trees.
   B. Cutting down too many trees, overgrazing, or using too much water for farming.
   C. Building schools.
   D. Keeping the land wet.

5. What can we infer from the last paragraph?
   A. Deserts have no life at all.
   B. Deserts have plants and animals that have adapted to dry conditions; they are not empty wastelands.
   C. All plants need a lot of water.
   D. Camels cannot live in deserts.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "The main reason deserts form is lack of rain. When an area gets very little rainfall — usually less than 250 mm per year"

**【解析】** 沙漠形成的主要原因是缺雨，年降雨量通常少于250毫米。B项准确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "far from the ocean. Moisture from the sea rarely reaches these places, so they stay dry"

**【解析】** 大陆内部离海远，海洋水汽难以到达，因此干旱。B项正确。A、C、D项与原因不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "the air that passes over to the other side has lost most of its moisture. That side gets very little rain... This is called a 'rain shadow'"

**【解析】** 雨影指山脉背风侧，空气失去水分后降雨稀少的一侧。C项正确。A、B、D项与定义不符。故选C。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "When people cut down too many trees, overgraze livestock, or use too much water for farming, the soil loses its ability to hold water"

**【解析】** 沙漠化可由滥伐、过度放牧、农业过度用水等引起。B项正确。A、C、D项与原因相反。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "Deserts are not empty wastelands. Many plants and animals have adapted to life there"

**【解析】** 可推知沙漠并非无生命，许多动植物已适应干旱环境。B项正确。A、C、D项与文意不符。故选B。
