# 阅读理解（说明文）

**Chinese Tea Culture**

① China is the homeland of tea. For thousands of years, tea has been an important part of Chinese life. According to legend, Emperor Shen Nong discovered tea about 5,000 years ago when some tea leaves fell into his pot of boiling water. He tried the drink and found it refreshing. Since then, tea has become one of the most popular drinks in the world.

② There are many kinds of Chinese tea. The most common types are green tea, black tea, oolong tea, and white tea. Green tea is the most popular in China. It has a light, fresh taste and is said to be good for health. Black tea has a stronger flavour and is often enjoyed with milk in Western countries. Oolong tea is somewhere between green tea and black tea in taste.

③ In Chinese culture, tea is more than just a drink. It is a way to show respect. When guests visit, the host often serves tea. When young people want to show respect to their elders, they offer tea with both hands. Tea is also important at weddings. The bride and groom serve tea to their parents to express gratitude.

④ The Chinese tea ceremony is a beautiful tradition. It involves careful preparation: warming the teapot, measuring the right amount of tea leaves, pouring hot water at the correct temperature, and waiting for the right time before serving. Each step has a meaning. The ceremony teaches people to slow down, pay attention, and enjoy the present moment.

⑤ Today, tea remains deeply rooted in Chinese daily life. From a morning cup of green tea to an afternoon chat with friends over a pot of oolong, tea connects people and carries culture from generation to generation.

**注释：** legend 传说；refreshing 提神的；gratitude 感恩

**Questions:**

1. Who is said to have discovered tea according to the legend?
   A. Confucius.
   B. Emperor Shen Nong.
   C. A famous doctor.
   D. A tea farmer.

2. Which type of tea is the most popular in China?
   A. Black tea.
   B. Oolong tea.
   C. White tea.
   D. Green tea.

3. What do young people do to show respect to their elders with tea?
   A. They drink tea very quickly.
   B. They offer tea with both hands.
   C. They pour tea on the table.
   D. They add sugar to the tea.

4. What does the Chinese tea ceremony teach people?
   A. How to grow tea.
   B. How to sell tea.
   C. To slow down, pay attention, and enjoy the present moment.
   D. To drink tea as fast as possible.

5. What is the passage mainly about?
   A. How to plant tea trees.
   B. Chinese tea culture and its importance.
   C. The price of different teas.
   D. Why tea is unhealthy.

---

**Answers and Explanations:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "According to legend, Emperor Shen Nong discovered tea about 5,000 years ago"

**【解析】** 原文根据传说，是神农帝发现了茶。A项孔子、C项著名医生、D项茶农均非原文所述。故选B。

**2. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "Green tea is the most popular in China."

**【解析】** 原文明确说绿茶在中国最受欢迎。A项红茶在西方国家更常配牛奶喝；B项乌龙茶和C项白茶虽被提及但并非最受欢迎。故选D。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "When young people want to show respect to their elders, they offer tea with both hands."

**【解析】** 原文说年轻人用双手敬茶来表达对长辈的尊重。A项"喝茶很快"、C项"把茶倒在桌上"、D项"加糖"均与原文无关。故选B。

**4. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "The ceremony teaches people to slow down, pay attention, and enjoy the present moment."

**【解析】** 原文说茶道教人放慢脚步、专注当下、享受此刻。A项"如何种茶"和B项"如何卖茶"文中未涉及；D项"尽快喝茶"与茶道的慢节奏精神相反。故选C。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 主旨大意

**【定位】** 全文从茶的发现、种类、文化意义、茶道传统到当代生活中的茶，系统介绍了中国茶文化

**【解析】** 文章围绕中国茶文化及其重要性展开全面介绍。A项"如何种茶树"未涉及；C项"不同茶的价格"文中未提及；D项"为什么茶不健康"与文章正面基调相反。故选B。
