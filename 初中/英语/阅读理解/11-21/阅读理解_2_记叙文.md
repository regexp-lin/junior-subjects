> **11-21 第2篇**

# 阅读理解（记叙文）

**Learning to Ride a Bike**

① When I was eight years old, all my friends could ride a bike. I was the only one who could not. Every time they rode around the neighbourhood, I had to run behind them. I felt left out and embarrassed.

② One Saturday, my dad said, "It's time to learn." He took out an old blue bike from the garage and pushed it to the park near our house. I looked at it and felt scared. "What if I fall?" I asked. "You will fall," Dad said with a smile. "But you will also get up."

③ Dad held the back of the seat while I climbed on. I held the handlebars so tightly that my fingers turned white. He pushed me forward gently. "Keep pedalling! Don't look down — look ahead!" he shouted. For the first few tries, I wobbled and fell. My knees got scraped and dirty. I wanted to cry, but Dad kept saying, "One more try."

④ After about an hour, something magical happened. I was pedalling, and suddenly I realised Dad was no longer holding the seat. I was riding on my own! The wind blew through my hair. The world seemed to open up. I was so excited that I shouted, "I'm doing it! I'm doing it!" Then I forgot to brake and crashed into a bush. But I was laughing, not crying.

⑤ That day I learned two things: how to ride a bike, and how to face fear. Sometimes the hardest part is just getting on and trying. Now, whenever I face something difficult, I remember that old blue bike and my dad's voice: "One more try."

**注释：** wobble 摇晃；scrape 擦伤；brake 刹车

---

**Questions:**

1. Why did the writer feel embarrassed?
   A. He could not swim.
   B. He was the only one who could not ride a bike.
   C. He did not have a bike.
   D. His friends laughed at his clothes.

2. Where did the writer's dad take him to learn?
   A. To school.
   B. To the neighbourhood road.
   C. To the park near their house.
   D. To a sports centre.

3. What advice did the dad give while teaching?
   A. Look down at the wheels.
   B. Stop pedalling when scared.
   C. Keep pedalling and look ahead.
   D. Hold on to Dad's hand.

4. What happened when the writer realised he was riding alone?
   A. He stopped immediately.
   B. He fell off and cried.
   C. He shouted with excitement but then crashed into a bush.
   D. He rode home without stopping.

5. What is the deeper lesson the writer learned?
   A. Riding a bike is dangerous.
   B. Parents should not teach children to ride bikes.
   C. The hardest part of facing fear is just trying, and you should keep going.
   D. He should never ride a bike again.

---

**Answers and Explanations:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "I was the only one who could not. Every time they rode around the neighbourhood, I had to run behind them. I felt left out and embarrassed."

**【解析】** 原文说作者是唯一不会骑车的人，总是跑在后面，感到被排斥和尴尬。A项不会游泳、C项没有自行车、D项朋友嘲笑衣服均非原文所述。故选B。

**2. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "He took out an old blue bike from the garage and pushed it to the park near our house."

**【解析】** 原文说爸爸把自行车推到家附近的公园。A项学校、B项社区道路、D项运动中心均不正确。故选C。

**3. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "'Keep pedalling! Don't look down — look ahead!' he shouted."

**【解析】** 爸爸喊道要继续蹬、不要低头看、要看前方。A项"看轮子"与爸爸"别往下看"的建议相反；B项"害怕时停止蹬"也不对；D项"抓住爸爸的手"原文未说。故选C。

**4. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "I was so excited that I shouted, 'I'm doing it! I'm doing it!' Then I forgot to brake and crashed into a bush. But I was laughing, not crying."

**【解析】** 作者意识到自己在独自骑车时兴奋地大喊，然后忘记刹车撞进灌木丛，但他在笑。A项"立即停下"不对；B项"摔倒并哭了"不对，他在笑；D项"一路骑回家"不对。故选C。

**5. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "Sometimes the hardest part is just getting on and trying. Now, whenever I face something difficult, I remember that old blue bike and my dad's voice: 'One more try.'"

**【解析】** 作者领悟到面对恐惧最难的部分是迈出第一步去尝试，并且要坚持"再试一次"。A项"骑车危险"只看到表面；B项"父母不该教骑车"与文章积极主题矛盾；D项"再也不骑"与作者的成长感悟相反。故选C。
