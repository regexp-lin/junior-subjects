# 阅读理解（记叙文）

**My First Time Teaching Children to Draw as a Volunteer**

① Last summer, I joined a community volunteer programme. I had never taught children before, but I love drawing. So when I heard they needed someone to help kids learn to paint, I decided to try. I was both nervous and excited.

② On the first day, I arrived at the community centre. About fifteen children, aged six to ten, sat around tables with paper and brushes. They looked at me with curious eyes. "Good morning! Today we will draw our favourite animals," I said. At first, the children were quiet. Then a little girl raised her hand. "Can I draw a cat?" she asked. "Of course!" I said. Soon, everyone started to talk and share ideas.

③ I walked around and helped each child. One boy could not draw the eyes of his dog. I showed him how to make two small circles. His face lit up when he saw it work. A girl wanted to draw a rainbow fish. I gave her different colours and she mixed them happily. I realised that teaching was not about being perfect. It was about helping each child find their own way to express themselves.

④ By the end of the morning, the room was full of colourful pictures: cats, dogs, fish, butterflies, and even a pink elephant! The children held up their work and smiled. "Thank you, teacher!" they said. I felt a warm feeling inside. I had come to teach, but I learned something too. Patience and encouragement can help anyone, including myself, do things we thought we could not do.

⑤ That experience changed how I see volunteering. It is not just about giving. It is about sharing, learning, and growing together. I still visit the community centre when I have time. And every time I go, I remember that first day — the day I discovered that a little help can bring big smiles.

**注释：** community 社区；patience 耐心

---

**Questions:**

1. Why did the writer join the volunteer programme?
   A. Because she had to do it for school.
   B. Because she loves drawing and they needed someone to help kids learn to paint.
   C. Because her parents asked her to.
   D. Because she had nothing else to do.

2. What did the children draw on the first day?
   A. Only cats.
   B. Their favourite animals.
   C. Only dogs.
   D. Nothing.

3. How did the writer help the boy who could not draw the dog's eyes?
   A. By drawing the whole picture for him.
   B. By showing him how to make two small circles.
   C. By giving him a different task.
   D. By asking him to stop drawing.

4. What did the writer learn from this experience?
   A. Teaching is very easy.
   B. Patience and encouragement can help people do things they thought they could not do.
   C. Children never listen to teachers.
   D. Drawing is the most important skill.

5. What can we infer from the last paragraph?
   A. The writer will never volunteer again.
   B. The writer values volunteering and continues to help at the community centre.
   C. The writer only taught once.
   D. The children did not enjoy the activity.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第①段 "I love drawing... when I heard they needed someone to help kids learn to paint, I decided to try"
**【解析】** 作者喜欢画画，听说需要人教孩子绘画，于是决定参加。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第②段 "Today we will draw our favourite animals"
**【解析】** 当天孩子们画的是自己最喜欢的动物。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第③段 "I showed him how to make two small circles"
**【解析】** 作者教男孩画两个小圆作为眼睛。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 推理判断
**【定位】** 第④段 "Patience and encouragement can help anyone, including myself, do things we thought we could not do"
**【解析】** 作者体会到耐心和鼓励能帮助人们完成原本认为做不到的事。B项正确。A、C、D项与文意不符。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 推理判断
**【定位】** 第⑤段 "I still visit the community centre when I have time"
**【解析】** 作者仍会抽空去社区中心，说明她重视志愿工作并持续参与。B项正确。A、C、D项与文意相反。故选B。
