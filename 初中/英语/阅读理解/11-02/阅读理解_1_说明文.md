# 阅读理解（说明文）

**DNA and Genes: A Brief Introduction**

① Have you ever wondered why you look like your parents? Why do you have your mother's eyes or your father's smile? The answer lies in something called DNA. DNA is a special chemical inside every cell of your body. It carries the information that makes you who you are.

② DNA stands for deoxyribonucleic acid. It is like a long instruction manual. This manual tells your body how to grow, what colour your hair should be, how tall you might become, and even whether you are good at music or sports. Every person has a unique set of DNA. That is why no two people look exactly the same, except for identical twins who share the same DNA.

③ Genes are small parts of DNA. Each gene has a specific job. One gene might decide your eye colour. Another might affect your height. We inherit genes from our parents. You get half of your genes from your mother and half from your father. This is why children often have a mix of features from both parents.

④ Scientists have been studying DNA for many years. Understanding DNA helps us learn about diseases. Some illnesses are caused by gene problems. By studying these genes, doctors can find better ways to treat or prevent certain diseases. DNA can also be used to solve crimes. Police can compare DNA from a crime scene with DNA from a suspect to see if they match.

⑤ DNA is truly amazing. It is the code of life. From the colour of a flower to the pattern of a tiger's stripes, DNA shapes every living thing on Earth. The more we learn about it, the more we understand ourselves and the world around us.

**注释：** inherit 遗传；identical 完全相同的

**Questions:**

1. According to Paragraph ①, what does DNA do?
   A. It makes us eat food.
   B. It carries the information that makes you who you are.
   C. It helps us run fast.
   D. It changes our names.

2. Why do no two people look exactly the same (except identical twins)?
   A. Because everyone eats different food.
   B. Because every person has a unique set of DNA.
   C. Because we wear different clothes.
   D. Because we live in different places.

3. Where do we get our genes from?
   A. Only from our mother.
   B. Only from our father.
   C. Half from our mother and half from our father.
   D. From our teachers.

4. According to Paragraph ④, how can DNA be useful?
   A. It can cook food.
   B. It helps us understand diseases and can be used to solve crimes.
   C. It can change the weather.
   D. It makes us sleep better.

5. What can we infer from the passage?
   A. DNA is only found in humans.
   B. DNA affects many things about living things, from appearance to health.
   C. Genes have nothing to do with our parents.
   D. Scientists have stopped studying DNA.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第①段 "It carries the information that makes you who you are"
**【解析】** DNA携带决定个体特征的信息。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第②段 "Every person has a unique set of DNA. That is why no two people look exactly the same"
**【解析】** 每个人都有独特的DNA，因此外貌各不相同（同卵双胞胎除外）。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】C**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第③段 "We inherit genes from our parents. You get half of your genes from your mother and half from your father"
**【解析】** 基因来自父母，一半来自母亲，一半来自父亲。C项正确。A、B、D项与原文不符。故选C。

**4. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第④段 "Understanding DNA helps us learn about diseases... DNA can also be used to solve crimes"
**【解析】** DNA有助于了解疾病并可用于破案。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 推理判断
**【定位】** 全文，综合第②③⑤段
**【解析】** 文章指出DNA影响外貌、身高、疾病等，并塑造地球上所有生物。可推知DNA对生物多方面有影响。B项正确。A、C、D项与文意不符。故选B。
