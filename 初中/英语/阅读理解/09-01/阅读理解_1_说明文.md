# 阅读理解（说明文）

① The giant panda is one of the most beloved (喜爱的) animals in the world. It is a symbol of China and a symbol of wildlife conservation (保护). For many years, giant pandas were in danger. Their number dropped because of habitat (栖息地) loss and low birth rates. But thanks to years of hard work, the situation has improved. In 2016, the giant panda was moved from "endangered" (濒危的) to "vulnerable" (易危的) on the world's species list. This is good news, but the work to protect them is far from over.

② Where do giant pandas live? They are found mainly in the mountains of Sichuan, Shaanxi, and Gansu in China. These areas have cool, wet bamboo forests, which pandas need to survive. Giant pandas eat almost only bamboo. An adult panda can eat 12 to 38 kilograms of bamboo every day. Because bamboo does not have much nutrition (营养), pandas need to eat a lot and spend most of their time feeding.

③ Why were giant pandas in danger? First, their habitat was being destroyed. Forests were cut down to make room for farms and cities. Second, pandas reproduce (繁殖) slowly. A female panda usually has only one or two babies at a time, and baby pandas are very weak. Many did not survive in the wild. Third, in the past, some people hunted pandas for their fur, though this is now illegal and rarely happens.

④ What has China done to protect giant pandas? China has set up more than 60 panda reserves (保护区). In these reserves, pandas are protected from harm. Scientists study pandas and help sick or injured ones. There are also panda breeding (繁殖) centers where experts help pandas have more babies. The famous Chengdu Research Base is one of them. Millions of people visit it each year to learn about pandas. In addition, China has planted more bamboo to provide food for pandas in the wild.

⑤ The story of the giant panda shows that when humans work together to protect wildlife, we can make a difference. We should continue to care for these wonderful animals and their home.

**注释：** beloved 喜爱的；conservation 保护；habitat 栖息地；endangered 濒危的；vulnerable 易危的；nutrition 营养；reproduce 繁殖；reserve 保护区；breeding 繁殖

**Questions:**

1. When was the giant panda moved from "endangered" to "vulnerable"?
   A. In 2010.
   B. In 2016.
   C. In 2020.
   D. The passage does not say.

2. What do giant pandas mainly eat?
   A. Meat and fish.
   B. Fruit and vegetables.
   C. Bamboo.
   D. Rice and bread.

3. According to Paragraph ③, which is NOT a reason why pandas were in danger?
   A. Habitat loss.
   B. Low birth rate and weak babies.
   C. Pandas eating too much bamboo.
   D. Hunting in the past.

4. What has China done to protect giant pandas?
   A. Cut down more forests for pandas.
   B. Set up reserves and breeding centers, and plant more bamboo.
   C. Send all pandas to other countries.
   D. Stop people from visiting pandas.

5. What can we learn from the passage?
   A. Giant pandas are no longer in any danger.
   B. Human efforts can help protect wildlife.
   C. Pandas can live anywhere in the world.
   D. Bamboo has no nutrition at all.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "In 2016, the giant panda was moved from 'endangered' to 'vulnerable' on the world's species list."

**【解析】** 原文明确写出大熊猫于2016年从"濒危"降级为"易危"。A、C项为错误年份；D项与原文不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "Giant pandas eat almost only bamboo."

**【解析】** 原文明确说大熊猫几乎只吃竹子。A项"肉和鱼"、B项"水果蔬菜"和D项"米饭面包"均与原文不符。故选C。

**3. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段列举了 habitat destroyed、reproduce slowly、hunting

**【解析】** 文中列举的濒危原因包括栖息地被破坏、繁殖慢、历史上盗猎，未提及"吃太多竹子"是濒危原因。故选C。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "China has set up more than 60 panda reserves... There are also panda breeding centers... China has planted more bamboo"

**【解析】** 中国采取的措施包括建立保护区、繁殖中心和种植更多竹子。A项"砍伐更多森林"与保护相反；C、D项原文未提及。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "The story of the giant panda shows that when humans work together to protect wildlife, we can make a difference."

**【解析】** 文章通过大熊猫保护的例子说明人类共同努力可以保护野生动物。A项"不再有任何危险"过于绝对；C项"可在世界任何地方生活"与原文不符；D项"竹子完全没有营养"表述不准确。故选B。
