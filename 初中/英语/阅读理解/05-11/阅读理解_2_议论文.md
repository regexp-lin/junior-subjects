# 阅读理解（议论文）

① In today's highly competitive (竞争激烈的) society, many parents send their children to numerous extra tutoring classes (课外辅导班) right after school or on weekends. Some parents believe this is the only way to guarantee a successful future for their kids. However, I hold the firm view that too many extra classes are harmful to teenagers physically and mentally (身心地).

② To begin with, an overloaded schedule (超载的日程) robs children of their necessary rest. Middle school students already spend about eight hours a day in school. If they have to attend math, English, and piano classes after that, they often study until midnight. The lack of sleep not only makes them tired during the day but also slows down their brain growth and ruins their physical health. 

③ Furthermore, attending endless tutoring classes kills creativity (创造力) and passion. When children are constantly told what to do and what to learn, they have no time to explore their own interests. They might stop loving learning and start treating it as a heavy, painful task. Without free time to play, invent, or just daydream, we might gain students who score high on tests, but lose future artists, thinkers, and innovators (创新者).

④ Defenders of extra classes say that the school tests are too difficult and extra help is necessary. It is true that some students need help in certain weak subjects. But this help should be targeted (有针对性的) and moderate (适度的). Taking a class for a subject they already excel at, or learning things three years ahead of school, is simply unnecessary pressure.

⑤ To sum up, while a successful future is important, it should not come at the cost of a child's health and happiness. Parents need to create a balance. Let teenagers have the time to rest, play, and grow naturally, rather than turning them into studying machines.

**注释：** competitive 竞争激烈的；tutoring class 辅导班；mentally 心理地；overloaded 超载的；creativity 创造力；innovator 创新者；moderate 适度的；excel 突出/擅长

---

### 选择题

1. What is the writer's attitude (态度) towards taking too many extra tutoring classes?
A. Firmly supportive because it guarantees a successful future.
B. Strongly against it because it is harmful to teenagers physically and mentally.
C. Uncertain because the school tests are indeed very difficult.
D. Uncaring, as long as the teenagers can sleep until midnight.

2. How does an overloaded schedule affect students' brains and bodies according to paragraph ②?
A. It makes their brains grow faster and stronger.
B. It helps them perform better during the day at school.
C. The lack of sleep tires them, slows down brain growth, and ruins physical health.
D. It teaches them how to play the piano properly.

3. Why does the writer think endless tutoring classes kill creativity?
A. Because they don't teach students how to paint or draw pictures.
B. Because students have no free time to play, invent, or explore their own interests.
C. Because all the tutors are old and boring people.
D. Because the classes are held in dark and crowded rooms.

4. Form paragraph ④, what kind of extra help does the writer think is acceptable (可接受的)?
A. Learning things three years ahead of the normal school schedule.
B. Targeted and moderate help only for certain weak subjects.
C. Taking full-day classes every weekend for all subjects.
D. Hiring tutors to do the students' homework for them.

5. What does the writer suggest parents do in the conclusion?
A. Turn their children into perfect studying machines.
B. Ignore school tests completely and only let children play.
C. Create a balance and let teenagers have time to rest and grow naturally.
D. Send their children to more successful schools instead of extra classes.

---

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 作者观点初定/细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段最后一句 "However, I hold the firm view that too many extra classes are harmful to teenagers physically and mentally (身心地)."

**【解析】** 开头抛出有家长认为必须上很多辅导班的现象后，作者表达了其坚定观点：我认为过多的课外班对青少年的身心是有害的。即强烈反对（Strongly against it）。选项B精准提取了作者的立场。A强烈支持、C不确定和D漠不关心均为错误理解。故选B。

**2. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节推断理解

**【定位】** 第②段最后一句 "The lack of sleep not only makes them tired during the day but also slows down their brain growth and ruins their physical health."

**【解析】** 超负荷的日程表对脑力和身体有何影响？原文指出，不仅导致白天疲惫不缺觉，而且会减缓大脑发育并摧毁身体健康。这与C选项的内容一一对应。A大脑长得更快、B白天表现更好都是彻头彻尾的反向描述。故选C。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节推断理解

**【定位】** 第③段第2-4句 "When children are constantly told what to do... they have no time to explore their own interests... Without free time to play, invent, or just daydream..."

**【解析】** 作者认为无休止辅导课为何会扼杀创造力？因为如果总是被按着头学，孩子们就没有空余时间去探索自我兴趣、没人指引地玩耍、发明或是哪怕发发呆（no time to explore their own interests / Without free time to play, invent...）。B项准确解释了扼杀创造力的根源。A不教画画、C辅导员老且无聊、D教室黑又挤都是没影的事。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段第2-3句 "It is true that some students need help in certain weak subjects. But this help should be targeted (有针对性的) and moderate (适度的)."

**【解析】** 有人反驳说因考试难课外辅导有必要。作者认为何种程度的辅导是可以接受的呢？只有针对某些薄弱科目（certain weak subjects）进行有针对性且适度的（targeted and moderate）帮扶才是对的。选项B正是对这点的重构。提前学三年（A）、周末全科上课（C）被直接批评为不必要压力。故选B。

**5. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解/主旨归纳

**【定位】** 总结段第⑤段第2-3句 "Parents need to create a balance. Let teenagers have the time to rest, play, and grow naturally, rather than turning them into studying machines."

**【解析】** 最后一段作者给了父母什么建议？他建议父母需要创造一种平衡（create a balance），让青少年有时间休息、玩耍并自然成长，而不是把他们变成学习机器。这完全符合选项C。A变成学习机器是作者极力反对的，B完全无视考试则是从一个极端走向另一个极端。故选C。
