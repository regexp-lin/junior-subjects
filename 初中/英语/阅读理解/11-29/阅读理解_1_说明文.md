# 阅读理解（说明文）

**Mobile Phones and Radiation: What You Should Know**

① Mobile phones have become a part of our daily life. We use them to call, text, play games, and study. But many people worry: do mobile phones send out harmful radiation? Let us look at what scientists say.

② All mobile phones send out a kind of energy called radio waves when they are turned on. This is a form of radiation, but it is different from the strong radiation you might find in X-rays or nuclear plants. The radiation from phones is "non-ionizing" — it does not have enough energy to damage DNA or cause cancer in the way that X-rays can. So far, major health organisations around the world have not found clear proof that normal use of mobile phones causes serious harm.

③ However, scientists still suggest we take some simple steps to reduce exposure. Do not hold your phone against your ear for hours. Use earphones or the speaker when you talk for a long time. At night, keep your phone away from your pillow — at least one metre away — instead of under your pillow. Do not charge your phone next to your bed while you sleep. These habits can lower the amount of radiation you receive without giving up your phone.

④ Children may be more sensitive to radiation than adults because their bodies are still growing. Some countries advise that children under 16 should use mobile phones only when necessary and for short periods. Parents can set rules such as no phones in the bedroom at night.

⑤ In short, there is no need to panic. Using your phone in a normal way is generally safe. But it is wise to follow the tips above — use earphones for long calls, keep the phone away when you sleep, and avoid overuse. Like many things in life, moderation is key.

**注释：** radiation 辐射；ionizing 电离的；exposure 接触；moderation 适度

**Questions:**

1. What kind of energy do mobile phones send out?
   A. Light.
   B. Radio waves.
   C. Heat only.
   D. Sound.

2. How is phone radiation different from X-ray radiation?
   A. It is stronger.
   B. It is "non-ionizing" and does not damage DNA like X-rays can.
   C. It is louder.
   D. It lasts longer.

3. What does the passage suggest for long phone calls?
   A. Hold the phone closer to your ear.
   B. Use earphones or the speaker.
   C. Turn off the phone.
   D. Speak very loudly.

4. Why might children be more sensitive to radiation?
   A. They use phones less.
   B. Their bodies are still growing.
   C. They prefer tablets.
   D. They study harder.

5. What is the main message of the passage?
   A. Never use a mobile phone.
   B. Normal use is generally safe; follow simple tips to reduce exposure.
   C. Phones always cause cancer.
   D. Only adults should use phones.

---

**Answers and Explanations:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "All mobile phones send out a kind of energy called radio waves when they are turned on."

**【解析】** 原文说手机开机时发出无线电波。A项光、C项仅热量、D项声音均不正确。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "The radiation from phones is 'non-ionizing' — it does not have enough energy to damage DNA or cause cancer in the way that X-rays can."

**【解析】** 手机辐射是"非电离"的，不足以像X光那样损害DNA或致癌。A项更强与原文矛盾；C项更响、D项持续更久均与辐射类型无关。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "Use earphones or the speaker when you talk for a long time."

**【解析】** 长时间通话建议使用耳机或扬声器。A项贴近耳朵与建议相反；C项关机、D项大声说均非建议。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "Children may be more sensitive to radiation than adults because their bodies are still growing."

**【解析】** 儿童因身体仍在发育可能对辐射更敏感。A项用得更少、C项更喜欢平板、D项更努力学习均非原因。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 主旨大意

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "Using your phone in a normal way is generally safe. But it is wise to follow the tips above... moderation is key."

**【解析】** 正常使用一般安全，但宜遵循文中的简单建议、适度使用。A项从不使用过于极端；C项总是致癌与原文矛盾；D项仅成人可用与建议不符。故选B。
