# 阅读理解（记叙文）

## Finding My Voice Through Colors

① When I was in Grade 7, I thought I was not good at anything. My grades were average. I was too shy to speak in class. I often felt lonely and had no idea how to express my feelings. Then one day, our art teacher, Mr. Chen, handed each student a paintbrush and a piece of paper. "Today, we will paint what we feel," he said. "There are no right or wrong answers. Just be honest."

② At first, I did not know what to draw. I stared at the blank paper for a long time. Then I remembered the sadness I felt when my best friend moved away last month. I picked up a blue brush and began to paint. Dark blue for the sadness. Light blue for the hope that we might meet again. I did not draw any clear shapes—just colors and lines. My hand moved freely across the paper.

③ When I finished, Mr. Chen walked over and looked at my painting. He did not say "good" or "bad." Instead, he said, "I can see you have a lot to say. Your colors tell a story." Those words surprised me. No one had ever said that my feelings mattered. For the first time, I felt that someone understood me.

④ From that day on, I started to paint more often. When I was happy, I used bright colors like yellow and orange. When I was worried, I mixed gray and purple. Painting became my way of saying things I could not put into words. Slowly, I began to feel more confident. I even made friends with classmates who also enjoyed art. We would sit together and share our paintings and our thoughts.

⑤ Now I am in Grade 9. I still paint whenever I need to understand myself better. I have learned that everyone has a unique way to express who they are. For me, it is through colors. For others, it might be through music, writing, or sports. The important thing is to find what works for you and to believe that your voice matters.

**注释：** paintbrush 画笔；average 普通的；blank 空白的；unique 独特的

### 选择题

1. What was the writer like when he was in Grade 7?
   A. He was shy, felt lonely, and did not know how to express his feelings.
   B. He was confident and outgoing.
   C. He had excellent grades in all subjects.
   D. He was good at speaking in front of the class.

2. What did Mr. Chen ask the students to do in the art class?
   A. Copy a famous painting.
   B. Paint what they feel, with no right or wrong answers.
   C. Draw something beautiful only.
   D. Finish the painting within ten minutes.

3. What did the writer paint when he thought of his friend who moved away?
   A. A clear picture of his friend's face.
   B. A map showing where his friend went.
   C. Dark blue and light blue—colors and lines without clear shapes.
   D. Flowers and sunshine.

4. How did the writer feel when Mr. Chen said "Your colors tell a story"?
   A. He felt upset and wanted to give up.
   B. He felt bored because the teacher did not praise his drawing skills.
   C. He felt angry at the teacher's comment.
   D. He was surprised and felt that someone understood him for the first time.

5. What does the writer want to tell us through this passage?
   A. Everyone has a unique way to express themselves, and it is important to find what works for you.
   B. Art class is more important than other subjects.
   C. Everyone should become an artist.
   D. Only painting can help people make friends.

---

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "When I was in Grade 7, I thought I was not good at anything. My grades were average. I was too shy to speak in class. I often felt lonely and had no idea how to express my feelings."

**【解析】** 作者七年级时成绩普通、害羞、常感孤独，不知如何表达感受。A 项正确。B 项自信外向、C 项成绩优异、D 项擅长演讲均与原文相反。故选 A。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "Today, we will paint what we feel," he said. "There are no right or wrong answers. Just be honest."

**【解析】** Mr. Chen 让学生画出自己的感受，没有对错，只需诚实。B 项正确。A 项临摹名画、C 项只画美丽事物、D 项十分钟完成均未提及。故选 B。

**3. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "I picked up a blue brush and began to paint. Dark blue for the sadness. Light blue for the hope... I did not draw any clear shapes—just colors and lines."

**【解析】** 作者用深蓝表达悲伤、浅蓝表达希望，没有清晰形状，只有色块与线条。C 项正确。A、B、D 项与原文不符。故选 C。

**4. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第③段 "Those words surprised me. No one had ever said that my feelings mattered. For the first time, I felt that someone understood me."

**【解析】** 老师的评价让作者惊讶，第一次感到有人理解自己，感到自己的感受被重视。D 项准确概括。A、B、C 项情感色彩与原文不符。故选 D。

**5. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 主旨理解 / 写作意图

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "I have learned that everyone has a unique way to express who they are... The important thing is to find what works for you and to believe that your voice matters."

**【解析】** 作者想传达：每个人都有独特的表达方式，重要的是找到适合自己的方式并相信自己的声音有价值。A 项准确概括主旨。B、C、D 项过于绝对或偏离中心。故选 A。
