# 阅读理解（说明文）

## How Camels Adapt to Desert Life

① The camel is often called "the ship of the desert." This is because it can travel through the hot, dry desert for many days without water or food. How does it manage to do this? The answer lies in its special body and habits that help it survive in such a difficult environment.

② One of the camel's most famous features is its hump. Many people think the hump stores water, but that is not true. The hump actually stores fat. When the camel cannot find food for a long time, its body uses this fat for energy. This allows the camel to go without eating for weeks. A camel can also drink a huge amount of water at one time—up to fifty liters. It keeps this water in its stomach and blood, and uses it slowly over the following days.

③ The camel's body is built to handle the heat. Its thick fur protects it from the burning sun during the day and keeps it warm at night when the desert gets cold. The camel has long, thin legs that keep its body high above the hot sand. Its feet are wide and flat, so they do not sink easily into the soft sand. The camel can also close its nostrils when a sandstorm comes, so that sand does not get into its nose.

④ The camel's eyes have another special feature. They have long eyelashes and a third eyelid that can move sideways. These help keep sand and dust out of the eyes when the wind blows. Even in the strongest sandstorms, the camel can still see and walk forward.

⑤ Thanks to all these amazing adaptations, the camel has become one of the most successful animals in the desert. For thousands of years, people in desert areas have depended on camels for transport, milk, and wool. The camel truly deserves its title as the ship of the desert.

**注释：** hump 驼峰；nostril 鼻孔；eyelid 眼睑；adaptation 适应（性）

### 选择题

1. According to paragraph ②, what does the camel's hump store?
   A. Fat.
   B. Water.
   C. Food.
   D. Blood.

2. How much water can a camel drink at one time according to the passage?
   A. About five liters.
   B. Up to fifty liters.
   C. About fifteen liters.
   D. Up to one hundred liters.

3. Why does the camel have wide and flat feet?
   A. To run faster than other animals.
   B. To swim in the water.
   C. So they do not sink easily into the soft sand.
   D. To catch small insects for food.

4. What helps the camel protect its eyes during a sandstorm?
   A. Its long legs.
   B. Its thick fur.
   C. Its ability to close its nostrils.
   D. Long eyelashes and a third eyelid that can move sideways.

5. What can we infer from the passage about people in desert areas?
   A. They have depended on camels for transport, milk, and wool for thousands of years.
   B. They never use camels.
   C. They prefer horses to camels.
   D. They only use camels for racing.

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "Many people think the hump stores water, but that is not true. The hump actually stores fat."

**【解析】** 人们常误以为驼峰储存水分，但实际储存的是脂肪。B 项正确。A 项水、C 项食物、D 项血液均与原文不符。故选 B。

**2. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "A camel can also drink a huge amount of water at one time—up to fifty liters."

**【解析】** 骆驼一次可饮用大量水，最多可达五十升。C 项正确。A、B、D 项数量均与原文不符。故选 C。

**3. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "Its feet are wide and flat, so they do not sink easily into the soft sand."

**【解析】** 骆驼脚掌宽而扁平，使其不易陷入松软沙地。C 项正确。A 项跑得快、B 项游泳、D 项捉虫均为文中未提及的功能。故选 C。

**4. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "They have long eyelashes and a third eyelid that can move sideways. These help keep sand and dust out of the eyes when the wind blows."

**【解析】** 骆驼眼睛有长睫毛和可横向活动的第三眼睑，在风沙中保护眼睛。C 项正确。A 项长腿、B 项厚毛、D 项闭合鼻孔均与眼部保护无关。故选 C。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "For thousands of years, people in desert areas have depended on camels for transport, milk, and wool."

**【解析】** 沙漠地区的人们数千年来依赖骆驼获得运输、奶和毛。B 项准确概括。A 项"从不使用"、C 项"更爱马"、D 项"仅用于赛跑"均与原文矛盾。故选 B。
