# 阅读理解（议论文）

**Do Famous Schools Decide Your Future?**

① Many parents dream of sending their children to famous schools. They believe that a famous school will give their children a better future. They work hard and spend a lot of money to make this happen. But does going to a famous school really decide your future? Let us think about it.

② It is true that famous schools have some advantages. They often have better teachers, more resources, and a stronger learning environment. Students there may have more opportunities to join clubs, competitions, and activities. The name of a famous school can also look good on a résumé when applying for jobs or university. In this sense, famous schools can help.

③ However, a famous school does not guarantee success. Many successful people did not go to the best schools. What matters more is how hard you work, how curious you are, and whether you never give up. A student at an ordinary school who studies hard and takes every opportunity can do just as well as—or even better than—a student at a famous school who does not try. Your attitude and effort matter more than the name of your school.

④ Besides, success means different things to different people. For some, it is a high-paying job. For others, it is happiness, good health, or helping others. A famous school may help you get certain jobs, but it cannot give you happiness or meaning in life. Those come from within yourself.

⑤ So, do famous schools decide your future? The answer is: partly, but not completely. A good school can open doors. But it is you who must walk through them. No matter which school you go to, the most important thing is to do your best, learn every day, and believe in yourself. Your future is in your hands, not just in the name of your school.

**注释：** résumé 简历；guarantee 保证

**Questions:**

1. According to Paragraph ②, which is an advantage of famous schools?
   A. They are always free.
   B. They often have better teachers, more resources, and more opportunities.
   C. They have no exams.
   D. They are very small.

2. What does the writer say matters more than the school you go to?
   A. Money.
   B. How hard you work, your curiosity, and whether you never give up.
   C. The size of the school.
   D. Your parents' jobs.

3. According to Paragraph ④, what can a famous school NOT give you?
   A. A good education.
   B. Happiness or meaning in life.
   C. Teachers.
   D. A certificate.

4. What is the writer's final view on the question?
   A. Famous schools decide everything.
   B. A famous school can help, but your own effort and attitude matter most.
   C. Ordinary schools are always better.
   D. School does not matter at all.

5. What can we infer from the passage?
   A. Everyone must go to a famous school.
   B. Success depends on both opportunities and your own effort; the school name alone is not enough.
   C. Famous schools guarantee happiness.
   D. Hard work is not important.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第②段 "famous schools have some advantages. They often have better teachers, more resources... more opportunities"
**【解析】** 名校优势包括更好的师资、更多资源和机会。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第③段 "What matters more is how hard you work, how curious you are, and whether you never give up"
**【解析】** 作者认为努力程度、好奇心和坚持比学校名气更重要。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
