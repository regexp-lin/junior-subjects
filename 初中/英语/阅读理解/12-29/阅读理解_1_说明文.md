# 阅读理解（说明文）

**Space Travel: From Dream to Reality**

① For thousands of years, people looked at the moon and stars and dreamed of going there. In 1969, American astronauts landed on the moon for the first time. Today, space travel is still expensive and difficult. But scientists and companies are working to make it easier and cheaper. Maybe one day, ordinary people will travel to space too.

② How do spacecraft work? A rocket carries the spacecraft into space. The rocket burns fuel at a very high speed. The hot gas pushes the rocket up. It needs great power to escape Earth's gravity. Once in space, the spacecraft moves around the Earth or flies to other places. There is no air in space. The spacecraft must carry its own oxygen for the astronauts to breathe. It must also protect them from extreme temperatures and radiation.

③ What is it like to live in space? Astronauts live in a space station. They float because there is no gravity. They sleep in special sleeping bags fixed to the wall. They eat food from packages. Fresh water is limited. They reuse as much as possible. Exercise is important. Without gravity, bones and muscles can become weak. Astronauts exercise about two hours every day to stay healthy. They also look at the Earth from above. Many say the view is amazing.

④ Can we go to Mars? Mars is the planet closest to Earth. Some countries plan to send humans to Mars in the future. The journey would take about seven months. Mars has a thin atmosphere. It is very cold. Living there would be a big challenge. But scientists are studying how to grow food, produce water, and build shelters on Mars. It may take many years, but the dream is alive.

⑤ Space travel teaches us about our planet and ourselves. It pushes technology forward. It inspires young people to study science. Who knows? Maybe you will be one of the next generation of astronauts.

**注释：** astronaut 宇航员；gravity 重力；radiation 辐射；atmosphere 大气层；shelter 居所

**Questions:**

1. When did humans first land on the moon?
   A. 1959.
   B. 1969.
   C. 1979.
   D. 1989.

2. Why do astronauts float in space?
   A. Because the spacecraft is big.
   B. Because there is no gravity.
   C. Because they wear special suits.
   D. Because the air is thin.

3. Why do astronauts need to exercise in space?
   A. Because they have nothing else to do.
   B. Because without gravity, bones and muscles can become weak.
   C. Because exercise is fun.
   D. Because the teacher asks them to.

4. How long would a journey to Mars take?
   A. About one month.
   B. About seven months.
   C. About one year.
   D. About two years.

5. What does the writer think about space travel?
   A. It is useless.
   B. It teaches us about Earth, pushes technology, and inspires young people.
   C. Only rich people can go.
   D. It will never happen again.

---

**Answers and Explanations:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "In 1969, American astronauts landed on the moon for the first time."

**【解析】** 1969年美国宇航员首次登陆月球。A、C、D项年份均不正确。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "They float because there is no gravity."

**【解析】** 宇航员漂浮是因为太空没有重力。A项飞船大、C项特殊服装、D项空气稀薄均非漂浮的主要原因。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "Without gravity, bones and muscles can become weak. Astronauts exercise about two hours every day to stay healthy."

**【解析】** 失重会使骨骼和肌肉变弱，所以宇航员每天约锻炼两小时。A、C、D项均非锻炼的主要原因。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "The journey would take about seven months."

**【解析】** 前往火星的航程约需七个月。A、C、D项时长均与原文不符。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "Space travel teaches us about our planet and ourselves. It pushes technology forward. It inspires young people to study science."

**【解析】** 航天让我们更了解地球和自己、推动科技发展、激励年轻人学科学。A、C、D项均与原文不符。故选B。
