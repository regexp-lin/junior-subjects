# 阅读理解（说明文）

① When you hear the word "desert", you probably think of a hot, dry place covered in yellow sand. You might picture camels walking slowly under the burning sun. However, did you know that not all deserts are hot? In fact, the largest desert in the world is covered in ice!

② A desert is defined (定义) by how much rain or snow it gets in a year, not by its temperature. Any large area that receives very little precipitation (降水, 比如雨雪) is a desert. Because of this rule, a desert can be extremely hot in the daytime and surprisingly cold at night, or it can be freezing cold all year round.

③ The Sahara Desert in Africa is the largest hot desert in the world. High temperatures there can reach over 50 degrees Celsius (°C) during the day. Plants and animals in the Sahara, like the fennec fox (耳廓狐) and cactus (仙人掌), have special ways to survive the heat and the lack of water. 

④ But the title of the "World's Largest Desert" belongs to Antarctica (南极洲). Antarctica gets almost no rain or snow. The ice you see there has been building up over millions of years. Because it is so cold, the ice never melts (融化) and acts just like dry sand in a hot desert.

⑤ So, the next time someone asks you about the biggest desert on Earth, don't say the Sahara. Remember the freezing, white landscape of Antarctica!

**注释：** define 定义；precipitation 降水；extremely 极其；survive 存活；melt 融化；landscape 景色/地形

### 选择题

1. What does the word "desert" usually make people think of according to paragraph ①?
A. A cold, icy place with penguins.
B. A hot, dry place covered in yellow sand.
C. A beautiful garden full of colorful flowers.
D. A deep, blue ocean with heavy rain.

2. How do scientists define (定义) a desert?
A. By how hot it is during the daytime.
B. By how many camels live there.
C. By how much yellow sand covers the ground.
D. By how much rain or snow it receives in a year.

3. Which of the following is the largest HOT desert in the world?
A. The Sahara Desert.
B. Antarctica.
C. A hot forest in Africa.
D. A large ice mountain.

4. Form paragraph ④, why is Antarctica considered (被认为) a desert?
A. Because it is extremely hot during the day.
B. Because it gets almost no rain or snow.
C. Because there are many camels there.
D. Because its high mountains block the sun.

5. What happens to the ice in Antarctica according to the text?
A. It melts quickly in the summer.
B. It turns into deep oceans.
C. It acts like dry sand because it never melts.
D. It disappears every ten years.

---

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段第1句 "When you hear the word 'desert', you probably think of a hot, dry place covered in yellow sand."

**【解析】** 本文开头提到，当人们听到“沙漠（desert）”这个词时，可能首先会想到的是布满黄沙、炎热且干燥的地方。A（长满企鹅的冰冷地）、C（有花的花园）、D（下暴雨的深海）均不相符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段前两句 "A desert is defined (定义) by how much rain or snow it gets in a year, not by its temperature. Any large area that receives very little precipitation... is a desert."

**【解析】** 原文中清楚无误地说明了科学上对“沙漠”的定义：是根据它每年有多少降水（雨或雪）来判定的，而不是根据温度高低。D项符合。其他依据高温（A）、靠野生骆驼（B）、或看沙子量（C）都是错误的世俗思维。故选D。

**3. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段第1句 "The Sahara Desert in Africa is the largest hot desert in the world."

**【解析】** 题干问的是世界上最大的“热沙漠（HOT desert）”是哪个。第三段首句明确回答是非洲的撒哈拉沙漠（The Sahara Desert）。而B项的南极洲是最大的冷沙漠/总面积最大沙漠。故选A。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节推断理解

**【定位】** 第④段第2句 "Antarctica gets almost no rain or snow." 结合第二段的定义。

**【解析】** 因为南极洲几乎得不到任何的雨雪（极少降水），符合了第二段所讲的成漠条件（receives very little precipitation）。因此它被视为沙漠。A极热、C多骆驼、D高山挡太阳都与南极相违背或跟“被称为沙漠”的成因无关。故选B。

**5. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段末句 "Because it is so cold, the ice never melts (融化) and acts just like dry sand in a hot desert."

**【解析】** 由于南极异常寒冷，那里的冰“从不融化（never melts）”，其作用表现得就像热沙漠中的干沙子一样。而A项说夏天融化得快，B项说化成深海，D项说十年消散一次等，皆为错误信息。故选C。
