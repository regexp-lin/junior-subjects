# 阅读理解 第2篇（说明文）

## 阅读下面短文，从每题所给的 A、B、C、D 四个选项中，选出最佳选项。

---

① Have you ever wondered what life is like in space? Since China launched its own space station, Tiangong, astronauts have been living and working hundreds of kilometers above the Earth. But daily life in space is very different from life on the ground.

② One of the biggest differences is gravity — or rather, the lack of it. In space, everything floats. Astronauts can't walk normally; instead, they float from place to place and hold onto handles on the walls. Even sleeping is different. Astronauts sleep in special sleeping bags that are attached to the wall so they don't float around during the night.

③ Eating in space is also a challenge. Food must be specially prepared so it doesn't create crumbs that could float into equipment and cause problems. Most space food comes in sealed packages. Astronauts can heat their meals, but they must be careful — a drop of soup could float away and land on important machines! Despite these difficulties, Chinese astronauts have enjoyed dumplings, rice, and even mooncakes in space.

④ Exercise is very important for astronauts. Without gravity, muscles and bones become weaker quickly. To stay healthy, astronauts must exercise for about two hours every day. They use special machines like running machines with straps that hold them down, and exercise bikes that are fixed to the floor.

⑤ Life in space may seem difficult, but Chinese astronauts say it is also amazing. They can watch 16 sunrises and sunsets every day because the space station travels around the Earth once every 90 minutes. Looking down at our blue planet from space, many astronauts say they feel a deeper love for the Earth and a stronger wish to protect it.

---

**注释：** Tiangong 天宫（中国空间站）；gravity 重力；float 漂浮；crumb 碎屑；sealed 密封的；strap 绑带

---

### 题目

1. Where do Chinese astronauts live and work according to the passage?
   A. On a ship in the ocean.
   B. On the moon.
   C. In a laboratory underground.
   D. On the space station Tiangong.

2. How do astronauts sleep in space?
   A. They sleep on regular beds like at home.
   B. They sleep in special sleeping bags attached to the wall.
   C. They sleep floating freely around the station.
   D. They take sleeping pills before going to bed.

3. Why must astronauts exercise about two hours every day?
   A. Because they want to become stronger than on Earth.
   B. Because the space station rules require two hours.
   C. Because without gravity, muscles and bones become weaker.
   D. Because exercise helps them float more easily.

4. We can infer from the passage that looking at Earth from space makes astronauts ________.
   A. care more about protecting the Earth
   B. feel bored and want to come home quickly
   C. want to live in space forever
   D. afraid of the darkness around them

5. What is the passage mainly about?
   A. How to become a Chinese astronaut.
   B. The history of space exploration in China.
   C. Daily life on China's space station.
   D. Why China built the Tiangong space station.

---

### 答案与解析

**1. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段第2句 "Since China launched its own space station, Tiangong, astronauts have been living and working hundreds of kilometers above the Earth."

**【解析】** 原文明确指出中国航天员在中国自己的空间站——天宫上生活和工作，它位于地球上方数百公里处。D 项"On the space station Tiangong"与原文完全一致。A 项"在海上的一艘船上"——空间站在太空而非海上；B 项"在月球上"——原文说的是空间站，不是月球；C 项"在地下实验室"与"hundreds of kilometers above the Earth"完全矛盾。故选 D。

---

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段最后两句 "Astronauts sleep in special sleeping bags that are attached to the wall so they don't float around during the night."

**【解析】** 原文说明航天员睡在固定在墙上的特殊睡袋里，以防止在夜间漂浮。B 项与原文完全对应。A 项"像在家一样睡在普通床上"与太空无重力环境矛盾；C 项"在空间站里自由漂浮着睡"——恰恰相反，睡袋是为了防止自由漂浮的；D 项"睡前吃安眠药"属于无中生有。故选 B。

---

**3. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段第1-2句 "Exercise is very important for astronauts. Without gravity, muscles and bones become weaker quickly."

**【解析】** 原文明确解释了原因：在没有重力的情况下，肌肉和骨骼会迅速变弱，因此航天员必须每天锻炼约两小时以保持健康。C 项准确概括了这一因果关系。A 项"因为他们想变得比在地球上更强壮"——锻炼目的是维持健康而非变得更强壮，偷换了概念；B 项"因为空间站规定要求两小时"——原文解释的是生理原因（肌肉骨骼变弱），不是单纯的规定要求；D 项"因为运动帮助他们更容易漂浮"与事实相反——漂浮是失重的结果，与运动无关。故选 C。

---

**4. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段最后一句 "Looking down at our blue planet from space, many astronauts say they feel a deeper love for the Earth and a stronger wish to protect it."

**【解析】** 原文说许多航天员从太空俯瞰蓝色的地球时，感受到对地球更深的爱和更强烈的保护愿望。由此可以推断，从太空看地球使航天员更加重视保护地球。A 项"更关心保护地球"是合理推断。B 项"感到无聊想尽快回家"与"amazing"和"deeper love"的积极情感矛盾；C 项"想永远生活在太空"属于过度推断——原文表达的是对地球的爱而非对太空的留恋；D 项"害怕周围的黑暗"原文未提及任何恐惧感。故选 A。

**【技巧点拨】** 推理判断题要注意情感词汇的指向。"deeper love"和"stronger wish to protect"都指向对地球的正面情感，而非对太空的态度。

---

**5. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 主旨大意

**【定位】** 全文结构：第①段引入太空生活话题 → 第②段失重与睡眠 → 第③段太空饮食 → 第④段太空锻炼 → 第⑤段太空体验与感受。

**【解析】** 全文围绕"中国空间站上的日常生活"展开，依次介绍了航天员在太空中的行走与睡眠、饮食、锻炼方式以及观看日出日落的独特体验。C 项"中国空间站上的日常生活"最全面地概括了文章内容。A 项"如何成为中国航天员"——文章没有讨论选拔或训练过程；B 项"中国太空探索的历史"——文章重点是日常生活细节而非历史发展；D 项"中国为什么建造天宫空间站"——文章没有讨论建造原因，而是描述在上面的生活。故选 C。

**【技巧点拨】** 说明文主旨题要抓住各段的共同主题。本文各段分别介绍了太空中的行走/睡眠、饮食、运动和体验——它们的共同关键词就是"daily life in space"（太空日常生活）。
