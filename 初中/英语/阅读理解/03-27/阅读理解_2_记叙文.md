# Grandpa's Garden

①When I was young, I didn't understand why my grandpa spent so much time in his small garden behind the house. Every morning, he woke up before sunrise, put on his old hat, and walked outside to check on his vegetables. "Come with me, Lily," he always said. But I preferred to stay inside and watch TV.

②One summer, my parents sent me to stay with my grandparents for a whole month. I was bored because there was no Internet and no friends nearby. On the third day, Grandpa handed me a small bag of seeds. "These are tomato seeds," he said with a warm smile. "Would you like to plant them yourself?" I had nothing else to do, so I said yes.

③Grandpa taught me how to dig small holes, put the seeds in, and cover them gently with soil. He showed me how to water them — not too much and not too little. "Plants are like children," he said. "They need the right amount of care and plenty of patience." Every morning after that, I followed Grandpa to the garden. I watered my tomato plants and watched them carefully, but nothing seemed to happen for days.

④"Grandpa, why aren't they growing?" I asked after a week. He laughed softly and said, "They are growing, Lily. You just can't see it yet. The roots are reaching down into the earth, getting ready to push up. Good things take time." I didn't fully believe him, but I kept watering them every day.

⑤About two weeks later, tiny green leaves broke through the soil. I was so excited that I ran into the house shouting, "They're growing! They're really growing!" Grandpa nodded and said, "You see? Your patience paid off." Over the following weeks, the plants grew taller and taller. By the end of the month, small green tomatoes appeared on the branches. I couldn't stop smiling.

⑥That summer changed me. I learned that some of the best things in life don't come quickly — they need time, care, and patience. Now, years later, I have my own small garden at home. Every time I work in it, I think of Grandpa and his gentle voice saying, "Good things take time."

**1. Why didn't Lily go to the garden with Grandpa at first?**
   A. Because she was afraid of insects.
   B. Because she liked watching TV more.
   C. Because Grandpa didn't invite her.
   D. Because the garden was too far away.

**2. What did Grandpa give Lily on the third day?**
   A. A bag of flowers.
   B. A gardening book.
   C. Some tomato seeds.
   D. A small tomato plant.

**3. What did Grandpa mean by "Plants are like children"?**
   A. Plants can talk to people.
   B. Plants grow faster than children.
   C. Plants and children both need proper care and patience.
   D. Plants should be grown by children only.

**4. How did Lily feel when she saw the green leaves?**
   A. She felt worried about the plants.
   B. She felt bored and wanted to go home.
   C. She felt very excited and happy.
   D. She felt surprised that the leaves were so big.

**5. What is the best title for this passage?**
   A. My Boring Summer Holiday
   B. How to Grow Tomatoes at Home
   C. A Lesson from Grandpa's Garden
   D. Grandpa's Favorite Vegetables

---

## 答案与解析

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "'Come with me, Lily,' he always said. But I preferred to stay inside and watch TV."

**【解析】** 原文明确提到 Lily 更喜欢待在家里看电视，所以不愿意和爷爷一起去菜园。A 项"害怕昆虫"原文未提及；C 项"爷爷没有邀请她"与原文矛盾，爷爷总是说"跟我来"；D 项"菜园太远"原文未提及，菜园就在房子后面。故选 B。

**2. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "On the third day, Grandpa handed me a small bag of seeds. 'These are tomato seeds,' he said."

**【解析】** 原文说爷爷在第三天递给 Lily 一小袋种子，并说"这些是番茄种子"。A 项"一袋花"偷换概念，不是花而是番茄种子；B 项"一本园艺书"原文未提及；D 项"一棵小番茄苗"与"seeds（种子）"不符。故选 C。

**3. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第③段 "'Plants are like children,' he said. 'They need the right amount of care and plenty of patience.'"

**【解析】** 爷爷说完"植物像孩子"之后紧接着解释"它们需要适量的关爱和大量的耐心"，可推断爷爷的意思是植物和孩子一样都需要悉心照料和耐心。A 项"植物能和人说话"过度推断；B 项"植物比孩子长得快"原文未涉及速度比较；D 项"植物只能由孩子来种"属于无中生有。故选 C。

**4. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "I was so excited that I ran into the house shouting, 'They're growing! They're really growing!'"

**【解析】** 原文描写 Lily 看到绿叶后兴奋地跑进屋子大喊，说明她非常激动和开心。A 项"担心植物"与兴奋的语气矛盾；B 项"感到无聊想回家"与原文描述完全相反；D 项"惊讶叶子这么大"属于无中生有，原文说的是"tiny green leaves（小小的绿叶）"。故选 C。

**5. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 主旨大意

**【定位】** 全文，尤其是第⑥段 "That summer changed me. I learned that some of the best things in life don't come quickly — they need time, care, and patience."

**【解析】** 文章讲述了 Lily 在爷爷的菜园里种番茄的经历，通过这个过程学到了耐心和坚持的道理，核心是从菜园经历中获得的人生启示。A 项"无聊的暑假"只反映了开头的情绪，不能概括全文主旨；B 项"如何在家种番茄"以偏概全，文章重点不是种植技巧而是人生道理；D 项"爷爷最喜欢的蔬菜"偏离主题，文章重点不在蔬菜本身。故选 C。
