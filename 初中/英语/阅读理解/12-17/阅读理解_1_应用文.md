# 阅读理解（应用文）

## **A Guide to Managing Your Pocket Money**

①Do you get pocket money from your parents? How do you spend it? Learning to manage money when you are young can help you for your whole life. Here are some simple tips for students.

②**Record your spending.** Use a small notebook or an app to write down what you buy and how much you spend. At the end of the week, look at your list. You might be surprised. A few small snacks each day can add up to a lot. Knowing where your money goes is the first step to managing it well.

③**Follow the 50-30-20 rule.** Divide your pocket money into three parts: 50% for needs (like bus fare or lunch), 30% for wants (like snacks or games), and 20% for saving. Put the saving part in a piggy bank or a separate box. Do not touch it unless there is a real need. Saving even a little each week will grow over time.

④**Set a goal.** What do you want to buy? A new book? A gift for your parents? Write it down and work out how much you need to save each week. When you reach your goal, you will feel proud. Goals make saving fun.

⑤**Ask before you spend.** If you want to buy something expensive, talk to your parents first. They can help you decide if it is worth it. Sometimes we want things in the moment but regret later. A quick chat can save you from wasting money.

| Rule        | Use                              |
|-------------|-----------------------------------|
| 50%         | Needs (bus fare, lunch, etc.)     |
| 30%         | Wants (snacks, games, etc.)       |
| 20%         | Saving                            |

## 题目

1. What is this passage mainly about?
   A. How to earn more pocket money.
   B. Tips for managing pocket money.
   C. What to buy with pocket money.
   D. How to ask parents for money.

2. Why should you record your spending?
   A. To show your parents.
   B. To know where your money goes—the first step to managing it.
   C. To get more pocket money.
   D. To avoid buying snacks.

3. According to the 50-30-20 rule, how much should you save?
   A. 50%.
   B. 30%.
   C. 20%.
   D. 100%.

4. What does the writer say about setting a goal?
   A. Goals are useless.
   B. Goals make saving fun and help you feel proud when you reach them.
   C. You should never save for a goal.
   D. Goals must be very expensive.

5. When should you talk to your parents before spending?
   A. Never.
   B. Only when buying snacks.
   C. When you want to buy something expensive.
   D. Every time you spend any money.

---

## 答案与解析

**1. 答案：B**

【题型】主旨大意题

【定位】第①段及全文

【解析】
- 第①段引出话题并说明“learning to manage money”的意义，后文给出记账、50-30-20 法则、设定目标、大额消费前询问父母等建议。
- A“如何挣更多”、C“买什么”、D“如何向父母要钱”均非主旨。故答案为 B。

**2. 答案：B**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第②段

【解析】
- 第②段写：“Knowing where your money goes is the first step to managing it well.”
- A、C 未提及。D“避免买零食”过于狭隘，记录是为了了解去向。故答案为 B。

**3. 答案：C**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第③段及表格

【解析】
- 第③段与表格均写明：50% 用于需要，30% 用于想要，20% 用于储蓄。
- A、B、D 与规则不符。故答案为 C。

**4. 答案：B**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第④段

【解析】
- 第④段写：设定目标能让储蓄变得有趣，达成目标时会感到自豪（“Goals make saving fun... you will feel proud”）。
- A、C、D 与原文相反或过度推断。故答案为 B。

**5. 答案：C**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第⑤段

【解析】
- 第⑤段写：“If you want to buy something expensive, talk to your parents first.”
- A、B、D 与原文不符。故答案为 C。
