# 阅读理解（说明文）

① Plastic pollution in the ocean is one of the biggest environmental problems today. Every year, millions of tons of plastic waste enter the sea. This plastic comes from many places: bottles and bags that people throw away, fishing nets left in the water, and rubbish carried by rivers from cities and farms.

② Why is ocean plastic so harmful? First, it never really disappears. Plastic can take hundreds of years to break down. During that time, it floats in the water or sinks to the bottom. Second, sea animals often mistake plastic for food. Turtles eat plastic bags because they look like jellyfish. Birds and fish swallow small plastic pieces. Many of these animals become sick or die because of plastic in their stomachs.

③ Plastic also breaks into very small pieces called microplastics. These pieces are smaller than a grain of rice. They get into the water and are eaten by tiny sea creatures. When bigger fish eat these creatures, the microplastics move up the food chain. In the end, they may reach our dinner plates when we eat seafood.

④ What can we do to help? We should use less plastic in our daily life. Bring your own bag when you go shopping. Say no to plastic straws and choose paper or metal ones instead. Recycle plastic bottles and boxes whenever possible. Small actions like these can make a big difference.

⑤ Governments and companies are also taking action. Some countries have banned plastic bags. Scientists are working on new kinds of plastic that can break down faster. Together, we can protect our oceans and all the life that depends on them.

**注释：** microplastic 微塑料；grain 谷物颗粒

**Questions:**

1. Where does plastic in the ocean come from?
   A. Only from fishing boats.
   B. Only from big cities.
   C. From bottles, bags, nets, and river waste.
   D. From the ocean itself.

2. Why do turtles eat plastic bags?
   A. Because they are very hungry.
   B. Because plastic bags look like jellyfish.
   C. Because plastic bags taste good.
   D. Because people feed them plastic.

3. What does the word "microplastics" mean in Paragraph ③?
   A. Big plastic bags in the sea.
   B. Very small pieces of plastic.
   C. Plastic made by fish.
   D. New kinds of plastic bottles.

4. Which of the following is NOT mentioned as a way to help reduce plastic?
   A. Bringing your own shopping bag.
   B. Using paper or metal straws.
   C. Recycling plastic bottles.
   D. Stopping eating seafood.

5. What can we infer from the last paragraph?
   A. Only individuals can solve the problem.
   B. Governments and companies are also trying to help.
   C. Plastic bags are allowed in all countries now.
   D. Scientists have found a perfect solution.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段第3句 "This plastic comes from many places: bottles and bags that people throw away, fishing nets left in the water, and rubbish carried by rivers from cities and farms."

**【解析】** 原文列举了塑料来源：瓶子、袋子、渔网以及河流从城市和农场带来的垃圾。C项概括全面。A项"只来自渔船"和B项"只来自大城市"均以偏概全；D项"来自海洋本身"与原文矛盾。故选C。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段第4句 "Turtles eat plastic bags because they look like jellyfish."

**【解析】** 原文明确说明海龟吃塑料袋是因为塑料袋看起来像水母。A项"非常饿"、C项"味道好"和D项"人们喂它们"均原文未提及。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 词义猜测

**【定位】** 第③段第1-2句 "Plastic also breaks into very small pieces called microplastics. These pieces are smaller than a grain of rice."

**【解析】** 根据上下文，microplastics被描述为"非常小的碎片"且"比一粒米还小"，可推知其意为"非常小的塑料碎片"。A项"大海里的大塑料袋"与描述相反；C项和D项与文意不符。故选B。

**4. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "Bring your own bag... Say no to plastic straws... Recycle plastic bottles and boxes whenever possible."

**【解析】** 原文提到的建议包括：自带购物袋、拒绝塑料吸管改用纸或金属吸管、回收塑料瓶。D项"停止吃海鲜"原文未提及，且与减少塑料污染的日常行动无关。故选D。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "Governments and companies are also taking action. Some countries have banned plastic bags. Scientists are working on new kinds of plastic that can break down faster."

**【解析】** 最后一段明确提到政府和企业也在采取行动，如部分国家禁用塑料袋、科学家研发可更快分解的新型塑料，由此可推知B项正确。A项"只有个人能解决问题"与段落主旨相反；C项"所有国家都允许塑料袋"与"some countries have banned"矛盾；D项"已找到完美方案"过度推断。故选B。
