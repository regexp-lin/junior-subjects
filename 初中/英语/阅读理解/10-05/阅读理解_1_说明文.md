# 阅读理解（说明文）

① Humans have dreamed of reaching the Moon for thousands of years. In the 20th century, this dream came true. The history of Moon exploration is full of courage, science, and teamwork. Let us look at some important moments.

② In 1959, the Soviet Union's Luna 2 became the first spacecraft to reach the Moon. It crashed on the lunar surface, but it proved that humans could send something to the Moon. A few years later, in 1969, the American Apollo 11 mission made history. Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first humans to walk on the Moon. Armstrong's words — "That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind" — are still remembered today. Millions of people watched this event on TV around the world.

③ After Apollo 11, the United States sent more astronauts to the Moon. In total, 12 people have walked on the Moon, all of them American. The last Moon landing was in 1972. For many years after that, no country sent humans to the Moon again. But robots and spacecraft continued to explore. They took photos, collected rocks, and studied the Moon's surface.

④ In recent years, China has made great progress in lunar exploration. In 2013, China's Chang'e 3 lander landed on the Moon and released a rover called Yutu, or "Jade Rabbit." It was the first soft landing on the Moon in nearly 40 years. In 2020, Chang'e 5 brought Moon rocks back to Earth. This was a very difficult task. No country had done it since 1976. China is now planning to send astronauts to the Moon in the future.

⑤ Exploring the Moon has taught us much about space and our own planet. It has also inspired many people to study science and become astronauts. The Moon may one day become a base for deeper space exploration. The story of the Moon is not over — it has just begun.

**注释：** lunar 月球的；lander 着陆器；rover 月球车

---

**Questions:**

1. Which country sent the first spacecraft to the Moon?
   A. The United States.
   B. China.
   C. The Soviet Union.
   D. The United Kingdom.

2. Who were the first humans to walk on the Moon?
   A. Soviet astronauts.
   B. Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin.
   C. Chinese astronauts.
   D. No one has walked on the Moon yet.

3. When did the last human Moon landing take place?
   A. In 1969.
   B. In 1972.
   C. In 2013.
   D. In 2020.

4. What did China's Chang'e 5 do?
   A. It only took photos of the Moon.
   B. It brought Moon rocks back to Earth.
   C. It sent astronauts to the Moon.
   D. It was the first spacecraft to reach the Moon.

5. What can we infer from the last paragraph?
   A. Moon exploration has stopped.
   B. Moon exploration has inspired people and may lead to deeper space exploration in the future.
   C. No country wants to explore the Moon anymore.
   D. The Moon is not important for science.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "In 1959, the Soviet Union's Luna 2 became the first spacecraft to reach the Moon"

**【解析】** 1959年苏联的 Luna 2 首次到达月球。C项正确。A、B、D项与史实不符。故选C。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first humans to walk on the Moon"

**【解析】** 阿姆斯特朗和奥尔德林是首批登月的人类。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "The last Moon landing was in 1972"

**【解析】** 最后一次人类登月在1972年。B项正确。A、C、D项时间错误。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "In 2020, Chang'e 5 brought Moon rocks back to Earth"

**【解析】** 嫦娥五号将月壤带回地球。B项正确。A、C、D项与嫦娥五号任务不符。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "inspired many people to study science... The Moon may one day become a base for deeper space exploration. The story of the Moon is not over"

**【解析】** 可推知探月激发人们学科学，未来可能成为深空探索基地。B项正确。A、C、D项与文意相反。故选B。
