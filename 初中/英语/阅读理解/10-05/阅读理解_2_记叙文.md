> **10-05 第2篇**

# 阅读理解（记叙文）

① One afternoon last autumn, I was walking home from school alone. It was a sunny day, and I was thinking about my homework. Suddenly, I heard a soft chirping sound near a bush. I stopped and looked. There, on the ground, was a small bird. It had brown feathers and was not moving much. One of its wings looked bent. I guessed it might have fallen from a tree or been hurt by a cat.

② I did not know what to do at first. I was afraid to touch it in case I made things worse. But the bird seemed so weak and helpless. I remembered that my science teacher once said: "If you find an injured animal, try to keep it safe and warm. Don't give it food or water without expert advice." I decided to help. I took off my jacket and wrapped the bird gently. It was so small that it fit in my hands. I held it carefully and walked quickly home.

③ When I got home, my mother was surprised. "What is that?" she asked. I showed her the bird and explained what had happened. She said, "We should take it to the vet or call the animal rescue centre." My mother found a number online and called. A lady from the centre said, "Keep the bird in a quiet, warm place. Bring it to us tomorrow morning. Do not try to feed it." We put the bird in a small box with a soft cloth and left it in a warm room.

④ The next morning, my mother drove me to the animal rescue centre. A worker there checked the bird. She said the wing was injured but could heal with rest and care. "You did the right thing by bringing it here," she told me. "Many injured birds die because people do not know how to help." I felt relieved. A few weeks later, the centre sent us a photo. The bird had recovered and was released back into the wild. I was so happy.

⑤ That experience taught me something important. When we see animals in trouble, we may not be experts, but we can still do something — keep them safe, seek help, and give them a chance. A small act of kindness can make a big difference.

**注释：** chirp 啾啾叫；vet 兽医；release 释放

---

**Questions:**

1. Where did the writer find the injured bird?
   A. In the classroom.
   B. On the ground near a bush on the way home.
   C. In the park.
   D. At the animal rescue centre.

2. Why did the writer think the bird was hurt?
   A. Because it was singing loudly.
   B. Because one of its wings looked bent and it was not moving much.
   C. Because it was eating.
   D. Because it was flying very fast.

3. What did the writer do with the bird at first?
   A. Gave it food and water immediately.
   B. Wrapped it gently with the jacket and took it home.
   C. Left it on the ground and ran away.
   D. Put it back in the tree.

4. What did the animal rescue centre tell them to do?
   A. Feed the bird at once.
   B. Keep it in a quiet, warm place and bring it to the centre the next morning.
   C. Release it immediately.
   D. Throw it away.

5. What did the writer learn from this experience?
   A. Birds are dangerous.
   B. We can help injured animals by keeping them safe, seeking expert help, and giving them a chance.
   C. We should never touch birds.
   D. Animal rescue centres are useless.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "I heard a soft chirping sound near a bush... There, on the ground, was a small bird" 及 "walking home from school"

**【解析】** 作者在放学回家路上、靠近灌木丛的地上发现受伤的小鸟。B项准确。A、C、D项与地点不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "One of its wings looked bent. I guessed it might have fallen from a tree or been hurt by a cat"

**【解析】** 一只翅膀看起来弯曲、几乎不动，作者推测可能受伤。B项正确。A、C、D项与判断依据不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "I took off my jacket and wrapped the bird gently... I held it carefully and walked quickly home"

**【解析】** 作者用外套轻裹小鸟，小心捧着带回家。B项准确。A项与老师建议矛盾；C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "Keep the bird in a quiet, warm place. Bring it to us tomorrow morning. Do not try to feed it"

**【解析】** 救助中心建议：保持安静温暖、第二天早上送去、不要自行喂食。B项正确。A、C、D项与指示矛盾。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "we can still do something — keep them safe, seek help, and give them a chance. A small act of kindness can make a big difference"

**【解析】** 作者体会到：可先保护受伤动物、寻求专业帮助、给它们机会，小善举也能起大作用。B项正确。A、C、D项与文意不符。故选B。
