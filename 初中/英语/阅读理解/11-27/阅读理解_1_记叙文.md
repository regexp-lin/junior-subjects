# 阅读理解（记叙文）

**My First Speech Contest**

① Last month, my English teacher asked me to take part in the school speech contest. I was shocked. I had never spoken in front of a large audience before. I was nervous just thinking about it, but I said yes because I did not want to let my teacher down.

② I spent two weeks preparing. I wrote my speech about "Why Reading Matters" and practised it every day. I read it to my mirror, to my mum, and even to my dog. My teacher helped me improve my pronunciation and taught me to look at the audience instead of my paper. "Speak slowly and smile," she said. "The audience wants you to do well."

③ The day of the contest arrived. When I walked onto the stage, my hands were shaking. I saw hundreds of faces in the hall. For a moment, I forgot my first sentence. Then I remembered my teacher's words. I took a deep breath and began. The first few words were difficult, but soon I found my rhythm. I looked at the crowd, and some people were nodding. That gave me courage.

④ When I finished, everyone applauded. I felt proud, though I was not sure if I would win. In the end, I came second. The winner was a girl from Grade 9. I was a little disappointed at first, but then my teacher said, "You did something much more important than winning — you overcame your fear." She was right.

⑤ That speech contest changed me. I learned that fear is normal, but we can beat it with practice and courage. Now I am not afraid to speak in class anymore. Sometimes the biggest victory is not the prize, but the person we become along the way.

---

**Questions:**

1. Why did the writer agree to join the speech contest?
   A. Because he loved public speaking.
   B. Because he did not want to let his teacher down.
   C. Because his parents forced him.
   D. Because he wanted to win the first prize.

2. What was the topic of the writer's speech?
   A. "My School Life."
   B. "Why Reading Matters."
   C. "How to Study English."
   D. "Friends and Family."

3. What did the teacher tell the writer to do?
   A. Read from the paper all the time.
   B. Speak slowly, smile, and look at the audience.
   C. Speak as fast as possible.
   D. Look at the floor.

4. How did the writer feel when he first stood on the stage?
   A. Very confident.
   B. His hands were shaking; he was nervous.
   C. Very happy.
   D. Sleepy.

5. What did the writer learn from this experience?
   A. Winning is the only thing that matters.
   B. Fear is normal, but we can beat it with practice and courage.
   C. Public speaking is easy.
   D. He should never try again.

---

**Answers and Explanations:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "but I said yes because I did not want to let my teacher down."

**【解析】** 原文说他答应参加是因为不想让老师失望。A项热爱演讲、C项父母强迫、D项想拿第一均非原因。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "I wrote my speech about 'Why Reading Matters'"

**【解析】** 演讲题目是"Why Reading Matters"（阅读为何重要）。A、C、D项均非演讲主题。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "taught me to look at the audience instead of my paper. 'Speak slowly and smile,' she said."

**【解析】** 老师教他看观众而非稿纸，并说语速要慢、要微笑。A项一直看稿与老师建议相反；C项尽可能快说与"slowly"矛盾；D项看地板原文未提及。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "When I walked onto the stage, my hands were shaking."

**【解析】** 作者上台时双手发抖，说明紧张。A项非常自信、C项非常开心、D项困倦均与"hands were shaking"不符。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 主旨大意

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "I learned that fear is normal, but we can beat it with practice and courage."

**【解析】** 作者领悟到恐惧是正常的，但通过练习和勇气可以战胜它。A项" winning is the only thing"与第⑤段"the biggest victory is not the prize"矛盾；C项"easy"与作者经历不符；D项"never try again"与作者后来敢于课堂发言矛盾。故选B。
