# 阅读理解（说明文）

① In nature, every living thing is connected. Plants, animals, and even the smallest insects form a chain that passes energy from one to another. This chain is called a food chain (食物链). Understanding it helps us see why keeping nature in balance matters so much.

② A food chain usually starts with plants. Plants use sunlight to make their own food. They are called producers (生产者) because they produce food for others. Next come animals that eat plants — like rabbits, deer, and many insects. These are primary consumers (初级消费者). Animals that eat those plant-eaters, such as foxes and hawks, are secondary consumers (次级消费者). Finally, when these animals die, their bodies break down and return to the soil. The soil then helps new plants grow. The circle continues.

③ When one part of the chain is damaged, the whole system can change. For example, if hunters kill too many wolves, the number of deer may rise quickly. Too many deer will eat too many plants, and other animals that depend on those plants will have less food. This can lead to ecological imbalance (生态失衡).

④ Human activities sometimes harm this balance. Cutting down forests, polluting rivers, and overfishing can break food chains. When we protect nature — by saving forests, keeping water clean, and not hunting endangered animals — we help keep the balance. Every small action counts.

⑤ The food chain teaches us that all life is connected. What happens to one species can affect many others. By caring for our environment, we not only protect animals and plants but also secure a healthier future for ourselves.

**注释：** food chain 食物链；producer 生产者；consumer 消费者；ecological 生态的；imbalance 失衡；species 物种

### 选择题

1. According to paragraph ②, where does a food chain usually begin?
   A. With plants that use sunlight to make food.
   B. With animals that eat other animals.
   C. With hunters in the forest.
   D. With dead animals in the soil.

2. What are "primary consumers" in a food chain?
   A. Plants that produce food.
   B. Animals that eat other animals.
   C. Animals that eat plants, such as rabbits and deer.
   D. Small insects in the soil.

3. What might happen if too many wolves are killed, according to paragraph ③?
   A. The deer population will drop immediately.
   B. Plants will grow faster.
   C. The number of deer may rise, and plants may be eaten too much.
   D. Wolves will become stronger.

4. What can we infer from the passage about human actions?
   A. Humans have no effect on food chains.
   B. Protecting nature helps keep ecological balance.
   C. Cutting down forests always improves the environment.
   D. Overfishing has no impact on marine life.

5. What is the main idea of this passage?
   A. Wolves and deer are the most important animals in nature.
   B. Food chains show how life is connected and why ecological balance matters.
   C. Plants can live without sunlight.
   D. Humans should stop eating meat.

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段第2句 "A food chain usually starts with plants. Plants use sunlight to make their own food."

**【解析】** 原文明确说明食物链通常从植物开始，植物利用阳光制造自身所需的食物。A项正确。B项"以吃其他动物的动物开始"与原文相反；C项"森林中的猎人"和D项"土壤中的死动物"文中未作为食物链起点。故选A。

**2. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "Animals that eat plants — like rabbits, deer, and many insects. These are primary consumers."

**【解析】** 原文清楚指出以植物为食的动物（如兔子、鹿和许多昆虫）是初级消费者。C项正确。A项"生产食物的植物"是生产者；B项"吃其他动物的动物"是次级消费者；D项"土壤中的小昆虫"表述不完整。故选C。

**3. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "if hunters kill too many wolves, the number of deer may rise quickly. Too many deer will eat too many plants..."

**【解析】** 原文举例说明猎杀太多狼会导致鹿的数量急剧上升，鹿过多会吃掉太多植物。C项准确概括这一因果。A项"鹿数量立即下降"与原文相反；B项"植物生长更快"和D项"狼变更强"均未提及。故选C。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第④段 "When we protect nature — by saving forests, keeping water clean, and not hunting endangered animals — we help keep the balance."

**【解析】** 原文指出人类保护自然（保护森林、保持水质、不猎杀濒危动物）有助于维持生态平衡，可推知保护自然有助于保持生态平衡。B项正确。A项"人类对食物链无影响"与第④段矛盾；C项"砍伐森林改善环境"与原文"harm this balance"矛盾；D项"过度捕捞对海洋无影响"与"overfishing can break food chains"矛盾。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 主旨大意

**【定位】** 全文：第①段引入食物链概念，第②段解释结构，第③段说明失衡后果，第④⑤段谈人类作用与启示。

**【解析】** 全文围绕食物链的结构、生态平衡的重要性及人类活动的影响展开，B项"食物链展示生命如何连接以及生态平衡为何重要"最能概括主旨。A项以偏概全；C项"植物可不靠阳光生存"与原文矛盾；D项"人类应停止吃肉"文中未提及。故选B。
