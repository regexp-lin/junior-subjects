# 阅读理解（议论文）

## **Say No to School Bullying**

①School should be a safe place where every student can learn and grow. But for some students, school is a place of fear. They are bullied by other students. Bullying means someone hurts you again and again on purpose—by words, by actions, or online. This is a serious problem that we must talk about and solve.

②Why do some students bully others? Sometimes bullies feel insecure themselves. They may think hurting others makes them look strong. Sometimes they copy what they see at home or in movies. But no matter the reason, bullying is wrong. The person who is bullied may feel sad, scared, or even lose interest in school. Some may get hurt both in body and in mind.

③What can we do if we see bullying? First, do not join in or laugh. That only makes things worse. Second, if it is safe, stand up and tell the bully to stop. Third, comfort the person who was bullied and let them know they are not alone. Fourth, tell a teacher or your parents. Adults can help.

④If you are being bullied, remember: it is not your fault. You do not deserve to be treated badly. Do not keep it to yourself. Talk to someone you trust—a parent, a teacher, or a friend. Schools have rules against bullying. Your voice matters.

⑤Let us all work together to make our schools safe. When we say no to bullying and support each other, we create a better environment for everyone. Kindness is stronger than fear.

## 题目

1. What is the main idea of this passage?
   A. School is always a safe place.
   B. We should say no to bullying and work together to stop it.
   C. Only teachers can stop bullying.
   D. Bullying is not a serious problem.

2. According to paragraph 2, why might some students bully others?
   A. They have too much homework.
   B. They feel insecure and may think hurting others makes them look strong.
   C. They are forced by teachers.
   D. They want to make more friends.

3. What should you do when you see bullying?
   A. Join in and laugh to fit in.
   B. Do nothing and walk away.
   C. Tell the bully to stop (if safe), comfort the victim, and tell an adult.
   D. Keep it a secret from everyone.

4. What does the writer tell students who are being bullied?
   A. It is their fault and they deserve it.
   B. Keep it to yourself.
   C. It is not their fault; they should talk to someone they trust.
   D. Fight back with violence.

5. What does the writer believe about kindness?
   A. Kindness is useless.
   B. Kindness is stronger than fear.
   C. Kindness only works for adults.
   D. Kindness makes people weak.

---

## 答案与解析

**1. 答案：B**

【题型】主旨大意题

【定位】全文，尤其第①段和第⑤段

【解析】
- 文章围绕校园霸凌的危害、成因、应对方法和支持被霸凌者展开，结尾呼吁共同努力、拒绝霸凌。
- A 与“for some students, school is a place of fear”矛盾。C“只有老师能阻止”过于狭隘。D 与“serious problem”矛盾。故答案为 B。

**2. 答案：B**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第②段

【解析】
- 第②段写：“Sometimes bullies feel insecure themselves. They may think hurting others makes them look strong.”
- A、C、D 未在文中提及。故答案为 B。

**3. 答案：C**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第③段

【解析】
- 第③段建议：不参与、不嘲笑；若安全则站出来制止；安慰受害者；告知老师或家长。
- A 与“do not join in or laugh”矛盾。B“什么都不做”与“stand up... tell... comfort... tell”矛盾。D 与“tell a teacher or your parents”矛盾。故答案为 C。

**4. 答案：C**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第④段

【解析】
- 第④段写：“it is not your fault... You do not deserve to be treated badly... Talk to someone you trust.”
- A 与“it is not your fault”矛盾。B 与“Do not keep it to yourself”矛盾。D“用暴力还击”未提及且不符主旨。故答案为 C。

**5. 答案：B**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第⑤段

【解析】
- 第⑤段最后一句：“Kindness is stronger than fear”（善良比恐惧更强大）。
- A、C、D 与原文相反或未提及。故答案为 B。
