# 阅读理解（说明文）

① Paper is one of the most important inventions (发明) in human history. Every day, we use paper to write, read, and even clean our hands. But have you ever wondered how people recorded information before paper was invented?

② In ancient (古代的) Egypt, people wrote on papyrus (纸莎草). It was made from a plant that grew near the river. In ancient Europe, people wrote on animal skins, which were very heavy and expensive. In ancient China, before the invention of paper, people wrote characters on turtle shells, animal bones, and bamboo strips (竹简). A bamboo book was so heavy that a cart (马车) was often needed to carry it.

③ Things changed around the year 105 AD during the Han Dynasty (汉朝). A Chinese official named Cai Lun invented a new way to make paper. He mixed tree bark (树皮), old rags, fishing nets, and water together. He beat the mixture, pressed it flat, and let it dry in the sun. This new kind of paper was light, cheap, and easy to write on. 

④ The invention of paper spread quickly to other places in the world. It allowed people to share knowledge more easily. A few hundred years later, printing (印刷术) was invented, working perfectly with paper to produce millions of books.

⑤ Today, although we have computers and smartphones, paper is still a big part of our lives. Thanks to Cai Lun's great invention, human culture and science have developed much faster.

**注释：** invention 发明；ancient 古代的；bamboo strip 竹简；bark 树皮；mixture 混合物

---

### 选择题

1. What did ancient Chinese people use to record information before paper was invented?
A. Papyrus and tree leaves.
B. Animal skins and heavy stones.
C. Turtle shells, animal bones, and bamboo strips.
D. Old rags and fishing nets.

2. Who invented the new way to make paper in ancient China?
A. A famous doctor in the Han Dynasty.
B. A Chinese official named Cai Lun.
C. An ancient Egyptian worker.
D. A teacher who taught people how to write.

3. Why was Cai Lun's paper better than bamboo strips?
A. Because it was made from expensive animal skins.
B. Because it was much heavier and harder to break.
C. Because it was light, cheap, and easy to write on.
D. Because it could only be used to read books.

4. Form paragraph ④, what worked perfectly with paper to produce millions of books?
A. The invention of printing.
B. Computer keyboards.
C. Special fishing nets.
D. Big wooden carts.

5. What is the writer's attitude (态度) towards the invention of paper?
A. He complains (抱怨) that paper creates too much rubbish.
B. He thinks computers should completely replace paper.
C. He believes paper is an unimportant and cheap thing.
D. He thinks it is a great invention that helped human culture develop faster.

---

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段第3句 "In ancient China, before the invention of paper, people wrote characters on turtle shells, animal bones, and bamboo strips (竹简)."

**【解析】** 原文提到在纸被发明之前，中国古人是在龟壳（turtle shells）、兽骨（animal bones）和竹简（bamboo strips）上刻写文字。A项是古埃及；B项是古欧洲；D项是蔡伦造纸的材料。故选C。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段第2句 "A Chinese official named Cai Lun invented a new way to make paper."

**【解析】** 根据文意，发明造纸新工艺的是一位名叫蔡伦的中国官员（official）。A项说他是医生是错的；C是古埃及工人；D项说是教人写字的老师也不符合。故选B。

**3. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段最后一句 "This new kind of paper was light, cheap, and easy to write on." 以及第②段讲述竹简的笨重。

**【解析】** 原文清楚地归纳了蔡伦发明的纸比竹简等物品优越的原因：它轻便（light）、便宜（cheap）且易于书写（easy to write on）。A项说用兽皮是古欧洲的；B项说大且难破与“轻便”相悖。故选C。

**4. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段末尾 "...printing (印刷术) was invented, working perfectly with paper to produce millions of books."

**【解析】** 印刷术的出现和纸完美结合（working perfectly with paper），从而产生了成百上千本的书籍。B项（电脑键盘）、C项（特别的渔网）、D项（木马车）不是与造纸“完美结合产出书本的内容”。故选A。

**5. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 作者态度题 / 主旨大意

**【定位】** 第①段第1句 "Paper is one of the most important inventions in human history." 和第⑤段最后一句 "Thanks to Cai Lun's great invention, human culture and science have developed much faster."

**【解析】** 作者在开头和结尾对纸的发明给出了极高的评价，并感谢蔡伦的发明使人类科技文化得以更快地发展。A项抱怨制造垃圾、B项让电脑完全替代、C项认为不重要等选项均是凭空捏造。故选D。
