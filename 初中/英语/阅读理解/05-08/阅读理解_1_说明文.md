# 阅读理解（说明文）

① People often say, "Eat your carrots, they are good for your eyes!" But is this old saying actually true, or is it just a trick parents use to make children eat vegetables? To find the answer, we need to look at both history and science.

② Let's start with the science. Carrots are rich in a chemical called beta-carotene (β-胡萝卜素). When we eat carrots, our bodies change beta-carotene into Vitamin A. Vitamin A is extremely important for our eyes. It helps the eye change light into a signal (信号) that can be sent to the brain. More importantly, Vitamin A helps us see in the dark. If a person lacks (缺乏) Vitamin A, they might suffer from "night blindness" (夜盲症), which means they cannot see well in low light. 

③ So, yes, the Vitamin A in carrots does keep our eyes healthy. However, eating ten carrots a day will not give you superhuman eyesight or allow you to throw away your glasses. Your vision (视力) won't get better than normal just by eating more carrots.

④ Now, let's look at the history. The idea that carrots give you amazing night vision actually started as a secret during World War II in the 1940s. The British Royal Air Force had invented a new kind of radar (雷达) technology that allowed their pilots to shoot down enemy planes at night. To keep this new radar a secret from the enemy, the British government spread a story: their pilots ate a lot of carrots, which gave them perfect night vision!

⑤ This clever lie worked perfectly. People believed the story, and soon everyone started eating more carrots hoping to see better in the dark. So, while carrots are certainly healthy for your eyes, their magic power is just a combination (结合) of good nutrition and a funny historical secret.

**注释：** beta-carotene β-胡萝卜素；Vitamin A 维生素A；signal 信号；lack 缺乏；night blindness 夜盲症；vision 视力；radar 雷达；nutrition 营养

### 选择题

1. What does the body change beta-carotene into?
A. Bright light.
B. Vitamin A.
C. A new kind of radar.
D. Good night vision.

2. What will happen if a person lacks (缺乏) Vitamin A?
A. They will need to eat meat immediately.
B. They will start to wear glasses the next day.
C. They might suffer from night blindness and cannot see well in low light.
D. Their eyes will become red and swollen.

3. Will eating ten carrots a day give you superhuman eyesight safely?
A. Yes, it will help you see through walls.
B. Yes, and you can throw away your glasses forever.
C. No, it only keeps eyes healthy but won't make vision better than normal.
D. No, because eating too many carrots will make you blind.

4. Form paragraph ④, why did the British government spread the story about pilots eating carrots during World War II?
A. To sell more carrots to other countries.
B. To make children eat more vegetables at dinner.
C. To test if the pilots really liked eating carrots.
D. To keep their new radar technology a secret from the enemy.

5. What is the main conclusion (结论) of this passage?
A. Carrots are entirely useless for our eyes.
B. The magic power of carrots is a mix of real nutrition (Vitamin A) and a historic lie.
C. Carrots are the only food that contains Vitamin A.
D. Everyone should eat carrots instead of wearing glasses.

---

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段第2句 "When we eat carrots, our bodies change beta-carotene into Vitamin A."

**【解析】** 当我们吃胡萝卜时，身体会把β-胡萝卜素转变成什么？文中明确指明身体将β-胡萝卜素（beta-carotene）转化为维生素A（Vitamin A）。故选B。A（强光）、C（新雷达）、D（夜视力）均不符合原文直接转化物的描述。

**2. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节推断理解

**【定位】** 第②段最后一句 "If a person lacks (缺乏) Vitamin A, they might suffer from 'night blindness' (夜盲症), which means they cannot see well in low light."

**【解析】** 缺乏维A会怎样？原文写道：如果一个人缺乏维生素A，他们可能会患上“夜盲症”，这意味着在弱光下视力不佳。这与C选项的内容一字不差。A立马吃肉、B第二天戴眼镜、D眼睛红肿都不是原文中所提的维A缺乏症。故选C。

**3. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解判断

**【定位】** 第③段第2-3句 "However, eating ten carrots a day will not give you superhuman eyesight or allow you to throw away your glasses. Your vision won't get better than normal..."

**【解析】** 每天吃十根胡萝卜能赋予你超人般的视力吗？文中否定了这一说法，指出胡萝卜只能保持眼睛健康，但吃再多也不能让你扔掉眼镜或让视力超越常人（won't get better than normal）。因此C项（不，只保健康不提超常视力）是对的。A、B夸大其词，D吃瞎则是荒诞言论。故选C。

**4. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段第2-3句 "The British Royal Air Force had invented a new kind of radar... To keep this new radar a secret from the enemy, the British government spread a story..."

**【解析】** 二战期间英国政府为什么散布飞行员吃胡萝卜的传言？文中解释是为了掩盖他们发明了一种新雷达技术的事实，不让敌人知道这个秘密（To keep this new radar a secret from the enemy）。D项准确陈述了这一军事动机。A卖胡萝卜、B让小孩吃菜、C测试口味均不符合历史事实。故选D。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 主旨意图归纳

**【定位】** 第⑤段最后总结词 "So, while carrots are certainly healthy for your eyes, their magic power is just a combination (结合) of good nutrition and a funny historical secret."

**【解析】** 全文的结论是什么？文章在最后一段做出总结：胡萝卜的确对眼睛健康有益（含维生素A提供营养），但其被称为有“神奇夜视魔力”其实是基于良好的营养加上一个有趣的历史谎言/秘密综合而来的。选项B准确概括了这一双重结论。A完全没用、C唯一含维A食品、D不戴眼镜吃萝卜皆错。故选B。
