# 阅读理解（应用文）

① **Campus Waste Sorting Guide — Green School Action**

② Our school is starting a waste sorting program. Please help us keep the campus clean and protect our environment. Follow this guide to sort your waste correctly.

③ **Four Types of Waste:**

| Type | Color of Bin | Examples |
|------|--------------|----------|
| Recyclable | Blue | Paper, plastic bottles, glass, metal cans |
| Kitchen Waste | Green | Fruit peels, leftover food, tea leaves |
| Hazardous | Red | Batteries, medicine, paint |
| Other | Gray | Dust, used tissues, broken pens |

④ **Rules:**
- Put empty plastic bottles in the blue bin. Squash (压扁) them first to save space.
- Throw fruit peels and food waste into the green bin only.
- Do NOT put batteries in any bin except the red one. Take them to the office if you cannot find a red bin.
- Used paper and notebooks go in the blue bin. Keep them dry and clean.
- If you are not sure which bin to use, check the posters on the wall or ask your teacher.

⑤ **Why It Matters:**
- Recyclable waste can be made into new things.
- Kitchen waste can become fertilizer (肥料) for plants.
- Hazardous waste must be handled with care to keep everyone safe.
- When we sort correctly, we help reduce pollution and save resources.

⑥ Thank you for joining our green school action. Every small step counts!

**注释：** squash 压扁；fertilizer 肥料；hazardous 有害的

---

**Questions:**

1. Which bin should students use for empty plastic bottles?
   A. The green bin.
   B. The blue bin.
   C. The red bin.
   D. The gray bin.

2. What should students do with fruit peels?
   A. Put them in the blue bin.
   B. Put them in the green bin.
   C. Put them in the red bin.
   D. Leave them on the desk.

3. Where should students put used batteries?
   A. In the blue bin.
   B. In the green bin.
   C. In the red bin only.
   D. In the gray bin.

4. According to the guide, why should plastic bottles be squashed before throwing?
   A. To make them look nicer.
   B. To save space.
   C. To keep them clean.
   D. To make recycling easier for workers.

5. What can we learn from the passage?
   A. All waste should go in the same bin.
   B. Students should ask teachers when they are unsure about sorting.
   C. Kitchen waste cannot be reused.
   D. The red bin is for paper and plastic.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段表格及第④段 "Put empty plastic bottles in the blue bin"

**【解析】** 原文明确说塑料瓶应放入蓝色（可回收）垃圾桶。A项绿色是厨余垃圾；C项红色是有害垃圾；D项灰色是其他垃圾。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段表格 "Kitchen Waste... Green... Fruit peels" 及第④段 "Throw fruit peels and food waste into the green bin"

**【解析】** 果皮属于厨余垃圾，应放入绿色垃圾桶。A项蓝色是可回收物；C项红色是有害垃圾；D项属于无中生有。故选B。

**3. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "Do NOT put batteries in any bin except the red one"

**【解析】** 原文明确说电池只能放入红色（有害）垃圾桶。A、B、D项均与原文不符。故选C。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第④段 "Squash them first to save space"

**【解析】** 原文说明压扁塑料瓶是为了节省空间。A项"更好看"、C项"保持干净"和D项"方便工人回收"原文均未提及。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第④段 "If you are not sure which bin to use, check the posters on the wall or ask your teacher"

**【解析】** 原文建议不确定时查看墙上的海报或询问老师。A项"全部放同一垃圾桶"与分类要求矛盾；C项"厨余不可再利用"与第⑤段"can become fertilizer"矛盾；D项红色桶用途描述错误。故选B。
