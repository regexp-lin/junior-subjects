# 阅读理解 第1篇（说明文）

## 阅读下面短文，从每题所给的 A、B、C、D 四个选项中，选出最佳选项。

---

① In many cities around the world, you can see rows of colorful bicycles parked on the sidewalk. These are shared bikes, and they have changed the way millions of people travel every day. In China, bike-sharing became very popular around 2016 and has grown quickly ever since.

② Using a shared bike is very easy. All you need is a smartphone. First, download the bike-sharing app and register with your phone number. Then, find a bike nearby and scan the QR code on it. The bike will unlock automatically, and you can start riding. When you reach your destination, just park the bike properly and lock it. The app will charge you based on how long you ride — usually about ¥1 to ¥2 per 30 minutes.

③ Bike-sharing has many advantages. First, it is cheap and convenient. You don't need to buy your own bicycle or worry about it being stolen. Second, riding a bike is good exercise and produces no pollution, so it is better for the environment than driving a car. Third, shared bikes solve the "last mile problem" — when you get off the bus or subway but your home or school is still a short distance away, a shared bike can take you the rest of the way.

④ However, bike-sharing also has some problems. Some people park bikes carelessly on sidewalks, blocking the way for others. Some riders don't follow traffic rules and ride too fast or ignore red lights. Also, many bikes get damaged because users don't take good care of them.

⑤ Despite these problems, bike-sharing remains a great invention. Many cities are now building more bike lanes and parking areas to make bike-sharing work better. With everyone's effort, shared bikes can make our cities cleaner, greener, and more convenient.

---

**注释：** scan 扫描；QR code 二维码；destination 目的地；bike lane 自行车道

---

### 题目

1. What do you need to use a shared bike?
   A. A special key from the bike company.
   B. A smartphone with the bike-sharing app.
   C. A student ID card.
   D. A monthly bus pass.

2. How much does it usually cost to ride a shared bike for 30 minutes?
   A. ¥5 to ¥10.
   B. ¥3 to ¥5.
   C. ¥1 to ¥2.
   D. It is always free.

3. What is the "last mile problem" according to Paragraph ③?
   A. The short distance between a bus or subway stop and your destination.
   B. The problem of riding a bike too far.
   C. The distance between two cities.
   D. The last mile of a running race.

4. We can infer from the passage that bike-sharing will work better if ________.
   A. all bikes are painted the same color
   B. only young people are allowed to ride
   C. cities build more bike lanes and parking areas
   D. people stop using buses and subways

5. What is the passage mainly about?
   A. How to repair a broken shared bike.
   B. Why cars are better than bicycles.
   C. The history of bicycles in China.
   D. The benefits, problems, and future of bike-sharing.

---

### 答案与解析

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段第1-2句 "Using a shared bike is very easy. All you need is a smartphone."

**【解析】** 原文明确指出使用共享单车只需要一部智能手机——下载应用、注册、扫码即可解锁。B 项"一部装有共享单车应用的智能手机"与原文完全对应。A 项"一把来自单车公司的特殊钥匙"——原文说是扫码自动解锁，不需要钥匙；C 项"学生证"原文未提及任何身份证件要求；D 项"月度公交卡"与共享单车的使用方式无关。故选 B。

---

**2. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段最后一句 "The app will charge you based on how long you ride — usually about ¥1 to ¥2 per 30 minutes."

**【解析】** 原文明确标出骑行费用为每30分钟约1至2元。C 项"¥1 to ¥2"与原文数据完全一致。A 项"¥5 to ¥10"远高于原文价格；B 项"¥3 to ¥5"也高于原文；D 项"总是免费"与原文"charge you"矛盾。故选 C。

---

**3. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段第4句 "...shared bikes solve the 'last mile problem' — when you get off the bus or subway but your home or school is still a short distance away, a shared bike can take you the rest of the way."

**【解析】** 原文用破折号解释了"最后一公里问题"：下了公交或地铁后，家或学校还有一小段距离，共享单车可以帮你走完剩下的路。A 项"公交/地铁站到目的地之间的短距离"准确概括了这一含义。B 项"骑车骑太远的问题"曲解了原意——问题不在于骑远，而在于这段短距离没有便捷交通工具；C 项"两个城市之间的距离"规模完全不对；D 项"跑步比赛的最后一英里"将交通概念误解为体育概念。故选 A。

---

**4. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段第2句 "Many cities are now building more bike lanes and parking areas to make bike-sharing work better."

**【解析】** 原文提到许多城市正在建设更多的自行车道和停车区域，目的是让共享单车运行得更好。由此可以推断，城市基础设施的改善是共享单车更好发展的关键。C 项"城市建设更多自行车道和停车区域"是合理推断。A 项"所有单车涂成同一颜色"——颜色与运行效率无关，属于无中生有；B 项"只允许年轻人骑"——原文未提及年龄限制，且这种做法与共享单车面向大众的理念矛盾；D 项"人们停止使用公交和地铁"——共享单车是对公共交通的补充而非替代，与第③段"最后一公里"的互补关系矛盾。故选 C。

**【技巧点拨】** 推理判断题要注意区分"文中已提到的事实"和"基于事实的合理推断"。本题的关键是从"cities are building more bike lanes"推断出"改善基础设施有助于共享单车发展"。

---

**5. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 主旨大意

**【定位】** 全文结构：第①段引入共享单车现象 → 第②段使用方法 → 第③段优势 → 第④段问题 → 第⑤段未来展望。

**【解析】** 全文围绕共享单车展开，依次介绍了使用方式、多项优势（便宜方便、环保、解决"最后一公里"）、存在的问题（乱停放、违规骑行、车辆损坏）以及未来改善方向。D 项"共享单车的好处、问题和未来"最全面地概括了文章的三个核心方面。A 项"如何修理坏掉的共享单车"——文章没有涉及维修方法；B 项"为什么汽车比自行车好"与文章倡导骑车的主旨相反；C 项"中国自行车的历史"——文章重点是共享单车这一现代现象，不是自行车的历史演变。故选 D。

**【技巧点拨】** 说明文的主旨概括需要覆盖各段核心内容。含有"and"连接多个方面的选项往往更全面。本题中 D 项涵盖了"benefits + problems + future"三个维度，与文章各段一一对应。
