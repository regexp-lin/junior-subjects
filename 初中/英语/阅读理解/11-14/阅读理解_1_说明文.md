# 阅读理解（说明文）

**The Compass and Sea Voyages**

① The compass is one of the four great inventions of ancient China. It was first used more than 2,000 years ago. At first, people used it to find direction when building houses or travelling on land. Later, it became very important for sea travel.

② Before the compass, sailors often got lost at sea. They could use the sun and stars, but on cloudy days they could not see them. Without knowing which way was north or south, ships could sail for days and never reach their destination. Many sailors died at sea.

③ The compass changed everything. A compass needle always points north (with some small differences in different places). Sailors could now find their way even when the sky was dark. They could sail farther and more safely. Trade between countries grew. Ideas and goods travelled across the ocean. The compass helped connect the world.

④ The Chinese compass was first made with a spoon-shaped piece of lodestone on a smooth board. Later, people made it with a needle that could turn freely. When the needle stopped moving, it pointed to the north. This design spread to the Arab world and then to Europe. It changed how humans explored the sea.

⑤ Today we have GPS, but the compass is still used. Hikers, sailors, and pilots keep it as a backup when technology fails. The compass remains a symbol of human wisdom and the spirit of exploration.

**注释：** lodestone 磁石；destination 目的地

**Questions:**

1. When was the compass first used in China?
   A. About 200 years ago.
   B. More than 2,000 years ago.
   C. Last century.
   D. In modern times.

2. What problem did sailors face before the compass?
   A. Ships were too slow.
   B. They often got lost; on cloudy days they could not use the sun or stars.
   C. There were no ships.
   D. The sea was too small.

3. How does a compass help sailors?
   A. It makes the ship go faster.
   B. The needle points north, so they can find their way even when the sky is dark.
   C. It catches fish.
   D. It predicts the weather.

4. How did the compass spread from China?
   A. It stayed in China only.
   B. It spread to the Arab world and then to Europe.
   C. It went directly to America.
   D. It was never used outside China.

5. What can we infer from the last paragraph?
   A. The compass is no longer useful.
   B. The compass is still valued today as a backup and symbol of exploration.
   C. GPS replaced the compass completely.
   D. No one uses the compass anymore.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第①段 "It was first used more than 2,000 years ago"
**【解析】** 指南针在中国首次使用超过2000年。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第②段 "Before the compass, sailors often got lost at sea... on cloudy days they could not see them"
**【解析】** 指南针发明前，水手常迷路；阴天时无法依靠日月星辰。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第③段 "A compass needle always points north... Sailors could now find their way even when the sky was dark"
**【解析】** 指针指北，水手在阴天也能辨别方向。B项准确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第④段 "This design spread to the Arab world and then to Europe"
**【解析】** 指南针经阿拉伯传入欧洲。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 推理判断
**【定位】** 第⑤段 "the compass is still used... as a backup when technology fails... remains a symbol of human wisdom and the spirit of exploration"
**【解析】** 指南针至今仍被用作备用工具，并象征人类智慧与探索精神。B项正确。A、C、D项与文意不符。故选B。
