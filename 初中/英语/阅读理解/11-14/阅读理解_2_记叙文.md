# 阅读理解（记叙文）

**Being a Little Teacher in My Community**

① Last summer, our community centre started a programme called "Little Teachers." They needed older students to help younger children with their homework. I was in Grade 8 then. I thought it might be fun, so I signed up.

② At first, I was not sure if I could do it. I had never taught anyone before. What if the kids did not listen? What if I could not explain things clearly? On the first day, I met three Grade 3 children. They looked at me with big, curious eyes. I felt nervous.

③ I started with maths. One boy had trouble with fractions. I drew pictures on the board — a circle cut into halves, then fourths. I asked him to colour one part. "So one fourth means one part out of four," I said. His eyes lit up. "I get it!" he said. That moment made me so happy. I had helped someone understand something new.

④ Week after week, I went to the community centre. The children began to trust me. They asked more questions. Sometimes they made mistakes, and we laughed together. I learned to be patient. I learned that everyone learns at a different speed. By the end of the summer, all three had made progress. One girl's mum thanked me and said her daughter now liked maths.

⑤ That experience taught me a lot. Teaching others helps you understand things better yourself. It also feels good to help. I plan to keep being a "little teacher" when I have time. Giving back to my community is something I want to do more of.

**注释：** fraction 分数；community 社区

**Questions:**

1. What is the "Little Teachers" programme?
   A. A programme where teachers learn new skills.
   B. Older students help younger children with homework in the community.
   C. Children teach their parents.
   D. A summer camp for sports.

2. How did the writer feel on the first day?
   A. Very confident.
   B. Nervous and unsure.
   C. Bored.
   D. Angry.

3. How did the writer help the boy understand fractions?
   A. By giving him a test.
   B. By drawing pictures — a circle cut into parts — and asking him to colour them.
   C. By telling him to memorise.
   D. By doing the homework for him.

4. What did the writer learn from the experience?
   A. Teaching is easy and requires no effort.
   B. To be patient; everyone learns at different speeds; teaching others helps you understand better.
   C. Young children never learn.
   D. Maths is not important.

5. What can we infer from the last paragraph?
   A. The writer will never teach again.
   B. The writer gained a lot and wants to continue helping the community.
   C. The community centre will close.
   D. Teaching does not help anyone.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第①段 "They needed older students to help younger children with their homework"
**【解析】** "小老师"项目由高年级学生辅导社区低年级孩子做作业。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第②段 "I was not sure if I could do it... I felt nervous"
**【解析】** 作者第一天不确定自己能否胜任，感到紧张。B项准确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
