# 阅读理解（说明文）

① Have you ever wondered why you forget your friend's birthday but remember a song from years ago? The answer lies in how human memory works. Memory is a powerful tool that helps us learn, think, and understand the world around us.

② Scientists often divide memory into different types. **Short-term memory** holds information for only a short time — maybe a few seconds to a minute. For example, when you look up a phone number and dial it, you use short-term memory. If you do not repeat or use the number again, it will soon disappear. **Long-term memory**, however, can store (储存) information for days, years, or even a lifetime. The things you remember from your childhood are kept in long-term memory.

③ How does information move from short-term to long-term memory? One important way is repetition. When you study the same words or facts many times, your brain works to store them more deeply. Another way is to connect new information with things you already know. For example, if you learn that "hippopotamus" (河马) means a large animal that lives in water, you might remember it better by linking it to the picture of a big, grey animal in a river.

④ Sleep also plays an important role in memory. When you sleep, your brain does not rest completely. It works to organize and strengthen (加强) the memories you made during the day. That is why getting enough sleep is not only good for your health but also helps you remember what you learned at school.

⑤ Although memory is amazing, it is not perfect. Sometimes we forget things because we did not pay enough attention, or because we have not used the information for a long time. Understanding how memory works can help us learn more effectively and keep our minds sharp.

**注释：** store 储存；strengthen 加强；hippopotamus 河马

### 选择题

1. According to the passage, what is short-term memory used for?
   A. Storing childhood memories for many years.

   A. To explain that sleep helps the brain organize and strengthen memories.
   B. To show that sleeping too much can hurt memory.

4. What can we infer from the passage about memory?
   A. Memory never changes once something is stored.
   B. Everyone has the same type of memory.
   C. Long-term memory is always more useful than short-term memory.

5. What is the main purpose of this passage?
   A. To tell funny stories about forgetting things.
   B. To explain how human memory works and how we can use it better.

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "Short-term memory holds information for only a short time... For example, when you look up a phone number and dial it, you use short-term memory."

**【解析】** 原文明确提到短期记忆仅短暂保存信息，并举了查电话号码并拨号为例。C 项正确。A 项对应长期记忆；B 项睡眠、D 项新旧知识连接均与短期记忆无关。故选 C。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "One important way is repetition... Another way is to connect new information with things you already know."

**【解析】** 信息从短期转为长期记忆的途径：一是重复（repetition），二是将新信息与已知事物联系。B 项完整概括。A 项"仅靠睡眠"过于片面；C 项"先忘不重要的事"未提及；D 项"每天学十小时"无依据。故选 B。

**3. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "When you sleep, your brain... works to organize and strengthen the memories you made during the day."

**【解析】** 睡眠时大脑会整理并强化当日形成的记忆。A 项正确。B 项"睡眠过多损害记忆"与原文相反；C 项"睡前不能学习"未提及；D 项"课上睡觉"为错误建议。故选 A。

**4. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "Sometimes we forget things because we did not pay enough attention, or because we have not used the information for a long time."

**【解析】** 反推可知：集中注意力、经常使用信息有助于记忆。D 项正确。A 项"记忆一旦存入永不改变"与"forget"矛盾；B 项"人人记忆类型相同"未提及；C 项"长期记忆总更有用"过于绝对。故选 D。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 主旨理解

**【定位】** 全文，尤其第①段 "how human memory works" 和第⑤段 "Understanding how memory works can help us learn more effectively."

**【解析】** 文章介绍记忆如何运作（类型、转化方式、睡眠作用、遗忘原因），并指出理解这些有助于更有效学习。B 项准确概括。A 项"有趣遗忘故事"非主旨；C 项"与动物记忆比较"未涉及；D 项"劝人多睡"仅为部分内容。故选 B。
