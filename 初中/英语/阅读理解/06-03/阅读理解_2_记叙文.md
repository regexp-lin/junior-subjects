# 阅读理解（记叙文）

① When Mei was twelve, her teacher asked her to join the school calligraphy (书法) competition. Mei had never tried Chinese calligraphy before. She thought it was old-fashioned and boring. "How can writing with a brush be interesting?" she wondered. But she did not want to disappoint her teacher, so she agreed.

② For the first few weeks, Mei found it difficult. Her hands shook when she held the brush. The ink spread (扩散) on the paper and her characters looked messy. She almost gave up. Then, one afternoon, her grandmother came to visit. She saw Mei practising and sat down beside her. "Let me show you," Grandma said softly. She took the brush and wrote the character "永" (yǒng, meaning "forever") with smooth, steady strokes (笔划). Mei watched in surprise. Each stroke had its own beauty — thick or thin, strong or light.

③ "Calligraphy is not just writing," Grandma said. "It is a way to feel peace and connect with our culture. For thousands of years, Chinese people have used it to express their thoughts and feelings." Mei began to understand. She practised every day after that. Slowly, her hands became steadier and her characters became clearer.

④ On the day of the competition, Mei felt nervous. She chose to write a famous poem about spring. As she moved the brush across the paper, she forgot about the judges and the other students. She only focused on each stroke, each character. When she finished, she looked at her work and felt proud. She did not win first place, but she did not mind. Something had changed inside her.

⑤ Now Mei loves calligraphy. She has learned that traditional culture is not boring — it is full of beauty and meaning. She hopes to pass this love to more young people one day.

**注释：** calligraphy 书法；spread 扩散；stroke 笔划

---

### 选择题

1. Why did Mei agree to join the calligraphy competition at first?
   A. Because she was very interested in calligraphy.
   B. Because her grandmother asked her to.
   C. Because she wanted to win a prize.
   D. Because she did not want to disappoint her teacher.

2. What happened when Mei first started learning calligraphy?
   A. She learned it very quickly and easily.
   B. Her hands shook, the ink spread, and her characters looked messy.
   C. Her grandmother taught her from the very beginning.
   D. She won the competition immediately.

3. What did Mei's grandmother help her understand?
   A. Calligraphy is only about writing characters correctly.
   B. Mei should give up because it was too hard.
   C. Calligraphy is a way to feel peace and connect with Chinese culture.
   D. Calligraphy was only for old people.

4. What can we infer from paragraph ④ about Mei's feelings during the competition?
   A. She focused on her writing and felt calm and proud when she finished.
   B. She was too nervous to write anything.
   C. She was angry because she did not win first place.
   D. She forgot the poem and could not finish.

5. What does the passage mainly want to tell us?
   A. A girl discovered the beauty of traditional culture through calligraphy and grew to love it.
   B. Calligraphy is the most important subject in school.
   C. Grandmothers are the best teachers.
   D. Everyone should win first place in competitions.

---

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "But she did not want to disappoint her teacher, so she agreed."

**【解析】** 原文明确写出 Mei 起初认为书法古板无聊，但不想让老师失望而同意参加。D 项正确。A 项"很感兴趣"与"thought it was old-fashioned and boring"矛盾；B 项"奶奶要求"此时奶奶未出现；C 项"想获奖"未提及。故选 D。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "Her hands shook when she held the brush. The ink spread on the paper and her characters looked messy."

**【解析】** 初学时 Mei 握笔手抖、墨迹扩散、字迹凌乱。B 项正确。A 项"学得很快"与"almost gave up"矛盾；C 项奶奶在"one afternoon"才出现；D 项"立刻获奖"与最终"did not win first place"矛盾。故选 B。

**3. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "Calligraphy is not just writing... It is a way to feel peace and connect with our culture."

**【解析】** 奶奶告诉 Mei 书法不仅是写字，更是感受内心平静、联结文化的方式。C 项正确。A 项"仅正确书写"与"not just writing"矛盾；B 项"应放弃"与奶奶示范、鼓励矛盾；D 项"只适合老人"未提及。故选 C。

**4. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第④段 "As she moved the brush... she forgot about the judges and the other students. She only focused on each stroke... When she finished, she looked at her work and felt proud."

**【解析】** 写作时她忘掉评判与旁人，只专注于每一笔，完成后感到骄傲，可推知她在过程中投入且平和，完成后满足。A 项正确。B 项"紧张到写不出"与"she finished"矛盾；C 项"因未得第一而生气"与"she did not mind"矛盾；D 项"忘词未完成"无依据。故选 A。

**5. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 主旨理解

**【定位】** 全文：从最初不感兴趣→奶奶引导→理解文化内涵→比赛后内心改变→如今热爱并希望传承。

**【解析】** 文章讲述女孩 Mei 通过书法比赛从抗拒到理解、最终爱上传统文化的成长过程。A 项准确概括主旨。B 项"最重要学科"过度推断；C 项"奶奶最佳老师"仅为情节，非主旨；D 项"人人都该夺冠"与文中"did not mind"不符。故选 A。
