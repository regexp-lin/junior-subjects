# 阅读理解（说明文）

① Dolphins are one of the smartest animals in the sea. They have large brains and can solve problems. Scientists have studied dolphins for many years and found that they can understand signs and follow simple instructions. Some dolphins have even learned to use tools. For example, they may use a piece of seaweed to protect their noses when looking for food on the ocean floor.

② Dolphins are also very social. They live in groups called pods. A pod may have from a few dolphins to hundreds. In the pod, dolphins work together. They hunt for fish together and share food. When a dolphin is sick or hurt, others in the pod will stay nearby and help it. They take turns to push the sick dolphin up to the surface so it can breathe. This shows that dolphins care about each other.

③ Dolphins communicate in many ways. They make clicks and whistles. Each dolphin has its own "signature whistle" — a sound that is unique, like a name. When two dolphins meet, they may use these whistles to say hello. Scientists believe that dolphins can also pass information to each other about food, danger, or their feelings.

④ Young dolphins learn from older ones. A baby dolphin stays close to its mother for several years. During this time, the mother teaches it how to hunt, how to avoid danger, and how to get along with other dolphins. Older dolphins in the pod also help to look after the young. This kind of learning and teaching is similar to what humans do in families.

⑤ Dolphins are friendly to humans too. They often swim near boats and sometimes help fishermen by driving fish toward their nets. However, dolphins need a clean and safe ocean. Pollution and fishing nets can hurt them. We should protect dolphins and their home so that future generations can still enjoy these wonderful creatures.

**注释：** pod 群体；signature 独特的；whistle 哨声；net 渔网

### 选择题

1. According to paragraph ①, what can dolphins do?
   A. They can understand signs, follow instructions, and some use tools.

   A. They teach humans how to swim.
   B. They never need to learn anything.
   C. They learn hunting, avoiding danger, and social skills from older dolphins.

5. What does the writer want to tell us in the last paragraph?
   A. Dolphins are dangerous to humans.
   B. We should protect dolphins and the ocean for the future.

   D. Dolphins only live in freshwater.

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "they can understand signs and follow simple instructions... Some dolphins have even learned to use tools."

**【解析】** 原文说明海豚能理解符号、遵循简单指令，部分海豚还会使用工具。A 项正确。B 项 "仅游泳快" 片面；C 项 "从不与人合作" 与第⑤段矛盾；D 项 "深海中独居" 与第②段群居不符。故选 A。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "When a dolphin is sick or hurt, others in the pod will stay nearby and help it. They take turns to push the sick dolphin up to the surface so it can breathe."

**【解析】** 原文写明海豚会留在患病同伴身旁，轮流将其顶出水面以助其呼吸。B 项正确。A 项 "弃之而去" 与原文相反；C 项 "大声呼救" 和 D 项 "每日送食" 文中未提及。故选 B。

**3. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解 / 词义理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "Each dolphin has its own 'signature whistle' — a sound that is unique, like a name."

**【解析】** 原文解释 signature whistle 为每只海豚独有的声音，如同名字。C 项正确。A 项 "仅人类能听" 与文意不符；B 项 "捕猎工具"、D 项 "一种鱼" 均错误。故选 C。

**4. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第④段 "the mother teaches it how to hunt, how to avoid danger, and how to get along with other dolphins. Older dolphins in the pod also help to look after the young."

**【解析】** 幼豚从母亲和群体长者处学习捕猎、避险和社交技能，可推知 C 项。A 项 "教人类游泳" 文中未提及；B 项 "无需学习" 错误；D 项 "出生即离母" 与 "stays close for several years" 矛盾。故选 C。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 写作意图 / 主旨理解

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "Dolphins need a clean and safe ocean... We should protect dolphins and their home so that future generations can still enjoy these wonderful creatures."

**【解析】** 末段呼吁保护海豚及其海洋家园，让后代仍能欣赏它们，B 项正确。A 项 "危险" 与 friendly 矛盾；C 项 "渔网从不伤害" 与 can hurt them 矛盾；D 项 "仅淡水" 与 ocean 矛盾。故选 B。
