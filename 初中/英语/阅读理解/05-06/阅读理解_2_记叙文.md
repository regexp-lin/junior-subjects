> **05-06 第2篇**

# 阅读理解（记叙文）

① Jason was the fastest runner in the 8th grade. He always wore his expensive, bright red running shoes, which he called his "lucky shoes". Every time there was a race, Jason would win easily. Because of this, he became a bit boastful (爱吹牛的). "Nobody can beat me when I'm wearing my lucky shoes!" he would tell anyone who listened.

② The school sports meeting was coming up in two weeks. A new student named David had just transferred (转学) to their class. David was quiet and a bit clumsy (笨拙的). He wore old, plain sneakers. When the teacher asked who wanted to join the 100-meter dash, both Jason and David raised their hands. 

③ Jason laughed when he saw David's hand. "Are you sure, new kid? You can't run fast in those old boats," he said, pointing at David's shoes. David just smiled politely and said, "I like running. Shoes don't make the runner."

④ On the day of the race, Jason couldn't find his red lucky shoes anywhere in his locker. He panicked (惊慌). The race was about to start, so he had to borrow a pair of plain white sneakers from the lost-and-found box. They were a little too tight. 

⑤ Standing at the starting line, Jason felt terrible. Without his lucky shoes, he felt slow and heavy. *Bang!* The starting gun fired. Jason ran as hard as he could, but his mind was full of worry. To everyone's surprise, it was David who crossed the finish line first, running gracefully (优雅地) despite his old shoes. Jason came in second.

⑥ Breathing heavily, Jason walked over to David. He expected the new boy to make fun of him, but David just offered him a bottle of water. "Good race, Jason," David said sincerely. Jason looked at David's old shoes, then at his own borrowed ones. He realized David was right: the magic wasn't in the shoes, but in the effort and the heart of the runner.

**注释：** boastful 爱吹牛的；transfer 转学；clumsy 笨拙的；panic 惊慌；gracefully 优雅地；sincerely 真诚地

---

### 选择题

1. Why did Jason become boastful according to paragraph ①?
A. Because he bought the most expensive schoolbag.
B. Because he always won races easily and believed his red shoes were "lucky".
C. Because his father was the school principal.
D. Because he was the tallest boy in the 8th grade.

2. What did Jason think about David's shoes?
A. He thought they were very fashionable and cool.
B. He thought they were magical shoes that could help David fly.
C. He laughed at them, calling them "old boats" that couldn't run fast.
D. He wanted to buy a pair for himself.

3. What happened to Jason on the day of the race?
A. He forgot the time and missed the race completely.
B. He ate too much and couldn't run.
C. He panicked because he couldn't find his lucky red shoes.
D. He broke his leg while warming up.

4. How did Jason feel during the race without his lucky shoes?
A. He felt faster than ever before.
B. He felt terrible, slow, heavy, and full of worry.
C. He felt extremely happy and relaxed.
D. He felt sleepy and wanted to lie down on the track.

5. What important lesson did Jason learn at the end of the story?
A. That he should always steal his competitors' shoes.
B. That the magic is not in the shoes, but in the runner's effort and heart.
C. That red shoes are slower than white sneakers.
D. That David was secretly a professional running coach.

---

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节推断理解

**【定位】** 第①段第3-5句 "Every time there was a race, Jason would win easily. Because of this, he became a bit boastful (爱吹牛的)... 'Nobody can beat me when I'm wearing my lucky shoes!'"

**【解析】** 杰森为什么会变得爱吹牛？由于他每次跑步都会轻松获胜，他便认为那双显眼的红跑鞋是他的“幸运鞋”，只要穿上它无人能敌。B选项恰如其分地总结了这点。A昂贵书包、C老爸是校长、D最高，均属无稽之谈。故选B。

**2. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段第1-2句 "Jason laughed when he saw David's hand... 'You can't run fast in those old boats,' he said, pointing at David's shoes."

**【解析】** 看到大卫也要参赛，杰森对他的鞋有何看法？文中写道，杰森嘲笑大卫，指着他的鞋说“你穿那种旧破船是跑不快的”。C项完美转述了嘲弄的情境（ laughed at them, calling them 'old boats' ）。A时尚、B魔法鞋、D想买，全非文中情节。故选C。

**3. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段前两句 "On the day of the race, Jason couldn't find his red lucky shoes anywhere in his locker. He panicked (惊慌)."

**【解析】** 到了比赛那天杰森遭遇了什么状况？由于在柜子里怎么也找不到他的红色幸运鞋，于是他惊慌失措（panicked）。这与C选项的内容丝毫不差。A错失比赛、B吃撑、D摔断腿都没有出现。故选C。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第⑤段第1、2、4句 "Standing at the starting line, Jason felt terrible. Without his lucky shoes, he felt slow and heavy... his mind was full of worry."

**【解析】** 没穿幸运鞋参加比赛时杰森感觉如何？文中细致描写了他感觉很糟，觉得自己又慢又沉重，满脑子都是担忧（terrible, slow, heavy, and full of worry）。选项B完全重合。选项A（比以前更快）和选项C（极度高兴放松）都是反向描述，D项想睡觉也是错的。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 主旨意图归纳

**【定位】** 故事结尾第⑥段最后一句 "He realized David was right: the magic wasn't in the shoes, but in the effort and the heart of the runner."

**【解析】** 题目问杰森最终学到了什么重要的一课？末尾点明主题：他意识到大卫是对的，所谓的魔法并不在鞋子上，而是在于奔跑者的努力与内心（the magic wasn't in the shoes, but in the effort and the heart of the runner）。B项准确提炼出中心升华。故选B。
