# 阅读理解（说明文）

① Have you ever looked at a map and noticed how the coastlines (海岸线) of South America and Africa look like two puzzle pieces that could fit together? For a long time, people thought this was just a funny coincidence (巧合). But in 1912, a German scientist named Alfred Wegener proposed a bold (大胆的) idea: the continents (大陆) were actually moving!

② Wegener called his idea "continental drift" (大陆漂移说). He suggested that hundreds of millions of years ago, all the land on Earth was joined together in one supercontinent called Pangaea (盘古大陆). Over time, this giant landmass broke apart, and the pieces slowly drifted away from each other to where they are today.

③ To prove his theory, Wegener presented several pieces of evidence (证据). First, as mentioned, the shapes of the continents seem to match. Second, scientists found the exact same kinds of ancient plant and animal fossils (化石) on both the eastern coast of South America and the western coast of Africa. These animals could not have swum across the huge Atlantic Ocean. Third, similar rock formations and mountains were found on different continents separated by water.

④ However, when Wegener first shared his idea, most scientists laughed at him. They asked, "How can giant rock continents move through the solid ocean floor? What force is strong enough to push them?" Unfortunately, Wegener could not explain the "how," and he died in 1930 without seeing his theory accepted.

⑤ It wasn't until the 1960s, with the help of new technology like sonar (声纳), that scientists discovered how the continents moved. They found that the Earth's outer shell is broken into large "plates" (板块) that float on hot, melted rock below. Wegener's crazy idea turned out to be right! Today, the movement of these tectonic plates (构造板块) explains not only continental drift, but also why earthquakes and volcanoes happen.

**注释：** coastline 海岸线；coincidence 巧合；continent 大陆；drift 漂移；fossil 化石；plate 板块

### 选择题

1. What did people originally think about the matching shapes of South America and Africa?
A. They thought someone had cut them with a giant knife.
B. They thought it was just a funny coincidence.
C. They thought aliens had moved the land.
D. They thought Alfred Wegener drew the map incorrectly.

2. What was Wegener's idea of "Pangaea" in paragraph ②?
A. A new kind of fast-moving ship.
B. A deep ocean that covered the whole Earth.
C. A German scientist who studied fossils.
D. One supercontinent where all the land was joined together.

3. Which of the following is NOT a piece of evidence used by Wegener?
A. The shapes of the continents matching like puzzle pieces.
B. Finding the same animal fossils on opposite sides of the ocean.
C. Similar rock formations on different continents.
D. Finding identical (完全相同的) modern human cities in Africa and South America.

4. Why did most scientists laugh at Wegener's theory at first?
A. Because he drew very ugly maps.
B. Because he could not explain what strong force pushed the continents.
C. Because they found out he made up the fossil evidence.
D. Because he said the Earth was flat.

5. How did scientists finally prove that Wegener was right in the 1960s?
A. They discovered that the Earth's shell is broken into plates that float and move.
B. They tied a rope between South America and Africa and pulled them.
C. They found Wegener's secret diary hidden in an old library.
D. They watched the continents move very fast with their naked eyes.

---

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段第2句 "For a long time, people thought this was just a funny coincidence (巧合)."

**【解析】** 开头提出南美洲和非洲海岸线像拼图可以拼一起，人们起初是怎么认为的呢？原文写道，很长一段时间里人们认为这只是一个有趣的巧合（a funny coincidence）。A巨刀切开、C外星人移动、D威格纳画错均非原文内容。故选B。

**2. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "...all the land on Earth was joined together in one supercontinent called Pangaea (盘古大陆)."

**【解析】** 魏格纳关于“盘古大陆（Pangaea）”的观点是指什么？原文解释说，几亿年前地球上所有的陆地都连在一起形成了一个超级大陆，名为盘古大陆。D项完整重现了这句话的意思。A快船、B深海、C德国科学家皆为混淆项。故选D。

**3. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 细节理解与判断

**【定位】** 第③段列举的三个证据 "First... shapes of the continents seem to match. Second... same kinds of ancient plant and animal fossils... Third, similar rock formations and mountains..."

**【解析】** 题干要求选出哪个不是魏格纳提出的证据。匹配的海岸线形状（A项）、隔海相望的同种动植物化石（B项）、不同大陆相似的岩层（C项）都在文中悉数列出。而D项“在非洲和南美洲发现完全相同的现代人类城市”显然是错误选项，文中未提及且不符合历史事实。故选D。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节推断理解

**【定位】** 第④段第1-3句 "...most scientists laughed at him. They asked... 'What force is strong enough to push them?' Unfortunately, Wegener could not explain the 'how'..."

**【解析】** 为什么起初多数科学家嘲笑他的理论？文章给出了原因：科学家们质疑巨大的岩石大陆何以在海底移动以及何种力量推得动。遗憾的是，魏格纳无法解释这个“怎么动（how）”的动力来源。这与选项B（因为他无法解释是什么强力推动了大陆）一致。故选B。

**5. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第⑤段第2句 "They found that the Earth's outer shell is broken into large 'plates' (板块) that float on hot, melted rock below. Wegener's crazy idea turned out to be right!"

**【解析】** 到了20世纪60年代，科学家是如何最终证实魏格纳是对的？文中指出这是因为他们发现地球的外壳分裂成了大板块（plates），这些板块漂浮在下方炽热熔化的岩石上移动。这正好是A选项的内容。B用绳拉、C找日记、D肉眼看均荒谬不经。故选A。
