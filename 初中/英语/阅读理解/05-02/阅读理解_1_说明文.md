# 阅读理解（说明文）

① Have you ever wondered how bees make honey? It is a fascinating process that involves teamwork and a lot of hard work. A single honey bee will never make enough honey for itself. Instead, thousands of bees work together in a colony (蜂群) to produce the sweet, golden liquid we love.

② The process starts with flowers. Worker bees, which are all female, fly out of their hive (蜂巢) to search for flowers. When a bee finds a good flower, it uses its long, tube-like tongue to suck up a sweet juice called nectar (花蜜). The bee stores the nectar in a special "honey stomach," which is different from its real stomach used for digesting food. 

③ While flying back to the hive, the nectar in the bee's honey stomach mixes with special enzymes (酶). These enzymes start breaking down the complex sugars in the nectar into simpler sugars. This is the first step of turning nectar into honey.

④ Once the worker bee returns to the hive, she passes the nectar to other house bees. These house bees chew the nectar for about 30 minutes, adding more enzymes. Then, they drop the nectar into the honeycomb (蜂窝), which is made of wax. At this point, the nectar is still very watery. To make it thick, the bees use their wings to fan (扇风) the honeycomb. This fanning helps the water evaporate (蒸发).

⑤ When most of the water is gone and the honey is thick and sticky, the bees seal (密封) the honeycomb with a wax cap. The honey is now safe and will not go bad. It is stored as food for the colony to eat during the cold winter months when there are no flowers. Next time you eat a spoonful of honey, remember the incredible journey it took!

**注释：** colony 蜂群；nectar 花蜜；enzyme 酶；honeycomb 蜂窝；evaporate 蒸发；seal 密封

### 选择题

1. Who does the job of flying out to search for flowers according to paragraph ②?
A. The male bees.
B. The queen bee.
C. The female worker bees.
D. The house bees.

2. How does a bee carry the nectar back to the hive?
A. In its mouth.
B. In a special "honey stomach".
C. On its wings.
D. In its real stomach used for food.

3. Why do bees use their wings to fan the honeycomb in paragraph ④?
A. To keep themselves warm.
B. To blow the dust away.
C. To help the water in the nectar evaporate.
D. To tell other bees where the flowers are.

4. What do the bees do when the honey is thick and sticky enough?
A. They eat it all immediately.
B. They seal the honeycomb with a wax cap.
C. They throw it out of the hive.
D. They mix it with more water.

5. Why do bees make and store honey?
A. To sell it to humans for money.
B. To feed the young bees in the spring.
C. To attract more flowers to grow near the hive.
D. To have food during the cold winter when there are no flowers.

---

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段第2句 "Worker bees, which are all female, fly out of their hive (蜂巢) to search for flowers."

**【解析】** 原文明示，飞出蜂巢去寻找花朵的是工蜂（Worker bees），并且作者特意补充说明了它们全都是雌性（which are all female）。因此，出去寻花的是雌性工蜂。A雄蜂、B蜂王、D内勤蜂均不符。故选C。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段最后一句 "The bee stores the nectar in a special 'honey stomach,' which is different from its real stomach used for digesting food."

**【解析】** 蜜蜂用什么把花蜜运回蜂巢？文中写道，蜜蜂将花蜜储存在一个特殊的“蜜胃（honey stomach）”里，这个胃和它们用来消化食物的真胃是不同的。由此可见是装在特殊的蜜胃中。故选B。

**3. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段最后两句 "To make it thick, the bees use their wings to fan (扇风) the honeycomb. This fanning helps the water evaporate (蒸发)."

**【解析】** 本段解释道：这时候的花蜜水分还很大，为了让水分变稠，蜜蜂会用翅膀给蜂窝扇风。这种扇风有助于水分蒸发（helps the water evaporate）。A取暖、B吹灰、D报信都不是扇风使蜜变稠的目的。故选C。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第⑤段首句 "When most of the water is gone and the honey is thick and sticky, the bees seal (密封) the honeycomb with a wax cap."

**【解析】** 当蜂蜜变得粘稠之后，蜜蜂会怎么做？它们会用蜡做成的小盖子将蜂窝密封起来（seal the honeycomb with a wax cap）。A马上吃掉、C扔出蜂巢、D加水都是不对的。故选B。

**5. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第⑤段第3句 "It is stored as food for the colony to eat during the cold winter months when there are no flowers."

**【解析】** 蜜蜂辛勤酿蜜储存的最终目的是什么？文中清楚地说，它是作为蜂群的食物储存起来，以便在没有花朵的寒冷冬季（during the cold winter months when there are no flowers）食用。这与D项内容完全一致。故选D。
