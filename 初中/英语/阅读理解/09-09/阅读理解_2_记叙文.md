# 阅读理解（记叙文）

① Mike and Tom had been best friends since primary school. They played basketball together, shared lunch, and helped each other with homework. But last month, they had a big fight. It started over a silly thing: Mike thought Tom had taken his favorite pen without asking. Tom said he had never seen it. Both of them got angry and said things they did not mean. After that, they did not talk to each other for two weeks.

② Mike felt terrible. He missed his friend. He began to think that maybe he had made a mistake. Perhaps the pen was in his bag all along, or he had lost it somewhere else. One day, he found the pen under his bed. He felt so sorry. He wanted to say sorry to Tom, but he did not know how to start.

③ On Friday, Mike saw Tom alone in the classroom. He took a deep breath and walked over. "Tom, I am really sorry," Mike said. "I found my pen under my bed. I was wrong to blame you. Can we be friends again?" Tom looked at Mike for a few seconds. Then he smiled. "I was wrong too," Tom said. "I should have stayed calm and talked to you. I have missed you, Mike." The two boys shook hands and hugged.

④ That afternoon, they played basketball again like before. Nothing had really changed, but something had. Their friendship had become stronger because they had learned to apologize and forgive. Sometimes a fight can teach us important lessons about friendship.

⑤ Now Mike and Tom both know: true friends are not those who never argue, but those who can say "I am sorry" and "I forgive you."

---

**Questions:**

1. What caused the fight between Mike and Tom?
   A. A basketball game.
   B. Mike thought Tom had taken his pen without asking.
   C. They had different ideas about homework.
   D. Tom did not want to share lunch.

2. How did Mike find out the truth about the pen?
   A. Tom told him where it was.
   B. He found it under his bed.
   C. His teacher found it for him.
   D. He bought a new one and forgot the old one.

3. What did Mike do to make up with Tom?
   A. He wrote Tom a letter.
   B. He asked the teacher to help.
   C. He went to Tom and said sorry, explaining that he had found the pen.
   D. He waited for Tom to say sorry first.

4. What can we infer about Tom from Paragraph ③?
   A. He was still very angry with Mike.
   B. He was also willing to apologize and wanted to be friends again.
   C. He did not care about the friendship.
   D. He thought Mike was lying.

5. What is the main message of the story?
   A. Never lend your pens to friends.
   B. True friends can apologize and forgive, which makes friendship stronger.
   C. Fights always end friendships.
   D. It is better to have no friends than to have fights.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段第4句 "Mike thought Tom had taken his favorite pen without asking."

**【解析】** 原文明确说明争吵的起因是Mike认为Tom未经允许拿了他的钢笔。B项准确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段第3句 "One day, he found the pen under his bed."

**【解析】** 原文说明Mike某天在床下找到了钢笔。B项正确。A、C、D项均与原文不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "He took a deep breath and walked over. 'Tom, I am really sorry,' Mike said. 'I found my pen under my bed. I was wrong to blame you. Can we be friends again?'"

**【解析】** Mike主动走向Tom道歉，说明找到钢笔并承认自己错怪了Tom，请求和好。C项准确。A、B、D项与原文不符。故选C。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第③段 Tom的回答 "I was wrong too... I should have stayed calm and talked to you. I have missed you, Mike." 以及 "The two boys shook hands and hugged."

**【解析】** Tom承认自己也有错、表示想念Mike，并与Mike握手拥抱，可推知Tom愿意道歉并希望重归于好。A、C、D项与文中态度相反。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 主旨大意

**【定位】** 第④段 "Their friendship had become stronger because they had learned to apologize and forgive" 及第⑤段 "true friends are not those who never argue, but those who can say 'I am sorry' and 'I forgive you.'"

**【解析】** 文章强调学会道歉与谅解能使友谊更牢固，真正的好朋友是能说"对不起"和"我原谅你"的人。B项准确概括主旨。A、C、D项均偏离主题。故选B。
