# 阅读理解（记叙文）

**Working with Classmates on a Science Experiment**

① Last term, our science teacher gave us a group project: design an experiment about plants. We had to work in teams of four. I was put in a group with Lin, Wang, and Chen. At first, I worried. Would we get along? Could we finish on time?

② We met after school to plan. Lin suggested we test whether music affects plant growth. "Some people say plants grow better with classical music," she said. Wang liked the idea. Chen and I agreed. We divided the work. Lin would bring the plants. Wang would play the music. Chen would measure the plants every day. I would record the data and write the report.

③ For two weeks, we cared for three small bean plants. One sat in silence. One listened to classical music for two hours a day. One listened to rock music. We watered them the same amount and placed them in the same spot. Every day, Chen measured their height. I wrote the numbers in a table.

④ At the end, we looked at the results. The plant with classical music had grown a little taller. The one with rock music was a bit smaller. The silent one was in the middle. We were not sure if it was the music or just chance. But we had collected real data and worked as a team. Our teacher said, "Good job. You learned how to do an experiment and how to work together."

⑤ That project taught me that science is not just about books. It is about asking questions, trying things, and learning from others. Working with my classmates made the task fun and the result better than any of us could have done alone.

---

**Questions:**

1. What was the group's science project about?
   A. Testing animals.
   B. Testing whether music affects plant growth.
   C. Building a robot.
   D. Studying the weather.

2. Who measured the plants every day?
   A. Lin.
   B. Wang.
   C. Chen.
   D. The writer.

3. What was the result of the experiment?
   A. All plants grew the same.
   B. The plant with classical music grew a little taller; the one with rock music was smaller.
   C. The rock music plant grew fastest.
   D. All plants died.

4. What did the writer do in the group?
   A. Brought the plants.
   B. Recorded the data and wrote the report.
   C. Played the music.
   D. Measured the plants.

5. What did the writer learn from this experience?
   A. Science is only in books.
   B. Science involves asking questions, trying things, and teamwork.
   C. Working alone is always better.
   D. Music has no effect on plants.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第②段 "test whether music affects plant growth"
**【解析】** 实验目的是测试音乐对植物生长的影响。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】C**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第②段 "Chen would measure the plants every day"
**【解析】** 陈负责每天测量植物。C项正确。A、B、D项与原文不符。故选C。

**3. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第④段 "The plant with classical music had grown a little taller. The one with rock music was a bit smaller"

**【解析】** 古典音乐组植物略高，摇滚乐组略矮。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第②段 "I would record the data and write the report"
**【解析】** 作者负责记录数据并撰写报告。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 推理判断
**【定位】** 第⑤段 "science is not just about books. It is about asking questions, trying things, and learning from others"
**【解析】** 作者体会到科学需要提问、实践和合作。B项正确。A、C、D项与文意不符。故选B。
