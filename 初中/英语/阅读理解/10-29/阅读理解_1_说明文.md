# 阅读理解（说明文）

**Bacteria and Humans: A Complicated Relationship**

① When we hear the word "bacteria," we often think of germs that make us sick. It is true that some bacteria cause diseases like sore throats and food poisoning. But not all bacteria are harmful. In fact, we could not live without many of them.

② Our bodies are full of bacteria. There are more bacterial cells in your body than human cells. Most of them live in our gut, helping us digest food. They break down what we eat and produce vitamins that we need. Some bacteria on our skin protect us from harmful germs. So, in many ways, bacteria are our friends.

③ Bacteria also play a big role in the environment. They help break down dead plants and animals. This returns nutrients to the soil so that new plants can grow. Without bacteria, the world would be full of waste. Some bacteria can even clean up oil spills or turn waste into useful products.

④ However, some bacteria are dangerous. They can cause infections when they get into cuts or when we eat bad food. To fight them, we use antibiotics. But we must use antibiotics wisely. If we use them too often, bacteria can become resistant. Then the medicine stops working. That is why doctors say we should only take antibiotics when necessary.

⑤ So, bacteria and humans have a complicated relationship. Some help us; some harm us. The key is to understand them better and use that knowledge to stay healthy and protect the environment.

**注释：** gut 肠道；antibiotic 抗生素；resistant 有抗药性的

---

**Questions:**

1. According to Paragraph ②, what do bacteria in our gut do?
   A. They make us sick.
   B. They help digest food and produce vitamins.
   C. They cause sore throats.
   D. They kill all germs.

2. How do bacteria help the environment?
   A. They make oil spills worse.
   B. They break down dead matter and return nutrients to the soil.
   C. They produce harmful waste.
   D. They destroy all plants.

3. Why should we use antibiotics wisely?
   A. Because they are too expensive.
   B. Because overuse can make bacteria resistant, and the medicine may stop working.
   C. Because they are useless.
   D. Because they taste bad.

4. What does "resistant" mean in Paragraph ④?
   A. Friendly.
   B. No longer affected by the medicine.
   C. Weak.
   D. Helpful.

5. What can we infer from the passage?
   A. All bacteria are bad.
   B. Bacteria can be both helpful and harmful; we need to understand and use that knowledge.
   C. We should avoid all bacteria.
   D. Bacteria are only useful in hospitals.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第②段 "help us digest food... They break down what we eat and produce vitamins"
**【解析】** 肠道细菌帮助消化并产生维生素。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第③段 "break down dead plants and animals... returns nutrients to the soil"
**【解析】** 细菌分解动植物的遗体，将养分归还土壤。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第④段 "bacteria can become resistant. Then the medicine stops working"
**【解析】** 过度使用抗生素会使细菌产生抗药性，药物失效。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 词义猜测
**【定位】** 第④段 "Then the medicine stops working"
**【解析】** 上下文指细菌不再受药物影响。B项准确。A、C、D项与文意不符。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 推理判断
**【定位】** 全文，尤其第⑤段 "Some help us; some harm us. The key is to understand them better"
**【解析】** 细菌既有利也有害，需理解并合理利用。B项正确。A、C、D项与文意不符。故选B。
