> **09-03 第2篇**

# 阅读理解（记叙文）

① One morning, I took the bus to school as usual. The bus was crowded. I found a seat near the back and sat down. After two stops, an old woman with a walking stick got on. She looked around for a seat, but all the seats were taken. She stood in the aisle (过道), holding the handrail (扶手) tightly. The bus started to move, and she swayed (摇晃) a little.

② I thought about giving her my seat. But I had a heavy schoolbag and I was tired from staying up late to finish homework. "Someone else will do it," I said to myself. A few minutes passed. No one stood up. The old woman still stood there, and she looked uncomfortable. A young man next to her was playing on his phone and did not seem to notice. A woman with a child was also sitting quietly. I felt ashamed of myself.

③ I stood up and walked to the old woman. "Please take my seat, Grandma," I said. She looked surprised at first, then smiled. "Thank you, young man. You are very kind." She sat down slowly and put her bag on her lap. I stood beside her, holding the handrail. My schoolbag was heavy, but I did not mind. I felt good inside.

④ At the next stop, the old woman stood up to get off. Before she left, she patted (轻拍) my arm and said, "Thank you again. Your parents have raised a good child." Her words made me blush (脸红), but I was happy. When she got off, I sat back down. A girl across from me gave me a thumbs-up and smiled. I smiled back.

⑤ That day, I learned that doing the right thing is not always easy. Sometimes we make excuses — we are tired, we are busy, we hope someone else will act. But a small act of kindness, like giving up a seat, can make a big difference to someone in need. Since that day, I have tried to offer my seat whenever I see elderly people or pregnant women on the bus. It is a simple habit that costs nothing but means a lot.

**注释：** aisle 过道；handrail 扶手；sway 摇晃；pat 轻拍；blush 脸红

---

**Questions:**

1. Why did the old woman stand in the aisle on the bus?
   A. Because she preferred standing.
   B. Because all the seats were taken and she could not find one.
   C. Because she was getting off at the next stop.
   D. Because the bus driver asked her to stand.

2. Why did the writer hesitate at first before giving his seat?
   A. Because he did not see the old woman.
   B. Because he had a heavy schoolbag and was tired, and hoped someone else would do it.
   C. Because the old woman refused his help.
   D. Because his seat was broken.

3. What did the old woman do before she got off the bus?
   A. She gave the writer some money.
   B. She patted the writer's arm and thanked him again.
   C. She asked for the writer's phone number.
   D. She complained about the bus.

4. What can we infer from the girl's thumbs-up?
   A. She thought the writer was funny.
   B. She approved of the writer's kind act.
   C. She wanted to sit in the writer's seat.
   D. She knew the old woman.

5. What did the writer learn from this experience?
   A. Buses are always crowded.
   B. Doing the right thing is not always easy, but a small act of kindness can make a big difference.
   C. Old people should not take the bus.
   D. Schoolbags are too heavy.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "She looked around for a seat, but all the seats were taken. She stood in the aisle"

**【解析】** 原文明确说所有座位都有人，老太太找不到座位只能站在过道。A项"喜欢站着"、C项"下一站下车"、D项"司机要求"均与原文不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "I had a heavy schoolbag and I was tired from staying up late to finish homework. 'Someone else will do it,' I said to myself"

**【解析】** 作者因书包重、熬夜疲惫而犹豫，希望别人会让座。A项"没看见"与后文矛盾；C、D项原文未提及。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "Before she left, she patted my arm and said, 'Thank you again. Your parents have raised a good child.'"

**【解析】** 老太太下车前轻拍作者手臂并再次道谢。A项"给钱"、C项"要电话"、D项"抱怨"原文均无依据。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第④段 "A girl across from me gave me a thumbs-up and smiled"

**【解析】** 竖大拇指通常表示赞同、表扬，可推断女孩对作者的让座行为表示认可。A项"觉得好笑"、C项"想坐座位"、D项"认识老太太"均无依据。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 主旨大意/写作意图

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "I learned that doing the right thing is not always easy... But a small act of kindness, like giving up a seat, can make a big difference to someone in need"

**【解析】** 作者感悟：做对的事并不总是容易，但小小的善举对需要的人意义重大。A、C、D项仅为细节或无关内容。故选B。
