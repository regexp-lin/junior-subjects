# 阅读理解（说明文）

① Have you ever wondered why food tastes sweet, sour, salty, or bitter? The answer lies in your tongue. Your tongue is covered with tiny bumps (突起) called taste buds (味蕾). These small structures help you sense different flavors in the food you eat. Without them, eating would be much less enjoyable.

② Each taste bud contains special cells that can detect five basic tastes: sweet, sour, salty, bitter, and umami (鲜味). When you put food in your mouth, chemicals from the food touch these cells. The cells then send signals to your brain. Your brain tells you what flavor you are tasting. This whole process happens very quickly, usually in less than a second.

③ Taste buds are not only on your tongue. You can also find them on the roof of your mouth and in your throat. An adult has about 5,000 to 10,000 taste buds. However, they do not last forever. Every one to two weeks, old taste buds die and new ones grow. That is why your sense of taste can change as you get older.

④ Your nose also plays an important role in tasting. When you chew food, tiny particles (微粒) travel through the back of your mouth to your nose. Your nose detects smells and works together with your taste buds. That is why food seems to have no taste when you have a cold — your nose is blocked and cannot help your tongue.

⑤ Scientists are still learning about how taste works. Some people are "super tasters" who can taste things more strongly than others. Knowing how your tongue and taste buds function helps you understand why everyone has different food preferences. It also reminds us that our bodies are wonderfully made.

**注释：** taste bud 味蕾；umami 鲜味；particle 微粒；preference 偏好

### 选择题

1. According to paragraph ①, what helps you sense different flavors?
   A. Tiny bumps called taste buds on your tongue.
   B. Your nose only.
   C. Your eyes and ears.
   D. Your hands when touching food.

2. How many basic tastes can taste bud cells detect, according to paragraph ②?
   A. Three.
   B. Five.
   C. Four.
   D. Six.

3. What can we learn from paragraph ③ about taste buds?
   A. They only exist on the tongue and never change.
   B. Adults have fewer taste buds than children.
   C. They are found on the tongue, roof of the mouth, and throat; old ones die and new ones grow every one to two weeks.
   D. Taste buds last a lifetime.

4. Why does food seem to have no taste when you have a cold?
   A. Taste buds stop working completely.
   B. Your brain refuses to receive signals.
   C. All taste buds disappear.
   D. Your nose is blocked and cannot help your tongue detect flavors.

5. What is the main idea of this passage?
   A. The nose is more important than the tongue for tasting.
   B. Super tasters are better than ordinary people.
   C. How taste works and how taste buds help us enjoy food.
   D. Children have better taste than adults.

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "Your tongue is covered with tiny bumps (突起) called taste buds (味蕾). These small structures help you sense different flavors in the food you eat."

**【解析】** 原文明确说明舌头上覆盖着名为味蕾的小突起，这些结构帮助人们感知食物中的不同味道。A项正确。B项"只有鼻子"、C项"眼睛和耳朵"、D项"双手触摸"均与原文不符。故选A。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "Each taste bud contains special cells that can detect five basic tastes: sweet, sour, salty, bitter, and umami (鲜味)."

**【解析】** 原文清楚列出味蕾细胞可感知五种基本味道：甜、酸、咸、苦、鲜。B项"五种"正确。A、C、D项数量均错误。故选B。

**3. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第③段 "Taste buds are not only on your tongue. You can also find them on the roof of your mouth and in your throat... Every one to two weeks, old taste buds die and new ones grow."

**【解析】** 原文指出味蕾不仅存在于舌头上，也存在于口腔顶部和喉咙；且每1-2周旧味蕾死亡、新味蕾生长。C项完整概括上述信息。A项"只存在于舌上且永不变"与原文矛盾；B项、D项原文未提及。故选C。

**4. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第④段 "That is why food seems to have no taste when you have a cold — your nose is blocked and cannot help your tongue."

**【解析】** 原文直接解释感冒时食物似乎无味，是因为鼻子堵塞、无法协助舌头辨别味道。D项准确复述该逻辑。A项"味蕾完全停止工作"、B项"大脑拒绝接收信号"、C项"所有味蕾消失"均非原文所述。故选D。

**5. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 主旨大意

**【定位】** 全文：第①段味蕾介绍，第②段味觉工作原理，第③段味蕾分布与更新，第④段鼻子的作用，第⑤段总结。

**【解析】** 全文围绕味觉如何工作及味蕾如何帮助我们享受食物展开，C项最能概括主旨。A项过度强调鼻子；B项"超级味觉者更好"仅为举例；D项"儿童味觉更好"原文未涉及。故选C。
