# 阅读理解（说明文）

## The Invention of Paper and Its Influence on the World

① Paper was first invented in China more than 2,000 years ago. Before paper, people wrote on bamboo (竹子), silk, or animal bones. These materials were either too heavy or too expensive for most people. A man named Cai Lun improved the way to make paper around the year 105 AD. He used tree bark (树皮), old cloth, and other cheap things. His method made paper easier to produce and much cheaper.

② The invention of paper changed how people recorded and shared information. Books became lighter and easier to carry. More people could learn to read and write because paper was cheap. Ideas and knowledge spread faster from one place to another. The art of papermaking soon became an important skill in China and was kept as a secret for hundreds of years.

③ Papermaking finally spread to other countries through the Silk Road. Around the 8th century, the skill reached the Arab world. From there, it moved to Europe. Before paper arrived, Europeans mainly used animal skin to write on. Paper was much better — it was lighter, cheaper, and easier to use. This helped the growth of schools, libraries, and the spread of science and culture across Europe.

④ Today, paper is used everywhere in the world. We use it for books, newspapers, money, packaging (包装), and many other things. Although we now have computers and the Internet, paper is still important in our daily life. It is hard to imagine a world without paper.

⑤ The invention of paper is one of the greatest gifts China has given to the world. It has helped humans share ideas, preserve (保存) history, and develop culture for over two thousand years. We should remember and be proud of this great invention.

**注释：** bamboo 竹子；bark 树皮；packaging 包装；preserve 保存

### 选择题

1. Before paper was invented, what did people use for writing?
   A. Bamboo, silk, or animal bones.
   B. Only silk.
   C. Tree bark only.
   D. Paper made from cloth.

2. When did Cai Lun improve the way to make paper?
   A. About 2,000 years ago.
   B. Around the year 105 AD.
   C. In the 8th century.
   D. In modern times.

3. Why could more people learn to read and write after paper was invented?
   A. Because schools were built everywhere.
   B. Because the government forced people to study.
   C. Because paper was cheap and books became lighter.
   D. Because paper was stronger than bamboo.

4. What can we infer from paragraph ③ about paper in Europe?
   A. Europeans never used paper.
   B. Europeans invented paper before the Chinese.
   C. Paper was only used for money in Europe.
   D. Paper helped the growth of schools, libraries, and culture in Europe.

5. What does the writer want to tell us in the last paragraph?
   A. The invention of paper is a great gift from China to the world.
   B. Paper is no longer useful today.
   C. We should stop using paper to protect trees.
   D. Paper was invented in Europe.

---

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "Before paper, people wrote on bamboo (竹子), silk, or animal bones."

**【解析】** 原文明确说明造纸术发明前，人们在竹子、丝绸或兽骨上书写。A 项正确。B 项 "仅丝绸" 片面；C 项 "仅树皮" 错误，树皮用于造纸；D 项 "布做的纸" 是造纸后的产物。故选 A。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "A man named Cai Lun improved the way to make paper around the year 105 AD."

**【解析】** 蔡伦大约在公元 105 年改进了造纸方法。B 项正确。A 项 "2000 年前" 过于笼统；C 项 "8 世纪" 是造纸术传入阿拉伯的时间；D 项 "现代" 错误。故选 B。

**3. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "Books became lighter and easier to carry. More people could learn to read and write because paper was cheap."

**【解析】** 原文指出纸便宜后书籍更轻便、更易携带，更多人得以读书写字。C 项正确。A 项 "到处建学校" 文中未提；B 项 "政府强迫" 未提及；D 项 "纸比竹子结实" 非主要原因。故选 C。

**4. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第③段 "This helped the growth of schools, libraries, and the spread of science and culture across Europe."

**【解析】** 原文说明纸张的到来促进了欧洲学校、图书馆的发展以及科学与文化的传播，可推知 D 项。A 项 "从不使用" 与文意相反；B 项 "先于中国发明" 错误；C 项 "仅用于货币" 片面。故选 D。

**5. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 主旨理解 / 写作意图

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "The invention of paper is one of the greatest gifts China has given to the world."

**【解析】** 末段强调造纸术是中国献给世界的伟大礼物，并呼吁我们铭记和自豪。A 项正确。B 项 "不再有用" 与末段矛盾；C 项 "停止用纸" 未提及；D 项 "欧洲发明" 与原文相反。故选 A。
