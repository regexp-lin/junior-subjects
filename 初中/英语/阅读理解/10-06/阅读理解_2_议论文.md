# 阅读理解（议论文）

① Should students learn to code? In today's digital world, this question is asked more and more often. Coding, or computer programming, means writing instructions that tell a computer what to do. Some people think every student should learn it. Others disagree. Let us look at both sides.

② Those who support coding in schools say that it is a useful skill for the future. Many jobs today need at least basic computer skills. In the future, even more jobs will require some understanding of technology. Learning to code helps students think logically. When you write a program, you must break a big problem into smaller steps. This way of thinking can help in maths, science, and even daily life. Besides, coding can be fun. Students can create games, websites, or small apps. It can spark (激发) their interest in technology and lead to new hobbies or even a future career.

③ However, those who oppose making coding compulsory (必修的) have different views. They argue that not every child is interested in computers. Some students may prefer art, music, sports, or languages. Forcing everyone to learn coding could make some students feel stressed or bored. Schools already have a full curriculum. Adding coding might mean less time for other important subjects. In addition, coding changes quickly. What students learn today might be outdated in a few years. They think it is better to teach general problem-solving skills than to focus only on one tool.

④ In my view, coding can be offered as an optional subject or an after-school club. Students who are interested can choose to learn. Those who are not need not be forced. Schools could also teach basic digital literacy — how to use computers safely, how to search for information, and how to tell real news from fake news. These skills are useful for everyone. The goal should be to give students choices and prepare them for a changing world, not to turn every student into a programmer.

⑤ Whether or not to learn coding is a personal choice. But understanding how technology works is becoming more important for everyone. Schools should find a balance.

**注释：** spark 激发；compulsory 必修的；curriculum 课程；literacy 素养

**Questions:**

1. According to Paragraph ②, why do some people think students should learn coding?
   A. Because coding is the only useful skill.
   B. Because everyone must become a programmer.
   C. Because it helps with future jobs, logical thinking, and can be fun; it may lead to a career in technology.
   D. Because schools have nothing else to teach.

2. What skill does coding help develop, according to the passage?
   A. Logical thinking and breaking problems into smaller steps.
   B. Only typing speed.
   C. Physical strength.
   D. Singing ability.

3. Why do some people oppose making coding compulsory?
   A. Because coding is too easy.
   B. Because not every child is interested; the curriculum is already full; coding changes quickly.
   C. Because there are no teachers.
   D. Because coding is useless.

4. What does the writer suggest?
   A. All students must learn coding.
   B. Remove all computer classes.
   C. Offer coding as optional; teach basic digital literacy for everyone.
   D. Only teach coding and nothing else.

5. The word "outdated" in Paragraph ③ probably means _____.
   A. very useful
   B. easy to learn
   C. no longer useful or current
   D. very popular

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "useful skill for the future... think logically... can be fun... spark their interest... lead to... a future career"

**【解析】** 支持者认为编程对未来就业有用、锻炼逻辑思维、有趣，可激发兴趣并发展为职业。C项概括准确。A、B、D项过于极端。故选C。

**2. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "Learning to code helps students think logically. When you write a program, you must break a big problem into smaller steps"

**【解析】** 编程有助于逻辑思维和将大问题分解为小步骤。A项正确。B、C、D项与原文不符。故选A。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "not every child is interested... Forcing everyone... could make some students feel stressed or bored... curriculum is already full... coding changes quickly... might be outdated"

**【解析】** 反对理由：非人人感兴趣、可能增加压力、课时已满、技术更新快。B项正确。A、C、D项与反对理由不符。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第④段 "coding can be offered as an optional subject... teach basic digital literacy... useful for everyone... give students choices"

**【解析】** 作者建议将编程设为选修，并教授基础数字素养。B项正确。A、C、D项与建议矛盾。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 词义猜测

**【定位】** 第③段 "What students learn today might be outdated in a few years"

**【解析】** 根据上下文，几年后所学可能"过时"，outdated 意为 no longer useful or current。B项正确。A、C、D项与词义不符。故选B。
