> **11-10 第2篇**

# 阅读理解（记叙文）

**Sharing a Book with a Stranger in the Library**

① Last Saturday, I went to the city library to do my homework. I found a quiet seat near the window. After an hour, I needed a break. I walked to the shelves and picked up a book about space. It looked interesting. I brought it back to my seat.

② A few minutes later, a girl about my age sat down next to me. She glanced at my book and said, "Oh, I've been wanting to read that! Is it good?" I was a bit shy. I had never talked to a stranger in the library before. But her smile was friendly. I said, "I've just started. It seems good. Do you want to read it with me? We can take turns."

③ She agreed. We took turns holding the book and reading aloud. Sometimes we stopped to discuss what we read. She knew a lot about stars and planets. I learned many new things. We read for almost an hour. When it was time for her to leave, she said, "Thank you. That was the best reading time I've had in a long time." I felt the same way.

④ Before she left, we exchanged WeChat. Now we often chat and recommend books to each other. A simple shared book brought us a new friend.

⑤ I learned that day that the library is not just a place for quiet study. It can also be a place to connect with others. Sharing a book with a stranger turned into something beautiful. I will always remember that afternoon.

**注释：** shelf 书架；recommend 推荐

---

**Questions:**

1. Where did the story happen?
   A. At school.
   B. In a bookstore.
   C. At the city library.
   D. At home.

2. What did the writer do when the girl asked about the book?
   A. Ignored her and left.
   B. Suggested they read it together and take turns.
   C. Gave her the book and walked away.
   D. Said the book was boring.

3. How long did they read together?
   A. About 10 minutes.
   B. About 30 minutes.
   C. Almost an hour.
   D. The whole day.

4. What did they do before the girl left?
   A. They bought the book.
   B. They exchanged WeChat.
   C. They argued about the book.
   D. They promised never to meet again.

5. What can we infer from the passage?
   A. The library is only for studying alone.
   B. A small act of sharing can lead to new friendships and meaningful connections.
   C. Strangers in the library are usually unfriendly.
   D. The writer will never go to the library again.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】C**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第①段 "I went to the city library to do my homework"
**【解析】** 故事发生在市图书馆。C项正确。A、B、D项与原文不符。故选C。

**2. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第②段 "Do you want to read it with me? We can take turns."
**【解析】** 作者提议一起阅读并轮流拿书。B项准确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】C**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第③段 "We read for almost an hour"
**【解析】** 他们一起读了大半小时。C项正确。A、B、D项与原文不符。故选C。

**4. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第④段 "Before she left, we exchanged WeChat"
**【解析】** 女孩离开前两人互加了微信。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 推理判断
**【定位】** 第④段 "A simple shared book brought us a new friend"；第⑤段 "Sharing a book with a stranger turned into something beautiful"
**【解析】** 一次简单的分享带来新友谊和美好回忆。B项正确。A、C、D项与文意不符。故选B。
