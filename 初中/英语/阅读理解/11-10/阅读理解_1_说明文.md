# 阅读理解（说明文）

**Animal Hibernation**

① When winter comes, some animals seem to disappear. Bears, bats, and hedgehogs sleep for months. They do not eat or drink. This is called hibernation. How do they survive? Let us find out.

② Before winter, these animals eat a lot. They store fat in their bodies. Fat is like fuel. During hibernation, their bodies use this stored fat very slowly. Their heart rate drops. Their breathing becomes slow. Their body temperature falls. They use very little energy. So they can live for months without eating.

③ Different animals hibernate in different ways. Bears sleep in caves or dens. They can wake up if the weather gets warm. Bats hang upside down in dark places. Hedgehogs curl up in nests made of leaves. Some animals, like squirrels, do not truly hibernate. They wake up now and then to eat stored food. We call this "light hibernation."

④ Hibernation helps animals save energy when food is scarce. In winter, plants die and insects hide. It is hard to find food. Sleeping through winter is a smart way to survive. When spring comes, they wake up, hungry but alive.

⑤ Hibernation is one of nature's wonders. Scientists still study it to learn more. Understanding hibernation might even help humans in the future — for example, in long space travel.

**注释：** hedgehog 刺猬；den 洞穴；scarce 稀缺的

**Questions:**

1. What is hibernation according to Paragraph ①?
   A. Animals travelling south.
   B. Animals sleeping for months without eating or drinking.
   C. Animals building nests.
   D. Animals hunting in winter.

2. How do animals prepare for hibernation?
   A. They swim in cold water.
   B. They eat a lot and store fat before winter.
   C. They move to warmer places.
   D. They hide their food underground.

3. What happens to an animal's body during hibernation?
   A. Heart rate and breathing speed up.
   B. Body temperature rises.
   C. Heart rate drops, breathing becomes slow, body temperature falls.
   D. They use a lot of energy.

4. What is "light hibernation" as mentioned in Paragraph ③?
   A. Sleeping without ever waking.
   B. Animals like squirrels that wake up now and then to eat stored food.
   C. Sleeping only at night.
   D. Hibernating in bright places.

5. What can we infer from the last paragraph?
   A. Scientists know everything about hibernation.
   B. Research on hibernation may have future applications, such as space travel.
   C. Hibernation is useless to humans.
   D. No animals hibernate anymore.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第①段 "They sleep for months. They do not eat or drink. This is called hibernation."
**【解析】** 冬眠指动物数月不进食不饮水地睡眠。B项准确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第②段 "Before winter, these animals eat a lot. They store fat in their bodies."
**【解析】** 动物在冬季前大量进食，在体内储存脂肪。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】C**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第②段 "Their heart rate drops. Their breathing becomes slow. Their body temperature falls."
**【解析】** 冬眠时心跳减慢、呼吸变慢、体温下降。C项准确。A、B、D项与原文矛盾。故选C。

**4. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第③段 "Some animals, like squirrels... wake up now and then to eat stored food. We call this 'light hibernation.'"
**【解析】** 浅度冬眠指像松鼠一样偶尔醒来进食储存食物的冬眠方式。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 推理判断
**【定位】** 第⑤段 "Understanding hibernation might even help humans in the future — for example, in long space travel"
**【解析】** 研究冬眠可能对未来人类有帮助，如长途太空旅行。B项正确。A、C、D项与文意不符。故选B。
