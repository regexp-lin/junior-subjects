# 阅读理解（议论文）

① Plastic bags are everywhere. We use them when we go shopping, buy food, or carry things. They are cheap, light, and convenient. But plastic bags also cause serious environmental problems. Should plastic bags be banned? This question has led to heated debate around the world.

② Those who support a ban on plastic bags argue that they harm the environment. Most plastic bags are made from oil and take hundreds of years to break down (分解). They pile up in landfills (垃圾填埋场) and float in rivers and oceans. Animals sometimes eat them by mistake and die. Plastic breaks into small pieces called microplastics (微塑料), which enter the food chain and may harm human health. In addition, making plastic bags uses a lot of oil and energy. Banning them, supporters say, would reduce waste and encourage people to use reusable bags instead. Many countries, including China, have already limited or banned single-use plastic bags in shops.

③ However, those who oppose a complete ban have different views. First, they say that plastic bags are very convenient, especially for carrying wet or heavy items. Paper bags and cloth bags have their own problems: paper bags use more energy and water to make; cloth bags need to be washed often, which also uses resources. Second, a sudden ban could hurt businesses, especially small shops that cannot afford to buy expensive alternatives (替代品). Third, the real problem may not be plastic bags themselves, but how people use and dispose (处理) of them. If we improve recycling and teach people not to litter, we might not need a full ban. Some suggest charging a small fee for plastic bags instead of banning them — this has worked in many places to reduce use.

④ In my view, we do not have to choose between a full ban and no action at all. We could reduce plastic bag use step by step. For example, ban them in supermarkets and large shops first, but allow a transition (过渡) period for small businesses. We could also encourage reusable bags by making them cheaper or giving them as gifts. Education is important too — teaching people why we should use fewer plastic bags can change habits. The goal is to protect our planet while still meeting people's daily needs.

⑤ Every small step matters. Bringing your own bag when you shop may seem small, but if millions of people do it, the difference will be huge.

**注释：** break down 分解；landfill 垃圾填埋场；microplastic 微塑料；alternative 替代品；dispose 处理；transition 过渡

---

**Questions:**

1. According to Paragraph ②, why do supporters want to ban plastic bags?
   A. Because they are too expensive.
   B. Because they harm the environment — they take long to break down, pile up, harm animals, create microplastics, and use lots of oil and energy.
   C. Because they are ugly.
   D. Because shops do not like them.

2. What do opponents of the ban say about paper and cloth bags?
   A. They are always better.
   B. Paper bags use more energy and water; cloth bags need frequent washing, which also uses resources.
   C. They are cheaper than plastic.
   D. They last forever.

3. What alternative do some people suggest instead of a full ban?
   A. Use only paper bags.
   B. Charge a small fee for plastic bags to reduce use.
   C. Stop all shopping.
   D. Move to another country.

4. What does the writer think we should do?
   A. Do nothing.
   B. Reduce plastic bag use step by step — e.g. ban in large shops first, encourage reusable bags, and educate people.
   C. Ban plastic bags everywhere at once.
   D. Use more plastic bags.

5. The writer's main message is that _____.
   A. plastic bags are harmless
   B. every small step matters; reducing plastic bag use can make a big difference when many people join in
   C. we should never use bags
   D. only the government can solve this problem

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "they harm the environment... take hundreds of years to break down... pile up... Animals sometimes eat them and die... microplastics... making plastic bags uses a lot of oil and energy"

**【解析】** 支持者认为塑料袋污染环境、难降解、危害动物、产生微塑料、消耗能源。A、C、D 项非主要理由。故选 B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "paper bags use more energy and water to make; cloth bags need to be washed often, which also uses resources"

**【解析】** 反对者指出纸袋耗能耗水，布袋需经常清洗同样消耗资源。A、C、D 项与原文不符。故选 B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "Some suggest charging a small fee for plastic bags instead of banning them — this has worked in many places to reduce use"

**【解析】** 有人建议对塑料袋收费而非全面禁止，以降低使用量。A、C、D 项未提及。故选 B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第④段 "reduce plastic bag use step by step... ban them in supermarkets first... encourage reusable bags... Education is important"

**【解析】** 作者建议逐步减少使用、先在大超市禁止、推广环保袋、加强教育。A、C、D 项与建议矛盾。故选 B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 主旨大意

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "Every small step matters. Bringing your own bag... if millions of people do it, the difference will be huge"

**【解析】** 作者强调每个小举措都有意义，众人参与能带来巨大改变。A、C、D 项与主题不符。故选 B。
