# 阅读理解（议论文）

**Education in China vs the West: What Can We Learn?**

① Education systems differ around the world. In China, students often have heavy homework, many exams, and long school hours. In some Western countries, school days are shorter, homework is lighter, and students have more free time. Which is better? There is no simple answer. Both systems have strengths and weaknesses. We can learn from each other.

② Chinese education is strong in basics. Students learn maths, science, and languages very well. They develop strong discipline and a good work ethic. Chinese students often score highly in international tests. This is because they practice a lot and take learning seriously. However, some say Chinese schools put too much pressure on students. There is less time for sports, art, and creativity. Students may feel stressed and tired.

③ Western education often focuses on critical thinking and creativity. Students are encouraged to ask questions and express their own ideas. They do more projects and group work. They learn to work independently and solve problems in their own way. Classes may be more relaxed. But some say Western students sometimes lack basic knowledge. Without enough practice, they may forget what they learned quickly.

④ So what is the ideal? Perhaps we need a balance. Keep the best of both: strong basics from the East, and creative thinking from the West. Some schools in China are already trying this. They reduce homework while adding more project-based learning. They encourage students to ask "why" and "how" instead of only memorising answers. They give more time to art, music, and sport.

⑤ The goal of education is not just to pass exams. It is to help young people grow into thoughtful, creative, and caring adults. No matter which system we are in, we can always ask: What am I learning? Why does it matter? How can I use it in real life? These questions can guide us toward a better education for all.

**注释：** discipline 纪律；ethic 伦理/习惯；critical thinking 批判性思维；memorise 背诵

---

**Questions:**

1. What is one strength of Chinese education?
   A. Short school days.
   B. Strong basics in maths, science, and languages; good discipline.
   C. No homework.
   D. Only art and sport.

2. What do Western schools often focus on?
   A. Memorising only.
   B. Critical thinking and creativity; students ask questions and do projects.
   C. Heavy homework.
   D. Long exam hours.

3. What problem do some people see in Chinese education?
   A. Students learn too little.
   B. Too much pressure; less time for sports, art, and creativity.
   C. Teachers are not good.
   D. Schools are too small.

4. What does the writer suggest as the ideal education?
   A. Only Chinese style.
   B. A balance: strong basics from the East and creative thinking from the West.
   C. Only Western style.
   D. No exams at all.

5. What does the writer say the goal of education is?
   A. To pass exams only.
   B. To help young people become thoughtful, creative, and caring adults.
   C. To make students tired.
   D. To copy one system only.

---

**Answers and Explanations:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "Chinese education is strong in basics. Students learn maths, science, and languages very well. They develop strong discipline and a good work ethic."

**【解析】** 中国教育在基础知识上强，数学、科学、语言学得好，纪律和勤奋习惯也强。A、C、D项均非中国教育的优势。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "Western education often focuses on critical thinking and creativity. Students are encouraged to ask questions and express their own ideas. They do more projects and group work."

**【解析】** 西方教育注重批判性思维和创造力，鼓励提问和表达，更多项目和小组合作。A、C、D项均与原文不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "However, some say Chinese schools put too much pressure on students. There is less time for sports, art, and creativity."

**【解析】** 有人认为中国学校压力过大，体育、艺术和创意时间较少。A项学得少、C项老师不好、D项学校太小均非文中指出的问题。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第④段 "Perhaps we need a balance. Keep the best of both: strong basics from the East, and creative thinking from the West."

**【解析】** 作者认为理想教育应是东西方之长结合：东方扎实基础，西方创意思维。A、C、D项均过于片面。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "The goal of education is not just to pass exams. It is to help young people grow into thoughtful, creative, and caring adults."

**【解析】** 教育目标不仅是考试通过，而是培养有思想、有创意、有责任感的成年人。A、C、D项均与原文矛盾。故选B。
