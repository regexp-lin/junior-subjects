# 阅读理解（议论文）

**Do Handwritten Letters Still Have Value?**

① Today we send messages by email, WeChat, or phone. It is quick and easy. So do people still write letters by hand? Are handwritten letters just old-fashioned, or do they still mean something? Let us think about both views.

② Those who say handwritten letters are outdated have reasons. First, typing is faster. You can send an email in seconds. A letter takes time to write and days to arrive. Second, most young people prefer digital tools. They text every day. Writing with a pen feels slow and unnecessary. Third, letters can get lost in the post. Emails stay in your inbox. So why bother with paper?

③ However, others believe handwritten letters still have value. When you write by hand, you put more thought into each word. You choose your paper and your words carefully. The receiver can hold something real — not just pixels on a screen. Letters can be kept for years. People treasure letters from family or friends. A handwritten letter shows that you care enough to spend time. It is personal in a way that a quick message is not. Many people still write thank-you notes or birthday letters by hand because they feel more meaningful.

④ In my view, handwritten letters will not replace quick messages, but they still matter. For important moments — a thank-you, an apology, or a message to someone you love — a letter can say more than a text. It shows effort and sincerity. We live in a fast world, but slowing down to write a letter can be a gift to both the writer and the reader.

⑤ So, do handwritten letters still have value? Yes. They may be less common, but they are not worthless. When you want to show someone you really care, a handwritten letter can still be the best choice.

**注释：** outdated 过时的；sincerity 真诚

**Questions:**

1. According to Paragraph ②, why do some people think handwritten letters are outdated?
   A. Because typing is faster, young people prefer digital tools, and letters can get lost.
   B. Because letters are too beautiful.
   C. Because everyone writes letters.
   D. Because letters are free.

2. According to Paragraph ③, what is one reason people still value handwritten letters?
   A. They are cheaper than emails.
   B. They are faster to send.
   C. They require no thought.
   D. They show effort and care; they are personal and can be kept for years.

3. What does the writer think about handwritten letters?
   A. They still have value; for important moments they can say more than a text and show effort and sincerity.
   B. They are useless and should be forgotten.
   C. They will replace all digital messages soon.
   D. Only old people like them.

4. For what kind of moment does the writer say a letter can be better than a text?
   A. When you are in a hurry.
   B. When you want to send a joke.
   C. For daily chat.
   D. For important moments like a thank-you, an apology, or a message to someone you love.

5. What is the writer's main purpose in writing this passage?
   A. To tell people to stop using phones.
   B. To argue that handwritten letters still have value in our digital age.
   C. To explain how to write emails.
   D. To say that all letters are the same.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "typing is faster... You can send an email in seconds. A letter takes time... most young people prefer digital tools... letters can get lost in the post"

**【解析】** 认为手写信过时的理由包括：打字更快、年轻人更爱数字工具、信件易丢失。A项概括准确。B、C、D项与原文不符。故选A。

**2. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "you put more thought into each word... The receiver can hold something real... Letters can be kept for years... A handwritten letter shows that you care enough to spend time"

**【解析】** 手写信的价值在于：更用心、有实体感、可长期保存、体现重视与关心。D项正确。A、B、C项与原文不符。故选D。

**3. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "handwritten letters will not replace quick messages, but they still matter... For important moments... a letter can say more than a text. It shows effort and sincerity"

**【解析】** 作者认为手写信仍有价值，在重要场合能更好表达情感、体现用心与真诚。A项正确。B、C、D项与原文不符。故选A。

**4. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "For important moments — a thank-you, an apology, or a message to someone you love — a letter can say more than a text"

**【解析】** 作者认为感谢、道歉或向所爱之人表达等重要时刻，信件比短信更能传情达意。D项正确。A、B、C项与原文不符。故选D。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 写作意图

**【定位】** 全文，尤其是第④⑤段观点总结

**【解析】** 作者旨在论证在数字时代手写信仍有价值。B项正确。A、C、D项与写作目的不符。故选B。
