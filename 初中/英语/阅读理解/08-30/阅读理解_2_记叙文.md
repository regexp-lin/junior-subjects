> **08-30 第2篇**

# 阅读理解（记叙文）

① Last Saturday afternoon, I was reading in my room when I heard a knock on the door. It was our neighbor, Mr. Zhang, an elderly man who lives alone. He looked worried and said his medicine had run out. He needed to buy his blood pressure medicine from the pharmacy (药店), but his legs hurt and he could not walk far. He asked if I could help.

② I agreed at once. Mr. Zhang wrote down the name of the medicine and the dosage (剂量). He also gave me some money. I took the note and walked to the nearest pharmacy, which was about fifteen minutes away. On the way, I thought about how often Mr. Zhang must feel lonely. His children work in another city and seldom visit him.

③ At the pharmacy, I showed the note to the staff. They found the medicine quickly and explained how to take it. I paid with the money Mr. Zhang had given me and put the change in my pocket. When I got back, Mr. Zhang was waiting at his door. He took the medicine and thanked me again and again. "You are such a good boy," he said. His eyes were a little red. I felt happy that I could help.

④ That evening, Mr. Zhang came to our apartment with a bag of oranges. He wanted to give them to me as a thank-you gift. My mother said it was not necessary, but he insisted. "Your son helped me when I needed it most. This is just a small thank you." We accepted the oranges and invited him to have dinner with us. He smiled and said he would come another time.

⑤ Helping Mr. Zhang that day made me understand something important. A small act of kindness can mean a lot to someone in need. I have decided to check on Mr. Zhang more often and help him with other small things. Neighbors should take care of each other.

**注释：** pharmacy 药店；dosage 剂量

---

**Questions:**

1. Why did Mr. Zhang knock on the writer's door?
   A. He wanted to borrow a book.
   B. His medicine had run out and he needed help buying more.
   C. He wanted to have dinner with the family.
   D. He was looking for his children.

2. How did the writer get the medicine for Mr. Zhang?
   A. He ordered it online.
   B. He walked to the nearest pharmacy with the note and bought it.
   C. He asked his mother to buy it.
   D. Mr. Zhang went with him to the pharmacy.

3. What can we infer about Mr. Zhang from the passage?
   A. He has many friends in the neighborhood.
   B. He lives alone and his children seldom visit.
   C. He does not like oranges.
   D. He works at the pharmacy.

4. What did Mr. Zhang do to thank the writer?
   A. He gave the writer some money.
   B. He brought a bag of oranges as a thank-you gift.
   C. He invited the writer to his apartment.
   D. He wrote a thank-you letter.

5. What did the writer learn from this experience?
   A. Medicine is expensive.
   B. A small act of kindness can mean a lot to someone in need.
   C. Oranges are a good gift.
   D. Old people should live with their children.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段第3句 "He looked worried and said his medicine had run out. He needed to buy his blood pressure medicine from the pharmacy"

**【解析】** 原文明确说张先生的药吃完了，需要帮忙买药。A项"借书"、C项"一起吃晚饭"、D项"找孩子"均与原文不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "I took the note and walked to the nearest pharmacy... I showed the note to the staff. They found the medicine quickly... I paid with the money Mr. Zhang had given me"

**【解析】** 作者拿着纸条步行至最近药店，出示纸条后购得药物并付款。A项"网购"、C项"让妈妈买"、D项"张先生同去"均与原文不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第①段 "an elderly man who lives alone"；第②段 "His children work in another city and seldom visit him"

**【解析】** 原文指出张先生独自居住，子女在异地工作很少探望，可推断他孤独。A项"朋友多"与"独居"矛盾；C、D项原文无依据。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段第1句 "Mr. Zhang came to our apartment with a bag of oranges. He wanted to give them to me as a thank-you gift"

**【解析】** 原文明确说张先生带来一袋橙子作为答谢。A项"给钱"是作者买药用的钱；C项"邀请去他家"原文未提及；D项"写信"原文无依据。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 主旨大意/写作意图

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "A small act of kindness can mean a lot to someone in need"

**【解析】** 作者总结感悟：小小的善举对需要帮助的人意义重大。A、C项仅为细节；D项"老人应与子女同住"非作者观点。故选B。
