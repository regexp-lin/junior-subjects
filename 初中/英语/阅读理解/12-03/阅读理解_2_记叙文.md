# 阅读理解（记叙文）

**Learning to Swim**

① I was twelve years old and still afraid of water. While my classmates enjoyed swimming in the pool during PE class, I sat on the side and watched. I always said I had a cold or a stomachache, but the truth was that I was scared. When I was five, I fell into a pond near my grandparents' house. My uncle pulled me out quickly, but the fear stayed with me ever since.

② This summer, my parents signed me up for swimming lessons at the community pool. "You need to face your fear," my dad said. I begged them to cancel it, but they would not change their minds. On the first day, I stood at the edge of the pool, my whole body shaking.

③ My coach was a kind woman named Mrs. Wang. She did not push me into the deep end right away. Instead, she started in the shallow area where the water only reached my waist. "Just walk around first. Get used to the feeling of water," she said gently. I took a deep breath and stepped in. The water was warm. It was not as scary as I thought.

④ Over the next two weeks, I learned to float, to kick, and finally to swim freestyle. Each day I went a little deeper. Each day the fear got a little smaller. There were moments I wanted to quit, but Mrs. Wang always encouraged me: "You're braver than you think." By the end of the lessons, I could swim 50 metres without stopping.

⑤ On the last day, I swam across the pool and climbed out with a huge smile. My parents were watching from the stands, clapping and cheering. That summer taught me that fear is like a wall — it looks impossible until you start breaking it down, one brick at a time.

**注释：** shallow 浅的；freestyle 自由泳；stands 看台

---

**Questions:**

1. Why was the writer afraid of water?
   A. He saw a scary movie about the sea.
   B. He fell into a pond when he was five.
   C. He did not like cold water.
   D. His friends told him swimming was dangerous.

2. Who signed the writer up for swimming lessons?
   A. His teacher.
   B. His coach.
   C. His parents.
   D. His uncle.

3. How did Mrs. Wang help the writer at first?
   A. She pushed him into the deep end.
   B. She let him start in the shallow area and walk around.
   C. She asked him to swim 100 metres right away.
   D. She told him to watch videos about swimming.

4. How far could the writer swim by the end of the lessons?
   A. 10 metres.
   B. 25 metres.
   C. 50 metres.
   D. 100 metres.

5. What did the writer compare fear to in the last paragraph?
   A. A river.
   B. A mountain.
   C. A wall that can be broken down one brick at a time.
   D. A storm that never ends.

---

**Answers and Explanations:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "When I was five, I fell into a pond near my grandparents' house."

**【解析】** 原文说五岁时掉进爷爷奶奶家附近的池塘，从此害怕水。A项看恐怖电影、C项不喜欢冷水、D项朋友说游泳危险均非原因。故选B。

**2. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "my parents signed me up for swimming lessons at the community pool."

**【解析】** 原文明确说是父母给他报了游泳课。A项老师、B项教练、D项叔叔均非报名者。故选C。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "she started in the shallow area where the water only reached my waist. 'Just walk around first. Get used to the feeling of water,' she said gently."

**【解析】** 王教练让他先在浅水区走动适应水感。A项推入深水区与原文"did not push me into the deep end"相反；C项立刻游100米太极端；D项看视频原文未提及。故选B。

**4. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "By the end of the lessons, I could swim 50 metres without stopping."

**【解析】** 原文说课程结束时能不停歇游50米。A项10米、B项25米、D项100米均不正确。故选C。

**5. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "fear is like a wall — it looks impossible until you start breaking it down, one brick at a time."

**【解析】** 作者把恐惧比作一堵墙，看似不可能但可以一块砖一块砖地拆除。A项河流、B项山、D项永不结束的暴风雨均非原文的比喻。故选C。
