# 阅读理解（说明文）

**How Do Typhoons Form?**

① Every summer and autumn, typhoons hit the coasts of China, Japan, and other Asian countries. Strong winds, heavy rain, and floods can cause great damage. But how do these powerful storms form? Let us find out.

② Typhoons need warm sea water to form. When the sun heats the ocean, water evaporates and rises into the air. This warm, wet air carries a lot of energy. As it rises, it cools and the water vapour turns into clouds. The process of evaporation and cloud formation happens over and over. More and more clouds gather, and the air begins to spin. This is because of the Earth's rotation — the spin of our planet causes the wind to curve. In the northern hemisphere, typhoons spin anticlockwise.

③ When the wind speed inside the storm reaches about 118 kilometres per hour, we call it a typhoon (or hurricane in other parts of the world). The centre of the typhoon is called the "eye." The eye is calm — there is little wind and sometimes even blue sky. But around the eye, the winds are at their strongest. That is the most dangerous part of the storm.

④ Typhoons usually form over warm tropical seas, at least 500 km away from the equator. They need water temperature of about 26°C or higher. That is why typhoons are rare in winter when the sea is colder. Once a typhoon moves over land or cold water, it loses its energy source and weakens.

⑤ Scientists can now predict typhoons a few days in advance using satellites and computers. When a typhoon is coming, governments will issue warnings. People living in coastal areas should follow the news, stay indoors, and prepare emergency supplies. Knowing how typhoons form helps us understand why we need to prepare and how to stay safe.

**注释：** evaporate 蒸发；hemisphere 半球；equator 赤道

**Questions:**

1. What do typhoons need to form?
   A. Cold air only.
   B. Warm sea water.
   C. Snow.
   D. Mountains.

2. Why does the air spin when a typhoon forms?
   A. Because of the sun.
   B. Because of the Earth's rotation.
   C. Because of rain.
   D. Because of clouds.

3. What is the "eye" of a typhoon?
   A. The strongest part of the storm.
   B. The calm centre with little wind.
   C. The outer part.
   D. The rainy part only.

4. Where do typhoons usually form?
   A. Over cold seas.
   B. Over warm tropical seas, at least 500 km from the equator.
   C. Over land.
   D. Near the North Pole.

5. Why are typhoons rare in winter?
   A. Because people stay indoors.
   B. Because the sea is colder and the water temperature is too low.
   C. Because the sun is brighter.
   D. Because there is less rain.

---

**Answers and Explanations:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "Typhoons need warm sea water to form."

**【解析】** 原文说台风形成需要温暖海水。A项仅冷空气、C项雪、D项山脉均非形成条件。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "This is because of the Earth's rotation — the spin of our planet causes the wind to curve."

**【解析】** 空气旋转是因地球自转使风发生偏转。A项太阳、C项雨、D项云均非直接原因。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "The centre of the typhoon is called the 'eye.' The eye is calm — there is little wind and sometimes even blue sky."

**【解析】** 台风眼是中心区域，相对平静、风小。A项最强部分在眼周围；C项外围、D项仅降雨区均不准确。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "Typhoons usually form over warm tropical seas, at least 500 km away from the equator."

**【解析】** 台风通常在温暖热带海面上形成，距赤道至少500公里。A项冷海、C项陆地上、D项北极附近均不符。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第④段 "They need water temperature of about 26°C or higher. That is why typhoons are rare in winter when the sea is colder."

**【解析】** 台风需要约26°C以上海水，冬季海水较冷故少见。A项人们待室内、C项阳光更强、D项雨水少均非原因。故选B。
