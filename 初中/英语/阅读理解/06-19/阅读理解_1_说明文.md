# 阅读理解（说明文）

## The Uniqueness and Uses of Fingerprints

① Have you ever looked at the tips of your fingers? You will see many lines and circles. These are called fingerprints. Scientists say that no two people in the world have the same fingerprints. Even identical twins have different ones. This makes fingerprints very special and useful.

② Fingerprints form when a baby is still inside its mother. The lines grow in a special way because of how the skin develops. They do not change as we grow older. A cut or burn may make a mark, but when the skin grows back, the fingerprint returns to almost the same as before. That is why fingerprints can be used to identify people for a long time.

③ For many years, police have used fingerprints to solve crimes. When someone touches an object, they may leave behind a fingerprint. Police can compare it with fingerprints they have stored. If they match, they can find the person. This has helped catch many criminals around the world.

④ Today, fingerprints are used in more ways. Many mobile phones and computers use fingerprint scanners. You can unlock your phone or pay for things by placing your finger on the screen. Banks also use fingerprint technology to make sure it is really you when you use their services. It is safer than using only a password because no one else can copy your fingerprint easily.

⑤ Scientists are still finding new uses for fingerprints. In the future, we might use them to open our doors, start our cars, or even visit the doctor. As technology improves, fingerprints will play an even more important role in our daily life.

**注释：** identical 完全相同的；identify 识别；scanner 扫描仪；criminal 罪犯

### 选择题

1. According to paragraph ①, which is TRUE about fingerprints?
   A. Identical twins share the same fingerprints.
   B. Fingerprints can change a lot when people grow up.
   C. No two people in the world have the same fingerprints.
   D. Only a few people have fingerprints.

2. When do fingerprints form?
   A. When people become adults.
   B. When a baby is still inside its mother.
   C. After people start school.
   D. When people use phones.

3. How do police use fingerprints?
   A. To take photos of criminals.
   B. To compare them with stored ones and find the person.
   C. To make phones safer.
   D. To open bank accounts.

4. Why is a fingerprint safer than a password according to the passage?
   A. Because it is shorter.
   B. Because no one else can copy your fingerprint easily.
   C. Because passwords are too long.
   D. Because fingerprints are free.

5. What might fingerprints be used for in the future?
   A. Only for solving crimes.
   B. Only for unlocking phones.
   C. For opening doors, starting cars, or visiting the doctor.
   D. They will not be used anymore.

---

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "Scientists say that no two people in the world have the same fingerprints."

**【解析】** 文章明确说明世界上没有两个人有相同的指纹。C 项正确。A 项错误，同卵双胞胎指纹也不同；B 项错误，指纹不会随年龄改变；D 项错误，所有人都有指纹。故选 C。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "Fingerprints form when a baby is still inside its mother."

**【解析】** 指纹在婴儿仍在母亲体内时形成。B 项正确。A 项成年、C 项上学后、D 项用手机时均与原文不符。故选 B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "Police can compare it with fingerprints they have stored. If they match, they can find the person."

**【解析】** 警察将现场指纹与已存储的指纹比对，若匹配则可找到嫌疑人。B 项正确。A 项拍照、C 项使手机更安全、D 项开银行账户均非警察用法。故选 B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第④段 "It is safer than using only a password because no one else can copy your fingerprint easily."

**【解析】** 指纹比密码安全的原因是别人很难复制你的指纹。B 项正确。A、C、D 项均未在文中提及。故选 B。

**5. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "we might use them to open our doors, start our cars, or even visit the doctor."

**【解析】** 未来指纹可能用于开门、发动汽车或看病等。C 项正确。A 项 "仅破案"、B 项 "仅解锁手机" 范围过窄；D 项 "不再使用" 与原文相反。故选 C。
