# 阅读理解（议论文）

① **Do Reality TV Shows Have Positive Effects on Teenagers?**

② Reality TV shows have become very popular among young people. These programs show ordinary people in competitions, daily life, or special situations. But people often ask: do such shows have any positive effects on teenagers, or are they mostly harmful? Different people hold different opinions.

③ **Those who see positive effects** say that some reality shows can teach teenagers useful lessons. For example, talent shows may encourage young viewers to work hard and follow their dreams. Cooking shows can help them learn about food and healthy eating. Some shows about teamwork can show how people cooperate (合作) and solve problems together. Besides, watching these programs with family can be a good way to spend time together and start conversations. Teenagers might also feel less alone when they see others facing similar challenges.

④ **Those who worry** argue that many reality shows focus on drama, conflict (冲突), and winning at any cost. This can make teenagers believe that being famous is more important than being kind or honest. Some shows present unhealthy body images or lifestyles. Spending too much time watching TV can also take away time from homework, exercise, and sleep. Studies suggest that teenagers who watch a lot of reality TV may care more about their appearance and less about their studies.

⑤ **The conclusion:** Reality TV is not all bad or all good. The key is what teenagers watch and how much. Parents and teachers can help by choosing programs with positive messages and by limiting (限制) screen time. Teenagers themselves should learn to think critically about what they see—not everything on TV reflects real life. Balance is important: some entertainment is fine, but real learning, exercise, and rest should always come first.

**注释：** cooperate 合作；conflict 冲突；limit 限制

---

### 选择题

1. According to supporters, what positive effects can reality TV have on teenagers?
   A. It can encourage hard work, teach useful skills like cooking, show teamwork, and provide family time or a sense of connection.
   B. It always makes teenagers famous.
   C. It replaces the need for school.
   D. It stops teenagers from using the Internet.

2. What do those who worry say about reality TV?
   A. All reality shows are educational.
   B. Teenagers never watch these shows.
   C. Many shows focus on drama and winning at any cost; they may present unhealthy images and take time away from study and rest.
   D. Reality TV helps teenagers sleep better.

3. What do studies suggest about teenagers who watch a lot of reality TV?
   A. They always get better grades.
   B. They never care about fame.
   C. They spend more time exercising.
   D. They may care more about appearance and less about studies.

4. What does the writer suggest parents and teachers do?
   A. Ban all TV shows completely.
   B. Choose programs with positive messages and limit screen time; help teenagers think critically about what they see.
   C. Let teenagers watch TV as long as they want.
   D. Only allow cooking shows.

5. What can we infer from the passage?
   A. The writer believes reality TV has no value at all.
   B. All teenagers should avoid reality TV completely.
   C. The writer thinks balance matters—some entertainment is fine, but real learning, exercise, and rest should come first.
   D. Reality TV is the best way for teenagers to learn.

---

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "talent shows may encourage young viewers to work hard and follow their dreams... Cooking shows can help them learn about food and healthy eating... shows about teamwork can show how people cooperate... watching these programs with family can be a good way... Teenagers might also feel less alone"

**【解析】** 支持者认为真人秀可鼓励勤奋追梦、教授烹饪等实用技能、展示团队合作、增进家庭互动、减少孤独感。A项全面概括。B项"让人出名"、C项"替代学校"、D项"减少上网"均与原文不符。故选A。

**2. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "many reality shows focus on drama, conflict, and winning at any cost... can make teenagers believe that being famous is more important... Some shows present unhealthy body images or lifestyles... take away time from homework, exercise, and sleep... may care more about their appearance and less about their studies"

**【解析】** 担忧者指出许多节目侧重戏剧冲突和不惜代价的获胜、传递不健康形象、占用学习运动睡眠时间、可能让人过度关注外表。C项准确概括。A、B、D项均与原文相反。故选C。

**3. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "Studies suggest that teenagers who watch a lot of reality TV may care more about their appearance and less about their studies"

**【解析】** 文中写明研究表明大量观看真人秀的青少年可能更关注外表而较少关注学业。D项正确。A、B、C项均与原文不符。故选D。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "Parents and teachers can help by choosing programs with positive messages and by limiting screen time. Teenagers themselves should learn to think critically about what they see"

**【解析】** 作者建议家长和老师选择有积极意义的节目、限制屏幕时间，并帮助青少年批判性思考所见内容。B项正确。A项完全禁止、C项无限制、D项仅烹饪节目均与原文不符。故选B。

**5. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "Reality TV is not all bad or all good... Balance is important: some entertainment is fine, but real learning, exercise, and rest should always come first"

**【解析】** 作者认为真人秀并非全好或全坏，关键在于平衡：适度娱乐可行，但真正的学习、运动和休息应优先。可推知作者强调平衡的重要性。C项正确。A、B项过于绝对；D项与作者观点矛盾。故选C。
