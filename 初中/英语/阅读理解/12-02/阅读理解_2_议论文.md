# 阅读理解（议论文）

**Is Reading Outside the Classroom Important?**

① Many students think reading is only about textbooks and exams. They spend most of their time studying schoolbooks and rarely pick up a novel or a magazine for fun. But teachers and experts say that reading outside the classroom is just as important. Is this really true?

② Supporters believe that extra reading has many advantages. First, it expands vocabulary. When students read stories, articles, or even comic books, they learn new words naturally. This makes reading easier over time. Second, reading helps improve writing. By reading different styles of writing, students pick up ideas about how to organise their own essays. Third, reading broadens the mind. A student who reads about history, science, and culture knows more about the world. This knowledge helps in school subjects and in daily conversations.

③ However, some students argue that they simply do not have time. With homework, tests, and after-school classes, there is little room for extra reading. They believe that focusing on textbooks is enough to get good grades. Some parents also worry that novels and comic books may distract children from their studies.

④ While these concerns are understandable, research shows that students who read for pleasure actually perform better in exams, including in subjects like maths and science. This is because reading improves thinking skills, not just language ability. Even 15 minutes of reading before bed can make a difference over time.

⑤ In my opinion, schools and parents should encourage reading outside the classroom. Libraries should be open longer, and teachers can recommend good books. Reading is not a waste of time — it is an investment in a student's future.

**注释：** investment 投资；broaden 拓宽

---

**Questions:**

1. What do many students think about reading?
   A. It is only about textbooks and exams.
   B. It is the most fun activity.
   C. It is only for adults.
   D. It is not important at all.

2. According to Paragraph 2, how does extra reading help students?
   A. It helps them play sports better.
   B. It expands vocabulary, improves writing, and broadens the mind.
   C. It makes exams easier to pass without studying.
   D. It helps them make more money.

3. Why do some students say they cannot read outside the classroom?
   A. Books are too expensive.
   B. They do not like stories.
   C. They have too much homework, tests, and after-school classes.
   D. Their parents read for them.

4. What does research show about students who read for pleasure?
   A. They get worse grades.
   B. They actually perform better in exams.
   C. They spend less time on homework.
   D. They become lazy.

5. What does the writer suggest?
   A. Schools should close their libraries.
   B. Students should only read textbooks.
   C. Schools and parents should encourage reading outside the classroom.
   D. Reading is a waste of time.

---

**Answers and Explanations:**

**1. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "Many students think reading is only about textbooks and exams."

**【解析】** 原文指出许多学生认为阅读只是关于课本和考试。B项"最有趣的活动"与原文所述学生态度不符；C项"只适合成年人"原文未提及；D项"完全不重要"过于绝对。故选A。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "it expands vocabulary" "reading helps improve writing" "reading broadens the mind"

**【解析】** 第②段从三个方面论述了课外阅读的好处：扩大词汇量、提高写作能力、开阔视野。A项运动、C项不学习也能过考试、D项赚钱均无依据。故选B。

**3. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "With homework, tests, and after-school classes, there is little room for extra reading."

**【解析】** 学生说作业、考试和课外班太多，没有时间课外阅读。A项书太贵、B项不喜欢故事、D项父母替他们读均非原文所述。故选C。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "research shows that students who read for pleasure actually perform better in exams"

**【解析】** 研究表明为兴趣而阅读的学生在考试中表现更好。A项成绩更差与原文相反；C项花更少时间做作业和D项变懒均无依据。故选B。

**5. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "schools and parents should encourage reading outside the classroom. Libraries should be open longer, and teachers can recommend good books."

**【解析】** 作者建议学校和家长鼓励课外阅读，图书馆延长开放时间，老师推荐好书。A项"关闭图书馆"与建议相反；B项"只读课本"与作者主张矛盾；D项"浪费时间"与最后一句"it is an investment"直接矛盾。故选C。
