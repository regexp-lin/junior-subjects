# 阅读理解（议论文）

**Should Students Be Allowed to Dye Their Hair?**

① In many schools, students are not allowed to dye their hair. The rules say hair must be black or natural. But some students want to dye their hair brown, red, or other colours. Should schools allow it? Let us think about both sides.

② Those who support the rule say that school is a place for learning, not for fashion. Uniform rules help students focus on study. Dyed hair can distract others. It may also cause comparison — everyone tries to look different, and that leads to competition. Besides, some hair dyes contain chemicals that can harm young people's health. Schools have a duty to protect students. Simple, natural hair keeps things fair and equal for everyone.

③ Those who disagree say that hair colour is a personal choice. It does not affect how well you learn. Expressing yourself through appearance can build confidence. In many countries, students are free to dye their hair. Why should we be different? As long as the colour is not too extreme, it should be acceptable. Young people need space to express who they are.

④ I think both sides have a point. School rules exist for good reasons. But we also need to respect students' wish to express themselves. Perhaps a middle way works: allow natural-looking colours like light brown, but not bright red or green. Or allow dyeing during holidays but not on school days. The key is to balance order with freedom.

⑤ In the end, this is a question each school must decide for itself. What matters is that we listen to students, explain the reasons, and find a solution that works for most.

**注释：** dye 染发；distract 分散注意力

---

**Questions:**

1. Why do some people support the rule against dyed hair?
   A. They think dyed hair looks beautiful.
   B. School is for learning; dyed hair can distract others and some dyes may harm health.
   C. They want students to be fashionable.
   D. Dyed hair helps students study better.

2. What do those who disagree say about hair colour?
   A. It affects grades directly.
   B. It is a personal choice; it does not affect learning; expressing oneself can build confidence.
   C. All students must dye their hair.
   D. Only teachers can dye hair.

3. What middle way does the writer suggest?
   A. No rules at all.
   B. Allow natural-looking colours or allow dyeing only during holidays.
   C. Ban all self-expression.
   D. Everyone must dye their hair the same colour.

4. What does "distract" mean in Paragraph ②?
   A. Help.
   B. Take attention away from something.
   C. Encourage.
   D. Teach.

5. What can we infer from the last paragraph?
   A. There is one correct answer for all schools.
   B. Schools should listen to students and find a solution that works.
   C. Students should never express their opinions.
   D. Hair rules do not matter.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第②段 "school is a place for learning... Dyed hair can distract others... some hair dyes contain chemicals that can harm young people's health"
**【解析】** 支持者认为学校重在学习、染发会分散注意力，且部分染发剂可能有害健康。B项准确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 细节理解
**【定位】** 第③段 "hair colour is a personal choice. It does not affect how well you learn. Expressing yourself through appearance can build confidence"
**【解析】** 反对者认为发色是个人选择、不影响学习，表达自我可增强自信。B项正确。A、C、D项与原文不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 推理判断
**【定位】** 第④段 "Perhaps a middle way works: allow natural-looking colours like light brown, but not bright red or green. Or allow dyeing during holidays but not on school days"
**【解析】** 作者建议折中：允许自然色或仅在假期染发。B项正确。A、C、D项与作者建议不符。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 词义猜测
**【定位】** 第②段 "Dyed hair can distract others"
**【解析】** 上下文指染发会让他人分心。distract 意为"分散注意力"。B项准确。A、C、D项与文意不符。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**
**【题型】** 推理判断
**【定位】** 第⑤段 "we listen to students, explain the reasons, and find a solution that works for most"
**【解析】** 结尾强调学校应听取学生意见、说明理由，并找到适合多数人的方案。B项正确。A、C、D项与文意不符。故选B。
