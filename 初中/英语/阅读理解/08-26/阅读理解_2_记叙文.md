# 阅读理解（记叙文）

① Last month, our school held a Chinese calligraphy (书法) competition. I had been practising calligraphy since primary school, but I had never joined a competition before. When my teacher suggested I take part, I felt both excited and nervous.

② The competition was held in the school hall. About fifty students from different grades took part. We were each given a piece of paper and asked to write a famous ancient poem in traditional Chinese characters. I took a deep breath, dipped my brush in ink, and started to write. My hands were shaking a little at first, but soon I calmed down and focused on each stroke (笔画).

③ When I finished, I looked at my work. It was not perfect — one character was a bit crooked (歪的), and the spacing was not even. I thought I would not win anything. But my teacher had always told me that the most important thing was to try my best and enjoy the process. So I handed in my paper and waited for the results.

④ A week later, the results were announced. I won the third prize! I could not believe it. The judge said that although my work had small mistakes, it showed good control of the brush and a clear style. They encouraged me to keep practising. I was so happy that I ran to tell my parents and teacher right away.

⑤ This experience taught me an important lesson: we do not have to be perfect to achieve something. What matters is that we dare to try and never give up. I will continue to practise calligraphy and maybe join more competitions in the future. After all, every expert was once a beginner.

**注释：** calligraphy 书法；stroke 笔画；crooked 歪的

---

**Questions:**

1. How did the writer feel when the teacher suggested he join the competition?
   A. Only nervous.
   B. Only excited.
   C. Both excited and nervous.
   D. Bored and tired.

2. What did the students need to do in the competition?
   A. Write a modern poem in English.
   B. Write a famous ancient poem in traditional Chinese characters.
   C. Draw a picture.
   D. Read a poem aloud.

3. What did the writer think of his own work when he finished?
   A. It was perfect.
   B. It was not perfect; one character was crooked and spacing was not even.
   C. It was the best of all.
   D. He did not look at it.

4. What prize did the writer win?
   A. First prize.
   B. Second prize.
   C. Third prize.
   D. No prize.

5. What lesson did the writer learn from this experience?
   A. Only perfect work can win.
   B. We do not have to be perfect; what matters is to try and never give up.
   C. Competitions are not important.
   D. Calligraphy is too difficult to learn.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "I felt both excited and nervous"

**【解析】** 原文明确说作者既兴奋又紧张。A、B项只提到一种情绪；D项与原文不符。故选C。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "We were each given a piece of paper and asked to write a famous ancient poem in traditional Chinese characters"

**【解析】** 原文明确说参赛者需用毛笔书写一首著名古诗。A、C、D项与要求不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "It was not perfect — one character was a bit crooked, and the spacing was not even"

**【解析】** 作者认为自己作品不完美，有一字略歪、间距不均。A、C、D项与作者自我评价不符。故选B。

**4. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "I won the third prize!"

**【解析】** 原文明确说作者获得三等奖。A、B、D项与原文不符。故选C。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 主旨大意/写作意图

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "we do not have to be perfect to achieve something. What matters is that we dare to try and never give up"

**【解析】** 作者领悟到不必完美也能有所收获，关键在于敢于尝试、永不放弃。A、C、D项与作者观点矛盾。故选B。
