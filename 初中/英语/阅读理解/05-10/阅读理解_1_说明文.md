# 阅读理解（说明文）

① Have you ever touched your forehead when you felt sick and found it was hot? This means you have a fever. We usually think of a fever as a bad thing, an enemy that makes us feel terrible, tired, and achy (疼痛的). But in fact, a fever is not an enemy. It is a powerful weapon (武器) your body uses to fight against sickness!

② To understand a fever, we need to look inside our brain. There is a small part of the brain called the hypothalamus (下丘脑). It acts like the body's thermostat (恒温器), keeping our normal temperature around 37°C. 

③ When bad germs (细菌) or viruses (病毒) enter our body and make us sick, our white blood cells (白细胞) start fighting them. As they fight, they send chemical signals to the brain. When the hypothalamus receives these signals, it turns up the body's thermostat. The body then produces more heat and stops sweating to keep the heat inside. This is how a fever starts.

④ But why does the body want to be hotter? The answer is simple: germs and viruses hate the heat. They grow best at our normal body temperature of 37°C. When a fever raises the temperature to 38°C or 39°C, the germs cannot multiply (繁殖) easily, and many of them die. Also, the higher temperature makes our white blood cells move faster and fight harder. 

⑤ Therefore, a mild fever (轻度发烧) is actually a sign that your immune system (免疫系统) is working well. Of course, if a fever gets too high (above 40°C) or lasts for several days, it becomes dangerous, and you must see a doctor. But for most common colds, a little heat is exactly what the doctor—and your body—ordered.

**注释：** weapon 武器；hypothalamus 下丘脑；thermostat 恒温器；germ 细菌；virus 病毒；multiply 繁殖；immune system 免疫系统

### 选择题

1. What do people usually think a fever is according to paragraph ①?
A. A powerful weapon to fight against sickness.
B. A bad thing and an enemy that makes them feel terrible.
C. A kind of healthy food.
D. A sign of being extremely happy.

2. Which part of our body acts like a "thermostat" (恒温器) to control temperature?
A. The heart.
B. The white blood cells.
C. The stomach.
D. A small part of the brain called the hypothalamus.

3. How does a fever start?
A. By drinking too much hot water.
B. The white blood cells send signals to the brain, which turns up the body's thermostat.
C. By standing in the hot sun for too long without a hat.
D. The germs produce a lot of heat themselves.

4. Why is a higher body temperature (like 39°C) bad for germs and viruses?
A. Because they love the cold and freeze to death.
B. Because it makes the white blood cells move slower and fall asleep.
C. Because they cannot multiply easily in the heat, and many of them die.
D. Because the heat turns them into good bacteria.

5. When does a fever become dangerous and require a doctor?
A. As soon as you feel a little hot on your forehead.
B. When your body temperature reaches 37°C.
C. When it helps your immune system work well.
D. When it gets too high (above 40°C) or lasts for several days.

---

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段第3句 "We usually think of a fever as a bad thing, an enemy that makes us feel terrible, tired, and achy..."

**【解析】** 我们通常认为发烧是什么？文章开头交待，人们一般认为发烧是一件坏事，是一个让我们感到难受、疲倦、疼痛的敌人（a bad thing, an enemy...）。此结论直接对应B选项。A项是作者揭示的真相（并非人们“通常”认为的），C、D都是胡说八道。故选B。

**2. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "...look inside our brain. There is a small part of the brain called the hypothalamus... It acts like the body's thermostat..."

**【解析】** 身体哪个部位扮演着“恒温器（thermostat）”的角色？原文指明，这是大脑中一个名为下丘脑（hypothalamus）的小区域。选项D将信息完整呈现。A心脏、B白细胞、C胃都不是控温中心。故选D。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段第2-3句 "As they fight, they send chemical signals to the brain... it turns up the body's thermostat."

**【解析】** 发烧是怎么开始的？根据第三段所述过程：白细胞在对抗病菌时会向大脑发送化学信号，下丘脑收到信号后，就会调高身体的恒温器。选项B完美精简了这一病理过程。喝热水（A）、烈日暴晒（C）和病菌产热（D）均完全违背了文中的医学原理解释。故选B。

**4. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节推断理解

**【定位】** 第④段第3句 "When a fever raises the temperature to 38°C or 39°C, the germs cannot multiply (繁殖) easily, and many of them die."

**【解析】** 更高的体温为什么对细菌病毒不利？因为在38度或39度的高温下，病菌无法轻易繁殖，且许多病菌会因此死亡。这与C选项的内容天衣无缝。A项冻死、B白细胞变慢、D变好细菌，均与文意相悖。故选C。

**5. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第⑤段第2句 "...if a fever gets too high (above 40°C) or lasts for several days, it becomes dangerous, and you must see a doctor."

**【解析】** 发烧何种情况会变得危险需要看医生？原文做了明确的界定：当发烧太高（超40°C）或者持续好多天（lasts for several days）时，就产生危险需看病了。选项D正确覆盖了这两种情形。A、B、C项都是不需要看大夫的轻微状况。故选D。
