# 阅读理解（议论文）

**Why Reading Matters for Teenagers**

① Many teenagers spend hours on their phones every day. They watch videos, play games, and chat with friends. But how many of them read books for fun? Studies show that reading has dropped among young people. Yet reading brings benefits that screens cannot give. Here is why reading still matters.

② First, reading helps improve your language skills. When you read, you meet new words and see how they are used in sentences. Your vocabulary grows. Your writing gets better because you learn from good writers. The more you read, the more naturally you can express your ideas. This is useful not only for English but for all subjects that require reading and writing.

③ Second, reading develops your imagination. When you watch a film, the pictures are already there. When you read a book, you create the pictures in your mind. You imagine the characters, the places, and the sounds. This exercise makes your brain stronger. It helps you think creatively and solve problems in new ways.

④ Third, reading can reduce stress. After a busy day at school, opening a good book can help you relax. You step into another world. You forget your worries for a while. Many people find that reading before bed helps them sleep better. Unlike screens, books do not emit blue light that can keep you awake.

⑤ So, even in the age of phones and computers, do not forget the power of books. Try to read for at least 15 minutes every day. Choose books that interest you. Start with short stories if long books feel too heavy. You will soon discover that reading is not a duty but a pleasure.

**注释：** vocabulary 词汇；imagination 想象力；emit 发出；creative 创造性的

---

**Questions:**

1. What does the writer say about reading among young people?
   A. More teenagers are reading than before.
   B. Reading has dropped among young people.
   C. All teenagers love reading.
   D. Reading is only for adults.

2. How does reading help improve language skills?
   A. By watching more videos.
   B. By meeting new words and learning how they are used; vocabulary and writing improve.
   C. By playing games.
   D. By chatting with friends online.

3. Why is reading good for imagination?
   A. Because books have more pictures than films.
   B. Because when you read, you create pictures in your mind.
   C. Because reading is faster than watching films.
   D. Because books are cheaper than films.

4. How can reading help with sleep?
   A. Books emit blue light that helps you sleep.
   B. Reading before bed helps you relax; books do not emit blue light like screens.
   C. Reading makes you feel stressed.
   D. Reading takes a long time so you fall asleep.

5. What does the writer suggest teenagers do?
   A. Stop using phones completely.
   B. Read for at least 15 minutes every day; choose interesting books.
   C. Only read long books.
   D. Read only before exams.

---

**Answers and Explanations:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "Studies show that reading has dropped among young people."

**【解析】** 研究显示年轻人阅读量下降。A项增加与原文相反；C项都喜欢过于绝对；D项仅成人无依据。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "When you read, you meet new words and see how they are used in sentences. Your vocabulary grows. Your writing gets better"

**【解析】** 阅读时接触新词、学习用法，词汇和写作都会提高。A项看视频、C项玩游戏、D项网上聊天均非阅读的作用。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "When you read a book, you create the pictures in your mind."

**【解析】** 读书时在脑海中创造画面，锻炼想象力。A项书比电影图多与"create"矛盾；C、D项与想象力无关。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "Many people find that reading before bed helps them sleep better. Unlike screens, books do not emit blue light that can keep you awake."

**【解析】** 睡前阅读有助于放松入睡，书本不像屏幕发出使人清醒的蓝光。A项书本 emit 蓝光与原文相反；C项增加压力错误；D项因时间长而睡着非主要原因。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "Try to read for at least 15 minutes every day. Choose books that interest you. Start with short stories if long books feel too heavy."

**【解析】** 作者建议每天至少读15分钟，选感兴趣的书，长篇难啃可从短篇开始。A项完全不用手机过于极端；C项只读长篇与建议矛盾；D项仅考前读与"every day"不符。故选B。
