# 阅读理解（说明文）

**Driverless Cars: How Do They Work?**

① Driverless cars, also called autonomous vehicles, can drive themselves without a human driver. They use cameras, sensors, and computers to "see" the road and make decisions. Many companies are testing such cars. Some experts believe they will change how we travel in the future.

② The car has many "eyes." Cameras take photos of the road, other cars, traffic lights, and pedestrians. Radar and lidar use waves to measure distance. They can "see" in the dark and in bad weather. All this information is sent to a powerful computer inside the car. The computer acts like a brain. It analyses the data and decides: speed up, slow down, turn left, turn right, or stop.

③ Driverless cars follow traffic rules. They do not get tired or sleepy. They do not use their phones while driving. In theory, they could reduce accidents caused by human mistakes. However, they still face challenges. What if the sensor fails? What if the road is unclear? What about heavy rain or snow? Engineers are working to solve these problems.

④ Some cities have already started testing driverless buses or taxis. In China, several companies run pilot programmes in certain areas. Passengers can book a ride through an app. The car arrives, they get in, and the car drives them to their destination. No driver is needed.

⑤ It may take many years before driverless cars become common everywhere. Safety must come first. But the technology is developing fast. Perhaps one day, driving will be a choice, not a skill everyone must learn.

**注释：** autonomous 自动的；sensor 传感器；pedestrian 行人；radar 雷达；lidar 激光雷达

---

**Questions:**

1. What do driverless cars use to "see" the road?
   A. Only human eyes.
   B. Cameras, sensors, and computers.
   C. Maps only.
   D. Other drivers' help.

2. What does the computer inside the car do?
   A. It drives the car by itself.
   B. It analyses data and decides how to drive (speed, turn, stop).
   C. It only takes photos.
   D. It talks to passengers.

3. According to the passage, what could driverless cars reduce?
   A. The number of cars on the road.
   B. Accidents caused by human mistakes.
   C. The cost of fuel.
   D. Travel time.

4. Where are driverless cars being tested in China?
   A. In every city.
   B. In certain areas only.
   C. Only in Beijing.
   D. Only on highways.

5. Why might it take many years before driverless cars become common?
   A. Because they are too expensive.
   B. Because safety must come first and challenges remain.
   C. Because people do not like them.
   D. Because there are no roads for them.

---

**Answers and Explanations:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段 "They use cameras, sensors, and computers to 'see' the road and make decisions."

**【解析】** 无人驾驶汽车用摄像头、传感器和电脑"看"路并做决策。A项仅人眼、C项仅地图、D项其他司机帮助均与原文不符。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "The computer acts like a brain. It analyses the data and decides: speed up, slow down, turn left, turn right, or stop."

**【解析】** 车内电脑分析数据并决定加速、减速、转弯或停车。A项过于笼统；C项仅拍照是摄像头的功能；D项与乘客对话原文未提及。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "In theory, they could reduce accidents caused by human mistakes."

**【解析】** 理论上可减少人为错误导致的事故。A项路上车辆数量、C项燃油成本、D项出行时间均未在减少事故的讨论范围内。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "In China, several companies run pilot programmes in certain areas."

**【解析】** 中国多家公司在部分地区进行试点。A项每座城市、C项仅北京、D项仅高速公路均与"certain areas"不符。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "It may take many years before driverless cars become common everywhere. Safety must come first. But the technology is developing fast."

**【解析】** 安全优先，挑战仍存，需多年才能普及。A项太贵、C项人们不喜欢、D项没有道路均非主要原因。故选B。
