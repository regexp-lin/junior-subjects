# 阅读理解（议论文）

① Should schools publish (公布) exam rankings? In the past, many Chinese schools used to put student rankings on the wall or hand them out to parents. Everyone could see who was first, who was second, and who was last. Now, some schools have stopped doing this. The topic has caused a lot of discussion among teachers, parents, and students.

② Those who support publishing rankings give several reasons. First, it can encourage students to work harder. When students see their rank, they may want to improve and do better next time. Second, it helps parents understand how their child is doing compared with others. They can see if their child needs more help. Third, it is honest and clear. Everyone knows where they stand. Some people also say that ranking is a part of real life — in jobs and exams, we are often compared with others. Students should learn to face it.

③ However, those who oppose publishing rankings have strong arguments. First, it can harm students' self-esteem (自尊). Students who rank low may feel ashamed and lose confidence. They may be laughed at by classmates or scolded by parents. Second, it can create too much pressure. Students may become overly focused on scores and rankings instead of actually learning. Third, ranking does not tell the whole story. A student who improves from 40th to 30th has made real progress, but the ranking still shows 30th. Fourth, some education experts say that comparing students publicly can damage their mental health, especially for teenagers who are still growing.

④ In recent years, the Chinese government has made rules to reduce the pressure on students. Many schools no longer publish rankings publicly. Instead, they may tell students their own scores and their rank in a private way. Some schools only use grades like "excellent," "good," or "needs improvement" without giving exact numbers.

⑤ There is no perfect answer. The key is to balance honesty about progress with care for students' feelings. Schools and parents should think about what is best for each child. For some students, knowing their rank may push them to do better. For others, it may cause unnecessary stress. Understanding and respect are more important than a single number on a piece of paper.

**注释：** publish 公布；self-esteem 自尊

**Questions:**

1. According to Paragraph ②, why do some people support publishing rankings?
   A. Because it is more fun.
   B. Because it can encourage students to work harder and help parents understand their child's level.
   C. Because it is free.
   D. Because teachers demand it.

2. Why do some people oppose publishing rankings?
   A. Because rankings are too expensive.
   B. Because it can harm students' self-esteem and create too much pressure.
   C. Because rankings are always wrong.
   D. Because parents do not want to know.

3. What have many schools done in recent years?
   A. They publish rankings more often.
   B. They no longer publish rankings publicly; some tell students privately.
   C. They only give rankings to the top 10 students.
   D. They have stopped giving exams.

4. The word "scolded" in Paragraph ③ probably means _______.
   A. praised loudly
   B. spoken to in an angry way for doing something wrong
   C. given a gift
   D. laughed at kindly

5. What is the writer's main point in the last paragraph?
   A. Rankings should always be published.
   B. Schools should balance honesty with care for students' feelings and consider what is best for each child.
   C. Rankings are useless and should be banned.
   D. Only top students matter.

---

**Answers and Analysis:**

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "First, it can encourage students to work harder... Second, it helps parents understand how their child is doing compared with others."

**【解析】** 支持者认为公布排名能激励学生、帮助家长了解孩子水平。A项"更有趣"、C项"免费"和D项"老师要求"均未作为主要理由出现。故选B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "it can harm students' self-esteem... it can create too much pressure"

**【解析】** 反对者认为公布排名会伤害自尊、增加压力。A项"太贵"、C项"总是错的"和D项"家长不想知道"均与原文不符。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "Many schools no longer publish rankings publicly. Instead, they may tell students their own scores and their rank in a private way."

**【解析】** 近年许多学校不再公开排名，改为私下告知学生成绩和排名。A项"更频繁公布"与文意相反；C项"只给前10名"和D项"停止考试"原文未提及。故选B。

**4. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 词义猜测

**【定位】** 第③段 "They may be laughed at by classmates or scolded by parents"

**【解析】** "scolded"与"laughed at"并列，同为负面行为，指父母因孩子排名低而生气责备。A项"表扬"、C项"送礼"和D项"善意嘲笑"与语境不符。故选B。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 主旨大意

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "The key is to balance honesty about progress with care for students' feelings. Schools and parents should think about what is best for each child."

**【解析】** 作者强调要在诚实反馈与照顾学生感受之间平衡，并考虑每个孩子的具体情况。A项"应始终公布"、C项"应禁止"和D项"只有尖子生重要"均与作者观点不符。故选B。
