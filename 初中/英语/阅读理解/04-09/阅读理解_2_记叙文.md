# 阅读理解（记叙文）

① Last month, my school organized a volunteer (志愿者) trip to a special community center. This center was built to help children whose parents were working in big cities far away from home. We call them "left-behind children" (留守儿童). I signed up immediately with my best friend, Sarah.

② When we arrived on a Saturday morning, the children looked a little shy. They stood behind their teachers and watched us quietly. We brought many books, new pencils, and footballs. Our teacher, Mr. Jones, told us to play with them and make them feel comfortable. 

③ I walked up to a little boy named Ping. He was holding a broken toy car. I smiled and said, "Hello! Let's read a storybook together, okay?" He nodded slowly. As we read the story of *The Little Prince*, his eyes began to shine. He asked me many questions about the planets and stars. Sarah played football with a group of girls on the playground. They ran and laughed happily.

④ Time flew by so fast. In the afternoon, we had to leave. Ping held my hand tightly and asked, "Will you come back again?" I was deeply moved (感动). "Yes, I promise," I said. 

⑤ On the bus back home, I felt a little tired but very warm inside. This volunteer experience taught me a lot. It made me realize that doing something small, like reading a book or sharing a smile, can bring so much light to someone's world. I can't wait to go back.

**注释：** volunteer 志愿者；nod 点头

---

### 选择题

1. Who are the "left-behind children" mentioned in paragraph ①?
A. Children who did not finish their homework.
B. Children who lost their way in the street.
C. Children whose parents work far away in big cities.
D. Children who like reading books at home alone.

2. How did the children behave (表现) when the volunteers first arrived?
A. They shouted happily and rushed to the bus.
B. They looked a little shy and watched quietly.
C. They cried loudly and asked for their parents.
D. They began to play football right away.

3. What did the writer do with the little boy named Ping?
A. Played football with him on the playground.
B. Read a storybook named *The Little Prince* together.
C. Gave him a new toy car.
D. Taught him how to draw stars.

4. What does the underlined word "tightly" in paragraph ④ mean?
A. With a lot of force (紧紧地).
B. With a very loud voice.
C. In a very cold way.
D. In an angry manner.

5. What did the writer learn from this volunteer experience?
A. Reading *The Little Prince* is very difficult.
B. Playing football is the best way to help kids.
C. It is better to give money than books.
D. Small acts of kindness can bring great joy to others.

---

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第①段第2-3句 "This center was built to help children whose parents were working in big cities far away from home. We call them 'left-behind children' (留守儿童)."

**【解析】** 根据原文，“留守儿童”指的是父母在远方大城市工作（parents were working in big cities far away）的孩子们。A（没做完作业）、B（迷路）、D（独自在家看书）皆不相干。故选C。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段第1-2句 "When we arrived... the children looked a little shy. They stood behind their teachers and watched us quietly."

**【解析】** 原文描述当志愿者抵达时，孩子们表现得有些害羞（a little shy）并且安静地在老师后面看着。A（快乐大喊）、C（大声哭闹）、D（马上开始踢球）与文中孩子们刚开始的胆怯表现相反。故选B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段第3-4句 "...Let's read a storybook together,... As we read the story of *The Little Prince*..."

**【解析】** 小男孩名叫Ping，作者和他一起读了一本叫《小王子》（*The Little Prince*）的故事书。A项提及的踢足球是Sarah在操场上与女孩们一起做的；文中虽提到Ping拿着弄坏的玩具车，但作者并未给他新玩具车（C项错误）；D项教他画星星未提及，只提到Ping问了星球和星星的问题。故选B。

**4. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 词义猜测

**【定位】** 第④段第2问 "Ping held my hand tightly and asked, 'Will you come back again?'"

**【解析】** 当作者下午要离开时，Ping问其能否再来。结合情境，一个舍不得你走的孩子应该是“用力地／紧紧地（with a lot of force）”握住你的手。而B项大嗓门、C项极冷漠、D项愤怒的方法都不符合语境。故选A。

**5. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 主旨大意

**【定位】** 第⑤段第3句 "It made me realize that doing something small, like reading a book or sharing a smile, can bring so much light to someone's world."

**【解析】** 作者在车上的感悟是：一件像读一本书或分享一个微笑这样微小的事（small acts of kindness），竟然可以给某人的世界带去如此多的光亮/快乐（bring so much light）。这也正是全文赞扬奉献精神和志愿者体验的主题。A、B、C项全是从个别表面行为得出的荒谬结论。故选D。
