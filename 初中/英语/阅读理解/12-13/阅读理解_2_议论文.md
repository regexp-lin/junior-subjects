# 阅读理解（议论文）

## **Staying Safe on the Internet**

①The Internet brings us many good things. We can learn, make friends, and have fun online. But the Internet also has dangers. As students, we need to know how to stay safe. This is a topic every teenager should think about seriously.

②First, protect your personal information. Never share your real name, address, phone number, or school name with strangers online. Some people may pretend to be your age or your friend, but they might have bad intentions. If someone asks for your photo or personal details, say no and tell your parents.

③Second, be careful what you post. Everything you put on the Internet can stay there forever. A photo or a message you send today might be seen by others years later. Before you post, ask yourself: Would I be happy if my parents or teachers saw this? If the answer is no, do not post it.

④Third, do not believe everything you read online. Fake news and scams are everywhere. If something sounds too good to be true, it probably is. Check the source before you share. Never click on strange links or download files from people you do not know.

⑤The Internet is a useful tool when we use it wisely. Let us learn to enjoy its benefits while avoiding its risks. Your safety is in your own hands. Make smart choices, and the online world can be a safe place for you.

## 题目

1. What is the writer's main purpose in this passage?
   A. To tell students to stop using the Internet.
   B. To teach students how to stay safe online.
   C. To introduce new online games.
   D. To praise the benefits of the Internet only.

2. According to paragraph 2, what should you do when a stranger asks for your personal details?
   A. Share them if they seem friendly.
   B. Say no and tell your parents.
   C. Give a fake name only.
   D. Block them and keep it secret.

3. Why should we be careful about what we post online?
   A. Because our phones have limited storage.
   B. Because posts can stay online forever and be seen by others.
   C. Because it costs money to post.
   D. Because teachers will give us bad grades.

4. What does the writer say about things that "sound too good to be true"?
   A. They are always real.
   B. They are probably not true.
   C. We should share them quickly.
   D. They come from trusted friends.

5. What can we infer from the last paragraph?
   A. The Internet is always dangerous.
   B. We can enjoy the Internet safely if we make smart choices.
   C. Students should never go online.
   D. Parents must control everything we do online.

---

## 答案与解析

**1. 答案：B**

【题型】写作目的/主旨题

【定位】第①段及全文

【解析】
- 第①段提出：网络有危险，学生需要学会如何安全使用；后文围绕保护个人信息、谨慎发帖、不信谣言等展开。
- A“停止上网”与“享受其好处”矛盾。C“介绍游戏”未提及。D“只表扬好处”与文中强调风险不符。故答案为 B。

**2. 答案：B**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第②段

【解析】
- 第②段写：若有人索要照片或个人信息，应拒绝并告知父母（“If someone asks for your photo or personal details, say no and tell your parents”）。
- A 与“say no”矛盾。C“只给假名”不完整，且文中未提。D“保密”与“tell your parents”矛盾。故答案为 B。

**3. 答案：B**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第③段

【解析】
- 第③段说明：发布到网络上的内容可能永久存在，多年后仍会被他人看到（“Everything you put on the Internet can stay there forever”）。
- A“手机存储有限”、C“发帖要花钱”、D“老师会给低分”均未提及。故答案为 B。

**4. 答案：B**

【题型】细节理解题

【定位】第④段

【解析】
- 第④段指出：“If something sounds too good to be true, it probably is”（听起来太好的事，多半不是真的）。
- A 与原文相反。C“应尽快分享”与“check the source”矛盾。D“来自可信朋友”未提及。故答案为 B。

**5. 答案：B**

【题型】推理判断题

【定位】第⑤段

【解析】
- 第⑤段总结：明智使用网络可享受其好处、规避风险；做出聪明选择，网络世界可以很安全（“Your safety is in your own hands... Make smart choices, and the online world can be a safe place”）。
- A“总是危险”与最后一句矛盾。C“绝不上网”与“enjoy its benefits”矛盾。D“父母控制一切”未提及。故答案为 B。
