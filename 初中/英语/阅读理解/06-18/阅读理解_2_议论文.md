# 阅读理解（议论文）

① Is getting up early good for teenagers? Parents and teachers often say that early risers do better in school and stay healthier. But many teenagers find it hard to wake up before 7:00 am. They feel sleepy in the morning and cannot think clearly. So, is it really good for teenagers to get up early?

② **Arguments for early rising:** Supporters of early rising say that teenagers who wake up early have more time in the morning. They can eat a proper breakfast, review their lessons, or do some exercise before school. This helps them feel more energetic and focused during the day. Also, many successful people get up early. They believe that forming the habit of early rising when young will benefit teenagers for the rest of their lives.

③ **Arguments against early rising:** However, scientists have found that teenagers' bodies work differently. During the teenage years, the brain produces melatonin (褪黑激素) later at night. This makes teenagers naturally fall asleep later and need to sleep longer in the morning. If they are forced to get up too early, they may not get enough sleep. Lack of sleep can lead to problems such as poor grades, mood swings, and even health issues. Some schools have tried starting classes later — for example, at 9:00 am instead of 7:30 am — and found that students performed better and felt happier.

④ **What is the balance?** Experts suggest that the most important thing is not when you wake up, but whether you get enough sleep. Teenagers need about 8 to 10 hours of sleep each night. If a teenager goes to bed at 11:00 pm, waking up at 7:00 am gives 8 hours, which is acceptable. But if they go to bed at midnight, waking up at 6:00 am would mean only 6 hours of sleep — that is not enough. The key is to find a schedule that allows enough sleep while still meeting school and family needs.

⑤ In conclusion, early rising may be good for some teenagers, but it is not right for everyone. What really matters is getting enough quality sleep. Parents and schools should think about teenagers' natural sleep patterns and help them develop healthy habits that work for their bodies.

**注释：** melatonin 褪黑激素；mood swing 情绪波动；schedule 作息安排

### 选择题

1. According to paragraph ②, which is a benefit of early rising?
   A. Teenagers have more time to eat breakfast and prepare for the day.
   B. Teenagers can sleep longer.
   C. Teenagers can skip school.
   D. Teenagers need less sleep.

2. Why do scientists say teenagers find it hard to get up early?
   A. Because they are lazy.
   B. Because their brains produce melatonin later, so they fall asleep later and need more morning sleep.
   C. Because they do not like school.
   D. Because they stay up playing games.

3. What have some schools found when they started classes later?
   A. Students came to school even later.
   B. Students performed better and felt happier.
   C. Students slept during class.
   D. Parents complained more.

4. According to experts, what matters most for teenagers?
   A. Waking up before 6:00 am.
   B. Going to bed before 9:00 pm.
   C. Getting enough sleep (about 8–10 hours each night), not the exact waking time.
   D. Never sleeping during the day.

5. What can we infer about the writer's view?
   A. All teenagers must get up early.
   B. Early rising is wrong for everyone.
   C. Schools should never change their start times.
   D. The writer thinks sleep quality and amount matter more than forcing early rising; schools and parents should respect teenagers' natural sleep patterns.

---

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】A**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "teenagers who wake up early have more time in the morning. They can eat a proper breakfast, review their lessons, or do some exercise before school."

**【解析】** 早起支持者认为早起者早晨有更多时间吃早餐、复习功课或锻炼。A 项正确。B 项睡更久、C 项逃课、D 项少睡均为反对早起的观点，非本段益处。故选 A。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "During the teenage years, the brain produces melatonin later at night. This makes teenagers naturally fall asleep later and need to sleep longer in the morning."

**【解析】** 科学家指出青少年大脑夜间较晚分泌褪黑激素，导致自然入睡较晚、早晨需更多睡眠。B 项正确。A 项懒惰、C 项不喜欢学校、D 项玩游戏均为主观推测，非科学依据。故选 B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "Some schools have tried starting classes later... and found that students performed better and felt happier."

**【解析】** 部分学校延后上课时间后，学生表现更好、更开心。B 项正确。A 项到校更晚、C 项上课睡觉、D 项家长投诉更多均未提及。故选 B。

**4. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第④段 "the most important thing is not when you wake up, but whether you get enough sleep. Teenagers need about 8 to 10 hours of sleep each night."

**【解析】** 专家强调关键是睡眠是否充足，而非起床时间；青少年每晚需约 8–10 小时睡眠。C 项正确。A 项 6 点前起床、B 项 9 点前睡觉、D 项白天不睡均非核心观点。故选 C。

**5. 【答案】D**

**【题型】** 推理判断 / 作者观点

**【定位】** 第⑤段 "early rising may be good for some teenagers, but it is not right for everyone... What really matters is getting enough quality sleep... Parents and schools should think about teenagers' natural sleep patterns"

**【解析】** 作者认为早起对部分人有益但不适合所有人；睡眠质量与时长更为重要；家长和学校应尊重青少年的自然睡眠规律。D 项准确概括。A 项"所有青少年必须早起"与原文相反；B 项"早起对所有人都是错的"过于绝对；C 项"学校绝不能改时间"与延后上课的积极结果矛盾。故选 D。
