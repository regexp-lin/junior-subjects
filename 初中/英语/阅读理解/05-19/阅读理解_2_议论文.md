# 阅读理解（议论文）

① Many teenagers today have a pet at home — a dog, a cat, a rabbit, or even a fish. Some parents wonder whether keeping a pet is good for their children. In fact, pets can bring both benefits and responsibilities (责任) to teenagers, and it is important for families to understand both sides.

② First, pets are good for teenagers' emotional health (情感健康). Studies show that young people who spend time with animals often feel less stressed and lonely. When a teenager comes home from school feeling tired or sad, a dog waiting at the door or a cat lying on the bed can make them feel loved and needed. Pets can also help shy children become more confident (自信的) because animals do not judge them. They simply offer unconditional (无条件的) love and friendship.

③ Second, taking care of a pet teaches teenagers responsibility. Feeding the pet, cleaning its living space, and taking a dog for walks — these tasks help young people learn to be dependable (可靠的). Parents often find that their children become more careful and patient after they start caring for a pet. This experience can prepare teenagers for future challenges in life, such as looking after their own family one day.

④ However, having a pet is not always easy. Pets need time, money, and attention. If a teenager promises to feed the dog but forgets, the dog will be hungry. If the family goes on a long trip, someone must take care of the pet. Teenagers must understand that a pet is not a toy — it is a living being that depends on them. Before getting a pet, families should discuss who will do what and whether everyone is ready for this commitment (承诺).

⑤ In my view, pets can be wonderful for teenagers' growth when families make a careful plan. Choose a pet that fits your home and lifestyle. Make sure your child is old enough to help with the care. When teenagers learn to love and respect animals, they often become kinder and more responsible people. The key is balance: enjoy the joy that pets bring, but never forget the responsibility they require.

**注释：** responsibility 责任；emotional 情感的；unconditional 无条件的；dependable 可靠的；commitment 承诺

---

### 选择题

1. According to paragraph ②, how do pets help teenagers' emotional health?
   A. By making them study harder at school.
   B. By reducing stress and loneliness, and making them feel loved.
   C. By teaching them how to cook.
   D. By helping them make more money.

2. What does paragraph ③ say about teenagers who take care of pets?
   A. They become less patient.
   B. They learn to be more dependable and careful.
   C. They have no time to do homework.
   D. They spend all their money on pets.

3. What does the word "commitment" in paragraph ④ most probably mean?
   A. A kind of pet food.
   B. A promise or duty that one must keep.
   C. A school activity.
   D. A type of game.

4. What can we infer from paragraph ④?
   A. Pets never need any care.
   B. Teenagers should treat a pet as a toy.
   C. Having a pet requires time, money and responsibility — it is a serious decision.
   D. Only adults should take care of pets.

5. What is the writer's main purpose in writing this passage?
   A. To tell readers that pets are not good for teenagers.
   B. To encourage families to discuss both the benefits and responsibilities before getting a pet.
   C. To advise teenagers to buy expensive pets.
   D. To explain why dogs are better than cats.

---

### 详细解析

**1. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第②段 "young people who spend time with animals often feel less stressed and lonely" 及 "a dog waiting at the door or a cat lying on the bed can make them feel loved and needed."

**【解析】** 原文指出与宠物相处的青少年压力更小、孤独感减少，宠物能让他们感到被爱和被需要。A 项"学得更努力"、C 项"学做饭"、D 项"赚更多钱"均与情感健康无关。故选 B。

**2. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 细节理解

**【定位】** 第③段 "these tasks help young people learn to be dependable" 及 "their children become more careful and patient after they start caring for a pet."

**【解析】** 照料宠物能让青少年学会可靠、更细心和有耐心。A 项"变得更没耐心"与原文相反；C 项"没时间做作业"、D 项"把钱都花在宠物上"文中未提及。故选 B。

**3. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 词义猜测

**【定位】** 第④段 "whether everyone is ready for this commitment"

**【解析】** 语境中，在养宠物前需讨论是否每个人都准备好承担这一 commitment，应为"承诺/义务"之意。A 项宠物食品、C 项学校活动、D 项游戏类型均不符合。故选 B。

**4. 【答案】C**

**【题型】** 推理判断

**【定位】** 第④段 "Pets need time, money, and attention" 及 "a pet is not a toy — it is a living being that depends on them" 及 "whether everyone is ready for this commitment."

**【解析】** 作者强调宠物需要时间、金钱和关注，是依赖主人的生命，养宠物是严肃决定。A 项"宠物从不需要照料"与原文矛盾；B 项"把宠物当玩具"与"not a toy"相悖；D 项"只有成人应照料宠物"过度推断。故选 C。

**5. 【答案】B**

**【题型】** 写作意图

**【定位】** 第①段 "pets can bring both benefits and responsibilities... it is important for families to understand both sides" 及第⑤段 "when families make a careful plan" 及 "The key is balance."

**【解析】** 作者旨在说明宠物既有好处也有责任，建议家庭在养宠物前仔细规划、讨论利弊。A 项"宠物对青少年不好"与作者观点相反；C 项"买贵宠物"、D 项"狗比猫好"均未在文中体现。故选 B。
